## Chapter 2807 — Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice

"You... you..." In front of the shabby wooden hut, the village chief stared at Yang Kai in shock, his lips opening and closing, his cloudy eyes filled with horror, unable to speak coherently.

After just one night apart, he discovered that Ah Niu had actually become a true Shaman Apprentice!

A Shaman Apprentice!

He had struggled his whole life and was only a Shaman Apprentice, yet Ah Niu had reached it in just one night. In comparison, it felt like he had lived his whole life in vain.

Sensing Yang Kai, he found the Qi and blood within him to be extremely vigorous, shamanic power swirling around his body—far stronger than his own as a Shaman Apprentice, by more than double.

"Village chief, time is short, let's not waste words. Whatever special skills you have, hurry and teach me." Yang Kai walked straight into the village chief's hut.

He had been in this village for three days already, with only two days left to use, so he naturally had to make the most of his time.

The village chief hesitated for a moment, then followed him in as told.

The whole morning passed with the two of them immersed in teaching and learning.

Besides teaching Yang Kai the few Shamanic Arts he knew, the village chief also explained the divisions of shamanic power.

Yesterday, Yang Kai had only just awakened shamanic power and gained the recognition of the Barbarian God. Strictly speaking, he wasn't yet a true Shaman, but today, he was a real Shaman Apprentice—the entry-level realm for Shamans.

Above Shaman Apprentice are Shaman Warrior, then Shaman Master, Great Shaman Master, Shaman King, Shaman Saint, and at the highest level, Shaman God!

Each realm is further divided into Upper, Middle, and Lower Levels.

For example, the village chief was a Middle-Level Shaman Apprentice! But now, Yang Kai was a genuine Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice, one grade higher than the village chief, just a step away from advancing to Shaman Warrior. After becoming a Shaman Warrior, he could learn even more powerful Shamanic Arts, but unfortunately, the village chief didn't know any himself and thus couldn't teach them.

According to the village chief, among all the realms, the most powerful Shaman God was a legend and hadn't appeared for many years. There were a few Shaman Saints, but they all resided in the strongest tribes.

As for Cangnan Village, it belonged to the Southern Barbarian Tribe, and the strongest Shaman in the tribe was only a Shaman King, one realm below a Shaman Saint.

After a morning of instruction, Yang Kai not only learned many ancient characters but also gained a deeper understanding of this ancient world.

More importantly, Yang Kai learned the Bloodlust Art!

This was his first real gain in this secret realm. The barbarian Shaman cultivation method he learned the day before wasn't of much value—just a way to cultivate in this world, but useless once he left. The Bloodlust Art, however, might come in handy at times.

But the village chief also said that the Bloodlust Art he knew was the lowest grade, with a short duration and severe side effects. The Great Shamans of the major tribes had much better versions, which, though still having side effects, wouldn't leave someone weak for days.

Of course, casting such advanced Bloodlust Arts also required powerful strength as support.

By noon, Yang Kai walked out of the village chief's house.

It wasn't that Yang Kai lacked ambition, but the village chief had nothing more to teach. Aside from those ancient characters, he had already imparted all his life's knowledge, and Yang Kai had absorbed it all at once, leaving the village chief in awe.

"Come learn characters for an hour each day from now on. As for cultivation, you'll have to rely on your own efforts. I have nothing more to teach you." When the village chief sent Yang Kai out, his tone was a bit desolate yet gratified.

No matter the era, the new always replaces the old, but Yang Kai's outstanding performance still made the village chief feel his own inadequacy and helplessness.

Suddenly, the sounds of strenuous shouts and miserable cries reached Yang Kai's ears. He was startled, thinking the village was under attack again.

Looking up, Yang Kai realized he was overthinking. In the spacious area of the village, the barbarians had assumed all manner of bizarre and outrageous postures, regardless of gender.

Their muscles trembled nonstop, and large beads of sweat rolled down their skin, soaking the ground.

In all sorts of postures, these barbarians seemed to be struggling greatly, occasionally letting out a scream or two, yet still persevering.

With his sharp eyes, Yang Kai immediately saw that this was a body tempering method. He walked over in curiosity. Though he didn't yet know what effect this training would have, judging by the villagers' physiques, this method was definitely extraordinary.

"Ah Niu!" With his head down and hands braced behind him, body arched like a bow, Ah Hu called out when he saw Yang Kai approach. But as soon as he spoke, his face changed as if struck, and his taut body suddenly sprang up, flying over a dozen zhang before crashing into a big tree and spitting blood.

Yang Kai was shocked and hurried over to help him up. "Are you alright?"

"I'm fine, I'm fine!" Ah Hu grinned, wiping the blood from his mouth as if he was used to it. "Congratulations! I heard from the village chief that you've awakened shamanic power?"

"Just lucky," Yang Kai replied with a smile.

Ah Hu shook his head. "This isn't luck, it's the Barbarian God's blessing!" He patted Yang Kai's shoulder heavily and said earnestly, "Ah Niu, you're a Shaman now, so you're the next village chief. Train well—the village's future depends on you."

Yang Kai responded with a wry smile, thinking to himself that he'd be leaving in two days, so what future was there? But facing what might be just a figure in an illusion, he didn't say more.

Changing the subject, he said, "I see you all are training..."

Ah Hu said, "That's right, want to join us?"

Yang Kai nodded, "Sure, but I don't know how."

"Come, I'll teach you!" Ah Hu was extremely enthusiastic, pulling Yang Kai into the training crowd, grinning from ear to ear.

Ah Niu really was different now. After receiving the Barbarian God's favor, he had truly changed. Before, Ah Hu had tried to get him to train together, but Ah Niu always refused, preferring to hide in his hut rather than interact with the villagers.

But today, Ah Niu actually took the initiative to train, so Ah Hu couldn't be happier.

Even though Ah Niu was now a Shaman, a strong body was still fundamental.

So, Ah Hu began teaching Yang Kai how to train. These barbarians weren't Shamans and hadn't awakened shamanic power, so their training naturally focused on the body—in other words, body tempering!

Following the barbarians around him, who were using all sorts of strange poses to temper their bodies, Ah Hu explained the various stances of this body tempering method to Yang Kai, who memorized them carefully.

The importance of the body wasn't just crucial for the Ancient Barbarian Tribe—even in the present world, it couldn't be ignored. Nowadays, most martial artists only focus on raising their cultivation realm, neglecting the tempering of the body, not realizing that the body is the vessel for all power. Only with a strong body can one unleash greater strength.

Yang Kai himself was already reaping the benefits of a powerful physical body, so he naturally understood this deeply.

He mastered the entire barbarian body tempering method in less than half an hour.

He was secretly amazed. Though he hadn't personally tested it, this barbarian body tempering method was undoubtedly a profound technique. Was this really what the Ancient Barbarian Tribe used? No wonder they were all as strong as tigers and dragons!

Whether this place was an illusion or not, and whether the people and things he encountered were real or not, just this body tempering method alone made Yang Kai's trip worthwhile.

Yang Kai's physical strength was already formidable, so this method didn't offer him much room for improvement. But what about the disciples of Lingxiao Palace? Or his friends and family still in the Star Domain?

If they trained with this barbarian body tempering method, their physical qualities would definitely improve by several levels, and raising their cultivation would become much easier.

After personally testing it, Yang Kai became even more convinced of his guess.

This barbarian body tempering method was truly extraordinary. Even with his current body, he felt a bit strained while training, his whole body aching, yet his flesh seemed to become even more refined.

The whole afternoon passed in training, and when night fell, the barbarians, exhausted, finally returned home.

Ah Hu warmly invited Yang Kai to his house for a visit.

Yang Kai thought he had nothing to eat at home, so he might as well go to Ah Hu's place for a meal and drink, and agreed at once.

For the next two days, Yang Kai repeated this life: spending an hour each morning at the village chief's learning ancient characters, then training with Ah Hu and the others, and meditating in his hut at night.

Without the essence and inner cores of Yao Beasts to supplement him, Yang Kai's cultivation slowed down. In two whole days, he still couldn't break through the Shaman Apprentice threshold.

But it didn't matter. This was already the fifth day, and the month-long period was almost up. It was time to say goodbye to this world.

He sat alone in his hut, seeing no one, waiting to be expelled from this world.

...

Three days later, Ah Hu came to knock on the door. Soon, the door opened, and Yang Kai looked at him with bloodshot eyes.

"Ah Niu, why haven't you shown up for days? The village chief sent me to check on you! Everyone's worried about you."

Behind Ah Hu, over a hundred villagers stood in neat rows. Even Ah Ni, who had been driven to tears by Yang Kai that night, was among them, her beautiful eyes full of concern.

"Did something happen?" Ah Hu asked nervously.

"It's nothing. I've just been practicing the Shamanic Arts the village chief taught me. Sorry to trouble everyone." Yang Kai forced a smile.

Only then did Ah Hu breathe a sigh of relief. "As long as you're fine. We thought... Oh, right, the village chief said we need to go to the canyon to bring back the dead beasts. You'll need to lead the way. It's about to snow again—if we don't go now, we won't be able to leave the village."

"Alright, you all go ahead. I'll catch up soon!" Yang Kai replied, then closed the door again.

What in the world was happening?! Yang Kai roared inwardly.
