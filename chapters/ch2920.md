## Chapter 2920 — The Demonic Intent Pierces the Void

Under the watchful eyes of all, Yang Tai slammed fiercely into the blood-red gate.

He used all his strength in this collision, making no effort to protect himself, so the outcome was predictable.

With a resounding bang, Yang Tai, just like Wu Kuangyi before him, instantly exploded into a cloud of blood mist.

Yet his blood and flesh did not splatter chaotically across the door as Wu Kuangyi’s had, but instead seemed to possess a certain life, slowly writhing in midair, gradually gathering and recombining.

The crowd had just begun to feel relieved, but now became anxious once more, unsure what this change signified. Yet everyone felt a deep sense of foreboding rise within their hearts—Yang Kai most of all. From the blood mist that Yang Tai had become, he could sense a trace of demonic energy, a presence that made him extremely uncomfortable, as if he had once again entered the ancient world, once more facing the experts of the Demon Domain.

In the blink of an eye, that mass of blood mist took shape.

“This is…” Gong Yue’s eyes widened as he let out a low cry.

“Demon Script!” Yang Kai gritted his teeth and muttered, the unease in his heart swelling to the extreme.

The solidified blood mist had taken the form of a Demon Script character—the very one Yang Tai had revealed when he used his secret technique earlier. Driven by a mysterious force, it slammed violently against the gate.

No one knew what effect this character, formed from the essence of Yang Tai’s blood and flesh as a blood sacrifice, would have on the door. But since Yang Tai had done this, he must have had some confidence.

A brilliant light erupted, so dazzling that it was almost impossible to keep one’s eyes open. As everyone was blinded by the light, a sudden cracking sound rang out.

Everyone was startled. When they realized what the sound was, their faces turned pale.

The blood-red gate had opened!

Though the Blood Sacrifice had been interrupted by Yang Kai, Yang Tai’s desperate final effort pushed it over the edge, becoming the final straw.

Whatever was inside would be revealed in the next moment.

In that instant, Yang Kai pushed his vision to the limit and finally caught a glimpse of something. The blood-red gate had indeed opened, but only a crack—not fully. The many seals and patterns on the door still seemed to be at work, generating a force opposite to that of the demonic rune, preventing the door from opening completely.

Just then, Yang Kai vaguely saw something fly out from the crack in the door.

As the light faded and the door closed again, everything settled as if it had all been an illusion.

Everyone was still shaken, exchanging uneasy glances.

But soon, someone noticed something unusual.

“That… what is that?” Shen Bingru’s voice trembled as she pointed at an object floating in front of the door.

Following the direction of her finger, everyone looked over, their expressions turning solemn.

At some point, a tiny black speck the size of a mung bean had appeared in front of the door. It was so small and emitted no particular aura, so no one had noticed it at first. Besides, the lighting here was poor, and Shen Bingru had only discovered it by chance.

The black speck floated there like a dead thing, utterly still and unremarkable.

But when Yang Kai swept it with his Divine Sense, his face changed dramatically and he shouted, “A demonic intent! Everyone, guard your minds—do not let it corrupt you! If you are corrupted, you will surely enter a demonic state!”

At his words, everyone’s expression changed. But since Yang Kai had just saved them from the jaws of death, even if they didn’t understand, they instinctively reacted, sealing their Seas of Consciousness and guarding their minds.

“Brother Yang, this demonic intent… is it the kind we know of?” Fang Zhuo asked.

The demonic intent he referred to meant the various distracting thoughts that arose on the path of cultivation—greed, lust, jealousy, and hatred. All were inner demons and could sometimes lead one to qi deviation.

Yang Kai shook his head, staring at that mass of black light as if facing a formidable enemy. Memories of his experiences within the Thousand Illusion Dreamscape surfaced in his mind, and he said, “There is a great world called the Demon Domain. The beings there call themselves the demon race, and in ancient times they invaded the Star Realm again and again. The ancient masters drove them back and slew many of their experts. But the demons possess a strange nature—their vitality is extremely tenacious. Some things are very difficult to destroy; even those ancient masters could not eradicate them completely, and could only seal them away. I do not know what is sealed behind this blood-colored gate, but this demonic intent escaped from within, and a demonic intent is a fragment of a demon expert’s soul.”

What he had just seen escape from behind the door was this very black speck.

The others listened, all looking uneasy. The name Demon Domain was new to them. Though they were all Emperor Realm cultivators, and could not claim to know all the secrets of the world, there was little they did not know.

But the term Demon Domain truly was a first for them. As for the demon race, they had only seen scattered references in ancient texts, never in detail.

“Brother Yang, are you saying there’s a demon sealed behind the door?” Fang Zhuo asked with a frown.

“It seems so.”

Shen Bingru said, “This ancient cave residence has existed for tens of thousands, or even over a hundred thousand, years. Even if a demon expert was sealed back then, they couldn’t possibly have survived until now, right?”

Yang Kai replied, “That’s why only a demonic intent appeared. The demons sealed inside are likely all dead, but their remnant souls remain.”

“Just a remnant soul… is it really worth all this fuss?” Shen Bingru forced a smile, feeling that Yang Kai was making too much of it. No matter what was sealed behind the blood-red door, so much time had passed that even if a remnant soul appeared, it probably wouldn’t amount to much.

“What do you know?” Yang Kai shouted angrily.

Shen Bingru fell silent, embarrassed. Her cultivation was higher than Yang Kai’s, and if he hadn’t just saved her life, she might have retorted. Even so, being rebuked like this left her looking rather displeased.

Yang Kai softened his tone and said, “As long as the demonic intent remains, we must never let our guard down, or else… Hm?”

As he spoke, the black speck, which had been motionless, suddenly darted straight toward Yang Kai, as if by instinct or design.

That tiny demonic intent carried a deeply unsettling aura, as if it had locked onto Yang Kai. At the same time, Yang Kai suddenly felt a burning sensation in his right eye.

He immediately understood—the demonic intent had chosen him not by chance, but by instinct.

His right eye had fused with the Demon Eye, containing the aura of an ancient demon. The demonic intent was the remnant soul of a demon expert, so it was naturally drawn to him.

He snorted coldly, chanting a shamanic incantation as he raised his hand and pointed forward.

A ring of light burst forth, sweeping over the oncoming black speck. The mung-bean-sized speck was instantly caught as if in a violent wind and swept back.

Yang Kai was at the end of his strength, unable to exert much power, but this demonic intent had been sealed for too long and was only a remnant soul, not very formidable. Swept by the shamanic art, it was immediately suppressed.

Sensing that Yang Kai was not to be trifled with, the black speck drifted off to the side.

Its new target was Gong Yue.

At this moment, Gong Yue was somewhat dazed. He had been deceived by Yang Tai and had helped him harm several Emperor Realm cultivators of the Southern Territory. Now, with Yang Tai sacrificing himself and a demonic intent emerging from behind the door, Yang Kai’s words had been proven true.

There were no ultimate secrets of the Martial Dao behind the blood-red door—only Yang Tai’s schemes.

Yang Tai wanted to release all the demonic intent sealed behind the door, which was why he had used Gong Yue’s expertise in formations.

As the former Family Head of the Gong family, everything he had done was tied to the fate of the Gong family in Tianhe Valley. After today, the Gong family’s reputation would be utterly ruined, and their future bleak.

Realizing this, Gong Yue fell into despair. Though he heard the others discussing the demonic intent, he barely reacted.

It was only when the demonic intent drifted toward him that Gong Yue finally moved, raising his hand and sending a solid palm strike at it.

Though a master of formations, he was still at the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm. Though this palm was not powerful enough to shake heaven and earth, it was still formidable—at least several times stronger than the shamanic art Yang Kai had just used.

He was confident he could sweep away or even destroy the demonic intent.

But something unexpected happened.

The palm strike passed right through the demonic intent, having no effect at all. The mung-bean-sized speck ignored the attack and passed straight through.

“Careful!” Yang Kai called out. Though Gong Yue bore much of the blame for the current situation, Yang Kai’s experiences in the Thousand Illusion Dreamscape made him unwilling to see Gong Yue corrupted, so he warned him.

Gong Yue snapped to attention, unleashing all his techniques. Though wounded by the Mountain and River Bell, he was still formidable, instantly erecting several layers of solid defenses before him.

The black speck ignored all the defenses, passing straight through and burrowing into his head.

Everyone was stunned.

Gong Yue’s eyes widened in an instant, a hoarse roar escaping his throat as if he were suffering immense torment.

Yang Kai was also dumbfounded. He couldn’t understand why this was happening. He had seen Gong Yue’s defenses and, by rights, they should have been enough to resist the demonic intent’s corruption.

But in reality, they seemed to have had no effect at all.

This shouldn’t be possible…

As Yang Kai was lost in confusion, Gong Yue suddenly let out a low roar. A violent aura burst from his pores, his hair and beard flying wildly, his roar shaking loose stones from the cave walls.

Everyone’s face changed as they nervously watched Gong Yue’s condition.

At this moment, none of them had the strength to resist. If Gong Yue went berserk, they would all be in danger.

Shen Bingru’s voice trembled: “Brother Yang, he… what’s happening to him?”

“He’s been corrupted by the demonic intent—he’s about to enter a demonic state!” Yang Kai gritted his teeth and shouted.
