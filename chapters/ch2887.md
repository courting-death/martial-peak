## Chapter 2887 — Kill

The giant skeletal hand descended, but this time, Yang Kai did not dodge.

As the hand closed, Yang Kai's figure vanished. Over a hundred thousand members of the Bone-Eater Tribe erupted in thunderous cheers, gazing at the five Shaman Kings with reverence.

Yu's face turned pale, nearly unable to accept the scene before her eyes, when a voice came from beside her—it was Die: "He's fine!"

Die was a unique presence in Shaman Niu's tribe. Though only a Great Shaman Master, she seemed to possess an insight even the Shaman Kings lacked. Her words often came true.

She had said before that Yang Kai would come.

And then, Yang Kai came.

So, after her words, everyone in Shaman Niu's tribe seemed to be infected by a sense of calm, their hearts settling considerably.

In contrast, the five Shaman Kings of the Bone-Eater Tribe suddenly changed expressions, showing no joy at their apparent success.

A low murmur came from within the skeletal hand. No one could make out what was said, but after the murmur, a high, piercing dragon's roar suddenly rang out. In the next instant, a massive golden dragon head manifested in a flash and plunged into the palm of the skeletal hand.

A terrifying aura spread out.

The five Shaman Kings of the Bone-Eater Tribe suddenly felt overwhelmingly insignificant.

Crackle...

The tightly clenched skeletal hand was forced open by an immense, invisible power. Through the gaps between the fingers, a tall figure could be vaguely seen standing in the palm.

As a sword light swept out, the skeletal hand trembled, and all five fingers were severed, falling from the sky like five small mountain peaks and sending the Bone-Eater Tribe members below into chaos as they fled in panic.

Having undergone Dragon Transformation, Yang Kai leapt onto the skeletal wrist, the Myriad Sword dragging behind him. He raced along the arm, the sword's edge scraping against the bone, producing a harsh screech and a shower of sparks.

In the blink of an eye, Yang Kai reached the elbow of the skeletal hand, standing alone like a sovereign above all, his stern gaze sweeping over everything below.

The five Shaman Kings of the Bone-Eater Tribe involuntarily looked up, witnessing a scene they would never forget in their lives.

The giant skeletal hand they had conjured together was actually broken open by the brute force of a newly advanced Shaman King. A massive crack ran from the wrist to the elbow, like a ravine on the earth, bearing witness to the passage of time and the changes of eras.

Yang Kai stomped lightly, and the entire skeletal hand split in two. The shamanic art was dispelled, and the giant hand instantly dissolved into motes of light and vanished.

Pointing the Myriad Sword downward, Yang Kai said coldly, "Just because I don't wish to fight you doesn't mean I can't deal with you. Now that the battle has begun, let me give you an unforgettable lesson."

He turned his gaze to Shaman Chi!

Shaman Chi panicked, his body retreating involuntarily. He saw a murderous intent burning in Yang Kai's eyes.

"Save me!" Shaman Chi shouted, trying to move closer to the other Shaman Kings. The bald Shaman King reacted first, rushing toward him.

But a sword light slashed through the air, blocking his path. When it faded, the bald Shaman King stood still, his gaze dark. Over there, Shaman Niu had somehow reached Shaman Chi's side, his broad longsword resting on Shaman Chi's neck.

"Over a hundred villagers of Cangnan Village died because of your Bone-Eater Tribe. Today, killing one Shaman King is just revenge for my people."

Shaman Chi was shocked and shouted, "You dare! The Shaman Saint will never allow this. If you really kill me, the Shaman Saint will hunt you to the ends of the earth."

Yang Kai sneered, "I've just been hunted by a Demon Saint. Do you think I'd fear your Shaman Saint? Besides, isn't he still recovering from his wounds? So, you might as well die now!"

"Stop!" the bald Shaman King suddenly shouted.

Yang Kai turned to look at him, calm as ever. "What else do you wish to say, sir?"

"Let Shaman Chi go, and I'll let you leave!" the bald Shaman King said in a deep voice.

Yang Kai smiled faintly and shook his head. “When I told you to stop earlier, you pressed on step by step. Now that things have gone wrong, you want to make peace? There’s no such convenience in this world. I’m afraid the decision now rests with me.”

The bald Shaman King said, "Killing Shaman Chi will do you no good. It will only make my Bone-Eater Tribe hate you!"

Yang Kai curled his lip. “You make it sound like you wouldn’t hold a grudge if I spared him. Your Bone-Eater Tribe has always repaid every slight in kind—if not for the demon invasion, you’d have already made a move on Cangnan Village. Since there’s already old enmity between us, I don’t mind adding new grudges to it.”

"In this war between the two races, a Shaman King is an indispensable force. You can't kill him."

"Nonsense!" Yang Kai shouted sternly. "I'm also a Shaman King. Who was it that just joined forces to kill me?"

"That was because we thought you had entered a demonic state."

"If I had truly entered a demonic state, all of you would be dead today." As Yang Kai's words fell, the Myriad Sword flashed with cold light in his hand. Shaman Chi's head flew skyward, blood spraying from his severed neck like a fountain.

The remaining four Shaman Kings of the Bone-Eater Tribe were stunned.

They had thought Yang Kai was merely speaking harshly, that he would not truly make a move to kill Shaman Chi. Yet never did they expect he would actually carry it out. Shaman Chi’s body still stood where it was, while his severed head flew off, his face filled with shock, as if even at the moment of death he had never imagined his life would end in such a way.

He did not die at the hands of the demons, but at the hands of the barbarians.

After killing Shaman Chi, Yang Kai instantly appeared before the bald Shaman King, Myriad Sword pressing three inches into his chest.

The bald Shaman King instantly stiffened, not daring to move.

"Don't think I'm joking. If I wished, I could kill all of you." Yang Kai stared coldly at the bald Shaman King. A bead of cold sweat rolled down the latter's forehead and slid along his cheek.

"But you still have value. You can still fight the demons, so I won't kill you today!" Yang Kai withdrew Myriad Sword, looking down at the bald Shaman King from above, his voice sharp. "You'd best remember, don't provoke my Southern Barbarian Tribe again. Otherwise, no matter who it is, they will pay the price."

The bald Shaman King remained silent, clearly deeply shaken inside.

Myriad Sword vanished. Yang Kai brushed past the bald Shaman King and walked straight toward the place where the twenty thousand members of Shaman Niu's tribe were imprisoned.

Along the way, Bone-Eater Tribe members blocking the path looked at him in fear, as if facing a monstrous beast, unconsciously moving aside.

When he arrived before Shaman Niu’s tribe, the Bone-Eater Tribe Great Shaman Master who had been guarding it all this time stood dumbfounded, frozen in place. Yang Kai merely raised his eyelids slightly, and the Great Shaman Master immediately flashed aside to avoid him, a chill spreading across his back as a gust of wind swept past.

"Sir!" Yu, Lu, and the others stepped forward to salute, their eyes brimming with worship.

"Let's go," Yang Kai called out.

Ah Hu stepped forward and said, "They took our mounts."

Yang Kai looked back at the bald Shaman King.

The bald Shaman King's expression was full of struggle. Yang Kai's smile grew meaningful.

After a moment, the bald Shaman King seemed to make a difficult decision and shouted, "Return those things to them!"

Immediately, Bone-Eater Tribe members rushed out. In no time, over a thousand demonic beasts and dozens of giant eagles burst from captivity, returning straight to their masters.

The cavalry mounted their demonic beasts, the Sky Cavalry took the giant eagles, and more than twenty thousand people marched out of Cang'er Valley in grand formation. The entire group was orderly, their steps uniform. Every member of Shaman Niu's tribe stood tall, like spears piercing the heavens.

Behind them were the complex gazes of over a hundred thousand Bone-Eater Tribe members.

Only when Shaman Niu's tribe disappeared from sight did the Bone-Eater Tribe finally breathe a sigh of relief. The four Shaman Kings were utterly exhausted.

Outside the valley, excitement spread. The army's silence was finally broken as people gathered in small groups, discussing what had just happened, growing more animated as they spoke.

No one stopped them.

These days, everyone had been imprisoned by the Bone-Eater Tribe, suffering mental torment and anguish. Now, finally rescued—and in such a shocking manner by Lord Shaman Niu—they naturally needed to vent their long-suppressed emotions.

"Want to say something?" Yang Kai turned to Yu, who had been hesitating to speak. But before she could, Yang Kai guessed, "Do you think I shouldn't have killed Shaman Chi?"

Yu nodded. "He was, after all, a Shaman King of the Bone-Eater Tribe, yet died by your hand. If word spreads, other tribes may shun us. Our Southern Barbarian Tribe is already in decline; we should keep a low profile, not draw attention."

Yang Kai smiled. "It's precisely because we're in decline that we can't endure in silence. We must let the other tribes know that even if the Southern Barbarian Tribe has only one Shaman King and twenty thousand people left, we are still the Southern Barbarian Tribe. Our fate is in our own hands, not subject to anyone's restraint, not a vassal to any tribe!" His gaze grew solemn. "The demon front is shrinking, and the war will only grow fiercer. If we don't show enough strength, sooner or later, other tribes will use us as cannon fodder."

Yu thought for a moment, unable to refute him, and could only nod. "Perhaps you are right."

Yang Kai said, "Besides, they started this. Killing a Shaman King is certainly wrong, but I doubt anyone will rush to avenge Shaman Chi. The Bone-Eater Tribe will continue to resent us, but if they want to act against us, they will have to wait until after the war. Let's hope they survive until then."

"What about us?" Yu looked up at Yang Kai, like a child who had yet to grow up.

"We will survive. We will drive out the demons and rebuild our home!"

Yu's gaze grew dreamy, as if she saw a beautiful future.

"Shaman Dang is dead!" Yang Kai suddenly said.

The longing on Yu's face vanished, her eyes lowered, and she fell silent, shrouded in sadness. Lu reached out and took her arm, the two sisters clinging tightly to each other.
