## Chapter 3079 — A Strange Thing

"Hm?" The cultivator responsible for monitoring their surroundings suddenly made a strange sound. Staring blankly at the Star Chart, he exclaimed in horror, "Young Lady, that asteroid, that asteroid..."

Lu Huaishuang looked down, and her expression changed at once.

The bright point of light was still present on the Star Chart, completely undamaged by the simultaneous barrage from ten Star Cannons.

"How can this be?!" Old Chen also looked incredulous. He had personally witnessed just how powerful a simultaneous barrage from ten Star Cannons was. Even the strongest experts in the Star Domain would not dare meet it head-on, so how could that asteroid be so hard?

What horrified him even more was that the barrage had not altered the asteroid's course in the slightest. It was still hurtling straight toward the Warship.

"Quickly, dodge it!" Old Chen shouted.

A bitter smile appeared on Lu Huaishuang's face. "It's too late!"

Had they been in open space, the Warship's superior performance might have allowed them to evade it and escape the danger, but what kind of place was this? This was an Asteroid Sea, surrounded by asteroids of every size. There was simply no room to maneuver, so where could they possibly dodge?

It truly was too late. On the Star Chart, the light representing the uninvited visitor was approaching the Warship at astonishing speed.

There was only one thing anyone could do now: pray that the asteroid would not cause too much damage to the Warship. That hope was clearly unrealistic, however. Though the asteroid was small, the energy fluctuations it radiated were terrifyingly powerful. If it truly struck them, no one knew whether the Warship could withstand it.

A point of light entered their sight through the crystal window ahead, streaking past like a shooting star.

Immediately afterward, a rumbling crash came from somewhere aboard the Warship, and the hundred-zhang-long vessel rocked violently several times. Everyone aboard went ashen in an instant, a single voice wailing endlessly in the depths of their hearts: It's over. This time, it's truly all over.

The noise diminished and gradually subsided, while the rocking Warship slowly stabilized.

Everyone in the cabin remained silent, only slowly coming back to their senses after a long while.

They were fine! They were actually fine!

The drastic reversal at the brink of life and death left everyone with an intensely unreal feeling.

The scene they had imagined, of the Warship being destroyed, had not occurred. It seemed they had all overestimated the asteroid's destructive power. However, the Warship had visibly slowed, clearly having suffered some damage. It needed to be repaired as soon as possible, or something could easily go wrong in this vast Starry Sky.

Lu Huaishuang was the first to react and called out sharply, "Pass down my orders! Inspect the Warship for damage and send people to make repairs. We must leave this Asteroid Sea as soon as possible and find somewhere to set down."

Only then did everyone recover and hurriedly spring into action.

Lu Huaishuang pursed her lips and gazed out at the Starry Sky through the crystal window ahead. It was said that surviving a great disaster would bring good fortune afterward. She wondered whether that would hold true for her.

The cultivators aboard the Warship quickly became busy.

Because the damaged Warship had slowed, a journey that should have taken only half an hour ultimately took a full two hours. Only after leaving the Asteroid Sea and casting the asteroids that wandered through the boundless Starry Sky behind them did everyone secretly breathe a sigh of relief.

Navigating a damaged Warship through an Asteroid Sea was no laughing matter. Fortunately, the cultivators aboard were all well trained and highly skilled at controlling the Warship, so they managed to avoid any major mishaps.

All that remained now was to find somewhere to set down and repair the Warship as quickly as possible.

Old Chen suggested a fairly suitable destination, and Lu Huaishuang immediately ordered them to head there.

Half a day later, on a silent, desolate star, a hundred-zhang-long Warship broke through the mist-like clouds and slowly sailed into view.

Countless desolate stars like this could be found in any Star Domain. Although the Starry Sky held many stars, few were suitable for living beings to inhabit and cultivate on. Only stars with complete Stellar Origins that could give rise to Spiritual Qi qualified, and such stars were known as Cultivation Stars, stars brimming with vitality.

In contrast, stars devoid of Spiritual Qi, where not even living creatures could survive and only deathly silence remained, were collectively known as Dead Stars.

Some Dead Stars had evolved from Cultivation Stars. When a Cultivation Star's Stellar Origin gradually weakened and eventually vanished, the passage of countless years would turn it into such a desolate place.

Other Dead Stars, however, might be nurturing new vitality. Perhaps one day in the future, such a star would possess its own Stellar Origin and shine even more dazzlingly than other Cultivation Stars.

But that process would inevitably be measured in units of ten thousand years, an exceedingly long time.

The Warship landed somewhere on the Dead Star, and its hatch opened. Cultivators continuously emerged and rushed toward the damaged section. Although they had made some simple repairs while underway, they would have to mend the damaged area completely before the Warship could set sail again.

Perhaps Lu Huaishuang had grown somewhat stifled after remaining aboard the Warship for so long, because she came out as well. Every cultivator she encountered saluted her respectfully. There was not the slightest hint of lust or covetousness in their eyes, only heartfelt admiration.

In the Wuji Star Domain, who would not praise the Lu Family's Eldest Young Lady of Morning Sun Star or declare that she was the equal of any man?

The Lu Family had no male heir, and its Family Head was already old and approaching his twilight years. The entire great family was managed and supported by the Lu Family's Eldest Young Lady alone. When Lu Huaishuang first took charge of the Lu Family's affairs, no one had thought well of her prospects.

A woman ought to stay home, support her husband, and raise her children. What great undertaking could she accomplish by showing her face in public?

But the facts proved everyone wrong.

Although the Lu Family had not risen to greater heights, at the very least it had not declined, and all of this was thanks to Lu Huaishuang. Even the Lu Family's enemies had no choice but to acknowledge that.

Lu Huaishuang's beauty and the Lu Family's holdings had also aroused the covetousness of countless people.

Beauty and wealth had always been what men pursued, and the Lu Family was such a large, fragrant morsel that it made mouths water. If someone could win Lu Huaishuang's favor, he would not only hold a beauty in his arms but also acquire the Lu Family's enormous holdings as her dowry. It would truly be a single step to the heavens, a blessing that would spare him several lifetimes of struggle.

Countless people visited each year to propose marriage, yet not one had ever caught Lu Huaishuang's eye.

"My man must be a great hero who stands tall between Heaven and Earth. His name will illuminate the Starry Sky as brilliantly as the round sun at noon. Whoever can accomplish that is the man I will marry."

This requirement could hardly even be called a requirement. It was simply asking the impossible.

No man would deny that he was a great hero who stood tall between Heaven and Earth. When they examined themselves, they all considered themselves outstanding and believed other men could not compare to them. But how could they make their names illuminate the Starry Sky like the round sun?

Only a handful of the strongest experts in the Star Domain could accomplish such a feat, and which of those old fellows did not already have one foot in the grave?

Many even declared that the Lu Family’s Eldest Young Lady was destined to grow old alone—that no one would ever cherish her or love her, and that she would go her entire life without ever knowing the affection of a man. Such sour-grapes thinking exists everywhere: if they could not have her, they would rather see that no one else did either.

The lofty notion of “Even if I can’t have you, I only hope you’re happy” was simply no match for such spite.

Stepping across the yellowed earth and lightly breathing in the somewhat stifling air, Lu Huaishuang frowned. She was a woman, after all. No matter how astute and capable she appeared in the eyes of others, she was still a delicate, fragile woman.

The environment on the Dead Star was harsh. Dust and sand filled the landscape as far as the eye could see. When the wind blew, dust billowed through the air, which itself was filled with haze, preventing anyone from seeing very far.

She walked for a while before stopping. She badly wanted to stretch without restraint, but even this simplest desire could not be fulfilled. She had to maintain her bearing and dignity.

Her heart felt somewhat weary.

She stood there quietly, imagining the history of this Dead Star.

Perhaps, countless ages in the past—or countless ages in the future—this Dead Star would give rise to a brilliant civilization filled with the richness of life. Upon the stretch of sand before her, perhaps a humble cottage would one day stand, where a husband and wife would live together, he tilling the fields while she wove cloth, their days warm with quiet happiness. Three or five years would pass, and they would welcome a son and a daughter into the world, raising them with loving care. Then, in their old age, they would journey the world hand in hand, until at last they were laid to rest together in some nameless corner of the world.

There was no need for much money, only enough to spend, nor any need for great strength, only someone by one's side.

The higher people stood, the simpler their wishes often became. Only those at the bottom would fantasize about plucking the stars, inviting the moon, and making their names known throughout the world.

"Young Lady..." a voice suddenly called out.

Lu Huaishuang's reverie was interrupted, but she did not become annoyed and merely turned her head to look over.

A guard of the Lu Family was staring at her in a daze, looking both shocked and incredulous.

He had actually seen the Eldest Young Lady smile. Though it was not very obvious, the slight curl at the corners of her lips was clearly an unconscious smile.

The Eldest Young Lady was already beautiful, but when she smiled, her beauty became boundless. He had joined the Lu Family more than ten years ago, yet ever since the Eldest Young Lady took charge of the family, he had never seen her smile again.

The curl at the corners of Lu Huaishuang's lips faded, and she asked indifferently, "What is it?"

The guard hurriedly composed himself and replied, "We found something strange."

Lu Huaishuang frowned upon hearing this. "What strange thing?"

The guard scratched his head, unsure how to explain. What they had just discovered was simply too inconceivable, leaving him at a loss for words.

Just then, a group of people emerged from the hole in the Warship. They seemed to be carrying something and were rushing over at incredible speed.

This unusual scene drew everyone's attention, and they all looked over.

In the blink of an eye, the group arrived before Lu Huaishuang.

With a thud, a person was tossed down in front of Lu Huaishuang. He rolled several times before coming to a stop.

Lu Huaishuang frowned and looked over. Lying before her was a young man in tattered clothes, as though he had endured some terrible ordeal. His eyes were tightly shut, his face pale, and after being thrown out, he had rolled several times across the sand before finally coming to a stop, where he now lay motionless.

Lu Huaishuang could not sense the slightest trace of life from him. He was not even breathing and had clearly already died.
