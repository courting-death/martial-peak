## Chapter 2907 — Southern Marsh

“Heavenly Sound Treasure Box.” Yang Kai reached out and tapped the box, and at once, the Heavenly Sound Treasure Box emitted a series of clear, melodious chimes, like a mountain spring or a spirit in a secluded valley, instantly invigorating all who heard it.

The chimes continued as the box rose layer by layer, gradually transforming into the shape of a pagoda with nine rotating tiers. Each tier spun—some left, some right, some fast, some slow—producing different sounds that, when combined, formed a beautiful melody.

Not only that, but as the Heavenly Sound Treasure Box spun, a pale blue mist began to spread, slowly expanding to fill the vast space. The mist resembled a flawless starry sky, dotted with countless stars, making it seem as if one were standing amidst the heavens, surrounded by breathtaking beauty.

“What a fine thing.” Nanmen Dajun’s eyes lit up. Although he felt the Heavenly Sound Treasure Box was of little use to him, for cultivators below the Emperor Realm, this wondrous melody would absolutely aid their cultivation, making it easier to enter a state of selflessness. In such a state, their training would yield twice the results with half the effort.

“You should be able to make full use of it,” Yang Kai said with a smile, looking at him.

Nanmen Dajun’s eyes brightened as he began to imagine using the Heavenly Sound Treasure Box as the core of a formation, supplemented by several other Secret Treasures, to set up a Heavenly Sound Treasure Hall.

This was not difficult for him, and once completed, the entire Heavenly Sound Treasure Hall would be filled with the sound of the Heavenly Sound Treasure Box, greatly benefiting any disciples who entered to cultivate in seclusion.

Yang Kai was right. The Heavenly Sound Treasure Box could only reach its full potential in the hands of someone like Nanmen Dajun, an Imperial-Grade Formation Master. Once amplified by a formation, the Heavenly Sound Treasure Box would become another foundation of Lingxiao Palace, a blessing for all disciples below the Emperor Realm.

“Leave it to me.” Nanmen Dajun carefully put away the Heavenly Sound Treasure Box and looked at Yang Kai with concern. “Palace Master, are you all right?”

He had already noticed that Yang Kai’s condition was off—his face was unusually pale.

Nanmen Dajun knew well how powerful Yang Kai was. Even the Sect Masters of top sects in the Northern Territory, like Mi Qi and Li Jiao, could not stir up any waves before him, and the three Yao Kings obeyed his every word. For such an expert to look so pale, the only explanation was that he had suffered a serious injury.

Nanmen Dajun could not imagine anyone in this world capable of injuring Yang Kai so badly, nor could he guess at the wealth of experiences Yang Kai had endured in the Thousand Illusion Dreamscape.

Yang Kai shook his head.

Nanmen Dajun continued, “Do you need me to come along? I don’t know what you’re planning, but I assume it’s related to a formation. If I go with you, I might be able to help break it.”

“No need.” Yang Kai waved his hand. “I’m fine, and it wouldn’t be convenient to bring you along this time.”

He was accompanying Hua Yulu at Xia Sheng’s request to explore an ancient cave residence. Hua Yulu had said she could only bring one helper; bringing another might cause unnecessary conflict.

Yang Kai refused, and Nanmen Dajun did not insist. He simply reported on Lingxiao Palace’s recent developments before using the Space Array to return to the Northern Territory.

Yang Kai did not leave immediately. Instead, he sat down cross-legged, took out some Spiritual Pills to repair his Divine Soul, and began refining the Flowing Cloud Shuttle.

He realized he had underestimated the impact of his experiences in the Thousand Illusion Dreamscape on his Divine Soul. He had thought that with the Warm Spirit Lotus protecting him, no matter how badly his Divine Soul was injured, it would recover in about half a month.

But in reality, more than ten days had passed, and his Divine Soul had barely improved.

He suspected this was due to his Divine Soul being overtaxed, not solely by the injuries sustained in the Thousand Illusion Dreamscape, but also by the distortion of time itself. Five days had encompassed two years of experience, placing an enormous burden on his Divine Soul. It was as if Yang Kai had consumed two years' worth of Divine Soul essence in merely five days. Replenishing such a loss could not possibly be accomplished in merely ten days or half a month.

Imperial-Grade Secret Treasures were not easy to refine, especially now that Yang Kai’s Divine Soul was weakened.

Still, he spent a few days imprinting a simple mark on the Flowing Cloud Shuttle. This was enough to control its flight, though he could not draw out most of its power.

That was sufficient. For Yang Kai, the Flowing Cloud Shuttle’s greatest use was as a means of travel.

He set out again, pausing to recall his destination. After the Thousand Illusion Dreamscape, many of his old memories were hazy.

The Southern Marsh, a perilous place in the Southern Territory, was not as inescapable as the Frozen Earth of the Northern Territory, but every year many experts perished there. What drew cultivators to the Southern Marsh were its abundant heaven-and-earth treasures and the many hidden cave residences.

It was said that long ago, the Southern Marsh was a place of martial prosperity for the Southern Territory. Countless sects and forces established themselves here and flourished, but a great disaster turned the prosperous land into a marsh, and the cultivators of the Southern Territory gradually abandoned it.

In that disaster, countless sects were destroyed and many cave residences buried. Adventurous cultivators sometimes discovered the ruins of ancient sects and the legacies of powerful experts, allowing them to soar to greatness.

The Southern Marsh also produced many rare spirit flowers and herbs, treasures that could not be found anywhere else in the Star Realm. Any one of them could fetch a sky-high price.

Faced with such countless benefits, the dangers of the Southern Marsh could not deter cultivators from exploring it. Several hundred years ago, the region had once bustled with activity, but as more and more treasures were discovered and claimed, it gradually fell into silence. Still, there were always some who braved the risks every so often.

Yang Kai had heard of the Southern Marsh before, but this was his first time entering it. He was bold and skilled, unafraid of anything. Even with his Divine Soul not yet recovered, he was no pushover.

By the time Yang Kai reached the Southern Marsh, half a month had passed. He found it just as the rumors described: marshes and hidden currents everywhere, with dangerous auras lurking below, like leopards waiting to pounce. The Southern Marsh was full of poisonous miasma. From above, the miasma appeared in all colors, forming a dazzling spectacle under the sunlight, but beneath the beauty lay deadly peril.

The mere presence of this miasma stopped all cultivators below the Dao Origin Realm. Only those at the Dao Origin Realm or Emperor Realm could resist the poison and explore the Southern Marsh safely.

But the miasma varied in strength. The strongest could not be easily resisted even by Emperor Realm cultivators, and if poisoned, even they might perish here.

Yang Kai took out his Transmission Compass and found Hua Yulu’s mark, sending her a message.

Half a day later, the compass received a reply.

After checking the message, Yang Kai flew off in that direction.

Two hours later, Yang Kai sensed several auras in the distance, one of which was Hua Yulu’s. He immediately rushed over.

The people there clearly sensed Yang Kai’s presence as well. Before he could approach, they all released their Divine Sense to investigate, and in the next moment, everyone—including Hua Yulu—looked surprised.

They had sensed the presence of the Flowing Cloud Shuttle.

The Flowing Cloud Shuttle was a flying Imperial Treasure of immeasurable value. Even among Emperor Realm cultivators, such treasures were extremely rare. If not for Hou Yu refining it for him, Yang Kai would not have his own flying Imperial Treasure even now.

Shortly after, the Flowing Cloud Shuttle descended before the group in a flash of light, and Yang Kai's figure emerged.

Hua Yulu looked at him with delight, then froze.

She saw a completely unfamiliar face—it was not Yang Kai’s at all.

But she quickly realized Yang Kai was wearing a Secret Treasure for disguise, altering his appearance.

This Secret Treasure was something Yang Kai had obtained from the Jixu Secret Realm years ago. It had limited utility—before Emperor Realm cultivators, the traces of disguise could hardly be concealed, and anyone with sufficient insight would notice he wore such an artifact. However, it served its purpose well enough: masking his pallid, weakened state.

Without this Secret Treasure, Yang Kai’s pale, weak appearance would be impossible to hide.

Hua Yulu did not dwell on why Yang Kai was doing this. Since he wanted to hide his identity, she naturally played along.

“Senior Brother Yang!” Hua Yulu called out in greeting.

“Palace Master Hua!” Yang Kai nodded to her. “Sorry I’m late.”

Besides Hua Yulu, four others had already gathered here, making the group complete. Hua Yulu had said she discovered the ancient cave residence with two others. Although they had broken through some restrictions and each gained something, they were stopped at the final barrier and could not enter.

The three of them discussed their plan: each would return and recruit a helper, limited to those at the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm or below. They would reconvene here after half a year to explore together.

Hua Yulu had asked Xia Sheng, but he was stationed at Linghu Palace and could not leave, so he asked Yang Kai to go in his place. Since Xia Sheng had asked, Yang Kai could not refuse, and besides, he was interested in the ancient cave residence himself.

Hua Yulu had obtained a small flower drum inside, which, like the Mountain and River Bell, was a Primordial Era treasure, though not very powerful.

According to Hua Yulu, one of the other two had greatly increased their cultivation inside. That person had originally been the weakest, but left with the strongest aura, while the other learned a secret technique, though its power was unknown.

Cultivation was not just about seclusion; all kinds of tempering and opportunities were essential. Such experiences could improve one’s strength more than bitter training.

This time, however, the timing was unfortunate. Yang Kai had just gone through the Thousand Illusion Dreamscape and needed rest, but since he had already agreed, he could only press on.

Yang Kai resolved that this time, safety would be his top priority. As long as he could bring Hua Yulu back safely, he would not have let down Xia Sheng’s trust.
