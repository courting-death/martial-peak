## Chapter 2717 — I Am the Dao

This refining process lasted for three months.

When he opened his eyes, Yang Kai's brow was tinged with a hint of helplessness.

The progress of refining the Golden Armored Heavenly Tome was actually quite decent. By now, he had already left his own Soul Brand within this Imperial Treasure and could activate several of its divine abilities. Although he couldn't wield the Golden Armored Heavenly Tome as effortlessly as Tan Junhao, at least he now had a few more means to face his enemies.

What left him helpless was his own cultivation.

He had clearly reached the peak of the First Stage of the Emperor Realm, and it felt as though one foot had already stepped into the threshold of the Second Stage, with only a thin barrier remaining. Yet, he simply couldn't break through to glimpse what lay beyond.

He had consumed millions of Upper-Level Source Crystals, and his Emperor Yuan had indeed become more robust and pure, but his realm remained stuck in place.

He wasn't anxious; rushing cultivation was the greatest taboo.

He was still young and had plenty of time. Compared to those Emperor Realm cultivators who had lived for hundreds or even thousands of years, he possessed an unparalleled advantage that others could only envy.

Being young was naturally an asset. There was still a long road ahead, so what was there to be anxious about?

So, for now, he set aside cultivation and focused his attention on managing Lingxiao Palace. After all, he was now the Palace Master of Lingxiao Palace. Even though there weren't many disciples, there were still several hundred people living here as guests. As the host, he ought to fulfill his duties and make proper arrangements for them, so they would feel at home—and, ideally, want to stay here for good…

That was what he thought, but when it came time to act, he realized he had no idea where to start.

Back on Dark Star, when he founded Lingxiao Sect, he had always left everything to Ye Xijun, having grown used to being a hands-off shopkeeper. Now, he found that personally handling such matters was completely unsuited to him.

In the end, he decided to go over to Thousand Leaves Sect to take a look. Maybe he could ask Ye Hen for some advice.

The entire Thousand Leaves Sect now resided together atop a major peak blessed with abundant Spiritual Qi—one of more than a dozen such peaks within Lingxiao Palace. No one remembered what this peak was originally called, so they simply named it Thousand Leaves Peak. In the end, it was just a name, and no one minded what it was called.

When he arrived, he found everything in perfect order. Of the five hundred disciples, some were cultivating, some were sparring, and some were refining puppets—no one was idle.

As Senior Brother, Du Xian was even holding a lecture on the peak's plaza, explaining the key points and essentials of puppet techniques. The less advanced disciples of Thousand Leaves Sect listened with rapt attention, their heads nodding, eyes full of admiration.

Yang Kai listened for a while, but it was all a fog to him—he had no idea what Du Xian was talking about.

It didn't matter if he couldn't understand. Among these people, perhaps quite a few would become disciples of Lingxiao Palace in the future. That chubby boy who kept nodding looked especially promising—he seemed clever and probably had his own insights into puppet techniques.

Once he grew a bit more, Yang Kai could tempt him with benefits and move him with emotion. Would he not then bow his head and join? By then, once he became a disciple of Lingxiao Palace, Ye Hen surely wouldn't object.

Suddenly, the voices stopped. Du Xian called out, "Brother Yang!"

He had been spotted. Yang Kai hadn't bothered to hide his presence, and Du Xian would never suspect him of trying to steal Thousand Leaves Sect's puppet techniques. If Yang Kai had truly wanted that, he could have kept those ancestral secret manuals the first time Emperor Heaven Valley was opened.

Yang Kai smiled and nodded. "I'm just looking around. Go on with what you were doing."

The disciples all turned around, stood up in unison, and greeted respectfully, "Greetings, Lord Yang!"

They were able to cultivate here entirely thanks to Yang Kai. They knew he was the master of this place, and as guests under his roof, how could they not show respect?

"Sit, sit!" Yang Kai waved his hand, feeling a bit embarrassed. He had just taken a casual stroll and ended up disturbing their cultivation—truly a sin.

Still, since he was here, he couldn't just do nothing. He cleared his throat and said, "Is everyone getting used to living here lately?"

Du Xian smiled, "This place is blessed with outstanding people and abundant Spiritual Qi. How could we not be used to it? The disciples couldn't be more satisfied."

The other disciples nodded vigorously, their faces beaming. Compared to their old Thousand Leaves Sect, this place was worlds apart. Their cultivation efficiency here was many times higher.

"As long as you're comfortable," Yang Kai said with a smile. "Don't stand on ceremony—just treat this place as your own home."

Only by treating it as their own home would they have no desire to leave…

"If you need anything, just look for Hua Qingsi. Sister Hua is now the Chief Steward of Lingxiao Palace and manages everything."

"Thank you, Brother Yang!" Du Xian said gratefully. "I'm sure we'll have to trouble Lady Hua in the future."

Yang Kai smiled and patted him on the shoulder.

The disciples were all deeply moved, finding Yang Kai's approachability unbelievable. He was an Emperor Realm cultivator and the master of the Palace, yet he had no airs at all and treated their Senior Brother as an equal.

Their gazes grew even more respectful. This, they thought, was the true bearing and magnanimity of the Emperor Realm. They'd heard that Emperor Realm cultivators usually looked down on everyone, spoke through their noses, and treated those below them as ants, their lives at their whim… Compared to Lord Yang, those people weren't even fit to carry his shoes.

"That's right!" Du Xian suddenly clapped his hands and turned to the disciples. "Weren't you all clamoring for me to share some cultivation insights? It's not that I don't want to teach you, but my own strength is limited and I fear misleading you. Now that Brother Yang is here, why not have him share his experience? As an Emperor Realm expert, his insights are far superior to mine."

Everyone was stunned for a moment, then cheered in unison.

Yang Kai said wryly, "But I know nothing about puppet techniques."

Du Xian laughed, "Cultivation has nothing to do with puppets. Besides, we can always learn from others. I, too, would like to hear Brother Yang's teachings."

"Uh…" Yang Kai was a bit flustered. Right, what was he here for again? How had he suddenly been pushed onto the stage to share cultivation insights? He had just managed to build a friendly image—if he refused now, wouldn't all that effort go to waste?

"Lord Yang!"

"Lord Yang!"

"Lord Yang!"

The disciples of Thousand Leaves Sect shouted in unison, their voices like a tidal wave, eyes filled with hope and longing.

"Brother Yang, just say a few words," Du Xian pleaded.

"Alright," Yang Kai agreed with some reluctance. "I'll just say a few things."

Du Xian was overjoyed and quickly invited Yang Kai to the front, then sat down and looked up at him expectantly.

Looking at the group of martial artists below, Yang Kai saw that their cultivation levels varied and their faces were still youthful. He couldn’t help but feel a little dazed.

He had gone through these stages himself—step by step, growing stronger, and finally advancing to the Emperor Realm. But he was different from them. After leaving Zhongdu, he had never had a sect or a master to guide him. He had struggled alone, often walking the line between life and death, meeting both villains and benefactors, sometimes escaping disaster, sometimes turning misfortune into fortune, and ultimately forging his own path.

Still… if one had a good teacher and friend on the road to growth, perhaps many detours could be avoided.

Could it be that one day he himself would leave upon them the mark of such a worthy teacher and true friend? No matter how much his instruction and explanations might help them today, years from now, when they looked back, they would surely remember that within the vast Lingxiao Palace, beneath the lush and verdant Thousand Leaves Peak, he had once stood before them like a bright lamp, guiding them along the road ahead.

All of a sudden, he felt his stature grow a great deal taller.

He hadn’t known what to say at first, but in this moment, his mind grew clearer than ever before. He opened his mouth and spoke: “A martial artist’s path is to forge ahead through thorns and brambles, to challenge the heavens and fight for their fate. In the end, it all comes down to two words—Martial Dao!”

“What is the Dao? Of the Great Dao’s fifty truths, Heaven reveals but forty-nine. Though there are three thousand paths in this world by which one may attain the Great Dao, in the end they all converge upon the same road.”

"Some say the art of war is the Dao, others say technique is the Dao, and still others claim that flowers, grass, and even the ants on the ground are all Dao!"

"As for me, I am the Dao. Wherever I am, there the Dao exists!"

…

His voice was not loud, yet it rang clearly in everyone's ears, carrying a subtle, enchanting power that drew them in without realizing it. As Yang Kai spoke, it was as if a grand, sweeping scroll slowly unfurled before their eyes—at times stirring their hearts with passion, at times filling them with boundless joy, and at other moments leaving them lost and helpless.

Standing there, Yang Kai was the very image of all the countless martial artists who pursued the Martial Dao—generation after generation, striving to grasp its true meaning.

There had originally been fewer than a hundred disciples of Thousand Leaves Sect present.

But as time passed, the crowd grew larger. More and more people heard the voice and came over, sitting cross-legged and listening intently.

After half an hour, nearly five hundred people had gathered before Yang Kai—almost all the disciples of Thousand Leaves Sect, except for a few who were in seclusion and couldn't come.

Having cultivated to this point, Yang Kai had never had a teacher, but now that he had reached the Emperor Realm, he naturally had his own insights.

Still, he didn't share the cultivation insights Du Xian had begged for. Instead, he spoke eloquently, his thoughts roaming freely. Rather than teaching these people, it was more like he was expressing his own understanding, searching for those elusive traces of the Dao in his own words.

Some understood, their faces thoughtful. Others frowned, feeling that Yang Kai's words were all over the place—nonsense, really. But even so, none dared to question him. They simply thought their own realms weren't high enough to comprehend, and felt secretly frustrated. As expected, the words of an Emperor Realm expert were profound—each sentence made sense on its own, but together, they became unfathomable.

"Walk your own path, forge your own Dao. If you follow someone else's path, you'll always be chasing their shadow!" Yang Kai wiped his mouth, looking a bit unsatisfied, and swept his gaze over the crowd. "Do you all understand?"

The whole place was silent—no one dared claim they understood.

Suddenly, the Spiritual Qi of heaven and earth surged. Thunder rumbled and lightning flashed in the sky above Thousand Leaves Peak, black clouds gathered, and a vortex of Spiritual Qi rapidly formed as the power of heaven and earth slowly converged.

"Oh? Someone has understood!" Yang Kai turned to look at the peak, grinning.
