## Chapter 2832 — One Month

Outside the stone fortress, after a night of rest, the people of the three tribes were ready to depart. Although almost everyone was injured, and the more seriously wounded were missing arms or legs and could only be carried by others, every face was filled with joy.

Rescuing their own tribesmen from the hands of the Bone-Eater Tribe was already an extraordinary achievement.

Not far away, Chi, Zhu, and Yang Kai walked together, speaking in low voices.

"Shaman Niu, you must be careful of the Bone-Eater Tribe's revenge!" Zhu warned seriously. "The Bone-Eater Tribe always seeks vengeance. This time, we slaughtered one of their small tribes. They will never let this go."

Chi nodded and said, "You'd best find time to meet with your tribe's Shaman King and have him handle this matter. After all, it was the Bone-Eater Tribe who were in the wrong first. If the Shaman King mediates, perhaps this can be settled."

"It's not that simple." Yang Kai shook his head and said, "Did you notice? The shamanic artifact You used is not something a Great Shaman Master could easily possess."

At these words, Zhu and Chi's expressions changed slightly, clearly having thought of this as well.

Yang Kai continued, "To possess such a shamanic artifact means You has quite a background. He might be a descendant of the Bone-Eater Tribe's Shaman Kings or Shaman Saints, or perhaps he is highly valued by them. Killing him will bring us no small amount of trouble. Even if the Shaman King mediates, it may not be resolved."

Zhu said, "No matter what, if the Shaman King steps in, there is at least a sliver of hope. With our current strength alone, we cannot oppose a Shaman King."

Yang Kai smiled slightly and said no more.

He was now at the Shaman Master Realm and was perhaps still no match for a Shaman King. But now that his Divine Sense had awakened and he could wield the Laws of Space, even if he faced a Shaman King, he would not be afraid.

Moreover, as long as he was given enough time, his strength would soar even further.

As for Zhu and Chi, if the Bone-Eater Tribe's Shaman King came for them, they would have no power to resist. It was only natural for them to seek the protection of their own tribe's Shaman King.

The three walked and talked along the way. Although Chi and Zhu's realms were much higher than Yang Kai's, after yesterday's bloody battle, they no longer dared to look down on him in the slightest.

To force a Middle-Level Great Shaman Master to self-destruct—this was something they could not do.

So the two of them took the initiative to lower their status and converse with Yang Kai as equals.

Half a day later, the three groups parted ways, and Yang Kai led two hundred villagers toward Cangnan Village.

Cangnan Village originally had more than three hundred people. But after this battle, their numbers had dropped by a third. As the only remaining Shaman in the village, Yang Kai naturally became its leader.

Three days later, Yang Kai led more than two hundred people back to Cangnan Village. Gazing at the ruins and broken walls that remained of their home, many villagers could not help but feel a wave of sorrow and loss.

At the entrance to the village, Die suddenly appeared, her figure flashing into view. She looked Yang Kai up and down, then gave him a slight nod of acknowledgment.

Behind Die, seven or eight children rushed out, found their parents, and embraced them, weeping bitterly.

The sudden appearance of Die, a stranger, made the villagers curious. Fortunately, since they had little contact with the outside world and were unfamiliar with the Drifting Tribe, they were only curious. Die's build was similar to Yang Kai's, not as robust as the average barbarian, which made the villagers feel close to her.

Yang Kai briefly introduced Die to the villagers, telling them she was his friend, and the tribe immediately gave her a warm welcome.

With the village destroyed, it naturally had to be rebuilt. Fortunately, the villagers were all strong and sturdy, so this was no problem for them.

The rebuilding took less than five days, and the once-ruined village regained its former appearance.

Five days later, everything was back on track. In a wooden house, Yang Kai sat cross-legged. After searching for a while in his Spatial Ring, he finally found a Ninth-Tier Yao Beast Inner Core.

In the past, Yang Kai would not have even looked at a Ninth-Tier Yao Beast Inner Core. Back in the Wild Ancient Land's beast grave, when collecting Inner Cores, he only picked those above Ninth-Tier. He didn't bother with anything below, or else the number of Inner Cores would have been even more terrifying.

But now, this Ninth-Tier Inner Core might still be a bit too advanced for him.

The Yao Beast Inner Cores Yang Kai had used for cultivation before were nothing special. The highest was only about the level of a Sixth-Tier Inner Core. Now, suddenly having a Ninth-Tier one was like going from plain soup to a table full of delicacies—he wasn't sure if he could handle it.

But things had come to this, and Yang Kai had nothing to hesitate about. His body was already extraordinary, so even if the energy in the Ninth-Tier Inner Core was a bit much for him, it wouldn't injure him.

Opening his mouth, he swallowed the Inner Core, and immediately began circulating a set of Shamanic Arts incantations.

This set of Shamanic Arts was not the one passed down by the village chief, but rather a profound technique Yang Kai had obtained from Qing’s knowledge. It was far superior to what the chief had taught him—there was simply no comparison between the two.

As soon as he began circulating the Shamanic Arts, Yang Kai immediately felt the difference.

After all, he was an Emperor Realm expert. He could tell the quality of a technique at a glance. This set of Shamanic Arts from Qing was truly remarkable, and his cultivation speed was more than ten times faster than before.

Most importantly, he had just swallowed a Ninth-Tier Inner Core.

Instantly, a burning sensation spread through his abdomen, as if flames were raging inside. The surging energy contracted and expanded, making Yang Kai's belly swell round. If not for his strong body, this alone would have been enough to make him explode.

His face flushed red, veins bulged on his forehead, and under this great nourishment, blood trickled from his nostrils. He remained motionless, guarding his mind and refining the energy with Shamanic Arts.

As he breathed, the air surged like the sea, and the wooden house trembled as if it would collapse. The surrounding Spiritual Qi churned, astonishing the villagers, who had no idea what was happening.

Only Die, who was teaching the children cultivation nearby, noticed something and stopped Ah Hu and the others from rushing into the wooden house.

Die quickly integrated into the village, helping a lot during the five days of rebuilding. To the simple villagers of Cangnan Village, she was already one of them. Everyone also knew that, like Ah Niu, she was a powerful shaman.

Naturally, no one dared disobey her words.

This strange phenomenon lasted a full day before the commotion from Yang Kai's wooden house gradually subsided.

Inside, Yang Kai's swollen belly returned to normal. He had refined the Ninth-Tier Inner Core with Shamanic Arts, merging it into his body and turning it into his own strength.

His Shaman Master Realm had improved significantly all at once.

Without hesitation, Yang Kai took out another Ninth-Tier Inner Core and swallowed it.

The astonishing commotion and surging airwaves once again erupted from the wooden house, shocking the villagers.

……

In the blink of an eye, a month had passed. During this time, the villagers of Cangnan Village had grown accustomed to the strange disturbances coming from Ah Niu’s wooden house. What once sparked curiosity now barely drew a reaction—after so many occurrences, these odd happenings had become a part of daily life.

At first, this only happened once a day and would gradually subside. But as time went on, the frequency increased and the disturbances calmed down faster and faster.

Die said that Ah Niu was cultivating.

The villagers didn't understand Shamanic Arts, but they felt this cultivation was different from the village chief's. The chief had cultivated before, but it was never this noisy. Still, since Die said so, they accepted it.

For a whole month, Ah Niu never showed his face. The villagers even worried he might starve to death inside. Ah Ni and the others tried to bring him food several times, but Die stopped them, so now Ah Ni looked at Die with a bit of grievance.

Strictly speaking, Ah Niu’s behavior was rather irresponsible. When the old village chief was in charge, he would personally oversee every detail, guiding everyone in their daily tasks. But ever since Ah Niu became the new leader, aside from working tirelessly during the five days of rebuilding, he had vanished without a trace, leaving the villagers to fend for themselves.

Ah Hu and the others could only do as they saw fit. If they really couldn't manage, they would ask Die. Although Die was inexperienced, she could handle the affairs of a small village and helped the villagers a lot.

Now, Die's prestige in the village, though not as high as Yang Kai's, was second only to his.

On this day, the peaceful village was suddenly startled by a sharp, piercing howl.

The villagers rushed out of their wooden houses. Ah Hu and the other young men looked solemn, ready to fight at any moment, because the howl came from the fence wall—a warning signal.

This sound meant an outsider had come to the village.

"What's going on!" Ah Hu leapt onto the fence and asked the archer on patrol.

"A Great Shaman is coming!" The archer pointed ahead, and Ah Hu looked over to see someone flying toward them from the sky.

A shaman! And a very powerful one!

Only shamans at the Shaman Master level could break free from the earth's pull and fly like birds in the sky.

Ah Hu's gaze grew serious.

"He's injured!" Die's voice suddenly sounded beside him. Ah Hu didn't know when she had arrived, but after more than a month together, he and the others understood that Die was also a formidable Great Shaman.

Since she said so, it must be true.

"Injured?" Ah Hu frowned and looked again, finally noticing something. The Great Shaman flying over was unsteady, swaying, and as he drew closer, a hint of blood could be seen.

The man was indeed injured.

"He's from the Raging Flame Tribe," Die said again.

"Lord Chi!" Ah Hu finally recognized the man and couldn't help but exclaim.

This was Lord Chi of the Raging Flame Tribe, whom they had met at the Bone-Eater Tribe's stone fortress more than a month ago. As far as he knew, Lord Chi was a Great Shaman Master. Who could have wounded him? And judging by his current state, things looked grim—his right arm was gone, blood gushed out, dyeing half his body red.

Judging by the wound, it didn't seem to have been caused by a sharp weapon, but rather... as if it had been torn off by hand.

At this thought, Ah Hu felt a chill run through his body.
