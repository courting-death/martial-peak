## Chapter 2935 — Zhu Lie

Offending a Dragon was no laughing matter. The Dragon Clan was proud, considering themselves the leaders of all living beings. Had they known the newcomer was actually a member of the Dragon Clan, no one would have dared to act rashly. Yet, at such a sensitive moment, this red dragon had charged in without a word—who could restrain the instinct to strike?

Attacking a Dragon was an insult to the Dragon Clan, and that was a huge problem. Fortunately, with the situation unclear just now, everyone had held back when they made their moves. Had they gone all out and injured the Dragon, everyone present would have been in trouble.

“You have the guts to strike but not to admit it?” the red dragon roared. “You lowly ants, all of you must die!”

At these words, the faces of many Emperor Realm cultivators changed dramatically. The Dragon Clan was hard to deal with, and an enraged Dragon was even more unreasonable. Even if over a hundred Emperors joined forces and could face a giant dragon without fear, so what? Behind him stood the Dragon Isle. If they truly provoked the Dragon Isle and the dragons there all set out, the entire Star Realm would be thrown into chaos. Such a result would be a hundred or a thousand times more serious than the revival of Demonic Intent.

The red dragon was clearly furious now, letting out an explosive roar as he prepared to strike. Wen Zishan's expression grew grave, and he silently resolved that even if it meant offending the Dragon Isle, he could not allow so many Emperor Realm experts to be slaughtered within Qingyang Shrine. He discreetly communicated with the Shrine's Grand Guardian Array, ready to activate it immediately if the situation deteriorated further.

“Wait!” Lei Hong called out in a low voice, stepping forward.

“Old thing, you struck just now too!” The red dragon circled in the sky, his massive body creating a powerful visual and spiritual pressure. Suddenly, he swooped down, his dragon head stopping less than three zhang from Lei Hong, his eyes—bigger than windows—fixing on him with ill intent.

Everyone was sweating for Lei Hong.

Lei Hong cupped his fists and said, “May I ask if you come from... the legendary Dragon Isle in the Eastern Sea?”

"So what if I do?" The red dragon snorted, jets of scorching breath erupting from his nostrils. Lei Hong's body flashed with light as his hair singed slightly from the heat.

“So it is…” Lei Hong’s face showed sudden realization. Smiling, he said, “It’s said there is a Dragon Isle in the Eastern Sea, home to giant dragons, unseen by the world. Yet even so, the fame of Dragon Isle rivals that of the Great Emperor Sects. To meet you today is truly a rare honor.”

It was rare to see the Great Elder of Star God Palace openly flatter someone. The group of Emperor Realm experts were all a bit dumbfounded.

Yet these harmless words had a miraculous effect.

Everyone could clearly feel the red dragon’s anger subside. The massive dragon head drew back a little from Lei Hong, nodding repeatedly, his voice booming like a bell: “Heh, old thing, you do have some insight, knowing the prestige of our Dragon Isle. That’s right. Who in the world doesn’t know the name of the Dragon Isle? What are the so-called Great Emperor Sects compared to us?”

Lei Hong gave a dry laugh. Though he felt quite displeased, he dared not show it.

He was, after all, a Great Elder of a Great Emperor Sect. The words “so-called” from the red dragon naturally didn’t sit well with him, but knowing the Dragon Clan’s temperament, he didn’t want to argue and risk making things worse.

The red dragon, however, seemed to be enjoying himself, boasting, “Our Dragon Clan simply doesn’t care to compete with you lowly ants for fame and fortune. Otherwise, if the elites of the Dragon Isle set out, all those so-called Great Emperors would have to step aside. You think I couldn’t drown all of you with a single breath?”

The more he spoke, the more outrageous he became. The group of Emperor Realm experts all frowned, and Lei Ting quickly interrupted, “The Dragon Clan has not appeared for a long time. This is our first time meeting you, so when you flew over, we didn’t recognize you and thought an enemy was attacking. That’s why we acted rashly and caused a misunderstanding.”

“Yes, yes, we were too impulsive just now. Please, sir, don’t take offense.”

“If you had revealed your true form earlier, we would never have attacked you.”

“To see a true dragon today is truly a rare honor.”

“…”

The crowd all spoke at once, everyone realizing that while this red dragon had a bad temper and was hard to get along with, he seemed rather soft-eared—just a few flattering words could calm his anger. This was a common flaw of the overly proud; being so arrogant, they naturally didn’t want to fuss with those they considered beneath them.

For a while, everyone spared no effort in their praise.

The giant dragon listened with great satisfaction, his anger, which had been like a volcano about to erupt, now completely dissipated. He snorted lightly and said, “Enough, enough. I can’t be bothered to argue with you. Let’s just forget what happened. Next time, make sure you open your eyes before you act.”

Everyone breathed a sigh of relief. Wen Zishan also released his gathered power, giving Lei Hong a grateful look.

The Shrine had suffered repeated upheavals, and he himself had been injured in the Divine Roaming Mirror world. He was feeling anxious and irritable. If Lei Hong hadn’t handled things so well, another disaster might have broken out. If the Emperor Realm experts and the red dragon had clashed, regardless of who won, Qingyang Shrine would have suffered.

Lei Hong smiled slightly, indicating Wen Zishan need not worry.

Wen Zishan cupped his fists toward the sky and said, “Since you come from the Dragon Isle, may I ask which lord of the Dragon Isle you are?”

“I am Zhu Lie!” The red dragon swung his head and tail, his voice carrying for miles.

“So it’s Lord Zhu Lie. May I ask what brings you to Qingyang Shrine?”

“I’m looking for someone!” Zhu Lie turned over a few times, his dragon head facing the crowd, his eyes sweeping over them. “Who is Yang Kai?”

Yang Kai had been watching the show, but when he heard this Zhu Lie call his name, he was stunned.

Over a hundred pairs of eyes turned to look at him. The Emperor Realm experts near Yang Kai quickly moved away, as if standing next to him would bring disaster.

Wen Zishan was also a bit surprised.

A giant dragon had appeared at Qingyang Shrine. He had thought it was the revival of Demonic Intent that had drawn the dragon here. The Dragon Clan, though proud and aloof, was a race with countless years of heritage. If there was anyone in this world who understood Demonic Intent best, it would be the Dragon Clan.

Who would have thought this dragon had come for Yang Kai, not for Demonic Intent.

He looked curiously at Yang Kai and saw that Yang Kai also wore a bewildered expression.

“You are Yang Kai?” Without anyone pointing him out, the looks and attitudes of the Emperor Realm experts around made Zhu Lie identify his target. He issued a command without any room for discussion: “Come with me.”

“Where to?” Yang Kai frowned.

“To Dragon Isle!” Zhu Lie replied impatiently.

The crowd gasped. Even as Emperor Realm experts, they couldn’t stay calm at those words. To set foot on Dragon Isle in one’s lifetime was a great honor, but seeing Zhu Lie’s attitude, they felt that if Yang Kai really went, it would likely not end well.

No one knew how Yang Kai had offended the Dragon Isle and the Dragon Clan.

But Yang Kai understood something, frowning as he asked, “Did Zhu Qing send you?”

“She’s not qualified to order me!” Zhu Lie snorted coldly, a trace of disdain flashing in his eyes.

“Then who gave you the order?” Yang Kai pressed.

Zhu Lie impatiently flicked his tail, clouds rolling for miles, and replied in a buzzing voice, “You don’t need to know. Just come back to the Dragon Isle with me.”

Yang Kai smiled and said, “I do need to go to the Dragon Isle, but not right now. I have things to handle here. So, Brother Zhu Lie, could you please tell your superior that I’ll visit when I’m free? There’s no need to send someone to fetch me.”

He suddenly became wary of the Dragon Isle and the Dragon Clan.

Previously, Zhu Qing had invited him to the Dragon Isle several times. At first, Yang Kai thought it was because of the Dragon Isle Token and the dragon secret treasures he had refined. Now, it seemed things were not so simple.

Zhu Qing had gone to the Frozen Earth in the Northern Territory and had not returned or sent word. Now, Dragon Isle had sent another Dragon to bring him back.

Yang Kai guessed it was related to the Golden Sacred Dragon Origin Power. He seemed to have underestimated how much the Golden Sacred Dragon Origin Power attracted the Dragon Clan. So, until he figured things out, he would not set foot on Dragon Isle, lest he suffer unknowingly. Besides, Demonic Intent had just escaped, and as someone involved, he could not simply walk away.

Zhu Lie’s dragon eyes widened in shock as he stared at Yang Kai for a long moment before saying in a deep voice, “Human, did you just refuse me?”

The anger that had subsided began to surge again. Lei Hong and Wen Zishan both changed expressions.

Before Yang Kai could say anything, Zhu Lie was already furious. “How dare you! I came in person, and you dare refuse me! You’re courting death!”

He rolled in the air, and a murderous aura filled the area for miles around.

Yang Kai’s face darkened. “I’ve made myself clear. Either you go back to Dragon Isle and report, or I’ll send you back myself.”

The many Emperor Realm experts turned pale at these words, staring at Yang Kai in shock. They thought he was truly reckless—just a First Stage Emperor Realm, yet daring to speak to a Dragon like that.

Wen Zishan, Gao Xueting, and the others looked at Yang Kai as if seeing him for the first time, all stunned.

No one had ever dared speak to the Dragon Clan like this. Those who did were probably already dead.

Lei Hong kept signaling to Yang Kai to restrain himself. After all, it had taken great effort to calm Zhu Lie’s anger, and Yang Kai’s few words would surely provoke him again.

As the Great Elder of Star God Palace, Lei Hong could endure humiliation, but Yang Kai was just a Guest Elder of Qingyang Shrine, far lower in cultivation and status. What harm was there in saying a few soft words?

It was already too late.

Zhu Lie’s anger reignited, several times stronger than before. He let out a thunderous, sinister laugh: “Lowly ant, you dare provoke the Dragon Clan? You truly don’t know your place…”

He didn’t finish, because Yang Kai had already flashed to his side.

The tiny figure was utterly insignificant compared to the twenty zhang long body.
