## Chapter 3020 — Is This Dragon Isle?

The sky was azure and the land vast. Two figures stood foolishly in the void, unable to react for a long time.

These two were naturally Yang Kai and Li Jiao, who had suffered one setback after another along the way. After following the orb of light to the exit, Yang Kai tore through space and brought them here, only to discover that the scene before them was completely different from what they had imagined. For a time, they could not help standing there in a daze.

"Palace Master Yang, is... is this Dragon Isle?" Li Jiao turned to Yang Kai, his face filled with astonishment.

"I don't know." Yang Kai was also bewildered.

He had thought that emerging from the Void Maze would definitely bring them to Dragon Isle. Yet at this moment, even he was uncertain whether this place was truly Dragon Isle, because it was not an island at all.

This was a small world!

After sensing his surroundings briefly, Yang Kai found that the World Laws here were extremely complete, even surpassing those of his Small Xuan Realm. Looking up, he could even see a blazing sun hanging high in the sky.

Anyone unaware of the circumstances would probably think this was somewhere in the Star Realm.

However, Yang Kai knew this was not the Star Realm. He had entered and left many small worlds and even possessed the Small Xuan Realm himself, so he was extremely sensitive to such existences.

This was a small world, a very complete small world, and its Spiritual Qi was also extraordinary.

It was not that the Spiritual Qi was exceptionally rich. The Star Realm was vast and contained countless Sacred Lands for cultivation. Which great Sect was not situated in a place blessed by nature where Spiritual Qi gathered? Although the Spiritual Qi in this small world could be considered rich, it was not much stronger than that of the Star Realm's Sacred Lands.

The key was its purity!

The Spiritual Qi here seemed extraordinarily pure, several times purer than that of any Sacred Land Yang Kai had ever encountered.

Such a cultivation environment was undoubtedly extremely favourable. Purer Spiritual Qi meant faster cultivation, and what cultivator would not yearn for that?

"We... couldn't have come to the wrong place, could we?" Li Jiao wore an expression caught between laughter and tears.

"Why do you say that?" Yang Kai turned to look at him.

"How does this place resemble an island?" Li Jiao was somewhat disappointed. In his imagination, Dragon Isle should stand aloof from the world, isolated above the vast sea, with giant dragons soaring over the island and breathing out clouds and mist.

This place was certainly excellent, but it differed greatly from what he had imagined.

"Who said Dragon Isle had to be an island?" Yang Kai asked in return.

Li Jiao was taken aback, but after thinking about it, Yang Kai had a point. The name Dragon Isle had been passed down by word of mouth for generations, and everyone assumed it was an island in the sea. However, no one had ever truly entered Dragon Isle and described what it looked like inside.

"I think this place is very likely Dragon Isle." Yang Kai took a deep breath. The pure Spiritual Qi left him refreshed in body and mind, and he felt every pore across his body relax. Grinning, he said, "No, not merely likely. This is definitely Dragon Isle!"

Beneath the Heavens, what place other than the legendary Dragon Isle could be better than this? None of the Sacred Lands Yang Kai had seen could compare with this place.

Li Jiao nodded lightly and did not argue with Yang Kai about it, instead asking, "Then what should we do now?"

Yang Kai frowned and thought for a moment before replying, "Since we're already here, we may as well settle in. Let's investigate the surroundings first."

"Good!" Li Jiao nodded.

The two immediately flew off in one direction. To avoid exposing their whereabouts, both did their utmost to restrain their auras. They also flew fairly low, and their speed was not fast.

This small world seemed enormous, at least as large as the Revolving World they had visited some days ago, if not larger. Along the way, the two flew over spirit mountains and great rivers, yet did not encounter a single member of the Dragon Clan.

Although the Dragon Clan was very small in number, as far as Yang Kai knew, there should still be some members of the Dragon Clan living and reproducing on Dragon Isle. It made no sense that there would be no trace of them at all.

Yang Kai could not help wondering whether his guess had been wrong. Could this truly not be Dragon Isle? Had he and Li Jiao somehow ended up in some inexplicable small world? If so, their situation would be rather awkward.

They did encounter some Yao Beasts, both strong and weak. Some of the more powerful ones even possessed the Dragon Clan's bloodline and radiated terrifying auras as they roamed freely through the mountains and forests.

The appearance of these Yao Beasts with the Dragon Clan's bloodline restored much of Yang Kai's confidence, but they still saw no sign of the Dragon Clan itself.

Two days passed in a flash, after which a huge city abruptly appeared in their field of vision.

The city had been built upon an enormous plain and occupied nearly a third of it. From afar, buildings packed the city densely, while streets both broad and narrow crisscrossed like interlocking teeth.

Such an enormous city could accommodate at least a million people.

Yang Kai and Li Jiao were instantly dumbfounded as an ominous feeling rose in their hearts.

"Brother Li, are there cities on Dragon Isle?"

Li Jiao smiled bitterly. "Although I've never heard of any cities on Dragon Isle, with so few members of the Dragon Clan, why would they build such an enormous city?"

Yang Kai thought about it and agreed. The Dragon Clan had no reason to build such a great city. Could this place truly not be Dragon Isle? At this thought, his expression darkened. Just two days ago, he had confidently declared to Li Jiao that this place was definitely Dragon Isle, only to be slapped in the face two days later.

But if this was not Dragon Isle, then where were they?

After pondering for a moment, Yang Kai said, "Let's go take a look."

No matter what this place was, since they were already here, they had to investigate it.

The two did not rashly enter the city. After all, neither of them knew what this place was or who lived inside. Therefore, they landed about thirty li from the city and concealed themselves in a hidden spot, preparing to observe it in secret for a while.

From closer up, they could indeed see many figures moving about within the city. Even if there were not a million people, there were at least several hundred thousand.

This discovery left Yang Kai and Li Jiao even more disappointed.

This really was not Dragon Isle. Otherwise, how could there be so many people here?

"Someone's coming!" Yang Kai suddenly shouted in a low voice, staring intently in one direction.

Hearing this, Li Jiao hurriedly looked over as well. Several figures were swiftly approaching from that direction and soon flew over their heads. From the looks of it, they intended to enter the city.

Only after they had passed did Li Jiao say, "Several humans in the Dao Origin Realm."

Yang Kai nodded lightly.

Those people were indeed human, and their cultivations were neither high nor low. People like them were a dime a dozen outside, so there was nothing unusual about them.

Not long after they left, another group flew toward the city.

This time, however, Yang Kai and Li Jiao discovered something strange. The leader of this second group actually possessed the Dragon Clan's bloodline.

He was a man with a cultivation around the First Stage of the Emperor Realm. Several patches of dragon scales marked his face, but aside from that, he displayed no other characteristics of the Dragon Clan. Nevertheless, this discovery greatly surprised Yang Kai and Li Jiao.

This man's circumstances were very similar to Li Jiao's. They should both be Dragon Descendants, though their bloodlines were impure. In fact, this man's bloodline was even inferior to Li Jiao's.

Yang Kai frowned slightly, feeling that something about this place was unusual.

For the next half-day, the two remained where they were.

From time to time, figures flew overhead and headed straight for the city.

The more they observed, the greater the shock in their hearts became.

The reason was simple: the number of dragon descendants in this place was astonishingly high.

In just half a day, at least a hundred people had flown overhead, and among them, no fewer than ten were dragon descendants. A ratio of one in ten was something that could never be seen anywhere else.

Although there were Dragon Descendants in the Star Realm, they were not particularly numerous.

Yet in this damned place, ten out of every hundred people were Dragon Descendants. Had he not witnessed it with his own eyes, even Yang Kai would not have believed such a ratio.

These Dragon Descendants also possessed cultivations of varying heights. The weakest were only in the Void King Realm, while the strongest had reached the Emperor Realm. Their Dragon Clan characteristics also varied greatly. Some had short dragon horns growing from their foreheads, some had dragon tails behind them, and some had misshapen dragon claws. Most commonly, they had several patches of dragon scales on their bodies. Of course, these Dragon Clan characteristics could not compare with those of the true Dragon Clan at all; the difference was as vast as Heaven and Earth. Rather than dragon scales, those scales would be better described as snake scales.

Besides humans and Dragon Descendants, Yao were also mixed among them.

"Palace Master Yang, what exactly is going on? Why are there so many Dragon Descendants here?" Li Jiao was becoming unsettled.

"Do I look like I know the answer?" Yang Kai shot him a sidelong glance.

Li Jiao smiled sheepishly. He had only asked casually, mainly because everything they had witnessed over the past half-day was simply too unbelievable.

"It'll be easy to find out. We just need to go inside and take a look." Yang Kai pursed his lips toward the city.

Hearing this, Li Jiao fell silent for a moment before nodding.

After observing for so long, they had discovered that the city's defenses were not strict. In fact, it could almost be said to be completely unguarded. Every human, Yao, and Dragon Descendant entering the city simply flew straight inside. No one questioned them, much less collected anything like an entrance fee.

Therefore, it would not be difficult at all for Yang Kai and Li Jiao to enter the city.

Once they had settled on a plan, the two immediately flew out of their hiding place and headed openly toward the city.

They quickly crossed the thirty-li distance. Imitating the people they had seen earlier, the two landed while still some distance away and continued toward the city on foot.

Upon reaching the city gates, Yang Kai looked up and saw three bold, flamboyant characters written above them.

Yang Kai froze.

Li Jiao was also briefly lost in a daze.

The reason was simple. Those three bold, flamboyant characters read: Half-Dragon City!

The two exchanged a glance and saw the surprise in each other's eyes.

A giant dragon had also been carved above the city gates. It was so lifelike that, at first glance, it seemed alive, baring its fangs and brandishing its claws as it lunged forward.

Li Jiao's face paled slightly, and he could not help quickening his pace.

After entering the city, the two were startled once more.

Although they had already guessed that the city had a large population, the scene before them still exceeded their expectations.

People came and went throughout the city, as numerous as carp crossing a river. Shops stood in rows on every side, while clamour, hawkers' cries, and the sounds of haggling rose and fell without pause.

Humans, Yao, and Dragon Descendants mingled together.

Humans were the most numerous, accounting for fully half the population. The Yao came next. Although there were fewer of them than humans, the difference was not great. Dragon Descendants were the least numerous of the three.

Just what was going on here?

Both men were utterly baffled.
