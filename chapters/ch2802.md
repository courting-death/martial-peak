## Chapter 2802 — Crushing All Opposition

Snow was falling heavily. At some unknown point, thick flakes had begun to drift down upon this ancient and primitive land, dyeing the world in white. The crimson blood on the ground stood out all the more, shocking to behold.

Despite the extreme cold, the villagers seemed completely unaware. Steam rose from every patch of exposed skin.

Outside the village, over a hundred villagers wielded stone axes, stone spears, and other primitive weapons, fighting the giant beasts in a bloody struggle. On the fence, more than a dozen master archers lent powerful support with their superb skills.

The hunched village chief still ran about the battlefield. From time to time, his pitch-black cane would emit a faint glow or two, using his meager Shamanic Arts to resist the invasion of the beast tide.

The blood-red glow on the villagers who had been cast with the Bloodlust Art was growing dimmer. This was a sign that the Bloodlust Art was about to lose its effect. Once it faded, these villagers would fall into a long period of weakness and exhaustion, losing all strength to fight. If the beast tide was not repelled before then, the village’s fate would be sealed.

The hundred strongest warriors would become the beasts’ winter feast, while the elderly, women, and children in the village would fare no better—none would escape this calamity.

By then, as the iron hooves of the giant beasts trampled past, the land would be filled with nothing but wails.

The beast tide would not retreat. The villagers fought to the death, for behind them was their home. No one would back down.

Swoosh, swoosh…

Yang Kai loosed three arrows in a single breath, and three giant beasts fell dead on the spot. This godlike display left Ah Hua utterly dumbfounded.

If Yang Kai’s earlier flawless archery had already shocked her, then what she saw now left her mind completely blank, unable to process it.

So this was another way to use a bow and arrow.

As the village’s finest archer, Ah Hua had her own pride. When it came to archery, no one in the entire tribe could surpass her. Yet Yang Kai’s technique of loosing three arrows at once was something she had never even heard of, let alone seen.

We can win!

We can definitely win!

Ah Niu’s sudden outburst let Ah Hua see hope for victory. As long as he could maintain this state, the beast tide was nothing to worry about.

“Sister Ah Hua, Brother Ah Niu, bad news! We’re almost out of arrows—these are the last five bundles. Aunt Li told me to let you know to use them sparingly.”

The youth in charge of logistics ran up with several bundles of arrows, shouting urgently. His words made Ah Hua feel as if she had fallen into an ice cave.

One bundle held ten arrows, so five bundles meant fifty arrows. At best, they could only kill fifty giant beasts, but how could the beast tide below possibly be counted in fifties?

Ah Hua’s face turned deathly pale. If they lost the support of the archers, Ah Hu and the others fighting below would surely die.

She turned and glared at the youth, shouting, “How can there be so few left? What about the arrows in the warehouse? Didn’t you take them out?”

The youth replied with a miserable face, “We took them all out, but you’re using them too fast. Aunt Li and the others are making more as quickly as they can, but they can’t keep up.”

Using them too fast? No, it was that the battle had lasted too long. Never before had a beast tide persisted for so long. Even after suffering such heavy losses, the giant beasts showed no sign of retreat. This was nothing like the beast tides of the past.

Looking up, thick snowflakes drifted down from the sky. The biting wind howled through the forest.

Winter had come. The fierce beasts needed to stockpile food for the cold months, or else the harsh environment would starve them in their dens.

What to do? What to do? This nearly hopeless news left Ah Hua reeling. Her pale lips were bitten so hard they nearly bled, yet she seemed not to notice. She turned to look outside the village, at the villagers who entrusted their backs to her and the others. Her heart twisted in agony.

“I’ll leave this to you!”

Ah Hua was startled by the words. She turned to look at Ah Niu, asking instinctively, “What?”

Yang Kai took a deep breath, lowered his giant bow, and tilted his head to smile at Ah Hua. “I’ll be right back!”

As soon as he finished speaking, he jumped down from the fence and charged toward the blood-soaked battlefield.

Ah Hua’s eyes widened instantly. She opened her mouth to shout, but her voice was swallowed by the howling wind. She didn’t know if Ah Niu had heard her.

This was suicide! Being able to draw the bow and kill giant beasts was impressive, but fighting them up close was a different matter entirely. To battle giant beasts three or four zhang long required extraordinary physical strength and the courage to face death without fear.

As for Ah Niu’s courage, that was one thing, but his physical strength was plain to see.

He was even smaller than the village’s children. What could someone like that do on the battlefield? He probably wouldn’t even fill the gap between a giant beast’s teeth.

After jumping down from the fence, Yang Kai casually grabbed a stone axe, braved the biting wind, and stepped onto the battlefield, his feet squelching through a mixture of flesh and blood. In the blink of an eye, he charged into the beast horde.

It was as if a newborn lamb had rushed into a pack of tigers—his figure was instantly swallowed up.

Ah Hua could no longer bear to watch. She picked up an arrow, silently vowing to avenge Ah Niu.

“Sister Ah Hua, look!” The youth hadn’t left yet. Suddenly, he pointed excitedly.

Following his finger, Ah Hua saw giant beasts inexplicably being sent flying, flailing in midair before crashing to the ground, bleeding profusely—dead in an instant.

More giant beasts exploded in midair, their massive bodies even splitting in two.

In the blink of an eye, more than a dozen giant beasts gathered in one spot were wiped out. A not-so-burly figure stood like a javelin, stone axe in hand, covered in blood and bits of viscera.

“How…?” Ah Hua was dumbfounded.

The dozen or so master archers atop the fence were just as stunned, forgetting to loose their arrows in support. All of them stared blankly at that frail figure. The very person they had once scorned, even wished to exile, now stood tall and mighty on this blood-soaked battlefield—like a savage god incarnate!

He stood there as if he could block all the wind and rain, giving everyone an unprecedented sense of peace.

It seemed a loud laugh came from that direction, but the wind was too strong to hear clearly. Still, everyone could see the look of excitement and joy on Ah Niu’s face.

The commotion quickly drew the attention of the other giant beasts. Pairs of blood-red eyes turned this way. A low growl sounded, and some of the beasts besieging the villagers broke off to surround Yang Kai.

With an axe in hand, Yang Kai’s aura soared. His sharp gaze swept over the giant beasts, making them all hesitate, unable to take a step forward.

All beasts have instincts. Theirs told them that this man… was no easy prey.

The low growl hidden in the cold wind sounded again, and only then did the giant beasts resume their advance.

Beside him lay the mangled corpse of a villager. Even in death, the man still gripped his stone spear, its tip buried deep in the soft belly of a giant beast. The two had fallen together—the villager torn to pieces, half his head bitten away, yet not before dragging the beast down with him.

The blood had already congealed.

Yang Kai reached out and took the man’s stone spear. With a spear and an axe, he spun them in his hands, took a deep breath, and stomped hard on the ground. Like a thunderbolt, he shot into the beast horde.

Thrust, chop, hack, sweep…

To any onlooker, Yang Kai’s every move seemed plain and unremarkable, no different from an ordinary person who had never cultivated. The weapons in his hands were nothing but the most primitive tools—a stone spear and a stone axe.

Yet in the hands of someone whose strength had been suppressed but who was once of the Emperor Realm, these simple weapons became miraculous.

Everywhere he went, he crushed all opposition. Not a single giant beast could withstand him. One after another, they were sent flying or hacked to the ground, each bearing shocking, gaping wounds.

Wounds that were fatal!

The beasts whimpered and retreated. The encirclement of over a hundred giant beasts was torn open by Yang Kai.

By the time Yang Kai broke through, a third of the giant beasts had already fallen.

Ahead, a hunched old man who had been hiding his presence and form stared at Yang Kai in disbelief, as if seeing him for the first time.

His shock was so great that his Shamanic Arts faltered, revealing his hidden form. The giant beast beside him immediately lunged to bite.

If nothing unexpected happened, the old man would surely die.

Whoosh—

An arrow whistled through the air, knocking the giant beast to the ground.

The old man turned to see Ah Hua on the fence, nodding slightly at him.

“Village Chief, give me one!” Yang Kai, covered in blood and beast entrails, radiated killing intent, yet grinned at the old man.

The old man shivered inexplicably, but still raised his cane and began chanting in an obscure tongue.

With a wave of his cane, a beam of light shot out, striking Yang Kai.

In the next instant, Yang Kai felt his blood boil as if set ablaze. A thick golden light appeared on his skin, making him look solemn and dignified.

The old man was even more shocked, his cloudy eyes suddenly shining with brilliance.

“So this is the Bloodlust Art!” Yang Kai took a deep breath, clearly feeling his strength increase. But it seemed the Bloodlust Art had a drawback: while it boosted strength, it dulled the senses.

Only Yang Kai, with his Emperor Realm foundation, could ignore this side effect. Ordinary people like Ah Hu could never withstand it.

No wonder the villagers under the Bloodlust Art fought so fearlessly. Their own courage played a part, but the technique’s effect was undeniable.

With their senses dulled, they felt little pain or fear, making them naturally fierce in battle.

However, the golden light on Yang Kai’s skin made him stand out. Others under the Bloodlust Art glowed red, but only he shone gold.

There was no helping it. The Bloodlust Art stimulated one’s innate Essence-Blood, and his blood was golden to begin with.
