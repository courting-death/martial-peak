## Chapter 2818 — Die

The tall barbarian hid in a secluded spot, pulled out a beast skin pouch from his chest, opened it, and peered inside. He immediately let out a low, sly chuckle.

Over a thousand Qing Coins—this was no small fortune, enough to cover his expenses for a whole year.

"Not a bad haul."

Suddenly, a voice sounded behind him, startling the barbarian. He quickly turned around, glaring warily at the speaker. Yet, the moment their eyes met, the barbarian felt a pang of guilt.

It was simple—he recognized this person as the owner of the money pouch.

In Frost Snow City, barbarians as thin and frail as him were a true rarity—he might well have been the only one of his kind.

But even after recognizing him, the barbarian had no intention of admitting anything. He put on a fierce, menacing look and said, "What do you want?"

Yang Kai grinned, "You stole my money pouch and still act like nothing happened. I have to say, you’ve completely overturned my understanding of barbarians."

"What money pouch of yours!"

“The one you’re stuffing into your coat right now,” Yang Kai said coldly, watching his movements.

"I don’t understand what you’re talking about." The barbarian nonchalantly stowed the pouch away, impatiently saying, "If you’ve got nothing else, I’m leaving. Don’t follow me."

Yang Kai was furious. “Shameless thief! How dare you act so brazenly? Do you believe I won’t call the Frost Snow Tribe’s guards to deal with you?”

The burly barbarian looked at Yang Kai with disdain, sneering, "Go ahead, call them!"

He was so fearless that Yang Kai was left dumbfounded. Calling the Frost Snow Tribe’s guards was clearly impossible. Even though he was certain this guy was a thief who stole his pouch, he had no real evidence. Bringing the guards over would just be a waste of time.

Besides, Yang Kai didn’t want to trouble others with this kind of thing.

Clicking his tongue twice, Yang Kai said, "Kid, you’ve messed with the wrong person."

The burly barbarian still sneered, "Enough nonsense. If you keep nagging, I’ll beat you to death!" As he spoke, he raised a fist the size of a sandpot at Yang Kai, looking quite intimidating.

As soon as he finished speaking, the burly barbarian’s expression suddenly changed. He saw Yang Kai shift his steps and charge straight at him, a fist rapidly enlarging before his eyes.

"Courting death!" the burly barbarian roared in fury. He never expected this seemingly frail tribesman to strike first.

Shocked and enraged, he threw a punch in return.

The moment their fists collided, Yang Kai let out a surprised sound. He realized there was no wild force coming at him—the opponent’s intimidating build and fists were nothing but a fancy pillow.

In the midst of the barbarian’s shock and terror, his massive body was sent flying backward.

While airborne, the burly figure suddenly twisted and transformed, turning into a petite, curvaceous silhouette.

With a sharp smack, it fell to the ground like a dead fish. At the same time, a single green leaf drifted down from the sky.

Yang Kai’s eyes nearly popped out of their sockets. He stood there dumbfounded for a long while before finally coming back to his senses.

To think a single punch could turn a burly man into a young girl—this definitely wasn’t his fault.

As Yang Kai hesitated, the girl who had fallen to the ground struggled to get up. When she looked at Yang Kai again, her gaze was full of wariness.

Now that he looked closely, Yang Kai realized the girl was only sixteen or seventeen, and she carried the aura of a Shaman. He couldn’t tell what rank of Shaman she was; he hadn’t been in this world long and hadn’t met many Shamans, so his judgment of their power was still imprecise.

After her body changed, the clothes she wore became loose, but they still couldn’t hide her proud figure. Clearly, this girl was quite well-endowed.

Suddenly exposed, the girl was extremely flustered. Glancing around, she hurried forward a few steps, picked up the green leaf that had just fallen, and placed it atop her head. Instantly, her petite body twisted again, reverting to the burly form.

"You’re from the Drifting Tribe!" A flash of insight struck Yang Kai, and he immediately called out her origin.

"Wh-what nonsense are you spouting!" The girl, now in her burly form, avoided his gaze and stubbornly retorted.

"Is that so!" Yang Kai smiled at her, looking confident. "I’m just making things up?"

"What Drifting Tribe? I’m from the Cloud Creek Tribe."

"Heh heh…" Yang Kai chuckled, then cupped his hands around his mouth and shouted, "Everyone, come take a look! There’s a thief from the Drifting Tribe here, everyone…"

"Shut up!" The girl’s expression changed drastically. She reached into her chest, pulled out the money pouch, and tossed it straight to Yang Kai. "Here’s your pouch, just stop shouting."

Yang Kai tossed the pouch in his hand, looking at the girl with amusement. "If you’re not from the Drifting Tribe, why are you so nervous?"

The girl forced herself to act tough. "Who’s nervous?"

According to the village chief’s teachings, the entire Ancient Barbarian Tribe valued strong physiques, but there was one tribe that was different—the Drifting Tribe! Its people wandered without a home, drifting from place to place, and their numbers were few, rarely seen by outsiders. They didn’t focus on training their bodies, so their physiques were vastly different from other tribes.

If that were all, it wouldn’t matter, but the key was that Drifting Tribe members had sticky fingers—every one of them was a born thief. So their reputation within the Ancient Barbarian Tribe was quite poor.

As Yang Kai traveled, he was constantly looked down upon. On one hand, his physique seemed too weak to earn respect; on the other, some mistook him for a member of the Drifting Tribe.

Yang Kai’s experiences were a reflection of how Drifting Tribe members fared outside their homeland.

Clicking his tongue, Yang Kai circled the girl a few times, intrigued. "I’ve heard about the Drifting Tribe’s uniqueness for ages, but today’s the first time I’ve seen it. Fascinating… Come, let me see your true form again."

His mischievous look made the girl’s skin crawl. She trembled, "What are you trying to do?"

Yang Kai shrugged, "Just want to take a look, nothing else."

"Don’t even think about it!"

Yang Kai cupped his hands around his mouth, about to shout again, when the girl said, "Enough!"

She glared at him fiercely. Yang Kai smiled back, unbothered.

In the end, the girl gave in. She reached up, plucked the green leaf from her head, and instantly her burly form shrank, reverting to her true appearance.

"You’ll regret this!" The girl bit her red lips, glaring at Yang Kai as if he were her mortal enemy, her eyes blazing with hatred.

"That’s better. Why would a perfectly good girl dress up as a man?" Yang Kai chuckled.

After seeing so much of the wild, rugged beauty of the barbarians, it was truly refreshing and pleasant to behold a young woman’s natural form.

"Have you seen enough?" the girl said impatiently, then placed the green leaf atop her head again, instantly returning to her burly form.

Yang Kai shook his head and sighed, feeling a bit unsatisfied, but still curious. "Is this an illusion? Why didn’t I sense any Shamanic Art energy when you changed?"

That magical green leaf seemed to have incredible effects. With the leaf, the girl’s form changed; without it, her true self was revealed. Yang Kai had thought the leaf was a tool for her illusion, but on closer inspection, something felt off.

The green leaf looked just like a green coin—clearly both were leaves from the Divine Tree. Besides, he’d never heard of any Shamanic Art requiring such a tool.

"Why do you care? The pouch is returned, what more do you want?" The girl was extremely impatient. Having her true form exposed was her biggest mistake; now that Yang Kai held this secret, she’d be at his mercy from now on.

Fortunately, Yang Kai wasn’t as despicable as she feared. He simply pondered for a moment and asked, "What’s your name?"

The girl was silent for a while, then replied, "Die!"

"I’m Ah Niu!" Yang Kai smiled back.

"Shaman Niu… I’ll remember. If there’s nothing else, I’m leaving!" Die said, and seeing Yang Kai had no objection, she immediately turned and left.

Yang Kai watched her figure disappear, chuckled softly, and continued shopping for medicinal herbs.

The next day, after Yang Kai finished refining enough healing medicine and walked out of the tree cave, he didn’t immediately fly down. Instead, he steered toward another tree cave nearby and knocked on the trunk.

A head quickly poked out from the tree cave, staring at Yang Kai in surprise. "How did you know I live here!"

This neighbor was none other than Die, whom he’d met yesterday.

Seeing Yang Kai again, and having him find her doorstep directly, Die’s gaze was full of panic.

"I happened to see you return here yesterday."

"What do you want!" Die was miserable. Even though Yang Kai caught her stealing his pouch yesterday, she wasn’t too afraid. But having her Drifting Tribe identity exposed left her at a loss.

She’d lived here for over ten years, but it was the first time someone had seen through her true form.

"Don’t be nervous." Yang Kai smiled gently. "I just need your help with something."

"What is it?" Die frowned.

"You know I’ve been selling medicine these days, right? That’s why you targeted me, isn’t it?"

Die frowned, answering with silence.

"Since you know, it’s easy." Yang Kai handed over the jar in his hand.

Die didn’t take it, just looked at him suspiciously. "What for?"

"Help me sell medicine. You know the price. I’ll give you a tenth of the profits!"

Die’s eyes lit up, exclaiming, "Really?"

“I’ve already given you everything—why would I lie to you?”

Die quickly took the jars, overjoyed. After all, Shaman Niu could earn over a thousand Qing Coins a day selling healing medicine. A tenth meant over a hundred—not a huge sum, but far more honorable than stealing.

"Why?" Die asked, a bit puzzled. By all rights, after she’d stolen his money pouch yesterday, this person could have made trouble for her or exposed her identity. Instead, he’d let her off the hook and now was even offering her a share of the profits. She truly couldn’t understand his reasoning.
