## Chapter 2843 — Chain of Life

"Want to fight?" Yang Kai raised his brow, a wild, defiant grin on his face. "Fine by me!"

As soon as he finished speaking, an incantation swiftly rang out from his mouth. With a wave of his hand, a halo of light suddenly spread, enveloping his thousand-strong force.

Suddenly, a crimson radiance surged across the bodies of the thousand warriors, as if they were shrouded in a mist of blood. From within Ah Hu and the others, crackling sounds erupted, their already towering frames swelling even larger in an instant. The beast-hide garments they wore stretched taut over their bulging muscles. Every pair of eyes turned blood-red, fearless and unyielding—as if even if a Shaman Saint stood before them, they would still raise their weapons and strike without hesitation.

The murderous aura of a thousand men gathered in one place, as if it would pierce the very heavens.

Endless power surged forth from within. The thousand warriors gripped their weapons tightly, as if reborn, each one brimming with excitement, eyes wide in disbelief that they could possess such overwhelming strength.

Bloodlust Art!

And not just any Bloodlust Art—this was an extremely high-level one! Though their eyes were blood-red and fearless, they had not lost their reason. Only the most profound Bloodlust Art could achieve this. Ordinary Bloodlust Art could indeed make Barbarian Tribe warriors stronger and dull their senses, but at the cost of their rationality. In severe cases, it could drive them mad, damaging their souls, and even after the effect faded, lingering side effects might remain.

But the Bloodlust Art cast by this Shaman Niu was different. Only a Shaman King-level Great Shaman could perform such a feat.

The many Great Shaman Masters were all visibly shocked. Just this one Bloodlust Art was enough to make them look at Shaman Niu with new eyes—this was not something just anyone could perform.

Especially the way he cast the Bloodlust Art, easily enveloping exactly a thousand people—not one more, not one less. What kind of precise control did that require? And it seemed he was far from his limit.

Was this truly about to become a battle?

Even the Bloodlust Art had been used—there was no turning back now.

Yet this was not the end.

After the Bloodlust Art, Yang Kai continued chanting, his hands rising and falling. With each motion, beams of different colored light burst forth, sweeping over the thousand warriors.

Lightness Art, Stone Armor Art, Great Strength Art…

Layer upon layer of light enveloped every warrior of Shaman Niu's tribe. Their bodies glowed with a mix of crimson and earthy yellow, as if clad in heavy armor, impervious to blades and spears. Breezes curled around their legs, making them swifter and fiercer. The golden light of the Great Strength Art added another thirty to fifty percent to their strength.

The thousand warriors' breathing grew heavy, their fighting spirit fully awakened.

The three to four thousand people surrounding them were so intimidated by this display that they involuntarily retreated several steps, faces full of dread.

There was no need to fight—just looking at this, it was clear that every one of Shaman Niu's tribe could take on two or more opponents alone.

Shaman Tu and another Great Shaman Master looked extremely grim. They had thought that bringing out their own people would be enough to intimidate the other side, that Shaman Niu would lose his composure. Who could have guessed this man would be so fierce, not only unafraid but immediately casting numerous empowering Shamanic Arts on his own tribe?

At this moment, the two Great Shaman Masters, like the others, were deeply shaken.

Even a Shaman King would struggle to unleash so many large-scale Shamanic Arts in succession without assistance. Shaman Niu was only a Lower-Level Great Shaman Master; how could he have so much shamanic power to spare?

Only now did they realize they had truly underestimated Shaman Niu. The amount of shamanic power stored in his body was far beyond that of an ordinary Great Shaman Master, and he was only a Lower-Level one at that. If he advanced to Upper-Level, what would he be capable of then?

While the two were still reeling, Yang Kai began chanting again, and another beam of light descended.

As the light fell, over a thousand people simultaneously shuddered. The crimson glow on their bodies seemed to come alive, merging and converging together.

In the blink of an eye, the thousand warriors seemed to become one, no longer separate individuals. The overwhelming aura they gathered made even heaven and earth tremble.

"Chain of Life!" Shaman Tu's face changed dramatically. He couldn't help but swallow, staring at Yang Kai as if looking at a monster.

The Chain of Life was a truly profound Shamanic Art, one that only Great Shaman Masters were qualified and able to learn. This art could link the life force of any number of Barbarian Tribe warriors together. Under its effect, any injury suffered by one would be shared among all, allowing them to withstand damage far beyond what they could bear alone.

This was the Shamanic Art the entire Barbarian Tribe relied upon most in large-scale wars.

However, while Great Shaman Masters could learn the Chain of Life, actually casting it often required the assistance of other shamans.

With a small group, it was manageable. An Upper-Level Great Shaman Master could link the life force of about two hundred people, but with the help of Shaman Masters, Shaman Warriors, and Shaman Apprentices, the number could be multiplied several or even a dozen times. The more helpers, the better the effect.

Yet when Shaman Niu cast the Chain of Life just now, he had no assistance at all.

Still, his Shamanic Art instantly enveloped a thousand people, linking their life force together, merging these scattered individuals into one.

If not witnessed firsthand, no Great Shaman Master would believe such a thing possible.

Only a Shaman King—an Upper-Level Shaman King at that—could link the lives of a thousand people with the Chain of Life.

Facing such a terrifying group, any numerical advantage vanished in an instant.

Shaman Tu and the other Great Shaman Master could also cast the Chain of Life to link their own warriors, but that would require preparation and the cooperation of their shamans. How could they possibly cast it as effortlessly as Yang Kai?

In other words, if a fight broke out now, Yang Kai's thousand men could crush ten thousand Barbarian Tribe warriors with little loss.

"This shaman is ready. When do we begin?" Yang Kai looked down at Shaman Tu, asking calmly, his unruffled demeanor making the other Great Shaman Masters' eyelids twitch.

To cast so many large-scale Shamanic Arts and still show no sign of fatigue—was he even human?

For any Great Shaman Master, just two or three of those arts would exhaust all their shamanic power and leave them drained.

Facing such an opponent, what was the point of fighting?

But was it really worth doing this? The other Shamanic Arts were one thing—dispersing them had little effect on the warriors. But Bloodlust Art was different. Once activated, it consumed the warriors' innate Essence-Blood. When the effect faded, they would be left weak for many days.

This Shaman Niu had cast Bloodlust Art on his own warriors without a word—was he insane? Did he not know what consequences that would bring?

Confronted by Yang Kai's aggressive stance, Shaman Tu's face flushed red, unable to utter a word.

What could he say? Start the fight now?

He had never intended to truly fight Yang Kai. Bringing out his own tribe was just for show, to intimidate. Who knew this man would be so wild, flaring up at the slightest provocation?

Now caught between a rock and a hard place, Shaman Tu bitterly regretted his actions, wishing he could turn back time.

"Oh?" Yang Kai looked mockingly at Shaman Tu, chuckling. "From the look of it, Lord Shaman Tu doesn't seem ready. Should I give you a few days to prepare?"

Shaman Tu's face turned crimson as he stiffened his neck and said, "Shaman Niu, don't get too arrogant. I just think that, outside the royal city, if we really fight, we'll surely alarm Lord Shaman King. If we anger him, no one will escape unscathed. It's not that I'm afraid of you."

These words reached everyone's ears, and the many Great Shaman Masters all had different expressions. Anyone could see that Shaman Tu had no chance of winning and could only use words to save face.

But after this commotion, Shaman Tu's reputation was ruined. No matter what he said, it was meaningless—he could no longer inspire confidence in his tribe.

As for Shaman Niu, with his record of sending two Great Shaman Masters flying and now displaying such powerful Shamanic Arts, he would only attract more wandering warriors to his side.

Fortunately, Lord Shaman King's order was for each Great Shaman Master to gather two to three thousand tribesmen. If Shaman Niu were left unchecked, he could probably gather tens of thousands. If that happened, many Great Shaman Masters would fail their tasks.

"So Lord Shaman Tu doesn't want to fight?" Yang Kai smiled, looking disappointed. "And here I prepared for so long."

Shaman Tu snorted coldly. "We're all from the Southern Barbarian Tribe. What's the point of fighting among ourselves? If you want to fight, go kill demons. Then we'll see who kills more!"

Yang Kai stroked his chin, pondered for a moment, then nodded. "Lord Shaman Tu has a point."

Shaman Tu's expression relaxed. "So you agree, then?"

Yang Kai smiled faintly. "In that case, let's settle things on the battlefield."

"Good! It's a deal! I won't lose to you," Shaman Tu declared, waving his hand. His two thousand subordinates immediately breathed a sigh of relief and scattered like birds and beasts.

As Shaman Tu turned away, a gust of wind hit him, and he realized his back was soaked with sweat. He couldn't help but shiver, cursing himself for failing to recognize that he had kicked an iron plate.

On the other side, Yang Kai quickly formed seals with his hands, dispersing the Chain of Life and Bloodlust Art.

To Ah Hu and the others' surprise, when the Bloodlust Art was dispersed, they didn't feel the usual dizziness. Though somewhat tired, it was within reason, and for the strong Barbarian Tribe warriors, such aftereffects were nothing—a little food would have them back to normal.
