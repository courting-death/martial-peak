## Chapter 2914 — Who Are You, Really

In the stone cavern, Gong Yue remained busy without pause, standing before the blood-red gate and continuously forming various hand seals, his expression shifting occasionally.

The others were all meditating and resting, but Yang Tai seemed unable to sit still. He paced back and forth, glancing toward the blood-red gate every so often, yet not daring to approach and disturb, looking extremely anxious.

Wu Kuangyi called him to sit, but Yang Tai declined with a gentle smile.

“Senior Brother Yang, is there still something amiss?” Hua Yulu quietly sent a voice transmission to inquire.

Yang Kai did not open his eyes, replying via voice transmission, “Let’s wait and see.”

Up to now, he hadn’t noticed anything particularly suspicious, aside from his earlier doubts about Gong Yue’s identity. But that was only a suspicion—he couldn’t be certain himself, so naturally he couldn’t give Hua Yulu any definite answer.

Whether this suspicion would become reality depended on Gong Yue’s next actions.

While resting, he immersed his mind into the jade slip Nanmen Dajun had given him, once again reviewing its contents and committing some key points to memory.

Half a day later, Gong Yue suddenly stepped back a few paces and gazed at the blood-red gate. A faint smile appeared on his face as he called out, “Everyone, please come over.”

At his words, everyone perked up, rising together and heading over. Yang Tai was especially impatient, hurrying to stand before Gong Yue and asking urgently, “Brother Gong, have you found any clues?”

Gong Yue stroked his beard with a smile. “A little.”

“Good, good! As long as there’s a clue.” Yang Tai’s face flushed with excitement.

“However, if we want to break the formation, I’ll still need everyone’s help.”

At these words, Yang Kai’s heart skipped a beat. His eyes narrowed, and his suspicion grew even stronger.

Wu Kuangyi said, “Family Head Gong, what do you need us to do? We’re not well-versed in formation arts—only Brother Fang knows a little.”

Gong Yue smiled. “No need for mastery, just cooperation. The key is that everyone must trust this old man unconditionally!”

Everyone looked at each other, unsure just how much trust he meant. Yang Tai said, “Since we invited Brother Gong to help, of course we trust you.”

“That’s good.” Gong Yue smiled slightly. “I’ll teach everyone a set of techniques. Once you’re familiar, we can form the array together. If this formation is set up, we can break this ancient restriction.”

“Then what are we waiting for? Please teach us, Brother Gong,” Yang Tai urged eagerly.

Gong Yue smiled, beckoned everyone closer, took out several blank jade slips, and inscribed information into them with his Divine Sense before handing them out one by one.

Yang Kai accepted his, swept it with his Divine Sense, and fully comprehended the contents, sighing softly in his heart.

Though he didn’t know what was in the others’ jade slips, he figured they must differ from his own. In a joint formation, everyone had different responsibilities, and at this time, a central figure was needed to coordinate everyone’s strength. That central figure would undoubtedly be Gong Yue.

The central position controlled the most intricate part of the formation. As the core, once the array was formed, he could, to some extent, control the others involved. While not enough to decide life or death, such control was not something Emperor Realm cultivators could easily accept.

That was why he’d just said everyone must trust him unconditionally.

Without trust, the formation couldn’t be completed together.

Yang Kai didn’t bother to study the jade slip’s contents in detail—he’d already seen them before.

The jade slip Nanmen Dajun had given him contained records on this very subject.

Back then, he’d had Bian Yuqing bring information on this ancient restriction formation back to Lingxiao Palace, hoping Nanmen Dajun would devise a way to break it. True to his reputation as an Imperial-Grade Formation Master, Nanmen Dajun had come up with three plans in just over a month.

One of them was to break the formation with another formation, just as Gong Yue was doing now. But if they really did that, they’d fall into someone else’s trap. Breaking a formation with a formation was only the beginning; to fully unravel the ancient restriction on the blood-red gate, another, extremely sinister method was needed.

Yang Kai had originally planned to use Nanmen Dajun’s plan to help Hua Yulu and the others break the formation, then explore inside for anything valuable. He hadn’t expected Gong Yue to appear halfway through.

With such a formation grandmaster in the group, there was no need for him to stand out.

But Gong Yue’s actions made him suspicious. If he’d only doubted Gong Yue’s identity before, now he was certain there was a problem. Yang Tai was also likely involved, and as for the other person, Yang Kai wasn’t sure yet.

He glanced at Yang Tai, who appeared focused on studying his jade slip but was actually circulating his Divine Sense to communicate silently with someone.

Yang Kai immediately understood.

Sensing his gaze, Gong Yue suddenly looked over and smiled. “Young Brother Yang, do you have any questions?”

“I do!” Yang Kai’s voice rang out, surprisingly loud, echoing through the stone cavern and drawing everyone’s attention. Wu Kuangyi and the others frowned, displeased.

Gong Yue said, “What is it, Young Brother Yang? Perhaps this old man can explain.”

“Just one question…” Yang Kai stared at him, eyes narrowed, and asked slowly, “Who are you, really?”

Gong Yue’s smile stiffened slightly. The other Emperor Realm cultivators, startled, looked up, forgetting about their jade slips, their faces full of shock.

After a brief daze, Hua Yulu immediately moved closer to Yang Kai, as if seeking a sense of security.

The stone cavern fell silent for a long while. Only Yang Kai’s piercing gaze remained, as if two blades were trying to cut open Gong Yue’s mind and expose the secrets hidden within.

“What do you mean?” The first to question wasn’t Gong Yue, but Wu Kuangyi, who seemed impatient by nature—if he had a question, he had to ask, or he’d feel uncomfortable.

“Exactly what I said,” Yang Kai replied blandly.

Wu Kuangyi frowned, secretly annoyed at Yang Kai’s attitude, but there was nothing wrong with his answer—he was just too surprised.

“Young Brother, do you have some grudge with me? Or with my Gong family?” Gong Yue asked, his calm smile returning.

“No grudge at all.” Yang Kai shook his head.

Gong Yue laughed. “If there’s no grudge, why do you keep questioning my identity?”

When Gong Yue had first arrived, Yang Kai had already questioned him once. Fang Zhuo had then tested Gong Yue’s formation skills, confirming his identity as the former Family Head of the Gong family.

Now it was the second time. Even a clay figurine has some fire in it. As the former Family Head of the Gong family from Tianhe Valley and a Second Stage Emperor Realm expert, Gong Yue’s composure in the face of such questioning showed his self-restraint. Someone with a worse temper would have fought Yang Kai by now.

“This old man is just this old man, the former Family Head of the Gong family, Gong Yue, who stepped down two hundred years ago.” Gong Yue’s voice suddenly rose, his posture straightening, words ringing with conviction.

“Brother Yang Jiu, enough. I’ll stake my life and reputation on it—there’s no problem with Brother Gong’s identity.” Yang Tai jumped in to smooth things over, but a trace of worry was obvious in his brows. He urged, “Now’s not the time for this. Let’s focus on breaking the ancient restriction first.”

“If the problem isn’t resolved, how can we work together to break the formation?” Yang Kai retorted.

“You…” Yang Tai's face flushed with anger, his expression filled with reproach.

“This Brother Yang has a point.” Shen Bingru, who had been silent, suddenly spoke up.

Yang Tai turned to her in surprise. “Madam Shen, you too…?”

Shen Bingru was a helper he had personally invited, and the two shared a close relationship. He never expected that at such a critical moment, Shen Bingru would actually side with Yang Kai. An inexplicable pain pierced his heart.

Shen Bingru smiled gently. "Brother Yang, please don't be upset. We who have cultivated to this realm have walked a treacherous path fraught with danger. Every step has required utmost caution, like treading on thin ice. Without such vigilance, we would have long since turned to dust like the common masses. So I'm rather curious to hear what young Brother Yang means—what is he trying to convey?"

Her beautiful eyes turned to Yang Kai, bright and expressive. Yang Kai nodded in acknowledgment.

She didn’t trust Yang Kai, but since there was a problem, it was only right to listen—it wouldn’t take much time anyway.

Wu Kuangyi hesitated for a long time before finally speaking. “What are you suspicious of? Tell us so everyone can hear.”

Yang Kai said, “I’ve already told you.”

Gong Yue sighed. “Your suspicion has no basis.”

Yang Tai looked at Hua Yulu in anguish. “Palace Master Hua, is this the helper you found? If he doesn’t want to help, he can leave. Why disrupt our unity? Now the formation isn’t even broken, and we’re already having internal problems—all because of a baseless remark.”

Hua Yulu blushed slightly. “I believe… Senior Brother Yang.”

Yang Tai’s face darkened as he snapped at Yang Kai, “Boy, if you don’t want to help break the formation, you can leave now. You’re an Emperor Realm cultivator—you know the way, just go back. This old man won’t force you!”

Yang Kai smiled faintly. “If I leave, how will the formation be completed?”

Yang Tai snorted. “With Brother Gong here, what does it matter if you’re gone? He can break the formation alone.”

Yang Kai turned to Gong Yue, grinning broadly. “Then you’d better ask the Gong Family Head—can the Six Harmonies Sole Sovereign Formation be formed with just five people assisting him?”

At these words, Gong Yue’s expression changed slightly. Fang Zhuo, who had never spoken, also looked up at Yang Kai in surprise.

Yang Tai’s mouth hung open, staring blankly at Yang Kai.

The others quickly realized Yang Kai was right.
