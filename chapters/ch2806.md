## Chapter 2806 — Mastering One Law, Mastering All Laws

"Village Chief, may you be blessed!"

In the shabby wooden hut, Yang Kai saw the village's only Shaman. Although old, the village chief was up early, currently cradling a bowl of something like porridge, drinking it with relish.

Hearing the voice, the village chief glanced at Yang Kai. Only after finishing his food did he set down the stone bowl and say, "Ah Niu, is there something you need?"

"I want to ask you for some Barbarian God Wine!" Yang Kai got straight to the point.

"Barbarian God Wine..." The village chief was momentarily stunned. He stood up, leaning on his cane, and asked, "Why do you still want Barbarian God Wine?"

"It tastes good. I want another bowl!" Yang Kai replied.

The village chief's mouth twitched, but soon he seemed to realize something. He looked Yang Kai up and down, and a startling light burst from his cloudy eyes as he trembled, "Did you... did you awaken the power of a Shaman?"

"What?" Yang Kai was bewildered. The village chief's train of thought jumped too quickly for him to follow.

"Give me your hand!" The village chief, excited, reached out to grab Yang Kai's hand.

Although Yang Kai was suspicious, he didn't refuse, letting that withered hand grasp his wrist. The next moment, he felt a faint power invade his Meridians, slowly traveling up his arm.

The village chief trembled even more violently, his old face flushed as if he'd suddenly become decades younger. He exclaimed in delight, "It's truly the power of a Shaman! My Cangnan Village finally has a successor!"

"You mean this?" Yang Kai's heart stirred. He circulated the little Emperor Yuan left in his body, causing a bit of light to burst from his fingertip.

The village chief stared intently, nodding repeatedly, "This is the power of a Shaman!" As he spoke, he walked out of the hut, knelt on the ground, and with a devout expression, pressed his hands together, "Thank you, Barbarian God, for your grace and protection!"

Yang Kai frowned slightly, stepping forward to help the old village chief up, lest he get too excited and accidentally pass away.

The village chief took a deep breath, patted Yang Kai's shoulder, and said, "Ah Niu, now you can be counted as one of the Shamans. I am very gratified."

Yang Kai said, "But I feel lost!"

The village chief chuckled, "No need to be lost. The Barbarian God will guide your path. You came to me because you sensed this power, right? That's why you wanted Barbarian God Wine?"

"Yes!" Yang Kai nodded, peering into the hut. Though the village chief lived here, the place was bare, and Yang Kai saw no sign of Barbarian God Wine.

"There's no more Barbarian God Wine. The bowl from yesterday was the last in the village," the old village chief explained, seemingly seeing through Yang Kai's intentions. "Barbarian God Wine is brewed to awaken the power of Shamans among us barbarians. It requires many ingredients and is extremely complicated to make. That bowl was left over from the ritual two years ago."

"Ritual?" Yang Kai frowned.

The village chief said, "Did you forget? Two years ago, you, Ah Hu, and the other young people in the village all participated in that ritual to determine if you had the qualifications to become a Shaman! Unfortunately, none of you passed at the time."

Yang Kai vaguely understood what he was talking about.

"I never expected you to possess the power of a Shaman now." The village chief looked utterly gratified, as if he could die without regret at this moment.

"There's really no more Barbarian God Wine?" Yang Kai asked, unwilling to give up.

He still wanted to test whether Barbarian God Wine could help him recover some of his strength, but hearing this bad news from the village chief dashed his hopes.

He had already tried before—circulating the cultivation techniques he practiced did not increase his strength at all. The Spiritual Qi of this era seemed different from his own, not matching what he had learned.

The village chief shook his head, "There's none left. Besides, now that you have the power of a Shaman, Barbarian God Wine will no longer have any effect on you."

Whether it works or not, you have to try to know, Yang Kai grumbled inwardly.

Suddenly, the village chief looked at Yang Kai with a bewitching smile and asked, "Ah Niu, do you want greater power? Do you want to become a stronger Shaman?"

Yang Kai immediately nodded his head like a pecking chick.

The village chief chuckled, looking pleased with his teachable pupil, and beckoned, "Come in!"

The two entered the inner room one after the other. The village chief rummaged around for a while before finding something that looked like a tortoise shell in a corner. He handed it to Yang Kai, "Cultivate according to the teachings on this. In time, you will become a Shaman Warrior, a Shaman Master, even a Great Shaman! I'm old, but you're still young. The village will be yours in the future."

Yang Kai took the tortoise shell, his expression solemn, feeling as if he had been entrusted with the fate of the world. He seemed to grow taller in that moment, as if cleansed and elevated.

Then he scratched his head and asked, "Village Chief, what is this?"

The village chief looked at him in surprise and explained, "This is the Shaman cultivation manual."

"What?" Yang Kai was shocked, staring at the tortoise shell in disbelief. "This is the ancient barbarian Shaman's cultivation technique?"

"Ancient or not, it doesn't matter," the village chief shook his head. "Just cultivate according to its teachings and you'll soon become stronger."

"But... I can't read it," Yang Kai said helplessly, looking at the village chief.

The tortoise shell was covered in densely packed small characters, but they were completely unfamiliar to Yang Kai—he couldn't recognize a single one.

The village chief's mouth dropped open. After a long while, he sighed, "Looks like we have to start by teaching you to read."

Yang Kai spent the entire day holed up in the village chief's hut, only coming out at dusk.

In one day, the village chief explained the barbarian Shaman cultivation method recorded on the tortoise shell to Yang Kai, and also taught him many ancient characters. At first, Yang Kai was reluctant to learn.

After all, as long as the village chief explained the technique on the tortoise shell, there was no need to learn ancient characters.

But one sentence from the village chief made him abandon that idea.

"When you become stronger, there will be more manuals for you to choose from. If you can't read, how will you cultivate then?"

Yang Kai thought it made sense. Learning more couldn't hurt, so he settled down to study carefully.

Yang Kai's comprehension was so high that the village chief was overjoyed.

The cultivation method on the tortoise shell had taken the village chief three years to fully understand, but Yang Kai mastered it in just one day. Even with the chief's guidance, this child's comprehension was truly unprecedented.

Of course, Yang Kai was an Emperor Realm cultivator, having learned countless techniques and secret arts, nearly reaching the state of mastering one law and thus mastering all laws. A mere ancient barbarian Shaman technique was not profound to him, so he grasped it instantly.

If not for being unable to read those ancient characters, Yang Kai wouldn't have needed the village chief's guidance at all.

When Yang Kai returned to his wooden hut, his stomach began to rumble loudly, hunger gnawing at him until it felt as though his belly were stuck to his back.

This feeling of hunger was rather nostalgic. With no other choice, he cut some beast meat, boiled it, and gulped it down.

After eating his fill, Yang Kai began cultivating the barbarian Shaman technique.

As the technique circulated, Yang Kai was overjoyed.

The Spiritual Qi he couldn't absorb no matter how he tried last night now began to pour into his body, gradually transforming into his own strength under the guidance of the barbarian Shaman technique.

At the same time, Yang Kai clearly felt an energy source form in his abdomen. Strands of mysterious energy spread from his belly, filling his Meridians and Dantian.

This energy source was somewhat different from the Spiritual Qi of heaven and earth, yet under the transformation of the barbarian Shaman technique, the myriad laws converged into one.

It was the essence of the Demonic Beast's flesh!

Yang Kai instantly saw the truth. What he had eaten earlier was the Demonic Beast's flesh, and now, under the technique's transformation, all the essence within that flesh became his own strength.

Moreover, refining the essence of Demonic Beast flesh was even more convenient and efficient than absorbing Spiritual Qi.

After just the time it takes for an incense stick to burn, his belly rumbled again—the Demonic Beast meat had been completely digested...

He felt hungry once more.

Yang Kai frowned. The sudden drop in cultivation efficiency displeased him, so he quickly got up and cooked another pot of beast meat.

Soon, after eating his fill, he cultivated again.

......

By midnight, Yang Kai had eaten an entire Demonic Beast, leaving only the bones.

If the other villagers saw this terrifying way of eating, their eyes would pop out. Demonic Beast flesh was not so easy to consume—without great strength to suppress it, the hidden Yao Qi would affect one's mind.

But with Yang Kai's powerful foundation, not to mention a mere insignificant Demonic Beast, even a Twelfth-Tier Demonic Beast would be no problem for him.

After this round of cultivation, Yang Kai deeply experienced the simple and brutal nature of ancient barbarian Shaman training.

Just like Demonic Beasts, which have no cultivation methods—their main way to grow stronger is to devour their own kind. The more they eat, the stronger they become.

In a sense, ancient barbarian Shaman cultivation mirrored the survival path of Demonic Beasts. After countless generations of improvement, this led to the flourishing diversity of techniques in the world today.

With the Demonic Beast meat gone, Yang Kai had a sudden idea and pulled out a round Inner Core from beside his bed.

Since flesh could be eaten, the Inner Core should be edible too, right?

For Yang Kai's current body, the Inner Core of a mere insignificant Demonic Beast could be safely digested.

Others would have to think twice—otherwise, the village chief wouldn't have told Yang Kai to soak the Inner Core in wine. But Yang Kai simply opened his mouth and swallowed it whole.

As the technique circulated, Yang Kai soon felt a surge of energy explode in his lower abdomen, far fiercer than before.

The Demonic Beast's Inner Core was the essence of its being, its most valuable part. For a Shaman Apprentice like the village chief, swallowing it directly would almost certainly mean death by explosion.

But for Yang Kai, it was nothing more than a bit of heat in his belly.

And then...

That was it.
