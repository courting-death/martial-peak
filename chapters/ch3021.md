## Chapter 3021 — Half-Dragon City

Half a day later, Yang Kai and Li Jiao sat facing each other in a teahouse.

Fragrant steam curled from the tea, yet both men remained silent.

Half a day of observation had been enough for them to notice many things.

This Half-Dragon City was simply too strange. Setting aside the astonishing number of Dragon Descendants, experts alone seemed to emerge without end. Of every thousand people on the streets, at least one was in the Emperor Realm.

The city's population was around five hundred thousand. In other words, as many as five or six hundred Emperor Realm cultivators lived in Half-Dragon City!

What did such a number represent? It was simply terrifying.

Looking across the entire Star Realm, there were countless cities large and small, yet none had ever possessed enough Emperor Realm cultivators to compare with Half-Dragon City. Usually, having several dozen Emperor Realm cultivators in a great city was already remarkable, and that included those merely passing through, to say nothing of a hundred or even five hundred.

Even though the Spiritual Qi here was extremely pure and aided cultivators in their cultivation and advancement, such a proportion was still far too frightening.

One had to understand that even a top Sect like Qingyang Shrine only had a little over a dozen Emperor Realm cultivators overseeing it. Compared with Half-Dragon City, that number was only a fraction.

In addition, the Dragon Descendants who possessed the Dragon Clan's bloodline seemed to enjoy generally higher status within Half-Dragon City. Among cultivators of the same level, those of Dragon Descendant origin were usually the ones everyone followed.

Yang Kai estimated that if the experts of this city were mobilized, they would be enough to sweep through any city or top Sect in the Star Realm. Only a Great Emperor Sect could contend with Half-Dragon City.

This city was a true colossus!

However, both Yang Kai and Li Jiao had discovered another rather strange matter.

Those Dragon Descendants, especially those with slightly purer bloodlines, all seemed to have unstable auras. Regardless of their cultivation, their steps were unsteady, as though they all carried hidden injuries. At the Emperor Realm level, the higher the cultivation, the fewer Dragon Descendants there were.

What was going on here? Neither Yang Kai nor Li Jiao could make sense of it.

Logically speaking, possessing the Dragon Clan's bloodline gave Dragon Descendants a natural advantage in cultivation. Li Jiao was one example. If he had not been a Dragon Descendant, the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm would have been the limit of his cultivation in this lifetime. It was precisely because he possessed some of the Dragon Clan's bloodline that he had been able to break through to the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm.

Yet in Half-Dragon City, the situation seemed to be reversed.

The purer a Dragon Descendant's bloodline was, the harder it was for their cultivation to rise, with the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm being their limit. Most Dragon Descendants in the Emperor Realm were only in the First Stage. By contrast, Third Stage Emperor Realm cultivators existed among the humans and Yao who possessed no Dragon Clan bloodline.

After sitting in the teahouse for a long time and listening to the conversations around them, Yang Kai and Li Jiao still failed to learn anything particularly useful. Only when one person inadvertently uttered the words "Dragon Palace" was their attention drawn.

Unfortunately, that person seemed to realize at once that he had misspoken and hurriedly changed the subject, leaving Yang Kai and Li Jiao unbearably curious.

Half-Dragon City, Dragon Palace, Dragon Descendants...

All the signs indicated that this place was very likely Dragon Isle, but the population of several hundred thousand made Yang Kai reluctant to believe it.

How could Dragon Isle have so many people?

Li Jiao was confused as well. This place differed so greatly from what he had imagined that he could not accept the reality before him.

While the two sat in silence, a sudden commotion came from a shop across the street, as though someone was arguing inside. Immediately afterward, Spiritual Qi surged and Law Power flared.

A bang rang out, followed by a cry of alarm, and a figure flew out of the shop and crashed heavily to the ground.

It was a woman dressed in a goose-yellow palace gown. Her black hair cascaded like a waterfall, and her figure was slender and graceful from behind, though her true appearance could not be seen. Her aura was not weak; she was actually a First Stage Emperor Realm cultivator.

No one knew whom she had clashed with inside the shop, but she had actually been beaten and thrown out. From the look of her, her injuries were quite severe.

Seeing this, the pedestrians on the street moved aside one after another.

Without knowing the cause or circumstances, no one was willing to meddle in another's business and invite disaster upon themselves.

"Mother!" A young girl's cry rang out, tinged with tears.

The woman lying on the ground struggled to her feet and wiped the blood from the corner of her mouth. She stared ahead with fire in her eyes, her delicate body trembling, though whether from anger or the beating was unclear.

“Hmph!” As the cold snort rang out, a man stepped out from the shop. His expression was dark and sinister, and he walked at an unhurried pace. One hand was raised, clutching a young girl by the hair and dragging her along behind him. Forced into such a position, she could only follow with her body bent nearly double.

The girl had delicate features and appeared quite young. Her body had not yet fully developed, with only two small swells on her chest, and she was exceedingly petite. Judging by her appearance, she was at most fifteen or sixteen.

Her eyes were red, and tears streamed down her cheeks. She seemed to have suffered a terrible fright, her face filled with fear and unease as she looked ahead and cried, "Mother, save me!"

From the way she addressed her, this young girl and the woman who had been beaten out of the shop were clearly mother and daughter.

“Let her go!” the woman growled through gritted teeth. Emperor Yuan surged violently around her as though she were about to charge forward and fight to the death. But with her daughter in the man’s hands, she was forced to hold back. No matter how furious she was, she did not dare act rashly.

Moreover, the sinister man was not only one Stage above her, but also a Dragon Descendant. Even if she truly fought with her life on the line, she would definitely be no match for him.

"Let her go?" The sinister man looked at the woman and sneered. "You say let her go, so this Lord should let her go? Who do you think you are?"

The disdain in his words was plain to see. Clearly, he did not take this woman seriously at all.

As he spoke, he even lowered his head and gently inhaled the scent of the girl's hair. A look of rapturous delight immediately spread across his face as he said, “What a wonderful fragrance. This Lord has always been fond of little girls like this.”

The girl had never experienced anything like this before. Feeling the hot breath against her back, her body immediately stiffened and she cried even harder, calling softly, "Mother..."

"You dare!" The woman flew into a rage at the sight and shouted through gritted teeth, “Do you even know whose daughter she is? And yet you dare to dishonor her like this!”

The sinister man sneered. "Of course I know whose daughter she is!"

The woman froze, clearly not expecting such an answer. She had thought he was merely relying on his power to bully others and that revealing her daughter's father's identity would make him hesitate, allowing mother and daughter to escape danger. Who could have expected it to have no effect at all?

Her heart sank as she realized the situation was dire. He had specifically targeted her, and he must have investigated everything beforehand. That identity might not be of any use after all.

The sinister man said, "She's only a mortal. Do you think those people over there would care? If they truly cared, they would not have driven you out."

At these words, not only did the woman's expression dim, but many among the surrounding crowd also looked dejected, as though they had suffered experiences similar to hers.

“Do you think anyone would dare interfere if this Lord stripped her naked right here and now?” As he spoke, the sinister man reached out and placed a hand on the young girl’s shoulder.

The girl's body abruptly stiffened, and tears streamed endlessly down her face like pearls from a broken string.

The woman opposite him paled in horror and cried out, "Don't!"

If he truly stripped her daughter naked, how could the girl face anyone afterward? She might even kill herself on the spot. Her daughter was her only hope in this life, so how could she bear to see such a thing happen?

The sinister man's words made many people in the crowd frown, feeling that he had gone too far. Someone immediately spoke up, "Friend, even killing someone only means taking their head. Whatever they may have done to offend you, there is no need to humiliate them like this, is there?"

The moment this man finished speaking, a companion beside him paled and hurriedly tugged at him before whispering something in his ear.

The speaker's expression changed, turning somewhat ashen.

The sinister man turned to look at him and sneered. "What did you just say? This Lord didn't hear you clearly. Say it again!"

The man's expression shifted several times. He cupped his fists lightly and said in a low voice, "Please forgive me, Sir. This one spoke carelessly just now and caused offense. I ask that Sir not take it to heart."

The sinister man stared at him coldly for a long moment before growling, "Scram!"

Hearing this, the man felt as though he had received a great pardon and hurriedly turned to leave. Within his capabilities, he was willing to stand up for justice, but if doing so would endanger his own life, he did not have the courage.

Seeing this, how could the others fail to realize that this Second Stage Emperor Realm Dragon Descendant had an impressive background? Perhaps he even had some powerful backer. Otherwise, how could he command such fear?

Some people recognized the sinister man and naturally knew where his confidence came from. They shook their heads and sighed, feeling that this mother and daughter were likely doomed.

Of all the people they could have offended, why did it have to be this fellow? Their days ahead would be difficult.

After flaunting his might, the sinister man finally turned back to the girl's mother and asked smugly, "Do you know what you should do now?"

How could the woman not know what he intended? Her fists were clenched so tightly that her fingernails dug into her flesh, yet she felt no pain. She looked up at her daughter, revealing a sorrowful, desolate smile, then slowly dropped to her knees and prostrated herself, her forehead resting upon the backs of her hands. Swallowing her humiliation, she said, “I… am willing to serve you, my Lord. I only beg that you spare my daughter.”

The sinister man's lips curled into a mocking sneer.

The woman's humble posture greatly satisfied him. His gaze lingered for a while on her pert, rounded buttocks before he slowly walked forward.

Without warning, he kicked the woman and sent her sprawling.

The kick was not light. The woman rolled several times before finally coming to a stop.

"Mother!" the girl cried out in grief.

"Shut up!" The sinister man slapped the girl across the face. With a crisp smack, one side of her face immediately swelled.

Perhaps she had been frightened senseless, for the girl truly did not dare make another sound, her limpid eyes widening.

The sinister man had kicked the woman once, but he was clearly not finished. He walked forward and planted a foot on her chest, immediately pressing her full bosom out of shape.

The woman's expression twisted with pain, yet she said to the girl, "Close your eyes!"

She did not want her daughter to see her like this.
