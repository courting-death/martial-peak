## Chapter 3042 — Killing to Silence Witnesses

As the group spoke, a wave of Dragon Pressure came from the far side of the island. Zhu Lie had clearly been right; Fu Chi really was coming.

Sensing this familiar aura, Madam Hua grew even more fearless, her expression venomous.

"Scram!" Zhu Lie suddenly shouted in a low voice, raising his hand and striking toward Yang Kai with his palm.

Seeing this, Yang Kai tapped one foot against the ground and drifted aside.

Without a sound, Madam Hua, who had been plotting how to torment Yang Kai, Lu Sanniang, and her daughter, suddenly spat out a mouthful of blood. Every bone in her body shattered, and heart-rending pain surged through her as her vitality rapidly drained away. With great difficulty, she turned her head and stared at Zhu Lie in disbelief. "Sir, you..."

Her neck lolled to one side, and she died in an instant, her eyes bulging wide.

Even at the moment of her death, she could not understand why Zhu Lie had suddenly struck her down so ruthlessly.

Lu Sanniang clearly did not understand either. Seeing Zhu Lie kill Madam Hua with a casual gesture, she could not help rubbing her eyes hard, wondering whether they had deceived her.

Could he have struck her by accident? Surely not. How could an Eighth-Tier Dragon make such a mistake?

While she was still filled with suspicion, Zhu Lie sent one palm strike to his left and another to his right.

Two muffled groans rang out. The two women who had accompanied Madam Hua also bled from all seven orifices and died miserably on the spot.

Lu Sanniang's beautiful face immediately lost all color.

How could this possibly have been an accident? This member of the Dragon Clan had clearly killed Fu Chi's three women on purpose. What was going on? By all rights, should he not have killed Yang Kai instead? Why had he not only left Yang Kai untouched, but killed Madam Hua and the other two instead?

Lu Sanniang felt as though her mind could no longer keep up. What she had just witnessed was simply beyond comprehension.

Zhu Lie turned his head and fixed his gaze on Lu Sanniang and her daughter. The murderous intent in his eyes could not have been clearer, and he raised his palm as though preparing to strike again.

Lu Sanniang's heart sank, and her face turned pale.

A figure flashed before her as Yang Kai stepped in front of them and said softly, "These two cannot be killed!"

Zhu Lie replied, "Better safe than sorry!"

Yang Kai shook his head.

Zhu Lie said angrily, "Suit yourself. If word gets out, it will be your problem." After speaking, he turned to look at Yuan Wu nearby.

Yuan Wu had remained unconscious ever since Yang Kai struck him earlier, but now his body finally began to tremble.

He was, after all, at the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm and a Dragon Descendant besides. Yang Kai's strike had not been very powerful, so how could it possibly have knocked him unconscious? He had merely been feigning it. He had seen everything that happened afterward perfectly clearly, and each bizarre turn of events had left him badly shaken.

In particular, Zhu Lie's killing of Madam Hua and the other two women filled him with absolute horror.

He had vaguely realized that Zhu Lie seemed to be helping Yang Kai. Otherwise, Zhu Lie would not have gone so far as to kill them and silence the witnesses before Fu Chi arrived.

Since Zhu Lie intended to silence every witness, Yuan Wu was undoubtedly one of his targets as well.

The moment Zhu Lie looked his way, Yuan Wu knew the situation was dire. He weighed begging for mercy against fleeing, then turned and ran, rushing directly toward the approaching Fu Chi as he shouted, "Sir, save me!"

Although he did not understand why Zhu Lie was killing everyone to silence them, he knew Zhu Lie was unlikely to spare him. Begging for mercy would be useless. If he wanted to survive, his only option was to rely on Fu Chi. His chances were slim, but that was still better than having no hope at all.

Zhu Lie stared coldly at his retreating back and raised one hand. A fire dragon immediately lunged forward, baring its fangs and brandishing its claws with terrifying momentum.

Flames soared into the sky. Yuan Wu paled in fright, and when he looked back, he was scared out of his wits. "Spare me!" he cried.

The fire dragon had already engulfed him. This was pure Dragon Flame, something Yuan Wu had no power to dispel. He screamed as raging flames wrapped around his body, then plunged downward and crashed heavily into the ground, where he rolled and struggled without pause.

In just five breaths, Yuan Wu stopped moving. His flesh and blood had been burned away completely, leaving behind only a charred mass.

Fu Chi finally arrived.

He appeared to be a man around thirty years old, with a sturdy build and a natural air of authority. Arcs of lightning coiled around him, crackling incessantly.

His gaze swept over Zhu Lie and darkened. He clearly did not care for him, and it seemed there was some other grievance between the two.

He glanced at Yang Kai next, but his gaze did not linger. A mere human was not worth taking seriously.

Lu Sanniang's presence, however, made him frown.

Naturally, he could not have forgotten Lu Sanniang. He was the one who had captured her from the Northern Territory and brought her to Dragon Isle, and he had also personally driven her away. Only a dozen or so years had passed; his memory was not that poor.

Lu Sanniang did not dare look at him. She simply shielded Lu Yuqin behind her, her delicate body trembling and her face as pale as paper.

Fortunately, Fu Chi did not care much about her either. His gaze rested on her for only a moment before moving on.

The next instant, Fu Chi flew into a rage and growled through gritted teeth, "Zhu Lie, what have you done?"

He had seen the corpse of his most favored woman sprawled across the beach, her beautiful eyes wide open in death and her mouth stained with blood. A faint trace of Dragon Yuan still lingered around her corpse, making it obvious that she had died at the hands of a Dragon.

Zhu Lie was the only member of the Dragon Clan present, so Fu Chi did not even need to ask to know that he was the killer.

Fu Chi was instantly enraged.

This was his territory, and she was his exclusive possession. She had died at Zhu Lie's hands for no apparent reason, something Fu Chi naturally could not tolerate.

Fu Chi seethed with fury.

Zhu Lie said indifferently, "That blind fool could not recognize her betters. I simply disposed of her for you."

Fu Chi's face turned livid. "What do you mean?"

He did not know what had happened here. Although this place was not particularly far from the other side of the island, he did not have the time to constantly monitor every disturbance here. He had only come specifically to investigate after sensing Zhu Lie's aura, never expecting to encounter such a scene upon arrival.

"A mere ant dared talk back to me. What do you think I mean?" Zhu Lie coldly snorted.

"She talked back to you?" Fu Chi was somewhat surprised, his doubts mounting. Madam Hua had lived on Dragon Isle for quite some time, so how could she have rashly offended a member of the Dragon Clan? Moreover, he remembered that Madam Hua had met Zhu Lie before.

There were not many members of the Dragon Clan, and high-ranking Dragons were rarer still. Since she had met him, she would certainly have remembered him. Fu Chi did not believe Madam Hua could have been so blind as to offend Zhu Lie. Would that not have been seeking death?

What the hell was going on?

"What? Are you reluctant to part with her?" A mocking smile appeared on Zhu Lie's face.

Fu Chi stared deeply at him, his thoughts unreadable. After a long while, he said, "They were only a few playthings. If you killed them, then so be it. Why would I be reluctant to part with them?"

He truly felt no reluctance. What he cared about was not whether Madam Hua lived or died, but his own dignity.

Zhu Lie had come to his territory and killed his people, which clearly showed no respect for him. However, this was a sensitive time, and Fu Chi did not want to become further entangled with Zhu Lie and risk a genuine conflict. Giving himself a way out, he said, "Since she offended you, she deserved to die."

"At least you know what's good for you," Zhu Lie coldly snorted.

Fu Chi asked indifferently, "Why did you come here today?"

Zhu Lie replied, "I came to see how construction of the palace was progressing."

"You came only for that?" Fu Chi frowned.

"This is no small matter!" Zhu Lie's expression darkened, and his tone grew considerably heavier.

Fu Chi nodded. "Indeed, it is important. Since you are already here, let us go take a look together. I do not know much about the situation at the palace either. I only took a cursory look earlier, but the progress appears satisfactory. These ants possess some skill after all."

Zhu Lie wore an indifferent expression as he stepped into the air and flew toward the palace, disappearing from sight in the blink of an eye.

Fu Chi's expression turned cold. Zhu Lie had repeatedly shown him no respect since arriving today. Even though Fu Chi had deliberately tried to ease their relationship, Zhu Lie remained so contemptuous of him, leaving Fu Chi deeply displeased.

However, Zhu Lie was no weaker than him in either strength or bloodline, so Fu Chi could not easily make an issue of it. Besides, he would not need to tolerate Zhu Lie for much longer. He only needed to wait one month; after that, he would no longer have to endure Zhu Lie's attitude.

His figure flickered, and he likewise vanished from the spot.

Only the corpses strewn across the ground and Yang Kai's group remained.

From beginning to end, Fu Chi had not given Lu Sanniang another glance, much less paid any attention to Lu Yuqin's existence. To him, the mother and daughter might as well have been empty air, utterly unworthy of notice.

Lu Sanniang's expression dimmed, not because of herself, but because of Lu Yuqin.

Her daughter carried half of Fu Chi's bloodline, after all, yet he was utterly cold-hearted. The thought left Lu Sanniang with a bitter ache, though fortunately, Lu Yuqin did not understand the situation.

However, she was more concerned about something else now. Looking at Yang Kai with lingering fear, she asked softly, "Brother Yang, that lord..."

Yang Kai said lightly, “Keep what happened today buried in your heart.”

Lu Sanniang's expression turned solemn, and she nodded repeatedly. "I understand. Rest assured, neither my daughter nor I will ever breathe a word of today's events."

Yang Kai nodded, then turned to look toward the palace.

Had Zhu Lie not stepped in today, matters truly would have been difficult to bring to a peaceful end, especially after Fu Chi arrived.

Zhu Lie had clearly understood this as well, which was why he struck first to seize the initiative. Not only had he killed Madam Hua and the other two women, but he had not even spared Yuan Wu, who had been pretending to be unconscious nearby. Had Yang Kai not stopped him, Lu Sanniang and her daughter would probably have been killed to silence them as well.

Yang Kai was not overly worried, however. If matters truly reached the point where reconciliation was impossible, at worst he would simply fight Fu Chi. Yang Kai genuinely did not regard an Eighth-Tier Thunder Dragon as a threat. It was because he possessed such confidence that he had no fear.

This outcome was fairly good. The only thing Yang Kai could not understand was why Fu Chi seemed willing to give ground to Zhu Lie.

Logically speaking, they both possessed Eighth-Tier Dragon Bloodlines, so neither should fear the other. Yet Fu Chi's attitude today was clearly intriguing.

Unable to understand it, Yang Kai could not be bothered to dwell on it.

Right now, he cared only about Zhu Qing's whereabouts.

It had not been easy to encounter Zhu Lie, so Yang Kai naturally could not let this opportunity pass. He had to learn Zhu Qing's exact location from him. However, Zhu Lie was currently together with Fu Chi, making it inconvenient to ask anything.

Yang Kai could only wait quietly.

He believed Zhu Lie knew why he had come to Dragon Isle. Since Zhu Lie had deliberately helped conceal Yang Kai's whereabouts and identity, he presumably had a plan of his own.

An incense stick later, a streak of red light rose from the palace and sped off in a certain direction.

Whether by accident or design, the red light swept directly over Yang Kai's head before vanishing into the depths of the sea in an instant.

On the island, Fu Chi stared after Zhu Lie's departing figure and let out a soft sneer before turning and leaving as well.
