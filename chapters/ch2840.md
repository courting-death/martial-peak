## Chapter 2840 — An Unreliable Youngster

The old village chief Shaman Li had taken great care of Yang Kai and personally witnessed his meteoric rise and dazzling brilliance. He also knew that a small place like Cangnan Village could no longer contain someone like Yang Kai, a Great Shaman with boundless prospects.

So, during the previous winter, he had written a letter and entrusted it to an eagle to deliver to the Royal City, requesting permission for Yang Kai to go there and further his studies.

That letter, at the time, was addressed to Shaman Dang. The two had once shared a master-disciple relationship. Though they hadn’t been in contact for a long time, the old village chief felt that, given the talent and aptitude Yang Kai had shown, Shaman Dang would not refuse.

If the Bone-Eater Tribe’s invasion hadn’t happened, then after winter, Yang Kai would have gone to the Royal City as arranged by the village chief. Unfortunately, when the Bone-Eater Tribe incident occurred, the village chief died on the way, not even having time to leave any last words.

In fact, Shaman Dang was also quite interested in the person the village chief mentioned in his letter. But the invasion of the demons left him overwhelmed, unable to spare any attention elsewhere. It wasn’t until this time, seeing Yang Kai in the great hall, that he vaguely felt this youth resembled the one Shaman Li had described in his letter. So he asked, and sure enough, that seemingly weak shaman was indeed Shaman Niu from Cangnan Village.

He’s actually become a Great Shaman Master!

Shaman Li had mentioned in his letter that Shaman Niu was only a Lower-Level Shaman Warrior, yet after just one winter, he had already become a Great Shaman Master.

Was it truly a miracle bestowed by the Barbarian God? And at such a sensitive time, with the demon invasion, what did the Barbarian God intend this miracle to signify?

Suddenly, Shaman Dang recalled a saying he’d once heard.

In times of great calamity, a savior will surely appear.

……

In the Royal City, the crowds surged like tides, shoulder to shoulder, no less bustling than any city Yang Kai had ever seen.

Unlike the other Great Shaman Masters who hurried back to gather their subordinates and consolidate their forces, he wandered the Royal City alone.

Before long, he walked straight into a shop and held a private discussion with the shopkeeper in the back room for quite some time before strolling out at his leisure. The shopkeeper followed him out, beaming with joy and showing the utmost respect, as if he’d gained some great benefit.

Without pause, Yang Kai entered another shop. After a short while, he emerged again, and this shopkeeper also saw him off with great respect.

Over the course of half a day, Yang Kai went in and out of dozens of shops, and each time, the situation was much the same.

Only after half a day did Yang Kai silently tally things up, nod slightly, and then take off toward the outskirts of the city.

In no time, he flew back to the original encampment. Die stood there quietly, and when she sensed his approach, she merely glanced up and nodded lightly.

A few of his Shaman Apprentices, however, hurried over, looking at Yang Kai with anxious faces.

One of the older Shaman Apprentices said, “Sir, where did you go? Why are you only just returning?”

“I had some matters to attend to. What’s wrong?”

The old Shaman Apprentice said, “The other sirs all seem to be recruiting people. I heard that the Shaman King gave the order for all the Great Shaman Masters to gather two or three thousand subordinates. Now that you’re only just back, nearly all the strong warriors have already been recruited.”

“Yes, Shaman King did give such an order.” Yang Kai nodded.

Hearing this, the Shaman Apprentices grew even more anxious. The old one said, “Then what are you waiting for, sir? Since you’re back, you should get started right away. I see there are some good warriors from a few villages who are still hesitating about which Great Shaman Master to join. You might as well go invite them—maybe you can persuade them to come over.”

“That’s right, sir! This is something that should be done sooner rather than later. If you wait any longer, all that’ll be left are the dregs and leftovers.”

The Shaman Apprentices all urged him at once.

On the way here, they had merged with the group from Cangnan Village led by Yang Kai. Now, they had no thought of joining any other Great Shaman Master. But seeing their own leader so calm and unhurried, they couldn’t help but worry for him.

In this situation, anyone could see that only those with more and stronger warriors under their command would have an advantage in the coming war. In just half a day, over a hundred Great Shaman Masters had roamed about, constantly recruiting barbarian warriors from various villages, while their own side remained completely inactive, making them stand out as particularly odd.

The tense atmosphere filled them with a growing sense of crisis.

“No rush, no rush!” Yang Kai waved his hand, looking utterly calm and composed, leaving the Shaman Apprentices at a loss for words, so anxious they felt like their mouths were on fire.

They glanced around at the other groups and saw the various Great Shaman Masters pulling out all the stops to recruit followers—some even unleashed powerful shamanic arts on the spot to draw attention. One by one, the strongest warriors gravitated toward those Great Shaman Masters, and to the Shaman Apprentices watching, it felt as if every shred of hope was slipping away from their grasp.

But since even their own Great Shaman Master Yang Kai said there was no rush, there was nothing they could do. After all, they were just a few Shaman Apprentices, unable to make a difference in such a setting—especially since they weren’t very familiar with Yang Kai.

Sure enough, youngsters couldn’t be relied upon. The Shaman Apprentices exchanged glances, the same thought surfacing in their minds.

Time dragged on in anxious waiting. The scattered groups of Barbarians gathered around the Royal City were gradually absorbed into the ranks of one Great Shaman Master after another. News of the demon invasion spread swiftly among the people, and all the barbarians began to huddle together for safety. Some didn’t even need to be persuaded—they took the initiative to seek out and join whichever Great Shaman Master seemed the strongest.

Yang Kai, looking so young and frail, was completely ignored. He himself seemed uninterested in recruiting anyone. After returning from the Royal City, he simply sat side by side with Die, meditating and cultivating, as if all the surrounding commotion had nothing to do with him.

This left his Shaman Apprentices feeling even more helpless.

Toward evening, a voice like muffled thunder suddenly resounded across the land: “Where is Lord Shaman Niu of Cangnan Village?”

The moment those words were spoken, the noisy plain fell abruptly silent. Everyone turned to look in that direction, wondering why this person was seeking Shaman Niu of Cangnan Village. Many had heard of Cangnan Village—it was nothing more than a small settlement. How powerful could a shaman from such a place possibly be?

In a gathering filled with Great Shaman Masters, how could a shaman from a tiny village dare to be addressed as “Lord”?

But when people saw what the owner of that voice was carrying, their eyes lit up and their breathing quickened.

This man’s back was laden with shamanic weapons forged from refined steel, each one gleaming brilliantly in the setting sun, dazzling all who looked upon them.

These were true weapons—genuine implements of war. Their blades gleamed with razor-sharp edges, their hilts thick and weighty. If someone were struck by one of these, they would surely be cleaved in two. Compared to these, the stone spears and axes in the hands of barbarians seemed utterly crude and primitive. Countless Barbarian Tribe warriors felt a deep sense of inferiority well up within them.

Among the Barbarian Tribe, only the truly powerful warriors were worthy of wielding such weapons. These were not arms that just anyone could obtain—they required a hefty price, something of great value in exchange. Yet, what treasures could the warriors from the scattered villages possibly offer? For most, such weapons were but distant dreams, forever out of reach.

In the entire Southern Barbarian Tribe, only the Royal City could forge such weapons. No village had that ability.

So when a man suddenly appeared, armed to the teeth, walking among a crowd of warriors carrying stone spears and axes, the contrast made the warriors from the mountain villages look utterly rustic.

And that wasn’t all. Behind him followed a group of men also armed to the teeth, each carrying, holding, or slinging weapons. With every step, even the earth seemed to tremble and groan.

“Over here!” Yang Kai flew into the air and waved, “Come this way!”

The leading barbarian looked up, saw Yang Kai, and grinned, revealing a mouthful of white teeth. He called for his men to follow and quickly led them toward Yang Kai.

“Huh… why is it that guy?” Not far away, a Great Shaman Master built like an iron tower caught sight of Yang Kai, his eyes flashing with surprise.

So his name is Shaman Niu!

The commotion here clearly drew a lot of attention. Many Great Shaman Masters flew into the air, looking down to see what was happening with Cangnan Village.

“Shaman Niu, sir, everything you wanted is here. Please inspect them.” The leading barbarian warrior spoke as he unloaded all the weapons he was carrying and tossed them to the ground.

Each weapon was different in shape, but all shared one thing in common: they were heavy. Any one of them weighed at least a hundred catties, and this man had carried more than a dozen, walking as if it were nothing. When the weapons hit the ground, they kicked up a cloud of dust and made a dull thud.

“Good, just leave them here. Thank you, everyone.” Yang Kai nodded slightly.

Boom, boom, boom…

One weapon after another was thrown down, one barbarian warrior after another walked by. Soon, a mountain of weapons piled up on the spot.

Ah Hu and the others were dumbfounded, and the surrounding barbarian warriors watching were also stunned.

The Great Shaman Masters hovering in the air were equally dumbfounded.

The sound of swallowing echoed as every gaze fixed on the mountain of weapons, filled with shock and longing. Weapons that were once priceless and unattainable for the barbarian warriors were now piled up here.

It was like a dream!

Even the Great Shaman Masters present had never seen so many weapons gathered in one place. Each weapon was worth a fortune, and it wasn’t over yet—more people kept coming, dropping off the weapons they’d brought.

Why? Why would the shopkeepers in the Royal City bring their weapons here? What was their relationship with Shaman Niu?

What was going on?

Did they really give all these weapons to Shaman Niu? That seemed impossible, but how else could you explain this scene? Many Great Shaman Masters recognized those shopkeepers and had even dealt with them before, so they couldn’t be mistaken.
