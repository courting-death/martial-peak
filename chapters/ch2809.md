## Chapter 2809 — Ya

Even for a Shaman, he shouldn't be this frail. The youth before him looked barely grown, with skinny arms and legs, and a face as ugly as a cracked melon. Ya was confident he could kill him with a single flick of his finger.

“You can't possibly comprehend Ah Niu's strength!” Ah Hu sneered.

The villagers all looked at this barbarian warrior with mocking eyes.

They used to think Ah Niu was weak and sickly, even less robust than the thirteen- or fourteen-year-old children, but the beast tide invasion a few days ago changed their opinion of him.

That terrifying combat power and unstoppable valor were beyond their wildest imagination.

If they hadn't seen it with their own eyes, it would be hard to imagine such strength hidden in that body.

The Raging Flame Tribe's barbarian warrior snorted, unconvinced, clearly not intimidated by Ah Hu's words. He simply turned and said, “Lord Ya is waiting inside. Follow me.”

With that, he led the way ahead.

Yang Kai stepped forward to follow, and over a hundred villagers trailed behind him.

Entering the canyon, Ah Hu and the others looked around. The Raging Flame Tribe's archers still stood on both sides of the cliffs, shadowing their every move, never lowering their bows. It was a clear warning: any rash action would be met with fierce retaliation.

Soon, the group reached the deepest part of the canyon, where the firelight revealed over a hundred Raging Flame Tribe members, all looking especially fierce and terrifying.

The leading barbarian warrior approached the bonfire and whispered to a burly barbarian sitting cross-legged on the ground. Only then did the barbarian lift his eyelids slightly, glancing at Yang Kai and the villagers behind him.

This was their Shaman!

Yang Kai had seen him before through the Eagle Eye Art, so he recognized him at a glance.

Ya quietly sized up Yang Kai, his eyes flashing with undisguised surprise. Like the previous barbarian, he clearly thought Yang Kai looked excessively weak. Shamans among the barbarians didn't focus much on physical strength, spending most of their time meditating, so their bodies were naturally less robust than others.

But someone as delicate as Yang Kai, Ya had never seen before.

“How should I address you?” Ya asked seriously after observing for a while.

“Ah Niu!” Yang Kai replied with a slight smile, calm and unyielding.

“Then, Shaman Niu... may I ask what brings you here?”

Yang Kai answered, “I've come to reclaim my spoils!”

Ya frowned. “Your spoils?”

Yang Kai pointed with his hand and declared, “Those beasts you’re roasting over the fire, and the ones piled over there—those are my spoils!”

The Raging Flame Tribe members who had been mocking Yang Kai instantly exploded, standing up and glaring at him, clearly intent on beating him to death right there.

Ya frowned, then laughed. “You say these dead beasts are your spoils? What proof do you have?”

Yang Kai shrugged. “Not much, really!” He paused. “A few days ago, our village was attacked by a beast tide. The villagers barely managed to drive them back. I chased them all the way to this valley and slaughtered them to the last. Unfortunately, I was alone and couldn’t carry all this food away, so I went back to seek help. Who would’ve thought you’d arrive a step ahead of us.”

Ya's expression suddenly became quite interesting. He stared at Yang Kai in astonishment. “You just said... you chased them here alone and killed all those beasts?”

“Yes.” Yang Kai nodded, then added, “Mm, there was also a Barbaric Beast.”

“A Barbaric Beast too?” Ya's expression grew even more dramatic.

“Hahahaha!” The Raging Flame Tribe members around burst into laughter, as if they'd heard the funniest joke in the world. Ya couldn't help but laugh as well, shaking his head.

If Yang Kai had said he and the villagers defeated the beasts in the canyon, it would be believable. But he claimed to have killed over three hundred beasts and a Barbaric Beast all by himself!

No one with any sense would believe such a thing.

The barbarian warrior who led Yang Kai and the others in suddenly looked at Ah Hu with a mocking expression. “Is your Shaman an idiot?”

“What did you say? Say it again!” Ah Hu was instantly enraged.

The Shaman was the leader of the village, the chief. Any Shaman was the supreme symbol of their village. Insulting a Shaman was insulting the village and the tribe!

How could Ah Hu and the others endure that?

“Ah Hu!” Yang Kai called out, shaking his head at him.

Only then did Ah Hu grit his teeth and swallow his anger.

“Alright, I roughly understand your purpose here.” Ya smiled calmly, clearly used to dealing with arrogant young men like Yang Kai. “Since we're all tribesmen and you've come from afar, I won't make things difficult for you...” He turned to the Raging Flame Tribe warrior. “Give them ten beasts and let them go!”

The barbarian warrior, though reluctant, had no right to refuse since the Shaman had spoken. He could only obey.

“Ten beasts... Lord Ya is generous.” Yang Kai smiled, but showed no gratitude.

“Young man, don't test my patience.” Ya's expression suddenly turned stern. “You should know the Raging Flame Tribe's reputation. If this were my younger days, you'd be lying on the ground by now. So... take what you can and leave.”

Yang Kai nodded. “Ah Niu will remember Lord Ya's advice. But... I still believe Lord Ya should return all my spoils. These things don't belong to you!”

With those words, he stared intently at Ya.

Ya stood up, his tall figure casting a shadow over Yang Kai, looking down. “You're asking for trouble!”

The two Shamans suddenly became tense, and the villagers reacted as well.

The archers on both sides drew their bows, aiming at the strongest warriors. Ah Hu and the others grabbed their stone axes and spears, snorting hot air from their nostrils, their fighting spirit high.

A great battle was about to erupt.

“You will die!” Ya said coldly.

“You won't fare any better.” Yang Kai smiled, seemingly unconcerned about who would live or die.

“Are you determined to fight?”

“I said these are my spoils. You don't believe me and only want to give me ten beasts, but you know the truth. When you arrived, these beasts had already been dead for days, so they don't belong to you!”

Ya shouted angrily, “Ownerless things belong to whoever picks them up!”

“The owner is here now!”

“Proof!”

Yang Kai said, “I can't provide any.”

Ya snorted, but before he could speak, Yang Kai continued, “Then there's only one way left: under the witness of the Barbarian God, we'll resolve this dispute as Shamans do.”

Ya's eyes narrowed, staring at Yang Kai in disbelief. “You're challenging me?”

Yang Kai smiled and said, “This is the only way to settle things. Would you really rather see our tribes fight until both sides are battered and broken? With winter approaching, if our people are wounded, surviving the cold will be even harder.”

Ya thought for a while, then nodded. “You're right, but...” He grinned fiercely. “Do you think you have a chance?”

“Who knows until we fight?” Yang Kai chuckled.

“Fine, since you insist, I, Shaman Ya, accept your challenge in the name of the Barbarian God!”

With that, he shouted, “Everyone, step back!”

The Raging Flame Tribe members put away their weapons and retreated.

Yang Kai called out, “You all step back too!”

Ah Hu and the others watched Yang Kai with deep concern. Though they had just faced off against the enemy, they had heard Yang Kai’s words clearly. While none of them had ever witnessed a Shaman duel, they were certainly familiar with its dangers.

It was a sacred battle under the witness of the Barbarian God, risking life and honor. Only the victor could claim all the spoils, and the loser usually met a miserable end.

“Ah Niu...” Ah Hu's lips moved, wanting to advise Yang Kai, but he knew it was useless now. Since Shaman Ya had accepted the challenge, the fight couldn't be avoided. To retreat would be to blaspheme the Barbarian God, something no one could tolerate.

All his words condensed into one: “Be careful!”

Yang Kai nodded, giving him a reassuring look.

Ah Hu and the others were full of worry. How could they not be?

Just a few days ago, Yang Kai was still the useless Ah Niu. Though he performed astonishingly during the beast tide and became a noble Shaman, it hadn't been long. The opposing Shaman wasn't as old as the village chief, but was in his prime, and his aura was much stronger than the chief's.

Could Ah Niu win?

Unlike the anxious villagers of Cangnan Village, the Raging Flame Tribe members were relaxed, gathering in groups to watch Yang Kai, pointing and laughing, clearly mocking him for overestimating himself.

Anyone could see that Shaman Niu couldn't possibly be Lord Ya's opponent. His fate would surely be miserable.

Soon, the area was cleared. In the vast canyon depths, only Yang Kai and Ya stood face to face, ten zhang apart—the usual distance for Shaman duels.

The biting cold wind was blocked by the canyon walls, but it still sounded like a horn, and the bonfires crackled all around.

Ya smiled and said, “Shaman Niu, I am a Middle-Level Shaman Warrior. Judging by your aura, you must be just a Shaman Apprentice?”

Yang Kai replied, “Lord Ya, you have a keen eye. The village chief says I'm now an Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice!”

Ya nodded. “Your courage is admirable, and you're still young. If you work hard, you might have a bright future. But you're too arrogant!”

Yang Kai said, “Lord Ya, if you want to lecture me, save it for later. If you don't make a move, I will!”

Ya snorted, clearly unwilling to bully the weak, obviously letting Yang Kai attack first.

Since that was the case, Yang Kai didn't hesitate. He shifted his feet and charged at Ya like a whirlwind.

Ya's mocking expression froze on his face.

What the hell! Why is he charging over?
