## Chapter 2933 — Clinging to Life

Although the Soul-Devouring Worms devoured at a decent pace, it would still take some time to finish the job. Yang Kai and Tian Yan chatted for a while longer before simply sitting cross-legged to recover.

Although Yang Kai had emerged unscathed from today's battle, the final crucial moment when he had teleported so many Emperor Realm cultivators had consumed a considerable amount of his energy, significantly draining his Divine Soul power.

There was no place more suitable for restoring Divine Soul power than within the world of the Divine Roaming Mirror.

While meditating and circulating his cultivation, Yang Kai could clearly feel the world’s energy being drawn to him, pouring into his Divine Soul and replenishing what he had lost. Before long, he was in excellent condition again.

After learning of Tian Yan's impending departure, Mirror Soul seemed perpetually disheartened, abandoning even his butterfly-chasing games. He sat idly to the side, kicking at pebbles out of boredom, while Tian Yan remained attentive to the dynamics of the demonic clouds in the distance. The three of them settled into an uneasy peace, exchanging nothing but silence.

Three days passed like this before Yang Kai finally opened his eyes, no longer listless, his gaze now bright and spirited.

He glanced into the distance, then checked on the state of the Soul-Devouring Worms. Yang Kai realized it would take at least half a day more to resolve the crisis in the Divine Roaming world. Rolling his eyes, he went over to Mirror Soul and tried to strike up a conversation.

Mirror Soul acted as if he hadn’t heard a word, ignoring everything Yang Kai said and leaving him rebuffed.

Helpless, Yang Kai could only give up and return to Tian Yan’s side to ask cultivation questions.

Although Tian Yan had no physical body and cultivated only Divine Soul power, the principle of the Great Dao was that mastering one method led to understanding all. From his lofty perspective, he saw things differently, and many issues that had puzzled or confused Yang Kai were clarified by Tian Yan’s few words.

In this way, Yang Kai benefited greatly.

Half a day passed in a flash. Yang Kai finally came to his senses, looked into the distance, summoned back his Soul-Devouring Worms, and said, “Senior, I must go ahead. The Shrine has suffered a sudden upheaval. There are still matters outside that need handling. I’ll come back in a few days to discuss that matter with you in detail.”

“Very well, there’s no rush,” Tian Yan nodded slightly.

Yang Kai glanced at the Mirror Soul, who merely raised his hand, and Yang Kai suddenly felt an immense repulsive force pressing in from all sides. Caught completely off guard, he was expelled from the world.

As his vision shifted, Yang Kai found himself back in the forbidden area.

Opening his eyes, he pushed open the stone chamber’s door. Hearing the noise, Chen Qian hurried over from outside.

“Junior Brother Yang, you’re awake. How is the situation inside?” Chen Qian asked nervously.

Yang Kai replied, “It’s been handled. What about outside?”

Chen Qian said, “Let’s talk as we walk.”

Naturally, Yang Kai had no objections. The two of them walked out together, chatting as they went.

Three days ago, the elders of Qingyang Shrine had come to the forbidden area where the Divine Roaming Mirror was kept, while Chen Wenhao and Feng Ming stayed at Spirit Sword Peak to investigate. After searching, they indeed found a sealed demonic land a thousand zhang underground at Spirit Sword Peak, with clear traces of Demonic Intent lingering.

If not for Gong Yue’s actions this time, no one would have imagined such a place existed within Qingyang Shrine. Back when Wen Zishan founded the sect here, he had thoroughly investigated the surrounding ten thousand li, yet knew nothing of the hidden demonic land.

Now, most of the restrictions in the demonic land had been destroyed, likely by Gong Yue. Otherwise, the Demonic Intent couldn’t have been released.

At present, the Shrine elders were all at Spirit Sword Peak, both to check for any fish that slipped through the net and to completely seal the place. After all, it was a demonic land—even if there was no more Demonic Intent inside, no one could be sure there was no danger.

Besides that, news of the Demonic Intent’s resurgence and escape from Qingyang Shrine had already spread. Countless sects and experts in the Southern Territory were urgently tracking the escaped Demonic Intent, hoping to eradicate them before they caused more destruction.

But everyone knew this would be difficult.

Demonic Intent was formless, a special kind of energy that might not even possess its own consciousness. It could only attach to living beings, corroding their minds and driving them into a demonic state.

So tracking them would be extremely difficult. Nevertheless, this matter could not be left unattended. During the three days Yang Kai had been absent, information had spread rapidly among the various sects throughout the Southern Territory. News of the Demonic Intent's resurgence had become the talk of the realm, known to all under heaven.

Before long, the two of them arrived at Spirit Sword Peak.

The demonic land wasn’t at the summit of the Spirit Sword Peak, but at the foot of the mountain. A stone cave stood open, pitch-black and bottomless, with waves of eerie, sinister aura wafting out, as if it led straight to the underworld.

A group of Emperor Realm cultivators stood outside the cave, each staring grimly at the entrance. Wen Zishan’s expression was especially grave.

Hundreds or even thousands of strands of Demonic Intent had escaped from Qingyang Shrine. As the master of the Shrine, he naturally couldn’t shirk responsibility. Especially now, with the Mortal Great Emperor in dire straits, the smile that usually adorned his face was nowhere to be seen.

Gao Xueting and the others stood beside him. Chen Wenhao and Feng Ming hadn’t left either—they needed to know the exact situation below to report back to their respective sects.

When Yang Kai arrived, everyone silently exchanged greetings.

“How’s the situation?” Yang Kai asked quietly, standing beside You Kun.

You Kun shook his head. “The Demonic Intent has probably all escaped. We’re trying to break the remaining restrictions to see the full extent of the demonic land—maybe we’ll find some clues. It’s just…”

“Just what?”

You Kun gave a dry laugh. “The Shrine’s Formation Masters need some time.”

Yang Kai more or less understood. The demonic land was filled with ancient restrictions and formations. Even though Gong Yue had broken some, many still remained. The Shrine’s Formation Masters might not be up to the task, so progress was slow. Normally, it wouldn’t matter, but time was tight now—every day’s delay made tracking the Demonic Intent harder.

With this in mind, Yang Kai looked at Wen Zishan and said, “Hall Master, I know an Imperial-Grade Formation Master. Should I invite him to help?”

Wen Zishan’s eyes lit up. “Is he reliable?”

“He’s from Lingxiao Palace.”

“Then hurry and invite him,” Wen Zishan said urgently, then gave a wry smile. “But your Lingxiao Palace is far away in the Northern Territory…”

Yang Kai smiled. “He’ll be here right away.”

As he spoke, he vanished in a flash, leaving everyone unsure what he was up to. The fact that Lingxiao Palace had an Imperial-Grade Formation Master surprised them, but with such a distance, by the time Yang Kai brought him from the Northern Territory, it might be too late.

Yang Kai was extremely glad he had set up a cross-territory Space Array in the back mountains of the Spirit Sword Peak.

The main reason he’d set up the array was to connect Lingxiao Palace and Qingyang Shrine for his own convenience. There were similar arrays elsewhere. For various reasons, he hadn’t told anyone and had even used many of Spirit Sword Peak’s formations to hide it, so Wen Zishan and the others knew nothing about it.

He headed straight for the back mountain, preparing to return to Lingxiao Palace and bring Nanmen Dajun over.

The demonic land was filled with ancient restrictions and formations, which Nanmen Dajun had always been fond of. He surely wouldn’t refuse.

As he neared the array’s location, Yang Kai suddenly paused, frowning and looking to the side.

He sensed a faint aura that felt familiar.

As he looked over, Yang Kai’s expression changed. He mobilized his Emperor Yuan, as if facing a formidable enemy.

In the forest ahead lay a small beast sprawled on the ground, its flesh torn and mangled, its body covered in wounds. From the few patches of fur that remained, it could be vaguely seen that it had once been black. It was about a foot long, its aura faint, its belly rising high with each labored breath, only settling after a long while.

Guixu!

It was actually still alive, and had made its way to the back mountain.

Yang Kai had spent the past few days in the Divine Roaming world, so he didn’t know what had become of Guixu. He only knew that during the chaos, Feng Ming had kicked it away, and it had vanished.

Yang Kai had always thought it was dead.

According to Gong Yue, this little black dog had a trace of Guixu blood from a Demon Domain beast, which Gong Yue had activated with some unknown secret art. With this innate divine ability, it had fought seven or eight Emperor Realm cultivators to a standstill on its own.

Yang Kai figured that if its bloodline hadn’t been impure or too weak, everyone present would have been in trouble.

That ability to devour everything and even reflect attacks was truly astonishing.

Guixu hadn’t died. After escaping Gong Yue’s control, it disappeared without a trace. Chen Wenhao and the others had been busy investigating the demonic land and hadn’t paid attention to its whereabouts, allowing it to crawl to the back mountain to cling to life.

But it probably wouldn’t live much longer.

Yang Kai had only met this little black dog twice and didn’t feel much attachment, but he was interested in its strange innate ability.

Confirming that it was barely breathing and posed no threat, Yang Kai slowly walked up to it.

Guixu seemed to sense something, opening its eyes a crack to look at the figure before it, letting out two weak whimpers.

Yang Kai raised his palm, Emperor Yuan surging in his hand.

Guixu’s eyes slowly closed, as if resigned to its fate, hot breath puffing from its nostrils.

Yang Kai brought his palm down hard, but stopped three inches above Guixu, turning the strike into a grab. He picked it up and sighed, “Whether you live or die depends on your own fortune.”

With that, he tossed Guixu into the Small Xuan Realm.

No matter how powerful this Demon Domain beast’s innate ability was, as long as it was in the Small Xuan Realm, Yang Kai could suppress it. He wasn’t worried it would recover and go berserk.

Whether it survived or not was uncertain.

Moreover, Yang Kai always felt that Guixu's true nature wasn't like this. During their first two encounters, Guixu had shown him no malice whatsoever—in fact, it had seemed somewhat affectionate toward him. Especially during the second meeting, Guixu had clearly come looking for him.
