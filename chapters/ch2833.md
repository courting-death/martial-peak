## Chapter 2833 — Shaman King Descends

To be able to tear off the arm of a Great Shaman Master, who could his enemy be?

Ah Hu reacted immediately, turning his head and shouting downward, "Go call Ah Niu!"

As soon as the words left his mouth, he froze, because he realized that at some point, Ah Niu had already appeared behind him, floating in midair, his gaze sharp as he stared ahead, his expression cold and severe.

Chi, who was flying over from the distance, clearly noticed Yang Kai's presence as well. A look of delight flashed across his face, but in the next moment, his body suddenly stiffened, his eyes widened in terror, and he began to tremble violently, as if an invisible giant hand had seized him in midair, leaving him utterly immobile.

His face alternated between green and purple as he gritted his teeth and growled, "Quick... run!"

As soon as he finished speaking, his body suddenly swelled, the simple beast-hide clothes he wore splitting apart, and his entire skin became transparent, the blood vessels within clearly visible.

Crash...

When he swelled to the limit, Chi's entire body exploded, turning into a mass of blood that rained down from the sky, leaving not a single bone behind!

"Ah!"

The villagers of Cangnan Village were all shocked, completely unable to understand what had just happened. Frightened by Chi's tragic end, they all retreated several steps.

As they looked again, a burly figure had suddenly appeared where Chi had been. The man was dressed in a bold, unrestrained manner, with several skulls of various sizes hanging from his waist. Those skulls emitted a chilling aura that enveloped the entire Cangnan Village.

At this moment, the strange man who had suddenly appeared was holding something in his hand, grinning menacingly at Yang Kai as he gnawed at the food in his grasp.

"Bone-Eater Tribe!" Ah Hu called out in a low voice, recognizing the identity of the newcomer from the distinctive markings on his face.

He had long heard that the Bone-Eater Tribe was a vengeful lot. Previously, when Ah Niu and people from two other tribes wiped out a small Bone-Eater Tribe settlement, Ah Hu had worried about retaliation. He had wanted to suggest to Ah Niu that they relocate, but Ah Niu had been shut away in his house for some time, making it impossible to bring it up.

Now it seemed his worries were justified—the Bone-Eater Tribe had really come for revenge.

And this person's strength was absolutely terrifying. After all, even Chi, a Great Shaman Master, had been killed by him. Could Ah Niu possibly be his match?

The villagers nearby, hearing Ah Hu's words, all tensed up. They had already experienced the terror of the Bone-Eater Tribe firsthand. When they saw what the Bone-Eater Tribe's Great Shaman was holding, their faces turned pale.

Because it was a small leg, and judging by its appearance, it belonged to a child no older than five. The skin was tender, and with every bite, the Bone-Eater Tribe's Great Shaman tore off a large chunk of flesh, chewing with relish, his appearance growing ever more hideous.

Many villagers felt extremely uncomfortable at the sight, their stomachs churning as if they might vomit.

Yang Kai lowered his gaze slightly, glancing at the bloodstains left on the ground after Chi's death, and let out a faint sigh.

Although he didn't know exactly what Chi had gone through, he could more or less guess.

The Bone-Eater Tribe's Great Shaman had likely spared Chi on purpose, tormenting and pursuing him to track down the location of Cangnan Village. He must have used many methods along the way, preventing Chi from reaching the main settlement of the Raging Flame Tribe, leaving him no choice but to seek out reliable allies.

But no matter what, there was only one possible explanation for how this Great Shaman could toy with a Great Shaman Master like this—he was a Shaman King!

"I've smelled your scent before."

The Bone-Eater Tribe's Great Shaman suddenly spoke, his eyes burning as he stared at Yang Kai. "Over by the stone fort."

Yang Kai sneered, "You was killed by me."

The Great Shaman let out a mocking laugh. "By you?"

He clearly didn't believe Yang Kai had the ability to kill You. After the incident at the stone fort, he had investigated, using some methods to recreate the battle. Though the results were unclear, it was enough to let him track the culprit.

Gale Tribe, Raging Flame Tribe—he had visited both, destroying two small settlements and killing everyone who had been at the stone fort that day. Only the last target eluded him, as if something had obscured his investigation. In the end, he had no choice but to follow Chi, letting him lead the way to the real killer.

The plan worked perfectly. Driven to desperation, Chi had indeed come to an unfamiliar place.

When he caught the scent of that final target, the Great Shaman immediately activated the hidden technique he had left on Chi, killing him.

All that remained was to destroy this little village. The Bone-Eater Tribe’s dignity could not be defiled—anyone who dared spill the blood of their tribesmen would face vengeance a hundred, a thousand times over.

"You think I can't do it?" Yang Kai smiled and retorted, while secretly sending a message to Die, telling her to hide the villagers. Although a month of closed-door cultivation had greatly increased his strength, facing a Shaman King head-on meant he had to go all out. He couldn't spare a thought for Cangnan Village, and if the villagers didn't hide, the power unleashed would be enough to obliterate them.

The Great Shaman snorted coldly, making no comment.

"Shaman You also thought I couldn't do it, and then he died!" Yang Kai chuckled softly.

The Great Shaman looked down from above and said, "Are you trying to say that I, Shaman Chi, will meet the same end?"

Yang Kai shrugged, but his attitude made it clear he agreed.

Shaman Chi stared at Yang Kai for a while, then burst into thunderous laughter, the sound rolling like thunder, carrying a soul-shaking effect. Many villagers who were quietly evacuating were startled, blood seeping from their mouths and noses.

Only Yang Kai faced him head-on, unmoved as a mountain.

The laughter abruptly ceased. Shaman Chi frowned as he scrutinized Yang Kai, realizing that he wasn’t merely putting on a brave front—he truly appeared completely unscathed. Now, this was truly strange. “For a mere Great Shaman Master… you have some skill.”

He was a Shaman King. Even if he was only a Lower-Level Shaman King, the power hidden in his laughter was not something a Great Shaman Master could withstand. Yet the man before him stood there like an unshakable mountain, rendering his methods completely ineffective.

As he spoke, Shaman Chi tossed aside the small leg in his hand and slapped his waist. As shamanic power surged, a chorus of ghostly wails erupted. The skulls hanging from his waist emitted a chilling aura, their hollow eyes flashing with green light as they all flew out together.

"Dare to kill my Bone-Eater Tribe tribesmen? Today, I will turn you all into food!"

As Shaman Chi roared, the flying skulls grew as large as houses, scattering to block all sides of the village. Each one spewed a frigid aura from its mouth, and wherever that aura touched, the ground and wooden houses froze, all vitality lost.

In the blink of an eye, the village seemed about to become a graveyard, filled with deathly energy.

Fortunately, Yang Kai had previously instructed Die in secret, so the villagers had already gathered in the center of the village, preparing to retreat into the tunnel. As a result, there were no casualties.

This tunnel had been dug under Die's supervision after the last Bone-Eater Tribe raid. Its existence ensured that if something similar happened again, the villagers would have a safe escape route.

So when Die made the suggestion, Ah Hu and the others had spared no effort to help.

In just a month, the tunnel had been dug to a hidden spot thirty li away, providing a safe buffer zone.

However, the deathly aura was spreading rapidly, and evacuating over two hundred villagers would take time. If nothing was done, they would never escape safely.

Fortunately, as soon as the skulls began to move, Yang Kai acted as well.

With a flicker, he suddenly appeared in front of one of the giant skulls.

Seeing this, Shaman Chi couldn't help but raise an eyebrow and sneer, "Courting death!"

Those skulls were his shamanic artifacts, refined over countless years and even more troublesome to deal with than Shaman Chi himself. If Yang Kai had attacked his main body, Shaman Chi might have had to put in some effort to repel him, but this fool had chosen to go after his artifacts instead—utterly clueless.

As he spoke, Shaman Chi's mind moved, and the green light in the giant skull's eyes flashed wildly. The frigid aura it spewed instantly grew several times stronger, completely enveloping Yang Kai.

This frigid deathly aura was refined from the resentment of those he had slaughtered and devoured. Its power was strange and formidable. Even a fellow Shaman King would be haunted by evil spirits and have their mind shaken if struck by it. At that point, Shaman Chi could toy with them as he pleased, deciding exactly how they would die.

Shaman Chi could already foresee Yang Kai's fate—frozen on the spot by the frigid deathly aura, his soul then collected by the skull.

Yet, within that dense frigid aura, a burst of five-colored light suddenly erupted. The powers of the five elements cycled endlessly within the light, standing firm like a pillar in a raging flood, unyielding and even expanding outward.

Shaman Chi frowned, instinctively sensing something was off.

The other party was indeed just a Great Shaman Master, and a Lower-Level one at that. There was no reason he should be able to withstand the power of Shaman Chi's artifact, yet what he saw defied all understanding.

Surrounded by the aura of the Undying Five Elements Sword, a constant hissing sound filled the air. The frigid deathly aura, enough to trouble even a Shaman King, was blocked outside.

Yang Kai raised his sword high, his power surging. A ball of light shot skyward, as if to pierce a hole in the heavens.

In the next instant, he slashed down at the skull. The sword light fell, seeming to split rivers and seas, dividing the frigid aura in two. The massive sword light struck the skull heavily.

A piercing, bone-chilling wail erupted as the green ghostly flames in the skull’s eye sockets flickered violently. Under the force of Yang Kai’s blow, the giant skull was sent flying, tumbling through the air without pause, its momentum undiminished, as if it might soar straight out of the heavens.
