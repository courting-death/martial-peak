## Chapter 2787 — Dantian Disturbance

Three days later, the group of three returned to Lingxiao Palace.

During this time, Hou Yu tried to escape several times, but each attempt ended with Yang Kai catching her and dragging her back. After a few rounds of this, she was completely out of steam, her temper thoroughly worn down.

“I don’t believe you can watch me for a hundred years! As soon as you’re distracted, there’ll be plenty of chances to escape!” Hou Yu sneered inwardly.

Unfortunately, this clever plan was ruthlessly shattered just an incense stick after returning to Lingxiao Palace.

“Yao… Yao Kings!? Three of them?” Hou Yu stared in shock at the trio before her, each one radiating overwhelming Yao Qi and an awe-inspiring presence. Her face was filled with terror and disbelief.

She had no idea Lingxiao Palace possessed such depth.

After all, Yang Kai was only at the First Stage of the Emperor Realm. How many experts could the Lingxiao Palace he founded possibly have? She hadn’t had a chance to ask about Lingxiao Palace along the way, and now, upon arrival, she was stunned.

With three Yao Kings overseeing the place, its foundation was extraordinary—no top Sect in the Northern Territory could compare.

“This is Lingxiao Palace’s newly recruited Chief Artificer,” Yang Kai introduced Hou Yu to the three Yao Kings. “Just call her Little Hou. She’s lively and energetic, but I hope she’ll be a bit more steady and disciplined here in Lingxiao Palace, so…”

Yingfei immediately said, “Rest assured, Young Master Yang. The three of us will take turns watching her. She won’t cause any trouble.”

Xi Lei and Xie Wuwei also voiced their agreement.

Hou Yu’s face turned pale, and she involuntarily stepped back a few paces, exclaiming, “How can you do this?”

Being watched by three Yao Kings in turns—how could she live like this? If it really came to that, not only would escape be impossible, she’d have no freedom at all.

“That’s excellent!” Yang Kai nodded gently, then said to Yingfei, “Take her to see the Chief Steward, register her, and arrange a place for her to stay.”

“Yes!” Yingfei replied, gesturing to Hou Yu, “Miss Hou, please.”

“I don’t want to!” Hou Yu gritted her teeth and turned to run.

Yingfei glanced at Yang Kai, who nodded slightly.

Yingfei snorted, transformed into a stream of light, and chased after her. In the blink of an eye, he caught Hou Yu, picked her up like a chick, and flew off unhurriedly.

Xi Lei and Xie Wuwei exchanged glances and followed slowly.

From afar, Hou Yu’s loud curses and shouts echoed, eventually fading away.

Yang Kai took out the Transmission Compass and left a message for Hua Qingsi, then looked at Nanmen Dajun and said, “Settle the Mountain Wave Diagram matter as soon as possible.”

“Rest assured, Palace Master. With Little Hou’s help, the Mountain Wave Diagram won’t be a problem.”

“Also pass word to the Chief Steward—once Little Hou has finished the Mountain Wave Diagram, have a flying Secret Treasure forged for me. Make it large and of the finest quality; spare no expense.”

“Yes!”

With the Chief Artificer’s matter settled, Yang Kai was in a great mood.

Moments later, he returned to Qingyang Shrine via the Space Array.

On Spirit Sword Peak, Yang Kai had just steadied himself when he suddenly sensed something and looked to the side. In the valley, a small, black beast with a sneaky appearance was curiously peering over, its eyes darting about as if searching for something.

However, since Yang Kai had hidden the Space Array’s location using the Earth Vein power of Spirit Sword Peak, the beast saw nothing special here.

Yang Kai smiled softly, recognizing the little beast as the one he’d encountered in the wine jar earlier. It looked odd, somewhat like a puppy, and was likely a native Demonic Beast of Spirit Sword Peak.

Its strength wasn’t high; Yang Kai didn’t sense any power fluctuations from it.

As he stepped forward and appeared, the little beast was startled, crawling back a few steps, its round eyes fixed on Yang Kai, shining with a hint of human-like wariness.

But after recognizing Yang Kai, that wariness quickly vanished. Instead, it stuck out its tongue, panting and acting cute.

Amused, Yang Kai grabbed it, patted its head, and chuckled, “Lost your way? This isn’t a place you can wander freely.”

The birds and beasts on Spirit Sword Peak weren’t very high-level, but many weren’t something this little beast could withstand. Especially in this valley, there were quite a few powerful Demonic Beasts—if it encountered one, it’d likely be swallowed whole in a single bite.

Yang Kai was curious how such a black dog-like beast managed to survive in Spirit Sword Peak.

As Yang Kai stroked the little black dog, it visibly relaxed, a look of delight spreading across its face. Its eyes squinted in contentment, and it lay quietly in Yang Kai’s palm, unmoving.

Shaking his head lightly, Yang Kai’s figure flickered, and he appeared outside Spirit Sword Hall.

He bent down to put the little dog down, but unexpectedly, it clung tightly to Yang Kai, refusing to let go.

Yang Kai laughed, curled a finger, and flicked its forehead.

At that moment, a sudden change occurred. The usually obedient little dog unexpectedly bit Yang Kai’s finger without warning.

Man and beast stared at each other. Yang Kai was stunned, never expecting such a thing.

Immediately, Yang Kai frowned as a sharp pain came from his finger. At the same time, a violent force surged in his Dantian.

His expression changed, and before he could react, the little dog tucked its tail and ran off without looking back, whimpering as if greatly frightened.

Yang Kai examined his palm before his eyes, showing disbelief.

With that bite, the little dog actually broke his finger, leaving several clear tooth marks, some deep to the bone, and golden blood quickly flowed out.

Bitten through? How could that be?
Yang Kai’s physical strength was exceptional—even among those in the Emperor Realm, he was considered top-tier. Aside from a few uniquely gifted Yao, Yang Kai feared no one when it came to the toughness of his body. Not to mention a seemingly insignificant little beast—even if an Emperor Realm cultivator took a fierce bite at him, without using any power, it would be impossible to break his skin or flesh.

Yet the little dog managed it with a casual bite, not even using much force.

Could those little teeth really be so sharp? Yang Kai frowned, feeling that the little dog was quite extraordinary.

What shocked Yang Kai most was that after the little dog bit his finger, the ancient Demonic Qi sealed in his Dantian trembled. That Demonic Qi had always been firmly sealed by the gold and silver power of the Firmament Tree, so stable that Yang Kai never felt anything unusual.

Today, the seal was unexpectedly triggered—and all because of a bite from a little black dog.

What was going on?

Yang Kai carefully examined the seal in his Dantian, confirming there were no hidden dangers. The earlier surge seemed to be nothing more than a sudden anomaly, but such a coincidence made him inevitably link it to the little black dog.

He swept out his Divine Sense and soon found the little dog in a nearby cave, hiding more than ten zhang underground, already fast asleep, trembling in its dreams as if still frightened.

Having bitten someone, it acted as if it was the one hurt. Yang Kai was speechless. He wanted to catch it and investigate, but seeing it like this, he let it be.

“What’s going on!”

A cold shout came. Yang Kai looked up to see Gao Xueting suddenly appear on Spirit Sword Peak, her beautiful eyes scanning around as if searching for something. She asked, “What happened here just now?”

Yang Kai’s heart skipped a beat, and he replied blankly, “What do you mean?”

Gao Xueting said, “I sensed a familiar power fluctuation—a very unpleasant one. Was there anything unusual here?”

“Nothing unusual,” Yang Kai denied. “I was just testing a newly mastered divine ability.”

He had to admit, Gao Xueting’s senses were extremely sharp. The disturbance in his Dantian’s Demonic Qi lasted only a moment, yet she noticed it.

Back when Yang Kai was first invaded by Demonic Qi and entered the Demonic State, he fought a three-on-one battle against Gao Xueting, Chen Wenhao of Tianwu Sacred Land, and Feng Ming of Wuhua Hall. At that time, Yang Kai was only at the Dao Origin Realm, facing three Great Emperors, and the pressure was immense. He only managed to escape by using his Demonic State trump card.

Even now, Yang Kai dared not confess this matter to Gao Xueting. If she ever learned he was the Demonic cultivator from back then, who knew whether she’d draw her sword and strike him without hesitation.

“Is that so?” Gao Xueting frowned, clearly still suspicious.

If it had just been Yang Kai testing a divine ability, she wouldn’t have felt any discomfort. But the power fluctuation from a moment ago instinctively made her uneasy—it was a force she found unpleasant. Yet the disturbance was so brief that, before she could sense it clearly, it vanished, leaving her unable to recall where she’d encountered it before.

“Of course.” Yang Kai broke out in a cold sweat, but kept a straight face and changed the subject, “By the way, Senior Sister, Purple Bamboo Peak is far from here. How did you get here so quickly?”

Gao Xueting replied, “Just passing by. I just came from the Hall Master.” She paused, then added, “By the way, where have you been these past few days? I couldn’t find you the other day.”

I was in the Northern Territory the other day, so of course you couldn’t find me.

Yang Kai replied, “I was just in seclusion for a bit.”

Gao Xueting looked at him suspiciously. Yang Kai’s evasive answer made her doubtful, but since he didn’t want to say more, she didn’t press, only nodded, “Suit yourself. I’ll be going now.”

“Senior Sister!” Yang Kai called out to her.

“What is it?” Gao Xueting stopped and asked.

Yang Kai said, “You seem worried and hurried. Are you facing some difficulty? Maybe your Junior Brother can help.”

Gao Xueting replied calmly, “This matter concerns the Shrine’s secrets. As a Guest Elder, you’re not qualified to know too much. However, if you’re willing…”

“Don’t say it, Senior Sister.” Yang Kai quickly interrupted. Becoming a Guest Elder of the Shrine was already forced; there’s no way he’d remove that ‘guest’ part. After all, he was also a Palace Master.

“By the way, Junior Brother is about to head out for a trip!”

“Go if you want. No one’s stopping you.” Gao Xueting’s voice faded as she disappeared.
