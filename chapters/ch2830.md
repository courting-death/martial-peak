## Chapter 2830 — Crushing

Although You was indeed a Middle-Level Great Shaman Master, only one grade above himself and Zhu, this fellow’s strength was far higher than he had estimated. Even with the two of them joining forces, they couldn’t gain the slightest advantage against him.

His shamanic artifact was a gourd, from which he could pour out a sea of blood. That blood sea possessed extremely strong corrosive power. When he and Zhu fell into it, they could only exert eighty percent of their strength.

If not for this, the situation just now wouldn’t have been so difficult.

You clearly intended to defeat them one by one, using the blood sea’s power to entangle and restrain Zhu, then ruthlessly attack him. In just a short exchange, he was already beaten so badly he couldn’t fight back. If You hadn’t retreated on his own, he would have been either dead or severely injured.

Why did he retreat? Chi didn’t know the reason, nor whether it was part of You’s tactics, so he could only move back to Zhu’s side, watching warily.

The next moment, he understood why You had retreated.

Because a shocking, extremely sharp aura suddenly pierced up from beneath the blood sea. Even though it wasn’t aimed at him, Chi’s Divine Soul was shaken, his mind unsettled.

Facing this aura head-on, You probably wasn’t faring any better.

Indeed, the always calm and composed You now seemed to have encountered a nemesis, his expression tense, chanting incantations nonstop. The blood sea surged into waves, gathering at a point and forming a vortex, from which terrifying power capable of shattering mountains and splitting seas emanated.

Chi gasped, his eyes full of shock.

It wasn’t because of You’s strength—he was just a Great Shaman Master. Even if a Middle-Level Great Shaman Master was one grade above him, how strong could he really be? You’s power was built upon that blood gourd artifact.

This was definitely not an artifact a Great Shaman Master could easily obtain!

It must have been bestowed by a Shaman King or Shaman Saint. Thinking of this, Chi’s heart sank.

He had no time to think further, as the sharp aura collided with the blood sea, instantly producing a sizzling sound. Sword energy appeared out of nowhere, weaving through the blood sea. Wherever the sword energy passed, blood mist boiled up, much of it destroyed.

Chi’s eyes lit up. Though he didn’t know where this powerful reinforcement had come from, he knew it was the perfect moment to counterattack. He glanced at Zhu, their eyes meeting, everything understood without words.

Each began chanting incantations, their shamanic artifacts shining, launching attacks at You.

The blood sea surged, waves gathered into a wall before You, causing the two Great Shaman Masters’ attacks to fail. Not only that, their artifacts were tainted by the blood sea, losing much of their spiritual power, making them cry out in distress.

Still, their actions managed to distract You a bit, making his power against the sharp aura somewhat unstable.

The blood-colored vortex suddenly became chaotic, then collapsed with a splash. From within, a sword light burst forth, man and sword as one, slashing toward You.

“Roar!” You remained unflustered even in the face of danger. With a low growl, he raised both hands, summoning countless blood arrows out of thin air. In an instant, they transformed into a torrential rain of arrows, shooting straight toward the sword light.

Sss sss sss sss…

The overwhelming rain of arrows hindered the sword light’s advance. Tainted by filthy blood light, the sword light suddenly dimmed, revealing a figure within.

With a cold snort, Yang Kai landed in the blood sea, curiously surveying his surroundings.

“It’s you!” Chi stared at Yang Kai in shock, unable to believe that this sudden powerful reinforcement was the Shaman Master from the Southern Barbarian Tribe.

Was this really a Shaman Master? How could he possess such formidable power?

Even Zhu’s eyes trembled, clearly unable to accept what she was seeing.

“Shaman Master?” You frowned, having thought the newcomer was at least an Upper-Level Great Shaman Master. But upon seeing clearly, he realized the other was just a Shaman Master. Soon, You understood, his gaze lingering on the Myriad Sword for a moment.

He saw it—the reason this Shaman Master could exert power far beyond his own was that sword. It was definitely an artifact that even a Shaman King or Shaman Saint couldn’t ignore.

Realizing this, You couldn’t help but sneer. His blood sea’s greatest strength was corroding shamanic artifacts. No matter how powerful, once tainted by a bit of blood, their spiritual power would be greatly diminished.

Relying on an artifact to fight him was the most unwise choice.

As long as he could corrode the sword’s spirit, then within this blood sea, he would be unstoppable. Whether his enemies were two or three, it made no difference—he cared not at all.

But the next moment, You’s whole body trembled, staring ahead in horror.

Because Yang Kai merely gave a slight shake, and all the blood on the Myriad Sword vanished, revealing its bright, broad blade once more, without a trace of corrosion.

How could this be?

In his moment of distraction, Yang Kai transformed into a bolt of lightning, charging at him again.

“Careful!” Chi cried out in alarm. Although Yang Kai’s dazzling strike had shocked him, this guy was still just a Shaman Master. Charging recklessly at the enemy like this could only end badly.

You burst out laughing, looking at Yang Kai as if at a dead man. With a wave, the blood sea parted, a tsunami of blood crashing down on Yang Kai, instantly making him disappear.

Chi’s mouth hung open, his face full of regret, cursing the Southern Barbarian Tribe’s Shaman Master for being more trouble than help. You’s blood sea was already hard to deal with; now, after corroding him and absorbing his power, You would only grow stronger. He and Zhu had no hope of victory.

Immediate retreat was the only chance for survival. Otherwise, once You recovered, neither he nor Zhu would escape. As for his tribesmen, they’d flee if they could—he couldn’t worry about them now.

The thought flashed through Chi’s mind, but he didn’t act, because he saw something off in You’s expression.

Logically, You should be happy after eliminating a strong enemy, but his expression showed no joy, only shock.

Was something else happening?

Before Chi could finish the thought, a figure burst forth from the blood sea, soaring through the air. His beast hide garments had been completely corroded away, revealing a body that, by Barbarian Tribe standards, appeared frail—yet that seemingly fragile form was utterly unscathed. Moving with the swiftness of the wind, he closed the distance in a flash, appearing right before You. The massive, broad sword in his hands was raised high, then came crashing down with ruthless force.

Under that single sword strike, You’s soul nearly scattered. The attack looked simple, yet no matter how he tried, he found himself utterly unable to dodge—it was as if he’d been locked in place, fated to be struck.

In desperation, he spat out a mouthful of Essence-Blood, forcibly twisting his body.

Sss…

With a soft sound, You’s left arm was severed, falling into the blood sea and instantly dissolving into pus, making the blood sea churn even more violently.

“You…” You staggered back, staring at Yang Kai in horror, clutching his severed arm, his mind reeling.

He had no idea how the other had survived under the blood sea’s shroud, or how he’d managed to cut off his arm.

As he spoke, a cold face suddenly appeared before him, almost nose-to-nose.

You was terrified, hurriedly retreating. As he moved, a blood-colored vortex appeared beneath his feet, pulling him down, as if to hide his figure.

At that moment, the other’s right eye suddenly turned gold, a vertical pupil appearing, radiating a mysterious power that seemed to tug at his Divine Soul, unsettling his mind.

That moment’s hesitation slowed You’s escape.

Yang Kai’s Myriad Sword slashed down again, severing You’s right arm.

The intense pain snapped You back to his senses. He screamed miserably, no longer daring to hesitate, vanishing into the blood sea.

Yang Kai shook his head, seeming a bit frustrated.

Suppressed by the laws of this ancient era and the secret realm, he couldn’t exert his full strength. Using the World-Ending Demon Eye was exhausting, but with both arms severed, You was gravely injured and likely wouldn’t dare show himself again.

In the void, the blood sea churned. Chi and Zhu stood dumbfounded, staring at Yang Kai, their minds in chaos.

What they’d just seen…was it real? Both doubted their own eyesight.

A single Shaman Master, with just two swings of his sword, had actually severed both of You’s arms? Yet looking at You, it was as if he hadn’t even tried to dodge—he’d just stood there, foolishly allowing himself to be wounded.

As Great Shaman Masters, neither could understand this battle.

Still, the situation wasn’t optimistic. Though You was badly wounded, he’d managed to escape into the blood sea, where he was truly at home. No one could guarantee he wouldn’t suddenly attack again.

Just as they thought this, Yang Kai suddenly turned to look at them, his cold gaze making Chi and Zhu shiver.

The next moment, a terrifying scene unfolded. Shaman Niu raised his hand, unleashing a pitch-black attack like a Moon Blade, carrying a deadly aura straight at them.

Neither doubted that if they took such a blow, they’d die on the spot.

“What are you doing?” Zhu shouted angrily, thinking Shaman Niu had lost his mind in the blood sea and was attacking his own people.

As she spoke, she and Chi joined forces to cast a Shamanic Art shield before them.

But the pitch-black Moon Blade sliced through the Shamanic Art shield like bamboo, striking straight at them.

The two Great Shaman Masters felt icy cold, a deadly aura enveloping them, their minds blank at the brink of death.

The next moment, a miserable scream sounded behind them.

It was You’s voice!

Chi and Zhu were shocked, not even thinking about why they weren’t dead or injured. Turning quickly, they saw You’s face full of resentment and terror, his right leg somehow severed, fleeing toward the blood sea and vanishing instantly.

“His target was you two,” Yang Kai’s voice came. “Be careful!”

Chi and Zhu looked ashamed upon hearing this.

When had You appeared behind them? They hadn’t noticed at all. If Yang Kai hadn’t acted first, one of them would surely have met a tragic end.
