## Chapter 3028 — I've Always Been This Bold

He had only made the suggestion offhandedly, mainly because he truly did not want to cultivate Dragon Blood Flowers. This was not his first time doing so, and every time he finished cultivating them, he would remain weak for six months to a year. The experience was simply too painful to endure.

To his surprise, Fu Qi considered it for a moment before actually nodding in agreement. "Very well. These people will be left in the care of a mongrel like you. If anything goes wrong, you alone will answer for it!"

Yuan Wu stared blankly for a moment before becoming overjoyed. "This lowly one will not disappoint Sir's expectations."

"Let's go, let's go." Fu Ling was already urging them on with extreme impatience. The two hundred Dragon Descendants had no choice but to follow her toward the Spirit Island where the Dragon Blood Flowers were cultivated. As they departed, many of them cast envious looks at Yuan Wu, filling him with smug satisfaction.

Suddenly, Yuan Wu's eyes flashed, and he fixed Li Jiao with a venomous stare.

Li Jiao happened to look back at that moment. When their eyes met, the corner of Yuan Wu's mouth curled into a savage grin.

Back in Half-Dragon City, he had suffered greatly at Li Jiao's hands and been thoroughly humiliated before so many people, causing him to lose all face. Now, however, the tables had turned. Li Jiao was being taken away to cultivate Dragon Blood Flowers, while Yuan Wu had landed the favorable assignment of overseeing the five hundred mortals. The contrast filled his heart with immense satisfaction.

So what if your bloodline is superior? So what if your cultivation is stronger? In the end, you are still being taken away to have your blood drained.

That mother and daughter had finally fallen into his hands as well. He was already considering how to torment Lu Sanniang and her daughter to vent the resentment in his heart.

A mother and daughter, both as lovely as flowers. It was not as though he had never toyed with such a pair before, but he truly had never encountered a mother and daughter as beautiful as these two. This time, he had to find an opportunity to thoroughly vent his pent-up resentment.

He looked at Lu Sanniang and her daughter, then cast Li Jiao a provocative glance and chuckled viciously. His meaning could not have been more obvious.

Unexpectedly, Li Jiao remained completely unmoved. He merely gave Yuan Wu a cold glance, and there was even a hint of mockery and pity in his eyes.

What did that mean? Yuan Wu's face immediately darkened. He pondered it for a long while but could not understand what Li Jiao meant.

Li Jiao had stood up for Lu Sanniang and her daughter earlier, so they were clearly on good terms. Why, then, was he unmoved by Yuan Wu's provocation? Yuan Wu frowned deeply, his head full of doubts.

Naturally, Li Jiao was not worried about Lu Sanniang and her daughter. With Yang Kai beside them, all would be well if Yuan Wu left them alone, but if he dared provoke them, his fate would likely be miserable.

He believed Yang Kai would not ignore Lu Sanniang and her daughter.

The two hundred Dragon Descendants gradually receded into the distance and soon disappeared from sight.

Fu Qi turned and sped off in another direction without saying a word.

Yuan Wu had no time to dwell on Li Jiao's unusual reaction. He hurriedly shouted in a low voice, "Keep up, keep up! Do not delay Sir Fu Qi's business!"

He was accustomed to flaunting borrowed authority, so his tone was exceedingly rude.

No one dared complain. The five hundred people hurried forward, and when Lu Sanniang and her daughter flew past, Yuan Wu sneered and immediately moved closer to them.

Lu Sanniang trembled, and her face paled slightly.

With Li Jiao gone, she had suddenly lost her support. Yuan Wu had some influence with the Dragon Clan, so if he truly tried to bully her and her daughter, she would be completely powerless to resist. She secretly resolved that even if she had to fight to the death, she could not allow her daughter to fall into Yuan Wu's hands.

Although her daughter was the product of that Dragon Clan member venting his lust, she was still the flesh and blood Lu Sanniang had carried for ten months and given birth to. She had only managed to persevere all these years because Lu Yuqin gave her something to live for. Otherwise, she would have died long ago, so how could she have survived until today?

Holding Lu Yuqin in her arms, Lu Sanniang shifted slightly to the side, widening the distance between herself and Yuan Wu.

How could Yuan Wu let such an opportunity pass? He moved directly closer to her, clearly intending to take advantage of the situation.

Lu Sanniang's expression immediately became unnatural. She lowered her head, her pretty face deathly pale.

Yuan Wu glanced ahead and saw Fu Qi flying at the front, paying little attention to those behind him. Emboldened, he reached out to grab Lu Sanniang's shapely buttocks.

He had coveted Lu Sanniang for a long time. A beauty like her was rare even in Half-Dragon City, and on top of that, she had been expelled from the Dragon Palace after once sharing an extremely intimate relationship with a member of the Dragon Clan. This filled Yuan Wu with a perverse sense of anticipation. Now that he finally had the chance to satisfy his wandering hands, he naturally would not let it slip away.

To his surprise, however, his hand grasped nothing but air.

Yuan Wu's face sank. Turning to the side, he discovered that Lu Sanniang, who had been right beside him, had somehow moved to the other side, while a young man dressed in a close-fitting cyan outfit now stood between him and the mother-daughter pair.

He had been so intent on taking advantage of Lu Sanniang that he had not noticed when this young man arrived.

When he looked up, he saw the young man staring at him indifferently.

Had this fellow done it deliberately? Yuan Wu immediately became furious.

Because he could exchange a few words with the Dragon Clan, he held a high position in Half-Dragon City. Whether Human, Yao, or a powerful Dragon Descendant, everyone would show him some respect.

The beating Li Jiao had given him in the city had already filled him with rage, yet now someone else had actually appeared to ruin his fun.

Looking more closely, Yuan Wu's expression turned cold, and he sent a transmission. "It's you!"

He had also recognized Yang Kai. This young man had previously sat at the same table as Li Jiao, so the two were clearly on good terms.

What was this? Now that the Dragon Descendant was gone, did Yang Kai intend to protect this mother and daughter pair too? With his paltry cultivation, could he even protect them?

Yuan Wu truly did not take Yang Kai seriously. He was at the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm and possessed a Second-Tier Upper-Level Dragon Bloodline. He could even contend with an ordinary Third Stage Emperor Realm Master. Even if he could not win, protecting himself should not be much of a problem. He had been unable to sense Yang Kai's cultivation before only because Yang Kai had not circulated his Emperor Yuan.

At this moment, everyone was flying through the air. Once Yang Kai circulated his Emperor Yuan, his cultivation was laid bare for all to see.

First Stage of the Emperor Realm!

Since when had a First Stage Emperor Realm cultivator become capable of challenging him? And a human at that! Yuan Wu was beside himself with fury. He felt that this brat simply did not take him seriously at all. The boy had truly gone mad!

He sent a sinister transmission. "Brat, you've got some nerve, daring to ruin this Lord's fun!"

The corner of Yang Kai's mouth twitched as he transmitted back, "This Young Master has always been this bold."

Yuan Wu stared blankly, clearly not expecting Yang Kai to be so unyielding. He had assumed Yang Kai had only forced himself to interfere out of loyalty. If so, a few harsh words should have been enough to frighten him away.

Yet not only had his words failed to frighten Yang Kai away, they had even provoked Yang Kai into retorting harshly. Was this brat trying to rebel?

In an instant, Yuan Wu came to hate Yang Kai. He secretly resolved to find an opportunity to make this brat's life worse than death. A mere First Stage Emperor Realm human actually dared act so presumptuously before him. If Fu Qi had not been just ahead, Yuan Wu would likely have attacked him at once.

Yuan Wu sneered coldly and nodded. "Good, very good. Remember what you just said. I hope you won't regret it later."

Yang Kai scoffed. "Rest assured, this Young Master never regrets anything he does."

Too arrogant! Too contemptuous of everyone around him! Yuan Wu was infuriated. In all of Half-Dragon City, no one had ever dared speak to him this way, yet not only had someone done so today, that person even wore an expression that seemed to ask what Yuan Wu could possibly do about it.

Gritting his teeth, Yuan Wu said, "Do you think this Lord doesn't dare attack you in front of Sir Fu Qi? If so, then you are gravely mistaken..."

"Fine, come on!" Yang Kai looked at him with complete sincerity. "If you want to attack, then go ahead. This Young Master absolutely won't fight back."

Yuan Wu choked on his words.

He truly did not dare attack.

Fu Qi was right ahead. If Yuan Wu attacked for no reason, Fu Qi would not let him off. He did not believe Fu Qi thought highly of him simply because Fu Qi was willing to exchange a few words with him. The Dragon Clan was arrogant and simply disdained speaking with people like them. Still, it was better to talk to a familiar face than a stranger.

Fu Qi did not need Yuan Wu specifically.

Yuan Wu understood this point perfectly well, so after Yang Kai rendered him speechless, his face and neck flushed with rage. He snarled, "Brat, I'll remember you. Once we reach the island, you'll learn what this Lord is capable of."

"Stupid fool!" Yang Kai glanced at him coldly.

Yuan Wu froze for a moment, then flushed crimson. "What did you say?"

Yang Kai's lips moved, and he slowly repeated those two words.

Yuan Wu nearly exploded.

Just then, a figure suddenly stepped between Yang Kai and Yuan Wu. He cupped his fists toward Yuan Wu with a fawning smile plastered across his face. His Divine Sense surged, though no one knew what he said to Yuan Wu.

He was a middle-aged man with a pointed mouth, monkey-like cheeks, and beady, rat-like eyes. One look was enough to tell that he was no good.

His cultivation, however, was not weak. He had reached the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm, and from his appearance, he seemed to be a Yao. The Yao Qi around him was dense, but not particularly pure.

This was quite normal in Half-Dragon City. Dragons were lustful by nature and coupled with all living creatures. The Dragon Clan had not only abducted Human women to Dragon Isle, but Yao women as well. The offspring born from their unions with the Dragon Clan often ended up like this, possessing no Dragon Clan bloodline while also having impure Yao power. In addition, given Half-Dragon City's special circumstances, Humans and Yao intermingled there, so the birth of some Half-Yao was unavoidable.

Such Half-Yao generally found it very difficult to improve their cultivation, so the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm was already an extremely high level for one.

While the man communicated with Yuan Wu through a Divine Sense transmission, he secretly shot Yang Kai a meaningful glance.

Yang Kai blinked and chuckled inwardly, thinking this Half-Yao was rather well-intentioned.

The man had clearly noticed the conflict between Yang Kai and Yuan Wu, so he had deliberately stepped in to defuse the situation. Little did he know that Yang Kai had no need for his help. Just as Yuan Wu did not take Yang Kai seriously, Yang Kai had never taken Yuan Wu seriously either.

Even so, this caused Yang Kai to form a somewhat favorable impression of the Half-Yao. It seemed one truly could not judge a person by appearances. Looking at this Half-Yao's face alone, anyone would think he was sinister and sleazy, certainly no good man. If they encountered him outside, they would surely keep as far away as possible to avoid being set up if they let their guard down.
