## Chapter 2834 — Endless Tricks

Shaman Chi was greatly shocked. He never expected that this Great Shaman Master could really achieve such a feat. He hurriedly gestured, and the flying skull instantly seemed to receive a summons, rapidly shrinking and returning to his hand.

Looking down, Shaman Chi’s heart burned with rage, his face suddenly contorting with ferocity.

His own shamanic artifact had actually been cracked!

This was a shamanic artifact belonging to a Shaman King! Under normal circumstances, no matter how fiercely it was attacked, not even the slightest mark would be left, let alone a crack. Yet now, that tiny fissure gaped like a mocking mouth, ridiculing his ignorance.

Just as he was inspecting the skull, he suddenly felt his mind violently tremble. Looking up, he immediately roared, “Enough!”

That unremarkable Great Shaman Master had already flown to another skull, raising his sword to strike. Having suffered once, Shaman Chi now fully understood his opponent’s abilities and quickly recalled all the shamanic artifacts that were spewing out chilling death energy, preparing to turn the village into a domain of death.

The six skulls flew back one after another, circling around him like meteors, all shrinking back to their original size.

With no more rampaging skulls, the deathly aura covering the village quickly melted away like snow under the blazing sun.

Yang Kai hefted the Myriad Sword onto his shoulder, looking at Shaman Chi with a careless grin. “I’m not bragging, but you’d better go all out.”

Shaman Chi’s eye twitched. If any other Great Shaman Master spoke to him like this, he would have scoffed, but this one… was different.

“Was it really you who killed You?” Shaman Chi asked coldly.

Yang Kai replied, “Yes and no. In the end, he died by self-destruction!”

“So it really was you!” Shaman Chi was furious. He had suspected it before—given You’s abilities, how could he be killed so easily? Even the stone fort had been bathed in blood. After all, neither Chi nor Zhu had the power to kill You. He had thought perhaps a Shaman King from the Gale Tribe or Raging Flame Tribe had acted, but after witnessing Yang Kai’s methods, he knew the truth.

You really did die at his hands. Otherwise, he couldn’t have known such details.

Most of You’s abilities were based on that sea of blood, which was formed from his shamanic artifact, the Blood Gourd. And this Great Shaman Master who killed You also seemed to possess a formidable shamanic artifact.

Ordinary shamanic artifacts could never harm his skulls.

But… he was just a Great Shaman Master. Even if he had some tricks, with Shaman Chi himself taking action, could he really escape alive?

“You’ll pay for your arrogance!” Shaman Chi growled, suddenly raising both hands. The six skulls twisted and howled, shooting straight at Yang Kai. This time, however, the skulls did not grow larger. Though they lost some might, they were undoubtedly more agile and unpredictable.

In an instant, the six skulls sealed off Yang Kai from all directions. Each one opened its mouth, spewing out streams of chilling energy.

This energy seemed able to freeze all things. Even a Shaman King would suffer if touched. Yang Kai stood with his sword, shaking out blossoms of sword light, defending himself without a single gap.

A series of clangs rang out. The six skulls’ full-force attacks were all blocked by Yang Kai’s sword aura, leaving the skulls themselves bobbing unsteadily.

Shaman Chi, however, remained calm, as if he had expected this. A disdainful smile curled his lips as he silently chanted an incantation. With a flick of his finger, he unleashed a mighty shamanic art without a sound, sending it toward Yang Kai.

With his side turned toward Shaman Chi, Yang Kai seemed completely unprepared. Shaman Chi sneered inwardly, waiting for the moment Yang Kai would be taken down.

But at that moment, a transparent shamanic shield suddenly appeared. As it formed, a semi-transparent fierce ghost crashed into it, letting out a miserable howl.

Yang Kai didn’t even look. He just grinned, blocking the six skulls with his right hand and hurling a washbasin-sized fireball at the ghost with his left.

An even more agonized scream rang out as the formless ghost was instantly reduced to nothingness.

Shaman Chi was dumbfounded! This brat still had the strength to fend off his sneak attack? How could he possess such powerful Divine Sense, able to guard against hidden tricks even in such an intense battle?

Shaman Chi’s face turned ashen, feeling inexplicably humiliated.

He was a Shaman King, yet when facing a Great Shaman Master, his every move was countered?

Enraged, Shaman Chi continued to pressure Yang Kai with the six skulls while his hands danced, unleashing shamanic arts one after another, bombarding Yang Kai relentlessly.

Being able to defend once didn’t mean this brat could defend forever. As long as he distracted him, his shamanic artifact would surely find a chance to kill him in one blow.

But what happened next left Shaman Chi utterly stunned.

Facing the unending barrage of shamanic arts, Yang Kai remained unflustered. His left hand continuously blossomed with all kinds of shamanic lights, methodically countering every attack.

In an instant, the two Great Shamans were locked in a contest of shamanic arts in midair.

Though their realms differed by a whole Realm, the shamanic arts they unleashed were evenly matched in both frequency and lethality, colliding and dissolving in midair, bursting into dazzling lights again and again.

Perhaps at first, Shaman Chi felt he held some advantage, with his opponent occasionally flustered. But as time passed, the other’s mastery of shamanic arts grew ever more refined, his control and power flawless.

Unbelievable!

This was just a tiny village, not even large enough to be called a tribe. Shaman Chi had wiped out at least eighty or a hundred such villages. In places like this, having a single Shaman Apprentice was already rare, yet this one had a Great Shaman Master.

What he couldn’t accept even more was that this Great Shaman Master could match him in shamanic arts.

How could he have mastered so many shamanic arts? No tribe’s shamanic arts were easy to learn—each shaman could only study the arts passed down in their own tribe, generation after generation.

As far as he knew, the Southern Barbarian Tribe’s shamanic arts weren’t particularly distinctive, rather mediocre. At the Great Shaman Master level, mastering a dozen or twenty shamanic arts was already impressive. Any more, and there simply wasn’t the energy to learn them. Of those, only about half were offensive; the rest were mainly for empowering the tribe’s warriors.

He himself, a dignified Lower-Level Shaman King, only knew about fifty shamanic arts! Of those, only twenty-seven were for attack, and he had long since mastered them all. Such achievements would make even some Middle-Level Shaman Kings bow their heads.

Yet compared to his opponent, it was like a minor shaman before a great one.

In less than half an hour of fighting, Shaman Chi had seen at least a hundred different shamanic arts from his opponent, none repeated, and all performed effortlessly, as if he had practiced them countless times.

Many of the shamanic arts made him envious, for they were secret techniques never passed outside their tribes, only known to the great shamans of those tribes.

Was this guy really a Great Shaman Master? Judging by his age, he didn’t seem old—where did he learn so many shamanic arts, and how did he have time to master them all?

If his opponent were an old man, Shaman Chi wouldn’t be so shocked. The elderly could always do amazing things, but this one looked only twenty or thirty years old.

The longer Shaman Chi fought, the more alarmed he became. He even felt as if his opponent was the Shaman King and he was the Great Shaman Master, because this Great Shaman Master’s performance was so dazzling, it made him want to capture him alive and savor his flesh while he still breathed.

Boom…

In another clash of shamanic arts, the two spells collided in midair, but instead of canceling each other out as before, the opponent’s lightning broke through his soul-reaping art and, with undiminished force, lunged at him. The flickering lightning was like a venomous snake, baring its fangs.

Shaman Chi was aghast. His shamanic art had been broken! In his haste, he could only conjure a shamanic shield in front of himself.

After successfully blocking the lightning, Shaman Chi’s face alternated between green and white. After a long silence, he said, “Are you… using me to cultivate?”

Recalling everything that had happened, Shaman Chi suddenly felt a sense of foreboding. If his guess was right, he had really fallen into a trap this time.

Yang Kai laughed loudly, sweeping away the six skulls circling him with a single sword, and called out, “Many thanks for your help, Shaman King!”

Having inherited vast knowledge from Qing, Yang Kai had never had time to fully digest it. During this month of seclusion and bitter cultivation, he had been absorbing Qing’s legacy.

But shutting oneself away could never compare to real combat. Now that a Shaman King had delivered himself to his door, Yang Kai naturally wanted to test the results of his month’s training.

The results pleased him greatly. All the ideas and plans he’d formed in seclusion were validated today. Though there were some differences, they didn’t affect the overall outcome.

With Yang Kai’s confirmation, Shaman Chi’s face grew even uglier. Overwhelmed by shame, he trembled all over and shouted, “You’re courting death!”

Competing in shamanic arts with him was a grave mistake. This guy was using him to cultivate, which was why his shamanic arts kept growing stronger, gradually seizing the initiative and breaking through his defenses.

This Great Shaman Master was truly terrifying, able to grow rapidly even in battle. His rate of improvement made Shaman Chi uneasy.

So he decided to stop playing around and end it quickly.

With a low shout, the six skulls that had been circling Yang Kai suddenly flew back, returning to Shaman Chi’s side in the blink of an eye.

Shaman Chi’s expression turned solemn as he began chanting. The six skulls spinning around him moved faster and faster, gradually turning into a streak of emerald light.
