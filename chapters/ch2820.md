## Chapter 2820 — Scratching an Itch

Yang Kai's expression became solemn. Although this move looked simple, it clearly demonstrated the old man's perfect control over his strength.

He had thought that, by joining forces with Die, even if they couldn't defeat this elder, at the very least they could cause him some trouble. But now it seemed he had been too naive—this elder's strength was simply unfathomable.

If the elder truly bore malice toward him, even with Die's help, they wouldn't be able to withstand a single breath from him.

At this moment, the old man was obviously very interested in the potion Yang Kai had refined. After sniffing it for a while, still unsatisfied, he scooped out a bit and tasted it. His brows rose, and he turned to Yang Kai, asking, "Did you concoct this potion?"

Yang Kai nodded, "That's right!"

"An excellent healing medicine," the old man praised without reservation. "But it can't be used too frequently. If overused, it may damage one's foundation."

He saw the flaw in the healing medicine at a glance. After all, Yang Kai's potion was made from very ordinary herbs, so it shouldn't have such powerful healing effects. Yet it could stimulate the vitality in the barbarian blood, speeding up self-healing. The Bloodlust Art worked similarly. Once or twice was fine, but too frequent use would harm the foundation and cause one to age faster.

Still, this wasn't a major issue. After all, it's an external medicine—no one would use it often.

"Who would have thought that apothecaries these days are so skilled," the old man remarked with clear interest in the healing salve, lifting the stone jar and carefully examining the herbs used within.

Taking advantage of the moment, Yang Kai pulled Die aside and quietly asked, "Who exactly is this elder?"

Die smiled, "Grandpa Qing didn't tell you?"

Yang Kai shook his head.

Die said, "Since Grandpa Qing didn't say, I can't either."

Yang Kai reached out, "Hurry up and return the Qing coins you earned recently."

Die was startled, jumped back warily, and glared, "Don't even think about it, they're mine!"

Yang Kai sneered, "Looks like you want your little secret to be known by everyone."

Having been caught at her weak spot, Die gritted her teeth and looked at Yang Kai with contempt, "Despicable, shameless..."

Yang Kai snorted, "Come up with some new insults!"

At that moment, the old man set down the stone jar, turned with a gentle smile, and said, "Young man, you already have your suspicions in your heart—why make things difficult for the girl?"

Yang Kai frowned, took a deep breath, his eyes unable to hide his shock, and said slowly, "I have my suspicions, but I can't be certain."

The old man smiled, "Whatever you think in your heart, that's how things are."

Yang Kai was startled, looking at the old man in amazement, and said in a deep voice, "Are you really... the Divine Tree?"

This old man had appeared mysteriously in his tree cave, and Die called him Grandpa Qing. With Yang Kai's meticulous thinking, it would be strange not to make the connection. Still, he had his suspicions but couldn't quite believe it.

The old man waved his hand, "Divine Tree and such are just exaggerations from the Frost Snow Tribe. Many years ago, I was just an ordinary evergreen tree."

Those many years—likely a thousand or ten thousand years ago...

Hearing him admit it, Yang Kai relaxed. It made sense; legend had it that the Divine Tree's tree cave couldn't be entered even by Shaman Kings or Shaman Saints. For the old man to suddenly appear in his tree cave, who else could do it but the Divine Tree itself?

Because of this, Yang Kai had nothing to worry about. He'd lived in the Frost Snow Tribe for so long, though he didn't interact much, he'd heard many tales of the Divine Tree—a tree of peace, a tree of protection, so naturally it wouldn't bear him any ill will.

"Please forgive me, Senior Qing. I spoke rashly just now and offended you. You are magnanimous; I hope you won't hold it against me," Yang Kai said solemnly, cupping his fists.

Qing laughed, "No need for that. Die is under your care, how could I blame you?"

Yang Kai asked curiously, "Senior, your relationship with Die..."

One is the Divine Tree, the other a barbarian from the Drifting Tribe. They seemed unrelated, yet Die was very familiar with Qing, even calling him Grandpa.

"Sixteen years ago, someone abandoned a baby girl under the tree!" Qing didn't explain much, just said this, gently patting Die's head.

Die didn't mind, just giggled, enjoying the rare affection.

Yang Kai understood. It seemed Die was raised by Qing, so the intimate address made sense. Die's transformation technique was likely due to Qing's protection.

Yang Kai had been curious about what shamanic art Die used to change her petite figure into a robust form—after all, she was just a green leaf. But now it seemed it wasn't Die's shamanic art, but Qing's power.

Under the Divine Tree, Qing could control everything, granting Die a mysterious green leaf to conceal her appearance with ease.

"Grandpa Qing, didn't you just wake up last year? How come you're awake again so soon?" Die asked curiously.

Hearing this, Qing looked at Yang Kai and said, "I sensed a familiar aura..."

He had said this before, but Yang Kai hadn't believed him. Now, hearing it again, Yang Kai no longer doubted.

"A familiar aura?" Die also turned her head in curiosity, looking at Yang Kai.

Yang Kai's heart stirred. Could it be the aura of the Everlasting Tree? If anything could make Qing feel kinship, it would be the Everlasting Tree—a treasure of heaven and earth, different from the Divine Tree, but ultimately both were trees.

Still, it didn't quite make sense. The Everlasting Tree was still inside the Xuan Realm Pearl; its aura shouldn't be leaking out.

Though he had doubts, Yang Kai couldn't explain further. The secret of the Everlasting Tree was known to very few.

Qing studied Yang Kai for a while, then shook his head, "Strange, this aura is of the same origin as mine, yet very different." As he spoke, he fell into deep thought, his expression constantly changing.

Yang Kai and Die looked at each other, not daring to disturb the elder, and could only wait idly.

After half a day had passed, Qing finally snapped out of his reverie, chuckling self-deprecatingly. "I'm getting old—almost drifted off to sleep again." Though he appeared aged, he spent most of his years in slumber, and this awakening was nothing more than a stroke of fate.

Die immediately pouted and said playfully, "Grandpa Qing, you're not old at all."

Qing smiled, "Since I'm awake, little Die, help Grandpa scratch his itch. My body isn't feeling well again."

"Alright, alright!" Die's eyes lit up as if she'd found money. She turned to Yang Kai, "Shaman Niu, do you want to join in?"

Yang Kai was bewildered—helping scratch an itch? But seeing Qing's age, he couldn't refuse, so he stepped forward, standing behind Qing, and began scratching his back.

Die paused, then burst out laughing.

Qing couldn't help but smile as well.

"What's so funny?" Yang Kai glared at Die, annoyed. He was working so diligently, yet she laughed and mocked him—too much!

Die laughed until her stomach hurt, bending over, "That's not how you scratch Grandpa Qing's itch..."

"Teach me then!" Yang Kai stepped back and gestured to Die.

Die stopped laughing, though her face still showed a suppressed smile. She looked at Qing, "Grandpa Qing, let's begin."

Qing nodded, pointed forward, and the not-so-spacious tree cave suddenly split open, revealing a dark passage leading who knows where.

Die said to Yang Kai, "Follow me!"

With that, she entered the passage and disappeared in the blink of an eye.

Yang Kai didn't hesitate and followed.

The moment he entered, his body slid downward uncontrollably, as if a slide had appeared beneath him, carrying him to an unknown place.

Moments later, when Yang Kai stood firmly, Die had already cast a lighting spell, dispelling the darkness. She pointed around, "Grandpa Qing feels itchy because of their presence. Our task is to clean them out!"

As she finished speaking, pairs of crimson eyes suddenly lit up all around.

Yang Kai looked up and was shocked, "So many Yao Beasts!"

Die explained, "These are all parasites living on Grandpa Qing. They multiply incredibly fast. I used to come clean them out once a year, but with your help this time, you’re actually getting a pretty good deal."

Yang Kai asked in surprise, "You say I'm here to help, but how exactly is this a good deal for me?"

Die smiled, "I noticed you've bought a lot of Barbaric Beast inner cores lately for cultivation. With so many Barbaric Beasts here, don't you have any ideas?"

Hearing this, Yang Kai's eyes lit up. Just when he was worried about how to earn Qing coins to buy inner cores, an opportunity appeared before him.

Inside Qing’s true form was like an entire world unto itself. Within this vast inner realm, countless demonic insects survived by feeding on Qing’s strength, multiplying and growing at astonishing speed. Each of these parasites, varying in size and appearance, was essentially a living inner core waiting to be claimed.

As they spoke, the demonic insects couldn't hold back any longer, emerging from their hiding places and surrounding the two, hissing as they approached.

In an instant, Yang Kai and Die were plunged into a world of demonic insects, surrounded layer upon layer.

"Just so we're clear, whoever kills them gets them. Don't try to snatch mine later!" Die warned Yang Kai, and as she raised her hand, three streaks of energy shot forward.

As soon as she attacked, Yang Kai felt a surge of powerful energy.

Great Shaman Master! Yang Kai's brows rose. He hadn't expected that Die, who had been working so hard selling medicine for him, was actually a Great Shaman Master. Though he couldn't sense her exact rank, her power was far beyond what a Shaman Master could display.
