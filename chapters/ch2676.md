## Chapter 2676 — Got You

Yang Kai’s heart was burning with rage. Battling the young woman brought back memories of his helplessness against Shi Huo in the Ancient Land more than a year ago.

If I couldn’t kill Shi Huo, does that mean I can’t kill you, you little brat?

That fury surged like magma in Yang Kai’s chest, making his blood boil. Though he still couldn’t match the girl’s terrifying brute strength and was mostly on the defensive, the longer he fought, the fiercer he became, his fighting spirit rising, and his mastery of power growing ever more fluid.

Boom boom boom…

Two figures tangled and clashed, darting through the mountains, causing countless peaks to collapse. Birds and beasts scattered across the land. Again and again, Yang Kai was blasted away, spitting blood in midair, but each time he stubbornly and fearlessly dove back into the fray.

His clothes were nearly shredded, hanging in tatters. His upper body was bare, and only a few scraps of cloth covered his lower half.

He had never used defensive Secret Treasures like armor—not because he hadn’t obtained any. Over the years, he’d killed many experts, and their rings always held such things. But Yang Kai never refined them, feeling he didn’t need them. Armor could provide a layer of protection, but it also dulled one’s sense of danger.

Relying too much on external power is never a good thing.

Ordinary clothes couldn’t withstand such wild combat. If the fight went on much longer, Yang Kai would be left completely naked.

Yet the girl seemed not to notice, showing little embarrassment. After all, she'd never eaten pork but had surely seen a pig run—what was the difference? Besides, for someone on the brink of death, a few extra glances hardly mattered.

Compared to Yang Kai’s sorry state, the girl was almost strolling leisurely. No matter how fierce his attacks, she could easily deflect them. Her terrifying brute strength was simply inhuman—even the strongest yao might not be able to match her.

“Pfft…” Yang Kai spat out another mouthful of blood, his whole body sent flying as he crashed to the ground, gouging a deep trench through the earth. The last scrap of cloth covering his waist finally disintegrated.

The girl frowned slightly, thinking this man was stubborn beyond belief.

If it were any ordinary Emperor Realm cultivator, they’d have been smashed into pulp long ago. But this guy was still lively, though he looked miserable. His fighting spirit kept climbing, thanks to the surging vitality hidden in his strange Golden Blood. Without it, he’d have died already.

That Golden Blood really was peculiar.

Yang Kai shakily climbed to his feet, spat out a mouthful of blood, and stared at the girl with a defiant gleam in his eyes.

The unyielding struggle in adversity, the desperate fight in hopelessness—it was as if he’d returned to the moment years ago when he obtained the Demon God’s Secret Codex. It was precisely because of his indomitable spirit that he was recognized by the Codex and set on the path of Martial Dao.

Today was so much like that day.

But after all these years, he’d nearly forgotten that feeling.

Yet this bitter battle finally let him reclaim what he’d lost.

Unyielding spirit—who in this world can truly subdue me?

His mind stirred, and he vaguely understood something. His blood surged even faster, and the wounds covering his body quickly clotted. Powerful recovery began to show; in the blink of an eye, all injuries vanished. Yang Kai wiped away the blood scab, revealing skin as fresh as new.

“So that’s it!” Yang Kai’s eyes flashed with brilliance.

Years of relying on secret techniques and Secret Treasures in battle had nearly made him forget his innate resilience. Today’s sudden Enlightenment was a great harvest.

“You…” The girl’s brows knitted, instinctively sensing Yang Kai had changed somehow, though she couldn’t say how.

Yang Kai took out a fresh set of clothes from his Spatial Ring and dressed himself. As his eyelids rose and fell, a sharp light flashed in his eyes, almost seeming to take physical form.

Rolling his wrist, Yang Kai hooked a finger at the young woman and called out, “Again!”

The girl grew angry, gritting her teeth. “Since you’re so eager to die, I’ll oblige you.”

Without any warning, she suddenly rushed at Yang Kai, throwing a plain, unadorned punch.

Yang Kai reached out to block. The moment they touched, his vision brightened, and his power erupted fully.

Boom…

Yang Kai was sent flying again, but the girl also involuntarily stepped back.

“What’s going on…” The girl was utterly shocked. Before, no matter how hard Yang Kai tried, he couldn’t budge her. But after this blow, she was forced back a step—the power in that punch was far greater than before.

In such a short time, what kind of change had he undergone to grow so much?

A peal of laughter rang out as Yang Kai came hurtling back like a tiger descending the mountain. “Show you a little color and you think you can open a dye shop? Soft and flimsy—just like a woman!” he shouted as he struck from midair.

The girl was furious, her fists flying like the wind, raining blows on Yang Kai.

In an instant, the sky was filled with fist shadows.

Soon, the figures separated. The ground beneath the girl’s feet cracked, and she was slightly out of breath. Yang Kai’s brand-new clothes were once again torn, his skin split open, Golden Blood streaming like little rivers all over him. He looked battered, but didn’t care—instead, he fought even harder.

The girl finally grew serious, sizing Yang Kai up and down. “What’s your name?”

She’d asked the same question before at the City Lord’s mansion, but back then it was just a casual inquiry, and she’d dismissed the answer, not caring about Yang Kai at all. She thought he was doomed, so why bother knowing his name?

But now, she asked again.

This human she’d once ignored was now worthy of her knowing his name.

Experts always command respect.

“Yang Kai!”

The girl’s red lips moved, murmuring softly, then said crisply, “Zhu Qing!”

Yang Kai was stunned, only then realizing that was her name. He nodded. “I’ll remember it.”

Zhu Qing looked at him in surprise and asked, “You hear my name, and you don’t feel anything?”

Yang Kai thought seriously. “It’s a good name—simple, honest, rolls off the tongue. Not bad, not bad.”

Zhu Qing couldn’t help but roll her eyes, realizing this guy truly hadn’t made any connection at all. Ignorance really is bliss, she thought—how did someone like him even manage to cultivate to the First Stage of the Emperor Realm?

Though she thought he was promising, since he’d committed a taboo, there was no place for him in the world—he was doomed.

“Enough!” Zhu Qing waved her hand, a bit disinterested. “I’m going all out now.”

“Hmph, I haven’t used my full strength either. Just now, I held back because you’re a woman.”

What’s with this little girl—was she trying to say she’d just been playing around until now? Such arrogance! Still, he couldn’t let his own momentum falter.

“I know.” Zhu Qing smiled slightly, looking harmless.

Yang Kai thought she’d spar with him, trading barbs, but was surprised by her answer.

“What do you know?” Yang Kai raised his brow.

Zhu Qing tucked her black hair behind her ear and said slowly, “You’ve refined those things, but I haven’t seen you use them. Clearly, you haven’t gone all out.”

Yang Kai frowned. “You keep talking about ‘those things’—what are they?”

Zhu Qing had mentioned before that he’d refined things he shouldn’t have, and this fight was because of that. But he hated people playing riddles with him. If not for her curves, he’d have slapped her already.

Zhu Qing’s expression turned cold. “You don’t even remember what you’ve refined?”

In an instant, her aura underwent a dramatic transformation. If just a moment ago Zhu Qing had seemed like the harmless girl next door, now she was a fierce Jiao emerging from the sea, ready to unleash havoc.

Yang Kai sneered. “I’ve refined plenty. If you don’t say, how would I know?”

“Is that so…” Zhu Qing touched her red lips with a delicate finger, smiling sweetly. “Then let me help you remember.”

As she spoke, she suddenly closed in on Yang Kai, her hand forming a claw and reaching for his chest with such speed it was impossible to react.

Maybe it was an illusion, but Yang Kai seemed to hear a dragon’s roar. Zhu Qing’s hand, in that instant, seemed to become a dragon claw, ready to tear through all defenses and rip out his heart.

So this girl really hadn’t used her full strength before. Yang Kai was shocked and hurriedly retreated.

But Zhu Qing stuck to him like a shadow, impossible to shake off, so close she was almost breathing on him. “If you don’t use it now, you won’t get another chance.”

At the brink of life and death, inspiration struck Yang Kai. He shouted, “Dragon Transformation!”

His already powerful aura soared to new heights, his might rivaling a Third Stage Emperor Realm expert. Scales suddenly appeared on his exposed skin, both hands transformed into dragon claws, and a golden light flashed behind him—a Golden Sacred Dragon shadow flickered and vanished.

Zhu Qing felt a sudden panic for no reason, looking behind Yang Kai but seeing nothing. She stared wide-eyed, as if stunned.

But that brief distraction made her aura plummet.

Yang Kai grabbed for her hand.

“Not good!” Zhu Qing’s pretty face paled as she tried to pull away, but at such close range, there was no escape. Yang Kai’s dragon claw clamped down on her hand, an overwhelming, unstoppable force surging from his grip. For the first time, Zhu Qing truly understood what pain felt like.

“Got you.” Dragon scales appeared faintly on Yang Kai’s face, his whole aura becoming strange and sinister.

“Let go!” Zhu Qing shouted, punching at Yang Kai’s chest with her other hand.

Yang Kai didn’t move, letting the punch land. It sounded like metal striking metal, sparks flying. His chest caved in an inch, and he staggered, but he still held Zhu Qing’s hand tight.

Zhu Qing was stunned—she hadn’t expected her attack to be so weak.

No, it wasn’t weakness—he’d become stronger.

As she tried to punch again, Yang Kai had already grabbed her other arm. In an instant, both her arms were restrained, and the two were extremely close—barely three inches apart.
