## Chapter 2647 — A Cushy Job

Yang Kai turned around again with a grin and looked at Luan Feng. “Lady, do you have any other instructions?”

Luan Feng nearly ground her silver teeth to pieces, fuming, “I really don’t know what debt I owe you.”

Yang Kai shook his head and said, “Lady, you’re mistaken. Helping others is the foundation of happiness. But does this mean you’re willing to lend a hand?”

Since she had loosened her stance, she naturally wouldn’t refuse. After all, she couldn’t be sure whether Zhang Ruoxi was truly from Ice Heart Valley, but with matters like this, it’s better to believe than to doubt. Since Yang Kai brought up Zhang Ruoxi, Luan Feng had no choice but to help.

She wasn’t helping Yang Kai, but rather the descendant of Heaven’s Punisher whom she feared.

“Just two Third Stage Emperor Realm cultivators? I can send a few Yao Kings at random, why would I need to act personally?” Luan Feng said with clear displeasure.

Yang Kai raised his brow. “Lady is wise. Then please send seven or eight Yao Kings to return with me to the Northern Territory and wipe out the misers!”

He never expected Luan Feng to act herself. His plan from the start was to have her send a few Yao Kings. Now that she said so, it was exactly what he wanted.

“Seven or eight…” Luan Feng rolled her eyes. “Keep dreaming!”

She didn’t have that many Yao Kings under her command. Yang Kai asking for seven or eight right away—anyone who knew the situation would understand he was going to resolve Ice Heart Valley’s crisis, but those who didn’t might think he was about to conquer the world.

“Then four or five…” Yang Kai chuckled.

Luan Feng didn’t answer, instead falling into deep thought. After a while, she said, “Since this involves that person, it concerns the entire Ancient Land. I alone can’t bear the responsibility. Here’s what we’ll do: stay at Fengluo Palace for a few days. Within three days, I’ll give you a satisfactory answer.”

Hearing this, Yang Kai seemed to realize something important. He smiled and nodded. “Thank you, Lady!”

After a pause, Yang Kai said, “There’s one more thing I need your help with!”

“What?” Luan Feng immediately became wary, finding Yang Kai more and more annoying. Whenever this brat opened his mouth, nothing good ever came of it.

Yang Kai said, “I want to build a Space Array in Fengluo Palace. Is there a suitable place?”

“A Space Array?” Luan Feng’s brows knitted. “You actually know the Dao of Arrays?”

“I know a thing or two.”

“Where will this Space Array lead?” Luan Feng asked, uneasy.

“Back to Ice Heart Valley, of course,” Yang Kai replied matter-of-factly. “It took me a month to get here. I can’t possibly go back the same way. If I did, there wouldn’t be enough time. So I need to build a Space Array directly to Ice Heart Valley.”

“A cross-territory Space Array!” Luan Feng was shocked. “You can really do that?”

After all, this was the Eastern Territory, while Ice Heart Valley was in the Northern Territory. If Yang Kai wasn’t just boasting, then he truly had the skill to set up a cross-territory Space Array. That was no small feat. All the cross-territory Space Arrays left in the Star Realm were ancient relics—no one could build new ones nowadays.

“Lady Feng, just wait and see.” Yang Kai smiled faintly.

Luan Feng nodded slightly. “I’ll allow it. Go find Tianlong, she’ll make arrangements for you.”

“Thank you, Lady!”

With everything settled, Luan Feng summoned Tianlong and instructed her to take Yang Kai to the same small building he’d stayed in last time.

Returning to the small building, Yang Kai told Old Ban and Ling’er to rest, then relayed his request to Tianlong. Hearing it was Luan Feng’s order, Tianlong didn’t object and personally found a secluded and spacious spot for Yang Kai.

Yang Kai set about arranging a Space Array in that secluded spot.

It didn’t take much effort. In less than a day, a brand new Space Array was complete.

After confirming there were no issues with the Space Array, Yang Kai returned to the loft to rest.

He had just left when a graceful figure suddenly appeared beside the Space Array, examining it with curiosity.

“Tianlong, did he really set this up in a single day?” Luan Feng asked in disbelief.

Standing nearby, Tianlong nodded, equally shocked. “I saw it with my own eyes. Although I kept my distance while Young Master Yang was working, I watched the array take shape from start to finish.”

“Is he really that skilled?” Luan Feng looked unconvinced.

Space Arrays weren’t easy to construct, let alone cross-territory ones. Luan Feng had thought Yang Kai would need at least ten days or half a month, maybe even longer. Who would have thought he’d finish in just a day?

It was almost miraculous.

“Ice Heart Valley…” Luan Feng muttered. “Tianlong, I’m going to Ice Heart Valley. Stay here and keep watch.”

She wanted to go to Ice Heart Valley for two reasons: first, to test whether the Space Array actually worked or was just for show; second, to ask if Zhang Ruoxi truly hailed from there.

She still felt Yang Kai was trying to fool her. That brat was full of tricks and never had good intentions.

Tianlong was alarmed. “Lady, you mustn’t! There’s no telling if the array will work. If something goes wrong…”

If an accident occurred during transmission, it was no joke. One could be banished to the void forever, unable to find a way out. Even with Luan Feng’s strength, being exiled to the void would be disastrous.

Luan Feng waved her hand. “I have my own plans.”

As she spoke, she embedded many Upper-Level Source Crystals into the grooves of the Formation Foundation, then stepped onto the array.

Tianlong waited anxiously.

Luan Feng was a little nervous herself. After all, Yang Kai had set this up in less than a day—who knew if it was defective?

But to their surprise, after waiting a long time, the Space Array didn’t react at all.

“What nonsense! All talk!” Luan Feng sneered, certain the array was useless. She stepped down and returned to the palace in high spirits.

She wanted to see Yang Kai’s face when he tried to use the array and found it broken.

For the next few days, Old Ban lived in constant fear, treading on thin ice.

After all, this was the palace of a Divine Spirit. To have the fortune to stay here even once in his life was thanks to Yang Kai. How could he dare overstep? As for Ling’er, innocent and lively, she was endlessly fascinated by the luxurious surroundings, touching everything in sight.

The spirit fruits on the table were almost all eaten by her. These fruits were Ancient Land specialties, rich in Spiritual Qi, and were rare treasures outside. Anyone who ate them would benefit greatly in their cultivation.

Ling’er stayed here for three days, eating dozens of spirit fruits. Her once sallow and thin body became rosy and plump, and even her yellowed hair turned jet black and shiny. She was now brimming with Spiritual Qi.

On the morning of the third day, outside Fengluo Palace, a sudden eagle cry rang out. A streak of azure light fell from the sky. Before the man standing outside the palace could even bow, another figure flew over from a different direction and landed nearby.

The two exchanged glances. The first smiled and raised his brow. “Xie Wuwei!”

The other replied coolly, “Yingfei?”

Yingfei looked Xie Wuwei up and down with curiosity. “Brother Xie, what brings you to Fengluo Palace? This isn’t Lord Fan Wu’s domain.”

Xie Wuwei curled his lip. “You’re here too, and this isn’t Lord Cang Gou’s domain either.”

After speaking, the two seemed to realize something and said in unison, “You’re here for that matter?”

No answer was needed; everything was clear without words.

Xie Wuwei exclaimed, “You’re really lucky. To get such a cushy job, the others must be furious!”

Yingfei laughed. “A few days ago, Young Master Yang passed through my territory, and I escorted him for a while. After Lord Cang Gou heard, he assigned me to assist Young Master Yang. But how did you snatch this job, Brother Xie?”

He knew that among the Yao Kings under Fan Wu, Xie Wuwei was neither the strongest nor the most favored. His temper was foul and stubborn, not likable. Yet Xie Wuwei had managed to stand out and seize this cushy job, which was truly surprising.

He’d managed to get the job without a fight simply because of a chance encounter with Yang Kai a few days ago. But what about Xie Wuwei?

Xie Wuwei replied, “None of your damn business.”

Yingfei was left speechless, rolling his eyes.

As they spoke, a thunderclap exploded and a bolt of lightning shot over, stopping before Xie Wuwei and Yingfei. The light dispersed, revealing a massive figure.

“Xi Lei!” Xie Wuwei’s brows lifted slightly.

Yingfei couldn’t help but laugh. “Brother Xi Lei, what happened to you?”

The burly figure was Yao King Xi Lei, one of the Thirty-Two Yao Kings. He had originally served under Shi Huo, but after Shi Huo was destroyed, he’d joined Luan Feng and was now one of her trusted subordinates.

Despite his rough and burly appearance, Xi Lei ranked solidly among the top three of the Thirty-Two Yao Kings in terms of strength.

Yet such a powerful Yao King was now covered in wounds, his face bruised and swollen, as if he’d just fought a brutal battle.

The yao race was naturally strong, and ordinary injuries healed quickly. But Xi Lei’s wounds still lingered, looking shocking—proof of how severe the fight had been.

Xi Lei snorted, clearly annoyed. “Got into a brawl with those bastards! Old Bull here didn’t have it easy, but those bastards didn’t fare much better either.”

Though there was no “bull” in Xi Lei’s name, his true form was a rhinoceros of ancient bloodline, which was why he had long referred to himself as “this Old Bull.”

Xie Wuwei and Yingfei exchanged glances, both feeling a bit uneasy. Seeing Xi Lei in this state, they immediately understood: if not for their luck and their connection to Yang Kai, they’d probably have had to fight those bastards for this cushy job themselves.
