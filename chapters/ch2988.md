## Chapter 2988 — Oasis

After cultivating to the Eleventh-Tier, this Yao Beast had undoubtedly developed its own intelligence even if it had not assumed human form, and it knew how to seek fortune and avoid disaster.

Yang Kai's seemingly casual finger strike had blown apart its enormous paw, immediately making it sense mortal danger. Without the slightest hesitation, it turned and fled. Sand and dust wrapped around its body, seemingly as part of some escape Divine Ability, and it shot into the distance like the wind.

Yang Kai was in a good mood after finding the little black dog and had no intention of exterminating it. An Eleventh-Tier Yao Beast was beneath his notice, so when he saw it sensibly flee, he did nothing to stop it.

"What's going on with you?" Yang Kai held the little black dog up before him and examined it carefully, unable to make sense of the situation.

A creature capable of devouring even Demon Kings had actually been chased into a dead end by a mere Eleventh-Tier Yao Beast. Had Yang Kai not witnessed it with his own eyes, he would never have believed it.

Thinking back carefully, Yang Kai recalled that when Gong Yue had been possessed by the Demonic Intent, he had said that the little black dog carried a trace of Guixu's bloodline, not that it was actually a Guixu cub. From this perspective, the bloodline within the little black dog was very likely impure.

When facing its favourite food, the Demon Race, its innate Divine Ability would certainly be stimulated. Against other enemies, however, this ability seemed to work only intermittently.

Back in the Revolving World, it had swallowed Steward Liao at the slightest disagreement, yet when it fought the princes and princesses in the Imperial Palace, its performance had been quite ordinary, and it had not displayed its devouring ability.

From the looks of things, it had most likely been chased by that Eleventh-Tier Yao Beast because it had been unable to use its Divine Ability.

This impure bloodline was rather troublesome.

Yang Kai did not care too much, however. It would naturally be good if the little black dog were capable, but it did not matter if it was not. After all, he had no need to borrow a dog's might to bolster his own. His main priority now was determining where Zhu Qing had gone.

Yang Kai lifted the little black dog onto his shoulder, then flew directly into the air without summoning his flying Imperial Treasure.

Over the next ten days, he continued searching the desert but found nothing.

Ten days later, an oasis suddenly appeared in the distance. Finding such a refreshing sight in this land of yellow sand and dust immediately lifted Yang Kai's spirits.

There seemed to be a small village within the oasis, and he could sense the auras of many living creatures inside.

Yang Kai swept the area with his Divine Sense and immediately discovered that there were no experts here. The strongest person had only reached the Dao Origin Realm, while everyone else was even weaker.

He landed directly in the oasis beside a clear pool, scooped up a handful of water, and washed his face. The little black dog leapt straight into the pool and began swimming around joyfully.

When he looked up again, he saw several children standing nearby, all staring at him with wide, curious eyes.

There were both boys and girls among these children. The youngest looked only four or five, while the oldest were perhaps eleven or twelve. Likely because of the local environment, they were dressed quite simply, and many of their clothes were even patched. Their complexions were poor as well, and every one of them was little more than skin and bones, clearly suffering from malnutrition.

The older children among them had probably already begun cultivating, but their cultivations were pitifully low.

Every pair of eyes shone with curiosity as they timidly watched Yang Kai from over a dozen zhang away.

Yang Kai smiled faintly at them and was just about to speak when his gaze suddenly sharpened. Looking at the top of one girl's head, he asked in delighted surprise, "Little girl, where did you get that?"

This girl was one of the oldest among the children, and a bow had been tied atop her head. What delighted Yang Kai was that the material used to make the bow was none other than an extremely familiar colored ribbon.

This was Zhu Lie's colored-ribbon Imperial Treasure!

Previously, while they were in the endless Void, everyone had used this colored-ribbon Imperial Treasure to connect themselves and prevent anyone from becoming separated. In the end, the Void Turbulence near the world barrier had severed it section by section. Yang Kai had possessed one piece, but as it was useless, he had already thrown it away.

He had not expected to see another piece here.

This was clearly not the piece he had discarded. After all, he had thrown it away far from here, and this little girl could not possibly have gone there to retrieve it.

In other words, this piece of colored ribbon had been discarded either by Li Jiao or by Zhu Lie.

This discovery further confirmed Yang Kai's earlier speculation. Everyone had indeed returned to the Star Realm, but they had landed in different places.

When Yang Kai suddenly spoke, all the children jumped in fright and retreated several steps together.

"Let me borrow that for a look. I'll return it in a moment." Yang Kai beckoned, causing the bow to unravel and fly into his hand.

Looking down, he confirmed that this was indeed a piece of the colored-ribbon Imperial Treasure. Although it was an Imperial Treasure, after being severed into several pieces, it had lost all its spirituality and was no longer of much use. Otherwise, the might of an Imperial Treasure alone would have been enough to crush this little girl into dust.

Yang Kai nodded repeatedly. When he raised his head to ask something else, however, the children suddenly scattered in all directions, likely frightened by his abrupt action.

A short while later, accompanied by the children's cries, several dozen cultivators of varying strength suddenly rushed out from every direction and surged toward Yang Kai.

In the blink of an eye, they had surrounded him in layer upon layer, each staring at him warily with an unfriendly expression.

Yang Kai could not help laughing. He knew his actions had been somewhat rash. Sweeping his gaze around, he fixed it on a middle-aged man and asked, "Are you in charge here?"

This middle-aged man was at the Second Stage of the Dao Origin Realm and was the strongest person here. For some unknown reason, however, his face looked especially weathered. His Dao Origin Realm cultivation had not prevented the passage of time from carving deep marks across it. The other villagers were the same, and Yang Kai also noticed something peculiar in their expressions.

Their eyes seemed filled with despair.

Hearing Yang Kai's question, the middle-aged man nodded. "Indeed. This one is the person in charge of Green Water Village. May I ask Your Excellency's honoured name?"

Although his cultivation was inferior to Yang Kai's, he could still tell that the young man before him was not someone he could afford to offend. His words were therefore filled with respect, for fear that he might bring a fatal disaster upon Green Water Village.

"My name doesn't matter. I only want to ask about something." Yang Kai raised the colored ribbon in his hand. "I saw that little girl wearing this just now and only wanted to ask where she got it. I didn't expect to frighten them. Could I trouble you to help me ask her about it?"

The middle-aged man said, "If it concerns that item, there is no need to ask her."

"You know?"

The middle-aged man nodded. "That item came from a red-haired young man."

"A red-haired young man!" Yang Kai was delighted. There was no need for any detailed questioning to know that it had been Zhu Lie, so he hurriedly asked, "Where is that red-haired young man now?"

"He left quite a few days ago." The middle-aged man thought for a moment before continuing, "He suddenly arrived here about half a month ago and waited for half a day. A woman then came to find him, and the two left together."

Yang Kai quickly asked, "What did that woman look like? Did she also have red hair?"

The middle-aged man nodded. "Indeed! Moreover, those two seemed extremely powerful, perhaps even stronger than Your Excellency."

"Good, good. They truly are stronger than me." Yang Kai beamed, and the heart he had been holding in suspense finally settled. Although finding the little black dog had already led him to suspect that Zhu Qing was safe, confirming it still filled him with relief.

Red hair was the distinctive mark of a Red Dragon. Zhu Lie had clearly arrived at this oasis and then used a Dragon Clan Secret Technique to contact Zhu Qing. Once she arrived, the two had left together.

The middle-aged man added, "This colored ribbon was discarded by that red-haired young man before he left. The child did not know any better and casually picked it up to play with. If this item is useful to Your Excellency, please take it back."

Yang Kai waved his hand. "That won't be necessary. I only wanted to ask a few questions, so none of you need to be nervous."

As he spoke, he flicked his finger, and the colored ribbon immediately flew back to the little girl. She glanced at Yang Kai several times before grabbing it and cupping it tightly in her palms like a precious treasure.

Once they were certain Yang Kai bore no ill intent, the group finally relaxed. They had all sensed his strength and knew that if he truly meant them harm, they would be utterly unable to resist.

"Which direction did that man and woman go? Could you tell me?"

The middle-aged man pointed. "That way!"

Yang Kai gazed into the distance, then cupped his fists. "Many thanks."

Turning around, he called to the little black dog and picked it up. His figure then flickered as he pursued Zhu Qing and Zhu Lie in the direction they had departed.

Seeing him leave, everyone secretly breathed a sigh of relief.

In the very next instant, however, their vision blurred, and the young expert unexpectedly returned.

The middle-aged man asked in surprise, "Does Your Excellency need something else?"

Yang Kai smiled. "I thought it over. Since you helped me, I should repay the favour."

"It was only a trivial matter. There is no need to take it to heart!" The middle-aged man shook his head.

"It may have been trivial to you, but it was a major matter to me. How about this? Whatever request you have, feel free to state it. I can fulfil it for you!"

As soon as he said this, the crowd stirred, and everyone turned to look at the middle-aged man.

The man in charge was clearly tempted as well, but after hesitating for a long time, he still shook his head. "Many thanks for Your Excellency's kind intentions, but we have no requests."

Yang Kai smiled faintly. "Judging from your complexions, you must be facing some difficulty, perhaps even one that threatens your lives. If you refuse to answer even when I ask, that means you are being constrained by someone, or perhaps you have no confidence in me. Has some expert set their sights on you?"

"Nothing of the sort." The middle-aged man shook his head again, looking somewhat nervous. "Your Excellency should hurry on your way. The people you seek left half a month ago. If you do not leave now, I'm afraid you will not be able to catch them."

Yang Kai ignored him and instead looked at the little girl who had picked up the colored ribbon. "You helped me, so now I want to help you. Tell me, has anyone been bullying you? I'll go teach them a harsh lesson so they can never bully you again."

The little girl asked in delighted surprise, "Really?"

"Really."

The little girl said, "Then could you go kill that big snake? It often comes here to eat people. My little brother and little sister were both eaten by it."

"A big snake?" Yang Kai raised his brow.
