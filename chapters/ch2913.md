## Chapter 2913 — The Blood-Red Gate

Yang Tai suddenly produced a mirror-like Secret Treasure in his hand, about the size of a human face. Its purpose was unclear. He swiftly formed a hand seal and sent several streams of Emperor Yuan into the mirror. In the next instant, the mirror shone brightly, shooting out a mysterious beam of light.

Wherever the mysterious light shone, everything became clear. This light seemed to have the power to distinguish reality from illusion—obviously, it was a Secret Treasure used for detection and scouting.

The group of seven continued forward, occasionally passing some stone chambers, but all were empty, with nothing noteworthy. Moreover, there wasn’t much danger in this ancient cave residence, as their journey had been completely safe so far.

Even so, Yang Tai still hadn’t found the path they’d taken last time.

Half an hour later, Yang Tai suddenly stopped. Seeing this, the others halted as well.

“What’s wrong?” Wu Kuangyi asked.

Yang Tai said, “There seem to be traces of a restriction. Let me take a closer look.” As he spoke, he raised the mirror and cast its light forward. Wherever the light passed, faint ripples distorted the air, clearly out of place, though barely perceptible.

Yang Tai urged his Emperor Yuan, pouring even more power into the mirror Secret Treasure, making the mysterious light even stronger.

Now, everyone could see more clearly. There were indeed remnants of a restriction ahead. What was invisible to the naked eye was now fully revealed under the mysterious light of the mirror—strands of energy of varying strength lay across their path, following some profound pattern, forming a barrier.

The method behind this restriction was extremely complex and ancient, clearly passed down from a very distant era.

Wu Kuangyi immediately turned to look at Gong Yue.

Since they’d encountered an ancient restriction blocking their way, naturally, the former Family Head of the Gong family needed to step in. Only he had the deepest understanding of such things.

To everyone’s surprise, Gong Yue merely smiled slightly, making no move to act. He showed no sign of taking the initiative.

Wu Kuangyi was about to speak, but Yang Tai suddenly said, “This should just be a minor restriction. No need to trouble Brother Gong. Let’s break it together.”

As soon as he finished, he raised his hand and struck toward the restriction.

Everyone was startled and quickly activated their Emperor Yuan, staying on guard.

After all, no one knew what effect this restriction might have. Recklessly touching it could trigger some deadly consequence. Yang Tai’s action was certainly a bit rash. However, seeing that Gong Yue didn’t stop him, they relaxed a little.

With a boom, a curtain of light appeared ahead. Suddenly, several enormous heads burst out from the light. These were not human heads, but the heads of bizarre Yao Beasts—more than a dozen in total, each of a unique species, unrecognizable to anyone present.

What made everyone’s faces change was the savage aura radiating from these heads.

As soon as the heads appeared, they opened their bloody maws and lunged at Yang Tai, their momentum fierce, as if they could devour the heavens and earth.

Yang Tai was shocked, finally realizing he’d underestimated the power of the ancient restriction. His Emperor Yuan surged, and he suddenly chanted a harsh incantation, pointing forward with his hand.

A dazzling golden character condensed from his Emperor Yuan and shot from his fingertip, flashing by in an instant—so fast that no one could see what character it was.

Only Yang Kai’s expression changed, his eyes filled with surprise.

Though Yang Tai’s technique was as fleeting as a flower’s bloom, Yang Kai still caught a hint of its nature.

That character… it was clearly an extremely ancient script. He’d seen it before while wandering with his Divine Soul in the Shaman God Hall within the Thousand Illusion Dreamscape.

In ancient times, humans condensed their power into such characters, which possessed world-shattering might. These were even older than the scripts of the shamans, and the records in the Shaman God Hall were vague at best.

Yang Kai suddenly recalled that Hua Yulu had mentioned Yang Tai had learned a secret technique in this ancient cave residence, but she didn’t know what it was or what power it held.

Now, it seemed the secret technique Yang Tai learned was precisely this one.

This was indeed a remarkable secret technique. Though Yang Tai’s cultivation was only at the First Stage of the Emperor Realm, the power of his strike rivaled that of the Second Stage. It seemed that, in a life-or-death moment, he couldn’t think too much and could only unleash his strongest attack to save himself.

Judging by this, the cave residence’s age surpassed even the world Yang Kai had experienced in the Thousand Illusion Dreamscape. No wonder there were treasures from the Primordial Era here.

At the same time, the others also took action. In an instant, the powers of various Secret Treasures surged in the narrow passage, shaking the Spiritual Qi of heaven and earth.

Then, an unexpected scene unfolded.

The heads that had burst from the restriction’s light curtain, though menacing at first, turned out to be all bark and no bite. As soon as they touched Yang Tai’s secret technique, they all disintegrated. Some heads broke apart the moment they emerged, never having any effect at all.

With a rumble, everyone’s attacks struck the light curtain ahead. The curtain merely trembled before bursting like a bubble.

Heavy breathing echoed through the passage, and everyone felt as if they’d just survived a great disaster.

“Heh, this restriction really was falling apart from age.” Yang Tai was the first to speak, looking a bit embarrassed.

Everyone had seen what happened. The restriction was indeed decrepit and collapsed after being triggered. Otherwise, just the heads’ attacks would have thrown them into chaos, not to mention the light curtain shattering so easily.

“Brother Gong, you must have seen through it already?” Yang Tai looked at Gong Yue. “You should have warned me, so I wouldn’t have made a fool of myself.”

Though he complained, there was little real resentment in his tone.

Gong Yue smiled mysteriously, saying nothing.

Wu Kuangyi frowned slightly, clearly annoyed at Yang Tai’s recklessness for making everyone worry for nothing. But since it was just a false alarm, he let it go.

After this encounter, everyone became even more cautious. Though the first restriction had lost its power due to age, who could guarantee the others would be the same?

The leader, Yang Tai, no longer dared to be reckless. He advanced steadily, step by step.

Along the way, they indeed encountered more restrictions, but as they probed each one, they found all were weakened by time, at most flaring briefly before fading. Many didn’t react at all.

Even so, everyone was deeply unsettled.

The restrictions gave off a powerful sense of danger. No one could imagine how terrifying they’d be if intact, or how formidable the one who set them must have been.

Two hours later, Yang Tai’s face lit up with joy. He looked around and exclaimed excitedly, “I’ve found the way!”

Wu Kuangyi and Hua Yulu exchanged a glance and smiled as well.

At last, they’d found the path they’d taken before. As long as they followed it back, they wouldn’t encounter further danger—until they reached the array that had blocked their advance.

Everyone was curious about what might be hidden beyond that array.

Though they’d found the old path, Yang Tai remained vigilant. The mirror’s mysterious light continued to lead the way, exploring ahead.

Another hour passed. Suddenly, they entered a massive stone cavern, spanning several mu. After being confined to narrow passages, the group felt as if they’d emerged into open space.

The cavern was also empty, with only some sharp stone pillars scattered about—some standing, some hanging down—strange and varied, a forest of stone.

A thick, heavy door drew everyone’s attention.

The door was several zhang tall, set seamlessly into the cave wall. Everyone gazed at it, eyes burning, as if some precious treasure lay hidden behind it.

Yang Kai had already seen this door in the image Hua Yulu had shown him, but seeing it in person was a different matter entirely.

Seeing it with his own eyes, Yang Kai felt an intense sense of foreboding, as if something ominous was stirring behind the door.

The door was red, as if forged from fresh blood. Even after endless ages, it remained as vivid as ever.

The door was engraved with intricate patterns and designs, exuding a mysterious aura. These patterns formed an ancient array, sealing the door and preventing anyone from glimpsing the world within.

The seven stood before the door, each with a different expression.

Yang Tai’s face showed an indescribable fanaticism, his lips trembling with excitement for reasons unknown. Wu Kuangyi and Hua Yulu were excited too, but more filled with longing and curiosity about what lay behind the door.

Fang Zhuo’s expression was as indifferent as ever, while Gong Yue narrowed his eyes, studying intently.

Shen Bingru suddenly asked, “Can’t this restriction be broken?”

Wu Kuangyi shook his head and replied, “Last time, the three of us joined forces but couldn’t do anything. If Madam Shen wishes, you can try it yourself.”

Shen Bingru said softly, “No need.”

Though her cultivation was at the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm, stronger than Hua Yulu and the others, she had no interest in wasting effort on something three people together couldn’t manage.

“Brother Gong, everything depends on you now.” Yang Tai suddenly turned, his expression grave, as if entrusting Gong Yue with a matter of life and death.

Gong Yue smiled and said, “I’ll do my best. Please rest for a while, everyone. I’ll study it carefully.”

“Alright.” Yang Tai nodded, then turned and said, “Everyone, take a break. We may need to act soon.”

Yang Kai smiled and said, “With the old Family Head here, why should we get in the way?”

Yang Tai replied, “You never know. We might really be needed.”

“True.” Yang Kai smiled and said no more, giving Hua Yulu a look. The two of them went to the side to rest.
