## Chapter 2819 — Grandpa Qing

Why? Any barbarian would never let such a good thing slip by, so why did it fall to her?

"Because I don't know anyone else." Yang Kai shrugged and smiled. "And since I have leverage over you, I'm not afraid you'll embezzle my property."

Die's lips twitched slightly. What he said was true—so long as this Shaman Niu held her weakness, she could never do anything to harm him.

But with a ten percent cut, none of that really mattered.

After sending Die off, Yang Kai immediately went to Frost Snow City, found the shop he had his eye on before, and spent a large amount of Qing Coins to buy many Inner Cores.

The accumulation over this period had given him enough wealth to purchase Inner Cores to support his own cultivation, so he needed someone to help sell medicine. After all, selling medicine was quite a waste of time.

Returning to the tree hollow, Yang Kai held a dragon-eye-sized Inner Core and let out a gentle breath.

The snowball had already started rolling. From now on, as long as he bought and refined some Inner Cores each day, once this winter passed, he would surely advance to Shaman Master. Yang Kai had no doubt about this.

Swallowing the Inner Core, Yang Kai circulated his cultivation technique. Soon, thunder rumbled in his belly, and his aura swelled at a speed visible to the naked eye.

In the afternoon, Die returned from selling medicine, obediently handing over ninety percent of the profits, then received new potions from Yang Kai to sell the next day. After just half a day apart, Die was surprised to find that this Shaman Niu had already become a Middle-Level Shaman Warrior!

That morning, he had clearly still been a Lower-Level Shaman Warrior. How had he become Middle-Level in just half a day?

Though puzzled, Die didn't ask. Everyone had their secrets, and digging too deeply was never wise—especially since Yang Kai still held hers.

The two cooperated seamlessly.

Each day, Yang Kai set aside some time to refine potions and go out to buy Inner Cores, while Die handled sales and bought back materials.

In a flash, a month passed.

During this month, Yang Kai's cultivation broke through again. Refining over a dozen Inner Cores daily allowed his strength to grow rapidly. Now, he was an Upper-Level Shaman Warrior! As long as he had enough Inner Cores, he could soon reach Shaman Master and gain his own Divine Sense.

By then, he would finally escape his current predicament.

Yet good things never go smoothly.

That day, Die didn't return from selling medicine until dusk. After entering Yang Kai's tree hollow, she set the stone jar in her hands down in front of him.

Yang Kai asked in surprise, "Why didn't you sell out?"

Every potion he refined each day had always sold out completely—this was the first time there were leftovers.

Die replied weakly, "Probably everyone who wanted to buy already has. Haven't you noticed I've been coming back later and later these days?"

Yang Kai paused, thought carefully, and realized it was true.

He had been focused on cultivation, intent on advancing his Shaman rank, and hadn't paid much attention to Die. Now that she mentioned it, he saw she was right. At first, she could sell out his potions in half a day, but as time went on, it took her longer and longer.

Today, she hadn't returned until dusk, and even then, the healing medicine hadn't sold out.

"Has the market saturated..." Yang Kai stroked his chin and muttered to himself.

Frost Snow City was large, and there were many barbarian warriors, but after more than a month of continuous sales, the market had started to shrink. After all, it wasn't easy for the warriors to earn Qing Coins. Though a healing potion worth ten Qing Coins wasn't a burden, they wouldn't buy endlessly.

Normally, everyone would keep one on hand as a backup and only buy a new one after using it up.

So the longer it went on, the less purchasing power the barbarians of Frost Snow City had.

Understanding this, Yang Kai nodded. "Take a rest and sell again tomorrow."

Die had no objections. After a month together, she and Yang Kai had grown familiar, and she was no longer wary or fearful of him. She turned and left.

In the tree hollow, Yang Kai frowned deeply. Now that the market was saturated, his healing potions would become harder and harder to sell. If he couldn't sell them, he wouldn't have enough Qing Coins to buy Inner Cores, which would delay his cultivation plans.

But after thinking it over, he couldn't find a good solution. Unless there was another city like Frost Snow City where he could operate. But in this cold winter, where could he find a second Frost Snow City among the barbarians?

As expected, things went just as Yang Kai had predicted. In the days that followed, healing potions became harder and harder to sell. Even after a whole day, Die couldn't sell even a third of what Yang Kai refined, and the amount kept shrinking.

The number of Inner Cores he could buy each day also dwindled. Though Shaman Master Realm was within reach, without enough Inner Cores, he could only sigh in frustration.

Given the situation, all he could do was take things one step at a time—use up the Inner Cores he had, then make plans when the time came.

One day, while Yang Kai was cultivating, he suddenly sensed something strange, as if a pair of eyes were watching him from the shadows.

It made him extremely uncomfortable. He was at a critical point in his cultivation and didn't want any trouble, so he thought it was just his imagination. But the feeling of being watched only grew stronger.

He opened his eyes abruptly, squinting as he saw a figure standing not far ahead.

It was an elderly man, not with the burly build common among barbarians, but looking frail and ancient, with white hair and beard. He stood there quietly, a faint smile on his lips, studying Yang Kai with great interest.

Outwardly, Yang Kai remained calm, but inside, alarm bells were ringing.

Impossible! He lived in a tree hollow of the Divine Tree, protected by its blessing. Without his permission, not even a Shaman King or Shaman Saint could enter—yet this old man had appeared before him without a sound.

How could such a thing happen!

Still, since the other party showed no hostility, Yang Kai didn't make a fuss and simply stared back quietly.

In the tree hollow, the two locked eyes. The old man didn't rush to speak, but instead scrutinized Yang Kai, occasionally nodding lightly.

Being stared at made Yang Kai uneasy, and he finally couldn't help but say, "Elder, you've trespassed in my home—what are your intentions?"

"Trespassed?" The old man raised an eyebrow, amused. "That's an interesting way to put it. But my being here doesn't really count as trespassing!"

Yang Kai sneered, "This tree hollow is my residence. Entering without my permission is trespassing. Elder, you're not young anymore—maybe your mind isn't so clear. While I'm still being polite, you'd best leave. Otherwise, if I get angry, don't say I don't respect my elders."

The old man smiled slightly. "What, are you going to hit me?"

Yang Kai replied matter-of-factly, "Using force when necessary is one way to solve problems."

The old man pondered for a moment, then nodded in agreement. "You have a point. I didn't expect someone your age to have such insight."

"Enough nonsense," Yang Kai waved his hand. "If you don't leave, I won't be so polite."

The old man hurriedly said, "Don't get worked up. I just woke up and sensed a familiar aura, so I came to take a look. I mean you no harm."

Yang Kai scoffed, "Even wolves can act friendly toward livestock."

The old man gave a wry smile, not knowing how to explain.

Just then, there was a knock outside the tree hollow. Yang Kai turned and saw Die standing outside. Delighted, he quickly let her in.

Though he had never truly fought Die, their time together had shown him she was a formidable Shaman, far above him in cultivation. Last time he knocked her down with one punch, it was only because she underestimated him. If she used her Shamanic Arts, he might not be her match now.

He still didn't know how she had cultivated so well at such a young age.

No matter who this old man was, appearing in his tree hollow for no reason, Yang Kai had to treat him as an enemy. He had worried he couldn't handle him alone, but now that Die was here, things would be easier.

Two against one—even if they still lost, at least they could make a scene and alert the people of Frost Snow Tribe.

Unaware of the situation, Die rushed in and said dejectedly, "I didn't sell anything today..."

As she spoke, she set down the stone jar in her hands.

The next moment, Die's eyes widened as she stared in shock at the white-haired old man in the tree hollow.

Yang Kai shot her a look, but Die ignored him, staring blankly ahead. Soon, a look of joy appeared on her face, and she called out, "Grandpa Qing, why are you here? Did you come to find me?"

As she spoke, she rushed over, affectionately grabbing the old man's arm and smiling sweetly.

Yang Kai was instantly dumbfounded, then shivered violently, goosebumps rising all over...

If Die had acted like this in her normal form, saying such lines with that affectionate look, it would have been nothing—after all, a beautiful girl acting cute is always pleasing.

But right now, she was a burly, broad-shouldered man.

This sudden display made Yang Kai want to gouge out his own eyes. He felt as if he'd been struck inside and out by a full-force blow from a Shaman Saint...

The old man, on the other hand, just smiled and asked, "Why are you here? Do you two know each other?"

Die's face turned a bit unnatural, clearly recalling how she first met Yang Kai. Still, she nodded and said crisply, "We do. We've been working together selling medicine and made quite a few Qing Coins."

"Selling medicine?" The old man glanced at the stone jars, then showed interest. With a wave of his hand—without any visible power—a jar flew into his grasp, and he examined it closely.
