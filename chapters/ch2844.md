## Chapter 2844 — Beast Taming

Shaman Tu's provocation not only failed to achieve its intended effect, but instead further boosted Yang Kai's prestige.

By now, everyone knew that this so-called Shaman Niu, though he appeared thin and frail, was in truth a Great Shaman Master whose strength was unfathomable. Following such a figure promised far greater safety and better prospects than throwing in their lot with anyone else.

Moreover, joining him meant being allocated weapons.

Who wouldn't be tempted by such conditions?

So after Shaman Tu left, Yang Kai's recruitment went incredibly smoothly.

In just the time it takes for an incense stick to burn, another two thousand people joined the ranks, dividing up the remaining weapons among themselves.

Yang Kai did not accept just anyone; he recruited by village units. Each village had two or three hundred people, so gathering two thousand only took eight or nine villages.

Among these people, ninety-nine percent were Barbarian Tribe warriors and archers, with only a scant few being shamans of varying strength.

Die organized the details of these newly recruited two thousand and reported to Yang Kai. Instantly, Yang Kai's subordinates gained several more shamans. Including those who had followed him to the royal city before, he now had as many as thirteen shamans under his command: four Shaman Warriors and nine Shaman Apprentices.

There were no Shaman Masters among them, but that couldn't be helped. Shamans were rare to begin with, and it was difficult for villages to produce Shaman Masters. Only some small tribes with thousands of people gathered together, or the royal city itself, had Shaman Masters.

But this was not a major problem, because Yang Kai still had Die.

Other Great Shaman Masters had Shaman Masters under them, but Yang Kai had another Great Shaman Master. Die was no ordinary Great Shaman Master; after years of guidance from Qing, the Shamanic Arts she mastered were far richer than those of other Great Shaman Masters. Once war broke out, the two of them working together could easily cover three thousand people with Shamanic Arts and still have power to spare.

Next, Yang Kai personally took action, breaking up the three thousand into ten squads, each with two hundred fifty to three hundred people, appointing captains and vice-captains from among the strongest warriors and archers.

He also selected a hundred elite from the three thousand to form a guard unit.

It wasn't that Yang Kai needed a guard unit—with his current strength, he hardly needed Barbarian Tribe protection. The main task of this special guard unit was to protect the thirteen shamans, aside from himself and Die.

These shamans were only Shaman Warriors and Shaman Apprentices. Alone, they weren't much, but in small- to medium-scale battles, they were the linchpin connecting the ten squads. Losing any one of them would be a huge loss.

So Yang Kai had to make extra plans for their safety.

Once the squads were assigned, the three thousand-strong force gained a clear and orderly structure. Each squad had its own captain and vice-captain, who reported directly to Yang Kai, allowing his orders to reach all three thousand people much more effectively.

The surrounding Great Shaman Masters, seeing the benefits, quickly imitated him. Soon, every Great Shaman Master's tribe became orderly and well-organized.

After these measures, the three thousand became utterly obedient to Yang Kai, feeling that this time they had truly chosen the right person to follow. In the coming great war, perhaps they would have an edge over others.

After ordering the three thousand to drill and familiarize themselves with formations and cooperation, Yang Kai and Die quietly left the royal city.

……

"Where are we going now?" Die, not knowing why Yang Kai had pulled her away, asked curiously.

Yang Kai flew ahead, leading the way without looking back. "When I was in the royal city, I saw some tamed Yao Beasts. We're going to catch a few."

"You want to tame beasts?" Die looked at him in surprise.

"Senior Qing taught you beast-taming arts, right?" Yang Kai glanced back at her.

Die nodded lightly. "He did, but I've never used them, so I don't know if they'll work."

"As long as you've learned them. Whether they work or not, we'll find out when we try. I've never used them either," Yang Kai smiled.

"But... do you know where to find barbaric beasts?"

"Just follow me. The ones in the royal city couldn't have appeared out of thin air. If they could catch them, so can we."

With that, Yang Kai pressed forward.

He had already inquired about the place. About half a day's journey from the royal city, there was a mountain range, and within it lay his target.

The two set out at night, and by dawn, a sprawling mountain range appeared ahead, like a giant dragon lying across the land. Entering the mountains, they pressed on without stopping.

After about half a day, a sudden shriek came from above.

Yang Kai paused and looked up. Through the scattered leaves, he saw a massive eagle-shaped Yao Beast swoop down from the sky into a mountain hollow. When it rose again, its sharp talons gripped a giant python, which struggled fiercely, even coiling around the eagle's claws.

The giant eagle lowered its head and pecked at the python's body. Blood instantly rained down from the sky. The python struggled even harder, making the eagle wobble as if it might fall at any moment.

But soon, the eagle opened its beak, and a wind blade-like attack shot out, slicing through the python and cutting it into several pieces. Its innards spilled out, and its writhing body gradually stilled.

The eagle shrieked again as it turned in midair, clutching its prey, and flew off in a certain direction.

"Is that what you want to catch?" Die, seeing Yang Kai watching with interest and excitement, immediately understood his intent.

"Of course!" Yang Kai called Die to follow, chasing after the direction the eagle had flown.

Die looked speechless. She had known Yang Kai wanted to catch barbaric beasts, but hadn't realized his target was flying ones. These were much harder to catch, and judging by the eagle's size and aura, its rank was not low. Her beast-taming arts might not even work.

Though uneasy, she said nothing and followed Yang Kai onward.

After an incense stick's time, they arrived at the foot of a valley. The valley was surrounded by sheer cliffs, jagged rocks everywhere, and among the rocks stood countless giant nests. Nearly every nest had a proud giant eagle standing in it, like kings surveying their domains.

More giant eagles soared in the sky, some out hunting, others returning with prey.

"So many!" Die exclaimed in surprise at the scene before her.

"The more, the better," Yang Kai grinned. In his view, the more the merrier—provided the beast-taming arts worked. If not, it was all for nothing.

"Wait here, I'll give it a try." With that, Yang Kai's figure flickered and vanished.

Die widened her eyes, looking around but unable to spot Yang Kai. Shock appeared on her pretty face—this was definitely not an effect any Shamanic Art she knew could produce.

The more she interacted with Shaman Niu, the more Die realized how unfathomable he was.

As she searched for Yang Kai, she suddenly noticed something odd on the opposite cliff. Looking up, she finally spotted him.

Somehow, he had gotten over there and was now gripping a giant eagle's neck, pinning it in its nest, his body pressed low to avoid being seen by the other eagles.

A faint light flashed and disappeared into the eagle's body.

Die clearly saw the struggling eagle suddenly go still. After a while, it stood up again, stretched its wings, and regained its proud bearing as if nothing had happened.

Success!

Die's eyes lit up. Beast-taming arts were extremely domineering Shamanic Arts that required a great deal of shamanic power to use. Like the Chain of Life, only Great Shaman Masters could master them, and the conditions for use were extremely harsh.

If successful, a brand would be left in the barbaric beast's body, allowing control over its life and death. But if it failed, not only would the Great Shaman Master suffer backlash, but the target would also die instantly.

So usually, to use beast-taming arts on a barbaric beast, it was best to capture it first and spend time building rapport, lowering its guard for a better chance of success.

Doing it as forcefully as Yang Kai had almost always ended in failure.

Yet, he had actually succeeded.

Even after seeing so many miracles from Shaman Niu, Die was still stunned.

She wondered if it had simply been luck.

The next moment, the eagle Yang Kai had subdued suddenly spread its wings and flew straight toward them.

Soon, as the eagle landed before them, Yang Kai, hidden on its back, poked his head out. "I have a great idea. I need your help!"

Die asked blankly, "What idea?"

Yang Kai's eyes sparkled. "I want to catch the Eagle King!"

The corner of Die’s mouth twitched involuntarily.

But after Yang Kai explained, she realized it was indeed a good idea.

After forcibly subduing a giant eagle with beast-taming arts, Yang Kai vaguely sensed its thoughts. From them, he learned that there was an Eagle King here, far stronger than all the others, to whom every eagle submitted.

If they could strike at the heart and subdue the Eagle King, all these eagles would be theirs for the taking.

This was a move that struck at the root, far more effective than Yang Kai's original plan of subduing them one by one.

The two quickly discussed and finalized their plan.

Soon after, the giant eagle took flight once more, with Yang Kai and Die concealed on its broad back. The eagle’s back was spacious enough for the two of them to sit side by side without feeling crowded, and the long feathers draping over them hid their figures completely from view.
