## Chapter 2849 — First Battle

Outside the royal city, the blaring of horns rose and fell in succession. One squad after another marched in neat formation, heading north—where the great battle was about to erupt.

Shaman Niu’s tribe, three thousand strong, stood assembled in silence. Ten regular squads, one Sky Cavalry unit, and one guard squad—twelve squads in all—had gathered in one place, yet not a single voice broke the quiet.

At the front of the formation, two Shaman Masters from the Shaman God Hall, four Shaman Warriors, and nine Shaman Apprentices all looked up, gazing ahead.

Yang Kai sat atop his Eagle King, like a true commander. His sharp gaze swept across the field, and every tribesman straightened their backs, showing their most imposing side.

There was no need for many words. Yang Kai simply waved his hand: “Move out!”

The cry of an eagle rang out. The Sky Cavalry took the lead, spreading their wings and soaring into the sky.

Led by Yu and Lu, the Shaman Warriors and Shaman Apprentices joined forces to cast a large-scale Lightness Art. Radiant energy enveloped the three thousand-strong squad, making every tribesman’s body lighter and more agile.

With a thunderous rumble, Shaman Niu's tribe departed the royal city at great speed. Though their numbers were many, their ranks remained perfectly orderly.

In midair, Yang Kai sat on the Eagle King's back, wasting not a single moment, meditating and cultivating all the while.

Earlier in the royal city, the order from Shaman Dang had been simple. The demons were now fortifying their defenses, constructing defensive lines and clearing the surrounding fields, seemingly to buy time for their strongest experts to recover.

The Barbarian Tribe would not sit idly by. All the great tribes had mobilized, hoping to reclaim the four territories the demons had occupied while their strongest experts recuperated.

On the Southern Barbarian Tribe's side, one or two hundred squads led by Great Shaman Masters would encircle the demons from the south, whittling down their strength and forcing them to retreat and gather. Afterward, the Shaman Kings of each tribe would strike, leading everyone in a decisive battle against the demons.

This strategy made the early stages of the war highly flexible. No Great Shaman Master's squad had any special assignment—whenever they encountered demons, they were to wipe them out. It was complete freedom of action.

This suited Yang Kai perfectly. He still needed to improve his strength, and if he had a special assignment, he would be stretched too thin. As things stood, this was the best situation for him.

As long as he could raise his strength before the final battle, he would surely have a place in the coming battle.

After so long in this ancient world, Yang Kai had unconsciously begun to see things from the Barbarian Tribe's perspective. Drawn into this war, he could no longer remain a mere bystander.

Over one hundred fifty squads led by Great Shaman Masters set out from the royal city. As time passed, squads gradually split off, heading to different destinations.

At first, Shaman Niu's tribe could still glimpse some allied forces nearby, but after ten days' march from the royal city, not a single ally could be seen within the Sky Cavalry's scouting range.

Hundreds of thousands had scattered across this vast land. Many barbarians, with a do-or-die resolve, rushed toward that brutal battlefield.

Ten days later, as Yang Kai meditated atop the Eagle King, he suddenly opened his eyes. Ahead, Ah Hua rode her giant eagle toward him, stopping abruptly and skillfully steadying herself before calmly reporting, “Sir, there is a village ahead.”

“Did you find anything?” Yang Kai asked.

Ah Hua shook her head. “Not a soul.”

Yang Kai nodded. “Let everyone rest there for a while. The road ahead won't be peaceful. Eat and drink your fill, and prepare for a big fight.”

“Yes!” Ah Hua accepted the order and left.

The squad continued forward.

An hour later, they indeed saw a small village. It was a bit larger than Cangnan Village, but not by much. Most of the houses were wooden huts built from timber and stone, and a dirt wall encircled the village.

Once inside, some immediately took up guard around the perimeter, while others started fires and began cooking. Everything proceeded in perfect order.

Though this squad had only recently formed, it was already a well-trained army.

Yang Kai had wanted to find a place to rest, but as he entered the village, he suddenly felt that something was amiss, as if an invisible gaze was fixed on him.

Startled, he quietly released his Divine Sense, carefully probing the village.

To his surprise, he sensed nothing unusual, and that feeling of being watched vanished. Shaking his head, he called Die over. “Tell everyone to be careful. Something might happen.”

Die looked at him in surprise. “Did you sense something?”

Yang Kai replied calmly, “Better safe than sorry. This is no longer a peaceful place.”

Die nodded. “Understood.”

After Die relayed the order, the tribesmen indeed became more vigilant.

Soon, the aroma of roasting meat filled the air. After ten days of marching, with only one full meal a day, even barbarians were feeling the strain. Now, smelling the meat, many stomachs began to rumble.

Before the meat was fully cooked, the tribesmen began dividing it up and eating, each with a look of satisfaction.

Just then, Yang Kai's heart suddenly leapt. Within his Divine Sense, dozens of strange and hidden auras appeared out of nowhere in the village.

There was no time to investigate. A furious shout rang out: “Enemy attack!”

The tribesmen, caught mid-bite, froze for a moment, but quickly reacted—throwing down their food, drawing their weapons, and scanning their surroundings. Over a dozen shamans hurriedly activated their shamanic power, ready to strike at any moment.

In the next instant, accompanied by a strange hissing, wisps of pitch-black energy erupted from beneath the earth, instantly shrouding the entire village.

The tribesmen were alarmed. No one knew what this was, but instinctively, they roused their blood and flesh power, faces full of vigilance.

“Demonic Qi!” Yang Kai's eyes narrowed in shock.

It was Demonic Qi! And not just any, but extremely pure Ancient Demonic Qi!

Though he had heard of demon invasions, Yang Kai had wondered if the demons of this ancient world were the same as the demons he knew. But with no direct contact or clues, he could not be sure.

Now, seeing this pitch-black energy with his own eyes, Yang Kai immediately understood—the so-called demons were indeed the demons he was familiar with.

Years ago, outside Fenglin City, the Ancient Giant Demon had awakened, Demonic Qi had besieged the city, and countless people had been corrupted and turned into demons. Even he himself had been tainted, but thanks to the sealing power from the gold and silver tree, the ancient Demonic Qi was locked in his Dantian. Otherwise, he would have lost his mind long ago.

This kind of Demonic Qi possessed a powerful corrupting nature. Anyone tainted by even a trace of it could be transformed into a half-human, half-demon creature and fall under the control of the Demon Race.

For such a desolate village to hide so much Demonic Qi—it was clearly a trap!

What shocked Yang Kai even more was how deeply the Demonic Qi had been concealed. Even when he probed with his Divine Sense earlier, he had sensed nothing.

Demonic Qi could not appear out of thin air. Even if it instinctively hid underground, someone had to trigger it. Those dozens of unfamiliar auras also caught Yang Kai's attention.

Sweeping his Divine Sense, Yang Kai immediately noticed something amiss.

On the ground, beneath the shroud of Demonic Qi, the earth bulged in places, and those unfamiliar auras emanated from these mounds. The mounds had appeared silently and suddenly, and as they swelled, they transformed into humanoid figures seemingly molded from mud and sand, with complete features. Their skin was as dry as earth, and their bodies exuded an indescribable Demonic Qi.

These were demons with strange innate abilities!

The sudden turn of events threw the three thousand-strong squad into chaos.

No one had ever experienced anything like this. In the darkness, you could not see your hand before your face, and the black energy seemed to stir up evil thoughts, making people itch to slaughter everything in sight.

Shields of shamanic light flared in the darkness, like beacons, guiding the three thousand tribesmen toward the light.

The light radiating from Yang Kai was especially dazzling, spreading out in a ten-zhang radius around him and forming an impenetrable barrier that not a trace of Demonic Qi could breach.

“Focus! Purify!” Yang Kai shouted, waving his hands as circles of milky-white light spread outward.

The dozen or so shamans, caught off guard and at a loss, instantly found someone to rally around and imitated the two shamanic arts.

With this, the difference in skill among the shamans became clear.

The shamanic light Yang Kai cast could envelop the entire village, with some to spare. Die's could cover about half the village, and Yu and Lu together managed about the same. The remaining Shaman Warriors and Shaman Apprentices were much less effective—the weakest Shaman Apprentice could only light up a radius of ten zhang.

The hissing grew louder.

As the shamanic light burst forth, the darkness that had engulfed the world seemed to meet its nemesis, vanishing in great swathes before their eyes. The thick darkness visibly thinned.

The Demonic Qi swirling through the air also darted about, as if alive, but could not escape the dispersing power of the shamanic light.

The situation looked promising, but Yang Kai felt a chill run through him.

The barbarians knew little about Demonic Qi, but he had experienced it firsthand and knew its terror and strength. Just now, nearly all three thousand of his subordinates had been tainted by Demonic Qi, except for the dozen or so who had quickly activated their shamanic shields.

Once tainted by Demonic Qi, there was no escaping transformation into a demon. When that happened, all bonds of kinship would be severed, and only bloodlust and savagery would remain.

As this thought crossed his mind, a shrill scream rang out. Following the sound, Yang Kai felt his heart sink.
