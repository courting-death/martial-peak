## Chapter 2890 — Seven-Colored Mud

The sound of whooshing arrows filled the air. After several rounds of arrow rain, both sides’ cavalry suffered losses, and the dozens of li between them were erased. The cavalry of both races finally clashed, and in an instant, blood splattered and flesh was torn apart.

One after another, riders from both sides were knocked to the ground. Riders who lost their mounts rarely lasted even ten breaths before dying on the spot—this was true for both the Barbarian Tribe and the demons.

Arrows continued to fly, but the targets were no longer the enemy cavalry. Instead, the archers on both sides began shooting at each other. This was a contest of archery skill—whoever was more skilled and agile would gain the upper hand.

The Barbarian Tribe suffered considerable losses. Their archers rode on flying yao beasts, so all their evasive maneuvers depended on their mounts. In contrast, the Demon archers were all flying demons, giving them a clear advantage in mobility.

The dull sound of war drums rang out continuously, as if echoing from ancient times, stirring the blood of every Barbarian Tribe member.

The cavalry on both sides clashed for the time it takes an incense stick to burn, leaving behind fields of corpses and blood-soaked ground.

The ordinary warriors following behind soon crashed together as well. Millions of Barbarian Tribe and Demon warriors, as if by prior agreement, launched a desperate charge at each other. In everyone’s mind was a single thought: kill all the enemies and seize the final victory.

Heaven and earth became a furnace, flesh and blood the fuel, and the flames of war burned ever fiercer.

Shaman Niu's tribe had yet to move, standing quietly at the rear of their formation, watching the battle ahead. The hundred thousand tribesmen could barely restrain their excitement.

“Take this beneath the boundary passage!”

Die suddenly appeared beside Yang Kai, handing him a lump of seven-colored mud.

Yang Kai took it, turned to look at her, and was slightly surprised.

“This is the key to victory!” Die said earnestly.

“Why is it you?” Yang Kai asked her.

Die shook her head without answering and urged, “Go quickly!”

Yang Kai composed himself, having no time for more questions. He gripped the seven-colored mud tightly in one hand, raised the Myriad Sword high in the other, and shouted in a deep voice, “Kill!”

At his command, the hundred thousand members of Shaman Niu's tribe were instantly invigorated, excitement written all over their faces.

They had waited too long. Watching all their kin charge ahead and seeing many fall to the Demons’ slaughter, their hearts had longed to rush forward. Even death was better than standing by and watching.

Yang Kai’s order came at just the right time—or rather, Die’s message did—just as the morale of Shaman Niu's tribe was reaching its peak.

Yang Kai charged ahead on his demonic beast, followed by several thousand cavalry led by Ah Hu. Among the army, hundreds of shamans of various ranks began chanting incantations. First, a nimble Lightness Art enveloped the army, allowing everyone to keep pace with Yang Kai and the cavalry. Then came all sorts of empowering Shamanic Arts.

In an instant, multicolored lights began to flash.

The Myriad Sword remained raised high, with the dazzling sword light Yang Kai unleashed serving as a striking banner that guided Shaman Niu's tribe forward.

He immersed his consciousness in the seven-colored mud in his hand, trying to discern what it truly was.

Die was undoubtedly the messenger mentioned by Shaman Saint Xu. Yang Kai couldn’t understand why it was her, but at this moment, pondering such things was meaningless. What he needed to figure out now was why the key to the war lay in a lump of mud.

Yang Kai couldn’t tell what material the seven-colored mud was made of, only that it was of extremely high grade. In later generations, it would surely be considered a rare heaven-and-earth treasure, the kind whose appearance would inevitably cause a bloody storm.

But that wasn’t the key.

The key was that Yang Kai sensed a vitality within the seven-colored mud—a vitality that felt familiar, though he couldn’t recall where he’d encountered it before. The mud clearly had the special effect of blocking Divine Sense; even holding it in his hand, Yang Kai couldn’t probe the source of the vitality within.

Did the Shaman Saints expect whatever was inside this mud to seal the boundary passage? Yang Kai couldn’t help but doubt, thinking it seemed too much like a joke. Yet, a few days ago, Shaman Saint Xu’s expression hadn’t looked like he was joking.

With the arrow nocked, there was no turning back. Shaman Niu's tribe bore a heavy responsibility, and now was not the time for hesitation. Yang Kai composed himself and led his army forward.

Suddenly, the earth and sky trembled at once, and a world-destroying aura rippled out.

The aura was so powerful that it left everyone feeling horrified and chilled to the bone.

Both sides, locked in bloody combat, froze for a moment and turned to look. Far behind the Barbarian Tribe, a seven-colored giant dragon suddenly soared into the sky, its body stretching over a thousand zhang as it twisted and charged toward the rear of the Demons.

Another terrifying aura erupted. In the rear of the Demons, a pillar of pitch-black energy shot into the sky, racing toward the seven-colored dragon at incredible speed.

The Shaman Saints and Demon Saints had made their move.

The attacks from both sides collided in the sky above the battlefield, producing a deafening roar. Seven-colored and black lights clashed, neither yielding an inch.

The sky was torn open, as if another boundary passage had appeared—a shocking sight.

Everyone felt unsteady, as if the space they occupied had suddenly become a storm-tossed sea, with deadly waves ready to swallow them at any moment.

In that instant, everyone felt incredibly small, their hearts trembling in fear.

The first round between the Saints of both sides ended in a draw. The seven-colored dragon and the black energy pillar vanished, and nine distant, imposing figures suddenly appeared in midair.

Five Demon Saints and four Shaman Saints stared at each other across the void. The Demon Saints wore sinister smiles, while the Shaman Saints’ expressions were indifferent.

Their standoff lasted only a moment before the nine Saints clashed. At first, the battle wasn’t too fierce, as they were wary of the warriors below. But as they ascended, every move they made carried the power to destroy everything.

The nine Saints disappeared from sight, fighting high above the clouds. No one could see how the battle unfolded—only the rumbling from the sky and the occasional burst of dazzling light, like a sun flickering between destruction and rebirth.

The bloodbath between the two races resumed, immediately reaching a fever pitch.

The hundred thousand members of Shaman Niu's tribe plunged into the battlefield of millions like a stone thrown into a lake, stirring only a few ripples before vanishing.

Their allies seemed to have received clear instructions.

Wherever Shaman Niu's tribe advanced, allied forces from all directions converged, charging ahead and using their own bodies to clear a path. Every Demon in their way was slain.

From above, Shaman Niu's tribe looked like an awl stabbing into the battlefield, advancing with unstoppable momentum. More allies joined from the flanks, engaging the Demons in their path and allowing Shaman Niu's tribe to pass quickly and safely.

In half an hour, aside from the few hundred Demons slain by Yang Kai’s sword, Shaman Niu's tribe hadn’t encountered any real fighting. In such a chaotic melee, this was unimaginable.

All the credit belonged to the allies’ swift response.

But half an hour was about the limit.

With the help of their allies, Shaman Niu's tribe advanced a hundred li, but this rapid progress stretched the battle lines. The Barbarians had to fight separately, and fewer allies could come to their aid.

Shaman Niu's tribe was nearly fighting alone.

Yang Kai had anticipated this.

He had dispatched ten Demon Kings to guard the flanks of the tribe, with the strongest, Fei Li and Mo Ke Tuo, assigned to the rear. So even without allied support, Shaman Niu's tribe never stopped advancing—in fact, they moved even faster.

Surrounded on all sides by Demons, the hundred thousand members of Shaman Niu's tribe finally found themselves in the battle they had longed for. Excited, they brandished their weapons and followed Yang Kai, cutting down every Demon they encountered.

“Mo Ke Tuo, Fei Li! You dare betray the Demons? You are a disgrace to our race—today, you die here!”

A furious roar came from ahead. More than twenty Demon Kings gathered, each exuding a powerful aura. The demonic qi they amassed formed an insurmountable wall, blocking Shaman Niu's tribe’s path.

Yang Kai drew his sword, sending a blade of light sweeping toward the Demon Kings. Their expressions changed at its terrifying power, and without hesitation, they scattered in all directions.

The wall of Demon Kings blocking the way was instantly shattered.

Before they could regain their footing, a barrage of Shamanic Arts swept toward them.

The Demon Kings flew into a rage and counterattacked.

Yang Kai’s figure flickered, vanishing from his mount. When he reappeared, he was behind a Demon King. The Myriad Sword slashed down, splitting the Demon King—whose strength rivaled a Middle-Level Shaman King—in two, his entrails spilling everywhere.

Without even glancing, Yang Kai teleported behind another Demon King, beheading him with one strike and shattering his body with a palm.

Like chopping vegetables, Yang Kai struck three times, killing three Demon Kings.

The remaining Demon Kings were horrified. Once, Demon Kings had been lofty, untouchable figures—when had they ever been slain so easily? No one knew what was different about this outsider or why he possessed such overwhelming power.

The Demon Kings’ morale plummeted. If not for the Demon Saints’ orders, they might have turned and fled immediately.

Fortunately, after killing three Demon Kings, the outsider seemed exhausted and had no intention of entangling with them further, returning to his mount.

The remaining Demon Kings exchanged glances, suppressed their fear, and surrounded them once more.

“Cut them off!” Yang Kai shouted in a low voice.

Bao Qi and Shaya, who guarded Yang Kai’s side, looked troubled upon hearing this and hesitated for a moment.

Yang Kai glared at them fiercely.

Only then did Bao Qi and Shaya grit their teeth and leap toward their former comrades.
