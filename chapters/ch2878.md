## Chapter 2878 — The Southern Barbarians Will Not Perish

Sword light swept through everything. Several Demon Commanders, confident in their own strength, were cleaved in two by Yang Kai, flesh and blood flying everywhere. The residual force of the sword qi cleared out a wide swath, reducing all the demons in its path to dust.

Only a single, towering member of the Barbarian Tribe remained standing there.

The light of a Shamanic Art shield flickered around his body, but it could not conceal the gruesome sight of half his body having melted into thick fluid. Before Yang Kai arrived, he had already slain dozens of Demon Generals and Demon Commanders with his own shamanic arts. The fire of life burned madly, supporting his broken body and keeping him upright.

The two locked eyes. The man grinned, "Heh heh, Shaman Niu..."

"Shaman Tu..." Yang Kai looked at the Great Shaman Master before him, his expression complicated.

Just a few months ago, the two had clashed in the royal city of the Southern Barbarian Tribe. Shaman Tu had challenged and provoked him in every way. Now, meeting again after these months, the Great Shaman Master still looked at Yang Kai with the same unfriendly, competitive glint in his eyes.

"I thought you weren't coming, but it turns out you arrived early." Shaman Tu chuckled, paying no mind to the fact that half his body had melted into thick fluid—a result of the Black Sun's eruption. Bones jutted out, even his organs were clearly visible, dissolving and dripping to the ground.

"I just got here," Yang Kai nodded lightly.

"Good!" Shaman Tu still wore a smile. Pain had not robbed him of his reason; instead, he was more lucid than ever. With a serious look, he said, "In these past months, over seven thousand demons have died at the hands of my Shaman Tu tribe. What about you?"

"Not as many as you," Yang Kai replied.

Shaman Tu burst out laughing, "Then I win! Hahahaha!"

He still remembered the wager he had made with Yang Kai before leaving the royal city.

Yang Kai nodded, "You did win." After a pause, he asked, "Can you hold on any longer?"

Shaman Tu shook his head, "No, you can see my state. Besides, all my tribesmen are dead. I'm the only one left of the Shaman Tu tribe. It's time I went to join them."

"Go well!" Yang Kai nodded in respect, placing a hand over his chest.

Shaman Tu raised his skeletal hand in return, then suddenly shouted, "Go find the Shaman King! The Southern Barbarian Tribe will not perish!"

With that, he turned and strode toward the densest cluster of demons. Strange, guttural incantations rang out, and suddenly a brilliant light erupted from Shaman Tu, blazing like a fire and engulfing him completely.

Sacrifice of Life!

This was a forbidden art—one no shaman was permitted to use lightly—yet in this moment, Shaman Tu used it to unleash the final brilliance of his life.

In this ancient era, all barbarians believed that after death, they would return to the embrace of the Barbarian God, their souls journeying to the Shaman God Hall. For them, death was not the end, but another beginning.

Only one kind of person was denied this right: those shamans who performed the forbidden Sacrifice of Life. So, in most cases, even if a Great Shaman died, they would not easily use such a forbidden art. To do so meant losing the right to return to the Barbarian God's embrace and enter the Shaman God Hall—a nearly unbearable fate.

But facing the demons invading his homeland, Shaman Tu had no other choice. Even if he would become a wandering ghost, even if the Barbarian God abandoned him, he would still give his last strength for his people.

The demons, unaware of the truth, closed in around him. Shaman Tu laughed wildly, letting their sharp weapons hack at him, but the fire of life burning within him only grew fiercer.

The demons gathered around him finally sensed something was wrong, but by the time they tried to flee, it was already too late.

With a loud bang, as if a dazzling firework had been ignited, Shaman Tu exploded into a cloud of blood mist, and the demons around him vanished in an instant.

Within a hundred zhang radius—utterly cleansed!

At this moment, Yang Kai was already charging toward the center of the battlefield.

He sensed a familiar aura there, belonging to the Shaman King Dang, though it was weak and faltering. Clearly, Shaman Dang's condition was dire.

After the Black Sun erupted, only a few tens of thousands of the Southern Barbarian Tribe remained. Now, after the demons' onslaught, their numbers had plummeted. One by one, barbarian warriors fell. Without their protection, the shamans could not exert their power. Even with the most exquisite shamanic arts, their frail bodies could not make up for the overwhelming difference in numbers.

Again and again, Great Shamans in desperate straits began chanting the incantations of the Sacrifice of Life. Again and again, people burned their last vitality, dragging varying numbers of demons with them to the grave.

Scattered barbarians were slaughtered to the last. Only the more than ten thousand gathered at the very center still maintained an orderly rhythm. Multicolored light from various Shamanic Arts enveloped them, and each person fought the demons with a look of utter resolve.

The defensive line shrank step by step, and the number of survivors dwindled rapidly.

Yang Kai's sudden charge stunned everyone. Even the demons stared at him in a daze, watching as he carved a bloody path and joined up with the surrounded barbarians.

Breaking into the innermost circle, Yang Kai quickly spotted Shaman Dang.

"My lord!"

Shaman Dang had lost one eye, thick fluid oozing from the socket, his face hideous. His condition was indeed terrible—like Shaman Tu, his body had been corroded by the Black Sun. Several other Great Shaman Masters clustered around him, each looking more miserable than the last.

Shaman Dang raised his single eye. Seeing that it was Yang Kai, he couldn't help but show a bitter smile. "You came too, huh."

Yang Kai knew what he was worried about and quickly explained, "Shaman Niu's tribe didn't come. I came alone."

Hearing this, Shaman Dang immediately looked relieved, nodding repeatedly. "Good, good!" As he spoke, he took out a carved wooden object from his bosom. No one knew what material it was made from, and it looked unremarkable, but Shaman Dang treated it as a treasure, solemnly pressing it into Yang Kai's hand. "This is the sacred relic of my Southern Barbarian Tribe! Take it and leave this place. The Southern Barbarian Tribe must not perish!"

Among the barbarians, every tribe had its own sacred relic. These relics were a spiritual anchor, not some rare treasure, but they symbolized supreme authority. Whoever held the sacred relic could command the entire tribe.

Normally, the sacred relic was always in the hands of the tribe's most powerful Shaman King.

Shaman Dang was the sixth Shaman King. For the Southern Barbarian Tribe's sacred relic to end up with him could only mean one thing: all the other Shaman Kings were dead!

Shaman Dang was the last Shaman King of the Southern Barbarian Tribe, and he knew he would not live much longer.

Of everyone present, Yang Kai was the only Great Shaman Master still unscathed. The sacred relic of the Southern Barbarian Tribe could only be entrusted to him—Shaman Dang had no other choice.

"Can you do it?" Shaman Dang gripped Yang Kai's hand, gazing at him solemnly.

Yang Kai nodded, making a promise: "The Southern Barbarian Tribe will not perish!"

Shaman Dang smiled faintly. "Those words are enough." In a final burst of energy, he regained the presence he had when Yang Kai first met him. The power of the Shaman King awakened within him, and he said in a deep voice, "We will create an opportunity for you to escape. Once you get out, take your Shaman Niu's tribe and seek out the other great tribes. Tell them what happened to the Southern Barbarian Tribe today, and let them take it as a warning."

Yang Kai opened his mouth, wanting to tell him that there was still a Demon Saint here. Shaman Dang probably didn't know that all of this was orchestrated by the Demon Saint, which was why he was so confident about creating a chance for Yang Kai to escape.

If he knew, he probably wouldn't think that way.

Still, Yang Kai didn't say it. If possible, he would have liked to use the Small Xuan Realm to take everyone away.

But even if he wanted to, he couldn't do it. All the barbarians he saw were, like Shaman Tu, being corroded by the Black Sun, their bodies slowly melting—even the strongest Shaman King was no exception.

If he used the Small Xuan Realm, all he could take away would be puddles of thick fluid.

The Chain of Life cast on them could not save their lives; it could only delay death a little longer.

He gripped the sacred relic tightly, more solemn than ever, and said in a deep voice, "The Southern Barbarian Tribe will be reborn in fire. I promise!"

Shaman Dang laughed, then suddenly asked, "Yu and Lu..."

"They're both well," Yang Kai replied. "The war has made them grow a lot. It shouldn't be long before they advance to Great Shaman Masters. Is there anything you want me to tell them?"

"No need!" Shaman Dang waved his hand. "They know their duties. I don't need to worry." He took a deep breath and said, "Are you ready?"

Yang Kai nodded.

Shaman Dang immediately waved his hand. Suddenly, a bolt of lightning shot from his palm, transforming in the blink of an eye into a vivid electric python that crashed into the demons ahead.

Crackling sounds rang out as the demons were thrown into chaos.

The electric python slithered forward, guiding the remaining Southern Barbarians. The incantations of the Great Shamans and the warriors' shouts wove a tragic melody. All the surviving tribesmen, with Shaman Dang at the center, united as one and charged forward, determined to carve out a path to survival.

The barbarians cut through the enemy ranks as easily as slicing tofu, advancing with unstoppable momentum even against forces ten or twenty times their number.

Unfortunately, this momentum could not last. When several Demon Kings arrived together, the Southern Barbarians' advance was halted.

Shaman Dang soared into the air, shamanic power surging wildly, and faced the fierce Demon Kings alone. At the same time, he shouted to Yang Kai, "Find a way to escape!"

He could no longer offer Yang Kai any more protection. Whether Yang Kai could escape now depended on his own efforts. Though hope was slim, Shaman Dang still gave his all.

Yang Kai nodded to him, not intending to reveal any more of his strength here.

From the moment he set foot on this battlefield, Yang Kai had felt a pair of eyes silently watching him.

He didn't need to check to know the owner of that gaze was the Demon Saint, Mo Duo.

So he had been hiding his true strength all along. He had never experienced just how powerful a Demon Saint was, but he knew he was no match right now. If the Demon Saint really made a move, he might not be able to escape alive.
