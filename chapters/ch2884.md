## Chapter 2884 — Death by a Thousand Cuts

The Bone-Eater Tribe was one of the four top tribes of the Barbarians. This tribe was home to a Shaman Saint, and compared to the other Barbarian Tribes, the Bone-Eater Tribe’s people were even more savage and violent.

Before the demons invaded, no other Barbarian Tribe was willing to clash with the Bone-Eater Tribe. Though everyone deeply hated them and, from the bottom of their hearts, refused to acknowledge them as fellow Barbarians, none dared provoke them.

Yet it was undeniable that in this war to resist the demon invasion, the Bone-Eater Tribe played a tremendous role. The Bone-Eater Tribe alone held the entire eastern front, pressing forward step by step, reclaiming lost territory and slaying countless demons.

The people of the Bone-Eater Tribe proved the mettle of the Barbarian Tribe with their own fearless bravery. At this moment, their savagery and violence became powerful weapons, making many demon experts aware of their existence and compelling them to pay close attention.

In Cang’er Valley, one of the Bone-Eater Tribe’s strongholds, more than a hundred thousand tribe members had gathered. No less than five Shaman Kings presided here. Close to the front lines, this was one of the most important war encampments for the Bone-Eater Tribe and even the entire Barbarian Tribe.

Since the Bone-Eater Tribe's hundred-thousand-strong force had been stationed here, they had clashed with a demon army a hundred li away more than six times, winning more than they lost and achieving impressive results.

However, for some reason, in the past month, those demons seemed to have quieted down a lot, leaving the Bone-Eater Tribe with little to do.

Today, the entire Cang’er Valley was immersed in a lively, clamorous atmosphere. Nearly everyone had gathered at the center of the valley, gazing at the hastily erected high platform, their eyes burning, faces excited.

Only when presented with the most delicious and novel food would the Bone-Eater Tribe’s people show such expressions.

On that high platform stood a thick wooden stake. A tall figure was bound to it by all manner of Shamanic Arts, unable to move. Strange and complex patterns were carved into the stake, and from those patterns, torrents of lightning would occasionally surge, coursing through the bound figure, making him convulse and twist in agony, his face contorted as if enduring immense pain.

All the Bone-Eater Tribe’s people stared feverishly at that tall figure. Some even licked their lips from time to time. If not for knowing the person’s true identity, someone might have already rushed up to bite off a piece of flesh to taste.

That was a Demon King! A Demon King of considerable strength!

He had been captured and brought back by several of the tribe’s Shaman Kings working together. Although the Bone-Eater Tribe had no taboos about eating people—in fact, they had done so for many years—no one dared harbor such thoughts toward a Demon King.

No one knew if eating a piece of Demon King flesh would cause them to be corrupted by Demonic Qi and turn into a demon themselves. Yet even this fear could not suppress the restless desire deep in their hearts.

The more they thought about it, the more they wanted to taste Demon King flesh, to know what would happen to them after eating it.

But for now, the ordinary tribe members could only indulge in fantasies, for a Shaman King already stood beside the Demon King.

That was Yang Kai’s old acquaintance, Shaman Chi!

Shaman Chi held a dagger forged from unknown materials, his face twisted with madness. He wielded the dagger, slicing piece after piece of flesh from the Demon King. His large hands were astonishingly nimble, each cut removing only a thin sliver of flesh—never too much, never too little.

Many Bone-Eater Tribe members were well-versed in eating living people, for to them, fresh flesh was far more delicious than the rotting meat of the dead. And if they could enjoy the struggles and screams of their food while eating, that was the greatest pleasure of all.

As one of the best among them, Shaman Chi especially delighted in this.

As the first piece of flesh was sliced off, Bao Qi’s expression did not change. He simply stood there coldly watching Shaman Chi, as if the flesh being cut away was not his own. As a Demon King, he naturally possessed a powerful will and great endurance. He had previously submitted to Yang Kai only because he had been forcibly branded with a Soul Brand and could not resist.

He truly did not fear the tricks of this Shaman King before him.

But Bao Qi clearly knew too little about the Bone-Eater Tribe.

When Shaman Chi, face full of excitement, stuffed that piece of flesh into his mouth and chewed it with gusto, showing an expression of ecstasy, Bao Qi’s face finally changed.

Slicing flesh from the bone was nothing. But Shaman Chi’s act of swallowing the flesh raw was clearly beyond Bao Qi’s expectations. His own flesh, now being chewed in the mouth of this Shaman King, made a smacking sound. Even among the demons, there were no such freaks.

Over a hundred thousand Bone-Eater Tribe members stared eagerly at Shaman Chi. Some kept swallowing their saliva, desperate to know if Demon King flesh was truly delicious and how it differed from what they had eaten before.

The look of ecstasy on Shaman Chi’s face never faded. He closed his eyes as if savoring the world’s finest delicacy.

After a while, he finally gulped down the chewed flesh and shouted, “Delicious!”

The tribe members erupted into a frenzy, each one howling and crying out as if possessed by wild fervor.

Shaman Chi turned to look at Bao Qi, a cruel smile on his lips. “It’d be a shame if you died too quickly. Demon Kings are hard to catch. You have to let all my people have a taste!”

Bao Qi’s face twitched slightly.

Though he hadn’t counted, he knew there were at least a hundred thousand Bone-Eater Tribe members here. In other words, this Shaman King intended to slice off a hundred thousand pieces of flesh before allowing him to die.

A Demon King’s vitality was incredibly strong. Even if a hundred thousand pieces of flesh were cut from him, Bao Qi would not die. But the prospect of such prolonged torment made the pain all the more unbearable.

Shaman Chi grinned savagely and struck again. Soon, another piece of flesh was sliced from Bao Qi and tossed backward without looking. “This is your punishment, demons! My Bone-Eater Tribe will eat every last one of you, so you’ll never dare set foot in our land again!”

That thin piece of flesh flew into the crowd, immediately causing chaos. Many Bone-Eater Tribe members fought fiercely over it, heads broken and blood flowing. In the end, a female tribe member seized it, swallowed it without hesitation, and, while crouching on the ground to endure the beating from her fellows, shouted excitedly, “Delicious, delicious! It’s so delicious!”

“See that? These are my Bone-Eater Tribe people! You demons are nothing!” Shaman Chi was not the least bit bothered by the commotion below. Instead, he was proud, grinning fiercely.

Bao Qi remained silent. Had he still stood with the demons, he could have retorted, but he had long since been subdued by Yang Kai. Though a Demon King, he had always served the Barbarian Tribe.

The sad thing was that now, having been captured by the Barbarian Tribe, he would have to endure a hundred thousand cuts before dying. He had tried to explain, but no one in the Bone-Eater Tribe believed him.

“Hold on, you must hold on!” Shaman Chi slashed again. “Don’t die so easily. You’re a Demon King—don’t disgrace the demons!”

Another piece of flesh was sliced off and thrown into the crowd by Shaman Chi, causing another uproar.

Next, Shaman Chi’s movements clearly sped up. He truly had excellent technique; with each slash, the blade flashed almost continuously, each glint marking another piece of flesh flying out, prompting the tribe members below to scramble for it.

In less than half the time it takes to burn a stick of incense, half of Bao Qi’s arm had vanished, exposing stark white bone. During this time, he had endured at least three thousand cuts.

Even as strong as a Demon King, he could not help but tremble, cold sweat pouring from his forehead. The intense pain made him clench his teeth tightly.

A dozen or so zhang away from the gathering of over a hundred thousand Bone-Eater Tribe members, more than twenty thousand others watched silently. These people were clearly different from the Bone-Eater Tribe, for they looked like prisoners. Around them, Bone-Eater Tribe experts kept watch, ready to scrutinize the slightest disturbance.

These were the people of Shaman Niu’s tribe, led by the twins Yu and Lu. They too watched the wooden platform, many showing looks of pity.

They all knew Bao Qi had already submitted to Yang Kai. Many of their victories had been thanks to Bao Qi. Though his demon identity made it hard for the tribe to accept him as a companion, they had indeed fought side by side.

Now, seeing Bao Qi endure such torment, many felt pity. Killing should be swift and clean, but the Bone-Eater Tribe’s Shaman King was simply too cruel.

Yu walked over to a Bone-Eater Tribe Great Shaman, stood behind him, and said through gritted teeth, “I want to see the Shaman King!”

The Great Shaman turned his head, glanced at her, and sneered without responding.

“I want to see the Shaman King!” Yu repeated in a low voice.

The Great Shaman looked at her coldly. “Get back!”

Yu did not move and said, “Bao Qi was subdued by Shaman Niu. He is now fighting for our Barbarian Tribe. You cannot kill him!”

The Great Shaman sneered, “He’s a Demon King. A Demon King can’t possibly be subdued by Shaman Niu. You’ve all been fooled by him.”

“Shaman Niu will explain everything. Release him first. When Shaman Niu arrives, you’ll understand.”

“Shaman Niu?” The Great Shaman showed a trace of a disdainful smile. “Who’s that? Never heard of him.”

“Shaman Niu is the Great Shaman Master of my Southern Barbarian Tribe. He—”

“Southern Barbarian Tribe?” The Great Shaman pretended to think, then showed a look of realization. “But the Southern Barbarian Tribe has already been wiped out!”

“As long as we’re here, the Southern Barbarian Tribe is not extinct!” Yu said through gritted teeth.

“The Southern Barbarian Tribe isn’t extinct!” a group behind her shouted, each showing righteous indignation as they stood behind Yu, glaring angrily at the Bone-Eater Tribe’s Great Shaman.

Since the Black Sun’s catastrophe a month ago, the destruction of hundreds of thousands of the Southern Barbarian Tribe had spread far and wide. The people of Shaman Niu’s tribe naturally knew. They might be the last remaining group of the Southern Barbarian Tribe. As long as they lived, the Southern Barbarian Tribe would not perish.
