## Chapter 2909 — Gong Yue

They did not wait long—barely half an hour had passed—when the six present suddenly sensed a strange and powerful aura flying toward them. Wu Kuangyi and Hua Yulu frowned inwardly, but Yang Tai’s face lit up with joy as he looked in that direction.

Moments later, a middle-aged man with graying hair arrived before them, standing in midair and nodding gently to the group.

“Brother Gong!” Yang Tai cupped his fists, his enthusiasm overflowing.

“Thank you for waiting, Brother Yang.” The elder surnamed Gong replied with a gentle smile, clearly quite familiar with Yang Tai.

“It’s nothing, it’s nothing. We only just assembled.” Yang Tai chuckled. It was true—they had waited here for Yang Kai for several days, and only when Yang Kai arrived did they truly come together.

Turning to Wu Kuangyi and the others, he said, “This is the one I was waiting for. Brother Wu, do you know who he is?”

Wu Kuangyi’s frown deepened as he glanced at him, recalling what Yang Tai had said earlier and vaguely guessing, “Your surname is Gong—could you be related to the Gong family of Tianhe Valley?”

At these words, Hua Yulu’s eyes brightened with interest as she sized up the elder surnamed Gong.

If this middle-aged elder truly came from the Gong family, he would indeed be a great asset.

Yang Tai laughed, “Brother Wu, you are indeed well-informed. Brother Gong is indeed from the Gong family of Tianhe Valley.”

Wu Kuangyi said coldly, “The Gong family of Tianhe Valley is renowned far and wide. Though I am not well-versed in the world, I have heard of them.” He was still somewhat annoyed by Yang Tai’s earlier actions, feeling that Yang Tai had broken their agreement. Looking up at the elder, he asked, “How should we address you?”

The elder surnamed Gong smiled slightly. “Gong Yue.”

“Gong Yue…” Wu Kuangyi pondered for a moment, then his expression changed and he cupped his fists. “So you are the former Family Head of the Gong family! I have been discourteous!”

He had clearly heard of Gong Yue’s name and knew his background. He dared not act arrogantly and showed him due respect.

Gong Yue waved his hand with a smile. “I am but a wandering old man now, traveling wherever I please. There’s no need for such formality.”

Yang Tai said, “Some days ago, I visited the Gong family in Tianhe Valley to consult about the restriction formation. I happened to meet Brother Gong as he emerged from seclusion. When he saw the formation, he was quite taken with it and wanted to come see for himself. I could hardly refuse his request, so that’s how today’s meeting came about. I apologize to everyone—Brother Wu, please don’t be upset.”

Wu Kuangyi replied coldly, “It’s nothing, Brother Yang, you worry too much.”

Gong Yue said, “It was I who came uninvited. If you wish to blame someone, blame me. Brother Yang was forced into this. Of course, I am only interested in that ancient restriction formation and wish to see it with my own eyes. If I can help unravel it, all the better. But if any of you feel it’s inappropriate, I can always return home.”

The Gong family of Tianhe Valley was not a top-tier force, but it was still quite remarkable. Its fame was tied to formations, as the family specialized in their study. There were many formation masters among them, and many sects in the Southern Territory would invite members of the Gong family to help set up formations.

Hua Yulu had also considered visiting the Gong family to consult about the ancient cave residence’s restriction formation, but it was only a passing thought.

Now it seemed she was not the only one with that idea—Yang Tai had thought the same, and he seemed to be quite familiar with Gong Yue, which was why he could speak so freely.

The Gong family was obsessed with formations, especially rare ancient ones. It was understandable that Gong Yue would come uninvited upon hearing of such a formation.

Realizing this, everyone present immediately understood why Yang Tai had said what he did earlier. If it was Gong Yue, he would indeed be a great help on their treasure-seeking journey. He was the former Family Head of the Gong family in Tianhe Valley, with peerless mastery of formations—few in the world could match him. With his help, unraveling the ancient formation should not be a problem.

Wu Kuangyi pondered for a long time, glancing at Hua Yulu and exchanging a few words with her via Divine Sense.

He did not hide it, so everyone could see clearly and guess what they were discussing.

After a moment, Wu Kuangyi spoke. “May I ask, Former Family Head Gong, did you come here solely for the ancient formation? You must know what we intend to do. If we gain any benefits in the ancient cave residence…”

Gong Yue immediately replied, “Formations are my Dao, my only pursuit, my eternity!”

That answer was enough. There were three thousand Daos in the world, and Gong Yue pursued the Dao of formations. No matter how precious other things were, they could not compare to a rare ancient formation.

Yang Tai laughed. “Brother Gong only wants to see the formation and help unravel it. He has no other demands. Of course, if anyone feels uneasy, as Brother Gong said, he can always return home and will not force anyone. But that’s only if someone among us can break the formation—otherwise, we’ll all leave empty-handed.”

He, Wu Kuangyi, Hua Yulu, and the others had prepared for half a year—not just to find a reliable helper, but also to make many preparations for the formation during that time.

Hua Yulu had wanted to visit the Gong family in Tianhe Valley, but a few months ago Yang Kai took charge of the formation matter, so she did not go.

As for Wu Kuangyi, the cold-faced man he had invited clearly had some knowledge of formations, for Wu Kuangyi was now looking at him, as if seeking his opinion.

The man named Fang Zhuo was silent for a long time, then nodded gently.

Wu Kuangyi understood and said, “Since no one has any objections, let us invite Former Family Head Gong to join us. We will be relying on you for this journey.”

“Not at all, I will do my utmost,” Gong Yue replied with a smile, stroking his beard.

At that moment, Yang Kai stepped forward and asked, “Are you truly Former Family Head Gong? Can you prove your identity?”

At these words, everyone’s expression changed slightly, and even Wu Kuangyi seemed to snap awake.

The reason he trusted the man’s claim to be Gong Yue was, first, because he had heard of Gong Yue and knew he was the former Family Head of the Gong family, and second, because Yang Tai’s words had planted the idea in his mind, making him believe it without question.

Now that Yang Kai brought it up, he suddenly became alert.

He had never seen Gong Yue and did not know what he looked like. The man before him might truly be Gong Yue—or perhaps not. If he really was the former Family Head of the Gong family, he could be trusted, but if he was an impostor, they would have to be careful.

With this in mind, Wu Kuangyi glanced meaningfully at Yang Tai.

Yang Tai still wore a smiling face as he turned to Yang Kai. “Young Brother Yang Jiu, you must be joking. Who would dare impersonate Brother Gong? That question is hardly appropriate.”

“If you don’t understand something, you should ask. What’s wrong with that?” Yang Kai replied, a smile tugging at his lips.

Yang Tai frowned and said, “I stake my reputation on Brother Gong truly being the former Family Head of the Gong family in Tianhe Valley.”

Yang Kai only smiled, saying nothing. Yang Tai also realized his guarantee carried little weight—he had never even met Yang Kai before, so how could he vouch for anything? Seeing Wu Kuangyi growing suspicious, he pulled a bitter face. “Ah, what should we do? Should we go to Tianhe Valley to verify?”

This was obviously impossible. Tianhe Valley was far from here, and a round trip would take at least two months—who had the time to waste like that? His suggestion was no different from saying nothing at all.

The more Yang Tai spoke, the more suspicious Wu Kuangyi became, his eyes darting between Yang Tai and Gong Yue, trying to discern something. But to his disappointment, Yang Tai showed nothing unusual, and Gong Yue, though questioned, remained calm and dignified, displaying the bearing of a true master.

This made him wonder if Yang Jiu was simply being overly cautious.

Gong Yue said, “I do have a token of the Gong family, but even if I show it, it may not help. Since this young brother has doubts, I should prove myself. But how should I do so? Do you have a suitable method?”

Yang Kai smiled. “Since this is about formations, let’s use formations as proof. Who here has studied formations?”

At these words, everyone vaguely understood his meaning.

Wu Kuangyi immediately looked at Fang Zhuo.

Though Fang Zhuo was a man of few words, his mind was sharp. He instantly grasped Yang Kai’s intent, stepped forward, and silently took out several formation flags. With a series of hand seals, he tossed the flags into the void, where they vanished from sight.

His movements were smooth and fluid—he clearly had some skill in formations, perhaps even considerable mastery.

Gong Yue’s smile did not fade; he seemed to have guessed Fang Zhuo’s plan and let him proceed.

As Fang Zhuo worked, a large area of space suddenly twisted. When he placed the last flag in position, the area where Gong Yue stood changed, and he vanished completely.

Everyone sent out their Divine Sense to investigate, but only felt a formation blocking their senses—nothing could be seen with the naked eye.

“Oh? So Brother Fang Zhuo is also skilled in formations?” Yang Tai said in surprise.

Fang Zhuo, ever terse, replied, “I know a little.”

He then clasped his hands behind his back and squinted into his own formation.

This was clearly a trapping formation. It had no killing power, but could confine someone in place, leaving them unable to move.

If Gong Yue, trapped within, was truly the former Family Head of the Gong family, breaking free should not be difficult. But if he was an impostor, he would surely lack the necessary skill.

This test was simple and direct. While it could not fully confirm Gong Yue’s identity, it would at least dispel their doubts.

For a moment, everyone held their breath, focusing intently on the formation, eager to see the outcome.
