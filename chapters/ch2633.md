## Chapter 2633 — A Grand Gift

Not long after, Liu Qianyun arrived.

She and Yang Kai had come to the Star Realm in the same batch. Although they hailed from different Star Domains, they had supported each other for a time in Biyu Sect as fellow disciples. They had even survived life and death together in the Jixu Secret Realm, so their bond was naturally deeper than most.

After leaving the Jixu Secret Realm, Liu Qianyun stayed in Ice Heart Valley, which was a good place for her.

She came to see Yang Kai for no other reason than that they had not met in years and she missed him dearly.

After a pleasant chat, Yang Kai learned that she was doing well. She had formally become a disciple under Bing Yun, becoming Bing Yun’s last disciple.

She was certainly qualified for this. After all, like Yang Kai, she was a cultivator who had fought her way up from the lower Star Domain. Back in the Great Wilderness Star Domain, her aptitude was in no way inferior to Yin Lesheng’s; the only difference was her background and foundation, which was why she was a bit weaker than Yin Lesheng.

Yin Lesheng was able to become the personal disciple of the Netherworld Sect Master, so Liu Qianyun could naturally do the same.

Over the years, her cultivation had progressed slowly, mainly because life was hard and she lacked cultivation resources.

Now, as Bing Yun’s last disciple, she had ample resources and a good teacher. Her potential was finally beginning to show. In just a few years, she had advanced to the Second Stage of the Dao Origin Realm and was striving for the Third Stage. Although her cultivation as a Senior Aunt was not as high as many of her Junior Nephews, her future was bright.

In Ice Heart Valley, she got along well with her many sisters, so she already regarded this place as her home.

Learning all this, Yang Kai felt relieved.

Afterwards, Liu Qianyun asked Yang Kai about his experiences over the past few years.

There was not much to say. Most of these years had been spent in the Shattered Star Sea. After leaving, he had hurried to the Eastern Territory’s Wild Ancient Land to look for Xiaoxiao, where he encountered the Four Great Saint Venerables.

Yang Kai picked a few things to talk about, and Liu Qianyun listened with great interest. She found that no matter when, Yang Kai’s life was always so exciting and full of ups and downs.

“By the way, Junior Sister, I have something for you,” Yang Kai suddenly remembered and said.

Liu Qianyun asked curiously, “What is it?”

Yang Kai said seriously, “Relax your body and mind, don’t resist.”

Hearing this, Liu Qianyun gently nodded, took a deep breath, and relaxed completely, even closing her eyes.

At this moment, she had no defenses against Yang Kai. In other words, if Yang Kai wanted to harm her, he could have killed her easily.

If she didn’t trust Yang Kai completely, she would never have done this.

Yang Kai searched in the Small Xuan Realm for a moment. Soon, a hazy ball of light appeared in his palm. As soon as it emerged, it radiated a mysterious aura of Origin. The ball of light seemed filled with countless stars, like a miniature starry sky.

Suspicious, Liu Qianyun suddenly opened her eyes.

When she saw what was in Yang Kai’s hand, she was stunned. “Senior Brother Yang, what is that?”

Yang Kai solemnly replied, “Stellar Origin!”

Liu Qianyun’s body trembled as she exclaimed in shock, “Stellar Origin?”

Coming from the Great Wilderness Star Domain, she naturally knew what a Stellar Origin was. It was the foundation of a star. Such a vibrant Stellar Origin could only be born from a complete Cultivation Star after countless eons. Once a star lost its Stellar Origin, it would become a Dead Star.

In the Star Domain, only by refining the Stellar Origin of a Cultivation Star could one become a Star Lord.

But becoming a Star Lord was not so simple. Even those at the Third Stage of the Void King Realm might not be able to refine it safely. Across the countless Star Domains, many Void King Realm experts had perished trying to refine a Stellar Origin.

Back then, in the entire Great Wilderness Star Domain, there were only a handful of Star Lords. Yin Lesheng was one of them.

Liu Qianyun had once thought of refining a star’s Origin Power to become a Star Lord, but in the Great Wilderness Star Domain, the Netherworld Sect controlled everything. She had no way to attempt it.

Only after coming to the Star Realm did she realize how rare Stellar Origins were here—ordinary people could hardly ever see one.

She had thought she would never have any connection to a Stellar Origin in her life, yet now one appeared before her eyes.

“Did you get this from the Shattered Star Sea?” Liu Qianyun asked, her voice trembling.

“Yes!” Yang Kai nodded.

In the Shattered Star Sea, he had gained many benefits: comprehending Years Like a Shuttle, obtaining the True Phoenix Fire and the Mountain and River Bell, and witnessing the battle between two Great Emperors, Hongchen and Wu Kuang. This Stellar Origin was one of those gains.

“No, no, this is too precious. I can’t accept it,” Liu Qianyun quickly shook her head and refused.

Yang Kai smiled, “Why not? It’s just a Stellar Origin. We met when we were nobodies, and now that your Senior Brother has achieved something, can’t I give you a gift?”

Although Liu Qianyun was tempted, she still shook her head firmly. “Senior Brother Yang, if it were anything else, I wouldn’t refuse. But a Stellar Origin... I can’t accept it. You should refine it yourself. If you do, your cultivation will progress much faster. I’ve heard that those who refine a Stellar Origin become extremely attuned to the laws of heaven and earth!”

“Of course I know that. But I’m already a Star Lord. Didn’t you know?” Yang Kai smiled faintly.

Back when he first met Liu Qianyun in the Starlight Passage, he had already revealed his identity as a Star Lord. He had long since refined a complete Stellar Origin, and his rapid cultivation over the years owed much to this.

Liu Qianyun said, “I know you have one, but you could refine a second. Although it’s not as effective as the first, it would still help you.”

Yang Kai shook his head. “Since I’m giving it to you, I won’t take it back. Besides, I have plenty of Stellar Origins!”

“Plenty...” Liu Qianyun was dumbfounded, unable to believe it.

She knew Yang Kai had gone to the Shattered Star Sea and had heard from Zi Yu that there was a sea of origins there, where many people obtained complete Stellar Origins. But even the most fortunate only got three or four. Yang Kai saying he had many was surely just to comfort her.

She had no idea that Yang Kai had obtained not just three or four, but thirty or forty Stellar Origins.

The complete Stellar Origins stored in the Small Xuan Realm were still there. As for their use, Yang Kai had his own plans. Giving one to Liu Qianyun was nothing.

“Take it.” As Yang Kai spoke, he directly pushed the ball of Stellar Origin toward Liu Qianyun, activating the Star Refining Technique so she had no chance to react before it merged into her body.

Liu Qianyun let out a muffled groan, her face showing some discomfort.

Stellar Origins were not so easy to refine. In the Shattered Star Sea, those who seized them would first seal them in their bodies, suppressing them with their cultivation, and only refine them slowly after returning. Whether they could succeed was uncertain.

It was possible to be devoured by the Origin Power during refinement, drawing the laws of heaven and earth and dying from an exploded body.

“I’ll teach you a technique. Listen carefully. With its help, you’ll refine the origin with half the effort and little risk,” Yang Kai said solemnly.

At this point, Liu Qianyun knew she could not refuse, so she sat up straight and listened intently.

Yang Kai taught her the Star Refining Technique and warned her not to reveal it. Liu Qianyun naturally agreed.

After spending most of the day memorizing the Star Refining Technique, Liu Qianyun stood up and said, “Senior Brother Yang, I’ll go refine the Origin now. If you need anything, just call. There are disciples outside.”

“I know. Go ahead,” Yang Kai smiled.

After sending Liu Qianyun off, Yang Kai returned to his room, sat cross-legged, and took out Source Crystals to cultivate.

On the third day, he suddenly opened his eyes and called out, “Come in.”

Soon, light footsteps approached, and the door was pushed open. A pretty female disciple respectfully asked, “Senior Uncle Yang, do you have any instructions?”

Yang Kai said, “I want to see Senior Bing Yun.”

“Please follow me, Senior Uncle Yang,” the disciple replied, leading the way.

To Ice Heart Valley, Yang Kai was not only a guest but also their closest friend. Orders had long been given that whatever Yang Kai requested, the disciples must fulfill.

Now, if he wanted to see Bing Yun, he didn’t even need to notify her in advance.

Soon, under the female disciple’s guidance, Yang Kai arrived in front of a palace.

As soon as he stopped, Bing Yun’s voice came from inside, “Yang Kai is here? Come in and speak.”

She had clearly already sensed him.

The female disciple gestured, and Yang Kai nodded and stepped inside.

This palace was likely Bing Yun’s usual place for closed-door cultivation. The decorations were not luxurious, but rather simple.

As Yang Kai entered, he saw Bing Yun stepping out from an inner room, with Ji Yao following closely behind.

Their eyes met, and Yang Kai felt a bit guilty. He forced a smile at Ji Yao, but her expression was indifferent, betraying no emotion.

“Greetings, Senior,” Yang Kai cupped his fists and saluted Bing Yun.

Bing Yun smiled slightly. “Sit and let’s talk.” She then instructed Ji Yao, “Yao’er, serve tea.”

“Yes!” Ji Yao responded and immediately began preparing.

Yang Kai sat down, feeling somewhat uneasy.

He hadn’t expected Ji Yao to be here, but thinking about it, it made sense. The master and disciple had not seen each other for thousands of years, so now that they were reunited, Ji Yao naturally stayed by Bing Yun’s side to fulfill her filial duty.

Soon, two cups of steaming fragrant tea were ready. Ji Yao first served Bing Yun, then brought a cup to Yang Kai and set it down. “Senior Brother Yang, please have some tea.”

“Thank you!” Yang Kai nodded in thanks.

He really didn’t know what Ji Yao was thinking. Would she settle accounts with him later? From her expression, he couldn’t tell at all. She treated Yang Kai as if he were a stranger, with none of her former warmth.
