## Chapter 2846 — Things in the Sky

Above the royal city, seventy-five giant eagles soared through the sky, all except for the Eagle King. Each eagle carried a single archer on its back. These archers clung tightly to the feathers on the eagles’ backs, their faces pale, and some even vomited midair, yet they all stubbornly held on.

They were not shamans and had never experienced flight at such heights. Suddenly riding giant eagles into the sky, it was only natural that they found it hard to adjust.

But they were all members of the Barbarian Tribe, born with remarkable resilience and adaptability. Given a few more days, they would surely adjust.

These were the Sky Cavalry under Yang Kai’s command. Ah Hua from Cangnan Village was the captain of this Sky Cavalry unit.

Appointing Ah Hua as the Sky Cavalry captain was not a matter of favoritism on Yang Kai’s part, but the result of competition among the archers. Every barbarian archer was a sharpshooter, with excellent eyesight, able to shoot a mosquito’s wing from a hundred zhang away. With such skills, none were willing to be subordinate to others.

When the Sky Cavalry was formed, every archer eyed the captain’s position hungrily. Yang Kai said nothing more, simply letting them compete in archery. Whoever was the best would be the captain of this Sky Cavalry unit.

After a round of rigorous selection, the chosen archers underwent a field trial. When they returned, Ah Hua wore a proud expression, and the others were all convinced.

Yet even now, as captain, Ah Hua struggled with the Sky Cavalry’s training. Every time she came down from the sky, she would collapse on the ground, taking a long time to recover.

In Yang Kai’s camp, there was a newly built wooden hut, extremely simple in construction, but at least it kept out the wind and rain.

At this moment, Yang Kai and Die were inside, sitting in meditation and cultivating.

Slowly withdrawing his Divine Sense, Yang Kai nodded and said, “Ah Hua and the others are improving quickly. In another two days, they should be ready for battle.”

Die opened her eyes and glanced at him. “I wonder what the demons are like. The Sky Cavalry may be agile, but they’re too obvious a target. If the demons have long-range attacks, this Sky Cavalry might not be of much use.”

Yang Kai smiled slightly. “Being able to scout the enemy is already their greatest use!”

Just then, the voice of that old shaman apprentice came from outside: “Lord Shaman Niu…”

Before he could finish, Yang Kai’s face darkened as he shouted, “Tell them to get lost! I don’t want to see a single one!”

Ever since Yang Kai brought back more than seventy giant eagles two days ago, the other Great Shaman Masters had come visiting one after another, all full of enthusiasm, as if they had known Yang Kai for years. They even brought small gifts, presenting them politely.

Normally, the gifts prepared by Great Shaman Masters would not be ordinary, but to the wealthy Yang Kai, these bumpkins’ offerings were hardly worth a glance.

After some back and forth, the Great Shaman Masters gradually revealed their true intentions.

They hoped Yang Kai would share a few giant eagles with them.

Everyone could see the value of these giant eagles for scouting the enemy, so each wanted to get a few from Yang Kai.

Faced with such shameless, unreasonable, and downright absurd demands, Yang Kai naturally refused them without the slightest courtesy.

What a joke! All seventy-five giant eagles had been subdued by Yang Kai himself, just enough to form a Sky Cavalry of moderate size. If others took some away, what was the point of all his hard work?

After being refused, the Great Shaman Masters did not give up easily. Some tried to reason with him, some appealed to his emotions, and some even tried to use force…

Without exception, all were thrown out of the hut by Yang Kai. Those who tried to fight him were beaten black and blue.

As a result, Yang Kai’s reputation in the entire Southern Barbarian Tribe was terrible. All the Great Shaman Masters knew he was selfish, had a bad temper, and never listened to anyone.

Even so, many Great Shaman Masters still shamelessly came to visit, hoping for a miracle that would make Yang Kai change his mind.

So, upon hearing the old shaman apprentice’s report from outside, Yang Kai assumed it was another fool trying to get a giant eagle from him, and his mood soured instantly.

Rather than wasting time arguing with them, he might as well cultivate diligently.

Suddenly, another voice sounded outside, somewhat familiar: “Lord Shaman Niu, the Shaman King has summoned you!”

“Hm?” Yang Kai was taken aback, then got up and opened the door. Seeing a familiar, honest face, he couldn’t help but grin. “It’s you!”

This was the shaman messenger he had met before in Cangnan Village. Yang Kai didn’t know his name, only remembered his honest face.

The shaman messenger chuckled. “Yes, it’s me.”

Yang Kai’s expression grew serious. “The Shaman King has summoned me?”

The messenger nodded. “Yes!”

Yang Kai said, “Then let’s go.”

On the way, Yang Kai asked the messenger what the Shaman King wanted with him, but the messenger was vague, either not knowing or not saying. Yang Kai didn’t press him and simply chatted with him instead, which made the messenger feel flattered.

In recent days, the name Shaman Niu had spread throughout the royal city. Everyone knew this skinny Great Shaman Master was not to be trifled with. In just a few days, he had beaten more than a dozen Great Shaman Masters stronger than himself and formed a Sky Cavalry unit using giant eagles. Those seventy or eighty giant eagles often circled above the city, occasionally dropping a pile of dung on someone’s head, making the city’s residents hate Shaman Niu to the bone, wishing they could go out and beat him until even his own mother wouldn’t recognize him.

The messenger had been nervous before coming, but after meeting Yang Kai, he found him nothing like the rumors. Instead of being fierce, he was very kind, making the messenger think the rumors were wrong.

The two chatted as they walked and soon entered the royal city, where they met the Sixth Shaman King in the palace.

“Shaman Niu greets Lord Dang.” Yang Kai placed a hand over his chest in salute. “May I ask why my lord has summoned me?”

Not far from Shaman Dang stood the captain of the guards, whose sharp gaze fixed on Yang Kai like two blades, as if trying to pierce his mind and uncover his secrets.

Yang Kai frowned and glanced back at him, thinking this guy was crazy. Every time he came, the captain stared at him like this, as if Yang Kai might harm Shaman Dang at any moment.

Shaman Dang turned around and said with a smile, “Sit and let’s talk!”

Only then did Yang Kai look away. He didn’t stand on ceremony and said, “Thank you, lord!”

As he spoke, he sat down.

Shaman Dang smiled kindly. “There’s nothing important this time. I’ve just heard some amusing stories lately.”

“Oh?” Yang Kai looked at him in surprise.

Shaman Dang smiled back, expecting Yang Kai to ask about the stories, but after Yang Kai’s “oh,” he said nothing more, leaving Shaman Dang awkwardly holding his expression. He could only cough lightly and say, “I heard you spent a fortune buying weapons in the city to equip your warriors?”

Yang Kai immediately put on a look of utter misery. “Shaman King, you must see the truth! For this war, I’ve spent my entire fortune, selflessly giving everything I have. A lifetime’s savings, all gone! My loyalty to the barbarian tribe is as clear as the sun and moon, witnessed by heaven and earth! But those unscrupulous merchants actually raised their prices at this time, increasing them by twenty or thirty percent! They are the cancer and shame of our tribe!”

By the end, he was spitting everywhere, his face full of righteous indignation.

Shaman Dang’s mouth twitched as he looked Yang Kai up and down, not quite sure how to respond.

As a Shaman King, he had seen many things, but this was his first time meeting someone like Yang Kai.

He had only said one sentence, yet the other party launched into a torrent of complaints, spouting all sorts of novel phrases. It was… rather interesting.

Yang Kai’s expression turned serious as he lowered his voice. “Shaman King, did you summon me to punish those unscrupulous merchants? With the tribe facing disaster, not only do they refuse to help, but they even raise prices! They really need to be taught a lesson. If you wish, I am willing to testify against them, and I guarantee none will escape punishment. Let’s clean up the business world of our Southern Barbarian Tribe!”

Even the guard captain, who had been standing quietly to the side the whole time, could not keep the corner of his mouth from twitching.

Shaman Dang waved his hand helplessly. “That’s not my intention. Business is a matter of mutual consent. You can’t force it. The merchants haven’t done anything seriously wrong.”

“Oh…” Yang Kai looked disappointed, but soon his eyes lit up. “Then is the Shaman King planning to reimburse me for some of the expenses?”

“What?” Shaman Dang was taken aback.

“Reimbursement!” Yang Kai looked at him earnestly. “I’ve spent all my money and have nothing left. If I want to buy anything else, I’m afraid I can’t afford it. The royal city is wealthy—if only…”

“Insolence!” The captain of the guards finally couldn’t take it anymore and shouted angrily, “How dare you speak such nonsense before the Shaman King!”

Yang Kai glanced at him, rolled his eyes, and shot him a look of utter disdain, making the captain’s anger flare up.

Shaman Dang smiled helplessly, realizing that chatting with Shaman Niu was unrealistic. He could only get straight to the point. “Shaman Niu, actually, I called you here today for a reason.”

Yang Kai sat up straight. “As long as it’s not about things in the sky, you can say anything!”

Shaman Dang replied, “It’s precisely to talk about things in the sky.”

Yang Kai put on a look of utter grievance. “Seriously? Even the royal city wants to get its hands on my Sky Cavalry? Shaman King, aren’t you all being a bit unreasonable?”

He’d already had a bad feeling before coming here. After all, he had only met with Shaman Dang a few days ago—if there were truly urgent matters of war, the Shaman King wouldn’t have summoned him alone. As for anything else that might catch the Shaman King’s eye, aside from those giant eagles, there was probably nothing else.

It seemed this trip would be fraught with peril...
