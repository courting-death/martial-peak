## Chapter 2700 — What This Young Master Desires

While everyone was still in a daze, Yang Kai had already unfurled his Wind-and-Thunder Wings and charged straight into the crowd.

The faces of the many Emperor Realm cultivators changed dramatically as they scrambled to dodge, not a single one daring to face him head-on.

Yang Kai burst out laughing, like a tiger among sheep, as if no one could stop him. He lunged directly at the martial artist surnamed Xia, his body shrouded in sword light, and slashed down with his sword.

Although Xia had been ravaged by time, his Qi and blood greatly diminished, he was still like a centipede that refused to die. Sensing the crisis, he raised his hand, fiercely circulating his Emperor Yuan, and an Imperial Treasure shaped like a flying dagger turned into a streak of light, shooting toward Yang Kai as he retreated in panic.

But even at his peak, he would never have been a match for Yang Kai, let alone now, gravely wounded. The flying dagger Imperial Treasure, though it looked fierce, only managed to graze Yang Kai’s waist, leaving a shallow wound.

Yang Kai twisted aside, dodging the flying dagger Imperial Treasure, and, sword in hand, passed through Xia's body in a flash of cold light.

Bang—

Xia exploded into a shower of blood, leaving no corpse behind. Only a Spatial Ring fell from midair.

When everyone looked again, they saw Yang Kai drenched in blood, standing tall in the void, as if he’d been forged from blood itself. His face was cold and hard, killing intent surging, yet he grinned savagely, revealing a mouthful of white fangs, like a god of slaughter descending upon the world—no one dared meet his gaze.

Unstoppable! Unopposable!

The same thought surfaced in everyone’s mind: if they continued to oppose Yang Kai, they would surely die.

Earlier, Yang Kai had killed a few people, but they were only First Stage Emperors, nothing much. But that cultivator surnamed Xia was a genuine Second Stage Emperor, and even he was as helpless as a chicken before Yang Kai. How could the rest of them hope to stand against him?

Fear swelled in their hearts, and no one dared to attack Yang Kai again. They all stood there, faces full of panic.

Yang Kai swept his sword, sending thousands of sword lights flying, instantly riddling several Emperor Realm cultivators who had been struck by the Years Like a Shuttle Seal with holes, killing them on the spot. These people didn’t have Xia's cultivation. After being hit by the Years Like a Shuttle Seal and eroded by the Power of Time, they’d lost most of their strength and died before they could even react.

“Do you want to live or die?” Yang Kai pointed his Myriad Sword at the crowd and shouted.

At these words, everyone was first shocked, then overjoyed.

They already didn’t dare oppose Yang Kai, but they’d been afflicted by the Heart-Controlling Gu. If they didn’t act, Tan Junhao would be the first to kill them. But if they did, with Yang Kai’s terrifying strength, how many could survive?

Now, hearing Yang Kai’s question, it sounded like he was willing to spare their lives.

Immediately, quite a few nodded and said, “We want to live, we want to live!”

After a lifetime of struggle, finally reaching the Emperor Realm, admired by millions, with beauty, power, and wealth at their fingertips—who would want to die when the good days had just begun?

“If you want to live, stand over there.” Yang Kai raised his sword and pointed in a direction.

The crowd looked at each other, hesitating. If standing over there meant survival, who would hesitate? The key was whether Tan Junhao would let them go.

Sure enough, when Tan Junhao saw Yang Kai rallying the Emperor Realm cultivators, he said coldly, “If you listen to him, you’ll only be that much closer to death.”

Yang Kai snorted, glanced at Tan Junhao with disdain, then turned to the crowd. “If you trust me, stand over there. I guarantee your safety. If you don’t, feel free to attack me again and see if I can’t kill every last one of you!”

“Arrogant brat! I’d like to see how you protect them!” Tan Junhao sneered. This world was already sealed by the Grand Square Five Elements Array, and he’d planted the Heart-Controlling Gu in everyone. Life and death were in his hands. Yang Kai might be impressive, but he wouldn’t be allowed to undermine Tan Junhao’s authority.

“Then open your dog eyes and watch closely.” Yang Kai sneered, looking at the crowd again. “You have ten breaths. After that, anyone not standing over there dies without mercy!”

At these words, everyone’s faces changed, stifled with frustration.

Tan Junhao and Yang Kai—neither was someone they could afford to provoke, yet here they were, caught in the middle. Their resentment was easy to imagine.

If they kept following Tan Junhao, they’d likely die anyway. But if Yang Kai could really keep them safe, maybe it was worth a gamble. Most importantly, compared to Tan Junhao, Yang Kai was less despicable. He might be arrogant, but at least he had some sense of honor. He hadn’t intended to start a massacre; the killings just now were forced.

Everyone’s thoughts churned as they weighed the pros and cons. Yang Kai and Tan Junhao didn’t rush them, just stood there quietly.

Yang Kai didn’t care. If these people didn’t know what was good for them, he’d just kill them all. But if they trusted him, he could save some strength. Killing them would come at a cost, and Tan Junhao was still eyeing him like a tiger. A Third Stage Emperor was no easy opponent.

“Young Master Yang, I trust you.” Suddenly, a woman called out and was the first to step forward, flying to the spot Yang Kai had indicated.

Yang Kai glanced at her and nodded slightly.

He had some impression of this woman. During the earlier fight, she hadn’t put in much effort, clearly not wanting to truly offend him.

Seeing him look over, the woman’s red lips moved as she sent a message: “I am a friend of Xia Sheng.”

Yang Kai raised his eyebrows and looked her over again, realizing she trusted him not because she knew his character, but because of Xia Sheng. She must have heard of him from Xia Sheng and knew he was also from Qingyang Shrine.

Heart stirred, he replied, “If you’re Xia Sheng’s friend, you’re my friend too. If I say I’ll keep you safe, I will.”

The woman was overjoyed, sensing Yang Kai’s confidence. She felt she’d made the right bet. She didn’t know what other tricks Yang Kai had, but since he said so, he must have a way.

Heart moved, she called out, “Perhaps you all don’t know, but Young Master Yang is a Core Disciple of Qingyang Shrine, highly valued by Lord Wen Zishan. If word of today gets out, do you think there’ll be any place for you in the Southern Territory?”

“Qingyang Shrine?”

The crowd was shocked. It was the first time they’d heard this, and they realized why this young man surnamed Yang was so powerful—he was a Core Disciple of Qingyang Shrine.

Although Qingyang Shrine wasn’t as great as Star God Palace in the Southern Territory, it was still a top sect. Since Yang Kai was a Core Disciple, maybe he could be trusted.

Even if they died, at least it would be their own choice—better than being Tan Junhao’s pawns.

With this thought, quite a few people flew over to the woman’s side, landing beside her. In an instant, more than a dozen responded to her call.

The rest, though hesitant, gradually gathered around her as the tide turned.

It wasn’t that they wanted to trust Yang Kai, but after so many had failed to do anything to him—and several had died—none of them dared face him anymore.

Tan Junhao’s face turned ashen at the sight, and he snorted, “Are you sure this is your choice?”

No one answered. They all looked to Yang Kai, thinking if he had any tricks, now was the time. Otherwise, if Tan Junhao acted, they’d all die here.

Yang Kai laughed loudly and declared, “Old Dog Tan, the will of the people is with this Young Master—how could your petty schemes ever hope to compete?”

Tan Junhao was so angry he spat blood, gritting his teeth. “Very well. In that case, none of you will leave alive.”

His gaze locked onto the woman, clearly intending to kill her first as a warning to the others.

The crowd’s faces changed dramatically, and they cried out, “Young Master Yang!”

“Don’t worry!” Yang Kai waved his hand, then reached into the void and grabbed. Suddenly, a small bell appeared in his palm. The bell was ancient, engraved with countless birds, beasts, mountains, and rivers. As soon as it appeared, it radiated a wild, primordial power, making everyone feel as if they’d been transported back to the Primordial Era.

“What… what is that!” Tan Junhao’s movements froze as he stared wide-eyed at the Mountain and River Bell, a sudden sense of dread welling up from deep within. Instinctively, he sensed the bell’s terrifying power.

“Country bumpkin!” Yang Kai shot Tan Junhao a look of utter disdain, then, with a grand flourish, tossed the Mountain and River Bell toward the crowd. “Grow!” he shouted.

Sure enough, the Mountain and River Bell swelled in the wind, instantly becoming larger than a house. With a thunderous crash, it fell, enveloping everyone inside.

“Whatever that thing is, anyone who betrays me dies!” Tan Junhao was completely enraged by Yang Kai’s insult. No longer holding back, he formed a hand seal to activate the Heart-Controlling Gu and kill all the Emperor Realm cultivators who had betrayed him.

Yang Kai watched coldly, arms folded, looking completely at ease.

In truth, he was a bit nervous. He knew the Mountain and River Bell was famed for its ability to suppress mountains and rivers, its Imperial Aura able to reverse the heavens, and that it could guard the world. But whether it could truly sever Tan Junhao’s connection to the Heart-Controlling Gu, he was only seventy percent sure.

If it failed, the more than twenty Emperor Realm cultivators trapped inside would die with grievances.

But as he watched, Tan Junhao’s face suddenly changed, his expression incredulous, and his hand seals stopped.

Yang Kai burst out laughing, knowing he’d guessed right. The Mountain and River Bell had indeed cut off Tan Junhao’s connection to the Heart-Controlling Gu.

“That… that’s the Primordial Era’s rare treasure, the Mountain and River Bell?” Tan Junhao finally realized, exclaiming in shock.

Yang Kai clapped his hands and praised, “Old Dog Tan, you’ve got a sharp eye after all.”

Sometimes, Yang Kai had to admire these old monsters who’d lived for countless years. They could often identify the origins of many things—perhaps that was the benefit of living so long.
