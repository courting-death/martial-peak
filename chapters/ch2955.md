## Chapter 2955 — The Dog’s Problem

Inside a teahouse, fine tea was being brewed, its fragrance filling the air. Yang Kai and Zhu Qing sat facing each other, while the little black dog lay curled on a corner of the tabletop, eyes closed as if dozing.

“What happened just now?” Zhu Qing asked in a low voice. Even now, she couldn’t figure out why Yang Kai had been injured. Although the wound wasn’t serious, he had still bled. It didn’t seem likely that the Third Prince had done something—she knew Yang Kai’s strength all too well. How could a mere First Stage Emperor Realm master possibly injure him? And it was just with a snap of his fingers.

If the Third Prince truly had such ability, then he wouldn’t be far from becoming a Great Emperor.

“This Human Sovereign City is not simple,” Yang Kai said, letting go of the handkerchief pressed to his nose. The bleeding had stopped, but the handkerchief was a mess, so he put it away casually.

Zhu Qing exclaimed in shock, “It was really the Third Prince who hurt you?”

“Yes,” Yang Kai nodded.

“How is that possible?” Zhu Qing’s expression changed slightly. “Could it be that he’s hiding his true strength?” She was shaken inside. What kind of expert could conceal their power so well that even she couldn’t see through it? If such a person existed, their strength would be truly terrifying.

“Not at all,” Yang Kai shook his head vigorously. “That guy isn’t hiding anything. He is exactly at the First Stage of the Emperor Realm.”

“Then how could…” Zhu Qing’s lips parted slightly in surprise.

“How should I put it…” Yang Kai frowned, recalling the scene from earlier. After a long moment, he said, “There’s an extremely powerful force within Human Sovereign City, and the Third Prince seems able to command it at will. It wasn’t the Third Prince who hurt me, but that omnipresent force.”

Though his words were a bit awkward, the meaning was clear enough. Naturally, Zhu Qing understood. She stared blankly and asked, “What kind of force is it?”

“I can’t say for sure,” Yang Kai shook his head. “Do you remember that woman who warned us not to provoke anyone with a leaf on their chest?”

Zhu Qing lightly tapped the table and said, “The Third Prince’s clothes did have a leaf emblem.”

Yang Kai grinned, “And it was a nine-leaf emblem!” He raised his hand and pointed to the sky. “Maybe you didn’t notice, but all those people flying around up there have leaf emblems on their chests, just in different numbers.”

Hearing this, Zhu Qing quickly released her Divine Sense to investigate, and found it was true. Everyone flying in the sky, regardless of their strength, was no ordinary person; all of them had leaf emblems embroidered on their chests.

“These people must have a very special status in Human Sovereign City,” Yang Kai said, tapping his finger on the table. “There’s a Human Sovereign here, and princes. I guess… all those with leaf emblems on their chests must be members of the royal family, which is why they have certain privileges.”

Zhu Qing frowned, “That’s an awful lot of royal relatives, isn’t it?”

Yang Kai laughed, “That’s why most of them only have one or two leaves. It’s rare to see more than five. The Third Prince has nine—he must be someone with real power.”

Zhu Qing said, “I remember now. Steward Liao from that cave before also had such an emblem on his chest.”

“Exactly,” Yang Kai nodded. “That’s why he could control the Space Array there and hold the main Human Sovereign Seal. Even though that couple was stronger than him, they had to swallow their anger. Tsk… having privileges really is nice.”

“What are you thinking?” Zhu Qing shot him a look.

Yang Kai replied seriously, “I mean, only these royal relatives can command that mysterious power in the city and easily let the weak overcome the strong. Of course, not every royal relative can do this—there must be some restrictions. Maybe only those with more than five or six leaves have the qualification. The more leaves, the greater the power they can command.”

“Hmm, what you say makes sense,” Zhu Qing didn’t think too deeply about it, only feeling that Yang Kai’s words were reasonable.

Yang Kai sneered, “In the end, it’s still survival of the fittest. It’s just that some people here can borrow a special power, and that power seems to be everywhere in Human Sovereign City.”

“Can we resist it?” Zhu Qing asked.

Yang Kai shook his head, “I can’t say. The Third Prince didn’t use his full strength just now, and I wasn’t on guard either. If things really turn ugly, I don’t know if we can withstand it. Right now, the most important thing is to figure out what that power actually is, so we can find a way to deal with it.”

“Should we go ask around?” Zhu Qing suggested.

Yang Kai smiled and shook his head, “No need.”

“Why not?”

“Because… someone’s coming to us, silly.” Yang Kai smiled at her.

Zhu Qing pouted, “You’re the silly one.” Yang Kai’s words reminded her that ever since entering Human Sovereign City, her intelligence seemed to have dropped. Thinking about it, it was probably because she felt she had someone to rely on, so her judgment and observation had both declined. It made her feel a bit uneasy, but she didn’t dislike the feeling.

“Since you’re here, sir, why not come over and sit with us? There’s no need to sit all alone with no one to talk to,” Yang Kai suddenly turned his head and looked at a nearby table.

There was only one guest at that table, a man who looked about fifty, with a goatee and a refined appearance. At first glance, he looked like a scholar. His cultivation was restrained, his aura elusive and unpredictable, making it impossible to judge his strength.

This man had entered the teahouse just after Yang Kai.

Upon hearing Yang Kai's words, the goateed man proved quite straightforward. He rose directly and moved over to their table. His gaze didn't linger on Yang Kai or Zhu Qing, but instead settled with some interest on the little black dog sprawled across the table, feigning slumber.

The aroma of tea filled the air. Yang Kai picked up the teapot and poured him a full cup, then asked, “How should I address you, sir?”

The goateed man picked up the cup, unafraid of the heat, and drank it all in one go. He said lightly, “A fine dog.”

Yang Kai laughed, “It’s just a little mutt, nothing special. You flatter it, sir.”

The little black dog had no particularly striking features. As long as it didn’t reveal its Guixu bloodline, outsiders wouldn’t notice anything unusual.

The goateed man shook his head, “I wasn’t talking about it.”

Yang Kai raised an eyebrow, “If not it, then you mean me?”

The goateed man stroked his beard and said lightly, “A promising youth.”

Zhu Qing’s face darkened, and she was about to teach this loose-tongued old man a lesson. How could she tolerate someone insulting her man right in front of her?

Yang Kai gave her a look, signaling her to calm down, then chuckled, “You’re quite amusing, sir. I’m a person—how can I be compared to a dog?”

The goateed man said, “Sometimes, people are worse than dogs. Being human has its frustrations, but being a dog has its advantages.”

Yang Kai sneered, “Then may I ask, sir, whose dog are you? The Third Prince’s?”

The goateed man’s face turned a bit cold, but he didn’t lose his temper. He nodded, “You could say that.”

Yang Kai said, “People often say that a dog that bites doesn’t bark, while those that bark are useless. If that’s the case, then sir, you must be a good dog.”

Zhu Qing snorted, “Don’t compare him to Ah Wang. Ah Wang is much better than him.”

She seized the chance to vent her anger, picking up the little black dog and gently stroking its fur.

The goateed man said, “Whether I’m a good dog or not isn’t up to me—it’s up to the Third Prince. If he thinks you are, then you are. If he thinks you’re not, then no matter how strong you are, you’re just a dead dog.”

Yang Kai narrowed his eyes and said, "So the question is, sir—have you come to beat the dog and eat its meat, or do you have something else in mind?"

The goateed man pushed his cup toward Yang Kai, signaling for a refill, and said lightly, “Dog meat isn’t tasty, and the Third Prince isn’t interested.”

Yang Kai let out an “oh” and said, “So you’re here to recruit hunting dogs.”

“Whether you’re a hunting dog or livestock depends on your performance.”

The goateed man picked up his cup again, sipped, and said, “It’s rare to see outsiders with your strength. The Third Prince thinks you’re not bad, so he’s willing to give you a chance. Whether you can seize it depends on you.”

Yang Kai burst out laughing and shook his head, “Sorry, sir, you have lofty ambitions. If you want to be someone’s house dog, that’s your business. My wife and I have no interest in performing for anyone. We’re content just raising a dog—we’re not interested in being dogs ourselves.”

The goateed man set down his cup, his expression unchanged as if he’d expected this. He nodded, “I’ve heard about things outside and know the situation. Maybe you were a powerful figure out there, able to call the wind and summon the rain, but this is Human Sovereign City, and it has its own rules. Outsiders like you always have to suffer a bit before you understand the rules here.”

As he spoke, he stood up and casually tossed down a wooden token. “This is the Third Prince’s token. If you can survive this ordeal, you’ll be qualified to be his hunting dog. If not, it just means you’re nothing special.”

With that, he drifted away.

At the same time, garments fluttering, more than a dozen figures rushed in from all directions, instantly surrounding the teahouse so tightly that not even a drop of water could leak through. The other patrons inside turned pale with fright and scattered in all directions, fleeing in haste, while the teahouse owner looked at the sudden scene with a near tearful expression, yet did not dare to make a move.

Because he recognized who these people were.

Yang Kai rubbed his head and said with a pained expression, “First a sweet date, then a club—what’s that supposed to mean?”

Zhu Qing looked around coldly and said, “It seems they’re not from the same group.”

Yang Kai suddenly understood, “If there’s a Third Prince, there must be a First and Second Prince too, right? I wonder whose people these are. Looks like they’re not all on the same side.”

The two of them didn’t lower their voices, so the martial artists surrounding the teahouse heard everything clearly, but none of them responded. Instead, it seemed like they were waiting for someone.

They really were waiting. Not long after, the sound of something breaking through the air came from the street, and a huge sedan bed was carried over in a grand display. The ones carrying it were all Dao Origin Realm martial artists, moving swiftly and steadily, the sedan flying smoothly down the street.
