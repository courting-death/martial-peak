## Chapter 2845 — Sky Cavalry

In an enormous eagle’s nest, the Eagle King was bound in place by a Shamanic Art cast by Yang Kai. Rays of beast-taming light blossomed from Yang Kai’s hands, enveloping the Eagle King, but to no effect. The Eagle King kept screeching, its crimson eyes full of wild, unyielding spirit.

All around the nest, dozens upon dozens of giant eagles launched fearless attacks, swooping in to try and rescue their king.

Die hovered in midair, shamanic power surging as a Shamanic Art shield wrapped around the entire nest. Every giant eagle that dove in was blocked outside, and even the Wind Blades they unleashed could only send ripples across the shield’s surface.

Earlier, Yang Kai and Die had fought their way straight into the Eagle King’s nest. Yang Kai had just subdued the Eagle King and hadn’t yet had time to use the beast-taming art on it when the Eagle King let out a shrill cry. After that cry, the surrounding giant eagles seemed to receive a command and all charged in at once.

If not for Die holding them off, Yang Kai wouldn’t have had time to deal with the Eagle King.

These giant eagles were all of a decent rank, though not too high. If Die truly went all out, she could slaughter them in short order. But Yang Kai hadn’t come here to kill—he wanted to subdue them. So Die could only block the rescue attempts, not harm the eagles. Though she possessed the strength of a Great Shaman Master, she couldn’t use it fully, leaving her in a passive position.

“Ah Niu, hurry up! I can’t hold on much longer.” Beads of sweat formed on Die’s forehead. Fending off the eagles’ relentless dives and Wind Blades for so long was taking a toll on her.

“I’m trying!” Yang Kai replied, sending out another ray of beast-taming light into the Eagle King’s body, but again, it had no effect.

Instead, his repeated attempts had already inflicted considerable harm on the Eagle King. The beast-taming art was a domineering Shamanic Art, and the Eagle King’s defiance rendered it ineffective. The impact of the shamanic power had already made blood flow from its seven orifices.

If it weren’t the Eagle King—so much stronger than ordinary giant eagles—it would likely have died already.

But even so, it wouldn’t last much longer in this state.

Eagles were proud, born to the sky. No shackle could restrain their freedom.

Staring coldly into those crimson eyes, Yang Kai said, “You should understand my words. Even if you don’t, you should know my intent. You have only two choices: submit… or die! My time is short, and you don’t have many chances. Choose well.”

As his words fell, Yang Kai sent out another ray of beast-taming light. But this time, the light failed to enter the Eagle King’s body—instead, its powerful will simply rebounded the art, forcing it away.

Yang Kai cursed, a savage look twisting his face. His eyes flickered coldly, and suddenly he raised his hand—sending out a Moon Blade that sliced through the air. The shimmering arc struck a giant eagle dead-on, cleaving it cleanly in two.

The shrill scream was cut short. Blood and entrails rained down as the two halves of the eagle fell into the deep ravine below.

Die glanced back at Yang Kai in astonishment, not knowing what madness had seized him.

Yang Kai grinned savagely at the Eagle King. “Each time you refuse me, I’ll kill one of your kin. Let’s see how many times you can refuse!”

With that, he unleashed the beast-taming art again.

This time, the light wasn’t repelled, but the Eagle King was still resisting, refusing to cooperate.

Yang Kai didn’t hesitate. With another wave, yet another giant eagle was cut down, falling from the sky.

“Screech!” The Eagle King cried out, clearly enraged. If not for Yang Kai’s shamanic bindings, it would have pounced to fight him to the death.

Yang Kai was not alarmed but pleased, nodding repeatedly. “Good, good, get angry! If you don’t want them all to die, lower your head and submit!”

The art of beast taming stretches back to ancient times and will endure for generations to come. In the Star Realm, there is even a renowned sect known as the Beast Taming Sect, famous for its mastery over Yao Beasts. Every disciple of this sect commands several battle beasts, giving them a decisive edge in combat.

However, the Beast Taming Sect’s methods were gentle and complex, and took a long time to perform.

Yang Kai didn’t know the intricacies of their techniques, but he figured they must have originated from these ancient beast-taming arts. No matter how underhanded the means, the goal was always the same: to make Yao Beasts submit and serve.

As long as the goal was achieved, any method was justifiable.

With Yang Kai’s current strength, if given enough time, taming an Eagle King like this would be no problem. But time was short—he had to hurry back to the royal city in case war broke out there.

The Eagle King was fiercely unyielding, so he had to use drastic measures.

Perhaps it didn’t care about its own life or death, preferring to die with honor than live in disgrace. Yet as the king of its kind, being powerless to protect its own kin was the greatest failure—a humiliation no proud creature could ever endure.

With every giant eagle that died by Yang Kai’s hand, the Eagle King grew more agitated, the shamanic chains binding it visibly warping under its struggles.

Yang Kai’s expression didn’t change. He cut down ten giant eagles in a row before the frenzied Eagle King suddenly stopped, a hint of pleading in its eyes.

It seemed to realize that if it truly refused to submit, Yang Kai would kill every last one of its kin.

Reading its expression, Yang Kai knew the time was right. He cast the beast-taming light again, and this time it wasn’t repelled or resisted—it easily broke through the Eagle King’s defenses and branded itself within its body.

Yang Kai’s body trembled. He could clearly feel a new connection with the giant eagle, as if he could control its life and death at will.

The once-proud Eagle King became docile, lowering its noble head.

“All done!” Yang Kai grinned, patting the Eagle King’s head and handing it a few Spirit Pills for healing.

The Eagle King looked at them with an almost human curiosity in its eyes, but still swallowed the pills in one gulp. As the medicine took effect, the energy it had lost during Yang Kai’s efforts was quickly restored.

Yang Kai tried communicating with it.

The Eagle King immediately responded.

The giant eagles circling around, who hadn’t retreated even as Yang Kai killed their kin, suddenly stopped attacking as if receiving an order, flapping their wings and circling overhead.

Seeing this, Die couldn’t help but breathe a sigh of relief. She looked back and asked, “Did it work?”

Yang Kai nodded.

Only then did Die withdraw the Shamanic Art shield, her face a little pale.

“Rest for a bit. I’ll handle the rest,” Yang Kai instructed, then continued communicating with the Eagle King.

Though this Eagle King hadn’t cultivated to the point of taking human form, it had already developed intelligence. Communicating with it wasn’t difficult for Yang Kai. He had it order all the giant eagles to return to their nests, and soon, the eagles circling above each flew back to their own lairs.

Yang Kai then led the Eagle King to visit each nest in turn.

If Yang Kai had come alone, the giant eagles would have attacked him as an enemy. But with the Eagle King at his side, things were different.

Under the Eagle King’s dominance, none of the giant eagles dared resist. They stayed obediently in their nests as Yang Kai branded them with the beast-taming light. The process went incredibly smoothly—every attempt succeeded, not a single failure.

After half an hour of busy work, Yang Kai had subdued all the giant eagles here. Counting them carefully, there were seventy-six in total.

This harvest left Yang Kai pleasantly surprised.

With so many giant eagles, a vague idea in his mind could finally be put into action.

After half an hour’s rest, Die was fully recovered. The two of them immediately mounted the Eagle King and set off along their return route.

Behind the Eagle King, seventy-six giant eagles followed in a vast flock, their combined shadow seeming to blot out the earth below.

The giant eagles flew at incredible speed, no slower than a Great Shaman Master at full power. Their flight was perfectly steady, without the slightest turbulence.

In less than half a day, they arrived above the royal city.

From afar, a commotion could be heard below, and someone shouted, “Barbaric Beasts are attacking!”

Barbarians from all over rushed to respond. Archers drew their bows, aiming at the Eagle King in the lead. Great Shaman Masters chanted incantations, flying into the air with Shamanic Art shields glowing around them, coming to intercept.

The distance between the two sides closed rapidly.

Waves of shamanic power, both bright and dark, surged from all directions.

Seeing the attack about to begin, Yang Kai quickly stood up on the Eagle King’s back and shouted, “Don’t panic! Don’t panic!”

The sudden appearance of a person on the eagle’s back left the barbarians below stunned.

The Great Shaman Masters flying to intercept were even more dumbfounded.

One of them rubbed his eyes in disbelief and exclaimed, “Shaman Niu?!”

Yesterday’s commotion between Yang Kai and Shaman Tu had drawn many Great Shaman Masters to watch from start to finish, so they all remembered the skinny Shaman Niu. Now, seeing Yang Kai, many immediately recognized him.

From the crowd, Shaman Tu stepped forward and asked in a daze, “Shaman Niu, where did you get so many Barbaric Beasts?”

Yang Kai smiled slightly. “Naturally, I caught them.”

“Caught… caught them…” The corners of everyone’s eyes twitched. If these flying Barbaric Beasts were really so easy to catch, no one would be this shocked.

If it were just one, it could be chalked up to Shaman Niu’s luck. But this was clearly a whole flock—seventy or eighty of them! How could he have caught so many?

Standing atop the eagle’s back, Yang Kai declared loudly, “These are my sky cavalry. What do you all think?”

“Sky cavalry…” The Great Shaman Masters weren’t fools. Though it was the first time they’d heard the term, they instantly understood Yang Kai’s plan. In the next moment, many of their eyes turned red with envy as they gazed at the giant eagles.

With a great battle looming, having such a sky cavalry would be an unparalleled advantage for reconnaissance, if nothing else.
