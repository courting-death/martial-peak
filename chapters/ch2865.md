## Chapter 2865 — Victorious in Every Battle

Outside the stone cave, battles of varying scale would erupt at intervals ranging from three to five days at the shortest to ten days or half a month at the longest.

Shaya played the role of traitor to perfection.

All the Demon Commanders within a ten-thousand-li radius were deceived by her, along with their subordinate squads. Every demon squad that rushed here for rescue, without exception, was ruthlessly struck down by Shaman Niu's tribe and utterly annihilated.

Three months of such warfare brought tremendous growth to the many members of Shaman Niu's tribe. Those baptized in blood broke through their shackles one after another and became true warriors. Even among the dozen or so Shaman Warriors and Shaman Apprentices, many unknowingly broke through their bottlenecks and advanced to higher levels.

Now, in Shaman Niu's tribe, aside from Yu and Lu being Upper-Level Shaman Masters, there were two newly advanced Shaman Masters as well, making the tribe's shamanic art blessings even stronger.

The increase in numbers was especially obvious.

At the start, Shaman Niu's tribe had only three thousand people.

But after three months, Shaman Niu's tribe had grown to five thousand, nearly double the original number.

The extra two thousand were all rescued captives from repeated battles. Freed from the erosion of Demonic Qi, they regained their senses and joined Shaman Niu's tribe without hesitation, becoming vital contributors in each fight.

In total, over three months, Shaman Niu's tribe fought in more than a dozen battles, killed at least ten thousand enemies, and slew over a dozen Demon Commander-level foes—an outstanding record. No other team led by a Great Shaman Master in the entire Barbarian Tribe could match Shaman Niu's tribe's achievements. After all, Demon Commanders were on par with Great Shaman Masters, and for others to kill even one required them to risk their lives.

But things were different for Shaman Niu's tribe. Every demon squad lured in had its leader killed by Shaya in a single encounter, after which the squad would fall into chaos and be mercilessly crushed by Shaman Niu's tribe from all sides.

After three months, not a single demon could be seen within a ten-thousand-li radius.

Shaya's efforts also earned her the recognition of the entire Barbarian Tribe. Though she was still a demon, everyone knew she was now one of their own.

In fact, these three months of warfare were basically orchestrated by Shaya, with the twins Yu and Lu assisting. As for Shaman Niu, the leader of Shaman Niu's tribe, he had not shown his face for three months, remaining in his stone cave.

Whenever any shaman wanted to meet Yang Kai, Shaya would block them, and even Yu and Lu were no exception.

If not for Shaya's unwavering efforts to lure and kill the enemy, everyone might have thought Yang Kai had fallen under her control.

One day, three months later, the shamans were discussing future plans with Shaya and the others when Shaya suddenly seemed to sense something, turned to look back, and respectfully bowed, "My lord!"

The shamans also looked up to see Yang Kai had appeared nearby at some point, and they hurriedly stood to salute him.

Yang Kai waved his hand and said, "Pass the order for everyone to prepare. We set out tomorrow."

"Set out?" Yu was slightly stunned. "My lord, are we going somewhere else?"

Yang Kai said, "There are no more demons around here. Staying is pointless. We're going where there are demons."

"My lord, you know..." Yu was a bit surprised.

Indeed, there were no more demons nearby. In three months, under Shaya's constant luring tactics, all the demons within a ten-thousand-li radius had been summoned and then slain.

Although there were still demons farther away, those were not under Shaya's command. Each Demon King ruled a limited area, and the farther demons belonged to other Demon Kings. Even if Shaya sent a call for help, they might not respond.

They had just been discussing whether to move, but with Yang Kai absent, no one could decide.

No one expected Yang Kai to appear at such a crucial moment.

Yang Kai smiled slightly. "Though I've been cultivating, I'm well aware of what's happening outside."

As he spoke, he glanced at Shaya. Having planted a Soul Brand in her, making her his slave, Yang Kai could easily peer into her thoughts. Checking every few days, he naturally understood the situation perfectly.

Upon hearing this, Yu's expression brightened immediately. "Then I shall pass down the order at once." She set about making arrangements, and the other shamans dispersed as well. The relocation of five thousand people was no small undertaking, and there was indeed much to be done.

Shaya was kept behind by Yang Kai.

When only the two of them remained, Shaya's brow twitched as she looked at Yang Kai in surprise. "My lord, your aura..."

After just three months, this foreign man's aura had grown several times stronger. By demon standards, he was almost at the Demon King level.

On the Barbarian Tribe's side, that meant he was close to advancing to Shaman King! After three months here, Shaya had learned a lot about the Barbarian Tribe.

She clearly remembered that when she first met Yang Kai three months ago, he was only a Middle-Level Great Shaman Master. Even with three months of closed-door cultivation, such a leap was unbelievable.

Suddenly, a rumor from within Shaman Niu's tribe echoed in her mind.

The rumor said that before last winter, Shaman Niu was just an ordinary person, weaker than even some Barbarian children, but one night he was blessed by the Barbarian God and soared to greatness.

The rumor supposedly came from villagers who grew up with Shaman Niu, and those who spoke of it seemed utterly convinced.

Shaya hadn't thought much of it before, but now it seemed there was truth to those rumors.

To go from Middle-Level Great Shaman Master to nearly Shaman King in three months—such growth was terrifying.

He had already gravely wounded her as a Middle-Level Great Shaman Master. Now, with his strength multiplied, Shaya felt that if they clashed again, she would not be lucky enough to escape.

Ignoring her shock, Yang Kai asked, "Tell me, where is the nearest Demon King?"

As Yang Kai's slave, Shaya naturally understood his intent and replied, "I know a fellow named Bao Qi, and I know where he is!"

Yang Kai glanced at her, smiling faintly. "You have a grudge against him?"

Shaya's expression grew slightly awkward. "He humiliated me before, though the fault was his to begin with. More importantly, his territory is the closest to ours, so..."

"No need to explain," Yang Kai waved his hand and nodded. "You've worked hard these months. Since you're my slave and you have a grudge against him, let's go teach him a lesson."

Shaya's beautiful eyes sparkled. "Yes!"

At dawn the next day, Shaman Niu's tribe set out, leaving the stronghold they'd defended for three months, heading toward a greater, unknown battlefield. Five thousand people marched in perfect order.

Three months of war had taught the Barbarians the meaning of coordination and discipline. Large-scale battles were not places for reckless bravery; only with trusted companions supporting each other could victory be won.

The Sky Cavalry scouted ahead, constantly sending back information. The five-thousand-strong team advanced without encountering any demon resistance.

It made sense. With Shaya's help these past months, all the demons within ten thousand li had been wiped out. Even if a few stragglers survived, they couldn't stir up trouble, so who would dare block their way?

After half a month, Shaman Niu's tribe finally stopped in a forest, using the tall trees and dense undergrowth to conceal themselves.

Shaya pointed ahead. "My lord, there's a Barbarian city over there. That's where Bao Qi is."

Yang Kai nodded thoughtfully. "You dwell in a stone cave while he occupies a city. For two Demon Kings, the difference in treatment is quite stark."

Shaya said a bit awkwardly, "Bao Qi is stronger than me. By your tribe's standards, I'm a Lower-Level Shaman King, while Bao Qi is a Middle-Level Shaman King."

"What clan is Bao Qi from?" Yang Kai asked.

Shaya's expression turned serious. "The Blood Demon clan! So if you fight him, be careful not to get hurt. Even a small wound would let him use his innate talent to drain your blood dry."

"I'll remember," Yang Kai nodded, gazing into the distance. "Rest for half a day. We'll set out at night."

Shaya left to carry out the order, feeling uneasy about Yang Kai's intentions. Though her master's aura was close to Shaman King and his true strength couldn't be measured by normal standards, she wasn't sure he could defeat Bao Qi—the Blood Demon Bao Qi.

But as a slave, it was pointless to overthink. Whatever Yang Kai decided, she could not disobey, nor even dare to question in her heart. She could only quietly find a place to rest and recover.

Time passed quickly. When night fell, Shaya suddenly opened her eyes, glanced into the darkness, and silently walked out.

A few li away, Yang Kai was waiting. When he saw her approach, he nodded lightly. "From now on, I'm your slave. Be careful not to give yourself away."

Shaya smiled confidently. "Acting is a Succubus's specialty. Don't worry, little one, big sister will take good care of you."

She instantly shed her previous respectful demeanor, giggling as if she'd reverted to how she was when Yang Kai first met her.

Yang Kai ignored her teasing, nodded, and followed behind her, head lowered in a slave's posture.
