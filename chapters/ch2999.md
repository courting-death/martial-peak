## Chapter 2999 — Who Did I Offend?

"Then we shall go to the City Lord's Mansion!" Li Jiao declared with a sweep of his sleeve.

Cai Shi hurriedly led the way.

A few breaths later, the group arrived before the City Lord's Mansion. Rivers of blood flowed here as well, and many cultivators dressed as Wind and Cloud Pavilion disciples lay dead. Even the mansion's gates had collapsed.

Pointing ahead, Cai Shi said, "Sir, they are inside."

Li Jiao swept the mansion with his Divine Sense and indeed detected the auras of Emperor Realm Masters within. However, there was a slight discrepancy with the information he had received. There were actually two people inside rather than the one he had been told about.

Their auras were not particularly strong. They were indeed only at the First-Stage Emperor Realm.

"Wait outside. This Lord will return shortly!" Li Jiao snorted. He was not at all concerned by the change in numbers. With a flicker of his figure, he charged into the City Lord's Mansion and vanished in the blink of an eye.

Immediately afterward, Cai Shi heard Li Jiao's thunderous shout ring out from within. "Audacious juniors! You dare offend Wind and Cloud Pavilion? This Li is here, so surrender at once and this Li may spare your lives!"

As his words fell, a tidal wave of dense Imperial Might erupted. With the City Lord's Mansion at its centre, the world within a radius of a thousand zhang began to hum.

Cai Shi's expression changed slightly, revealing a look of admiration. A Third-Stage Emperor Realm Master truly was a Third-Stage Emperor Realm Master. The might displayed the moment he acted was indeed extraordinary, far beyond what First-Stage and Second-Stage Emperor Realm Masters like them could compare with. It now seemed that when the Pavilion Master had said this man was no weaker than himself—perhaps even stronger—he had not been merely being modest. He truly was the more formidable of the two.

Everything would be fine now. With Lord Li taking action, there should be no further mishaps. It was only a pity that Second Elder and so many disciples of the Pavilion had died miserably at the hands of that wretched couple. If Lord Li had arrived earlier, perhaps the outcome would have been different.

At this thought, Cai Shi sighed deeply. Such were fate and destiny. His cultivation was inferior to Second Elder's, yet in the end, he had managed to cling to life. He had to admit that his luck was quite good. Cultivators cultivated in defiance of the Heavens, but was luck not also a form of opportunity, and was it not also a kind of strength? Faintly, his narrow escape from death gave rise to a flash of insight, and the bottleneck that had constrained him for many years actually showed signs of loosening.

Realizing this, he was overjoyed. He was currently at the First-Stage Emperor Realm. If he could break through, he would reach the Second-Stage Emperor Realm, and such strength would be enough to rank among the top three in Wind and Cloud Pavilion's Elder Hall. He secretly resolved that once this matter was settled, he would immediately return and enter secluded cultivation, refusing to emerge until he had broken through.

A series of thunderous booms rang out. While Cai Shi's thoughts churned, the sounds of battle came from within the City Lord's Mansion. It was the commotion of Emperor Realm Masters clashing. The overflowing power seemed capable of overturning the world and shattering the four poles, causing large swathes of buildings to collapse.

Cai Shi stared without blinking, his heart pounding with fear, and his admiration for Lord Li grew even deeper.

The sounds of battle abruptly ceased, and the violent commotion instantly subsided.

It was over? Cai Shi was astonished. Was that not a little too fast? He had fought that wretched couple before and knew how fierce they were, yet he had never expected Lord Li to deal with them so easily.

A figure flew out from the ruins of the collapsed City Lord's Mansion and sped toward them.

Cai Shi focused his gaze. Who else could it be but Lord Li?

He went forward to welcome him with a delighted expression, cupping his fists from afar. "Many thanks for taking action, Sir. Congratulations on your triumphant return!"

Contrary to Cai Shi's expectations, Lord Li did not greet him with a smile. Instead, his brow was tightly furrowed, as though he had encountered some difficult problem. Most surprising of all, there was actually a dark bruise around Lord Li's eye. He had clearly been struck by a fist, and the blow had been heavy. Even someone as powerful as Lord Li had suffered a split at the corner of his eye, and the socket was badly swollen. There was no telling how long it would take to recover.

"Sir, what happened to you..." Cai Shi was dumbfounded, unable to understand what had occurred.

"Ha..." Li Jiao heaved a long sigh and looked up at the sky, unable to speak as tears choked him. Only after a long while did he tilt his head toward Cai Shi and ask, "Who did I ever offend?"

"What?" Cai Shi did not understand and stared at him blankly.

"Your name is Cai Shi, correct?" Li Jiao asked.

"Yes!" Cai Shi nodded, finding Lord Li increasingly strange. The spirited confidence he had shown moments ago had vanished without a trace, replaced by an expression of desolation and heartache.

What could possibly make a Third-Stage Emperor Realm Master show such an expression?

"This Lord wishes to borrow something from you," Li Jiao said, staring at him expressionlessly.

Cai Shi's heart clenched as an instinctive sense of unease came over him. He subconsciously retreated two steps and asked hoarsely, "May I ask what Sir wishes to borrow?"

"I wish to borrow your life!" As Li Jiao spoke, he suddenly reached out and grabbed at Cai Shi.

Although both of them were Emperor Realm Masters, there was a difference of two Stages between them, and each Stage was as different as Heaven and Earth. How could Cai Shi possibly withstand Li Jiao's surprise attack? In a daze, he felt his neck tighten and found himself seized in Li Jiao's hand.

A terrifying surge of power poured from that great hand, wantonly rushing into his flesh and meridians. His whole body trembled, and his mind fell into chaos, leaving him unable to summon any strength to resist.

"S-Sir... why are you doing this?" Cai Shi struggled ceaselessly, staring at Li Jiao in disbelief.

Was Li Jiao not the helper invited by the Pavilion Master? Had he not just said that he would deal with those villains? How had he changed his mind in the blink of an eye and turned from friend to foe?

Cai Shi could only feel that the world was changing too quickly for him to accept.

"Blame Wind and Cloud Pavilion for provoking someone it should never have provoked. The person inside... is someone this Lord cannot afford to offend. Ha, forget it. There will be so many fellow disciples accompanying you on the Yellow Springs Road that you will not be lonely!"

What? What had he just said? Even Li Jiao could not afford to offend the person inside? Cai Shi wondered whether he had heard incorrectly.

Just as he was about to say something more, violent power pressed in on him from all directions.

With a loud bang, Cai Shi's entire body exploded into a cloud of blood, leaving no corpse or bones behind.

"Ah!"

The remaining Wind and Cloud Pavilion disciples were all dumbfounded, never having expected such a scene to unfold.

Their saviour and hope had suddenly become their executioner. Even Ninth Elder Cai Shi had been crushed into a bloody mist with one hand. What in the world was happening?

With no time to think, they scattered like frightened birds and beasts.

The wind rose and clouds surged as a gigantic azure palm suddenly descended from the sky, enveloping a vast area before slamming down.

Several miserable screams rang out, and in an instant, every last Wind and Cloud Pavilion disciple was wiped out.

Their cultivations were not high, so how could they withstand Li Jiao's attack? With a single strike, they were all flattened into meat patties.

"Woof, woof!" Two barks came from behind him.

The cold indifference on Li Jiao's face instantly vanished, replaced by boundless flattery. Turning around, he bowed and scraped as he said, "Heh heh, Palace Master Yang, I have already killed them all. You see..."

Not the slightest trace remained of the imposing bearing of a Third-Stage Emperor Realm Master. The dignified Palace Master of the Northern Territory's Li Dragon Palace was now no different from a fawning dog.

"At least you know what is good for you!" Yang Kai snorted lightly.

Li Jiao sweated profusely. Knowing that Yang Kai's anger had not yet subsided, he hurriedly explained, "Palace Master Yang, this Li has been wronged! This Li knew nothing about this matter. Had I known it was you, nothing could have made me come here."

Inwardly, he had already cursed Hua Xing from head to toe.

He had only just managed to part ways with Yang Kai, Zhu Qing, and the others some time ago, and had enjoyed barely a few carefree days. Yet he had actually delivered himself right back into their hands and been caught red-handed. Nothing in the world could be more tragic or infuriating.

Yang Kai glanced sideways at him. "From the sound of it, you really dislike me."

Sweat poured down Li Jiao's face like rain, and he hurriedly said, "No, no, no! What this Li meant was that had I known Palace Master Yang was here, I would have come to join you at once. How could I possibly dare oppose you? What happened just now was nothing more than a misunderstanding."

"Enough." Yang Kai waved his hand. "I know what you are thinking. Tell me, what is your relationship with Wind and Cloud Pavilion? Why are you working for them?"

Li Jiao was terribly embarrassed and hurriedly began to explain.

As Yang Kai listened, he glanced over at Lin Yun'er.

The little girl was holding the little black dog and playing with it. Opening her small mouth, she pretended to bite it while making playful chomping sounds. The little black dog seemed to enjoy this childish game as well, wagging its head and tail in delight.

Strangely enough, when Yang Kai and Lin Yun'er had reunited some days ago, the little girl had not paid much attention to the little black dog. Yet after spending some time together, she had grown so fond of it that she could hardly bear to let it go, clinging to it all day long.

The little beast was rather cute, after all, and always managed to win the affection of women.

After Li Jiao finished explaining, Yang Kai asked, "So, you and the Pavilion Master of Wind and Cloud Pavilion are quite close?"

Li Jiao hurriedly replied, "We merely met by chance and are little more than nodding acquaintances. Besides, we have not contacted each other in a hundred years, so whatever friendship we once had has long since faded. Heh heh heh..."

His friendship with Hua Xing truly was not particularly deep, but even if they had been friends through life and death, he could not possibly admit it now.

He truly could not afford to offend Yang Kai. They all had to make their living in the Northern Territory, and Yang Kai was also a major creditor of his. If he truly offended Yang Kai, Li Dragon Palace would likely have no way to survive in the future, to say nothing of Yang Kai's incomparably close relationship with the Dragon Clan.

By comparison, what did his meagre friendship with Hua Xing amount to? If not for Hua Xing, how could he have walked straight into a trap and been caught by Yang Kai?

Thinking this, he instead began to resent Hua Xing, feeling that the man had acted dishonourably by giving him incomplete and inaccurate information. Now that he had once again fallen into Yang Kai's hands, it was entirely Hua Xing's fault.

"Palace Master Yang, this young lady is..." Seeing Yang Kai remain silent, Li Jiao hurriedly searched for something to say and tried to learn about Lin Yun'er.

This little girl was incredible! Her punch had struck him squarely in the eye just now and nearly cost him his life. Fortunately, he possessed the body of a Dragon Descendant. Otherwise, he would not have escaped with only a minor injury. That punch would have been enough to leave him reeling, and in a battle of life and death, that single instant of disorientation would have been enough to get him killed.

This little girl possessed astonishing strength. Could she be another member of the Dragon Clan? Li Jiao's heart leapt in fright.

At the same time, his admiration for Yang Kai reached new heights. Yang Kai had only recently won over a Ninth-Tier Red Dragon, yet just a few days later, how had he managed to get his hands on another one?

As he maliciously speculated, he wondered whether Zhu Qing and this little girl would turn the world upside down fighting if they ever met, and which of them would be stronger. Yang Kai would certainly be driven frantic when the time came. As these scenes flashed through his mind, Li Jiao's mood improved greatly.
