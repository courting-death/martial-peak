## Chapter 2699 — Whoever Strikes, Dies

Tan Junhao burst out laughing. “Mere space divine abilities are nothing to fear! What are you all waiting for? Kill him, now!”

The Second Stage Emperor Realm cultivator surnamed Xia heard this and barked, “Continue! Don’t give him a chance to catch his breath!”

The crowd’s morale soared. Each formed hand seals, unleashing the might of their secret techniques and Imperial Treasures.

Yang Kai’s expression turned cold as he shot Tan Junhao a venomous glare, his mind racing. At this point, as long as he took care of Tan Junhao, the rest would be no threat. They were only fighting him to the death because of the Heart-Controlling Gu. If Tan Junhao died, no one could use the Heart-Controlling Gu to threaten them, and they naturally wouldn’t trouble him any further.

With a plan in mind, Yang Kai threw his head back and roared, “Old dog Tan!”

The moment the first word left his lips, Yang Kai hurled himself at Tan Junhao, his face twisted with fury. Behind him, a pair of Wind-and-Thunder Wings unfurled, stirring up a storm of crackling gales and rumbling thunder. In a flash of radiant light, he shot forward, closing the distance to Tan Junhao in the blink of an eye.

Tan Junhao’s face changed slightly at the sight.

To be so savage in such dire straits, facing dozens of Emperor Realm experts without fear and growing even more frenzied—such courage and temperament were truly chilling. He suddenly raised his hand, and a mass of five-colored light condensed before him, forming blade after blade of five-colored light that slashed toward Yang Kai.

This was the power of the Grand Square Five Elements Array. With a Third Stage Emperor Realm expert personally controlling it, each five-colored light blade carried the force of an Emperor Realm strike.

Sss sss sss sss…

Countless five-colored blades swept down like a tidal wave. Yang Kai charged straight in, not slowing in the slightest. With a fierce clap and pull of his hands, a massive Moon Blade took shape, its pitch-black edge surging with intense spatial power. It looked like a round moon with a huge chunk bitten off by some nameless beast, its inky darkness as deep as the void, radiating a chilling aura.

The Moon Blade swept out, shattering the countless five-colored blades as if smashing mirrors, each fragment dissolving into motes of light that scattered and faded into the world.

Tan Junhao’s eyes bulged in shock. “Space divine ability!”

It was his first time witnessing Yang Kai unleash space divine abilities in actual combat, and the sight left him utterly astounded. Had he not seen it with his own eyes, he would never have believed that space techniques could wield such devastating power. With just a single Space Blade, Yang Kai had shattered his attack as if it were nothing.

Yang Kai grinned savagely. “That’s right, mere space divine abilities—now die!”

He waved his hands again and again, sending out Moon Blades much smaller than before, but so many more that their number was impossible to count.

Tan Junhao’s eyes narrowed. His hand seals changed rapidly as he growled, “Power of the Five Elements, condense for me!”

The five-colored light gathered once more, radiating the auras of metal, wood, water, fire, and earth—mutually generating and restraining, endlessly cycling—forming a five-colored shield before him, solid as a fortress.

But that wasn’t all. Tan Junhao pointed, and a small shield spun out, growing in the wind until it was the size of a door, blocking him completely.

The shield radiated the aura of an Imperial Treasure, clearly an extremely sturdy defensive artifact.

It was clear how wary Tan Junhao was of Yang Kai’s ferocious assault, to set up such impregnable defenses.

Boom boom boom…

The Moon Blades struck the five-colored shield, making it flicker and dim in an instant. It lasted less than a breath before shattering, but the remaining Moon Blades were blocked by the Imperial Treasure shield. Not a single one harmed Tan Junhao.

Yang Kai was about to strike again when wild energy surged behind him—the attacks of the other Emperor Realm cultivators had arrived.

Gritting his teeth, he activated his space technique, trying to teleport away.

But Tan Junhao waved his formation flag again, and the shockwaves in the void left Yang Kai with nowhere to escape.

“Kill him for this old man!” Tan Junhao shouted in a rage.

He had thought that with everything arranged so thoroughly, and with himself personally taking action, capturing Yang Kai—a mere First Stage Emperor—would be a simple matter. Who could have expected Yang Kai to be so fierce and unyielding? Not only had he failed to subdue him quickly, but he’d even been forced into a frantic defense, to the point of having to summon his own Imperial Treasure for protection.

As a Third Stage Emperor, he simply couldn’t accept this.

Lacking the courage to face Yang Kai head-on, he could only command the other Emperor Realm cultivators to fight for him.

With his second attempt at teleportation foiled by Tan Junhao, Yang Kai wasn’t too flustered—he’d been prepared. Facing dozens of secret techniques and Imperial Treasures, he twisted and dodged, evading as best he could.

Even so, he managed to dodge less than half of the attacks; the rest tore into him, ripping his flesh and sending blood streaming everywhere. If not for the Dragon Transformation Secret Technique, he would have fallen right then and there.

But after transforming, his defense soared. The dragon scales on his body blocked most of the force, allowing him to survive.

“He’s still not dead…?”

Everyone was stunned.

The first time they hit Yang Kai, they’d only used thirty percent of their strength. This time, they’d used at least seventy percent. In this world, what other First Stage Emperor could withstand so many attacks and remain standing?

In that instant, many felt a surge of respect.

No matter where they are, true experts are always respected. Yang Kai’s display was enough to move and humble even these fellow Emperor Realm cultivators. If not for Tan Junhao and the Heart-Controlling Gu, they would have gladly befriended him. But now, with their lives not their own, they had no choice but to fight—much to their helpless regret.

Amid the aftershocks, Yang Kai suddenly turned, his blood-red eyes sweeping over the Emperor Realm cultivators. Killing intent surged from him, almost tangible.

Originally, Yang Kai had planned to capture the ringleader first—take down Tan Junhao, and everything else would fall into place. But now, he realized this plan wouldn’t work so easily. Tan Junhao’s own strength was formidable, and with the protection of the grand array, killing him would be no simple feat; it would take considerable effort to break through.

Besides, if he focused solely on Tan Junhao, the other Emperor Realm attacks couldn’t be ignored. A few more rounds—just two would be enough—and he’d be finished.

“Then I’ll deal with you all first!” Yang Kai roared, locking onto the cultivator surnamed Xia. This man, a Second Stage Emperor, had attacked twice without mercy. The two worst wounds on Yang Kai’s body were his doing. The others mostly followed along, but he had truly gone all out, hoping to impress Tan Junhao and earn his freedom from the Heart-Controlling Gu.

Xia met Yang Kai’s gaze and felt his scalp tingle. Sensing Yang Kai’s intent, he slipped into the crowd, shouting, “He’s badly injured! He’s almost done for! Everyone, give it your all!”

Yang Kai sneered. “Whoever strikes, dies!”

At the same time, his hands flew, seals changing, his expression solemn as he chanted, “The years wither and flourish, like a shuttle, like a dream!”

A strange aura suddenly spread out. It was as if time itself had stopped in this world, a bizarre sense of time standing still settling over everyone’s hearts.

For a brief moment, everyone was dazed. By the time they snapped back to their senses, Yang Kai had already slammed his palm fiercely toward the spot where Xia was hiding.

The power of eroding years exploded. The cultivator surnamed Xia couldn’t dodge in time and was struck by the Years Like a Shuttle Seal, coughing blood as he was sent flying.

And not just him—several others nearby were caught as well. Earlier, the cultivator surnamed Xia, feeling guilty, had tried to hide himself among the crowd. But Yang Kai’s palm strike covered a vast area, not caring in the slightest if others were dragged in. After all, every one of these people had attacked him; in his eyes, they were all mortal enemies, sharing a life-and-death grudge.

“This is…” Not far away, Tan Junhao’s eyes widened as if recalling something. He stared at the flying cultivator surnamed Xia, watching closely, not missing a detail.

Xia staggered to his feet, looking listless. Outwardly, nothing seemed amiss, but he appeared gravely wounded.

He stared blankly at his hands, only to see them wither at a speed visible to the naked eye. The surging power of his qi and blood rapidly faded, as if centuries of time had passed over him in an instant.

Gulp…

The sound of swallowing echoed. Everyone felt a chill—what kind of divine ability was this, to make a Second Stage Emperor Realm expert age so rapidly?

Looking at the other Emperor Realm cultivators caught in the blast, they saw the same: hair turning white, cheeks hollowing, eyes bulging, and their clothes hanging loose on their now-gaunt bodies.

“The Years Like a Shuttle Seal! The Great Emperor of Time’s divine ability!” Tan Junhao screamed inwardly, trembling all over.

As an elder of the Star God Palace, his knowledge far surpassed that of ordinary Emperor Realm cultivators. He’d only suspected before, but seeing the changes in those struck, he immediately recognized Yang Kai’s move as the Years Like a Shuttle Seal.

It was said that this was a divine ability entwined with the Laws of Time. The Laws of Time—far more profound and elusive than even the Laws of Space—were something few could ever hope to comprehend, let alone master.

That’s right, this brat had once gone to the Four Seasons Land, which was one of the Great Emperor of Time’s secret palaces. It seemed the Years Like a Shuttle Seal was his harvest from that place.

But how could one person master both the Laws of Space and comprehend the Laws of Time? What terrifying talent and fortune! Just what kind of monster had he provoked?

Gripped by fear, Tan Junhao was even more determined to kill Yang Kai here. If such a little monster were allowed to grow, how could he ever feel safe?

If struck by a space technique, there might be a sliver of hope. But if hit by the Years Like a Shuttle Seal, even if you survived, your qi and blood would be nearly exhausted and your lifespan greatly reduced.

Back in the day, the Great Emperor of Time rampaged across the world with the Years Like a Shuttle Seal—invincible, his fame resounding everywhere!
