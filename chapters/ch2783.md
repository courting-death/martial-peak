## Chapter 2783 — Elder Sister Will Let You Die Knowing Why

After downing several jars of fine wine in succession, the woman finally let out a loud, satisfied burp. Wearing a look of utter comfort, she wiped the wine from the corner of her mouth, stood up, and took a deep breath. “I finally feel alive again.”

The listlessness on her face vanished, replaced by a healthy, rosy glow.

She didn’t drink the two jars that remained, instead treasuring them as she put them into her Spatial Ring. Looking up, she asked, “Dajun, did you come to rescue me?”

Nanmen Dajun gave a cold laugh. “What do you think?”

“Then when are we having our wedding?” the woman pressed.

“Get lost!”

The woman pouted, muttering something under her breath—judging by her lips, probably cursing. In the next instant, she glanced at Yang Kai, grinned, and asked Nanmen Dajun, “Who’s this young man? Nice figure. Is he rich? Does he have any wine?”

“Watch your mouth!” Nanmen Dajun snorted coldly. “This is the Palace Master of my Lingxiao Palace. You foolish woman, hurry up and pay your respects.”

“Lingxiao Palace?” The woman tilted her head, thought for a moment, then giggled. “Never heard of it. Where did that pop up from?”

Nanmen Dajun said solemnly, “Whether you’ve heard of it or not doesn’t matter. What matters is that this Lord is now the Chief Formation Master of Lingxiao Palace!”

The woman’s playful smile froze on her face. She stared at Nanmen Dajun in shock, then glanced at Yang Kai before stepping forward and reaching out to pat Nanmen Dajun on the head.

“What are you doing!” Nanmen Dajun retreated a step, wary.

“Are you sick?” the woman asked curiously.

Nanmen Dajun snapped, “You’re the one who’s sick! Your whole family’s sick!”

The woman giggled, spun around gracefully, and landed atop a shining stone, looking down from above. “If you’re not sick, why would you join a Sect? I remember you always looked down on all those big and small Sects.”

“Since I joined Lingxiao Palace, I naturally have my reasons,” Nanmen Dajun replied after a moment’s thought.

The woman’s expression suddenly turned serious. She and Nanmen Dajun had known each other for many years, ever since they were both weak. They’d worked together many times, growing step by step to where they were now, knowing each other inside and out.

She was well aware of Nanmen Dajun’s attitude toward the recruitment efforts of those Sects. If he’d wanted to join any Sect, practically any in the Star Realm, big or small, would have welcomed him— even those of the Great Emperors were no exception. But the last time she saw him, he was still a lone wolf. Now, suddenly, she heard he’d joined some Sect called Lingxiao Palace.

The woman was utterly surprised.

Palace Master of Lingxiao Palace? With only the First Stage of the Emperor Realm, how strong could a Sect he founded possibly be? What could possibly attract someone like Nanmen Dajun to join?

The woman tilted her head, scrutinizing Yang Kai as if trying to see something special about him. Yang Kai smiled back, but inside he was a tangled mess, thinking maybe he should just leave. With such an unreliable artificer, even if he did recruit her to Lingxiao Palace, it might not be a good thing.

After a long moment, the woman suddenly pounded her fist into her palm, her face lighting up with realization. She looked at Nanmen Dajun in surprise. “Dajun, after all these years, I just realized you don’t like women! No wonder you never wanted to marry me.”

“You, you, you…” Nanmen Dajun was so furious that smoke nearly poured from his seven orifices. He pointed at the woman, trembling. “Stupid woman!”

He turned to the ashen-faced Yang Kai and hurriedly said, “Palace Master, this woman is crazy. Just ignore her!”

“Who are you calling crazy?” the woman glared.

Yang Kai stepped forward and said calmly, “I heard from Dajun that you’re a top-tier artificer. I came here with great sincerity and high expectations, but now… tsk tsk…”

“What do you mean, ‘tsk tsk’?” The woman was instantly displeased. She darted in front of Yang Kai, eyes wide, grabbed his collar, and demanded, “Explain yourself! Or else you’ll be sorry.”

“Let go!” Nanmen Dajun was startled and quickly shouted. He wasn’t worried about Yang Kai, but if Yang Kai got angry, the woman would definitely suffer. Though she was also at the First Stage of the Emperor Realm and quite strong, she was no match for Yang Kai.

But Yang Kai raised a hand to stop him, looking at the woman with disdain and sneering, “If you’re a master, you should act like one. From what I see, you don’t have a shred of a master’s bearing—more like a common street woman. Honestly, I’m very disappointed.”

The woman released his collar and snorted. “What a joke. In your eyes, a so-called master has to be all solemn and proper? I was born this way.”

Yang Kai chuckled. “So you really think you’re a master?”

The woman snorted coldly. “I won’t speak for anything else, but when it comes to the art of artifact refinement, there’s no one in the world who can surpass me.”

“With a mouth that big, be careful you don’t bite your tongue.”

The woman sneered, her confidence overflowing.

“Anyone can talk big. Let’s see what you can actually do.”

“You want to see me in action?” The woman glared at Yang Kai. Seeing his provocative look, she grew angry. “Fine, fine, Elder Sister will let you die knowing why.”

She reached out to Nanmen Dajun. “Give me some materials!”

Nanmen Dajun glanced at Yang Kai for confirmation. By now, he understood Yang Kai’s intentions—he clearly wanted to see the woman’s artifact refining skills, which was why he’d been so harsh with his words.

The woman wasn’t blind to Yang Kai’s motives either, but being challenged in her area of expertise, she couldn’t help but get angry. Even knowing Yang Kai was provoking her, she could only go along with it.

Seeing Yang Kai nod, Nanmen Dajun asked, “What materials do you want?”

“Anything!”

Only then did Nanmen Dajun start searching through his Spatial Ring. As the Chief Formation Master of Lingxiao Palace, he’d been busy with formation work lately, which required a lot of materials, so his ring was well-stocked.

He found a chunk of ore about the size of a human head and tossed it to the woman.

Yang Kai glanced at it and recognized it as a piece of Red Feather Steel, famous for its toughness. When refining Secret Treasures, adding a bit of it could increase their durability. But for that very reason, Red Feather Steel could only be used as an additive, not as the main material.

Yet Nanmen Dajun had chosen this piece of ore and nothing else, clearly wanting the woman to show off her skills.

“Keep your eyes wide open, Junior Brother. Don’t blink.” The woman took the Red Feather Steel, shot Yang Kai a cold smile, and her fingers began to dance. The chunk of ore spun rapidly in her hands.

She didn’t rush to refine it, but instead took out a jar of wine, took a big swig, then put the jar away. Opening her mouth, she sprayed a mist of wine out.

Whoosh!

A burst of fire shot out, and the temperature in the cave soared. The fire wasn’t a normal color, but a faint white—clearly a kind of Heavenly Flame.

This made Yang Kai’s eyes light up.

Whether artificers or alchemists, both had extremely high requirements for fire. Having an excellent flame could greatly improve the efficiency and quality of artifact and pill refinement.

With just this move, Yang Kai could tell the woman truly had real skill.

Not to mention, she hadn’t even taken out a Refining Furnace—she was clearly going to refine in midair. This kind of refining required an extremely high level of skill; ordinary people couldn’t do it at all.

What surprised Yang Kai even more was the woman’s exquisite control over the flame. That mouthful of spirit wine turned to fire and wrapped the Red Feather Steel perfectly—not a bit more, not a bit less, not a trace wasted.

Under the intense heat, the chunk of Red Feather Steel quickly melted. The woman held it in her delicate hand, her fingers moving nimbly, picking out impurities one by one as the ore shrank.

Just like condensing medicinal liquid during alchemy, the first step in artifact refinement was to remove impurities. This was the most basic step, but also the one that most tested an alchemist’s or artificer’s skill, directly affecting the final product’s quality.

Yang Kai extended his Divine Sense, carefully examining the state of the Red Feather Steel.

The result was astonishing. After about the time it takes to burn one stick of incense, the woman had removed every last impurity from the Red Feather Steel—nothing remained.

In this step, she had achieved perfection!

Just this alone was worthy of the title of master.

Yang Kai’s face showed approval. His previous prejudice against the woman, caused by her behavior, lessened a bit. As long as she had real skill, a few quirks in her lifestyle could be tolerated.

After a stick of incense, the chunk of Red Feather Steel had become a ball of pure molten ore, now only the size of a fist.

The woman looked up at Yang Kai with pride. “Junior Brother, there’s still time to beg for mercy. If you can give me a few million Source Crystals, Elder Sister will mercifully forgive your earlier rudeness.”

“Heh!” Yang Kai sneered.

“Won’t cry until you see the coffin, huh? I hate stubborn men like you the most!” The woman bit her silver teeth, talking to Yang Kai while her hands never stopped. Her fingers moved rapidly over the molten ore, sending out one seal after another, branding them into the liquid metal. The white flame rose and fell, clearly under her precise control.

Visibly, the molten metal was slowly stretched out, gradually taking on the shape of a sword.

As time passed and the woman continued, the sword’s form became clearer, its edges gleaming with a cold light, and intricate patterns appeared on the hilt.

This round of refining was rather dull—there were no particularly thrilling scenes—but both Yang Kai and Nanmen Dajun watched without blinking.

About an hour later, the woman suddenly grabbed the wine jar and took another swig. As she sprayed out a mouthful, the strange flame was instantly withdrawn. With a sizzling sound, the sword shimmered with a faint, ghostly light.

“It’s done!” The woman casually tossed the sword into the air. It fell straight down and landed firmly in the ground, sinking more than a foot deep.
