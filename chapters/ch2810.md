## Chapter 2810 — Sharing in the Glory

The barbarian tribe was always known for their simple, brutal fighting style. Charging forward was their instinct, but that only applied to ordinary barbarians.

Battles between Shamans were not reckless brawls, but contests of cultivation and mastery of Shamanic Arts.

When Ya accepted this Shaman Niu’s challenge, he had already devised several ways to defeat him instantly. He was just an Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice, nothing more, and under Ya’s hand, he could not stir up any waves. Whether in realm or battle experience, this Shaman Niu was utterly incomparable to him.

So when Ya saw Yang Kai charging straight at him, his mind was momentarily dazed.

Wasn’t he a Shaman? Why wasn’t he using Shamanic Arts, but instead acting as wild as an ordinary tribesman?

The sudden shock made him hesitate for a moment, but after all, he was an experienced Middle-Level Shaman Warrior. Ya quickly regained his composure, uttered ancient and rough words, and lightly waved his wooden staff forward. The air before him rippled, forming a solid, transparent shield.

Then, without pause, he pointed his staff ahead.

A burst of fire shot forth, a fireball the size of a basin howled toward Yang Kai.

Victory!

The two simplest Shamanic Arts, one defensive and one offensive, perfectly showcased Ya’s extraordinary skill as a Shaman. That fireball was something even a Lower-Level Shaman Warrior could not withstand, and this Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice could only meet his end.

Ya stood calmly in place, his eyes full of mockery, quietly enjoying the scene of the Shaman Niu being defeated by him.

As the fireball shot out, the villagers of Cangnan Village cried out in alarm, every face filled with worry and dread. Ah Hu shouted a warning, but Yang Kai ignored it, heading straight toward the fireball.

With a loud boom.

As the firelight blazed, the canyon was illuminated brightly, and no one could open their eyes. The hearts of the hundred villagers of Cangnan Village instantly sank to the bottom.

It’s over, it’s over, Ah Niu is finished. Hit head-on by such a huge fireball, how could he survive? The future hope of Cangnan Village had fallen just like that. Ah Hu clenched his fists, full of guilt, thinking he should have stopped Ah Niu from dueling Ya, even at the risk of offending the Barbarian God. At least that would have been better than losing his life.

Moments later, as Ah Hu and the others regained their sight and glared angrily toward Ya, their eyes suddenly widened, as if they’d seen a ghost. Some villagers even rubbed their eyes hard, wanting to see more clearly.

The Raging Flame Tribe members, who had been gloating, suddenly changed their expressions, stunned on the spot.

Ya stood stiffly in place, and the Shaman Niu, who should have fallen under that massive fireball, was somehow right in front of him, smiling. Ah Niu had no serious injuries, only his hair seemed a bit singed.

“Lord Ya, I think I won. What do you say?” Yang Kai looked up at Ya with a smile.

Ya’s face twitched a few times. No matter how he thought, he couldn’t understand how this scene had happened. His fireball should have hit the opponent, so how did he dodge it?

But to admit defeat just like that…

“The battle has only just begun!” Ya growled, ancient and rough words sounding again as he raised his staff and pointed it at Yang Kai.

But this time, he had no chance to cast Shamanic Art. As soon as the staff was raised, Yang Kai threw a hook punch.

The Shamanic Art shield in front of Ya twisted, like a balloon being squashed. The terrifying force quickly exceeded the shield’s limit, and it shattered with a bang.

Ya’s face contorted in rage, his whole body turning cold.

Having let Yang Kai close the distance, he didn’t dodge but insisted on casting a Shamanic Art because he had absolute confidence in his shield, believing this Shaman Niu couldn’t break it. So he stood his ground.

Who could have guessed that his proud Shamanic Art shield couldn’t even withstand a single punch?

The Shamanic Art was cast correctly, so why was it so fragile?

Boom…

His fist smashed into Ya’s face, twisting it instantly. Teeth flew as Ya’s body—nearly half again Yang Kai’s height—was sent soaring through the air like a paper kite before crashing heavily to the ground with a loud thud.

Before he could get up, Yang Kai pounced like a leopard, grabbed his neck with one hand, raised a clenched fist with the other, and grinned: “Lord Ya, will you admit defeat yourself, or shall I beat you until you do?”

The body that should have been weak now appeared as mighty as a towering mountain in Ya’s eyes.

“You… you’re cheating!” Ya shouted angrily, his eyes full of unwillingness.

If not for the Shaman Niu’s cunning, catching him off guard, he might not have lost this fight. He was a Middle-Level Shaman Warrior, and the other was just an Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice, two grades apart.

Yang Kai sneered, “Next time you meet a Barbaric Beast, will you expect it to compete in Shamanic Arts and not attack you up close?”

Ya’s expression froze, suddenly speechless.

“Let go of Lord Ya!”

“Release him now!”

The Raging Flame Tribe barbarians surged forward, surrounding Yang Kai layer upon layer, eyes red and hostility surging.

Hurting a noble Shaman was a grave crime for any barbarian, but seeing their Shaman pinned to the ground, the Raging Flame Tribe members couldn’t stand by.

Yang Kai turned, his gaze cold as he swept over the Raging Flame Tribe barbarians, curling his lips in a disdainful smile.

“Stand down!” Ya waved his hand and shouted.

The Raging Flame Tribe members, though reluctant, obeyed and retreated, but remained vigilant. If Yang Kai really harmed Ya, they would surely retaliate madly.

After experiencing defeat and humiliation, Ya’s expression calmed, and he sighed, “You’re right, I was careless!”

He didn’t admit his strength was inferior to Yang Kai, only that he was too careless, so he suffered defeat.

Yang Kai understood his thoughts and didn’t bother to say more, tilting his head, “So this battle…”

Ya said dejectedly, “You win. All the spoils are yours.”

There was no need to continue. Being pinned to the ground like a grasshopper, Ya had no face to struggle further.

“Thank you!” Yang Kai grinned, finally letting go of Ya’s neck and helping him up.

Ya spat out a mouthful of blood, his cheek burning with pain. Yang Kai’s punch had knocked out two teeth, and his face was numb. Now, as the numbness faded, the pain set in.

He said nothing more. The loser had no right to speak. He glanced at Yang Kai with a complicated look and waved, “Let’s go!”

The Raging Flame Tribe members said nothing, lining up behind their Shaman, ready to return to their village by night.

“Lord Ya, wait!”

Yang Kai called out to him.

“What is it?” Ya turned, puzzled, looking at Yang Kai.

“Does Lord Ya not want these beasts?” Yang Kai pointed at the pile of frozen beast corpses nearby.

Ya’s expression changed, shouting, “Shaman Niu, are you mocking me?”

Having lost the duel, he had no right to claim the beasts. Yang Kai’s question felt like a deliberate provocation.

Yang Kai shook his head, “I mean no offense. Lord Ya, you brought so many tribesmen from afar, surely to find winter provisions?”

Ya took a breath, “Aren’t you as well?”

Yang Kai smiled, “Since that’s so, I’m willing to share some with Lord Ya!”

“What?” Ya was stunned.

Even the Raging Flame Tribe barbarians were shocked, staring at Yang Kai in disbelief.

“I didn’t count closely last time, but there should be three or four hundred beasts in this canyon. Lord Ya, my villagers number just over a hundred. Even if each drags back two beasts, many will remain. With heavy snow coming, we won’t return after this. In other words, the remaining beasts will be frozen here. If Lord Ya doesn’t mind, you can take them back to your village. Though not many, if rationed, it should be enough for the winter.”

“You… you… you really mean it?” Ya could hardly believe his ears.

This Shaman Niu was actually willing to share his spoils? He was right, there were indeed three or four hundred dead beasts in the canyon, and they didn’t have enough people to carry them all. Even taking what was left, there would be at least a hundred. Each beast was huge, enough to feed a tribesman for a month. If rationed, it could last the whole winter.

But how could this be? Someone willing to hand over food already in their grasp.

It was like, in today’s world, sending your own wife to be toyed with by others. To Ya, it was utterly incomprehensible.

Still, if the Shaman Niu was sincere, the whole village would be grateful.

“Of course. In the name of the Barbarian God, I, Shaman Niu, give the remaining beasts to you!”

Yang Kai’s expression was solemn.

Ya no longer doubted. When a Shaman spoke in the name of the Barbarian God, there could be no falsehood.

He gazed quietly at Yang Kai for a long time, then bent over, hand on his chest, and said deeply, “Shaman Niu, please forgive my previous rudeness and arrogance. Thank you for your generosity and magnanimity. The villagers of Raging Flame Tribe’s Lianhuo Village will remember your kindness. You will always be our friend!”

The other villagers of Raging Flame Tribe’s Lianhuo Village also bent over, hands on their chests.

That was the highest ritual of the barbarian tribe.

Ah Hu and the others stood behind Yang Kai, their faces shining with pride!
