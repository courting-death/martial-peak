## Chapter 2812 — The Barbarian Family

As soon as the words were spoken, several barbarian warriors suddenly turned around, gripping their stone spears and axes, watching Yang Kai with wary eyes, their spines tingling with chills.

Although their hearts were concerned for their companion’s safety just now, their instincts as warriors still remained. Yet, not a single one had noticed someone approaching from behind—simply unbelievable. If the person approaching had any ill intent, they would all have met their end here.

After all, that was a Shaman Warrior!

The woman known as a Shaman also shuddered, but her reaction was not as intense as the warriors. She turned around and pressed her hand down, signaling her companions not to be so tense.

She immediately spoke, "I know he’s poisoned. I’ve already performed the Poison Extraction Art."

"The effect isn’t ideal," Yang Kai tilted his head, thought for a moment, and said, "Should I help?"

"You?" The woman frowned, her eyes showing distrust. First, this Shaman Warrior was a stranger—who knew if he was good or evil? It was normal for Shamans and barbarians from different tribes to clash outside. Second, Yang Kai was only a Lower-Level Shaman Warrior, while she, an Upper-Level Shaman Warrior, was helpless. What could Yang Kai possibly do?

Yang Kai shrugged, "I was just saying."

"Which tribe are you from?" the woman asked.

Yang Kai replied, "Southern Barbarian Tribe!" He paused, then said, "You’re from the Frost Snow Tribe, right?"

Thanks to the village chief’s crash course before he set out, Yang Kai could recognize the origins of these barbarians. In this ancient world, some tribes bore distinct traits—like the Raging Flame Tribe he’d encountered at the canyon, whose faces were painted with fiery lotus totems, the mark of their sacred flame.

The Frost Snow Tribe didn’t have any totem marks on their faces, but when the woman performed her shamanic art, the attribute of her spiritual power gave Yang Kai a clue.

Not every barbarian tribe had clear features. For example, the Southern Barbarian Tribe where Cangnan Village was located had none, so the woman couldn’t identify his origin.

Yang Kai’s words left the woman with no reason to hide. She nodded, admitting, “That’s right, we’re from the Frost Snow Tribe. Do you have a way to cure his poison?”

“We can give it a try.”

The woman frowned. Though she did not truly believe Yang Kai possessed such ability, there was no better option now—she could only treat a dead horse as if it were still alive. Her poisoned tribesman’s wound was suppressed by her shamanic art, and she had extracted some poison, but not all. The Bone-Eating Wolf’s poison was fierce—any residue could turn even the strongest barbarian into a puddle. If left alone, he’d die in three days!

But three days wasn’t enough to return to the tribe.

"Then try!" The woman stepped aside.

Yang Kai walked forward.

A Frost Snow Tribe warrior called out, "My lady..."

The woman raised her hand, stopping him.

Still, the warriors’ eyes were full of distrust as they watched Yang Kai.

Yang Kai didn’t mind. He went straight to the injured Frost Snow tribesman, squatted down, and examined the wound carefully. He was impressed.

It was thanks to the barbarian warrior’s robust physique and vigorous Qi and blood that he survived; otherwise, such a wound would have claimed his life ten times over. Now, though missing an arm and ravaged by poison, he uttered not a single sound, his mind astonishingly clear. Pain twisted his pale features, but his resolve remained unbroken.

After a while, Yang Kai suddenly raised his hand and slapped the warrior several times—not too hard, not too soft. His spiritual power surged. After a few strikes, the warrior’s pale face flushed red, and he spat out a lump of black blood.

Yang Kai was ready, dodged aside, and without hesitation shattered the frozen part of the broken arm, gripping his shoulders with both hands. As spiritual Qi surged, it poured into the warrior’s body.

Under the watchful eyes of the Frost Snow Tribe, the injured man’s body trembled violently, then he let out a roar like a wounded beast, startling everyone. The next moment, blood gushed from the broken arm, sizzling as it hit the ground, corroding holes—showing the poison’s ferocity.

The blood spurted for ten breaths. If not for the female Shaman’s intervention, the other warriors would have stopped Yang Kai long ago.

After ten breaths, Yang Kai withdrew his hands and turned to the woman, "Seal his wound."

Hearing this, the woman raised her hand, and cold air rushed to the broken arm, freezing the wound in the blink of an eye.

The injured warrior, having lost too much blood, was now pale as paper, his aura so weak it was almost undetectable, as if dead.

As Yang Kai stepped back, the warriors rushed over to check his condition.

Soon, one tribesman looked up, "He’s much better, just lost too much blood."

He glanced at Yang Kai, his gaze complicated.

The female Shaman finally nodded, turned to Yang Kai, and said, "I’m Yu. What’s your name?"

"Ah Niu!" Yang Kai smiled.

"So you’re Shaman Niu. Thank the Barbarian God for letting us meet here!" The always-cold Yu finally smiled.

Yang Kai said, “Though the poison in your companion has been removed, leaving him like this could still cause problems. It would be best to take him back for proper treatment.”

"I understand. Shaman Niu, please wait a moment."

As Yu spoke, she went to the warriors, whispered a few words, and soon one carried the injured man out of the cave—apparently to return to the village for treatment.

Returning, Yu had another warrior bring some dried meat and water, handing them to Yang Kai herself. "Thank you for saving my tribesman."

"Barbarians are one family, Lady Yu, no need for thanks." Yang Kai grinned, accepting the meat and water, eating and drinking heartily, without a care.

This bold behavior won Yu’s favor. The previous disdain she felt seeing Yang Kai’s thin frame vanished.

Barbarians are one family! That’s an interesting saying.

Yu asked sincerely, “Earlier, I also used the Poison Extraction Art, but why was your result so much better?” Before coming to this cave, she had already treated her tribesman’s wound, but some poison remained stubbornly inside. Unable to draw it out, she could only freeze the injury and have him take herbal medicine, hoping it would neutralize the toxins.

Though she knew the chances were slim.

"You mean your rank is higher than mine, but your Poison Extraction Art wasn’t as effective?" Yang Kai understood, smiled, "Sometimes, subtle control is more important than sheer power."

Yu pondered for a while, then nodded, "Thank you!"

Yang Kai finished the meat, drank some water, and said, "It’s freezing—what are you doing outside?"

Though the Frost Snow Tribe didn’t fear the cold, they wouldn’t normally go out. In winter, beasts and Barbaric Beasts are active searching for food, making it much riskier than usual.

"What about you, Shaman Niu?" Yu didn’t answer, but asked back.

"Cultivating!" Yang Kai grinned.

Yu was stunned, showing admiration. "We’re out gathering herbs for the spring ritual."

“Rituals…” Yang Kai raised his brows. Cangnan Village was just a small place—he wasn’t sure if they even held such ceremonies. But he knew that in ancient times, rituals were common. Even powerful Shamans would frequently perform them, offering sacrifices either to the heavens or to their tribe’s totem—usually the latter.

He smiled, "Looks like your progress isn’t smooth."

Yu said sadly, "Those herbs are guarded by several Bone-Eating Wolves. We were ambushed."

Yang Kai raised an eyebrow, interest flickering in his eyes. “How many Bone-Eating Wolves were there, exactly?”

Yu replied, "Six. If not, with my strength, they wouldn’t have hurt my tribesman."

Yang Kai’s eyes flashed. After a moment, he sat upright and asked seriously, "Lady Yu, when you go gather herbs again, do you need a strong helper?"

Yu stared at Yang Kai, her eyes brightening.

She had just been wondering if she could ask Shaman Niu for help, but they weren’t familiar, not from the same tribe, and she had nothing to offer. She hesitated to ask, but he volunteered.

Though the self-proclaimed strong helper sounded a bit odd, it didn’t matter. Two Shamans were enough to ensure the others’ safety.

"Shaman Niu, what’s your condition?" Yu was happy but remained cautious. A Southern Barbarian Shaman Warrior, suddenly offering help, must want something.

"Those Barbaric Beasts are mine," Yang Kai replied with a smile.

Normally, Yu wouldn’t agree. The herbs were precious, but the beasts were valuable too. They had several people, an Upper-Level Shaman Warrior, and Yang Kai was only Lower-Level. Giving him two beasts was acceptable.

But since Yang Kai had saved her tribesman, Yu quickly agreed, "Fine, the beasts are yours, the herbs are ours!"

With her decision, the others had no objections.

Yang Kai stood up, "No time to waste, let’s go!"

Yu frowned, "But my clansmen just fought a battle—they need a day to rest..."

The barbarians possessed formidable physiques, but after the fierce battle, Yu had cast the Bloodlust Art to empower them. Though its effect lasted only a short while, the loss of innate Essence-Blood left them somewhat weakened, no longer at their peak strength and vigor.

“No need to make things so complicated—just follow me,” Yang Kai said, striding out of the cave and beckoning to Yu and the others.

The warriors hesitated, looking at Yu. Yu was exasperated, thinking Shaman Niu was so impulsive, acting on a whim.

But things had come to this, so she could only lead the team and follow.
