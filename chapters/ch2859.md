## Chapter 2859 — A Thunderous Strike

Under Shaya's scrutinizing gaze, Yang Kai appeared calm, as if he truly was just curious about the demon heart.

Shaya smiled faintly and, unexpectedly, tossed the demon heart to Yang Kai, saying lightly, "Be careful, don't break it."

Yang Kai took it and fiddled with it, turning it over and over in his hands.

Yu was so nervous she could hear her own heartbeat. She looked at Yang Kai with hope, momentarily forgetting he was her sister's killer. Only one thought echoed in her mind: Crush that demon heart!

If Yang Kai could really crush the demon heart and kill the Demon King before her, then Lu's death would not be in vain. If Yang Kai truly did this, it would mean everything before was a lie, and he had only surrendered to the demons to find a chance to kill the Demon King. Even if Lu died for this, she could forgive him.

But to her disappointment, Yang Kai simply observed the demon heart for a long time, showing no intention of acting.

"What are you hesitating for? Crush it already!" Yu finally couldn't help but shout.

Shaya glanced at her with a smile, her eyes full of mockery.

Yang Kai raised his hand and tossed the demon heart back, saying, "So what now? You didn't just take out your demon heart to show me something interesting, did you?"

This scene made Yu's face instantly pale. Her body went limp and she collapsed to the ground, as if all her strength had left her.

Shaman Niu had truly defected to the demons, and Lu had died for nothing. How laughable that she had just now held a sliver of hope for this spineless coward. Having such thoughts was truly shameful.

Shaya, having retrieved her demon heart, now wore a radiant smile, her expression one of deep satisfaction. She said, "Of course, it's not just for you to look at."

As she spoke, without any visible movement, a strand of pitch-black blood seeped from the Demon Heart in her hand. The droplet was round and full, brimming with unimaginable demonic power. Shaya guided it before Yang Kai and smiled, “Swallow it. From this moment on, you belong to me.”

Without hesitation, Yang Kai swallowed the demon blood in one gulp. As he grunted, dark energy surged across his body.

Shaya pursed her lips and giggled, looking at Yang Kai approvingly. "You made a wise choice."

"Thank you for giving me this opportunity, my lady!" Yang Kai replied calmly.

"Now then..." Shaya stepped forward, her gaze sweeping over the chaotic battlefield. The demons were already routed, and even her strongest cavalry could not reverse the situation. Yet there was no panic in Shaya's eyes—only confidence in victory. She raised her hand and declared, "Let us end this ridiculous war!"

The Barbarian Tribe's commander had already defected. Though many remained, they were no longer a concern. As long as she and her newly recruited slave joined forces, victory would be assured.

"By the way, I haven't asked your name yet," Shaya asked without turning her head.

"Shaman Niu! My name is Shaman Niu!" Yang Kai replied.

As he spoke, power surged within him. Suddenly, a golden light burst from beneath his feet, piercing deep into the earth. The next moment, muffled groans echoed from underground.

One by one, the hidden Sand Demons were killed by Yang Kai without resistance.

Shaya was momentarily stunned. Before she could turn around, she felt a sharp pain in her chest. Looking down, she saw the broad tip of a sword piercing through her ample bosom, trailing a stream of blood.

Shaya's beautiful eyes trembled violently. She could hardly believe what she was seeing.

On the other side, Yu also cried out in shock, stunned by the sudden turn of events.

"How does it feel, my lady?" Yang Kai's voice came from behind, utterly calm, as if he were casually asking whether she had eaten. Shaya coughed several times, blood spilling from her lips, her face twisted as she snarled, "You swallowed my demon blood—how can you still defy my will?"

If not for this, she would never have let her guard down and exposed her back to Shaman Niu.

Shaman Niu had passed every test she set for him.

When she demanded proof of loyalty, he brought over two of his own shamans and even killed one in front of her.

Tossing him the demon heart so casually was also a test. If Shaman Niu had attacked then, Shaya had ways to counter. Even if the demon heart was crushed, she would not have died. But he showed no sign of rebellion, and after examining the demon heart, he simply returned it.

If he truly intended to rebel, that would have been the best moment to strike.

From that moment, Shaya truly believed him.

Having him swallow her demon blood was the final safeguard. Once he did, he would be hers, his life and death in her hands, unable to resist in the slightest.

That was why she felt safe exposing her back to him.

Yet what she received was genuine betrayal—a thunderous, overwhelming strike. His attack came without warning, as if on a whim, but such decisive action clearly showed it was premeditated.

Agonizing pain shot through her demon heart. Shaya could feel it being pierced by a sharp weapon. Even as a Demon King, she couldn't help but tremble, nearly collapsing to the ground.

"Hahaha!" Shaya laughed instead of raging, tilting her head with difficulty to glance at Yang Kai from the corner of her eye. "To gain my trust, you even killed one of your own?"

"To get what you want, you always have to pay a price," Yang Kai replied lightly.

Shaya looked at Yu in the distance and said, "Did you hear that? In his eyes, you are nothing but pawns for victory. When necessary, he can sacrifice you all."

Yu frowned, but her emotions did not fluctuate much. When she saw Yang Kai suddenly attack Shaya, she knew her earlier guess was correct.

Lord Shaman Niu had indeed gone to such lengths to get close to the Demon King, even seemingly killing her own sister.

Lu's death... must have been worthwhile, right?

"Enough with your lies. Your life is in my hands now," Yang Kai sneered coldly.

Shaya's expression was venomous. "How did you do it?"

What puzzled her most was how Yang Kai could defy her will. By rights, after swallowing her demon blood, none of his thoughts should escape her perception. Any hint of rebellion would have been detected in advance.

Yet even after he struck, even as her demon heart was pierced, she sensed no ill intent from him.

How could the demon heart's blood have failed?

"No comment!" Yang Kai replied. As he finished speaking, his Divine Soul power surged forth like a tidal wave, enveloping Shaya.

Shaya instinctively tried to resist, but Yang Kai's Myriad Sword merely twisted slightly, and the searing pain rendered all her efforts futile.

An invisible vortex appeared and began to swirl. As it spun, Shaya suddenly vanished without a trace.

Yang Kai swung the Myriad Sword, flinging the blood from its blade, and let out a light sigh.

The Shadow Demons who had always guarded Shaya were all stunned, still unable to react.

Yang Kai ran his fingers along the Myriad Sword. As the sword's light flared, screams rang out and blood sprayed everywhere. All the Shadow Demons were slain.

Yu regained her freedom and looked at Yang Kai with a complicated gaze, trying hard not to resent him. Yet the image of her sister's tragic death kept replaying in her mind.

"Lu isn't dead!" Yang Kai grinned at her.

"What?" Yu was stunned. "What did you say?"

Did she mishear? Or did he misspeak? Lu had been crushed before her eyes—how could she not be dead?

Yang Kai said, "I thought you sisters had a special bond and would sense something, but it seems you can't... Well, that's understandable. After all, there's a world barrier between you. If you could sense her, that would be too strange."

Yu had no idea what he was talking about, but the next moment, what she saw made her burst into tears.

Without a sound, Lu—who should have exploded into a cloud of blood—suddenly appeared before her. All Yu saw was Yang Kai wave his hand.

"The one I crushed was another corpse," Yang Kai explained.

Earlier, he had his back to Shaya, so she hadn't seen the details. Just before crushing Lu, Yang Kai tossed her into the Small Xuan Realm, and the body that burst apart was a corpse he had picked up from the battlefield.

Such a method was no different from snatching chestnuts from a blazing fire—any slight mistake would have been enough for Shaya to see through the ruse.

Fortunately, though Yang Kai was only a Great Shaman Master, he had the foundation of the Emperor Realm, so the switch went smoothly.

"Lu..." Yu's eyes instantly filled with tears.

Lu was a bit dazed, completely unaware of what had happened. She only remembered Shaman Niu grabbing her, then her vision blurred and she entered a mysterious, silent world.

In that world, she was trapped in a space ten zhang across, unable to escape or see anyone. Just as she was trying to get out, she suddenly returned to where she started.

Hearing her sister's voice, Lu instinctively moved closer to her, then warily watched Yang Kai.

Yu said, "Lord Shaman Niu didn't betray us. Everything just now was an act to gain the Demon King's trust."

Lu asked blankly, "It was fake?"

When Yang Kai grabbed her by the neck, she thought she was dead for sure, because his eyes showed no emotion—he truly looked like he was going to kill her.

Yu nodded. "Yes, thanks to Lord Shaman Niu. The Demon King... was killed, right?"

Shaya had vanished mysteriously, and Yu didn't know what had become of her.

Yang Kai smiled at them and explained, "I dealt with her a couple of days ago, and she suffered a loss. So if I didn't use some tricks, I wouldn't have been able to get close, let alone make her drop her guard. She also had Sand Demons to help her and could escape at any time. Who knows what other trump cards she had? I had no choice but to use this risky plan. Sorry for making you worry."

The twins shook their heads in unison. Yu said, "It's nothing. I was the one who misunderstood you earlier..."

Yang Kai waved his hand. “In that situation, your doubts were only natural. Enough talk—let us end this war together.”
