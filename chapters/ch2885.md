## Chapter 2885 — Shaman King

The Great Shaman glanced at everyone, revealing a meaningful smile. "The Southern Barbarian Tribe will be destroyed."

Yu furrowed her brows, and suddenly her heart sank as she understood the meaning behind the other party's words. The thought that emerged made her shudder, and though Lu seemed to want to say more, her elder sister tugged her sleeve, so she fell silent.

Yu walked back and went straight to Die, lowering her voice, "Can't you escape?"

Among everyone present, Die was the strongest, at the realm of Great Shaman Master. If anyone could escape from here, it would be her. Yet ever since being captured, Die had quietly stayed put, showing no intention of fleeing.

Die shook her head at these words.

"Aren't you from the Drifting Tribe? You're best at hiding."

Die replied, "But I'm only a Great Shaman Master. There are five Shaman Kings here."

Yu had no words to respond. The vast difference in strength meant Die had no hope of escaping from here.

Yu said, "They'll eat us. Someone must escape and find Lord Shaman Niu!"

Die softly said, "He will come. We won't be eaten."

"But..." Yu's face was anxious. The Bone-Eater shaman's words just now made her realize something: the more than twenty thousand people of Shaman Niu's tribe were imprisoned here not just because they mingled with the Demon King, but because the Bone-Eater Tribe treated them as stored food.

Over a hundred thousand Bone-Eater Tribe members were gathered here, and they needed to eat and drink. But in this war-torn world, where would so much food come from? The twenty thousand of Shaman Niu's tribe were a feast delivered to their door.

That was why the Great Shaman said the Southern Barbarian Tribe would be destroyed.

"Isn't he here now?" Die suddenly pointed forward with her lips, smiling slightly, as if she had expected this all along.

Yu, Lu, and the others quickly turned to look, and the next moment, their eyes lit up.

On the high platform, Lord Shaman Niu's figure had appeared at some point, standing between Bao Qi and Shaman Chi, grabbing Shaman Chi's knife-wielding right hand.

The sudden appearance of a person stunned the more than one hundred thousand Bone-Eater Tribe members for a moment. Shaman Chi's face changed dramatically, instinctively channeling his shamanic power to resist, only to find his bound right hand couldn't break free at all.

Looking up, the face that had haunted his sleepless nights appeared before his eyes.

"It's you!" Shaman Chi growled in a low voice, his expression suddenly turning ferocious.

"Shaman Chi, you've grown old," Yang Kai looked at him indifferently.

Shaman Chi's face twitched at these words, and he shouted harshly, "What Shamanic Art did you use on me?"

Today's Shaman Chi was indeed much older than half a year ago. When Yang Kai first met him, Shaman Chi looked about forty, but now he seemed ten or twenty years older.

For a Shaman King, this was most unusual.

But a Shaman King struck by Years Like a Shuttle was an exception.

Years Like a Shuttle was a divine ability of the Great Emperor of Time, possessing the power to control the flow of time. Outside Cangnan Village, Yang Kai, at the Lower-Level Great Shaman Master realm, activated the Years Like a Shuttle seal, and Shaman Chi was struck. Though not fatal, it still affected him, and this aging of ten or twenty years was the mark left by Years Like a Shuttle.

Shaman Chi didn't know what this Shamanic Art was, and every time he thought of it, he felt a chill. His hatred only grew deeper.

"You won't understand, nor do you need to!" Yang Kai replied calmly. This was a divine ability from a Great Emperor tens of thousands of years in the future—how could the Shaman Kings of this era comprehend it? With a forceful tug, Shaman Chi staggered back several steps.

"Bao Qi is mine!" Yang Kai stared straight at Shaman Chi. "He shouldn't have to endure such torment!"

"My lord!" Bao Qi, suppressing his trembling body, called out softly. He had thought he would surely die here, but Yang Kai's appearance gave him hope of survival.

Though there were five Shaman Kings here, he believed Lord Shaman Niu could save him. It was pure instinct, and Bao Qi believed it completely.

Yang Kai nodded. Light flashed around his hand as he tapped Bao Qi. The Shamanic Art binding Bao Qi instantly collapsed, granting him freedom once more.

"How dare you release him!" Shaman Chi roared. The Bone-Eater Tribe had gone to great lengths to capture this Demon King, and now Shaman Niu had released him as soon as he arrived, completely disregarding the Bone-Eater Tribe.

"Open your mouth!" Yang Kai ignored him and instructed Bao Qi.

Bao Qi obediently opened his mouth, and the next moment, something shot from Yang Kai's hand into his throat.

"Refine it."

Bao Qi immediately sat cross-legged, channeling his power to refine what he had swallowed. Instantly, a cool sensation spread throughout his body, greatly reducing his pain, especially in the arm that had been stripped to the bone, which now tingled pleasantly.

The sight nearly made Shaman Chi explode with rage. He glared at Yang Kai, not rushing to act, but instead sneered, "Shaman Niu, I should thank you."

"Thank me for what?" Yang Kai looked up at him.

Shaman Chi channeled his shamanic power, and a powerful aura instantly spread out.

Yang Kai raised an eyebrow. "Oh? Middle-Level Shaman King now? Congratulations."

Shaman Chi said, "If not for you, I wouldn't have advanced to Middle-Level Shaman King so quickly. Last time I suffered a great loss at your hands and was badly injured, but I learned from my shame. While healing, I gained some insight, and after fighting the demons these days, I broke through from Lower-Level to Middle-Level Shaman King."

"No need to thank me. It was your own effort," Yang Kai smiled faintly.

Shaman Chi's face twisted. This was not the response he wanted. He shouted, "Shaman Niu, the Southern Barbarian Tribe is already destroyed. If you want to live, you'd better know your place!"

"The Southern Barbarian Tribe isn't destroyed!" Yang Kai's expression was solemn. "The Southern Barbarian Tribe still has me and my subordinates."

Shaman Chi laughed loudly. "Just you, a Great Shaman Master? All the Shaman Kings of the Southern Barbarian Tribe are dead. Without Shaman Kings, a tribe has no right to exist. Don't you know that?"

Among the Barbarians, tribes often swallowed each other up. For example, Cangnan Village—if Yang Kai hadn't appeared, once the old village chief died, a village without a shaman would soon be swallowed by others.

The same was true for all the large and small tribes.

The most important standard for a large tribe's existence was having a Shaman King. Without one, a large tribe lost its foundation.

"So as long as there's a Shaman King?" Yang Kai closed his eyes as he spoke. "Then as you wish."

When he opened his eyes again, a brilliant light flashed within them, and an immensely powerful aura suddenly surged from his body.

Shaman Chi's expression changed. He thought Yang Kai was about to attack, which he actually hoped for, so he could retaliate and settle old scores.

But to his shock, Yang Kai had no intention of fighting him. Instead, his aura grew stronger and fiercer, quickly surpassing the level of a Great Shaman Master and approaching that of a Shaman King.

"This is..." Shaman Chi was dumbfounded.

Before he could finish, Yang Kai's aura suddenly soared, as if some invisible shackle had been broken, raising his realm to a new height.

Shaman Chi's eyes trembled, almost unable to believe what he was seeing.

"So this is the Shaman King Realm... nothing special," Yang Kai grinned at him.

Not far away, the twenty thousand people of Shaman Niu's tribe, who had been imprisoned, suddenly erupted in cheers after a brief silence, all of them overwhelmed with excitement—especially Yu and Lu. A Shaman King! The Southern Barbarian Tribe still had a Shaman King! That meant in this war between the two races, the Southern Barbarian Tribe still had autonomy and didn't have to depend on or become a vassal of other large tribes.

The cheers were quickly suppressed.

Several figures suddenly appeared around the high platform, taking positions at the four corners to surround it. Each exuded a deep aura, revealing the presence of Shaman Kings. They were the Bone-Eater Tribe's Shaman Kings stationed in Cang’er Valley. Previously, they hadn't shown themselves because they thought it unnecessary—Yang Kai was just a Great Shaman Master, easily handled by Shaman Chi.

But after Yang Kai inexplicably advanced to the Shaman King Realm, they had no choice but to appear.

Among them, one bald Shaman King's aura was the strongest, seemingly higher than the others. He looked at Yang Kai with a dignified gaze and said, "I've never seen a Shaman King advance so easily."

"That just means your vision is too narrow." Even facing an Upper-Level Shaman King two Stages above him, Yang Kai remained calm and spoke confidently.

"It's said you've received the Barbarian God's favor. I didn't believe it before, but now it seems possible." The bald Shaman King scrutinized Yang Kai, as if trying to see through him.

"You've heard of me?" Yang Kai asked, somewhat surprised.

The bald Shaman King glanced at Shaman Chi and spoke, "One who can defeat the strong despite being weak and injure Shaman Chi—naturally, I've heard a thing or two about such a person."

Shaman Chi's face turned ugly, glaring at Yang Kai with hatred, as if an unhealed wound had been sprinkled with salt.

"However..." the bald Shaman King continued, "even if you broke through to Shaman King on the spot, it doesn't matter. The Southern Barbarian Tribe is already destroyed. That's a fact no one can change."

"I'm still alive, and my twenty thousand subordinates are still alive. We can rebuild the Southern Barbarian Tribe!" Yang Kai said in a deep voice. "Don't speak so absolutely."

The bald Shaman King shook his head. "The Southern Barbarian Tribe's sacred artifact is lost. You... where did you get that?"

His gaze suddenly fixed on the wooden carving Yang Kai was tossing in his hand. The Bone-Eater Tribe also had a similar wooden carving, but it was in the hands of the Shaman Saint. Even as an Upper-Level Shaman King, he had no right to touch it.

The sacred artifact was the symbol of authority for every large tribe. Most tribesmen thought it was just a symbol, but only a few Shaman Kings knew it had many other uses.

Obtaining the sacred artifact was of immense help to a Shaman King's cultivation.
