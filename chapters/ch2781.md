## Chapter 2781 — To the Sea

It was certainly unrealistic to train one from scratch; cultivating an artificer required not only a massive consumption of resources, but also the accumulation of time.

It might take decades, or even centuries…

Lingxiao Palace had only just been founded—how could it possibly have had the time to nurture a true Artifact Refiner? Perhaps once the Sect had taken firm root, they could consider cultivating their own refiners. But for now, such a thing was simply impossible…

Hua Qingsi had clearly considered this as well and asked, “Palace Master, why not recruit an artificer? The Sect isn’t lacking Source Crystals these days. If we offer a good price, someone will surely be willing to serve.”

“En.” Yang Kai nodded. “I’ll leave this matter to you.”

“Palace Master, I have someone in mind.”

Nanmen Dajun suddenly interjected, “If it’s that person, the Mountain Wave Diagram is nothing at all.”

“Oh? You know an artificer?” Yang Kai looked at him in surprise. “If that’s the case, then invite him over. If this works out, Lingxiao Palace will not treat him poorly.”

Nanmen Dajun chuckled awkwardly, a bit embarrassed. “That person is indeed skilled, and his artificing methods are extraordinary, but he’s a bit troublesome. If I go alone… I might not be able to persuade him.”

“Such a big shot?” Yang Kai was amazed. “You’re a Formation Master—who would dare not give you face?”

Nanmen Dajun scratched his face. “Actually, I’m quite easygoing…”

Hua Qingsi looked over with utter disdain. After spending quite some time together, she had a fair grasp of Nanmen Dajun’s temperament. It was only because Lingxiao Palace had shown impressive wealth and strength, and because Yang Kai had once casually destroyed Wenqing Sect, that Nanmen Dajun, this Formation Master, could be kept in check. Otherwise, if it were someone else or another Sect, Nanmen Dajun would show you what it meant to be arrogant and look down on everyone.

Any Artificer, Alchemist, or Formation Master with some skill was bound to be proud. Only the Palace Master, this Imperial-Grade Alchemist, usually kept a low profile—he was an exception.

“Then let the Chief Manager accompany you.” Yang Kai waved his hand.

“Alright.” Hua Qingsi nodded.

Nanmen Dajun said awkwardly, “I think, Palace Master, you should go in person.”

Yang Kai laughed, “This Palace Master is busy with countless affairs. How could I have time to run around everywhere?”

Hua Qingsi was also angry. “I am the Chief Steward of Lingxiao Palace! Who would dare look down on me? Unless that person is an Imperial-Grade Artificer?”

Nanmen Dajun’s expression turned solemn, and he nodded slightly.

Yang Kai’s expression changed, surprised. “An Imperial-Grade Artificer?”

“Exactly. So, Palace Master, it’s best if you go yourself. That will show our sincerity.”

Yang Kai stroked his chin, his eyes turning thoughtful. “If that’s the case, it’s certainly worth making the trip. But, Dajun… in your opinion, is there any chance this Imperial-Grade Artificer could be persuaded to work for Lingxiao Palace?”

Nanmen Dajun’s expression became strange. “Palace Master, you want to recruit this fellow into the Sect?”

“Yes.” Yang Kai nodded as if it were obvious. “From what you said, this Imperial-Grade Artificer should be unaffiliated. Lingxiao Palace is in need of such talent. It’s rare to encounter one—how could we let him slip by?”

“Hmm…” Nanmen Dajun suddenly looked troubled. After hesitating for a while, he said, “Palace Master, if it’s just a short-term cooperation—after he helps me set up the Mountain Wave Diagram and leaves—he’s a great choice. But if you want to keep him in Lingxiao Palace… I wouldn’t recommend it.”

Yang Kai chuckled. “They say those in the same trade are rivals. You’re a Formation Master, he’s an Artificer. You’re skilled in different domains—there shouldn’t be any conflict, right?”

“That’s not the issue. It’s just… sigh, Palace Master, if you see what he’s like, you’ll understand.”

Regarding this Imperial-Grade Artificer, Nanmen Dajun seemed rather apprehensive and didn’t explain in detail. The more Yang Kai asked, the more he insisted Yang Kai see for himself, lest his words give Yang Kai preconceived notions.

But since there was such a free-spirited Imperial-Grade Artificer, Yang Kai could only go with Nanmen Dajun to pay a visit.

Fortunately, there was nothing urgent to attend to lately.

“By the way, is there still no news from Zhu Qing?”

Before leaving, Yang Kai suddenly turned to ask Hua Qingsi.

Hua Qingsi shook her head. “Palace Master, are you worried about her? If you are, I can have on of the Yao Kings look for her.”

“No need.” Yang Kai waved his hand and casually tossed out a jade slip without looking back. “This is Star God Palace’s decision regarding you. Take a look.”

Hua Qingsi trembled, reached out to catch it, and sent her Divine Sense inside to check. Soon, her nervous expression faded, replaced by a beaming smile.

The decision regarding her in the jade slip was simple: Hua Qingsi was still a disciple of Star God Palace, but Star God Palace would not restrict her freedom. In other words, she could stay wherever she wished—even if she never returned to Star God Palace, it didn’t matter.

Although it was just a simple jade slip and not an official document from Star God Palace, it bore Princess Lan Xun’s Soul Brand. With this jade slip, even though Hua Qingsi still held the title of Star God Palace disciple, it was as if she had left the sect. She would never have to worry about this matter again.

This was also the result of Yang Kai’s discussion with Lan Xun a few days prior.

Lan Xun, as the daughter of the Radiant Moon Great Emperor, held a position second only to one in Star God Palace—higher than even the Elders. But Hua Qingsi was now an Emperor Realm expert. Even Lan Xun had no right to expel her at will. She gave Yang Kai face; since she couldn’t expel Hua Qingsi, she would no longer restrict her freedom.

Outside the gates of Lingxiao Palace, Nanmen Dajun casually tossed out an object, which suddenly expanded in midair, transforming into a small boat. He smiled, “Palace Master, please!”

Yang Kai glanced at the flying boat, raising his brows. “This is a fine item.”

This was clearly a Secret Treasure for flight, and it was even of Imperial Treasure grade. “Fine” was hardly enough to describe it.

A flying Secret Treasure of Imperial-Grade—there probably weren’t many in the entire Star Realm. Its value was at least tens of millions of Upper-Level Source Crystals. For Nanmen Dajun to own one showed his considerable wealth.

Nanmen Dajun gave a dry laugh. “I won it in a bet. I couldn’t afford to buy it.”

“Such good fortune.” Yang Kai clicked his tongue in amazement and stepped onto the flying boat.

The flying boat didn’t look large to the naked eye—about one zhang long, enough for three to five people to stand side by side. But as Yang Kai lifted his foot, Nanmen Dajun suddenly formed a hand seal.

Instantly, a vortex-like passage appeared on the flying boat.

Yang Kai stepped into the vortex, and his vision changed abruptly—he froze on the spot.

What met his eyes was no simple flying boat. The interior space was vast, with a grand hall at the front that could easily accommodate dozens of people without feeling crowded. Sweeping his Divine Sense inside, he found several luxuriously decorated private rooms.

There was even a staircase at the side, winding upward.

The flying boat actually had two levels inside—it was practically a massive multi-deck ship.

Nanmen Dajun’s figure appeared beside Yang Kai.

Yang Kai turned to glance at him. “You really won this treasure in a bet?”

Previously, Yang Kai had thought this was just a simple flying Secret Treasure, worth a few tens of millions of Upper-Level Source Crystals. But now, its value was at least a hundred million—maybe even more.

“Just lucky!” Nanmen Dajun chuckled awkwardly, clearly not wanting to discuss the topic further. He gestured, “Palace Master, find a place to rest. The destination isn’t far or near—about three days’ journey.”

“Thank you for your trouble.” Yang Kai nodded, ascended the staircase to the second floor, and casually picked a private room. Pushing the door open, he swept his gaze around and found the room luxuriously decorated, yet with a refined touch. Off to the side stood a lifelike sculpture of a monkey’s head, one hand shading its eyes as it gazed into the distance, vivid and full of spirit.

Yang Kai frowned slightly, suddenly feeling that the monkey head looked familiar.

Just then, the flying boat hummed and shot forward at high speed. In the blink of an eye, it was moving as fast as a Third Stage Emperor Realm  expert at full speed.

This made Yang Kai appreciate the treasure even more.

He leapt onto the bed, sat cross-legged, and closed his eyes to meditate.

The journey was extremely smooth—there wasn’t the slightest vibration, nor did any blind fools dare try to rob them.

Then again, with the flying boat moving so fast, probably only a Third Stage Emperor Realm expert could hope to stop it.

Thus, three days passed in a flash.

The flying boat suddenly slowed down, then hummed again and came to a stop.

Yang Kai opened his eyes as a knock sounded at the door.

He got up and opened the door to find Nanmen Dajun standing outside. “We’re almost there, Palace Master. We’ll have to fly the rest of the way.”

“Is there some restriction blocking the flying boat?” Yang Kai asked.

“Not exactly.” Nanmen Dajun gave an embarrassed laugh. “It’s just that we can’t let that guy see the flying boat, or it’ll cause trouble.”

He was vague, but Yang Kai didn’t press. He disembarked with Nanmen Dajun.

Immediately, the sound of surging waves filled his ears, and a salty sea breeze brushed past, making him feel refreshed. The sky was a clear blue, not a cloud in sight.

After this journey, they had arrived above the sea.

“That person lives on an island?” Yang Kai asked in surprise.

“Exactly.” Nanmen Dajun nodded and led the way. “This way.”

The two of them sped off. Although Nanmen Dajun had Emperor Realm cultivation, he wasn’t skilled in combat, and his Emperor Yuan wasn’t especially pure, so his speed wasn’t very fast.

Yang Kai followed at a leisurely pace. Looking down, the sea stretched endlessly, with the occasional sea beast surfacing—quite interesting.

After an hour, the cries of seabirds suddenly reached Yang Kai’s ears. He immediately understood—they must be close. On this vast sea, only near islands would such ordinary birds appear.
