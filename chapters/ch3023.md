## Chapter 3023 — You Insist on Pretending Not to Know Me?

On a street in Half-Dragon City, the crowd was utterly silent.

When Li Jiao turned around again, his face had regained its icy expression, and he advanced step by step toward the sinister man.

After being viciously slapped, the sinister man was left dizzy and disoriented. Once he steadied himself, he suddenly saw Li Jiao standing right in front of him. Humiliated and enraged, he opened his mouth and cursed, "You fucking dare hit me?"

Li Jiao lowered his eyes and swept out another slap.

A crisp smack rang out, and the sinister man's other cheek swelled high as well.

Suppressed by both bloodline and cultivation, he had no power to fight back against Li Jiao and could only passively endure the beating.

"Bastard! Fuck you..."

Before he could finish, Li Jiao punched him in the face, immediately splitting his skin and flesh.

"You..."

Bang...

Another punch.

The surrounding onlookers fell silent as cicadas in winter, feeling a chill run from their heads all the way to the soles of their feet.

Li Jiao seemed to have flown into a complete rage. Ignoring everything else, he threw himself forward, straddled the sinister man, and pummeled his face with both fists. Smacks and thuds rang out without end, leaving the sinister man without even a chance to speak.

Although his attacks were ruthless, he exercised perfect restraint.

After all, he was a stranger in an unfamiliar place. Though he wanted to help the mother and daughter vent their anger, he did not strike to kill. Thus, despite the sinister man's miserable state, his life was not in danger.

"Friend, show mercy where you can. This man... is not someone you can afford to offend."

A voice suddenly drifted into his ears. Someone had likely sent Li Jiao a secret transmission. Hearing it, Li Jiao frowned, and his movements slowed considerably. After giving the man such a beating, much of his fury had also been vented, and he was no longer as irrational as before.

The woman he had rescued finally returned to her senses and cried out urgently, "Stop hitting him! Please stop, Sir!"

She was not pleading for the sinister man. After suffering such humiliation at his hands and having her own daughter witness it, she wished she could kill him herself and wash away the shame. How could she possibly plead for him? She was worried about Li Jiao. If this man were truly beaten to death, Li Jiao would likely have no way to survive either.

Her heart was filled with guilt and unease, and she felt that she had dragged Li Jiao into this.

Li Jiao viciously punched the sinister man twice more before finally stopping, then coldly snorted, "What a piece of shit!"

The woman seized the opportunity to rush forward and grab his arm, pleading, "Please stop, Sir. Don't hit him anymore."

Li Jiao took the opportunity to rise, staring coldly at the sinister man lying on the ground as he declared in a chilling voice, "I'll spare your life this time. If there is a next time, I will tear you into ten thousand pieces."

The sinister man's face was now covered in blood and beaten almost beyond recognition. Whether from shame and fury or because he was genuinely badly injured, he lay motionless on the ground as though dead.

Li Jiao kicked him once more before turning to the woman. With a somewhat complicated expression, he asked, "Are you all right?"

The woman shook her head, not daring to meet his gaze, and gently released his arm.

She was also in the Emperor Realm. Although she had been bullied and suffered some minor injuries just now, they were nothing serious.

"Good." Li Jiao nodded.

The woman lowered her head and lightly bit her red lips. "I thank Sir for stepping in to help, but Sir... should leave quickly. You cannot remain in Half-Dragon City."

Li Jiao frowned upon hearing this. Both the woman's current reaction and the earlier secret transmission conveyed something unusual. Li Jiao was no fool, so how could he fail to realize that the man he had beaten had some sort of background?

He had landed himself in trouble.

Li Jiao could not help feeling uneasy, though he revealed none of it in front of the woman. Even if he were given another chance to choose, he would still have acted. It was not because he was warmhearted, but because...

"Do you insist on addressing me like that?" Li Jiao looked at the woman and sighed softly.

The woman's tender body trembled, but she feigned ignorance. "Sir... what do you mean?"

"Haah..." Li Jiao shook his head. He did not dwell on the subject and merely said, "Come with me."

With that, he took the lead.

The woman hesitated for a moment, but still called to her daughter, took her hand, and followed behind Li Jiao.

Upon entering the teahouse from before and going up to the second floor, Li Jiao immediately saw Yang Kai looking at him with a faint, knowing smile. His old face could not help reddening, for he knew Yang Kai had witnessed his impulsive display.

After all, he was a Third Stage Emperor Realm Master and the Palace Master of a great force. He rarely lost his composure like this.

Without saying anything, he sat back down in his original seat. Yang Kai pushed a cup of tea toward him, and Li Jiao drained it in one gulp, his face sullen as he remained silent.

The mother and daughter stood gracefully at the side. Upon closer inspection, one could see many similarities between them, and both were exceptionally beautiful. Although the woman was injured and still had blood at the corner of her mouth, it could not conceal her peerless beauty.

Li Jiao said nothing, and neither did the woman. She simply stood there with her head lowered, as though she had done something wrong. Only the young girl curiously studied Yang Kai and Li Jiao, though most of her attention was on Li Jiao. After all, it was Li Jiao who had stepped in and rescued her and her mother just now, so she naturally felt closer to him and was filled with gratitude.

"Sit down and talk." Yang Kai smiled at them.

The woman shook her head and softly replied, "I appreciate Sir's kindness, but standing is fine."

Li Jiao felt stifled upon hearing this and took another sip of tea.

The woman continued, "Sirs, you should hurry and leave Half-Dragon City. It is not wise to remain here for long."

Yang Kai asked, "What is that man's background?"

The woman replied, “He himself does not have much of a background. It’s just that… he has connections with those on the other side."

Her explanation was vague, as though she were afraid of something. Seeing Yang Kai and Li Jiao remain completely unmoved, the woman looked anxiously at Li Jiao and said, "Sir, you truly must leave quickly. If you do not go now, it will be too late."

Li Jiao turned his head and stared at her in vexation, asking in a low voice, "Are you still going to pretend you don't know me?"

As soon as he said this, the woman lowered her head again, not daring to look at him.

The young girl beside her widened her eyes in curiosity. Looking first at her mother and then at Li Jiao, she asked, "You know my mother?"

Li Jiao snorted, "You'll have to ask her!"

He appeared somewhat angry, though even he did not know what he was angry about.

Innocent and naive, the young girl turned to her mother and asked, "Mother, do you know this Sir?"

The woman said nothing, but her delicate shoulders began to tremble softly. Drip, drip... Tears fell one after another like pearls from a broken string, as though she had recalled something painful.

"Mother..." Seeing this, the young girl's eyes also reddened, and she reached out to embrace her mother.

"Why are you crying? It's been so many years, so why are you still like this?" Li Jiao was somewhat speechless. Realizing that his words just now had probably been too harsh, he immediately felt guilty. He had no idea how to comfort her, and after fretting for a while, he could only look to Yang Kai for help.

Yang Kai smiled slightly and said, "Elder Sister, please sit down first."

Although they were both at the First Stage of the Emperor Realm, this woman was certainly older than him, so there was nothing wrong with calling her Elder Sister. Moreover, Li Jiao seemed to be an old acquaintance of hers, so naturally Yang Kai had to give Li Jiao some face at a time like this.

When Li Jiao had played the hero and rescued the beauty just now, he had cut such a gallant figure that he seemed to have grown several hundred years younger.

Sure enough, the moment Yang Kai said this, Li Jiao's face immediately lit up, though he still maintained a stern expression and said, "Sit down. What do you think you're doing, standing here like this? Anyone who didn't know better would think I was bullying you."

The woman was still quietly weeping, but she sat down with her daughter, probably also feeling that standing there and crying was somewhat improper.

Her emotions were unstable. Whether it was because of the humiliation she had just suffered or because she had seen Li Jiao again after so many years, her tears did not stop for a single moment.

It was not convenient to speak with her at this time, so Yang Kai simply looked at Li Jiao and secretly transmitted, "You know her?"

Yang Kai had already guessed as much when Li Jiao made his move earlier. Everything that followed showed that Li Jiao and this woman were indeed old acquaintances. Yang Kai could not help finding it quite a coincidence that they had actually run into someone they knew in this strange Half-Dragon City.

Li Jiao nodded lightly, his expression becoming complicated once more as the light of reminiscence flickered in his eyes.

Yang Kai did not ask further. It was not appropriate to pry into such private matters. In any case, he felt that the relationship between Li Jiao and this woman was rather unusual.

"Palace Master Yang, have you heard of a Sect called Ruyi Sect?"

After a moment of silence, Li Jiao suddenly asked through a secret transmission.

"Ruyi Sect?" Yang Kai thought for a moment, then nodded. "I've heard of it."

Ruyi Sect was also in the Northern Territory, but it was a tier below Li Dragon Palace, Mitian Sect, Wenqing Sect, and Ice Heart Valley because it had no Third Stage Emperor Realm expert overseeing it. Its strongest cultivator was only at the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm. Thus, although Yang Kai had heard of Ruyi Sect, he did not know much about it. Now that Li Jiao suddenly mentioned it, he realized that this woman was probably connected to Ruyi Sect.

Li Jiao continued through the secret transmission, "The Sect Master of Ruyi Sect is Lu Qiu, a Second Stage Emperor Realm cultivator. Although he cannot be considered a top expert, he is still fairly strong. Ruyi Sect is also a Sect with a long inheritance. Though its Sect Master, Lu Qiu, has never accomplished anything remarkable, he has at least preserved the foundation left by his ancestors. He has many sons and daughters, one of whom ranks third among them. Her beauty is renowned far and wide, and everyone calls her Lu Sanniang."

At this point, he turned his head and glanced at the woman sitting nearby, quietly sobbing.

"It was about three hundred years ago. While this Li was traveling, I encountered a group of villains committing violence. Palace Master Yang should be no stranger to such things. Since I did not know who was right or wrong or what grievances lay between them, it was not convenient for me to interfere. However, a woman stepped forward out of a sense of justice. Her cultivation was quite good, but at the time she was only at the Third Stage of the Dao Origin Realm. Outnumbered, she was soon in grave danger, so this Li stepped in and saved her. That was how we became acquainted, and that woman... was Lu Sanniang of Ruyi Sect."

"I am not afraid of Palace Master Yang laughing at me, but this Li is also a lustful man. Faced with such peerless beauty, I naturally fell for her. Later, after spending some time together, I felt that she also had a good impression of me, so this Li personally went to Ruyi Sect to propose marriage."

Such was human nature, especially since Li Jiao possessed the Dragon Clan's bloodline. Lu Sanniang's beauty could topple cities and kingdoms, so it was only natural that Li Jiao would fall for her.
