## Chapter 2778 — Qiu Ran’s Fury

Gao Xueting turned her head away and muttered under her breath, “We’ve already admitted our mistakes, what more do you want? No wonder you can’t find a dual cultivation partner—serves you right to be lonely for the rest of your life!”

“What did you say?” Qiu Ran’s forehead veins bulged.

“Nothing.” Gao Xueting faced Qiu Ran’s stormy rage with a cold face and said with great responsibility, “But since this matter started because of me, if Senior Brother wants to punish someone, just punish me. It has nothing to do with them.”

As soon as she spoke, the many Elders all nodded in agreement.

You Kun said, “That’s right, Senior Brother, this was all caused by Junior Sister Gao. We’re all victims here. If you must punish someone, punish her—and punish her heavily! Anything less won’t appease the public’s anger!”

Yang Kai stared at him, as if seeing You Kun for the first time.

Qiu Ran let out a cold laugh. “And you? Were you forced to drink?”

Di Rong scratched his face and said, “Senior Brother, your insight is as sharp as ever—that’s exactly what happened!”

“Nonsense!” Qiu Ran roared in anger. “None of you will escape responsibility for what happened yesterday!”

Upon hearing this, everyone looked as if they’d lost their parents, utterly dejected.

“If you’re going to punish us, just do it. Why so much nonsense?” Gao Xueting snapped impatiently, her expression full of annoyance.

“You, you, you... You have no sense of hierarchy at all.” Qiu Ran slammed the table again, fumed for a while, and then said, “Yesterday’s incident had a terrible impact. If the disciples learn from your example, what future will my Shrine have? In view of this, this Lord will deduct three thousand Elder Contribution Points from each of you!”

“Ah?”

“Don’t, Senior Brother Qiu... Three thousand Elder Contribution Points? You might as well kill me.”

“Senior Brother, please show mercy!”

The group of Elders immediately began to wail and shout, as if Qiu Ran had cut several pounds of flesh from them. Chen Qian looked at Qiu Ran with pitiful eyes and said, “Senior Brother... please don’t do this...”

She even tried to use a gentle attack, nearly resorting to acting cute.

“Stop disgracing yourselves in front of this Lord! If any of you dare utter another word, I’ll deduct another two thousand points.” Qiu Ran’s face was stern and impartial, his words as unyielding as iron.

With that, it was as if he’d grabbed everyone by their lifeline—the clamor stopped instantly.

“Hmph.” Qiu Ran glared coldly at Gao Xueting. “As for you, since you’re the ringleader, in addition to the three thousand points, I’ll deduct another one thousand from you. That’s four thousand Elder Contribution Points in total, plus three months of confinement. If you repeat this, you’ll be severely punished!”

Gao Xueting curled her lips, clearly unconvinced, but said nothing more.

Yang Kai felt a pang in his heart. Back when he was just a Registered Disciple of Qingyang Shrine, he rarely interacted with these Elders. Even if they did cross paths, their statuses were too different for him to see their true personalities.

Now, as an Elder himself, standing on equal footing, he finally got a full sense of their noble characters. For a moment, Yang Kai felt his entire understanding had been refreshed.

A sharp gaze swept over, and Yang Kai swallowed nervously, forcing a dry smile as he met it.

Qiu Ran pondered for a moment and said, “Junior Brother Yang, since this is your first offense, this Lord will let it go this time. You’re exempt from all punishment.”

Yang Kai was overjoyed and quickly said, “Many thanks, Senior Brother Qiu! Senior Brother, you truly are a peerless good man.”

As soon as he finished, seven or eight pairs of resentful eyes glared at him...

“Just this once. Don’t let it happen again. If it does...”

“Never again!” Yang Kai swore solemnly.

“That’s for the best.” Qiu Ran sighed deeply and earnestly advised, “Junior Brother Yang, you’ve achieved so much at such a young age. Your future is limitless. Don’t let yourself be dragged down by this lot—always stay true to yourself.”

Yang Kai was left awkwardly hanging, not knowing whether to accept or refuse.

Fortunately, Qiu Ran didn’t say more. After scolding everyone a bit more, he waved them away.

The group of Elders felt as if they’d been pardoned and hurriedly took their leave.

Leaving Spirit Jade Peak, the Elders all looked dejected. Leading them, Gao Xueting kept muttering under her breath, clearly cursing, her whole body radiating cold, making her intimidating to approach.

“Sigh, just yesterday we talked about the Shrine’s three great taboos, and in just two days, Junior Brother Yang, you’ve already witnessed two of them.” You Kun sighed, patting Yang Kai’s shoulder with concern. “You weren’t scared, were you?”

“I’m fine.” Yang Kai nodded, then asked curiously, “Why is everyone so afraid of Senior Brother Qiu?”

Chen Qian leaned in and whispered, “Senior Brother Qiu isn’t just the Deputy Hall Master of the Shrine—he’s also the Elder in charge of the Disciplinary Hall. Above, he oversees even the Hall Master and the Elders; below, he governs disciples of every rank.  The entire Shrine’s laws and punishments are in his hands. Who wouldn’t be afraid of him? Even the Hall Master has to show him some respect.”

“So that’s how it is.” Yang Kai understood, then asked, “What’s the deal with Elder Contribution Points?”

Earlier, when Qiu Ran said he’d deduct three thousand Elder Contribution Points, the Elders looked like they’d lost their parents. Clearly, these points are valuable.

You Kun explained, “Junior Brother Yang, you’ve only been an Elder for a few days, so you haven’t encountered this yet. Every major Sect has them. In the Shrine, disciples have Contribution Points, and Elders have Elder Contribution Points. These can be exchanged for many benefits in the Shrine. To put it simply, one Elder Contribution Point equals one thousand Upper-Level Source Crystals!”

“That expensive?” Yang Kai was shocked. He quickly calculated—three thousand points meant three million Upper-Level Source Crystals!

Just one drinking session cost three million Upper-Level Source Crystals. No wonder the Elders felt so much pain. Three million Upper-Level Source Crystals was no small sum for them.

“Although you can exchange Source Crystals for Elder Contribution Points, there’s a yearly limit. Each person can only exchange up to two thousand points per year. If you want more, you have to do things that benefit the Shrine, like completing various Elder tasks.”

“Generally, we only manage to save up five or six thousand points a year.”

“That’s right. Elder Contribution Points are very useful for us. For example, if you want to enter the Divine Roaming Mirror, it costs two thousand points each time.”

“It takes Elder Contribution Points to enter the Divine Roaming Mirror?” Yang Kai was shocked—he really hadn’t known that.

You Kun patted Yang Kai’s shoulder and said earnestly, “Junior Brother Yang, let’s all work hard together from now on.”

Yang Kai casually agreed, but then saw You Kun lean in mysteriously and say, “Let me give you a heads-up. In three months, it’ll be the Hall Master’s birthday. When that time comes, if you can go out, go out. If not, just go into seclusion—whatever you do, don’t show yourself.”

Chen Qian nodded vigorously at the side, looking like she’d learned from experience. “That’s right, Junior Brother Yang. You’ve already seen two of the Shrine’s three great taboos. You definitely don’t want to see the third.”

Yang Kai’s face turned pale. “Is it scarier than Senior Sister Gao drinking?”

Chen Qian considered carefully and said, “Not quite, but... it’s definitely not a good thing.”

You Kun shuddered. “Enough, enough. I need to go home and recuperate—my heart hurts so much...”

“I’m leaving too. Junior Brother, come visit my Little Spring Peak when you have time.” Chen Qian turned and sped off in another direction.

Soon, all the Elders dispersed, each returning to their own peak. Gao Xueting, who’d left first, was already gone—she’d probably returned to Purple Bamboo Peak. After being confined for three months by Qiu Ran, she’d definitely be angry for a while.

Yang Kai returned to his own Spirit Sword Peak. Looking at the mess of wine jars in the Spirit Sword Hall, he sighed.

He reached out, picked up a wine jar, and casually tossed it down the mountain.

When he got to the tenth or so jar, a black shadow suddenly shot out from inside and lunged straight at him.

Yang Kai remained calm. He pointed a finger at the shadow, and immediately heard a whimper as it bounced back and landed on the ground.

Looking down, Yang Kai was a bit surprised.

On the ground was a pitch-black little beast, about the size of a palm. At first glance, it looked like a puppy, crouched on the ground, baring its teeth at Yang Kai and growling in challenge.

“Where did this little thing come from?” Yang Kai was amazed.

The main thing was, he hadn’t noticed this little beast at all before. Even though he hadn’t been paying special attention, it was odd that a living creature hiding in a wine jar could escape his notice.

But in fact, Yang Kai only noticed it when the little beast suddenly attacked.

Sweeping it with his Divine Sense, he found nothing unusual about the little beast. It probably came to the Spirit Sword Hall’s wine jars attracted by the aroma.

There were plenty of birds and beasts on Spirit Sword Peak, so this little beast was probably a native resident.

After watching for a while, Yang Kai chuckled, reached out to grab the little beast. Sensing danger, the seemingly ordinary little beast tried to dodge, but was still caught by Yang Kai and dangled in his hand.

Once caught, the little beast immediately stopped being hostile, stuck out its tongue, and panted at Yang Kai like a real puppy.

“You really know how to adapt.” Yang Kai grinned, then with a flick of his hand, tossed the little beast away.

After dealing with this unexpected visitor, Yang Kai took out his Elder Jade Token and activated the Spirit Sword Peak’s barrier. Following Qiu Ran’s instructions, he prepared to refine the Earth Vein of Spirit Sword Peak together with the Jade Token.

Only by doing this could he truly become the master of Spirit Sword Peak.

The refining wasn’t difficult, but it consumed a lot of Divine Sense. Once he located the Earth Vein and grasped its fluctuations, it was easy for Yang Kai.

After refining for three to five days, a faint stream of light flashed across the Jade Token. At the same time, Yang Kai felt a strange sensation, as if the Jade Token and the entire Spirit Sword Peak had merged into one, inseparable.

With a single thought, the entire Spirit Sword Peak was under his control.

Refinement complete!

Just as he put away the Jade Token, Yang Kai suddenly sensed a familiar power fluctuation outside the barrier. Looking surprised, he opened a gap in the barrier to let the person outside in.

Soon, a stream of light landed in front of the Spirit Sword Hall, revealing Gao Xueting’s figure.
