## Chapter 3078 — Return to the Star Domain

Sometimes, happiness came just that suddenly.

The Dragon Clan Secret Technique that had left Mo Huang helpless was broken by Yang Kai with a mere flick of his hand, and the joy in Mo Huang's heart was simply beyond words. He had originally held quite a few misgivings about Yang Kai because of Mo Xiaoqi, otherwise he would not have treated a junior so coldly today. At this moment, however, he felt nothing but gratitude toward Yang Kai and believed he now owed him an enormous favor.

With the Dragon Binding Seal lifted, Fu Xuan was no longer branded a criminal of the Dragon Clan, so naturally, there was no need for her to be exiled to the Dragon Tomb again.

He looked up at Zhu Yan and Fu Zhun and secretly made up his mind that he would take Fu Xuan away today no matter what. If these two still insisted on stopping him, then they would have no choice but to fight.

More than a dozen years of longing and self-reproach made it impossible for him to abandon Fu Xuan again.

What made him secretly breathe a sigh of relief, however, was that neither Zhu Yan nor Fu Zhun seemed to react strongly to the lifting of the Dragon Binding Seal. The two merely stared in silence at the gradually shrinking Void Passage.

Zhu Qing was also staring at it.

A moment later, the passage vanished. Zhu Yan turned to Mo Huang and said, "Wu Kuang has escaped. We must inform the others and make preparations early, especially the Star Court."

Mo Huang nodded. "Naturally, I understand that. I will personally make a trip to the Star Court shortly and discuss with Iron Blood how this matter should be handled."

Zhu Yan said, "My Dragon Clan cannot shirk responsibility for the matter of Wu Kuang. If there is anything you need, Brother Mo, you need only ask."

"Since Great Elder has said so, I fear there will be no shortage of occasions when I must trouble the Dragon Clan in the future. This Mo will take his leave for today." As he spoke, he prepared to take Fu Xuan away.

Fu Zhun suddenly said, "You may leave, but Fu Xuan stays!"

Mo Huang raised his brow. "Does Second Elder wish to fight this Lord again?"

This woman was simply unreasonable. Mo Huang's anger also flared in earnest. If she truly insisted on continuing to make things difficult for him and his wife, he would turn Dragon Isle upside down today.

Fu Zhun said indifferently, "More than a dozen years of confinement have damaged Fu Xuan's foundation. My Dragon Clan has a Secret Technique that can help her recover sooner. Does your Spirit Beast Island have one? If so, you may take her away as you please, and I will have nothing more to say."

Mo Huang was taken aback, then immediately became overjoyed. He chuckled. "So that is what Second Elder meant."

Fu Zhun said, "What else did you think I meant?"

Mo Huang coughed lightly and nodded. "That works too. Then... Xuan'er, you stay here. I will go to the Star Court first, and when I return, I will bring Xiaoqi to see you." As he spoke, he glanced at Fu Zhun, seemingly intentionally yet unintentionally. Although she frowned, she did not voice any objection, which delighted him even more.

It seemed that what that brat Yang Kai had done today had dealt her quite a blow, otherwise she would not have changed so much. Although she had not agreed to let him bring their daughter to Dragon Isle, at the very least, she no longer objected.

In the past, Fu Zhun would certainly never have behaved this way.

Hearing him mention Xiaoqi, a look of anticipation also appeared in Fu Xuan's eyes, and she nodded. "Good."

"Wait for me!" Mo Huang reached out and stroked Fu Xuan's cheek. He truly could not bring himself to kiss her in full view of everyone as Yang Kai had.

That brat was far too thick-skinned!

Turning around, he strode out of the Dragon Temple with light steps.

Zhu Qing stepped forward and supported the weak Fu Xuan. The two Dragon women, bound by a similar fate, could not help growing close to one another.

...

Amid the vast Starry Sky, countless stars twinkled as a pitch-black Warship sailed forward like a giant beast. It stretched a hundred zhang in length, its sleek lines belying its massive size. Yet despite its enormous bulk, it moved with remarkable speed.

The surrounding region was filled with drifting asteroids. Great and small alike streaked past the Warship, and from time to time one would crash into its hull, causing ripples of light to shimmer across its protective barrier and spread outward. Fortunately, smaller asteroids posed no threat to a Warship of this caliber; even when they struck its surface, the defensive barrier absorbed the impact, reducing them to fragments.

Only the larger asteroids were capable of making the Warship shudder, but despite the dangers along the way, the journey had involved close calls without any real mishap.

Inside the Warship, the various Spirit Arrays hummed continuously. In the central control chamber, a group of cultivators wore solemn expressions as they watched chunks of drifting rock streak past through transparent crystal windows, each of them breaking into a cold sweat.

An Asteroid Sea!

When a Warship sailed through a Star Domain, there were two things it feared encountering most.

One was an Asteroid Sea, and the other was a Starry Sky storm.

The former was still manageable. Most Asteroid Seas could be avoided in advance, with only exceptionally large ones being impossible to evade. However, in the vast Star Domain, the chances of encountering one were not high.

Starry Sky storms, on the other hand, were enough to make anyone in any Star Domain turn pale at their mere mention. They were true natural disasters capable of claiming lives. Even a hundred-zhang Warship such as this one would likely meet with disaster if caught in a Starry Sky storm.

Every year, countless Warships and cultivators perished in Asteroid Seas and Starry Sky storms.

The Warship before them had not been particularly lucky. It was originally filled with experts, and one of the finest Star Chart Masters had plotted its route. By all rights, it should not have encountered any danger and should only have needed time to reach its destination, yet an enormous Asteroid Sea just happened to sweep toward it from ahead.

By the time they noticed it, it was already too late to evade.

The Warship could only brace itself and charge in, weaving and dodging through the Asteroid Sea.

At the center of the cabin stood a woman dressed in palace robes who appeared to be in her twenties. Her black hair fell like a waterfall, and her face was peerlessly beautiful. At this moment, her expression was solemn as she continuously called out orders. From nervousness, a faint sheen of sweat had even appeared on the tip of her nose. A subtle fragrance spread outward from her, and all the cultivators aboard the Warship, men and women alike, breathed more frequently than usual, seemingly wishing to draw her bodily fragrance deep into their lungs, imprint it in the depths of their minds, and never forget it for all eternity.

"How much longer?" The woman suddenly turned her head and looked in a certain direction.

An old man there was holding a Star Compass and examining it carefully. A three-dimensional image glowed above it, clearly depicting the region of the Starry Sky where the Warship was located. No one knew how the old man had drawn it. Within the image, a point of light representing the Warship slowly moved forward while countless densely packed specks of light streaked past on all sides. Each speck represented an asteroid that posed a threat to the Warship.

Hearing the woman's question, the old man pondered for a moment before replying, "An hour. We can pass through this Asteroid Sea within an hour. We have already covered half the distance."

The woman nodded upon hearing this. "Good. I will have to continue relying on Elder Chen from here on."

The woman was clearly in charge of this Warship, and her cultivation was also quite extraordinary, yet she still treated this old man surnamed Chen with great courtesy. The reason was simple: Elder Chen was one of the most renowned Star Chart Masters in the entire Star Domain, and she had paid an enormous price to hire him to accompany them on this voyage.

Star Chart Masters held very high status in the Star Domain, no lower than Alchemists or Artificers. When traveling through a Star Domain, a Star Chart was vitally important, and a good Star Chart Master could help a Warship avoid many unpredictable dangers.

Elder Chen said with shame, "Young Lady Lu is too polite. This old man should have detected the existence of this Asteroid Sea in advance. That danger now lurks on every side is this old man's responsibility."

"Eight or nine times out of ten, life does not go as one wishes." Lu Huaishuang offered a word of comfort, but seemed to be reminded of something. Her flower-like face dimmed slightly before she forced a smile and said, "Elder Chen need not blame himself. We also owe much to Elder Chen on this journey, otherwise who knows how many crises we would have encountered before this? That we were able to reach this place safely is also thanks to Elder Chen."

Elder Chen said, "This old man is deeply grateful for Young Lady Lu's high regard."

As they spoke, a sudden cry of alarm came from nearby. "Young Lady, something is wrong!"

Lu Huaishuang's expression changed, and she turned to look over there. "What happened?"

At such a sensitive moment, what she feared most was hearing bad news, so she could not help feeling somewhat nervous.

"An asteroid is hurtling toward us from a hundred li away."

"How big is it?"

"It is not very large, but... its energy fluctuations are terrifying."

As they spoke, Lu Huaishuang had already hurried over. Leaning closer to look, she indeed saw a bright point on the array's Star Compass hurtling toward the Warship at tremendous speed. In just the brief time that had passed, it had already covered nearly half the distance.

Lu Huaishuang frowned after just one glance. Elder Chen also exclaimed in astonishment, "Strange. This asteroid is not very large, so why are its energy fluctuations so terrifying?"

He could be considered experienced and knowledgeable, yet this was his first time encountering anything so strange, leaving him quite unable to make sense of it.

"Star Cannons!" Lu Huaishuang shouted sharply, then immediately added, "Ten of them!"

Sailing through the vast Starry Sky always involved encountering dangers of one kind or another. For threats that could not be avoided, the best solution was to destroy them, and Star Cannons were the most effective means of doing so.

Star Cannons were not used lightly because they required a vast quantity of Crystal Stones to be converted into energy for their attacks. Every use meant watching wealth drain away.

It had to be said that although Lu Huaishuang was a woman, she possessed a decisiveness many men lacked.

Firing ten Star Cannons at once required tremendous resolve.

The order was quickly carried out. Around the Warship, ten Star Cannons extended their fierce muzzles. Dazzling white light began to gather as terrifying energy fluctuations spread outward.

The next moment, a recoil force struck one side of the Warship, causing the entire vessel to shudder. Ten dazzling white beams shot toward a certain point in the void and converged there with perfect precision.

Light burst forth, and the dark Starry Sky seemed to have suddenly gained a dazzling round sun, forcing everyone in the cabin to narrow their eyes.

Boom...

An earth-shaking explosion followed, making everyone's eardrums ache. The area engulfed in light became an apocalyptic scene. All the asteroids around the target, regardless of size, were reduced to dust and vanished in that instant.

The group breathed gentle sighs of relief. Although they had long known the terrifying power of Star Cannons, the impact of seeing ten of them strike the same point still left them speechless for a long time.

Whatever was strange about that incoming asteroid, it had most likely vanished without a trace by now.
