## Chapter 2851 — Succubus

This was obvious. The reason Ah Hu and the others remained unharmed after being tainted by Demonic Qi was because their physical bodies and Qi and blood were strong enough. The hundreds of tribesmen who were demonized were, by comparison, the weakest among them.

After Yang Kai cast the Bloodlust Art, their Qi and blood surged two or threefold, enough to force out the invading Demonic Qi.

Earlier, Yang Kai had only intended to try it, but he hadn’t expected such a good result.

Now it seemed that if the Bloodlust Art was cast upon the tribesmen as soon as the Demonic Qi surged, their Qi and blood would be roused, and they would not be so easily corrupted by the Demonic Qi.

The Barbarian Tribe’s understanding of Demonic Qi was far too shallow, and there was too little useful information.

When the demons invaded, none of the four great tribes escaped. Naturally, not much news got out. Afterwards, the four Shaman Saints acted, directly confronting the strongest experts of the demon race, and a great battle ensued with no clear victor.

Before departing from the royal city, Shaman Dang had said that the Great Shaman Masters should seek out the demon race's weaknesses in battle. Now, Yang Kai felt he had found a way to counter Demonic Qi.

However, if the Bloodlust Art was used too many times, even the strongest tribesmen couldn’t avoid early aging. But at this moment of life and death for the tribe, who could care about that?

As he pondered, Yang Kai felt something was off.

If mere Qi and blood were enough to resist the invasion of Demonic Qi, then back when Fenglin City was besieged, there would have been no reason for so many cultivators to be transformed into demons.

Though later generations of martial artists mostly neglected physical cultivation, their bodies had been nourished by Spiritual Qi for years. Their physiques weren’t necessarily inferior to those of Ah Hu and the others.

Yet, before Demonic Qi, they had no ability to resist. Anyone tainted by Demonic Qi became a demon without exception, far inferior to Ah Hu and the others.

Of the three thousand under his command, only a little over a hundred failed to resist.

Was it the laws of heaven and earth? This ancient world was at least a hundred thousand years removed from later generations. The laws and tidal forces of the two eras were completely different.

Different laws of heaven and earth also nurtured different kinds of experts. He himself, an Emperor Realm cultivator, had been unable to exert any of his strength upon arriving here.

The Barbarian Tribe lived in this ancient world, under the influence of intangible yet omnipresent laws of heaven and earth. Perhaps they possessed something later martial artists lacked, allowing them to resist Demonic Qi with just Qi and blood.

The more he thought, the more likely this seemed.

After Yang Kai explained, the dozen or so shamans were suddenly enlightened. Yu and Lu, for the first time, looked at Yang Kai with respect.

The two were the most talented shamans of the Southern Barbarian Tribe. Given enough time, reaching the Shaman King realm was no mere dream. As elites from the Shaman God Hall, all their training and arts were the most powerful and profound of the time.

Though assigned by Shaman Dang to follow Yang Kai’s orders, Yu and Lu honestly didn’t think this Shaman Niu was much stronger than them.

All along, the two only regarded Yang Kai as an equal, never showing him proper respect. They believed they would soon surpass Shaman Niu, reaching heights he could never attain.

But after what just happened, they realized not all powerful shamans came from the Shaman God Hall—there were experts among the common folk.

Leaving everything else aside, the effortless casting of that Bloodlust Art without an incantation left the twin sisters deeply shaken. It was a technique neither of them could perform, not even if they joined forces.

Now, finding a way to save demonized tribesmen in such a short time was something no ordinary shaman could do. It required broad vision and vast, profound knowledge.

Yang Kai’s unintentional display had already earned the twin sisters’ respect.

Meanwhile, under Yang Kai’s direction, nearly a hundred demonized tribesmen gathered together. Each had dark eyes, and though bound by Shamanic Art chains, they still growled like wild beasts, baring their teeth at the others, making everyone uneasy.

As Yang Kai waved his hand, a crimson light enveloped the tribesmen. Each demonized tribesman was shrouded in a blood mist, and red light slowly bloomed in their black eyes.

After their Qi and blood were stimulated, some quickly regained their senses, while others needed more time.

Black Demonic Qi kept flowing from their seven orifices. The shamans joined forces to cast purification arts, and under the light, any Demonic Qi that appeared was instantly cleansed.

Just as everyone watched with joy, an accident occurred.

With a dull thud, a demonized tribesman’s head exploded, red and white splattering everywhere, and his body collapsed limply.

Everyone was shocked, and Yang Kai’s eyes narrowed.

It wasn’t over. The same fate quickly befell others.

With a series of explosions, more than ten tribesmen fell dead.

Everyone saw clearly that before dying, the red and black in their eyes clashed repeatedly, as if two armies were fighting for control of their bodies.

When the conflict reached its limit, the tribesmen could no longer endure.

Fortunately, among the hundred-plus, only those ten or so suffered accidents. The rest were saved, the Demonic Qi was forced out, and they fell unconscious.

After everything was handled, Yang Kai said solemnly, “Remember today! Today’s ordeal is the shame of Shaman Niu’s tribe! One day, the demons will pay a hundredfold!”

Three thousand strong, yet over a hundred died in a sneak attack by dozens of Sand Demons. Though much was due to lack of information, it was still hard to accept.

Many tribesmen silently clenched their fists, breathing heavily, their eyes sweeping over their fallen comrades, suppressing their anger, waiting for the right moment to erupt.

After instructing the tribesmen to care for the unconscious, Yang Kai called, “Ah Hua!”

“Sir!” Ah Hua stepped forward.

“Send someone to inform our nearby allies of today’s events, so they may be warned!”

“Yes!” Ah Hua accepted the order and immediately went to arrange it.

Though Shaman Niu’s tribe lost over a hundred today, it wasn’t without value. At least they learned the Sand Demons’ elusive methods, and discovered ways to counter Demonic Qi and save demonized tribesmen.

This was extremely important information that had to be spread quickly. It might help the forces led by other Great Shaman Masters avoid heavy losses.

“Sir, since this is a trap, we can’t stay any longer,” Yu stepped forward and said quietly. Just now, it was only dozens of Sand Demons. If they delayed, who knew how many more demons might appear.

Yang Kai nodded, “Pass the order: rest here for a quarter hour, then move out.”

After a quarter hour, Shaman Niu’s tribe, fully prepared, continued north in grand procession.

After everyone left, in the center of the village, a patch of empty ground suddenly bulged. The bulge slowly rose, turning into a shriveled Sand Demon.

With emotionless red-black eyes, he glanced in the direction Yang Kai and the others had gone, then abruptly burrowed underground and disappeared.

Half a day later, in a deep mountain cave, the Sand Demon appeared mysteriously.

In the cave, a tall woman dressed revealingly, with large swathes of snow-white skin exposed and a short tail behind her, glanced at him, then suddenly pressed her hand on the Sand Demon’s head.

Pitch-black Demonic Qi surged from the woman’s body, turning into visible beams that wrapped around the Sand Demon.

The Sand Demon immediately seemed to suffer inhuman torment. His always emotionless eyes now showed terror, but even so, he dared not resist before the female demon, letting her do as she pleased.

Light flickered in the female demon’s eyes, and scene after scene flashed by—all from the earlier events in the village.

Who knew what method she used, but she could replay everything the Sand Demon had witnessed. This technique was clearly extremely domineering, not something ordinary people could endure.

After a moment, the female demon withdrew her hand. The Sand Demon collapsed weakly, looking as if he might die any moment.

She ignored him completely and simply let out a cold laugh. “Those damn outsiders—so they’ve finally attacked.”

Beside her, a short demon wrapped in black Qi asked, “My lady, what happened?”

The female demon turned, sat on a wide stone chair, propped her chin on her snow-white hand, and said lazily, “Nothing much, just dozens of Sand Demons died. And these foreigners seem to have a way to counter us.”

The short demon said, “Should I take people to deal with those foreigners…?”

The woman shook her head, “No need. Before the Demon Saints went into seclusion to heal, they ordered us to defend, not to take the initiative.”

“So… the Sand Demons just died for nothing?” the short demon asked.

“For nothing?” The female demon laughed charmingly, but her gaze was icy. “How could that be? I’ve remembered the leader’s face. When the Demon Saints recover, I’ll personally settle accounts with him.”

As she spoke, she ran her rosy tongue across her lips, a look of hungry anticipation flashing in her eyes. The sight made the short demon shudder, and he hurriedly looked away as cold sweat trickled down his back.

The Queen was the famous succubus, ranked among the top in the hundred demon clans. Any man she targeted never had a good end. Even as a Demon Commander, if he fell under the Queen’s charm, he’d soon lose his mind and become her plaything.

Spending a night of passion with the Queen would certainly be delightful—but if it meant becoming a mindless husk afterward, that would be nothing short of a disaster.
