## Chapter 2858 — Ruthless Hands Destroy the Flower

Yang Kai reached out with a large hand and seized the twin sisters Yu and Lu as easily as grabbing two chicks.

Under the watchful eyes of all, the twin sisters' faces filled with alarm and unease. Instinctively, they drew closer to Yang Kai, only to be carried off by him as he flew toward the rear of the demon army.

The remaining Shaman Warriors and Shaman Apprentices could only watch helplessly as a chill spread from every pore of their bodies, leaving them frozen in place.

Shaya's triumphant laughter echoed across the battlefield, extremely harsh to the ears.

A figure suddenly flashed before them. It was Die, blocking their path.

Yu and Lu simultaneously revealed looks of joy and hope, crying out in unison, "My Lady, Shaman Niu has defected to the demon race, stop him quickly!"

Through their recent interactions, Yu and Lu had learned that Die was also a Great Shaman Master, equal in realm to Shaman Niu. If she acted, perhaps she could make a difference.

Die ignored them, standing motionless, simply gazing calmly at Yang Kai.

Yang Kai also stopped, squinting at Die and saying, "You want to stop me?"

Die was silent for a while, then wordlessly stepped aside. The hope in Yu and Lu's eyes was instantly extinguished.

Yang Kai nodded, passing by Die with Yu and Lu in tow.

"Be careful!" Die whispered softly, her voice as faint as a mosquito's. Without listening closely, one might not even know she had spoken.

Yang Kai had already gone far, soon disappearing from sight.

"The battle isn't over yet, why are you dazing off?" Die looked at the thirteen shamans below, shouted sharply, and was the first to move. Her figure danced and weaved across the battlefield, sometimes reaping demon lives with her extraordinary agility, sometimes her hands bursting with the light of a Shamanic Art, bestowing various enhancements upon the Barbarian Tribe warriors.

Seeing this, the thirteen Shaman Warriors and Shaman Apprentices finally found their backbone again and gradually stabilized their formation. Incantations rang out once more, and the light of Shamanic Arts flickered frequently.

On the other side of the battlefield, Yang Kai landed gracefully and waved his hand. Yu and Lu immediately fell to the ground, their eyes blazing with hatred like volcanoes ready to erupt. They glared at Yang Kai, then at the scantily clad Shaya. Their silver teeth were clenched tight. They felt both humiliated by their predicament and furious at Yang Kai's cowardice.

Had they known Shaman Niu was this kind of person, they would never have agreed to follow him.

"Is this sincere enough?" Yang Kai smiled at Shaya as he spoke.

Shaya smiled meaningfully, her beautiful eyes roaming over Yu and Lu before she looked at Yang Kai and slowly shook her head.

Yang Kai frowned: "These two are the backbone of my three-thousand-strong tribe. I captured them to show my loyalty. Why isn't it enough?"

Shaya raised her hand slightly and casually pointed at Lu: "Kill her!"

At these words, Yu and Lu's faces both changed.

"Kill her, and I'll believe you," Shaya tilted her head at Yang Kai, her gaze carrying a hint of provocation.

She wasn't a fool. Although this Barbarian Tribe Great Shaman had been poisoned by the Green Demon and seemed willing to defect, if it was only to this extent, it wasn't enough to earn her trust.

This was the final test. If this Barbarian Tribe Great Shaman could really kill his own subordinate, then his intention to defect would be beyond doubt. Otherwise, it was a trick—a ploy to get close to her!

Shaya's feelings were conflicted. On one hand, she hoped this Barbarian Tribe Great Shaman would hold out to the end, refusing to submit even in the face of death. Prey that struggled between life and death was the most satisfying. On the other hand, she felt it would be a pity if he died like that and hoped he would obey her. But a slave who was too obedient... what use was such a person? She had no shortage of such people under her command.

Shaya's feelings were conflicted. She found herself uncertain what decision she truly hoped this Barbarian Tribe Great Shaman would make.

She watched intently, hoping to see hesitation or struggle on his face, which would bring her some pleasure.

But to her amazement, after hearing her order, Yang Kai walked forward without a word, showing not the slightest hesitation.

The twins' faces changed dramatically. Yu forced herself to stand in front of her younger sister, staring fiercely at Yang Kai and shouting, "Kill me, let her go!"

Yang Kai stood before her like an insurmountable mountain, looking down at her and nodding slightly: "Sisterly affection is touching. Unfortunately..."

With a thud, Yu was sent flying by a kick from Yang Kai, soaring more than a dozen zhang away before crashing heavily to the ground. As she rolled a few times, blood trickled from the corner of her mouth.

"This is Lord Shaya's first order. I must obey it well!" As Yang Kai spoke, he had already gripped Lu's slender neck, slowly lifting her up.

Lu struggled desperately, her face turning red, but with her strength sealed, how could she possibly break free from Yang Kai's grasp with just her physical body? Her flailing limbs looked utterly ridiculous.

"Let... cough, let her go!" Yu crawled up from not far away, screaming hoarsely, trying to rush over, but was firmly subdued by a shadowy figure.

Yang Kai's grip tightened further and further as his shamanic power surged, radiating an extremely dangerous aura.

As if their hearts were connected, the struggling Lu turned to look at her sister, while Yu, heartbroken, looked back. Their eyes met, and Lu gently opened her mouth, but no sound came out.

Yet Yu knew she was saying goodbye.

"No!" Yu screamed hysterically.

Thud...

Suddenly, a cloud of blood exploded. Lu burst apart in Yang Kai's grasp, leaving not a trace of her body—only blood and flesh rained down.

Yu's scream was cut short. She stared blankly at the blood mist, unable to believe that her sister, who had just been so close, had met such a tragic end.

Two streams of bloody tears slid silently down her cheeks. She did not cry out; her gaze turned icy cold as she glared at Yang Kai, filled with searing hatred. If she could, she would bite Shaman Niu to death, devour his flesh, drink his blood, and even gnaw his bones to swallow them!

"Ha, hahahahaha!" Shaya suddenly burst into a fit of maniacal laughter, her body trembling with delight, as if the scene before her had touched some sensitive nerve, bringing her unprecedented satisfaction.

Ignoring Yu’s gaze, sharp as a bone-scraping blade, Yang Kai turned to Shaya and said, “My Lady, is this show of sincerity enough?”

"It's enough!" Shaya nodded in satisfaction.

Only now did she truly believe Yang Kai had come to defect.

"Come here!" Shaya called, as if summoning a pet.

Yang Kai walked over and stood before her.

"Kneel!" Shaya ordered again.

Yang Kai frowned and shook his head: "Sorry, my legs hurt. I can't kneel!"

Shaya's smile instantly turned icy as she stared coldly at Yang Kai: "You dare disobey me? Do you want to die?"

Yang Kai replied calmly, "In life, there are things one can give up, and things one must hold on to. My Lady, you are a woman, you wouldn't understand a man's persistence."

Shaya curled her lips: "How ridiculous. You can kill your own subordinate without a second thought, yet you refuse to kneel to me?"

Yang Kai shrugged: "I'd rather die!"

The two locked eyes, Yang Kai calm, Shaya full of authority.

After a long while, Shaya pursed her lips. She realized this Great Shaman was truly stubborn, with no intention of kneeling for survival. This kind of resolve didn't anger her; instead, it excited her.

Training such an unruly slave was truly satisfying.

"Fine, since you've shown your sincerity, it's my turn now."

As she spoke, Shaya gracefully stepped up to Yang Kai and tapped his forehead with her finger.

A jet-black strand of Demonic Qi suddenly flowed from Shaya's fingertip.

This Demonic Qi was somewhat similar in essence to the one Shaman Niu's tribe had encountered in that abandoned village, but compared to it, Shaya's Demonic Qi was undoubtedly superior—purer and more corrosive.

After forcing out this Demonic Qi, Shaya's face showed a trace of fatigue, clearly indicating it was a burden even for her.

The Demonic Qi circled Shaya's fingertip like a living thing, then shot into Yang Kai's body and disappeared.

Yang Kai grunted, stumbling back two steps.

Instantly, a faint black aura spread over his body, like burning flames, incinerating all traces of the Green Demon poison.

The green glow vanished, replaced by a strange, oppressive blackness.

"Is that all?" Yang Kai frowned slightly.

Shaya chuckled, "If you can withstand my Demonic Qi and survive, you are one of the demon race. But to become my slave, a bit more is needed."

Yang Kai said, "I am willing to serve under you, My Lady."

Shaya nodded in satisfaction: "Very good."

As she spoke, she suddenly opened her mouth and spat out something the size of a fist.

Yu, who had been watching coldly, narrowed her eyes, for this thing looked like nothing else but a heart—a pitch-black heart, rhythmically beating.

Shaya held it in her hand, and it pulsed gently, each beat radiating a powerful force and a strange energy that made one's mind restless and dizzy.

Yang Kai, however, watched with great interest.

Shaya smiled slightly: "This is my demon heart."

"Is it alright for you to take it out like that?" Yang Kai asked with concern. "Won't it affect you?"

Shaya laughed: "Among the demon race, any expert who has achieved mastery has their own demon heart. As long as the demon heart is not destroyed, a demon cannot die! Didn't your ancestors tell you this?"

Yang Kai replied, "This is the first I've heard of it."

Staring intently at the demon heart, Yang Kai said, "So, in other words, this thing is your foundation?"

"That's right." Shaya nodded slightly.

"May I take a look?" Yang Kai asked curiously, eyeing the demon heart.

Shaya looked up, her gaze meaningful, as if trying to discern whether he was just asking casually or had other intentions.

Yu suddenly held her breath, her expression growing tense.
