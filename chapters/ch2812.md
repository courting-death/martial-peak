## Chapter 2812 — Luobai Mountain

"This won't do, absolutely not!" In the wooden hut, the village chief seemed to have been provoked, shaking his head fiercely, his face full of anguish as he looked at Yang Kai and said, "Ah Niu, do you even know what you're saying?"

Yang Kai sighed, "I'm just going out to temper myself for a while. I'll be back when spring arrives."

The village chief said, "Now that the harsh winter has arrived and the mountains are sealed by snow, even if you've reached Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice... Wait, you—you—when did you become a Shaman Warrior?"

Just now, when Yang Kai arrived, the village chief hadn’t taken a close look at his condition. Now, with a careful glance, he was utterly shocked—Ah Niu had already become a Shaman Warrior! It wasn’t that the chief could precisely identify a Shaman Warrior, but his experience was keen; the aura Yang Kai radiated was far beyond what any Shaman Apprentice could possess.

How could this be!

Just over half a month ago, Ah Niu received the favor of the Barbarian God, awakened the power of the Shaman, and broke through to Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice overnight, which was already astonishing. Now, he was a Shaman Warrior!

That was the Realm he had longed for his whole life but never reached.

Yang Kai smiled slightly, "Village chief, do you think this Shaman Warrior still doesn't have the strength to protect himself outside?"

The village chief said, "Even if you're a Shaman Warrior, how many Shamanic Arts do you know? All the Shamanic Arts you possess were taught by me..."

Before he finished speaking, the chief's mouth hung open, staring blankly as a Shamanic Art Shield appeared in front of Yang Kai, along with a Fireball spell in his palm.

The chief opened and closed his lips, but found he could no longer find a reason to stop Ah Niu from venturing out.

Yang Kai said, "Village chief, I just came to inform you. Even if you disagree, I'll find a chance to slip away."

The chief sighed heavily, and after a long while said, "Three days. Give me three days, I'll teach you a few more things."

"Alright!"

For the next three days, Yang Kai stayed in the chief's house, living together with him.

In those three days, the chief didn't teach Yang Kai any particularly powerful Shamanic Arts, nor any cultivation methods. When it came to cultivation, the chief was powerless; as he said, he had nothing better to teach Yang Kai.

He only knew that Ah Niu had always been isolated, never interacting with others in the village, let alone understanding the outside world.

This journey wasn't just about having strength to protect oneself, so the chief taught Yang Kai about the world, local customs, and emergency responses to various dangers.

When he was young, the chief had also ventured outside...

After three days, the chief had nothing left to teach Yang Kai, even the ancient characters had all been passed on.

"I'll tell the villagers you're in seclusion cultivating, so they won't disturb you. Tomorrow, leave quietly before dawn. Remember to come back early!" The chief spoke like a parent sending off a child about to travel, his expression full of concern and worry.

"I understand."

"Go rest. Tomorrow, you'll face a new journey!"

...

The next day, before dawn, Yang Kai set out. At this time, most villagers were still fast asleep. As Yang Kai left the village, he felt something and looked back, only to see Ah Hua, who was on night duty, standing quietly by the fence. Their eyes met, and they nodded slightly, everything understood without words.

Yang Kai strode away, quickly disappearing from Ah Hua's sight.

Five days later, at the foot of a mountain a thousand li from Cangnan Village, Yang Kai took out a yellowed piece of beast skin, checked his route, and looked at the continuous mountains ahead: "This should be the place."

Luobai Mountain!

This was Yang Kai's destination for this trip.

When the chief ventured outside years ago, he didn't go very far. Luobai Mountain was one stop on his journey, and the yellowed map in Yang Kai's hand was given to him by the chief before departure.

According to the chief, many Barbaric Beasts lived in Luobai Mountain, some so powerful they could rival a Great Shaman Master.

The chief repeatedly warned Yang Kai to act within his limits, but Yang Kai's purpose was to seek trouble with the beasts, so he paid little heed to the warnings.

Soon, Yang Kai stepped into Luobai Mountain.

Half a day later, in a cave, Yang Kai exhaled hot breath, standing atop the corpse of a Yao Beast, dissatisfied: "So weak."

In his eyes, this Yao Beast was only about Fourth-Tier level. Now, even without using Shaman power, he could easily kill it with his physical strength alone.

Still, better than nothing.

He took out the Yao Beast's Inner Core, casually cleaned it, swallowed it whole, sat cross-legged, and began refining it.

His abdomen rumbled like thunder, strange sounds emanating. In less than the time it takes to burn a stick of incense, Yang Kai had finished refining the Inner Core.

He quietly felt his body, frowning slightly.

The result was disappointing. After refining a Yao Beast Inner Core, his strength barely improved. This beast seemed even weaker than the last one he killed in the canyon, so the energy stored in its Inner Core was naturally limited.

At this rate, even if he refined a thousand Inner Cores, he might not advance to Shaman Master.

His goal wasn't far—once he became a Shaman Master, he'd possess his own Divine Sense.

Though dissatisfied, Yang Kai had no better solution for now. All he could do was persevere, hoping to encounter stronger Yao Beasts, and in the meantime, slowly accumulate progress through sheer numbers.

In the days that followed, Yang Kai wandered through Luobai Mountain, delving deeper, specifically seeking Yao Beasts to kill.

A month passed in a flash. During this time, Yang Kai slew many Yao Beasts, obtained many Inner Cores, but even after consuming so many, his cultivation only reached the peak of Lower-Level Shaman Warrior Realm, unable to break through to Middle-Level.

Weak, too weak. The Yao Beasts of Luobai Mountain were too low-level to meet his needs.

He originally planned to advance to Shaman Master within a month, then open his Spatial Ring, retreat for two more months, and finally return to the village as a Shaman King or even Shaman Saint. But now, it seemed he was too optimistic.

He could only keep going deeper, holding onto a vague hope.

One day, while refining a freshly obtained Inner Core, Yang Kai's ears twitched and his brows furrowed.

He heard footsteps approaching the cave where he was hiding.

And it sounded like more than one person.

He was surprised—how could anyone else be in this forsaken place? The bitter cold of winter was at its peak; aside from himself, who would dare wander outside, risking death by freezing or starvation?

While he was puzzled, several people entered the cave. Someone immediately said, "Careful, there's a smell of blood!"

The footsteps fell silent, even breathing was inaudible, but Yang Kai could feel them slowly approaching.

Sure enough, before long, a barbarian suddenly appeared, stopping five zhang away from Yang Kai. At first sight of Yang Kai, the barbarian was visibly startled, his expression turning utterly bewildered.

Obviously, he hadn't expected to encounter a tribesman here.

Following him, several others appeared, all looking battered, as if they'd just survived a fierce battle. The most miserable one had lost an arm, blood pouring out, his face pale with pain. Yang Kai glanced and knew it had been bitten off.

But barbarians are barbarians. Even with such a severe injury, he didn't utter a sound, gripping a cracked stone spear in his other hand.

"Who are you?" The leading barbarian sized up Yang Kai and barked, seeing this delicate fellow who didn't look like a barbarian at all—where did he come from?

Yang Kai glanced at him but didn't bother to respond.

This arrogant attitude annoyed the other, who was about to explode when a tall woman suddenly stepped forward. Her face was cold as she said, "Don't disturb him, he's cultivating!"

The woman seemed highly respected, so after she spoke, the previously angry barbarian still simmered, but said nothing more, instead eyeing Yang Kai suspiciously, frowning, "Cultivating? Is he a Shaman?"

The woman also looked at Yang Kai in surprise, "He is indeed a Shaman, and a Shaman Warrior!"

"Shaman Warrior!" The barbarian warriors were startled, hardly believing their ears.

But since it came from their own Shaman, even if they didn't want to believe, they had to accept it.

This guy—he's a Shaman Warrior?

The woman stepped forward, looking at Yang Kai, "My companion was injured in the recent battle. We need a place for him to heal, and there's nowhere suitable nearby, so we have to stay here. But don't worry, we won't disturb you."

Yang Kai nodded lightly.

He found it amusing. They probably thought he was at a critical moment in cultivation and couldn't speak or be disturbed. She had no idea he just didn't want to deal with them, only wanting to finish refining the Inner Core in his abdomen.

"Thank you!" The woman thanked him, then led the others a bit further away, settling the injured tribesman and treating his wounds.

Clear spiritual energy fluctuations came. Yang Kai didn't know what methods the woman used, but from his senses, she was also a Shaman Warrior, likely an Upper-Level one!

In other words, her realm was higher than his.

Still, she was decent, showing no hostility. After all, in her eyes, he was in an uninterruptible cultivation state. If she had any ill intent, now would be the best time to strike.

Outside the cave, several barbarians gathered, watching their Shaman treat the wounded companion. The woman's Shamanic Art was extremely cold, freezing the wound quickly. She then took some herbs from a beast skin pouch and had him swallow them.

Soon, the injured barbarian's expression eased, apparently in less pain.

The woman also relaxed a bit.

At that moment, a strange voice suddenly sounded: "He's poisoned. If you don't help him expel the poison, he won't live much longer."
