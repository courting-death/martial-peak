## Chapter 2947 — Life or Death Unknown

Back in the day, Li Jiao narrowly escaped death from the Frozen Earth, so he naturally paid more attention to it. However, there was simply too little information about the Frozen Earth in this world. Even though he wanted to gather more, he could not find much that was useful.

He knew that there was a kind of Heavenly Might in the Frozen Earth called the Cold Tide, and that under the Cold Tide, no one could survive. But as for how the Cold Tide formed, how to avoid it, or what it looked like, he had no idea.

Only now, witnessing it with his own eyes, did he finally realize—this was the legendary Cold Tide.

The three of them were not moving fast. Even though they tried their hardest, they could not escape the pursuit of the Cold Tide. As the distance grew shorter, the expressions on Yang Kai and Zhu Lie’s faces became increasingly grim.

In the face of life and death, no one could remain calm.

Yang Kai tried to mobilize the Laws of Space, but found it incomparably difficult. The chill in the Frozen Earth seemed to freeze even space itself, forcing him to abandon the idea of teleporting.

The Cold Tide drew ever closer to the three, and the bone-chilling cold struck their backs like a giant hammer, making them shiver uncontrollably.

Suddenly, Li Jiao’s face twisted as he shouted, “If I die because of you this time, I won’t let you off even as a ghost!”

The grievances and anger he had bottled up for days finally erupted at this moment. With death looming, what was there left to care about? He opened his mouth and said, “Yang Kai, aren’t you so powerful? Even Yao Kings and Divine Spirits obey your commands. Why don’t you think of something now?!”

Seeing Yang Kai remain silent, he raged, “All you can do is bluff! Do you really think I’m afraid of you? If it weren’t for Grandmaster Ji Ying’s sake, I would have already led people to flatten your Lingxiao Palace. I’d have wiped out everyone in your Lingxiao Palace, made you wish you were dead! Just a mere First Stage Emperor dares to act so arrogant in front of me—how ridiculous.”

After his tirade, he still was not satisfied. He turned to Zhu Lie and snapped, “Dragon Clan? So what if you’re a Dragon? Does that mean you can look down on others? My bloodline may not be pure, but so what? That’s how I was born and raised. I cultivated to the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm with my own effort. If you didn’t have your Dragon Clan bloodline, you might not even be as strong as me. Always putting on that arrogant face—who are you trying to impress? If you’ve got the guts, don’t use your bloodline to suppress me. I’ll show you what I’m made of. Damn it, why did I get involved with you two? You’re both disasters—I should have known nothing good would come from following you to the Frozen Earth. I should have left earlier. It’s all your fault! I already brought you here, so why are you still holding onto me? Do you really think I’m easy to bully? Just wait, if I survive this, I’ll bring people to storm Lingxiao Palace and Dragon Isle, and kill you all!”

He kept rambling on, almost as if he had lost his mind, becoming somewhat hysterical. But no matter how much he vented, Yang Kai and Zhu Lie paid him no attention.

The two exchanged a glance. Zhu Lie said, “If you have any trump cards, use them now. If you wait, it might really be too late.”

Yang Kai curled his lip and said, “You Dragons have so many secret techniques, and you’re relying on me? I’m only at the First Stage. What trump card could I possibly have?”

Zhu Lie looked at him deeply and sighed, “Fine. Since you want to hide your strength, I won’t force you. I’ll do it.”

Li Jiao’s cursing stopped abruptly, as if an invisible hand had clamped his mouth shut. He looked at Yang Kai, then at Zhu Lie, and forced a smile, asking cautiously, “Sir, do you have a way out of this?”

Zhu Lie replied coldly, “I can give it a try.”

Li Jiao immediately said, “Then what are you waiting for? Hurry up!”

As Yang Kai ran, he glanced at Li Jiao with a faint, playful smile.

Li Jiao’s face, pale from the cold, flushed with a hint of red. Embarrassed, he said, “Sir, you must take me with you. Just now... I don’t know what came over me. I lost my head for a moment. That wasn’t what I really meant. Please don’t take it to heart.”

He wished he could slap himself. His mouth had run faster than his brain, and now he had brought trouble upon himself. He did not know if this Dragon would be willing to help him.

Fortunately, his worries were unnecessary. As a dragon’s roar rang out, a massive crimson dragon appeared above Zhu Lie’s head, coiling around and instantly enveloping all three of them.

The biting cold seemed to be blocked out, giving them a sudden sense of safety.

Zhu Lie gave a low shout, and signs of Dragon Transformation appeared on his body. Then he turned around and charged straight toward the Cold Tide.

Li Jiao was so terrified his soul nearly left his body. He screamed, but did not dare to leave the protection of the red dragon, and could only watch as he drew closer and closer to the Cold Tide.

Yang Kai was also slightly startled, but quickly understood Zhu Lie’s intention.

At their current speed, there was no way they could outrun the Cold Tide. Rather than be overtaken, it was better to go against it. Maybe, with the strength of all three, they could break through.

It was not a great plan, but it was worth a try. Maybe they really could escape danger.

As the cold wind howled, the Cold Tide drew ever closer. Li Jiao’s face was drained of color. If not for his strong will and cultivation, he might have already fainted.

After more than a dozen breaths, the three finally collided head-on with the Cold Tide, instantly swallowed by the darkness.

Zhu Lie’s expression changed drastically as he suddenly sensed something was wrong.

This Cold Tide was nothing like what he had anticipated. It did not seem to be a calamity wrought by the convergence of Spiritual Qi, but rather felt like some unfathomable existence. All his preparations were rendered useless, and he was swallowed up in an instant.

Yang Kai cried out in surprise, “Laws of Space?!”

He never imagined that the Cold Tide in the Frozen Earth would actually contain an extremely dense power of the Laws of Space. The moment he was enveloped by the Cold Tide, the surrounding space became chaotic and turbulent, giving him a sense of teleportation even more intense than a cross-domain transfer. It felt as if his whole body was being torn apart, reduced to countless tiny fragments swept into a void passage.

Almost by instinct, Yang Kai also activated his own Laws of Space.

The next moment, his head spun, and he could no longer sense Zhu Lie or Li Jiao. No one knew where the two of them had gone. Yang Kai only felt as if he were falling endlessly, the dizziness growing worse and worse. Only when he used the Laws of Space did he feel a little better.

He did not know how long it lasted before the dizziness suddenly vanished. He crashed heavily onto solid ground, bounced up, and rolled several times before finally coming to a stop.

It felt as though every bone in his body had been shattered. A wave of nausea unlike anything he had ever known surged up, and he could not help but gag a few times.

All around was silent and covered in snow, but there was no sign of Li Jiao or Zhu Lie.

Slowly getting up, Yang Kai’s expression grew grave.

Although the scenery looked much the same as before, the bone-deep chill was gone. He keenly sensed that this place was different—it seemed to be a completely different world, not in the same plane as the Frozen Earth.

He tried to sense the laws of this world, and after a moment, his suspicions were confirmed.

This was indeed another world. The laws here were complete, but seemed chaotic, very different from those of the Star Realm. In other words, he was no longer in the Star Realm.

He recalled what had just happened and began to form a guess, though he was not sure.

The only thing he could be certain of was that his arrival here was closely related to the Cold Tide. Perhaps the Cold Tide was the entrance to this world, and its destructive power was just a disguise.

Suddenly, a flash of red light caught his eye. Yang Kai turned his head and saw that the Dragon Blood Coral he had been holding was shining a hundred times brighter than ever before.

The misty light atop the coral had condensed into a thick red band, drifting in a certain direction, guiding Yang Kai.

“Zhu Qing!” Yang Kai’s brows rose as he immediately understood why the Dragon Blood Coral had changed so dramatically.

This was something Zhu Lie had created with his own dragon blood using a Dragon Clan secret technique. Its main purpose was to find Zhu Qing’s whereabouts. When searching in the Frozen Earth before, it had indeed pointed the way.

But it had never been as intense and obvious as it was now.

Zhu Qing was nearby! She might not be far from him.

When the Cold Tide swept through, its range was vast. He had been swept into this world by it. If Zhu Qing had also been swallowed by the Cold Tide, she should have appeared in this world as well. That was why the Dragon Blood Coral was reacting.

With this thought, Yang Kai quickly pulled himself together, picked up the Dragon Blood Coral, and rushed off in the direction it pointed.

Alone, Yang Kai traveled through the boundless snowy mountains.

Half an hour later, Yang Kai really did find that familiar figure less than three hundred li from where he had first appeared.

Crimson hair like fire, a petite and delicate body, lying on her side in the snow, her life or death unknown.

Yang Kai hurried over, lifted her up, and brushed aside the hair covering her face. Sure enough, it was Zhu Qing. He let out a slight breath. Although he still did not know where he was, at least there was some good news.

Zhu Qing had been found. As for Zhu Lie and Li Jiao, Yang Kai figured they probably were not in any real danger. The Cold Tide looked terrifying, but it did not seem to cause any real harm.

The reason they had been separated was entirely because, at the very instant the Cold Tide engulfed them, Yang Kai had instinctively activated the Laws of Space.

Zhu Qing’s condition seemed a bit off. She was a Dragon, with strength comparable to the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm. There was no reason for her to be unconscious for no reason. It was probably related to being swallowed by the Cold Tide. Even Yang Kai, who was proficient in the Laws of Space, had felt sick for a while after being swallowed.

But that was not the main reason.

Yang Kai felt her body was icy cold, as if she were a block of ice. Even her vitality was extremely weak.
