## Chapter 2654 — You Truly Kept Me In The Dark

Throughout the Northern Territory, about half the land was covered in snow and ice, though not all of it. Still, since Ice Heart Valley’s legacy was rooted in ice-based cultivation techniques, it was only natural that when Bing Yun founded the sect, she chose a place of bitter cold as its home.

Although Ling’er had also begun cultivating, she was still young and weak, so it was normal for her to feel cold.

“Young Master Yang…” Old Ban said anxiously from the side, “Can Ling’er really join Ice Heart Valley?”

Although he could tell that Yang Kai’s relationship with Ice Heart Valley was anything but ordinary—otherwise, they wouldn’t have been able to enter the valley directly through the Space Array—this matter concerned Ling’er’s future, so he couldn’t help but worry.

“Don’t worry, there’s absolutely no problem with her joining Ice Heart Valley. As for how much she can achieve in the future, that will depend on her own effort and fortune.” Yang Kai smiled slightly.

“I don’t ask for great achievements, only that Ling’er can live her life in peace.” Old Ban sighed sincerely. Having received Yang Kai’s assurance, he was somewhat relieved.

Back in Desolate City, that chaotic place teeming with all sorts of people, Old Ban had always worried for the little girl’s safety every time he led someone through the Ancient Land’s passage. Yet if he didn’t guide others, he and his granddaughter would have had no way to survive there.

Now, as long as the little girl could join Ice Heart Valley, he would never have to live those days of constant fear again.

“Not far from Ice Heart Valley is Binglun City, which is Ice Heart Valley’s property…”

Since they were waiting for the battle to end, Yang Kai chatted idly with Old Ban, telling him about the situation around Ice Heart Valley. When he learned that Binglun City was truly not far from Ice Heart Valley, Old Ban was greatly comforted.

This way, if Ling’er missed him in the future, or if he wanted to see her, it wouldn’t be too troublesome. As long as he could find a livelihood in Binglun City, he could support himself, and there would be no need to worry about Ling’er at all.

As the two chatted, the commotion outside grew quieter and quieter. In the end, there were no more sounds of fighting, only waves of women’s cheers echoing in all directions.

Clearly, Ice Heart Valley had won a great victory, which was why the disciples were so delighted.

Seven thousand against one hundred thousand. Yet in the end, they achieved victory—this was nothing short of a miracle.

Buzz, buzz, buzz…

Strange noises came from all directions, and then swarms of pitch-black insects, gathering like clouds, rushed from every corner of Ice Heart Valley into the small island in the ice lake.

Upon seeing this, Yang Kai naturally took out the Slave-Insect Bracelet and collected the Soul-Devouring Demonic Worms into it.

In today’s battle, the Soul-Devouring Demonic Worms also played a significant role. As soon as Yang Kai returned to Ice Heart Valley, he had released all the Soul-Devouring Demonic Worms and ordered them to slaughter the enemy.

With the current strength of the Soul-Devouring Demonic Worms, they couldn’t help Yang Kai in the fiercest, highest-level battles, but for large-scale slaughter like this, they were perfectly suited.

With no Emperor Realm masters left to oversee the hundred-thousand-strong army, no one could stop the Soul-Devouring Demonic Worms’ killing spree. Wherever the insect clouds passed, the army gathered by Wenqing Sect fell like straw.

For killing, there were the Soul-Devouring Demonic Worms. For defense, there was the Black Tortoise Seven-Section Formation. Add in the Emperor Realm masters and the three Yao Kings from Ice Heart Valley, and victory was already a foregone conclusion.

“Young Master Yang, Young Master Yang…”

At that moment, a shout came from not far away.

“Young Master Yang, someone’s looking for you,” Old Ban said, glancing in that direction.

“Ignore him,” Yang Kai said with a curl of his lips, continuing his conversation with Old Ban.

Old Ban didn’t know why Yang Kai seemed so averse to that person, but wisely chose not to ask.

A moment later, Nanmen Dajun finally spotted Yang Kai. His face lit up, and he hurried over, cupping his fists. “So you were here, Young Master Yang.”

It was all thanks to Yang Kai killing some enemies along the way, which allowed many Ice Heart Valley female disciples to spot his trail. Otherwise, Nanmen Dajun might not have found this place at all. He had to ask quite a few people before following the traces here. Seeing Yang Kai really was here, he was overjoyed.

This place was originally the forbidden area of the ice lake, well hidden. Ordinary people would never find it.

Nanmen Dajun had completely lost his earlier indignation and instead became obsequious, grinning from ear to ear. Anyone who didn’t know the truth would think he was a master flatterer, with not a shred of dignity left.

“Young Master Yang, you truly kept me in the dark,” Nanmen Dajun said with a beaming smile. “Had I known that formation came from your hands, why would I have troubled Senior Bing Yun?” Though his face was all smiles, his heart was anything but at ease—bitterness, frustration, resentment and helplessness churned within him, a jumble of conflicting emotions.

After parting from Yang Kai just now, he had gone to find Bing Yun to ask about the Black Tortoise Seven-Section Formation. But at that time, Bing Yun was commanding the battle and he couldn’t interrupt, so he waited until everything was settled before approaching.

After asking, Nanmen Dajun was dumbfounded.

It turned out that the Black Tortoise Seven-Section Formation of Ice Heart Valley had been taught to them by Yang Kai just over a month ago. It wasn’t originally Ice Heart Valley’s technique at all. Bing Yun also made it clear to Nanmen Dajun that they couldn’t discuss the formation with him, and if he wanted details, he’d have to ask Yang Kai himself.

Nanmen Dajun was left speechless.

Only then did he realize what Yang Kai’s last words meant when they parted ways earlier.

At the time, he had scoffed and paid it no mind, but it turned out Yang Kai had already dug a pit, just waiting for him to fall in.

For a moment, Nanmen Dajun was both regretful and angry. He was, after all, a formation grandmaster. Though he had no great background or powerful backers, he was still respected. Even Sect Masters at the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm treated him with courtesy—after all, who didn’t need a grand formation to protect their sect?

Now, if he had to swallow his pride and beg Yang Kai for a formation method, wouldn’t that be slapping his own face? He’d already been humbled once over the Wild Ancient Land affair—if he turned back now, it would be like offering up his other cheek after being slapped on the first.

How could he be so shameless! Nanmen Dajun inwardly berated himself.

No matter how he thought about it, there was no way out. Even if he had to swallow his pride, he couldn’t resist the allure of that peerless treasure. He resolved that no matter how difficult Yang Kai made things, he would grit his teeth and ask to see the method for arranging the Black Tortoise Seven-Section Formation.

“Are we that close?” Yang Kai glanced at him, his expression cool.

Nanmen Dajun let out an awkward chuckle. “Well, the first meeting is unfamiliar, the second is easier, and by the third, we’ll already be well acquainted.”

“No need.” Yang Kai said with a fake smile, “Grandmaster Nanmen’s reputation resounds throughout the Star Realm—a formation master of an entire generation. This Young Master is but a mere wanderer; I would hardly dare to presume such lofty company.”

Nanmen Dajun’s face fell. “You flatter me, Young Master Yang. I’ve only done a bit of research into formations, I wouldn’t dare call myself a grandmaster. Please don’t overpraise me.”

If anyone else had praised him like this, he would have accepted it without hesitation. But in front of Yang Kai, he just couldn’t muster any confidence.

One who eats another’s food speaks softly, and one who takes another’s gifts finds their hand weakened—but he had neither eaten nor taken anything, yet he still felt a head shorter before Yang Kai. Nanmen Dajun felt deeply aggrieved.

Yang Kai ignored him and continued chatting with Old Ban.

Although Old Ban didn’t know what had happened, he could tell that this so-called Grandmaster Nanmen had a favor to ask of Yang Kai. Naturally, he didn’t butt in, nor did he have the right to. He simply pretended to know nothing and kept up the conversation with Yang Kai.

Yang Kai’s disregard made Nanmen Dajun furious, but he could only swallow his anger and accompany them with a forced smile, hoping that sincerity would move even stone, and that Yang Kai would eventually change his mind.

After a while, Nanmen Dajun’s eyes suddenly lit up. He looked down at Ling’er and said kindly, “Little girl, what’s your name?”

Ling’er glanced at him and replied crisply.

Nanmen Dajun smiled. “Such a clever and adorable girl, with such pure eyes. Fate has brought us together. Here, let this Lord give you a little something.”

As he spoke, something suddenly appeared in Nanmen Dajun’s hand—a pendant, resembling jade at first glance, but its color was not the usual translucent white. Instead, it was a simple, pale yellow, exuding an ancient aura. Its material was unfamiliar, neither jade nor stone, and its craftsmanship hinted at something extraordinary.

Without another word, he hung it around Ling’er’s neck.

Yang Kai glanced over, raising his eyebrows.

Old Ban, who had been talking with Yang Kai, was shocked. “Grandmaster, this won’t do! Such a valuable thing, the girl can’t accept it.”

Although he couldn’t recognize what the pendant was, he knew that someone like Nanmen Dajun wouldn’t give something insignificant.

Nanmen Dajun chuckled. “You’re making too much of it, Elder. Since you know Yang Kai, you’re a friend of mine as well. It’s our first meeting, and I wasn’t prepared, so I’m just giving a small gift to the younger generation. It’s nothing much!”

Old Ban opened his mouth, not knowing how to refuse, and could only look to Yang Kai for help.

They were strangers, so there was no reason to give Ling’er a gift out of the blue. Clearly, he was trying to curry favor with Yang Kai, so this matter could only be left to Yang Kai to handle.

Yang Kai said coolly, “Grandmaster Nanmen is truly generous—just gifting a Formation Talisman without a second thought.”

Nanmen Dajun’s eyes lit up. “So Young Master Yang recognized it.”

He had worried Yang Kai wouldn’t realize how valuable it was, and had planned to drop a few hints, but he had underestimated Yang Kai’s eye for such things.

“What, a Formation Talisman?” Old Ban was shocked, his face turning a bit pale.

If it really was a Formation Talisman, then the gift was far too valuable.

A Formation Talisman contained a sealed formation. In battle, one only needed to activate it to easily set up a grand formation. Old Ban had only heard of Formation Talismans because he’d spent so long in the Wild Ancient Land and met many people—his experience was not lacking.

“It’s just a little thing I refined in my spare time. It’s useless to me, so please don’t refuse, Elder,” Nanmen Dajun said with a laugh.

Yang Kai didn’t reply. He simply reached out and gently touched the Formation Talisman, nodding as he said, “Not bad. If someone at the Third Stage of the Dao Origin Realm enters, it should be able to trap them for a while. Anyone below that level will be killed on the spot.”

Now it was Nanmen Dajun’s turn to be surprised. “Young Master Yang, you have a sharp eye.”

Old Ban, listening at the side, gasped. “Anyone below the Third Stage who enters will be killed?”
