## Chapter 2902 — When Falsehood Becomes Truth

The enormous palm above pressed down, a wave of annihilation rushing forth. Mo Duo sighed, closed his eyes, and stretched out his neck to meet his fate.

At that moment, Yang Kai’s furious roar exploded in his ear: “Aren’t you going to act? Do you want to die?”

That furious roar was like a commanding decree filled with strange power. Mo Duo suddenly snapped his eyes open and, driven by his instinct to survive, unleashed the full strength of a Demon Saint, raising both fists to strike upward.

Suddenly, he realized the power that had been restraining him had vanished. The chaotic currents binding him had dissipated, allowing him to exert his full strength.

But the instant he struck, Mo Duo regretted it.

He asked himself, what was he doing? As a Demon Saint, was he actually resisting the Great Demon God?

All his actions were instinctive, without a single conscious thought—driven by the urge to survive. On the edge of life and death, no one could think too much.

Regardless, this was a full eruption of his Demon Saint power.

Meanwhile, not far away, Yang Kai’s expression was solemn. He raised his Demonic Warhammer, pouring all his Demonic Qi into it, and smashed it down hard at the giant palm.

Laws of Space swirled around the Demonic Warhammer, and wherever it passed, the void shattered in chaos. Not only that, Yang Kai also infused it with some Laws of Time. In this life-and-death crisis, Yang Kai unleashed a blow that surpassed his own limits.

This strike was stronger than anything he had ever shown before—a blow he had never imagined possible.

In the passage between the two worlds, one acted with intent, the other without. Yang Kai and Mo Duo joined forces perfectly, launching a stunning counterattack.

The giant palm paused slightly, a massive hole appearing in its center, from which endless demonic power gushed and flowed in all directions.

But in the next instant, the palm pressed down upon Yang Kai and Mo Duo as if crushing two insignificant ants, then continued downward.

Mo Duo’s muffled groan was snuffed out as his thirty-zhang-tall body instantly shattered, turning into pieces of flesh drifting in the endless void. Yang Kai also felt himself shatter, his body vanishing completely, leaving only his Divine Soul falling, falling... as if into a bottomless abyss.

Was this death? Yang Kai didn’t know what death was like, but if even Demon Saint Mo Duo couldn’t withstand that blow, there was no way he could have survived.

He was probably truly dead.

In a daze, he suddenly felt he had forgotten many things, yet also remembered something.

In his perception, he was still falling endlessly. The fall seemed to have no end, destined to go on forever. For no reason, he felt completely relaxed, as if he no longer needed to worry about anything that troubled him. Just falling like this didn’t seem so bad.

Suddenly, an anomaly occurred. The sensation of falling vanished abruptly, and his vision shifted. When Yang Kai regained his senses, he found himself adrift in a vast expanse of starlit void. He turned his head, surveying the emptiness around him. All was silent and still, devoid of any substance—even he existed only as formless emptiness.

Sounds of battle drifted up from below, along with fluctuating waves of energy.

He looked down, then his eyes widened.

He saw a colossal tree, reaching from earth to sky, standing tall on a battlefield strewn with blood and corpses. Its vast crown pierced a rift in the sky, growing ever larger.

He saw robust humans hiding beneath the tree, evading the slaughter of the demon race.

He also saw millions of humans and demons locked in a life-and-death struggle across the vast wilderness.

Elsewhere in the sky, eight towering experts were locked in battle—saints of both races, evenly matched.

A jolt ran through him, and he suddenly remembered what he had just forgotten, and even older memories returned.

He was Shaman Niu, Shaman King of the Southern Barbarian Tribe, commander of the elite warriors of Shaman Niu’s tribe.

He was also Yang Kai, Palace Master of Lingxiao Palace in the Northern Territory of the Star Realm.

Especially that last identity—he didn’t know when he had completely forgotten it. When he first arrived in this world, he would occasionally remember that he was just a visitor here, that he had come to this secret realm for training. But as the war between the two races broke out, that identity grew ever more distant, and he gradually adapted to being Shaman Niu, eventually forgetting the name Yang Kai altogether.

Yet he had just dragged a Demon Saint into a suicidal assault against the Great Demon God himself, attempting to emulate the Divine Tree's noble self-sacrifice for righteousness. What had he been thinking?

If he had remembered he was Yang Kai, he would never have acted so recklessly. After all, this was just a secret realm—everything here could be an illusion.

But from the perspective of Shaman Niu of the Barbarian Tribe, it all seemed perfectly natural.

Shaman Niu would absolutely make that choice. Any barbarian would, without hesitation—even if it meant death, as long as there was the slightest chance of success, it was worth it.

The only thing he couldn’t figure out was what was really going on—was all this an illusion? Was he truly dead?

“Isn’t it amazing?” a voice suddenly came from behind.

Yang Kai was startled—he hadn’t expected anyone else to be here. But he didn’t turn around, because he recognized the voice.

Standing in the void, he pointed below and asked, “Is all this real or not?”

“It’s both real and false!” A petite figure slowly walked to his side, her beautiful eyes gazing downward, watching with Yang Kai.

Yang Kai turned to gaze at her, curiosity flickering in his eyes. "Explain."

“There’s falsehood in truth, and truth in falsehood.” The woman winked at him playfully—a look she had never shown before, leaving Yang Kai momentarily stunned.

Yang Kai continued to look at her, saying nothing.

The woman said, “This battle was real. In ancient times, such a war between the two races truly happened, not much different from what you see now—just on a larger scale.”

Yang Kai frowned. “What about Shaman Niu?”

“It was precisely because there were countless Shaman Nius in that war that this land was preserved and the demons were driven back!”

Yang Kai realized, “What I saw and experienced as Shaman Niu was just a fragment?”

“Exactly!”

Yang Kai said, “I understand. Everything I experienced was just an illusion, but this illusion wasn’t empty fantasy—it was something that truly happened in ancient times.”

And this illusion lasted quite a long time—two whole years.

But Yang Kai believed that the ancient war could never have ended in just two years. Maybe it was twenty, or even two hundred. What he saw was just a condensed shadow, with the time greatly shortened.

“That’s right, so congratulations on your achievements in this battle.” The woman smiled.

Yang Kai let out a scornful laugh. "I'm already dead, so there's nothing to congratulate."

“Only in this world. You’re still alive.” The woman chuckled softly.

Yang Kai shook his head and said, “I want to see how this battle ends.”

The woman nodded gently, reached out and swept her hand through the void. In the next moment, Yang Kai seemed to become a god—wherever his thoughts went, everything below was clear and vivid.

First, the passage between the two worlds. The blow delivered from afar by the Great Demon God, after destroying Demon Saint Mo Duo and Shaman Niu’s resistance, continued downward.

But the sacrifices of Mo Duo and Shaman Niu were not in vain. The giant palm was pierced, and a torrent of demonic qi poured out, absorbed by the surrounding void, greatly weakening the palm’s power. And as it pressed down, its strength diminished further.

Finally, when the palm struck the tree’s crown, heaven and earth shook.

The vast crown suddenly shrank. Countless broken branches and leaves flew, and the towering trunk was riddled with cracks, as if it might fall at any moment.

But the Divine Tree only swayed for a while before stabilizing again. Emerald light burst from every leaf and branch, unleashing a surge of astonishing vitality.

The crown, which had reached into the passage, suddenly expanded, as if spring had come anew, instantly sealing the rift in the sky.

Then, countless green lights, like fireflies, flew into the sky, rushing into the sealed Boundary Passage and vanishing.

Soon, the Boundary Passage was filled with green radiance.

The Divine Tree rapidly withered as the life essence it had accumulated over tens of thousands of years was exhausted. All its vitality turned into green light to seal the Boundary Passage.

When the Boundary Passage was completely filled with green light, the Divine Tree suddenly shook, its branches falling away, leaving only a bare trunk.

The Boundary Passage, under some mysterious force, gradually contracted, finally closing and disappearing.

The whole process lasted a long time. After the Boundary Passage vanished, the Divine Tree seemed to lose all life, its trunk turning to dust and drifting away on the wind.

The Divine Tree sacrificed itself, and the Boundary Passage was finally sealed.

Seeing this, all the barbarians cheered in delight, while the demons fighting them were plunged into despair.

Cut off from the Demon Domain, all the demons, from the lowest soldiers to the Demon Kings, were greatly weakened and unable to fight at full strength.

The barbarians rallied and slew countless enemies.

Even the stalemated battle between the saints finally changed.

The four Demon Saints gradually lost ground. Pursued relentlessly by the Shaman Saints, they fled in disarray. In less than half a day, two Demon Saints were slain on the spot, and the other two, seeing the situation was hopeless, fled in opposite directions.

The Shaman Saints split up to pursue them.

The Demon Saints were either dead or fleeing, and the demons below could do nothing more. The war was over; all that remained was to clean up the battlefield.
