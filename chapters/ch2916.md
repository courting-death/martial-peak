## Chapter 2916 — Human Strength Has Its Limits

What Yang Kai hadn’t expected was that the former Family Head of the Gong family could be so vicious, disregarding the family’s reputation for the sake of an ancient cave residence.

“Of course Brother Gong is genuine. I don’t know why you’re doubting him,” Yang Tai sneered at Yang Kai, as if mocking his supposed cleverness.

Yang Kai sighed, “Anyway, you and Gong Yue are in league together. I wasn’t wrong about that.”

But Yang Kai had made one mistake—he thought Fang Zhuo was also their accomplice, because during the previous contest of formation arts, Fang Zhuo lost too quickly. That match had led everyone to confirm Gong Yue’s identity.

Yang Kai had thought it was all an act, so he’d suspected Fang Zhuo as well. Only now did he realize that Gong Yue was the real deal, truly possessing the strength to crush Fang Zhuo in formation arts.

“Are you looking for the flaw in this formation?” Yang Tai, who also possessed the cultivation of the First Stage of the Emperor Realm and was as cunning as an old fox, naturally noticed Yang Kai’s intentions. Yet he appeared utterly unbothered, not rushing to stop Yang Kai’s actions, instead wearing a mocking smile. “Then you’d better look carefully. There aren’t many flaws in the formation Brother Gong set up.”

Yang Kai suddenly turned his head, his gaze locking onto him as he said in a low voice, “So that’s how it is.”

Yang Tai burst out laughing. “You figured it out?”

Yang Kai nodded. “Thanks to your reminder. You’re the flaw in this formation—the Formation Core lies with you.”

Yang Tai admitted readily, “That’s right. I entered this formation and control the Formation Core. If you want to break the formation, it’s not hard—just kill me.” He smiled faintly, like a fox that had stolen a chicken. “But… do you have that ability?”

It wasn’t that he looked down on Yang Kai. Though their cultivations were similar, he’d seen Yang Kai make a move when they passed through the poisonous barrier earlier—his attacks had been weak and unsustainable, his aura unstable, confirming everyone’s earlier suspicions that Yang Kai was injured. Moreover, as the Formation Core of this illusion formation, Yang Tai had been granted control by Gong Yue, allowing him to exert strength far beyond the First Stage of the Emperor Realm within the formation.

That’s why he was so fearless. Even if Yang Kai saw through the location of the Formation Core, he didn’t have the ability to break the formation.

“Why?” Yang Kai frowned deeply.

“Why what?” Yang Tai wasn’t in a hurry to make a move. At this moment, he held an absolute advantage and felt that capturing Yang Kai would be effortless—he could act at any time.

“No one knows what’s hidden behind that blood-red gate. Maybe there’s treasure, maybe it’s a trap. Who can say for sure what’s left from ancient times? Yet you’re willing to kill others for it. Why?”

Yang Tai laughed loudly and shook his head. “You don’t understand. You don’t understand anything.”

“That’s why I want to know.”

Yang Tai frowned, muttering to himself or perhaps whispering, “There’s something very important in there. Very important. I must obtain it!”

Yang Kai was slightly shocked. “You know what’s inside? How could you possibly know?”

There was an ancient restriction on the blood-red gate. Half a year ago, Yang Tai, Wu Kuangyi, Hua Yulu, and the others had been unable to break the restriction, so they’d agreed to go out and find help, returning half a year later to try again. With the restriction unbroken, no one could know what secrets lay beyond the blood-red gate.

To kill four or five Emperor Realm cultivators for an uncertain outcome—no one in their right mind would consider it. Yet Yang Tai had done so, even inviting Gong Yue as an accomplice.

His muttering clearly showed he wasn’t completely ignorant of what lay behind the blood-red gate. This was what shocked Yang Kai most. What made him uneasy was that Yang Tai’s current state seemed off—almost mad, nothing like the demeanor of an Emperor Realm master.

Yang Kai quietly roused his Divine Soul power, a trace of golden light flickering in his left eye. Divine Soul energy surged from his mind as he shouted in a low voice, “Tell me, what’s inside?”

“Inside…” Yang Tai blurted out, but after just a few words, his expression suddenly changed.

At the same time, Yang Kai let out a muffled groan, his face instantly turning pale.

His Divine Soul had not yet healed, and forcibly using its power again would have been fine if Yang Tai hadn’t resisted. But as soon as Yang Tai realized what was happening, he immediately counterattacked, causing Yang Kai to be injured once more.

“You dare try to manipulate me!” Yang Tai’s face twisted in fury. No longer willing to waste time with Yang Kai, he spread his arms wide. Behind him, seawater surged into the sky, gathering into a lifelike water dragon that lunged at Yang Kai, head swaying and tail lashing.

The dragon’s roar shook the world, its massive body rising from the sea, connecting heaven and earth. Its gaping maw seemed ready to swallow Yang Kai whole.

Cold sweat slid down Yang Kai’s forehead. Though he knew all this was the power of the illusion formation, he dared not take it head-on. In his current state, carelessness could lead to disaster.

So, faced with such a terrifying attack, Yang Kai’s first reaction was to dodge.

His figure flickered, flashing dozens of zhang away. Before he could steady himself, the seawater beneath his feet swirled violently, and in the blink of an eye, a vortex appeared. Another water dragon burst forth from it.

Yang Tai sneered, “Boy, just surrender. You’re no match for this old man!”

Even without relying on the power of the formation, Yang Tai was confident he could capture Yang Kai, though it would take more effort. Now, as the Formation Core of the illusion formation, he could control its power, making it even less likely for Yang Kai to be his match.

Splitting his focus to control two water dragons as they chased Yang Kai, Yang Tai taunted, “Though a Blood Sacrifice is needed, it doesn’t necessarily mean you’ll die. You just need to help this old man open the ancient restriction. If you cooperate, I might spare your life. But if I catch you… hmph!”

He didn’t say what would happen, but the threat was clear.

How could Yang Kai believe him? Without a word, he dodged the two water dragons’ attacks, repeatedly escaping danger as he gradually closed in on Yang Tai.

“Refuse a toast only to drink a forfeit!” Yang Tai, frustrated by Yang Kai’s silence, raised his hand. Two more water dragons rose from the sea, joining the chase.

Yang Kai’s situation grew ever more perilous, like a battered hut in a raging storm, ready to collapse at any moment. Yet each time, he managed to evade the water dragons at the last possible instant, driving Yang Tai into a furious rage.

Yang Tai had completely lost the composure befitting an Emperor Realm master. Ever since laying eyes on that blood-red gate, he had displayed an unusual fanaticism and wild emotional fluctuations. He had somewhat concealed them before, but once he entered the illusion formation and engaged Yang Kai in combat, they became glaringly apparent.

Four water dragons became six, then eight, then ten…

When fifteen water dragons filled the sea surface, Yang Tai's robes fluttered without any wind, snapping audibly around him. The Emperor Yuan surrounding his body churned like boiling water, and his face twisted with rage, his eyes blood-red as he fixed his burning gaze on Yang Kai's flickering form darting back and forth. He resembled a volcano on the verge of eruption, his anger barely contained beneath the surface.

Yang Tai couldn’t help but be angry. He’d thought that with his own strength and the formation’s power, capturing Yang Kai would be as easy as pinching a snail. But no matter how dangerous Yang Kai’s situation looked, he always managed to escape at the critical moment. The gap between expectation and reality made Yang Tai’s fury soar.

That fury peaked after he summoned fifteen water dragons.

Carefully controlling the water dragons, he finally managed to trap Yang Kai in one spot on the sea, leaving him with no room to escape.

The dragons roared, their massive bodies lashing at Yang Kai.

Yang Tai sneered, “Boy, you should be grateful you’re still useful. This old man won’t kill you yet, but afterward, you’ll taste something worse than death.”

Yang Kai stood tall above the sea, watching the fifteen water dragons charge at him. He seemed resigned, no longer dodging as before, but his face showed little panic.

The violent aura pressed in from all sides. Even before the attack landed, Yang Kai felt a chill all over.

But just then, a sudden change occurred.

The fifteen water dragons suddenly convulsed, then burst like bubbles, turning into a torrential rain that fell from the sky, pattering on the sea like pearls on banana leaves.

Yang Tai was stunned, his face twisted as he screamed, “What did you do?!”

He thought Yang Kai had secretly used some trick to break his control over the formation, but sensing carefully, he was still the Formation Core—nothing had changed. This left him confused.

Yang Kai grinned, “I may not be an expert in formations, but I do know one thing… Human strength has its limits, especially for old men like you.”

Yang Tai’s face grew even uglier at these words.

But Yang Kai’s words made him realize that the water dragons hadn’t shattered because of any trick, but because he’d pushed himself past his limits, causing the illusion to spiral out of control. Such a simple thing, yet he hadn’t noticed until Yang Kai pointed it out.

He tried to regain control of the illusion, determined to be more careful this time. After fighting Yang Kai for so long, he’d figured out his tricks—speed was his only real advantage; nothing else was worth worrying about.

But Yang Kai wasn’t going to give him another chance.

Having dodged and fled all this time, he’d been waiting for this very moment. How could Yang Kai not seize it?

His hands formed seals rapidly, and a mysterious aura surged forth. As this aura washed over him, Yang Tai suddenly found it nearly impossible to form a single thought. His mind seemed to freeze, as if time itself had been disturbed.

When he finally came to, he heard Yang Kai’s low chant beside his ear.

“The years wither and flourish, like a shuttle, like a dream!”

A palm imprint descended from the sky. Only when it was nearly upon him did Yang Tai snap awake. Until then, he hadn’t even noticed Yang Kai making a move, as if the memory of that instant had been erased.
