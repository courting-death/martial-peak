## Chapter 3084 — Gazing Up at a Lofty Mountain

Eyeballs nearly popped out all over the place.

Whether Sheng Yao had chosen to die rather than yield or to swallow his pride and compromise, either would have been within everyone's expectations. But for this fellow to come forward and immediately bow his head in submission went far beyond anything they had imagined.

Was this man really Gale's First Chief? Did he really possess a Second Stage Void King Realm cultivation? Everyone felt as if none of it were real.

But on second thought, this was what it meant to be a wise man who understood the times.

Sheng Yao clearly had no confidence in opposing this young man, which was why he had taken such a humble posture. An expert would only lower his head before an even stronger expert.

The young man lowered his head and looked at Sheng Yao. A light flashed in his eyes, and the corner of his mouth curved up, as if he had discovered something interesting.

But soon, he frowned again. He had not expected this fellow to be so spineless. As the saying went, one did not strike a smiling face. Although Sheng Yao had been hiding nearby from beginning to end, he had never attacked him, and there was no grudge between them.

Now that Sheng Yao had gone this far in lowering himself, killing him outright would be somewhat inappropriate.

Ma Chaoqun and He Wuzui had died because they had brought it on themselves, but Sheng Yao had an instinct for seeking fortune and avoiding calamity.

This fellow had handed him a problem. The young man thought for a while, unable to decide how to deal with him, so he simply ignored him, closed his eyes, and stood in place.

Sheng Yao remained motionless, maintaining his posture with one knee on the ground, while beads of sweat the size of beans slid down his cheeks. Being the meat on another’s chopping block was an utterly miserable feeling, but with the odds stacked against him, he had no choice but to bow his head.

The young man gave no response, and the scene fell quiet again.

Lu Huaishuang stood where she was and waited for a full half hour, yet still saw no movement from the young man.

Since she did not move, the Lu Family members naturally would not move either. Gale's Star Pirates also remained still, but the He Family elites exchanged looks, already thinking of seizing the chance to slip away.

Lu Huaishuang cast a cold glance in that direction. How could she allow such a thing to happen?

The Lu Family and He Family had fought each other to the death on Morning Sun Star, and their strengths were nearly equal. Both sides had Void King Realm experts holding the fort, but after today, the He Family would likely no longer have the strength to resist.

He Wuzui was dead, leaving the He Family without a leader, so the Lu Family could naturally take the opportunity to swallow up many of the He Family's industries.

If they could keep these several hundred He Family elites here as well, that would naturally be best.

An opportunity missed would never come again. Lu Huaishuang glanced at the young man once more and immediately made her decision. A flash of radiance bloomed from her deep blue longsword as she pointed it at the He Family members and spat out one icy syllable, "Kill!"

One word decided the fate of the He Family members.

She was a woman, but she was by no means soft-hearted. The merciful did not hold power, and the kind did not command soldiers. From the day she had taken over the Lu Family's authority, she no longer lived for herself.

The battle was completely one-sided.

Originally, the Lu Family and He Family cultivators had been roughly equal in number and quality, but the He Family's Family Head had died, and without a leader, morale had collapsed. Even with several hundred elites, they were nothing more than loose sand. By contrast, the Lu Family had Lu Huaishuang personally in command, their hearts united, and they swept through everything in their path.

In just half an hour, the He Family's several hundred people were almost entirely dead or wounded, with only a few slipping through the net.

Although those few had been lucky enough to escape with their lives, on this Dead Star they were merely a few trapped beasts and could no longer stir up any waves.

While the Lu and He Family cultivators were fighting, Gale's Star Pirates also took the chance to vanish without a trace. The Lu Family had neither the mind nor the strength to deal with such a band of Star Pirates. Their target was only the He Family elites.

So Gale's group left this place very easily, without even drawing any pursuit.

Sheng Yao had been thoroughly abandoned.

Sharing fortune was easy; sharing hardship was difficult. Their First Chief might not even live to see tomorrow's sun, so naturally they were unwilling to stay and place their lives in someone else's hands.

Sheng Yao still knelt on one knee, inwardly cursing those Junior Brothers without restraint. In better times, they had eagerly followed him around, eating meat, drinking wine, and chasing women, each more enthusiastic than the last. Yet now that their boss had fallen into trouble, they all scattered, each trying to flee faster than the others. In that moment, the fickleness of human nature was laid bare.

Half an hour later, the Lu Family sounded the signal to withdraw. With only several hundred people, they had paid a very small price to obtain a great victory. In today's battle, hundreds of He Family elites had been killed or wounded, their Family Head had died, and even the He Family's only Void King-Grade Warship had been captured by them.

Such battle results could only be called astonishing.

Thinking of how the Lu Family would dominate Morning Sun Star in the future, standing alone as the sole great power, everyone grinned from ear to ear.

Everyone knew that all of this was thanks to that young man who had appeared out of nowhere. If not for him killing He Wuzui, who would have emerged victorious today was still unknown.

Lu Huaishuang hurried back, her clothes neat, her hair immaculate, and a charming smile blooming on her peerless face. But when she looked over, she could not help freezing.

Because the place where the young man had been was now empty.

Only Sheng Yao was still half kneeling on the ground, motionless, the sand beneath him almost soaked through by his sweat.

Lu Huaishuang frowned and looked around, but still could not find any trace of the young man. A sudden sense of loss rose in her heart.

She walked forward and asked, "Where is he?"

Sheng Yao did not answer.

"He's gone," Lu Huaishuang said again.

Sheng Yao was stunned. Only then did he carefully raise his head and look. Finding that there really was no one in front of him, his whole body shuddered.

There was no joy, only fright.

He actually had no idea when the young man had left.

They had been no more than two steps apart, almost within arm's reach. With his powerful Second Stage Void King Realm cultivation, even if a Third Stage expert wanted to leave, he would definitely have noticed.

Yet the young man had vanished so miraculously, without making the slightest sound. This terrified Sheng Yao, but also left him somewhat glad, glad that he had made a prompt decision just now. Although he had lost all face, at least he had preserved his life and had not followed in Second Brother, Third Brother, and Fourth Brother's footsteps.

"Can I get up?" Sheng Yao asked.

Lu Huaishuang coldly snorted. "You're asking me?"

She had no favorable impression of this First Chief of Gale. Almost all cultivators in the Star Domain had a poor opinion of people like Star Pirates, so she naturally showed him no courtesy.

Sheng Yao smiled awkwardly and tried to straighten his hunched body, but he did not stand up right away. Instead, he slowly rose while carefully looking around.

It was as if a figure might leap out at any moment and beat him flat.

This act of standing took a full half cup of tea's worth of time.

Only after he had fully stood did he let out a light breath and wipe the sweat from his forehead.

Nothing had happened. Since nothing had happened, it meant that Sir had no intention of doing anything to him. He felt somewhat discouraged. He was, after all, a Second Stage Void King Realm master, yet he had not even caught that man’s attention. Otherwise, why would he have been so completely ignored?

"Young Lady Lu, I have long admired your great name, heh heh." Sheng Yao piled a smile onto his face and cupped his fists toward Lu Huaishuang.

"Where did he go?" Lu Huaishuang had never before wanted to find someone as urgently as she did now, but the only person she could ask was Sheng Yao.

"This... I don't know either." Sheng Yao smiled bitterly again and again.

Lu Huaishuang immediately looked at him in surprise, sensing something else in his words, and her expression could not help shifting.

"But he should still be on this Dead Star, right?" Sheng Yao did not dare be certain either. In his heart, he had already begun calculating. If that man really had left, then he could take the chance to leave too. He no longer dared entertain any thoughts harmful to the Lu Family; after surviving this disaster, he only wanted to flee as far away as possible, so how could he have any mind for anything else? He hesitated, then said, "How about... we search together?"

Lu Huaishuang glanced at him, said nothing, and summoned a Star Shuttle. It turned into a streak of light and sped off in one direction.

The corner of Sheng Yao's mouth twitched. He hesitated briefly between fleeing and searching, then finally clenched his teeth and chased after her, calling out, "Young Lady Lu, wait for me."

It was not only reverence and fear. From that young man, he had seen traces of a higher Martial Dao, a goal he had always pursued but had never had the strength to grasp.

On a bare stone mountain, the young man's figure sat quietly cross-legged.

There was not much vitality on the Dead Star. There was not even the slightest hint of green. Stone mountains like this could be seen everywhere across the Dead Star. The mountain was covered in scattered sand and stones, and when wind swept by, dust filled the sky so thickly that nothing could be seen within three zhang.

The stone mountain was not tall, only a hundred zhang.

Sheng Yao and Lu Huaishuang stood at the foot of the mountain, looking up. Yet it felt as though they were gazing upon a towering peak ten thousand zhang high. The mountain itself was only a hundred zhang tall, but the figure seated at its summit seemed loftier than nine thousand nine hundred zhang.

Half a month ago, the two had searched together and finally found this place, where they saw the figure that inspired awe in them.

They did not dare disturb him and simply waited silently at the foot of the mountain.

This wait lasted a full half month. During that time, the young man sat there motionless, as if dead, his aura nearly absent.

No one knew what he was doing.

It did not look like cultivation, nor did it look like secluded retreat. Instead, it looked like he was sleeping.

Half a month of waiting was dull and tedious, but fortunately, both of them were Void King Realm cultivators. On ordinary days, even a single secluded retreat would take quite some time, so they were used to it.

Sheng Yao very much wanted to know this young man's origins and had probed Lu Huaishuang indirectly many times, but how could Lu Huaishuang know what background this young man had? He had appeared on her Warship out of nowhere, and at the time she had even thought he was already dead.

Now, she somewhat believed her Third Uncle's earlier speculation.

The hole in the Warship might well have been knocked open by this man. Only such an inconceivable existence could withstand a volley from ten Star Cannons and remain completely unharmed.

But she was somewhat irritated now, for no other reason than that this First Chief of Gale was a chatterbox. Every day, he chattered endlessly with her, not at all like a Second Stage Void King Realm expert, but more like a resentful woman shut away in the inner chambers.

Moreover, Lu Huaishuang had also discovered that when Sheng Yao looked at her, from beginning to end, he never once showed a covetous gaze.

This should not be merely because of his reverence toward the young man; there ought to be some other reason as well.

But this was fine too. Lu Huaishuang detested that sort of gaze.

"Young Lady Lu, this time, we can be considered friends made after a fight. Another day, if you have free time, why not come sit for a while in my Gale Territory? My Gale Territory has nothing else, only..."

"Say that after you live long enough to return," Lu Huaishuang interrupted his endless chatter without the slightest politeness.

Sheng Yao's face instantly turned bitter...
