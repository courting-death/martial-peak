## Chapter 3080 — Where the Gale Passes, Not a Blade of Grass Remains

Lu Huaishuang was no stranger to corpses. On the contrary, she had personally killed quite a few people.

Back when she first took charge of the Lu Family, the He Family, another great family on Morning Sun Star, had raised an army and invaded. The two great families immediately went to war, resulting in countless deaths and injuries. It was precisely her outstanding performance in that battle that established Lu Huaishuang's position.

Morning Sun Star was now divided between the Lu Family and the He Family, each ruling its own share. The two great families had split all its resources almost evenly.

However, the appearance of this Young Man made Lu Huaishuang frown in puzzlement rather than disgust.

She carefully studied the Young Man's face for a long while before finally looking up and asking an old man, "Third Uncle, is he one of ours?"

There were several hundred cultivators aboard the Warship. Although she could not call them all by name, she had seen nearly every one of them before, yet this Young Man was utterly unfamiliar to her.

She was certain she had never seen this man before.

Lu Bing shook his head and said, "No!"

"No?" Lu Huaishuang's beautiful eyes narrowed.

If he was not one of theirs, why had he appeared aboard the Warship? Countless possibilities flashed through her mind, the most likely being that an enemy of the Lu Family had planted him aboard as a spy.

If that was the case, there were very likely more troubles in store for her.

Seemingly guessing what she was thinking, Lu Bing said, "This man was found at the damaged section of the Warship."

"Third Uncle, are you saying..." Lu Huaishuang looked up at him. Despite her extraordinary intelligence, she could not work out what he was implying.

Lu Bing's expression became complicated. "From what I observed, it seems the Warship was damaged when this man crashed into it."

The moment he said this, the surroundings fell silent. Everyone pondered the meaning behind Lu Bing's words, and a shocking thought soon surfaced in each of their minds, filling their eyes with disbelief.

Lu Huaishuang also caught his meaning and stared blankly at her Third Uncle. Had she not known what kind of man he was, she would have thought he was playing a joke on her.

Lu Bing gave a bitter smile. "At first, I also thought the Warship had been struck by a meteorite, but after investigating carefully, I found no trace of one. Instead, I found this man, and the breach in the Warship looks somewhat as though it was made by a person crashing into it."

A chorus of sharp inhalations rang out as everyone stared at Lu Bing with strange expressions.

Lu Huaishuang remained silent, her expression solemn.

Lu Bing said, "I know this sounds inconceivable, but I truly cannot find any more reasonable explanation."

It was more than inconceivable. It was downright shocking.

A single person had actually smashed a hole in this Warship, slowing it down and forcing it to stop here for repairs? This was one of the two finest Warships on all of Morning Sun Star, a Void King-Grade vessel that could withstand the attacks of a Void King Realm expert for a long time without breaking. How could a mere person have damaged it simply by crashing into it?

If a Warship like this could be damaged so easily, it would never be able to travel through the Starry Sky. A few random asteroid impacts would likely destroy the ship and kill everyone aboard.

Even taking a step back and assuming that someone in this world could truly accomplish such a feat…

Had all ten Star Cannons firing in unison been nothing but everyone's imagination?

All ten Star Cannons had clearly struck their target. What kind of person could withstand their attacks? Yet judging from the Young Man's condition, he was completely uninjured from head to toe. Only his clothes were somewhat tattered.

Even if his body had been forged from steel, he could not possibly have endured that kind of damage.

Their thoughts were a hopeless tangle. The sudden discovery of this Young Man at the damaged section had thrown everyone into utter confusion, as though all common sense and understanding had been overturned.

"There is no point discussing this any further. Repair the Warship as soon as possible," Lu Huaishuang said, bringing the matter to a close.

Lu Bing sighed inwardly, knowing she did not really believe his conjecture. She was hardly alone; even he did not believe it and could not shake the feeling that he must be wrong.

Thus, he offered no argument.

"As for this man, find a place to bury him." Lu Huaishuang lowered her head and looked at the dead Young Man. No matter who he was or why he had appeared aboard the Warship, he was already dead. It was time for dust to return to dust and earth to return to earth.

Streaks of light suddenly shot in from every direction as waves of chaotic energy surged. Before the attackers themselves arrived, their attacks were already upon them.

Amid a dense barrage of hissing sounds and flashing light, the place where the Lu Family members stood became a sea of energy.

"Enemy attack!" someone cried out in terror, but his voice abruptly cut off as though an invisible hand had seized his throat. A moment later, his aura vanished entirely. He had clearly died.

Screams erupted at once.

Lu Huaishuang's expression changed. She had never imagined they would be attacked in a place like this. This was a Dead Star, and they had only decided to stop here on a whim. Who would come here to ambush them?

With no time to think further, she reached up to her delicate earlobe and crushed an earring. A radiant white light burst forth and abruptly transformed into a huge, bowl-shaped protective barrier that enveloped the surrounding Lu Family cultivators.

With a series of thunderous booms, the incoming attacks struck the protective barrier and sent layers of ripples across it. The barrier's light dimmed at a rate visible to the naked eye. In barely ten breaths, a crisp crack rang out and the protective barrier shattered.

However, the breathing room bought by those ten breaths allowed the Lu Family members to finally react. They all summoned their Secret Treasures and unleashed their Secret Techniques at the enemies surrounding them.

In an instant, energy ran wild, and the earth and mountains trembled.

A man suddenly swooped down from the sky. As he crossed his hands, they transformed into a vast curtain that blotted out the heavens and slammed down toward Lu Huaishuang's head.

Lu Huaishuang pursed her lips. With a flick of her fair hand, an azure longsword slid gracefully from its sheath, and her entire bearing transformed in that instant.

She was breathtakingly beautiful and possessed a graceful figure, but she was also a Void King Realm expert.

If she lacked strength, how could she possibly command the entire Lu Family? Besides, she was a woman; no matter where one went, strength was what commanded respect.

The azure longsword swept through the void, and a Sword Domain instantly took shape. Sword Intent surged unchecked, making everyone's skin sting.

The longsword in her hand abruptly became a blur as she met the attack from above. She thrust her sword more than a hundred times in an instant, and a hundred streams of Sword Qi converged into a single point of cold light before rapidly blazing outward like a prairie fire.

The terrifying pressure abruptly vanished. As a surprised exclamation rang out, the enemy swooping down from above flipped away and slid to one side.

Their clash lasted only an instant, but both Lu Huaishuang and her ambusher sensed how powerful the other was.

Each had met an opponent not the slightest bit weaker than themselves!

The clamorous battlefield became eerily silent after the exchange between its two strongest experts. Everyone felt as though they had just passed before the Gates of Hell, and when they came back to their senses, their backs were drenched in cold sweat.

Lu Huaishuang focused her gaze and saw a fat, fair-skinned fellow standing there with a beaming smile and an expression harmless to man and beast. His upper body was draped in an extremely loose garment that he had not even fastened, leaving his snow-white belly exposed. He looked like a fat grub that had eaten its fill, and together with his eyes, which were narrowed into almost imperceptible slits, he gave off an inexplicably comical air.

Yet no one dared to laugh. Instead, the sight made their blood run cold.

Lu Bing's expression turned ugly as he growled, "Ma Chaoqun!"

A cold gleam flashed through the fair-skinned fat man's eyes, though his smile remained amiable. "Oh? You recognize this Lord?"

Lu Bing swallowed hard.

With such an unmistakable build and features, coupled with such brutal methods, how could he fail to recognize him?

Ma Chaoqun of Gale!

Gale was not Ma Chaoqun's nickname, but the name of a force that caused people throughout the Star Domain endless headaches.

Mountains and forests were rife with fierce bandits, the seas with pirates, and in the Starry Sky, there were also those known as Star Pirates.

Their deeds differed little from those of bandits and pirates. Their trade required no capital: they waylaid and robbed others while committing every evil imaginable.

Star Pirates were numerous throughout the Star Domain, but only a handful were famous. Gale was one of them because this Star Pirate force had a Void King Realm expert overseeing it.

Void King Realm experts stood at the very peak of the entire Star Domain and were overlord-level existences on any Cultivation Star. If an expert of that caliber took to waylaying and robbing others, it was nothing short of a disaster.

Several top forces in the Star Domain had once joined hands and laid plans to wipe out these Star Pirates, but Star Pirates were like weeds. Wildfire could not burn them all away, and they sprang to life again when the spring wind blew. Destroy one or two Star Pirate forces, and even more would spring up like bamboo shoots after rain.

Gale was also extremely cunning and never provoked anyone they could not afford to offend. They only targeted forces wealthy enough to be worth plundering but too weak to resist. They had no moral limits whatsoever, and their victims never met a good end: everyone was killed, and everything was taken.

Where Gale passed, not a blade of grass remained!

It could be said that Gale was the most notorious band of Star Pirates in the Wuji Star Domain.

All around the Lu Family members, Gale's Star Pirates drove bizarrely shaped Fierce Tiger Chariots, completely surrounding them. Every one of them wore a malicious smile. In particular, when their eyes fell on Lu Huaishuang, they shone with startling light and swept brazenly over the shapely parts of her body as though staring at a delicious feast.

The smile on Ma Chaoqun's face grew even wider, and his fat belly quivered in rolling waves.

"You must be Young Lady Lu. You truly possess beauty capable of toppling nations, and your cultivation is extraordinary as well. Seeing you in person is indeed better than hearing of your fame. This Ma has long admired you."

Lu Huaishuang kept her expression rigid as her gaze swept over the Lu Family members.

Although the attack had come suddenly, the events of today were far too suspicious.

It was as though the people of Gale had known they were here and had deliberately sought them out.

As the current de facto head of the Lu Family, she could not help considering certain possibilities.

A Void King Realm expert had extremely keen eyes, so she quickly noticed a clue.

It was one of the Lu Family clansmen, a clan uncle by seniority. Yet when their gazes met, he looked away evasively.

Her heart suddenly ached, and she felt somewhat disillusioned.

She did not understand why one of her own kin would betray her.
