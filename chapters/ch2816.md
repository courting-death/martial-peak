## Chapter 2816 — The Opportunity Arrives

Yu led several warriors of the Frost Snow Tribe toward a tall building. Yang Kai stood within Frost Snow City, pondering for a moment. He did not hurry to find his lodging as Yu had instructed, but instead began to wander through the bustling Frost Snow City, observing as he walked.

The only reason he agreed to come to Frost Snow City with Yu was simply to quickly obtain some Inner Cores for cultivation.

However, he now possessed nothing of value. Aside from seven Bone-Eating Wolf Inner Cores, he only had a pouch of wolf blood. Yet both were essential to him, so Yang Kai could only first investigate the local market before making any plans.

He believed that, despite starting from humble beginnings, his skills would soon allow him to gain the upper hand and set off a snowball effect, quickly building momentum in his favor.

As he walked and observed, Yang Kai appeared quite out of place in this city dominated by the barbarians. The reason was simple: his figure looked far too frail, lacking any of the barbarian's aesthetic appeal.

So wherever he went, he attracted the barbarians' curious and disdainful gazes.

Yang Kai had long grown accustomed to this. It seemed that every barbarian who saw him for the first time would look at him this way.

Frost Snow City was truly prosperous. Even compared to the great cities of later generations, it was not lacking in the slightest. Amidst the chaotic buildings were countless stalls and goods, and Shamans from different tribes gathered here. As a mere Lower-Level Shaman Warrior, Yang Kai was completely insignificant.

Relying on the knowledge the village chief had taught him before his departure, Yang Kai carefully identified the origins and backgrounds of the various Shamans.

From all corners of the land, they gathered in one place. Such a grand occasion was rarely seen.

This was a scene that could only be witnessed in Frost Snow City during the winter.

In winter, the other tribes' cities did not have the unique advantages of Frost Snow City. Here, the Divine Tree offered its protection. Even in the harshest winter, this place was full of spring's vitality. The other tribes' cities could not compare, so naturally, many Shamans were drawn here to gather and exchange.

Once winter passed, most of these visiting Shamans would return to their own tribes and cities.

Thus, every winter, Frost Snow City was the most bustling city of all.

Yang Kai lingered at stall after stall, inspecting the goods and haggling with the vendors.

He had thought that in this ancient era bartering would be the norm, but after looking around he realized he had been completely mistaken.

To his surprise, Frost Snow City actually had its own form of currency—something resembling green leaves, known as Qing Coins.

The village chief had never told Yang Kai about the existence of Qing Coins, so he deduced that this must be a unique feature of Frost Snow City.

Qing Coins were useless for cultivation. Legend had it they were fallen leaves from the Divine Tree, incorruptible and indestructible. They could only be used for transactions, and only within Frost Snow City.

But that was enough. Having a currency as an equivalent for goods greatly facilitated the flow of resources.

Transactions in Frost Snow City continued around the clock. Yang Kai spent three days wandering without rest, and a vague plan had already formed in his mind.

Three days later, he finally strolled to the base of the Divine Tree, took out the waterskin Yu had given him, and poured the wolf blood onto the roots of the Divine Tree.

The crimson blood hit the ground and was quickly absorbed, leaving not a trace. In the next instant, a green light shot down from the tree's crown, striking Yang Kai's body.

At the same time, a mysterious force guided Yang Kai, as if an invisible giant hand was lifting him, slowly raising him upward.

After ascending several dozen zhang, the supporting force finally stabilized. In front of Yang Kai was a not-too-large, clean, and comfortable tree hollow.

This would be his residence for the next thirty days.

Outsiders seeking lodging in Frost Snow City had two options. One was to stay in an inn-like place, which required payment in Qing Coins. The second was to do as Yang Kai had done—pour Demonic Beast blood onto the roots of the Divine Tree, and the Divine Tree would grant the pourer a tree hollow to reside in.

Such tree hollows were countless on the towering Divine Tree. Each was a natural dwelling, protected by the Divine Tree. No matter what one did inside, they would not be disturbed.

The tree hollow was not large, barely enough for three people to live in, but for Yang Kai, it was more than sufficient.

The environment inside was extremely comfortable. Yang Kai sat cross-legged, cultivating while refining his plan in his mind.

The next day, he emerged from the tree hollow, slowly descending to the ground under the Divine Tree's guidance, then headed straight in one direction.

Before long, Yang Kai arrived at a shop specializing in purchasing Inner Cores and sold all seven Inner Cores he had.

A moment later, Yang Kai left the shop with a bag of Qing Coins and immediately disappeared into the crowd.

After a stick of incense's time and some hard bargaining, Yang Kai reached an agreement with a stranger at a stall. For fifty Qing Coins, he bought out all the herbs at the stall. The vendor was overjoyed, feeling he had met a big customer, and enthusiastically packed the herbs for Yang Kai.

Soon after, the same scene played out at another stall.

It did not take long for Yang Kai to spend all his Qing Coins, but in exchange, he had acquired a large pile of common, low-value herbs.

He returned to the tree hollow and took out all the herbs.

Without needing to examine them closely, he casually picked a few herbs. A bright, warm flame rose in Yang Kai's hand, and he tossed the herbs into the fire, focusing intently on refining them.

Just as alchemists were rare and esteemed in later generations, so too were apothecaries in this ancient era.

Every apothecary was a rare talent for their tribe, and each tribe's apothecary would only prepare medicines for their own people. In this era, the Martial Dao had not yet flourished, and the art of alchemy was still in its infancy. Apothecaries' methods were simple and crude, merely mixing various herbs together with little attention to detail, unable to fully bring out the herbs' effects.

Yang Kai was an Imperial-Grade Pill Master. Even with his cultivation sealed and unable to use his Divine Sense, with his current skills, anything he casually refined would not be inferior to the products of the great tribes' apothecaries.

And these medicines he refined would be his means of making a living—the starting point for his snowball to grow.

The era was different, so he dared not be too astonishing. Besides, his strength was currently low, so he could not achieve perfection in the refining process. Even so, what he produced was the result of tens of thousands of years of accumulated knowledge, utterly unmatched by the apothecaries of this age.

Half a day later, all the herbs were used up, and Yang Kai was left with a pile of bottles and jars.

In this era, jade bottles for storing pills were extremely rare, but Yang Kai did not mind. What he had refined was not pills, but a pile of paste-like substances, so storing them in stone jars was perfectly fine.

After tidying up briefly, Yang Kai left the tree hollow again.

In the busiest part of Frost Snow City, where people surged like a tide, Yang Kai sat cross-legged in a corner, laid out his wares before him, took a wooden board, and, channeling his energy into his finger like a sword, wrote a large, flamboyant character on it, then planted it beside him. He assumed an aloof posture and closed his eyes to meditate.

A nearby barbarian vendor glanced at Yang Kai, then at the large "medicine" character on the board, and could not help but snicker.

Were these black, mud-like things really medicine? Did he truly think himself an apothecary?

Apothecaries were always esteemed. How could one end up setting up a stall? If not for the shamanic aura coming from this fellow, the barbarian vendor would have doubted he was even a barbarian.

Unless the Barbarian God performed a miracle, these things would never sell.

And as expected, for two days straight, no one paid any attention to Yang Kai's stall. Though this was a bustling area with a constant flow of people, every barbarian who passed by merely glanced at the paste and walked away.

But Yang Kai remained unmoved, sitting cross-legged without impatience or irritation, which earned some admiration from his neighbor for his composure.

After two days, the neighbor's goods were nearly sold out, and he was about to pack up.

Perhaps out of sympathy, the barbarian vendor could not help but say, "You won't sell anything like this."

Only then did Yang Kai open his eyes, grin, and reply, "Then in your opinion, how should I sell them?"

The vendor said, "At the very least, you should call out a few times. Even if these medicines don't look great, if you shout enough, someone will eventually buy."

Yang Kai chuckled and replied, “Fine wine needs no hawker. These are good medicines—buyers will come even if I don’t call out.”

The vendor curled his lips, clearly disagreeing, though it sounded reasonable. He shook his head and said, "Unless a miracle happens, you'll never sell them in your life."

Yang Kai smiled, "No miracle is needed, just an opportunity. And the opportunity has arrived!"

As he spoke, Yang Kai's eyes suddenly sparkled.

The neighboring vendor was momentarily stunned. Following Yang Kai's gaze, he saw several Frost Snow Tribe barbarians supporting each other as they approached. Judging by their blood-soaked appearance, they had just returned from a fierce battle, and it must have been a grueling one, for each bore wounds of various sizes, one even having a gash over a chi long on his abdomen, with flesh turned out—a terrifying sight.

What kind of opportunity was this? The neighboring vendor was puzzled.

While he was still wondering, the group of barbarian warriors had already passed by.

Just then, the medicine seller suddenly spoke with a beaming smile, “Warriors, please wait! I see that your foreheads are broad and your chins full, your blood and vigor surging to the heavens—you share a bit of fate with this Shaman. I happen to have an item to present as a gift. I hope you warriors will accept it with a smile!”
