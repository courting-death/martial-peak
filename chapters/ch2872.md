## Chapter 2872 — The Struggle for the Sacred Artifact

Sacred Artifacts were treasures that only Demon Saints were qualified to wield. Across the entire Demon Domain there were not many of them, and each possessed world-destroying, heaven-shattering power.

As Demon Kings, all of them had seen at least a few Sacred Artifacts and felt the destructive power emanating from those treasures, so they naturally had some understanding of them.

Now, upon suddenly hearing Shaya say she had caught a Sacred Artifact, everyone was momentarily stunned, their first reaction being disbelief.

However, Shaya’s demeanor did not seem fake; she truly had grabbed something.

So, after a brief moment of hesitation, several burly Demon Kings immediately rushed forward, standing beside Shaya and reaching into the vortex. Yet, their hands grasped only emptiness—there was nothing there at all.

The group turned their heads, looking at Shaya with suspicion.

Shaya anxiously shouted, “Grab my hand!”

Only then did those Demon Kings come to their senses and hurriedly grab Shaya’s arms. As soon as they did, their expressions changed, for they truly felt a tremendous force pulling against her from within the vortex.

They all let out low growls, demonic qi surging. Shaya, as if aided by divine power, suddenly staggered back a step, and her arms, which had been hidden in the vortex, were instantly pulled out.

In the next instant, over a dozen pairs of eyes stared at the object in Shaya’s hand, shocked and greedy. Strictly speaking, the object had not been fully pulled out yet—only a portion was exposed.

It was the hilt of a weapon. No one knew exactly what kind of weapon it was, but the part that was revealed radiated an extremely pure and dense demonic qi.

Its aura far surpassed the demonic qi of the Demon Kings.

“It really is... a Sacred Artifact!” one Demon King muttered, his eyes instantly turning red. The other Demon Kings were also visibly excited.

Although the object in the vortex had not been completely pulled out and they had only caught a fleeting glimpse, with so many Demon Kings present, none doubted what they saw.

This was definitely a Sacred Artifact—one that only a Demon Saint could control!

Just as everyone was shocked and dazed, an even stronger force suddenly surged from the vortex. The power was so overwhelming that several Demon Kings felt they could not resist, crying out in alarm as the demonic qi within them became chaotic.

Shaya let out a miserable scream. Blood spurted from her snow-white hands as she suddenly fell backward, landing hard on the ground, while the Demon Kings who had helped her stumbled back as well.

The vortex ahead quickly returned to calm. The half-exposed Sacred Artifact had vanished without a trace, but the Demon Kings still stared intently at the vortex, as if trying to peer into its depths to find the artifact’s whereabouts.

Shaya’s ragged breathing brought everyone back to their senses. Only then did the Demon Kings remember to look at her, wanting to ask what had happened.

What they saw was Shaya’s hands, a bloody mess, with bone showing through her palms. It was clear she had been injured in the struggle with the Sacred Artifact. Though the wounds looked gruesome, for a Demon King, they were not a big deal. What was noteworthy was the unique strand of demonic qi lingering around Shaya’s wounds.

Every Demon King was watching, each one’s expression filled with excitement, for they could tell that the strand of demonic qi was the aura left by the Sacred Artifact. This proved that what had just happened was not an illusion.

Shaya really had pulled out a Sacred Artifact from within, but failed at the crucial moment before anyone could see its true form, which was rather regrettable.

“Shaya, what kind of Sacred Artifact was it?” the Shadow Demon asked. Usually calm, his voice now trembled, showing his excitement.

Shaya’s eyes flickered with recollection. “A warhammer!”

“A warhammer!” the Demon Kings gasped softly.

Bao Qi said in a deep voice, “What else did you see? Was there any danger inside?”

Shaya shook her head. “No danger. It seemed like a very special world. I saw the Sacred Artifact, and I also saw... the corpse of an ancient saint!”

The Demon Kings’ eyes lit up, and they all turned to look at the vortex.

The information Shaya brought back was not much, but it was enough. That fleeting glimpse of the Sacred Artifact was a fatal temptation for the Demon Kings.

Bao Qi took a deep breath and said loudly, “You all heard her. Since there’s no danger inside...”

Before he could finish, the Shadow Demon suddenly let out a strange laugh. To everyone’s surprise, his voice came from a different direction. Looking over, they saw the Shadow Demon dart into the vortex, while the shadow left outside was just a decoy, dispersing as he entered.

“Mo Ke Tuo!” Bone Demon Fei Li shouted angrily, unwilling to fall behind, and instantly chased after him.

Seeing this, the other Demon Kings had no interest in listening to Bao Qi’s words. If they hesitated any longer, they might miss out, so they rushed into the vortex one after another, disappearing from sight.

Bao Qi remained where he stood, watching with a strange expression as his former companions threw themselves headlong into the trap. He had no interest in speaking further. Only after they had all disappeared did he glance over at Shaya. “What was that, exactly?”

He did not believe it was truly a Sacred Artifact, but what he had just witnessed defied explanation.

Shaya gracefully stood up, her bloody hands healing at a speed visible to the naked eye, becoming fair and clean again. She smiled lightly. “Didn’t you see it clearly yourself?”

Bao Qi frowned. “But...”

But where would Lord Shaman Niu get a Sacred Artifact? Only a few Demon Saints had come for this invasion, and although there had been a great battle with the Shaman Saints of the foreign race, no one had died, so no Sacred Artifact should have been left behind.

“Why not go in and see for yourself?” Shaya giggled, stepping into the vortex without any hindrance, entering the Small Xuan Realm.

Bao Qi was reluctant to follow. After all, he had schemed against so many Demon Kings on Yang Kai’s orders this time. Though it was not his intention, he was too embarrassed to face them. Still, curiosity got the better of him, and after hesitating for a while, he gritted his teeth and entered the vortex.

Inside the Small Xuan Realm, a group of Demon Kings were already fighting, as if they were mortal enemies, battling fiercely. Demonic qi surged as powerful techniques burst forth, each move ruthless and vicious.

Bao Qi, who had just entered, was startled by the scene before him, his mind going blank. He instinctively thought he was hallucinating, but upon closer inspection, he realized he was not mistaken. The Demon Kings who had just been gathered together were truly fighting, and they were going all out.

While Bao Qi was distracted, his gaze was drawn to a warhammer floating in midair. Its hilt looked familiar—it was the same part they had seen earlier. Now, the entire warhammer was on display, confirming it was a Sacred Artifact.

The pure demonic qi emanating from the warhammer was unmistakable.

In the midst of the battle, the sacred warhammer changed hands repeatedly. Any Demon King who managed to seize it was immediately beset by the others, so no one could keep hold of it. Even the likes of Shadow Demon Mo Ke Tuo and Bone Demon Fei Li, who were close to Demon Saint level, could not manage it.

“A bunch of fools!” Shaya’s light laughter came from the side.

Bao Qi turned to see Lord Shaman Niu and Shaya standing beside him, though he did not know when they had appeared. Shaya watched the battle below with a mocking expression, while Lord Shaman Niu looked indifferent, as if watching children fight.

“My lord... that Sacred Artifact...” Bao Qi could not help but ask the question in his heart.

“It’s real,” Yang Kai replied.

Bao Qi was shocked. He wanted to ask more but dared not speak further.

The Demonic Warhammer was indeed real. It was a trophy Yang Kai had obtained outside Fenglin City years ago, the weapon of an ancient one-eyed giant demon. After Yang Kai refined it, he gave it to his Incarnation to use.

However, the Incarnation had been fusing with the Divine Spirit Shi Huo’s source and had not awakened until today, so the Demonic Warhammer had remained unused.

Yang Kai brought it out today, and it proved to be an excellent lure. Even the Demon Kings could not resist the temptation of the Demonic Warhammer, stepping into the trap one after another.

“My lord, is it alright for you to show yourself like this?” Shaya asked curiously. Strangely, while she and Bao Qi stood here watching the show, the other Demon Kings, though suspicious, had no time to think about it.

Yet Yang Kai stood there openly, and the other Demon Kings seemed not to notice him at all, not casting a single glance his way.

Yang Kai smiled. “Since I control everything, I naturally have a way to keep them from seeing me.”

Shaya responded with an “Oh,” indicating her understanding.

Yang Kai continued, “It’s your turn to take the stage!”

Shaya said, “My lord, you must protect me carefully!”

As she spoke, she shouted, “The Sacred Artifact is mine! I found it!” Then, with a shake of her delicate body, she joined the battle, acting as if she wanted to snatch the Demonic Warhammer. But her strength was at the bottom among the Demon Kings, so no matter how hard she tried, she could not break into the inner circle, looking anxious and helpless.

The Demon Kings’ battle was bloody and brutal. In their struggle for the Demonic Warhammer, they used every means possible. Before long, some were injured, but the wounds did not make them stop. Instead, they only made them fiercer, and the fight grew even more intense.

The Demonic Warhammer changed hands again and again. After several rounds, the weaker Demon Kings no longer dared approach the center of the battlefield and could only circle the outskirts, looking for opportunities. In this way, Shaya finally had a few companions and did not stand out as much.

This melee among the Demon Kings lasted for more than an hour. Finally, an angry roar rang out: “Everyone, stop!”

The Demon Kings instinctively stopped fighting, quickly distancing themselves from one another and looking toward the source of the voice.

Over there, many of Bone Demon Fei Li’s bones had been knocked loose, and every movement produced cracking sounds. The ghostly fire in his eye sockets seemed to have dimmed considerably.

The other Demon Kings were the same. Their once-powerful auras had weakened, and all were panting heavily.

At this moment, Shadow Demon Mo Ke Tuo, shrouded in black qi, was the master of the Demonic Warhammer, keeping it far from everyone else.

Fei Li ignored the other Demon Kings and instead looked up at Bao Qi, the ghost fire in his eyes fixed on him.
