## Chapter 2838 — Gathering of a Hundred Shamans

The next day, when Yang Kai stepped out of the wooden hut, more than two hundred pairs of eyes instantly focused on him. Every man, woman, and child in the village had gathered here. Though the crowd was large, not a sound could be heard; a sense of unity and resolve connected every villager.

Amidst the crowd, the petite Die stood at the very front, her presence especially striking.

Yang Kai looked around, and every villager who met his gaze showed a simple, honest smile.

Perhaps they did not know what the Demons were, nor what their invasion meant, but each of them understood that this journey might be one from which they would never return, and that they might die far from home.

After all, Ah Niu had made things perfectly clear the day before.

Yet not a single person shrank back. The Barbarians were a people destined to press ever forward, never turning back even after crashing into a wall.

Sorrow was hidden in Yang Kai’s eyes, but he said nothing more. He simply waved his hand and declared, “Let’s go!”

He took the lead, heading toward the Royal City. The more than two hundred villagers followed closely behind, their steps in unison, like a well-trained army.

With the pace of the Barbarian Tribe, the journey to the Royal City would take at least three days. If an ordinary shaman were leading these two hundred people, they would have to travel at full speed to reach the destination within the allotted time.

But now that Yang Kai was a Great Shaman Master well-versed in all kinds of shamanic arts, a single application of the Lightness Art increased the villagers’ marching speed by a third.

Though their speed was fast, everyone remained calm and orderly.

Two days later, when Yang Kai led the villagers to the Royal City, his group of over two hundred had astonishingly grown to a thousand!

The reason was simple: along the way, they encountered several groups from other villages. These villages also had shamans presiding over them, but compared to Yang Kai, a Great Shaman Master, they were far inferior. The strongest among them was only an Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice, while the rest were at the Lower-Level or Middle-Level Shaman Apprentice stage.

The strength of these villages was about the same as Cangnan Village back when the old village chief, Shaman Li, was still alive.

The Barbarian Tribe had always had the custom of submitting to the strong. Since they were all people of the Southern Barbarian Tribe, and Yang Kai was a Great Shaman Master, the groups from these villages naturally joined his and followed his lead.

After witnessing with their own eyes that Yang Kai’s shamanic arts could completely envelop an army of a thousand, these Shaman Apprentices revered him even more. The villages already had some contact with each other, so many villagers were familiar. Once gathered together, they quickly became one, with no distinction between them.

After this battle, if these thousand people survived, Yang Kai could easily establish a small tribe with his own strength and prestige.

Before the Royal City of the Southern Barbarian Tribe, the thousand-strong army halted. From afar, the Royal City was no less grand than Frost Snow City. The only difference was that Frost Snow City was guarded by a Divine Tree, while the Southern Barbarian Tribe had no such guardian.

Outside the Royal City, on the surrounding open ground, many Barbarians had already gathered. Groups of a hundred, bands of a thousand, each small group had a shaman presiding over them, their strengths varying. Clearly, they had all come in response to the Royal City’s summons, rushing in from all directions. With so many people arriving at once, it was impossible for them all to crowd into the Royal City, as there simply wasn’t enough space inside.

So everyone could only wait outside, eating in the wind and sleeping in the dew. But such things were nothing to the Barbarian Tribe.

Yang Kai and his group were neither early nor late. The spots closest to the Royal City had already been taken by other small tribes. Sweeping his Divine Sense over the area, Yang Kai found a patch of open ground that was neither too big nor too small and led his group over.

Once they arrived, there was no need for instructions. The thousand-strong group immediately got busy—some took out their rations, others started fires and began cooking. Everyone was organized and efficient.

At that moment, a Shaman Master flew out from the Royal City, shouting as he flew, “The Shaman King has ordered all Great Shaman Masters to assemble in the Royal City!”

He shouted several times in a row, drawing everyone’s attention.

Immediately, Great Shaman Masters flew out from the crowd, heading straight for the Royal City. With someone taking the lead, it was like a chain reaction—one after another, experts exuding the aura of Great Shaman Masters leapt up, flocking toward the Royal City like swallows returning to their nests.

Yang Kai glanced over, then turned back and said, “I’ll go for a bit. Keep an eye on things here.”

Die nodded slightly.

Although she was a Great Shaman Master, she was from the Drifting Tribe, so it wasn’t appropriate for her to show herself on such an occasion.

The Royal City was vast. Though Yang Kai set out later, he arrived before many others. Like the other Great Shaman Masters, he landed in front of a palace and waited quietly.

Quite a few people had already gathered here. With the Demon invasion and a single order from the Shaman King, the entire Southern Barbarian Tribe had assembled. Great Shaman Masters, who were rarely seen in ordinary times, were now as common as cabbages and radishes.

With a sweep of his Divine Sense, Yang Kai could tell there were at least a hundred Great Shaman Masters here, and that wasn’t even all of them—there were surely more still on the way.

With no summons, the Great Shaman Masters dared not barge into the Shaman King’s palace. They could only wait outside. Those who were acquainted gathered in groups of three or five, quietly discussing the Demon invasion.

Apparently, they had all received some news from the messengers, but no one could say exactly what kind of beings the Demons were. The more they discussed, the more confused they became. Still, the massive mobilization by the Royal City left everyone feeling uneasy.

Everyone knew that the coming battle would likely be unprecedented in history.

Just as Yang Kai was growing bored, a shadow suddenly appeared before him, looming over him like a mountain.

Yang Kai turned his head and found himself facing a broad, sturdy chest covered in black hair.

“You’re from our Southern Barbarian Tribe too?” The owner of the shadow looked down at Yang Kai and asked in a buzzing voice.

The surrounding chatter immediately quieted. Many Great Shaman Masters turned their attention this way, watching with interest.

Truth be told, when everyone had first gathered here, quite a few people had already been sizing up Yang Kai. His physique was rather out of place here—like a goat among tigers, he stood out awkwardly.

Being so different, he naturally attracted attention. Few people in the Barbarian Tribe had such a build. Even the old village chief Shaman Li of Cangnan Village, though aged before his death, had clearly been burly in his youth.

Still, Yang Kai did exude the aura of a Great Shaman Master. Though others were curious, they hadn’t paid him much mind—until someone confronted him directly.

Ever since coming to this ancient world, Yang Kai had gotten used to looking up when speaking to people, as if he always had to look up at everyone. It was extremely irritating.

This time, he simply stared at the man’s thick black chest hair and nodded. “Yeah.”

“Heh!” The man laughed. “Kid, I’m talking to you. Look up at me.”

“My neck’s sore. Why don’t you squat down and talk to me?” Yang Kai shook his head, his face serious.

The burly Great Shaman Master’s aura suddenly grew heavier. Someone nearby burst out laughing, “Shaman Tu, this guy’s looking down on you! Give him a lesson he won’t forget!”

Barbarians were hot-tempered, and there was never a shortage of people eager to stir up trouble. Everyone was bored waiting here anyway, so a bit of fun was welcome.

In an instant, all eyes turned this way, eager to watch the show.

“Enough of your nonsense!” Shaman Tu turned and barked at the person who had spoken, then looked back at Yang Kai and snorted coldly. “What? Don’t have the guts to look at me?”

Yang Kai kept staring at that patch of black hair and said, “I just prefer people to lower their heads when talking to me.”

Shaman Tu was momentarily stunned, but quickly caught Yang Kai’s meaning and flew into a rage. “You brat, you’re asking for a beating!”

As soon as he finished speaking, a fist the size of a cooking pot came crashing down toward Yang Kai’s head.

The onlookers were not alarmed but delighted. Someone even whistled, clearly entertained. After all, Barbarians were tough, and everyone here was a Great Shaman Master. A punch like that wouldn’t kill anyone—at most, it would draw blood, and blood was always a favorite sight among the Barbarians.

Yang Kai didn’t move a muscle, as if he hadn’t even noticed the attack. The wind from the punch whipped his hair into the air.

Just then, the tightly closed palace doors suddenly opened, and a Barbarian warrior with a steel blade at his waist strode out, shouting, “The Shaman King wants you all inside.”

The massive fist stopped an inch above Yang Kai’s head. Shaman Tu’s face twisted, and it took great effort for him to suppress his urge to strike. He withdrew his fist and said, “Count yourself lucky. Next time, I won’t let you off so easily.”

With that, he turned and left.

The Shaman King’s order was not to be ignored. That punch could have landed, but if he angered the Shaman King, the consequences would not be worth it. Barbarians might be simple-minded, but they were not fools—they could weigh the consequences.

More than a hundred Great Shaman Masters lined up and filed silently into the grand hall, the previous commotion gone.

Yang Kai brought up the rear. As he passed the Barbarian warrior, he glanced at him, seemingly by accident.

He sensed that the warrior’s qi and blood were so vigorous that it was almost abnormal. Compared to him, even Ah Hu and the others were nothing. Faintly, Yang Kai felt this Barbarian warrior was not someone to be trifled with.

Even an ordinary Great Shaman Master might not fare well against him.

Yet Yang Kai sensed no shamanic power from him at all. In other words, this man had cultivated only his physical body.

Sure enough, there were Three Thousand Great Daos—each path leads to the heavens. It was said that in ancient times, some could achieve sainthood through sheer physical cultivation. Yang Kai hadn’t thought much of it before, but after seeing this Barbarian warrior, he realized it was no empty tale.

Reportedly, among the current Four Great Shaman Saints, one had achieved sainthood through physical cultivation alone.

Yang Kai stared openly, and the Barbarian warrior naturally noticed. He lifted his eyelids, and his sharp gaze shot straight at Yang Kai like two blades, making Yang Kai’s eyes sting.
