## Chapter 3041 — Fu Chi Is Here Too

"Are you deaf? Hurry up and get out of the way!" Madam Hua also shouted shrilly.

"Shut up!" Yang Kai slapped her across the face.

A crisp smack rang out. Madam Hua was utterly stunned, feeling only excruciating pain in one cheek as it rapidly swelled.

Although Yang Kai had injured her earlier, that had been the result of losing an exchange. It was in no way comparable to being slapped across the face like this.

"You actually dare..."

Pa...

Another slap landed.

Her other cheek swelled as well, and the taste of blood filled her mouth. Madam Hua immediately fell silent, though her venomous eyes continued to glare fiercely at Yang Kai. She finally understood that this young man, who had remained silent until now, was a real hard case; the more arrogantly she challenged him, the more ruthlessly he struck. Naturally, Madam Hua had no desire to suffer any further immediate losses. All she could do now was wait for the Dragon Clan to arrive and uphold justice for her.

A streak of red light rapidly approached, arriving nearby in the blink of an eye.

Lu Sanniang's delicate body began trembling, and her face turned deathly pale.

Countless thoughts raced through her mind as she wondered how she should explain the scene before her when the Dragon Clan arrived. Yet no matter how she considered it, she could think of no good solution. The only thing awaiting Yang Kai and the mother and daughter was the Dragon Clan's towering wrath.

She reached out and pulled Lu Yuqin into her arms, seemingly resigned to her fate and prepared to spend their final moments holding her daughter close.

The speeding red light swept over everyone's heads and continued toward the other side of the Spirit Island.

Lu Sanniang froze for a moment.

Madam Hua was also stunned, but her beautiful face soon paled as she cried out, "Sir, save me!"

She thought the passing Dragon Clan member had not noticed her being bullied, so she naturally hurried to call out and attract his attention.

Yang Kai coldly snorted.

The speeding red light suddenly halted, and a soft exclamation of surprise rang out. Immediately afterward, the figure revealed his true form and swept his gaze in their direction, his pupils abruptly contracting.

"Sir, please save me!" Madam Hua shouted again, her voice mournful and her appearance pitiful.

The Dragon Clan member turned, transformed into a streak of red light, and in the blink of an eye landed not far in front of everyone, his gaze latching onto Yang Kai like a leech.

Dragon Pressure spread through the air. Lu Sanniang's eyes filled with terror and alarm, and she knew there was probably no hope of salvaging the situation now. She secretly studied the Dragon Clan member and found that he appeared roughly the same age as Yang Kai. He had sword-like brows, bright eyes, and a handsome, distinguished bearing, while his fiery red hair was particularly striking.

What frightened Lu Sanniang most was that the Dragon Pressure emanating from this red-haired Dragon was in no way weaker than Fu Chi's.

In other words, he was unquestionably an Eighth-Tier Dragon.

It was over. This time, it was truly over. The world spun around Lu Sanniang, and she nearly fainted on the spot.

"Sir Zhu Lie, save me!" Madam Hua, still pinned beneath Yang Kai's foot, called out for the third time.

Only then did Zhu Lie look toward her and ask indifferently, "You know me?"

The members of the Dragon Clan lived scattered across different islands and rarely interacted with one another. Thus, even the women who had lived on Dragon Isle for several hundred years might not recognize every member of the Dragon Clan. The fact that this woman actually recognized him clearly meant her status was somewhat unusual.

Madam Hua hurriedly replied, "I once had the good fortune to see Sir from afar and would not dare forget you."

Zhu Lie nodded slightly and said thoughtfully, "You are Fu Chi's woman!"

Madam Hua nodded. "Yes!"

"What happened here?" Zhu Lie frowned and glanced at Yang Kai, inwardly feeling a headache coming on.

Never had he expected to see Yang Kai here. This fellow was far too bold, actually daring to come to Dragon Isle, and of all places, he had just happened to appear on Fu Chi's island. Could he have heard some news? If so, that would be bad. Zhu Lie had personally experienced Yang Kai's lawlessness and knew that if today's matter was not handled properly, it would become a problem for the Dragon Clan as well.

Madam Hua hurriedly said, "Sir, this is what happened." Her expression instantly became pitiful, and she explained between soft sobs, "A pet this Mistress has raised for more than ten years was injured by these people for no reason. In my grief, I came here to demand an explanation, but this man proved completely unreasonable. Not only did he refuse to admit fault, but at the slightest disagreement, he attacked this Mistress and my two sisters. We sisters have not fought anyone in many years, so how could we possibly be his opponents? All three of us were humiliated. This Mistress begged for mercy, yet he only became more overbearing. Not only did he refuse to stop, but he even... even tried to kill me! Please uphold justice for this Mistress and my sisters, Sir!"

As she spoke, she began weeping bitterly, looking like a little girl who had been bullied and was inconsolably heartbroken.

Zhu Lie looked grimly at Yang Kai, wondering what exactly was wrong with this fellow and how he managed to cause trouble wherever he went. Naturally, Zhu Lie would not blindly believe Madam Hua's account. He understood Yang Kai to some extent and did not believe he was the sort who relied on his strength to bully others.

Reading his expression, Madam Hua embellished her story further. "Sir, just look at this man! He clearly has no respect for you. Even in your presence, he still dares act so viciously and refuses to take his foot off this Mistress. Before you arrived, he was even... Wu wu wu..."

Lu Sanniang hurriedly said, "Sir, that is not what happened."

Madam Hua had glossed over every important detail, saying nothing of her own arrogant and domineering conduct. Instead, she shirked all responsibility and shifted every bit of blame onto Yang Kai. Naturally, Lu Sanniang feared Zhu Lie would believe only Madam Hua's one-sided story and then do something to Yang Kai.

At this moment, she was the only one who could step forward and offer an explanation on Yang Kai's behalf.

Without even turning his head, Zhu Lie said, "It is not your place to speak."

His mood seemed extremely poor, and he showed Lu Sanniang no courtesy whatsoever.

Lu Sanniang immediately swallowed the words on the tip of her tongue, her face filled with anxiety.

Letting out a weary sigh, Zhu Lie looked at Yang Kai and said, "How can you keep a woman pinned beneath your foot like that? Let her go first, then we can talk. You are a man, after all."

Yang Kai raised his chin slightly and stared defiantly at Zhu Lie, his expression cold and indifferent.

Zhu Lie's anger immediately flared. He had never gotten along particularly well with Yang Kai to begin with, and what Zhu Qing had suffered because of Yang Kai only added fuel to the fire. He shouted in a low voice, "Let her go!"

Seeing this, Madam Hua's courage immediately returned, and she gritted her teeth and shouted, "You still dare act presumptuously before a Dragon Clan Master? Do you really think you are someone important? Take your filthy foot off me!"

As soon as she finished speaking, she suddenly let out another muffled groan. Yang Kai had clearly exerted more force through his foot, and Madam Hua's arrogant expression immediately twisted with pain as beads of sweat the size of beans rolled down her forehead.

Her face filled with horror, and her beautiful eyes trembled.

He still dared act violently in front of the Dragon Clan? Was something wrong with this fellow's head? Yet despite the pain, Madam Hua secretly felt delighted, knowing that Yang Kai could no longer explain away his actions. She was Fu Chi's woman, after all. Now that she had suffered such extraordinary humiliation, Sir Zhu Lie would certainly uphold justice for her.

She could already picture Yang Kai kneeling and begging for mercy before being brutally tortured to death, and a delighted smile immediately appeared on her face.

"Are you talking to me?" Yang Kai picked at his ear, looking completely unconcerned.

The moment he said this, Lu Sanniang and Madam Hua were both dumbfounded.

They knew Yang Kai was bold, but was he not being far too bold? How could he dare speak this way when facing a member of the Dragon Clan? Was he not simply courting death?

Lu Sanniang repeatedly signaled Yang Kai with her eyes, terrified that he did not understand how fearsome and powerful the Dragon Clan was. The Dragon Clan stood at the head of all Divine Spirits, and the one before him was an Eighth-Tier Dragon who could take his life with the flick of a finger.

She felt as though Yang Kai were walking a tightrope over a cliff, where the slightest misstep would leave his body shattered beyond recognition.

Yet to her even greater shock, Zhu Lie did not attack him immediately. Instead, he merely asked in annoyance, "What exactly do you want?"

"Nothing much. I am only seeking justice!" Yang Kai sneered.

Zhu Lie glanced at Madam Hua and said, "She is Fu Chi's woman, and Fu Chi also possesses an Eighth-Tier Dragon Bloodline. If you really kill this woman, Fu Chi definitely will not let the matter rest."

"Then let him come find me. I wonder if he has the courage."

Zhu Lie flew into a rage. “You’re nothing but a foul lump of filth!”

Yang Kai's expression turned cold. "You can eat whatever you like, but you cannot say whatever you like. Watch your mouth, or I will beat you up."

B-Beat him? Lu Sanniang's eyes went wide, her face blank with shock.

Madam Hua shrieked, "Sir, there is no need to waste any more words on him! This man has no respect for the Dragon Clan whatsoever. Please take action and kill him, Sir!"

The corner of Zhu Lie's mouth twitched as he looked at Madam Hua as though she were an idiot.

If anyone else dared act so presumptuously before him, he would have attacked long ago. How could the pride of the Dragon Clan tolerate being trampled so casually? But the person saying these things was Yang Kai. What was he supposed to do? What could he possibly do?

Zhu Lie was no match for him, and it was not as though they had never fought before. Unless he went to the Dragon Temple to seek its protection, he could not defeat Yang Kai, but where would he find the time for that now? Besides, deep down, he was still inclined to side with Yang Kai.

Although he found Yang Kai unpleasant, Madam Hua was nothing more than an ant in his eyes. He had urged Yang Kai to release her only because he did not want Yang Kai to escalate matters beyond any hope of resolution.

How could he possibly fight Yang Kai for Madam Hua's sake?

Lu Sanniang's heart also rose into her throat, and her face was filled with worry.

She could never have imagined that Yang Kai would remain so overbearing even before a member of the Dragon Clan. Yet that Dragon showed no sign of attacking, which left her feeling even more bewildered.

Since when had the Dragon Clan become so reasonable? Yang Kai had repeatedly caused Zhu Lie to lose face and had even spoken rudely to him, yet Zhu Lie could endure all of that?

Zhu Lie's behavior completely overturned Lu Sanniang's understanding of the Dragon Clan. In her experience, members of the Dragon Clan had never possessed good tempers; the slightest displeasure was enough to drive them to kill.

Suddenly, Zhu Lie turned his head toward another part of the Spirit Island and said with a gloomy expression, "Fu Chi is here."

"Sir is coming?" Madam Hua was overjoyed when she heard this. Her cultivation was insufficient, and she possessed no Dragon Bloodline, so she naturally could not sense the approaching Dragon aura. However, since Zhu Lie had said so, there could be no mistake.

Zhu Lie said, “He probably sensed my arrival and felt somewhat uneasy, so he came over specifically to take a look.”

Hearing this, Yang Kai asked curiously, "What grudge do you have with him?"

Zhu Lie’s gaze flickered, and he said faintly, “There is no great grudge between us, but you are about to have one with him.” After saying so, he glanced at Madam Hua, who had been pinned beneath Yang Kai’s foot all this time.

Madam Hua immediately regained her confidence. Although she was dissatisfied that Zhu Lie had not stood up for her at once, now that Fu Chi was coming, she had nothing left to fear. She only needed to say a few words to Fu Chi, and neither Yang Kai nor Lu Sanniang and her daughter would live to see tomorrow's sun.
