## Chapter 2733 — The Outcome Is Clear

As he watched, Ji Ying was momentarily stunned, for to his shock, he realized that the medicinal liquid Yang Kai was refining seemed no less pure than his own…

What shocked him even more was that the timing and order in which Yang Kai added the Spiritual Medicine was exactly the same as his own.

A single Spiritual Pill does not have only one pill recipe.

Every Alchemist has their own understanding of the Dao of Alchemy, their own preferences and abilities, which leads to subtle differences in the process of refining Spiritual Pills—such as the order and timing of adding Spiritual Medicine.

Some Alchemists believe it is better to begin with one Spiritual Medicine, while others prefer to start with a different one. As a result, a single Spiritual Pill can have multiple recipes, and in some cases even the required ingredients may vary.

A pill recipe is not merely a list of ingredients—it is a complete guide to alchemy. A true recipe details not only the required herbs, but also the precise steps and methods for refining them. In essence, a pill recipe is like a comprehensive instruction manual for crafting a Spiritual Pill.

This is what makes a pill recipe so precious—it is the crystallization of an Alchemist’s painstaking effort.

Now, however, Ji Ying was astonished to find that Yang Kai’s method and process for refining this Emperor Yuan Pill were strikingly similar to his own, as if they had both been carved from the same mold.

In other words, the Emperor Yuan Pill recipe Yang Kai had mastered was the same as the one he knew.

How could this be possible?

The Emperor Yuan Pill was a Spiritual Pill used by those in the Emperor Realm to restore their Emperor Yuan, and it was also the most common Imperial-Grade Spiritual Pill. According to Ji Ying’s knowledge, there were at least eight or ten different recipes for this pill. While most were similar, each had its own subtle differences.

The best Emperor Yuan Pill recipe, of course, was the one he possessed, for it had been personally improved by Great Emperor Miaodan many years ago. There was simply no better recipe in existence.

Great Emperor Miaodan had only ever passed this recipe down to a few of his disciples; no outsiders had ever learned it.

Now, seeing traces of this recipe in Yang Kai’s hands, Ji Ying was naturally astonished.

Ji Ying was not the only one surprised; the other onlookers also wore strange expressions.

They noticed that Yang Kai and Ji Ying’s alchemy processes were almost identical. The only difference was that Yang Kai moved a bit faster, while Ji Ying was a step slower. This wasn’t because Ji Ying’s alchemy skills were inferior, but simply because he had started a bit later.

Even though they understood this, it still looked off—almost as if Ji Ying was copying Yang Kai’s alchemy. Whatever ingredient Yang Kai took, Ji Ying would take the same. Whenever Yang Kai put an ingredient into the pill furnace, Ji Ying would do the same…

Ji Ying’s doubts grew stronger and stronger. At first, he thought it was just a coincidence, but by the time they reached the middle of the process, he was certain: the recipes they had mastered were truly the same.

“Where did you get this recipe?” Ji Ying couldn’t help but ask.

This was a recipe passed down by his Master—how could it have ended up in an outsider’s hands?

“Master Ji, you’re distracted,” Yang Kai said calmly. “If you keep this up, you’ll lose this match.”

Ji Ying’s whole body trembled, cold sweat breaking out all over him. He had been so preoccupied with Yang Kai’s recipe that he’d lost focus, and as a result, hadn’t given his all during the refining of the medicinal liquid. Now, looking at it, his own liquid seemed slightly inferior to Yang Kai’s.

Refining the medicinal liquid is the first and most crucial step in alchemy. If this step isn’t done well, it will inevitably affect the quality of the Spiritual Pill.

Alchemy is like playing chess—one wrong move leads to another. Any detail done poorly is a fatal flaw.

He could not lose! This was a contest concerning Medicine Pill Valley and his Master’s reputation—he absolutely could not lose.

With a sudden change in expression, Ji Ying seemed like a different person. An indescribable aura radiated from him as he immersed himself fully in alchemy, his eyes seeing nothing else.

Yang Kai’s expression was equally serious. Although he was an Imperial-Grade Pill Master, this was his first time competing in alchemy against another Imperial-Grade Pill Master—and this person was even a direct disciple of Great Emperor Miaodan.

If he didn’t give it his all, there was no way he could win.

What he truly relied on was not the Divine Sense Fire that Ji Ying saw—this was just a minor advantage. His real trump card was the Great Dao Pill Tone!

Back then, on Shuiyue Star, the main star of the Hengluo Commerce Guild, Yang Kai once triggered the Great Dao Pill Tone during a pill refinement. In that moment, he was granted the mysteries of alchemy by the heavens themselves. From then on, his progress in the Dao of Alchemy soared like a dragon rising to the clouds.

If not for that stroke of fortune, how could Yang Kai have advanced so quickly in alchemy? Over the years, he had hardly spent any time on alchemy at all. Normally, at this point, it would have been lucky for him to become a Dao Origin-Grade Alchemist, let alone Imperial-Grade.

Yet the foundation bestowed by the Great Dao Pill Tone allowed him to easily reach the level of an Imperial-Grade Pill Master.

Even so, he still couldn’t compare in experience to a veteran Imperial-Grade Pill Master like Ji Ying. All he could do was give it his all.

His mind suddenly became empty and ethereal, as if he had merged with the world itself. A wondrous feeling rose in his heart.

Yang Kai’s movements changed. Suddenly, he began flailing his arms and legs as if he’d gone mad.

“Pfft…” Li Jiao and Mi Qi nearly burst out laughing at this sight, but under the watchful eyes of the three Yao Kings, they could only hold it in.

Meanwhile, Ji Ying’s movements were as smooth as flowing water, meticulous and precise. Just watching him was a pleasure. Compared to Yang Kai’s frenzied display, the difference was like night and day—the outcome was clear.

And he still thinks he can beat Master Ji? He really doesn’t know his own limits.

Hua Qingsi also noticed this, and a trace of anxiety appeared on her face. She had no idea what Yang Kai was doing. He had been performing just fine before—why did he suddenly seem like a different person?

“Hmm?”

Among everyone present, only one person suddenly stared fixedly at Yang Kai, an expression of utter shock on his face, his gaze never wavering.

This person was Ji Ying’s eldest disciple.

This eldest disciple had never thought much of Yang Kai—not because of Li Xuan’s slander, but because, thanks to Yang Kai, Li Xuan had been expelled from the sect by their Master. Although he disdained Li Xuan’s past actions, they had been fellow disciples for many years, and there was still some attachment.

He had originally planned to wait until his Master’s anger subsided before persuading him to take Li Xuan back. Junior Brother was still young, after all—who doesn’t make mistakes? As long as he knew to repent, it would be fine. But Yang Kai had given him no chance at all.

He looked down on Yang Kai in this contest with Ji Ying, never believing Yang Kai could win, and had watched him with a hostile gaze, wanting to see what expression he would make when his Master defeated him.

Who could have guessed that, with a single glance, he would see something extraordinary?

To others, Yang Kai’s spasms and wild hand movements looked utterly graceless, but to him, it felt completely different. He seemed to glimpse something, but whenever he reached for it, it slipped away. This made him so anxious that sweat beaded on his forehead as he stared at Yang Kai, not daring to miss a single detail.

Inside the great hall, the scent of medicine grew even stronger. All the Spiritual Medicine had been added to the pill furnaces by the two, refined into pure medicinal liquid. The lids were sealed, and it was now time to nurture the pills.

Power surged in waves. Inside the pill furnaces, invisible to the crowd, spirit arrays appeared and vanished, and the various medicinal liquids mixed, undergoing wondrous transformations.

“Thud!” Ji Ying’s eldest disciple suddenly dropped to one knee.

This sudden movement startled everyone except Yang Kai and Ji Ying.

Hua Qingsi looked over and saw that the eldest disciple was drenched in sweat, as if he had just survived a life-and-death struggle. His face was pale, his eyes bloodshot, yet he still stared fixedly at Yang Kai.

“What’s wrong?” Hua Qingsi hurried over, trying to help him up.

This was Ji Ying’s eldest disciple—if anything happened to him here, it would be hard to explain to Ji Ying.

“Don’t touch me!” the eldest disciple barked, veins bulging on his forehead, looking terrifying.

Hua Qingsi’s outstretched hand froze in place, and she grew angry, not knowing what madness had seized him.

“Oh?” Yang Kai, in the midst of his work, glanced at the eldest disciple and grinned. “You actually noticed something? Watch carefully—what you can comprehend depends on your own fortune.”

Nearby, Mi Qi and Li Jiao’s eyes widened at these words, sneering inwardly. This guy really dares to boast. That’s Ji Ying’s eldest disciple—if he wanted to learn anything, he wouldn’t come to you. Who do you think you are? Besides, with your meager skills, you’re probably not even as good as the eldest disciple, yet you dare to brag like this. Aren’t you afraid of biting your own tongue?

No sooner had this thought crossed their minds than they saw the disciple clench his teeth, eyes bulging even wider, and say in a ringing voice, “Many thanks, Master Yang!”

“What?” Li Jiao and Mi Qi were dumbfounded. They exchanged glances, both seeing the shock in each other’s eyes.

What was going on with this eldest disciple, actually thanking Yang Kai and even calling him Master Yang…

That couldn’t be! His Master was right there—wasn’t he afraid of making him angry?

What on earth was happening!

Although Ji Ying was fully focused, he was still startled by his disciple’s outburst. Hearing him address Yang Kai so respectfully, Ji Ying couldn’t help but frown. The little brat was siding with an outsider at a time like this—after all these years of raising him.

Curious, he glanced over at Yang Kai.

With a single glance, Ji Ying’s hands paused, his eyes slowly widening, even forgetting about the Spiritual Pills being nurtured in the furnace.

His attainments in alchemy far surpassed his disciple’s. If even his disciple could see something, how could he, as the Master, not?

What Ji Ying saw was not Yang Kai’s strange, spasmodic movements, nor his wildly flailing hands. Instead, before his eyes unfolded a great path—a road leading to the pinnacle of alchemy itself! Yang Kai’s actions flowed with effortless grace, perfectly attuned to the Dao of Alchemy, as if guided by some profound truth he could not fully grasp.

He had only ever seen this before in his own Master, Great Emperor Miaodan. He never imagined that today, he would see the same traces in another person.
