## Chapter 2864 — Massacre

Shaya shuddered inexplicably, silently resolving that she must never be alone with this young woman again. The sense of danger was far too intense—even when facing Shaman Niu himself, Shaya had never felt such a thing.

After getting the report, Yang Kai immediately summoned all the shamans and the captains of each squad.

Shaman Niu's tribe originally had twelve squads: ten regular squads, one guard squad, and one Sky Cavalry squad. Now, an additional cavalry squad had been formed, with Ah Hu serving as its captain.

So at this moment, Shaman Niu's tribe had a total of thirteen squads.

After briefly explaining his arrangements, all the shamans and the thirteen captains became visibly excited, rubbing their fists in anticipation.

Several days of recuperation had left Shaman Niu's tribe in peak condition, and the victory in the previous great battle had only boosted their confidence. Now, they were eager for another fight to maintain this state.

Moreover, this was the best way for the Barbarian Tribe to grow.

After the great battle a few days ago, many Barbarians clearly felt themselves becoming much stronger, especially the captains and vice-captains of each squad. They were already the elite among them, and after the baptism of blood and wounds, their latent potential had been awakened. The shackles of their past body tempering had been broken, and even more Barbarian warriors had achieved tremendous growth.

So now, Shaman Niu's tribe was extremely eager for war.

The peace of these past days had made them restless. Now that Yang Kai had suddenly notified them to prepare for battle, everyone was naturally delighted.

After the arrangements, the shamans and captains all clarified their tasks and positions, then dispersed.

At noon the next day, a group of uninvited guests arrived outside the stone cave.

This group numbered about a thousand, with a mixed composition, just like the squad Shaya had previously commanded.

Reinforcements from the nearby Demon stronghold had arrived.

Shaya's summons had indeed been effective. After all, she was a Demon King, while the strongest demon at the nearest stronghold was only a Demon Commander. So, upon receiving Shaya's order, he had rushed over with his men, hoping to perform well before the Demon King and perhaps earn her special favor.

For this, the Demon Commander had brought all his forces, a mighty group of over a thousand. But upon arrival, he immediately sensed something was off.

The Demon King's orders mentioned an attack by outsiders. Though she was occupied with cultivation and unable to spare herself, to this Demon Commander, there were no signs of battle here. The surroundings were eerily quiet—far too quiet to be normal.

This instinctively made him uneasy.

He did not advance rashly, but stopped about ten li from the stone cave. He summoned his Shadow Demons and Sand Demons, ordering them to scout for information.

But just then, a black shadow suddenly flew over from ahead.

Sensing the aura within that black shadow, the Demon Commander immediately became solemn. He dismounted from his steed and stood respectfully in place, waiting for the shadow to arrive.

Moments later, Shaya appeared before the Demon Commander, exuding boundless charm.

“Greetings, my lord!” The Demon Commander immediately saluted, his gaze lingering on Shaya’s alluring figure. Although he was well aware of the terror of a Succubus, he still could not control his eyes.

The Demon King was truly beautiful! If only he could spend a night with her... He could not help but swallow, his breathing growing heavy.

“Did you bring all your people?” Shaya glanced down and asked.

“All present!” the Demon Commander replied respectfully, a hint of curiosity on his face. “My lord, didn’t you send word that there was an attack by outsiders? Has the matter already been resolved?”

It made sense—since the Demon King had appeared, she must have handled it personally. With a Demon King taking action, how could those outsiders possibly survive? The Demon Commander felt regret, thinking he had missed a chance to impress the Demon King.

“Yes, it’s been resolved.” Shaya smiled slightly, her smile blooming like a flower bud. The whole world seemed to lose its color. The Demon Commander stared at her dumbly, seeing nothing else. Shaya continued, “But since you are so loyal and came at my call, I must reward you.”

The Demon Commander exclaimed excitedly, “I dare not accept such an honor!”

Though he said this, his expression was extremely eager, clearly anticipating what kind of reward Shaya would give him.

The answer came quickly. Shaya simply waved her hand lightly, and the Demon Commander suddenly felt his vision turn upside down. Blinking in confusion, he saw a familiar figure standing before him, blood gushing from its cleanly severed neck.

How could this be... Was that his own body?

His head had been cut off? As this final thought flashed through his mind, the Demon Commander’s consciousness quickly faded into nothingness.

The moment Shaya struck, chaos erupted among the demons under the Demon Commander’s command. No one knew why the Demon King had suddenly killed their own leader without a word, but a Demon King was still a Demon King. Even in panic and fear, the demons remained in place, staring at Shaya, hoping for an explanation.

A wave of enchanting light rippled out—this was Shaya’s answer.

The vast halo instantly enveloped all thousand demons. Below the rank of Demon General, every demon became dazed. Even the Demon Generals struggled to resist, but it was clearly futile; they would soon succumb.

The cry of an eagle rang out as the Sky Cavalry rushed in from afar. The ground trembled as the squads of Shaman Niu’s tribe surrounded from all directions. Leading the charge was Ah Hu’s cavalry. The Demonic Beasts galloped and leapt, carrying their new masters into battle for the first time, putting in extra effort. Dozens of Demonic Beasts gathered together, their momentum soaring.

The ten regular squads followed close behind, giving it their all, unwilling to be outdone.

The distance of a dozen or twenty li was closed in just a few dozen breaths.

At this moment, Shaya’s face turned slightly pale, showing signs of exhaustion.

Although she was a Demon King and her enchanting light was her innate divine ability, enveloping over a thousand people at once was a heavy burden. She held on until Shaman Niu’s army arrived, then immediately felt weak.

The enchanting light vanished, and the demons regained their senses—only to be met by the wild charge and joint assault of Shaman Niu’s warriors.

Screams rang out one after another as demon after demon fell.

Rays of shamanic light burst forth, making even the sun lose its brilliance. Blood pooled on the ground, flowing in streams.

The demons showed expressions of grief and indignation. Even in death, they could not understand why their own Demon King was helping outsiders slaughter them. Not even the Sand Demons, who were best at escaping, could flee under the shamanic arts.

They were surrounded on all sides by outsiders, with Sky Cavalry circling above. Arrows rained down, reaping the lives of their kin.

There was no room to escape; only slaughter awaited them.

Realizing this, the demons fought back desperately, but their overwhelming numerical disadvantage and lack of preparation made their resistance seem almost laughable.

Ah Hu led his cavalry of dozens of Demonic Beasts through the demons, leaving chaos in their wake. The cavalry was like an invincible sword, carving bloody paths across the battlefield.

Everyone’s weapon was stained with blood, and everyone was filled with murderous intent. The Barbarian warriors, empowered by shamanic arts, were like invincible war gods. Their overwhelming momentum moved even heaven and earth—let alone these demons.

This was a one-sided massacre.

The first battle could still be considered fairly evenly matched, with both sides trading blows. Had Shaya not been captured by Yang Kai and thrown into the Small Xuan Realm at the final moment, the Barbarian Tribe would have suffered far greater losses. But in this ambush, the Barbarian Tribe completely dominated the battlefield.

No matter how fierce the resistance, it was meaningless under the crushing force of Shaman Niu’s tribe.

After half a day, the battle ended. Shaman Niu’s tribe had lost only a dozen or so, but had nearly annihilated the demons.

The only survivors were those who had been demonized!

This time, Shaman Niu’s tribe did not slaughter those people, because under Shaya’s control, the demonized tribesmen had not participated in the battle.

The demonized tribesmen possessed no consciousness of their own, merely obeying the commands of higher-ranked demons by instinct. In other words, any demon had absolute power over their life and death, as well as the right to command them.

Among those present, Shaya was the strongest demon, so she naturally gained control over these demonized people.

As soon as the battle began, she had them leave the battlefield.

Now, they were all alive and well—not a single one was injured.

There were quite a few of them, more than three hundred.

This was one of the main reasons for the demons’ crushing defeat. They had only a thousand to begin with, and losing over three hundred at once meant they were down by nearly a third. Facing three thousand warriors of Shaman Niu’s tribe, it was no wonder they were doomed.

As usual after the battle, the field was cleared. The demons had many weapons, which were naturally collected by Shaman Niu’s tribe.

The shamans immediately rushed to the demonized tribesmen to begin treatment.

With over three hundred tribesmen, it was unclear how many could be saved. The twins had been working on safer treatment methods, but time was short and results were limited. This batch of demonized people would serve as their test subjects.

With Shaya’s deterrence, these demonized people offered no resistance, giving the twins more room for research.

Throughout the entire battle, Yang Kai never appeared, and even Die did not take action. Yet Shaman Niu’s tribe still won with almost negligible losses. Although this was expected, everyone was still excited.

They looked forward to more wars, hoping to kill more demons and one day drive all the demons from this land.
