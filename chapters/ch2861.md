## Chapter 2861 — My World

In this peaceful and tranquil world, Shaya furrowed her brows as she sped forward. The fatal, piercing wound on her chest had long since vanished without a trace, leaving only a few bloodstains on her clothes.

The vitality of the demon race had always been formidable, especially for experts like Shaya. The saying that “so long as the Demon Heart remains, a demon cannot die” was no joke.

She had been trapped here for several days.

There was no sun or moon here, nor were there any stars, yet the place was filled with light. The environment was extremely pleasant, and the Spiritual Qi of heaven and earth was unexpectedly rich.

But she felt no joy at all. A few days ago, she had been ambushed from behind by that fellow called Shaman Niu, and while gravely wounded, she had inexplicably arrived in this world of birdsong and fragrant flowers, with beautiful scenery everywhere.

Shaya had thought she was dreaming.

But the piercing pain in her chest made her realize this was reality.

What was this place? Why had she come here? She couldn't figure it out. After carefully investigating her surroundings and still finding no clues, Shaya could only keep moving forward, searching for a possible way out.

Yet, even with her strength as a Demon King, after several days of effort, she still hadn't discovered a thing.

This world seemed like an endless cage, and she was the beast imprisoned within. No matter how hard she tried, she could not escape its confines.

Shaya's mood was extremely heavy. Thinking of that fellow who had stabbed her from behind, she was so angry she nearly ground her silver teeth to dust.

She simply couldn't understand—after swallowing her demon heart's blood, how could Shaman Niu still betray her? Why hadn't she sensed any hint of betrayal from him?

By the demon race's standards, Shaman Niu's aura was only that of a Demon Commander, a whole stage below her as a Demon King. How could he possibly resist her demon heart's blood?

There were far too many things she couldn't figure out! Ever since she met this Shaman Niu a few days ago, she had suffered loss after loss, to the point that now she didn't even know where she was.

But no matter what, she had to spread the word. That brat was far too strange—even she had suffered at his hands. If the other Demon Kings encountered him, they'd better be careful.

Just as she was thinking this, Shaya suddenly stopped, her delicate brows knitting together.

In that instant, she suddenly felt as if she was being watched. This was something that hadn't happened in all these days—she seemed to be the only person in this world, with no sign of any other living soul.

With suspicion, Shaya tilted her head to look. The next moment, hatred and anger blazed in her beautiful eyes as she gritted her teeth and growled at the figure quietly floating in midair: "Shaman Niu!"

Yang Kai gave her a slight smile. "Good morning, Demon King."

"Good morning, my ass!" Even with Shaya's composure, she couldn't help but curse. In this godforsaken place devoid of sun, moon, or stars, how could she possibly tell if it was morning or evening? Of course, that wasn’t the point—the real issue was that at the sight of the enemy, her eyes burned with hatred.

Although her wound had already healed, the moment she saw Shaman Niu, Shaya could still clearly feel the pain surging from her Demon Heart. It was as if the wound that had once been pierced was about to split open again, sending a wrenching agony through her.

Yang Kai didn't mind her rudeness, only smiling faintly. "Looks like I guessed right. Even if I really destroyed your demon heart, you still wouldn't die. Are all demons this hard to kill?"

Shaya's expression was cold. Instead of answering, she asked, "What is this place? Why did you bring me here?"

"This place..." Yang Kai lifted his eyes, his gaze piercing the void as if he could see the end of the world. Spreading his arms, he declared, "This is my world. Here, I am the master!"

Shaya sneered, clearly thinking Yang Kai was spouting nonsense. A mere Demon Commander dared to claim dominion over a world? Even the Demon Saints couldn't do that.

"As for why I brought you here," Yang Kai smiled gently, "of course, it's to discipline you."

"Discipline me?" Shaya was stunned, then burst into a soft laugh. "Interesting. This is the first time anyone has said that to me."

She had always been the one to say such things to others. Hearing it for the first time herself, she found it oddly novel.

"You think I'm joking?" Yang Kai looked at her seriously.

Shaya curled her lips. "I just think you have too much time on your hands."

Yang Kai slowly shook his head. "You should be grateful that, in my eyes, you still have some value. Otherwise, you'd already be dead."

"Oh?" Shaya struck a seductive pose, her allure blossoming as she gazed at Yang Kai with misty eyes. "Then what kind of value do I have in your eyes?"

"Definitely not the kind you're thinking of," Yang Kai smirked. "I've told you before, I have no interest in sluts—especially rotten meat that even wild dogs wouldn't sniff at."

The charm on Shaya's face vanished instantly, replaced by bone-chilling cold.

Yang Kai’s insulting words had clearly touched her bottom line, leaving her unable to tolerate it any longer.

Shaya opened her mouth and let out a shrill cry. Pure Divine Soul power rippled out, forming a massive shockwave that surged toward Yang Kai's mind. At the same time, a soft whip appeared in her hand, which she lashed down at Yang Kai.

The movement was gentle, yet it contained the power to overturn rivers and seas.

When a Demon King struck, it was truly extraordinary—even the Spiritual Qi of this small world rippled in response.

Yang Kai stood motionless, a mocking smile on his lips. He raised his hand, thumb and middle finger pressed together, and flicked lightly, as if flicking away a mosquito.

Shaya, however, was struck as if grievously wounded. Her abdomen caved in, and she was sent flying by a tremendous force, coughing up blood in midair.

It took all her effort to steady herself. Shaya was no longer calm or composed—her eyes were filled with panic as she stared at Yang Kai, her beautiful eyes trembling violently.

How could this be? The other party hadn't shown any sign of using power, yet with a casual gesture, he had sent her flying. The force was just right—enough to cause pain, but not to damage her foundation.

Was he really this powerful?

Absolutely impossible.

Although she had only fought Shaman Niu once, Shaya knew his abilities. He was strong, but there was a limit to that strength.

What had just happened was beyond her comprehension. Not even a Demon Saint could display such power—how could it appear in someone equivalent to a Demon Commander?

Experiencing such a blow firsthand was completely outside Shaya's realm of understanding.

She steadied herself and scrutinized Yang Kai, as if trying to discern some clue.

Yang Kai flicked his middle finger again. Shaya's expression shifted dramatically as she instinctively unleashed a torrent of Demonic Qi, transforming herself into a shadowy silhouette to obscure her form.

But in the next instant, her head felt as if it had been struck by a meteor, snapping back violently. Her whole body spun through the air, leaving her dizzy and unable to steady herself under the massive impact.

Who knew how far she flew before the force finally lessened. Shaya barely managed to steady herself and stand again, but warm, wet liquid flowed from her nose and mouth. She wiped it with her hand—it was all blood.

Her forehead was completely numb, as if her skull had shattered.

Looking up, she saw no sign of that outsider.

Before she could breathe a sigh of relief, a chilling voice sounded behind her: "I told you, in this world, I am the master! Even if your Great Demon God were to come, he would have to kneel here and submit to me!”

Shaya pursed her lips, wanting to retort, but didn't know what to say.

The two attacks had left her terrified. She had thought that, with her strength, Shaman Niu would be no match for her. But now, it was clear—she was the one who couldn't compare. Against his methods, she had no time to resist, not even to react.

She couldn't fight him. It was impossible! Shaya made her judgment.

That left only escape.

With that thought, Shaya didn't hesitate. Without a word, she pushed her body to the limit and sped off in one direction, vanishing in the blink of an eye.

Her Divine Sense surged. Only when she could no longer sense Shaman Niu's aura did Shaya finally breathe a sigh of relief, as if she had escaped death.

As a Demon King, there were few things or people in this world that could make her feel fear. Even when facing a Demon Saint, she would only show respect and caution.

But Shaman Niu truly made her feel fear—a fear of the unknown.

The Demon Saints were strong, but Shaya knew exactly where their strength lay and how strong they could be. But Shaman Niu's strength had no limits.

His wild words seemed to echo in her ears again. Shaya couldn't help but wonder—had he really become the master of this world?

How could that be? Who could possibly rule a world?

Fleeing, Shaya's eyes suddenly widened in shock as she stared ahead. There, in the void, stood that terrifying figure, still wearing that mysterious, unfathomable smile. Seeing her approach, he made no move to stop her, simply standing with his hands behind his back, as if watching a show.

This was madness!

She hadn't sensed any fluctuation of power—how had he appeared before her?

In a panic, Shaya hurriedly stopped and turned to flee in the opposite direction.

The same scene played out again a stick of incense later, plunging Shaya into despair.

Shaman Niu appeared before her once more, standing in the same posture as if he had never moved. Looking around, the scenery was familiar…

There were even some bloodstains on the ground nearby that hadn't dried yet.

That was the blood she had just coughed up.

Why had she inexplicably returned? She had clearly fled in a straight line—how could she have come back?

The smile on that tribesman’s face was like a devil’s summons, sending chills down Shaya’s spine despite her being a Demon King. Her blood turned cold, her face went pale, and she turned to flee once more, not even daring to face him again.
