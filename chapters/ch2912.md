## Chapter 2912 — Stone Forest

Sixth-Tier yao beasts were not worth the attention of a group of Emperor Realm cultivators. With a few casual waves, they slaughtered a large number, each yao beast's body bursting apart and turning into streams of blood that flowed into the swamp. Rather than frightening off the remaining beasts, this only further provoked their ferocity, and more and more yao beasts leapt up from the ground.

The shrill cries never ceased for a moment. At a certain instant, these chaotic screeches suddenly became unified, and the cries of thousands converged into a single wave of sound, forming a visible shockwave that swept toward the seven people.

Yang Tai, who was leading at the front, sensed the danger and roared urgently, "Hurry! Move!"

Hua Yulu and Wu Kuangyi's expressions also grew much more grave.

Just as the others were still confused, a bizarre scene unfolded.

After the shrill cries resonated together, the auras of those yao beasts from beneath the swamp began to intertwine closely, their Sixth-Tier strength rapidly climbing toward Seventh-Tier, Eighth-Tier, and even higher levels.

Yang Kai casually unleashed an attack, and in the midst of the chaos, glanced downward, immediately witnessing a scene that made him click his tongue in amazement.

He saw that the overwhelming mass of yao beasts was actually merging together, as if they were parts dismantled from a single colossal creature, now reassembling due to some opportunity.

A monster over a dozen zhang long suddenly appeared before Yang Kai's eyes. This creature had two horns on its head, its entire body covered in scales, and eight claws. It resembled a Jiao yet was not one, its appearance extremely bizarre. At this moment, it no longer possessed just Sixth-Tier strength, but exuded the aura of a Tenth-Tier yao beast.

More and more colossal monsters joined the pursuit from below.

Yang Kai sensed the auras of several Twelfth-Tier yao beasts, with Eleventh-Tier and Tenth-Tier ones everywhere.

Those Sixth-Tier yao beasts that the Emperor Realm cultivators had dismissed actually possessed the strange ability to merge and grow stronger, and the speed at which they fused and transformed was simply beyond imagination.

Capable of surviving in such an environment, these yao beasts truly could not be underestimated.

Amid a rumbling din, these colossal creatures raced across the ground, their heads raised high as they snapped at the people in the sky, foul and pungent odors wafting from their gaping jaws. When they missed, they spat out clusters of multicolored orbs from their mouths.

Yang Kai saw clearly that those orbs were the toxic miasma they had previously swallowed. Even though Yang Kai could face Twelfth-Tier yao beasts without fear, he could not ignore the power of this potent poison.

The other six also turned pale, each summoning their Secret Treasures to protect themselves.

Compared to the others, Yang Kai had no defensive Secret Treasure to use. His only Feihong Black Dragon Armor, though at the level of an Imperial Treasure, was mainly used to cover his body after Dragon Transformation. He had never thought to use it for defense. Helpless, he could only circulate his Emperor Yuan to form a protective layer around himself.

The poisonous orbs flew over, most shattered by the group's combined efforts, while a few struck their Secret Treasures and dissipated, but the strength of the poison still made their scalps tingle.

Several Imperial Treasures flickered after blocking the poison, clearly their spirituality had been damaged. The Emperor Yuan Yang Kai maintained outside his body was also badly corroded, forcing him to continuously channel power to maintain his defense, leaving him rather disheveled.

The group fled, unwilling to tangle with these monsters any longer.

It seemed as though an eternity had passed, yet in another sense, it was merely an instant. The moment the group rushed onto a patch of dry land, the poisonous miasma that had shrouded the surroundings dissipated completely. The yao beasts that had been relentlessly pursuing them suddenly came to an abrupt halt, as if something ahead filled them with dread. They paced back and forth along the edge of the poison, their enormous eyes fixed warily upon the group.

"This is far enough," Yang Tai said with a cheerful smile, clearly relieved. Pointing at the yao beasts, he added, "Those things won't come any closer. Who knows why."

The yao beasts gathered at the edge of the poison only watched warily for a while before suddenly scattering. They reverted to individual monsters about two chi long, with only Sixth-Tier auras, then retreated along their original path, vanishing in the blink of an eye.

Everyone began to examine their surroundings.

This was a patch of dry land. For such a place to appear within the Southern Marsh was extremely unusual, as the Southern Marsh was full of puddles and swamps. This patch of land was like a lone island in a vast sea.

The island was not large, only a few dozen li across, and was surrounded by the kind of poison that even Emperor Realm cultivators would avoid. If not for the path opened by the yao beasts, they likely would never have reached this place.

Yang Tai's expression grew solemn as he said in a low voice, "The ancient cave residence is here."

Though his expression was serious, his eyes could not hide a trace of fervor.

Wu Kuangyi looked somewhat excited as well. "Last time, Brother Yang, Palace Master Hua, and I gained some benefits inside, but were blocked by a restrictive formation. This time, with more people and Family Head Gong's generous assistance, breaking that formation shouldn't be a problem."

Gong Yue smiled modestly. "This old man will do his best."

"Many thanks to Family Head Gong in advance," Wu Kuangyi cupped his fists, then added, "I must remind everyone, once inside the ancient cave residence, don't touch things at random or wander off. We'll follow Family Head Gong. There are still many lingering restrictions inside, and though they're ancient, they're still extremely powerful. If triggered carelessly, they could cause unnecessary trouble."

As he spoke, he glanced at Yang Kai, seemingly directing the warning at him.

Yang Kai's expression was distant, his mind wandering far away, which made Wu Kuangyi's face darken considerably.

"Alright, alright, we're not children. We all know what to do. Let's set out," Yang Tai said with a smile, leading the group toward the ancient cave residence.

The order of the group did not change; Yang Kai and Hua Yulu still brought up the rear.

"Senior Brother Yang, if there really are treasures, we’ll snatch what we can, but if not, we won’t force it. At least we’ll broaden our horizons," Hua Yulu quietly transmitted.

"Mm," Yang Kai replied absentmindedly, somewhat out of sorts.

This made Hua Yulu worry. No one knew what dangers might lie beyond the restrictions. If something truly perilous appeared, Yang Kai’s current state would likely spell disaster. She wanted to remind him, but could not find the words, so she resolved to be extra cautious herself.

Yang Kai was indeed distracted. Ever since stepping onto this island, he had felt a strange sensation, as if something on the island was trying to connect with him. Yet, whenever he tried to investigate, he found nothing, unable even to determine the direction of the feeling.

This left him completely baffled. When Wu Kuangyi and Hua Yulu spoke to him, he was still trying to figure it out, so he seemed absent-minded.

The island was not large. After a short while, the group arrived at a stone forest, where a dozen or so stone pillars of varying heights stood. The surfaces of the pillars were mottled, bearing the marks of ages past. They seemed randomly arranged, but a closer look revealed a deeper pattern.

Upon closer observation, the stone pillars were slowly shifting, and their speed gradually increased until they became a dazzling blur.

Anyone could see that there was something wrong with this stone forest.

Perhaps in ancient times, this was a highly concealed entrance. Even if an Emperor Realm expert passed by, they would never have noticed anything. But the outermost restriction had long since fallen into disrepair, its effect greatly weakened, exposing many traces.

"Follow me!" Yang Tai called, taking the lead and heading straight for the center of the stone forest without hesitation.

Under everyone's gaze, he seemed to pass through an invisible wall. After walking through the center, he mysteriously vanished.

"Interesting, interesting!" Gong Yue stroked his long beard, watching with great interest, his eyes bright. Clearly, he had discovered something, but did not explain, instead stepping forward himself.

A moment later, Gong Yue also disappeared.

Next were Fang Zhuo and Wu Kuangyi.

When it was Hua Yulu's turn, she looked back and reminded, "Senior Brother Yang, keep up!"

Yang Kai smiled at her. "I'll stick close to you."

Only then did Hua Yulu proceed with confidence.

Finally, it was Yang Kai's turn. As he crossed the invisible boundary, he distinctly felt a sensation of traversing the void. The restriction at the center of the stone forest must have had the effect of a teleportation array.

The others who had entered were all there, and the place seemed to be a stone chamber only a dozen zhang across. The air was dry, with a faint unpleasant odor, but it was not unbearable.

Some dim light came from strange stones embedded in the cave walls, used specifically for illumination.

However, after so many years, most of these stones had lost their effect. The few still working flickered like candles in the wind, as if they might go out at any moment.

There was nothing else in the cave, just emptiness. No one knew what this stone cave had been used for in ancient times.

"This isn't the place we entered last time." Yang Tai looked around, scratching his head with an embarrassed expression.

Wu Kuangyi and Hua Yulu both nodded. The last time the three of them entered through the stone forest, they had not arrived in this cave.

Gong Yue smiled. "The stone forest controls the entrance restriction. Since we don't know how to control it, we entered a random place by luck. Still, we're inside the cave residence, so let's look around. We might even find something you missed last time."

Yang Tai's eyes lit up at these words, and he nodded. "Brother Gong is right. Let's go."

As he spoke, he took the lead, and the others followed closely.

After leaving the stone chamber, a deep and secluded passage stretched ahead. Just like inside the chamber, the surrounding walls were embedded with strange stones that provided faint illumination—though the light was dim, it was better than nothing.
