## Chapter 2704 — Exhausted

Yet a stubborn obsession still burned in his heart. He immediately charged at Yang Kai again, roaring, “Give it back!”

Yang Kai stared at him coldly, taking a deep breath to steady the churning qi and blood in his chest. Only when his opponent drew near did he suddenly throw a punch.

Tan Junhao was sent flying once more, half his body blasted apart.

But this time, he was unable to get up again.

The devastating strike from the Soul-Severing Blade had inflicted unimaginable damage. Amidst the chaos in his Divine Sense, he had unconsciously and frantically pushed the Golden Armored Heavenly Tome to its limits, unleashing its full power.

The Golden Armored Heavenly Tome was a mighty treasure, but every activation demanded a tremendous amount of strength—the more power it released, the greater the cost.

Tan Junhao had nearly summoned every divine ability within the Golden Armored Heavenly Tome. The price was obvious. Even as a master at the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm, his Emperor Yuan was nearly depleted.

With his Divine Sense wounded and Emperor Yuan exhausted, his strength was all but gone. On top of that, he bore the Years Like a Shuttle seal from Yang Kai. At this point, Tan Junhao was nothing more than a trapped beast making a last stand. Now, even an ordinary cultivator at the First Stage of the Emperor Realm could easily subdue him, let alone Yang Kai.

“Give it back!” Even with half his body shattered and his organs exposed, Tan Junhao still called out obsessively from where he lay on the ground, though his voice was barely a whisper.

“Give it back, my ass.” Yang Kai muttered softly, his own body on the verge of collapse.

Today’s battle had been perilous beyond compare, and the wounds he’d suffered were severe—especially the massive gash across his chest, deep enough to expose bone, with flesh rolled back in a gruesome sight. There were also two bloody holes in his abdomen.

The wound on his chest had been left by that enormous bird, whose strength rivaled the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm. The creatures summoned from the Golden Armored Heavenly Tome weren’t much for defense, but their attacks were terrifyingly powerful—even Yang Kai’s Dragon Transformation body couldn’t withstand them.

As for the bloody holes in his abdomen, those had been pierced clean through by two golden beams from the Golden Armored Heavenly Tome, injuring his internal organs…

Countless other wounds, large and small, covered his body. Several ribs were broken. “Miserable” hardly began to describe his condition.

Yet Yang Kai’s expression was one of exhilaration. This was the first time he had defeated a peak Third Stage Emperor Realm master in a head-on clash. With this experience, he would no longer be flustered when facing others of the same level in the future.

No martial artist can grow without life-and-death battles. The more combat experience one has, the farther one can walk on the Martial Dao.

“Give…” Another faint cry came from the other side, but only a single word escaped before silence fell. The last trace of life in Tan Junhao’s body vanished in that instant.

“Finally dead!” Yang Kai felt as if his whole body was about to fall apart. His wounds and depleted strength left him utterly exhausted. Now that his mortal enemy was dead, the tension in his nerves finally eased. An overwhelming fatigue swept over him—he wanted nothing more than to sleep for days.

Suddenly, a cold gleam flashed—a murderous intent struck from behind.

“Die!”

Wu Ming, eyes blazing, slashed down with his blade. His full power as a Second Stage Emperor Realm cultivator poured forth without reservation.

Earlier, he had been knocked out by Yang Kai and thus avoided the most dangerous moments. He only awoke when a stray attack struck him—an accident caused by Tan Junhao’s frenzied activation of the Golden Armored Heavenly Tome.

He was clever enough not to get up immediately after regaining consciousness, instead secretly observing the battle.

What he saw shocked him. His master had gone mad, while Yang Kai was still lively. He couldn’t help but feel anxious and uneasy, unsure if his crazed master could still defeat Yang Kai. If not, his own fate would be grim.

Sure enough, his master was no match. In no time, he was spent and shattered by Yang Kai’s punch, lingering for a moment before dying.

His master, a Third Stage Emperor Realm expert, had actually been slain by Yang Kai!

Wu Ming’s heart plummeted into an abyss. It felt as if all light had vanished from the world.

But just then, he noticed Yang Kai’s body sway. He immediately realized that, though victorious, Yang Kai was gravely wounded and had paid a steep price.

Now was the perfect moment for a sneak attack!

If he could kill Yang Kai, not only could he avenge his master, but he would also claim countless treasures. He had long coveted his master’s Golden Armored Heavenly Tome. With that treasure, he could even challenge Third Stage Emperor Realm cultivators and rise to prominence in the Star God Palace. Becoming an Elder would no longer be a dream.

Otherwise, once Yang Kai recovered, he would be the first to die.

He made his move without hesitation, striking at Yang Kai’s weakest moment, determined to finish it in one blow!

Yang Kai seemed unable to react, taking a blade to the shoulder that cut five inches deep.

Wu Ming was overjoyed and was about to wrench Yang Kai’s shoulder off when Yang Kai suddenly turned, grinned, and said, “Couldn’t hold back any longer, could you?”

Wu Ming was startled. “You knew all along…?”

Yang Kai spat out a mouthful of blood and sneered, “Take a guess!”

Wu Ming roared in fury, “You’re just a spent arrow, yet you still play tricks? This Lord will cut you down!”

With that, he fiercely channeled his Emperor Yuan. His long blade rang out, carrying the power to shatter sun and moon, aiming to cleave Yang Kai in two.

“If you’d stayed put, maybe I wouldn’t have bothered with you. But since you jumped out, you can die now!” Yang Kai shouted, grabbing the back of the blade with his bare hand. With a surge of physical strength, the blade could not move an inch further.

Wu Ming’s face twisted in horror. He couldn’t believe this youth, clearly at his limit, could still muster such strength.

A fist swung toward him. Having seen his master’s body shattered by a single punch, Wu Ming knew all too well how deadly it was.

He hesitated for a split second—whether to abandon the blade or press on—but the fist had already struck his face.

A tremendous force slammed into him. Wu Ming’s head spun, his nose flattened, and he thought in despair, “It’s over, I’m dead for sure.”

But after a dazed moment, he realized his head hadn’t exploded, nor had he died. He immediately understood that Yang Kai truly was at the end of his strength—otherwise, how could that punch be so weak?

Both angry and relieved, he was about to attack again when a faint, sharp sound rang out.

In the next instant, Wu Ming felt his whole body lighten, as if something vital had been lost.

Instinctively, Wu Ming lowered his head and looked down, only to see that at some point, a crescent-shaped hole had appeared in his chest. The gaping wound spanned most of his torso, and through it, he could see his internal organs writhing—half of his heart still faintly beating within…

Wu Ming’s eyes widened in disbelief. He desperately tried to seal the wound with his Emperor Yuan, but Yang Kai raised his hand and flicked his fingers, sending several Moon Blades slashing toward him.

Pfft, pfft, pfft…

Every Moon Blade passed cleanly through Wu Ming’s body. One even severed his neck, sending his head rolling aside, eyes still wide open in death. His headless corpse remained standing.

“I’d planned to torment you for a while, but it seems you got off easy. Be grateful and die.” Yang Kai snorted coldly at Wu Ming’s severed head, his face deathly pale.

Then he grabbed the blade embedded in his shoulder and yanked it out with force.

“Splurt…”

A jet of golden blood spurted out, splashing all over Yang Kai’s face.

“Tsk tsk…” Yang Kai turned to look at his shoulder with a look of surprise. “Like a fountain…”

Before he could finish, his legs gave out and he collapsed to the ground.

He truly was exhausted.

After gasping for breath a few times, he forced himself to focus, channeling Emperor Yuan to seal his wound. He then took out a handful of Spiritual Pills and stuffed them into his mouth, finally feeling as if he’d snatched his life back from the brink.

Lying on the ground, unwilling to move, he turned his head and, with effort, opened his palm. With a gesture, he summoned a formation flag from the ground into his hand.

Tan Junhao had used this flag against him earlier. If he guessed right, it was the control flag for the Grand Square Five Elements Array.

With this flag, he could break the array’s seal and let Yingfei come find him.

Tan Junhao was dead, so the Soul Brand on the flag was easily erased by Yang Kai. After studying it for a while, he began to understand its function.

With a wave of his hand, the darkness shrouding the world suddenly vanished, revealing the true face of the land once more.

This place seemed to be a plaza, apparently behind the branch of the Qiyao Commerce Guild, and it covered a vast area.

Yang Kai lay motionless, quietly waiting.

Before long, he heard the sound of someone cutting through the air, landing beside him.

Yang Kai forced a weak smile and said, “Yao King…”

But as soon as he spoke, he realized something was off—a familiar fragrance lingered at his nose.

Yang Kai frowned and turned his head. Three zhang away, a pair of delicate feet appeared in his view. Looking up, he saw a long, pale green dress, a slender waist cinched with a silk sash, a full and shapely bosom like twin jade peaks, a long, graceful neck, and a beautiful face—lovely enough to steal one’s breath. Her black hair cascaded like a waterfall, and her features were exquisite.

“You?!” Yang Kai’s eyes widened.

He hadn’t expected that, after breaking the Grand Square Five Elements Array, the first to arrive at his side wouldn’t be Yingfei, but that dragon girl, Zhu Qing.

Hadn’t she left? Since their last meeting at the inn, Yang Kai hadn’t seen her for nearly a month.

He’d thought she couldn’t stand his unreasonable behavior and would never bother with him again.

Was this demoness here to take advantage of his weakness? Yang Kai’s heart skipped a beat. Last time, in the wilderness, he’d been quite rough with her. Though he’d been confused and lost in the moment, he’d definitely done something wrong.

This demoness didn’t seem the forgiving type—she might have been waiting for a chance to take revenge all along.

Now things were truly dire. With no strength left to even truss a chicken, and blocked here by her, there was no way this would end peacefully.
