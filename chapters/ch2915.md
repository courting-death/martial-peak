## Chapter 2915 — Exposed

The formation that Gong Yue wanted to set up was indeed called the Sole Sovereign Formation, but no one knew this before Yang Kai spoke up. The jade slips Gong Yue handed them only contained the method for arranging the formation, with little explanation. All they needed to do was channel their strength according to the method and cooperate with the others. It didn’t seem too complicated.

Everyone believed that the jade slip Yang Kai received must have been the same.

So here was the question—how did he know the name Sole Sovereign Formation? Moreover, from his words, it sounded like this formation required exactly six assistants to set up, not one fewer.

Everyone turned their heads in unison to look at Gong Yue, only to see his brows tightly furrowed, his expression one of utter surprise.

“Brother Yang, are you also skilled in formations?” Fang Zhuo looked at Yang Kai with great interest, feeling this man was truly full of hidden depths. He himself had some knowledge of formations—otherwise, Wu Kuangyi wouldn’t have invited him here. If not for Gong Yue, he would have been the most accomplished in formation arts among those present, and breaking the ancient restriction would likely depend on his abilities.

Yet even he knew nothing about this Sole Sovereign Formation. It was clearly an extremely ancient and obscure formation, but Yang Jiu had seen through it at a glance, which made Fang Zhuo secretly admire him.

But to his surprise, Yang Kai actually shook his head. “I don’t understand formations; I just happened to know about this Sole Sovereign Formation.”

The only formation he truly mastered was the teleportation array! That was all thanks to Yang Yan’s patient guidance back in the day, and his own innate spatial abilities. Otherwise, he would have been completely clueless about formations.

Hearing this, Fang Zhuo said nothing more, simply assuming Yang Kai was being modest. Someone who even knew about such an obscure formation clearly couldn’t be ignorant of formation arts.

“I wasn’t mistaken, was I, Family Head?” Yang Kai tilted his head toward Gong Yue.

Gong Yue’s smile vanished. He nodded solemnly. “That’s right. What this old man wanted to set up was indeed the Sole Sovereign Formation.”

Although they had already suspected as much, hearing Gong Yue admit it himself still left everyone shocked.

“But what does this have to do with this old man’s identity?” Gong Yue demanded. “This old man is still this old man. Young Brother, are you not mistaken about something?”

Shen Bingru nodded. “That’s right, Brother Yang. What does this Sole Sovereign Formation have to do with the Family Head’s identity? I don’t know much about formations, but from what’s recorded in the jade slip, this formation is vast and profound. What’s rare is that the Family Head explained it so simply and clearly that even someone like me could learn it at once. That’s not something just anyone could do.”

Yang Kai frowned slightly, pondering for a moment before saying, “Fine, let’s assume there’s nothing wrong with the Family Head’s identity. That’s not the main point.”

“Then what is the main point?” Wu Kuangyi pressed, a bit impatient, but Yang Kai’s serious expression made him realize something was wrong. Since it concerned his own safety, he had to ask more.

“The Sole Sovereign Formation,” Yang Kai said to the others, though his gaze remained fixed on Gong Yue. “You’ve all seen the contents of the jade slip and should have some understanding of the formation’s principles and structure. What I want to tell you is, this formation is called the Sole Sovereign Formation. Once we cast the spell together, the six of us will become the six poles, while the Family Head, as the formation’s core, will stand alone at the center. The longer the formation is maintained, the stronger his control over us becomes. Once it reaches a certain limit, he can do as he pleases, and no matter how strong we are, we won’t be able to break free from his restraint.”

As soon as he finished speaking, everyone’s expression changed dramatically, and they all turned their gaze toward Gong Yue.

If what Yang Kai said was true, then Gong Yue’s actions were deeply suspicious. After the Sole Sovereign Formation was set up, the core could actually control the others. That was simply unacceptable.

Under everyone’s watchful eyes, Gong Yue said calmly, “That’s why this old man said earlier that you must trust me unconditionally! Only then can we combine all our strength to break the formation.”

Yang Kai snorted coldly. "What breaks the formation isn't the Sole Sovereign Formation itself, but some other means. You want us to perform a blood sacrifice, don't you?"

“Blood sacrifice?” Wu Kuangyi was startled. Even just hearing Yang Kai say it made cold sweat break out on his back.

Yang Kai sneered, “Brother Wu, you probably don’t know—breaking this ancient restriction can be simple or difficult. In fact, you just need to shed enough blood and it can be broken. You didn’t try this method last time you came, did you?”

“Shed… blood?” Wu Kuangyi’s face turned ashen. “How much blood?” he asked in shock.

“Who knows? Maybe draining all our blood would be enough.” Yang Kai shrugged, as if talking about something that had nothing to do with himself.

Blood sacrifice wasn’t the only way to break the formation, but it was the simplest. And ordinary cultivators’ blood wouldn’t do—the stronger the blood, the better the effect. The fresh blood of several Emperor Realm experts would definitely suffice.

Wu Kuangyi turned to Gong Yue, unable to believe it. “Family Head, is what he said true?”

Fang Zhuo and Shen Bingru also turned their gazes toward him, their eyes filled with inquiry. As for Hua Yulu, she naturally stood firmly on Yang Kai's side, trusting him without question. Among those present, only she knew Yang Kai's true identity. She believed that, as an Elder of the Qingyang Shrine, Yang Kai would never deceive her—not out of familiarity with him, but because of the Shrine’s reputation and honor.

Only now did everyone understand why Yang Kai had doubted Gong Yue’s identity.

If Gong Yue really was the former head of the Gong family from Tianhe Valley, there was no reason for him to act so ruthlessly. The Gong family’s reputation was at stake, and ruining it would do them no good.

They secretly felt fortunate—if not for Yang Jiu’s knowledge of the Sole Sovereign Formation, they might have been tricked. But then again, how did Yang Jiu know that a blood sacrifice could break the formation? He’d just said he didn’t understand formations, yet he spoke so authoritatively about the Sole Sovereign Formation. His words were full of contradictions.

“So Young Brother Yang is also a fellow expert. I was truly disrespectful.” Gong Yue looked at Yang Kai with a half-smile, a hint of surprise in his eyes, but no panic.

This expression caught Yang Kai’s eye, making him uneasy. He felt as if he’d overlooked something.

“Family Head, is… is everything he said true?” Wu Kuangyi’s eyes widened. “Do you really want us to perform a blood sacrifice to break the formation?”

Gong Yue smiled faintly. “That was indeed my intention.”

The moment he finished speaking, a sudden cracking sound came from Gong Yue’s body, as if something had shattered.

At the same time, Yang Kai’s expression changed. He finally realized what he’d overlooked. He threw the jade slip in his hand away and was about to warn the others, but it was already too late.

With several sharp cracks, the jade slips everyone had been holding exploded, each turning into a stream of light that wrapped around them.

Everyone present was in the Emperor Realm, so their reactions were anything but slow. The instant the jade slips shattered, each of them moved to counterattack. But Gong Yue had planned this in advance, and he moved faster.

He raised his hand and sent out two streaks of black light—one turned into a dark curtain that enveloped Wu Kuangyi and the others, while the other shot toward Yang Kai.

Yang Kai hurriedly retreated, but the more he backed away, the closer the black light came. Caught off guard, he was enveloped by the black light, and his vision suddenly shifted.

When he regained his senses, he heard the sound of surging waves in his ears. All around him, the sea rose and fell, the sky was cloudless, and the sun blazed overhead.

He had actually arrived above a vast, boundless sea, unable to tell north from south or east from west. The sky was high, the sea was wide, and he stood utterly alone.

“Formation Talisman!” Yang Kai’s face turned grim.

This was an illusion, of that Yang Kai was certain. To set up such a grand and realistic illusion formation in an instant—Gong Yue alone couldn’t have done it. The only explanation was a Formation Talisman!

This kind of illusion had been sealed within the Formation Talisman. When needed, it could be activated instantly—convenient and swift. The only drawback was that it couldn’t compare to a painstakingly arranged illusion formation; its power was somewhat less.

Still, the illusion formation Gong Yue had produced with this talisman was of extremely high grade. It could trap Yang Kai for a while, especially since his Divine Soul was still recovering and his strength was greatly diminished.

But he believed that as long as he had enough time, he could find the flaw in this illusion formation.

The jade slips containing the Sole Sovereign Formation’s information were clearly also Formation Talismans. Gong Yue was truly a cunning old fox—he’d left himself a backup plan long ago. Even if Yang Kai exposed his plot, he could still subdue the others in an instant.

Yang Kai alone reacted in time and threw away his jade slip, but he still couldn’t avoid being trapped.

By now, Hua Yulu and the others were surely caught in Gong Yue’s formation. As a formation grandmaster with Second Stage Emperor Realm cultivation, Gong Yue’s power within his own formation was definitely comparable to a Third Stage Emperor.

In other words, even if Hua Yulu and the others joined forces, they probably wouldn’t be able to defeat him.

Yang Kai had to hurry to help them.

But he clearly had no such opportunity.

Over the sea, a figure flickered into view—Yang Tai had appeared, his face cold as he stared at Yang Kai. Gone was his previous amiable demeanor; now, it was as if he and Yang Kai had a mortal grudge. His eyes were full of resentment and malice.

“So you really are an accomplice,” Yang Kai said coldly to Yang Tai, searching for a flaw in the illusion as he spoke.

Gong Yue was brought here by Yang Tai. Since Yang Kai had suspected Gong Yue, Yang Tai was naturally also under suspicion. Now, seeing Yang Tai enter the illusion so easily, his earlier doubts were confirmed.

“Accomplice?” Yang Tai sneered. “Call me an accomplice if you want. I was most wary of Fang Zhuo, but it turns out you were the one who ruined things. I underestimated you.”

Yang Kai’s face was extremely dark. After thinking for a moment, he asked, “So Gong Yue really is the former head of the Gong family?”

He had doubted Gong Yue’s identity twice before, but only after being trapped in this illusion did he realize he was wrong. Gong Yue was probably genuine. Only someone from the Gong family could carry so many Formation Talismans and set up such a profound illusion formation.
