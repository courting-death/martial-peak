## Chapter 2889 — The Final Battle

"My lord!" Yang Kai stood in the grand hall, raising his hand to his chest in salute.

Xu turned around. The smile that had long lingered on his face vanished this time, replaced by a stern and solemn gaze. In a deep voice, he said, "You should know why I called you here, right?"

Yang Kai replied, "I heard it's time for the decisive battle!"

"That's right!" Xu nodded lightly. "It's the final battle. Whether we succeed or fail will be decided here!"

Yang Kai frowned and said, "Forgive my boldness, but how will the boundary be sealed? Have you found a way?"

Only then did Xu reveal a smile. "We have! But I can't tell you the specifics just yet. However, I can guarantee it's a workable method."

He was a Shaman Saint and had no need to guarantee anything to a Shaman King, yet he still said so, clearly showing great confidence in this method.

Yang Kai nodded in understanding. "Then, my lord, what orders do you have for me?"

Xu's expression grew serious again. "I need you to carry out this method!"

"Me?" Yang Kai was taken aback. "The Saints are still here, why me?"

Xu said, "This method requires the element of surprise, so neither I nor the other three can handle it. Only you Shaman Kings have a chance of success. And among all the Shaman Kings, you are the strongest, and your Shaman Niu's tribe is the most elite. So we need you to handle it, and we need your Shaman Niu's tribe to handle it!"

Yang Kai pondered for a moment, then replied without hesitation, "What exactly do I need to do?"

"You don't need to know now. Someone will tell you before the decisive battle. Just tell me, can you shoulder this responsibility?" For the first time, Xu's gaze became truly commanding, like a blazing fire fixed on Yang Kai.

Yang Kai grinned. "If I couldn't, my lord wouldn't have called me here, would you?"

Xu laughed heartily, reaching out to pat Yang Kai's shoulder in a friendly manner. "Don't worry. On the day of the decisive battle, the entire Barbarian Tribe will create opportunities for you. You only need to do one thing."

"When is the decisive battle?" Yang Kai looked up and asked.

"In five days!"

After returning from his meeting with Xu, Yang Kai no longer secluded himself for cultivation.

Five days was no longer enough time to improve his strength. He wandered slowly through the place where Shaman Niu's tribe was stationed, looking at all the familiar and unfamiliar faces.

Every Barbarian Tribe member who saw him greeted him respectfully, expressing their highest esteem.

News of the decisive battle seemed to have spread. All one hundred thousand members of Shaman Niu's tribe were eager for action. The warriors sharpened their weapons even further, while the cavalry fed their mounts to ensure they could give their all in battle.

The archers made arrows, and the Shaman Masters mostly secluded themselves to regulate their breathing, keeping themselves in peak condition as they waited to unleash their power five days later.

Yang Kai met many old acquaintances, those who had followed him from the very beginning.

Of the two hundred villagers from Cangnan Village, after two years of war, less than half remained. Most had died in the flames of battle, and those who survived had grown even stronger.

But there were even more unfamiliar faces—people gathered by Shaman Niu's tribe from all over in the past year or so. They came from different backgrounds, but now all belonged to Shaman Niu's tribe.

In one part of the camp, Yang Kai saw Ah Hu feeding his mount.

Spotting Yang Kai, Ah Hu was delighted, abandoning his mount and running over.

"Why are you here?" Ah Hu asked.

"Just came to see you all." Yang Kai smiled slightly, his gaze sweeping over the dozens of scars, large and small, on Ah Hu's body. Each was proof of his bravery in battle. War made people grow quickly. The current Ah Hu was no longer the villager from Cangnan Village, but the captain of several thousand cavalrymen. By strength alone, he could now fight a Shaman Master head-on without falling behind.

"Come, come, look at my mount." Ah Hu pulled Yang Kai over to a Demonic Beast. This beast, like its master, was covered in marks of courage. It pressed its huge head close to Ah Hu, nuzzling him affectionately.

Ah Hu grinned. "Not bad, right?"

Yang Kai reached out to stroke the Demonic Beast's head and asked, "Have you named it?"

Ah Hu scratched his head, a little embarrassed. "It's also called Ah Niu!"

Yang Kai burst out laughing. "Just like me."

"Exactly. It used to be a bit unruly, so I said I'd call Ah Niu to deal with it. After hearing it so much, it must have thought its name was Ah Niu. Now, whenever I call Ah Niu, it listens right away. Heh, this fellow bullies the weak and fears the strong." Ah Hu grinned, still wearing the same honest smile as the villager from the small village two years ago, despite how much he'd grown.

The Demonic Beast Ah Niu beside him let out a few low calls, though their meaning was unclear.

"Let's go see Ah Hua and the others." Ah Hu smiled. "I heard the decisive battle is soon. They've been working hard too."

Yang Kai nodded gently, looking at Ah Hu. "Survive!"

Ah Hu replied with a smile, "You too!"

Yang Kai turned and left.

Ah Hu called out from behind, "Ah Niu, this is the final battle, right?"

Yang Kai paused, then said in a deep voice, "The final battle, I promise!"

Ah Hu's smile grew even more cheerful. He turned to a group of cavalry nearby and shouted, "Did you hear that? The lord said this is the final battle! When the time comes, give it your all, kill all those demon brats, and then we can go home!"

Roar, roar, roar…

The group of cavalry howled like ghosts and wolves, so excited that it was as if they'd already returned victorious.

Five days—neither long nor short.

Yang Kai walked all around the camp, always wearing a faint smile, nodding to every Barbarian Tribe member he met, and chatting briefly with some of them.

Everyone was asking him if this was truly the final battle.

Two years of war wasn't long, but it had brought endless pain and countless partings. The Barbarian Tribe was tired and weary. The talk of a final battle gave them hope, and they longed for a definite answer.

Yang Kai gave them the hope they wanted.

But in the end, how many would actually see that hope fulfilled? Fifty thousand? Twenty thousand? Or just ten thousand?

Though he didn't know what the Shaman Saints wanted his tribe to do, it was certainly a difficult task. To accomplish it, they had to be prepared to pay the price.

Most of Shaman Niu's tribe would die. This battle was different from all before—there would be no room for retreat or cowardice. In this fight, it was either you die or I die. Neither the Barbarian Tribe nor the demons had any way out.

On the morning of the fifth day, Shaman Niu's tribe was fully prepared to set out.

One hundred thousand people, nearly a hundred squads, lined up in perfect order. Though their numbers were great, not a sound was heard. Only hot blood flowed within their bodies. Every gaze was fixed on the scar in the distant sky, filled with determination.

On both sides were allied forces—other great tribes' squads. Like Shaman Niu's tribe, no one spoke. Tension and excitement spread through the crowd, making the Barbarian Tribe members unconsciously grip their weapons tighter, seeking a measure of comfort.

Several million Barbarian Tribe members were all ready for battle at this moment.

Without warning, a dull horn sounded.

The Shaman Kings of each great tribe all shuddered and gave the order to advance. Shaman Niu's tribe did the same.

Yang Kai walked at the very front, maintaining a triangular formation with the allied forces on either side. He looked around but did not see the person the Shaman Saints had mentioned. Suppressing his doubts, he thought no more of it.

The horn's sound was steady and deep, just like the footsteps of several million Barbarian Tribe members. The earth trembled gently.

In the distance, the demon army began to move as well. Five days had not only allowed the Barbarian Tribe to prepare, but also gave the demons time to respond. So as soon as the Barbarian Tribe moved, the demons followed suit.

The two armies approached each other slowly, their warriors' breathing growing heavier. A solemn killing intent gradually gathered and condensed, transforming into an unstoppable momentum.

The war drums sounded, their rhythm quickening. The Barbarian Tribe army's pace quickened as well, from walking to a brisk march, and as the drums grew more intense, the march became a run.

The distance between the two armies closed rapidly.

When only fifty li separated them, the din suddenly rose to a new level!

Roars rang out as squad after squad of cavalry charged out from the various Barbarian Tribe divisions. The warriors urged their mounts forward, launching a fearless assault.

On the demon side, Demonic Qi billowed and their numbers were vast. Though the Barbarian Tribe had fewer people, their momentum was even stronger, for every cavalryman was empowered by various Shamanic Arts. Each Barbarian Tribe cavalryman glowed with multicolored light, with a green light especially prominent, like a green ribbon binding each squad tightly together.

The Chain of Life technique—it was thanks to this powerful Shamanic Art that many Barbarian Tribe members had survived the war.

The cavalry had not yet arrived, but the arrows had already flown.

Archers from both sides shot deadly arrows into the enemy ranks. The arrows flying through the sky were like a plague of locusts, dense and overwhelming, the sound of them tearing through the air unending.

Arrows imbued with great power rained down, and one after another, cavalrymen fell from their mounts in mid-charge. The cavalry behind did not slow, trampling over their fallen comrades. Even the strongest warriors were crushed into pulp.

Ah Hu looked back, his gaze passing over the crowd to Yang Kai. Beneath him, the Demonic Beast Ah Niu let out a low roar, as if unable to suppress its urge to join the battle.

All the cavalry of Shaman Niu's tribe were the same.

But they did not move, because Yang Kai had not given the order!

Shaman Niu's tribe was the only unit whose cavalry had not charged.

Ah Hu's gaze was questioning, but Yang Kai pretended not to notice, only looking off into the distance.

Disappointed, Ah Hu withdrew his gaze and suppressed his mount's restlessness.
