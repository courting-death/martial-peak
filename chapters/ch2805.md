## Chapter 2805 — Ah Ni Is Fifteen Today

For the people, food is heaven itself—an eternal truth unchanged since ancient times.

In ancient times, being able to eat until full was the most extravagant enjoyment.

The cooking methods of the barbarian tribe were extremely simple—roasting!

Large chunks of beast meat were skewered on wooden branches, bonfires were lit all over the village, and villagers placed the meat over the flames to roast. The golden color and sizzling fat made everyone’s mouth water.

Although the beast meat had been obtained yesterday, not a single person in the village, including the village chief, had eaten a bite, because everyone was waiting for the hero’s return!

Now that Ah Niu was back, it was naturally time to celebrate.

The aroma of roasted meat quickly drifted through the village. There were no complicated seasonings; people simply grabbed the meat and tore into it, swallowing it down. Some pieces still had streaks of blood, not fully cooked, but the villagers relished it, eating with great gusto.

In front of Yang Kai was the most succulent part of the beast’s body. Even the most respected village chief hadn’t enjoyed such treatment, for Yang Kai was the greatest contributor in repelling the beast tide and slaying the wild beasts, so he naturally received the best reward.

Yang Kai had intended to just go through the motions, since at his cultivation, eating or not made no difference.

But as the aroma of roasted meat wafted to his nose, his stomach suddenly rumbled.

Yang Kai was stunned.

He actually... felt hungry!

How could such a thing happen? He was in the Emperor Realm, long past the need for food. Usually, absorbing Spiritual Qi was enough to sustain his body.

Yet in this strange place, he truly felt hungry.

And a bit tired.

This world was so real!

Without thinking further, infected by the villagers’ enthusiasm, he reached out and grabbed the roasted meat, eating heartily.

The village women brought out their treasured dried fruits, and the village chief ordered the sealed fruit wine to be fetched. The whole village was filled with a lively, festive atmosphere. Even so, vigilance was not neglected—some villagers kept watch outside to prevent the aroma from attracting wild beasts.

A full day of celebration.

Yang Kai didn’t know how much he ate or how much fruit wine he drank. In the end, he felt a bit tipsy, stumbling back to his wooden hut and falling asleep.

In a haze, he sensed someone entering his hut.

Though tired and drunk, Yang Kai’s Emperor Realm foundation remained. In an instant, he opened his eyes and called out in a low voice, “Who’s there!”

As soon as he spoke, he felt a hot, feverish body slip under his beast-skin blanket, arms wrapping around his waist, and a head burying into his chest.

A faint fragrance lingered at his nose, and his cheek was tickled by loose, long hair.

There was no hostility, but the person in his arms was breathing rapidly, trembling slightly with nervousness.

Yang Kai suddenly sobered up, tilting his head back and, by the dim moonlight from the window, saw the face of the person in his arms.

“Why is it you!” Yang Kai stared in astonishment at the girl.

It turned out to be the tall girl who had offered him a bowl of wine earlier that day. Now, with her warmth and fragrance in his embrace, Yang Kai could feel the astonishing elasticity of her body.

“Ah Niu...” the girl murmured, raising her head to meet Yang Kai’s gaze, shy yet boldly hinting, her red lips moving seductively.

Yang Kai couldn't help but feel a bit dazed, instinctively asking, "What are you doing here?"

As soon as he spoke, he realized his mistake. She was being so obvious—what else could she want?

Sure enough, the girl whispered, “Ah Niu, Ah Ni is fifteen today.”

There wasn’t much explanation, but Yang Kai vaguely guessed what turning fifteen meant for a barbarian girl—it must mean coming of age, becoming a woman!

On her fifteenth birthday, she should receive a fine gift, and offering herself to the village’s bravest warrior was her choice.

Realizing this, Yang Kai immediately felt uneasy.

If this had been the real world, it would have been one thing. The Barbarian Tribe was far more open about such matters, not bound by the rigid conservatism of women in the present age. At most, it would have been nothing more than a fleeting dalliance, with neither side feeling shortchanged.

But the key was, this was a secret realm!

Yet even now, Yang Kai couldn't tell whether this place truly existed or if he'd fallen into an illusion. If it were the latter, what meaning could any of this hold? At most, it would be nothing more than a fleeting spring dream!

As Yang Kai’s thoughts raced, the girl snuggled closer, her hot body pressed tightly against him, as if to melt him, whispering, “Ah Niu, do you like Ah Ni?”

Yang Kai braced himself and replied, “Yes, yes!”

Everyone loves beauty, especially such a pure, ancient-style maiden.

Ah Ni beamed, flipping over to sit atop him. With a tug, her simple beast-skin garment slipped off, and under the moonlight, her graceful curves were fully revealed.

Yang Kai almost had a nosebleed.

How many years... how many years had it been since he’d been so close to a woman? Since leaving his home Star Domain, he’d been alone. Seeing such a stimulating scene, even with his strong will, Yang Kai found it hard to handle.

“Wait, wait, wait...” Yang Kai quickly spoke up.

“What’s wrong?” Ah Ni looked at him curiously.

Yang Kai’s mind raced, swallowing nervously, “I’m injured, seriously injured!”

Ah Ni covered her mouth, seemingly frightened, and asked with concern, “Is it serious?”

Yang Kai replied awkwardly, “It’s nothing major.”

“Then it’s fine.” Ah Ni reached to undo Yang Kai’s clothes.

Yang Kai held on tightly, torn inside, saying, “Ah Ni, don’t be impulsive!”

Ah Ni paused, looking at him in disbelief, “Ah Niu, do you dislike me?”

“No!” Yang Kai quickly denied it.

“Then why...” Ah Ni bit her red lips, as if struck by a giant beast, deeply hurt.

“I just think we should be more careful about this!” Yang Kai said seriously.

Ah Ni stared at Yang Kai for a while, then turned to grab her discarded clothes, jumped off him, eyes brimming with tears, and rushed out the door.

“Ah Ni...” Yang Kai called, but there was no response.

Yang Kai suddenly felt a sense of loss.

After being stirred up by the girl, Yang Kai couldn’t sleep. He got up, sat cross-legged on the bed, his mind a mess, and after a while calmed down, pondering what to do in this secret realm.

This strange place had completely suppressed and sealed his Emperor Yuan, leaving only his physical strength to rely on.

When chasing wild beasts earlier, Yang Kai had tried to use his Divine Soul power, but found it sealed just like his Emperor Yuan—he couldn’t sense a trace.

Only five days remained until the one-month deadline, and he’d already spent two days in the village. In other words, only three days were left before leaving the Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda. What could he gain from this secret realm in three days?

No matter how he looked at it, what could such a shabby, ancient village offer him?

At his peak, a single sneeze could wipe out dozens or hundreds of such villages.

Instinctively, he circulated his cultivation technique, and suddenly, Yang Kai froze.

He actually felt a trace of power flowing in his Meridians. Though faint, almost illusory, it was definitely Emperor Yuan.

How did Emperor Yuan suddenly return?

Yang Kai frowned, suddenly recalling that after slaying the Yao Beast and returning, Ah Ni toasted him, and after drinking that bowl of Barbarian God Wine, he vaguely sensed something unusual in his Meridians.

He’d wanted to check, but was interrupted by the villagers’ enthusiasm.

Was it thanks to that bowl of wine?

At today’s celebration, he’d also drunk, but it wasn’t quite the same as the bowl Ah Ni brought—the one she served was definitely specially given by the village chief.

This discovery made Yang Kai pleasantly surprised.

If a bowl of cloudy wine could produce a bit of Emperor Yuan in his Meridians, what if he had more? Could he recover some strength? Maybe even return to his peak?

He couldn’t wait to find the village chief and ask for more wine to test his theory.

Though he hadn’t encountered any danger since entering this secret realm, Yang Kai had to stay vigilant.

In this ancient era, there were Shamans. Powerful Shamans weren’t as clumsy as the village chief—they were experts not inferior to today’s martial artists.

He glanced out the window. Moonlight poured in, and Yang Kai sighed. Everything would have to wait until tomorrow.

He didn’t know when he fell asleep again.

When dawn came the next day, Yang Kai woke up immediately and stepped out of his hut.

Many villagers were already up, busy in the village. When they saw Yang Kai, they showed respect, nodding slightly, but then turned away, laughing quietly. Some even gathered together, pointing and whispering in his direction.

One or two would be fine, but every villager he met acted this way, making it impossible for Yang Kai to even ask where the village chief’s house was.

What’s going on? Yang Kai was full of confusion.

“I heard Ah Ni went to your house last night, then left soon after?”

Ah Hu appeared out of nowhere, sidling up to Yang Kai with a mischievous grin.

Yang Kai was shocked: “How do you know?”

Ah Hu chuckled, pointing, “Everyone knows.”

Yang Kai’s face darkened. No wonder the villagers were acting so strangely—it was because of this.

“How does everyone know?” Yang Kai asked awkwardly. He didn’t mind, but worried it might affect Ah Ni’s reputation. The village wasn’t big, and such news would spread quickly.

Ah Hu replied, “Someone on night watch saw it.”

Then he leaned in mysteriously, whispering, “Are you... not up to it?”

“You’re the one who’s not up to it!” Yang Kai snapped.

Ah Hu burst out laughing.
