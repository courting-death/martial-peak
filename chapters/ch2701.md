## Chapter 2701 — Golden Armored Heavenly Tome

“You actually obtained the Mountain and River Bell?” Tan Junhao cried out in shock.

Since he recognized the Mountain and River Bell, how could he not know its origins? It was said this bell was a rare treasure from the Primordial Era, passed down since ancient times. It had always been in the Wild Ancient Land, and even the powerful Divine Spirits of the Ancient Land were helpless before it, unable to subdue it.

Tens of thousands of years ago, an Emperor Realm master entered the Wild Ancient Land and, by a stroke of fortune, gained the Mountain and River Bell’s recognition, taking it out of the Ancient Land.

Within a hundred years, this Third Stage Emperor Realm expert glimpsed the Grand Dao and ascended to the Great Emperor Realm.

That person was none other than the legendary Origin Cauldron Great Emperor.

In that era, the Origin Cauldron Great Emperor, wielding the Mountain and River Bell, was an absolute powerhouse—his strength among the Great Emperors ranked in the top three. Unfortunately, he was slain by the Heaven Devourer Great Emperor during the war of the Great Emperors. Since then, this Primordial Era treasure vanished without a trace.

Rumor had it that it was left behind in the Shattered Star Sea, but though the Shattered Star Sea had opened countless times over the millennia, no one had ever been fated to see it.

Tan Junhao never imagined that, after so many years, this treasure would once again shine brilliantly in Yang Kai’s hands.

The Shattered Star Sea only opened a few years ago. It seemed this Yang brat must have entered it and obtained the Mountain and River Bell there.

He already possessed the Xuan Realm Pearl, a treasure that formed a world of its own, and the Everlasting Tree, a supreme treasure of heaven and earth. Now, even the Mountain and River Bell had appeared in his hands…

For ordinary people, obtaining even one of these treasures would be a heaven-defying stroke of fortune, yet now they were all gathered on a single person!

Was this guy even human?

What kind of heaven-defying luck and fortune does it take to gather all these treasures in one net?

Tan Junhao’s eye twitched, and a faint sense of dread crept into his heart. No matter how he looked at it, Yang Kai was no ordinary First Stage Emperor Realm cultivator. Not to mention his terrifying, unreasonable combat strength—even his fortune was something ordinary people could never possess. Such people always carried great destiny and could walk a path that billions of cultivators could never tread, forging ahead through countless obstacles to stand at the very peak of the Martial Dao.

Such an existence could never be erased by minor calamities.

Was it truly wise to tear all pretenses with him and make things so irreconcilable?

His hesitation lasted only a moment before Tan Junhao’s expression turned resolute and ferocious. Now that things had reached this point, it was a life-and-death struggle—either you die or I perish. There was no possibility of reconciliation.

As long as he could kill this Yang brat here, all these treasures would become his. At that point, breaking through his shackles and ascending to the Great Emperor Realm would no longer be a pipe dream.

Perhaps he could even seize his destiny.

“Foolish!” Tan Junhao looked at Yang Kai with a mocking sneer. “If you used the Mountain and River Bell against this old man, I might have had to be wary. Yet you actually used it to save those enemies who just tried to kill you—how laughably soft-hearted.”

The Mountain and River Bell’s reputation was immense. After all, it was a Primordial Era treasure and the life-bound Imperial Treasure of the Origin Cauldron Great Emperor. If Yang Kai really used it against Tan Junhao, he would have to be extremely cautious, not knowing the extent of its power. But now, with the Mountain and River Bell guarding over those twenty-some Emperor Realm cultivators, it was impossible to use it against him.

This made Tan Junhao secretly rejoice.

“Give and take, that’s the way of the world—only by letting go can you gain.” Yang Kai laughed carelessly, looking down on him. “Old fool, what do you know about any of this?”

For Yang Kai, he’d rather face a Third Stage Emperor Realm than be besieged by so many First and Second Stage Emperors. The former was certainly dangerous, but at least he wouldn’t have to worry about being stabbed in the back. If he faced the latter, even if he killed them all, he’d still have to deal with Tan Junhao. Using the Mountain and River Bell to directly cut off Tan Junhao’s wings was a huge gain for him.

“Without the Mountain and River Bell, what do you have to fight this old man with!” Tan Junhao roared, his aura surging. His powerful Imperial Might rolled like a raging sea, utterly domineering.

Yang Kai sneered, “Why would I need the Mountain and River Bell to deal with you?”

Tan Junhao was furious. “Arrogant brat!”

For a mere First Stage Emperor to look down on him was simply unforgivable.

Yang Kai raised the Myriad Sword, pointing it straight at Tan Junhao. Gritting his teeth, he shouted, “Old dog Tan! I’ve never wronged you in the past, nor have I crossed you recently—yet you schemed to wipe out the entire Thousand Leaves Sect and abducted my friends. Today, I will kill you!”

Tan Junhao sneered coldly, “If you want to kill me, let’s see if you have the ability.”

As soon as he finished speaking, he thrust both hands forward. A massive five-colored blade of light formed, as if to tear the world apart, and slashed straight at Yang Kai.

Yang Kai brought down the Myriad Sword, and as his Emperor Yuan surged, a sword light like a white silk ribbon met the attack head-on.

With a thunderous boom, spiritual energy roiled. The massive sword light and five-colored blade shattered together, dissolving into motes of light that scattered across the world.

At the same time, Tan Junhao lunged, his palms unleashing a flurry of shadows that enveloped Yang Kai.

The Myriad Sword’s intent surged. Yang Kai met him head-on, his movements bold and unrestrained.

In an instant, the two were locked in fierce combat. Emperor Yuan clashed violently, figures weaving and colliding in a chaotic dance.

The clang of metal rang out as Yang Kai, wielding the Myriad Sword, found himself unable to gain the slightest advantage over Tan Junhao’s bare-handed assault. Instead, he was gradually forced onto the defensive by the other’s pure, overwhelming Emperor Yuan.

Feeling frustrated, Yang Kai cursed inwardly—this old bastard really wasn’t easy to deal with. Tan Junhao's Third Stage of the Emperor Realm cultivation couldn’t be underestimated; anyone at this level would have a few life-saving trump cards and unique skills up their sleeve.

Back in the Jixu Secret Realm, he’d killed a Third Stage Emperor named Yao Changjun. The Imperial Treasure, the Five-Colored Long Spear that Hua Qingsi now possessed, was the spoils of that battle.

But at that time, Yao Changjun’s Divine Soul had been wounded for years, so although he had the cultivation of a Third Stage Emperor, he couldn’t display its full power. That was how Yang Kai managed to take advantage and kill him in the Jixu Secret Realm.

But Tan Junhao was a true Third Stage Emperor Realm master—far beyond what Yao Changjun had been back then.

His heart was filled with awe, but little did he know that Tan Junhao was even more deeply shaken.

He’d seen Yang Kai act several times before and knew this youth couldn’t be judged by common sense, possessing the ability to fight and kill above his realm. But only after truly crossing hands did he realize Yang Kai was even more troublesome than he’d imagined.

As an elder of the Star God Palace, he could defeat any ordinary First Stage Emperor Realm, and even those at the Second Stage would last no more than ten moves against him.

Yet after going all out against Yang Kai, he found he could only barely suppress him. Killing him was simply impossible.

How could a First Stage Emperor’s Emperor Yuan be so vast and refined, nearly matching his own? Just how did this guy cultivate?

Tan Junhao’s mind was shaken. Each move became fiercer, trying to force out Yang Kai’s trump cards and see how long he could last.

But reality left him bitterly disappointed.

No matter how hard he pressed, this youth could always barely cope, as if he grew stronger the tougher things got, his strength bottomless. Though he was sometimes in a sorry state, after a long struggle, Tan Junhao still hadn’t managed to injure him.

The wide sword in Yang Kai’s hand carried a hint of sharp sword intent, but his swordsmanship was nothing special. Tan Junhao could tell Yang Kai wasn’t a sword cultivator, yet he could always use that Imperial Treasure sword to neutralize his attacks.

Looking from afar, Yang Kai was like a lone boat sailing through a stormy sea, swaying left and right, seemingly on the verge of capsizing at any moment, yet still able to ride the waves.

“Damn it!” Tan Junhao cursed inwardly. He knew that if this continued, it would be pointless. If he wanted to kill Yang Kai, he’d have to use his true skills.

With that thought, he clenched his fists and suddenly punched forward.

In an instant, his fists seemed to transform into two great mountains pressing down on Yang Kai, or like a tsunami erupting, the momentum overwhelming.

“The old bastard really is fierce!” Yang Kai muttered to himself, gripping the Myriad Sword with both hands and hacking forward as if wielding a giant saber that could split mountains.

The power of this strike was such that even a real mountain would be shattered.

The fist shadows burst apart, but Yang Kai was shaken, his qi and blood surging in his chest as he staggered back two steps.

Ahead, a five-colored blade of light condensed, turning into a rainbow that slashed toward Yang Kai.

“This move again?” Yang Kai curled his lip disdainfully. With a flick of the Myriad Sword, he scattered the five-colored blade.

At the same time, Yang Kai’s eyes narrowed as he instinctively sensed danger approaching. He quickly leapt aside.

With several sharp sounds, a few golden rays suddenly shot out from the scattered motes of light, boring two small holes into where Yang Kai had just been standing.

Though the holes were small, they plunged several hundred zhang deep into the earth, a testament to the terrifying penetrating power of that golden light.

Looking up, he saw Tan Junhao standing in midair beyond the motes of light, an ancient-looking tome floating above his head, its pages rustling as they turned.

The entire tome gleamed with a golden hue, each page seemingly cast from pure gold, giving it an incredibly heavy appearance at first glance.

The aura of an Imperial Treasure slowly spread in all directions.

This strange golden tome was actually an Imperial Treasure!

Yang Kai’s vigilance soared. He fixed his gaze on the golden tome, sending out his Divine Sense to investigate carefully.

The earlier battle had seemed intense, but it was really just him probing Tan Junhao’s strength. But wasn’t Tan Junhao also testing him? Now that the probing was over, both sides were about to get serious.

For Tan Junhao to suddenly bring out such a treasure at this moment clearly meant he was preparing to unleash a big move. A single misstep could mean defeat and death—how could Yang Kai not be cautious?

He had never seen such an Imperial Treasure before and had no idea what powers it possessed. But throughout the world, among billions of cultivators, there was a consensus: the more unique a Secret Treasure, the greater its power.

This golden tome was undoubtedly such an Imperial Treasure.

Those golden rays just now had clearly been unleashed by this tome. Unfortunately, Tan Junhao had used a bit of misdirection, so Yang Kai hadn’t seen it clearly.

“To make this old man use the Golden Armored Heavenly Tome against you, you can die without regrets.” Tan Junhao sneered, his words brimming with confidence, as if this Golden Armored Heavenly Tome was something truly extraordinary.
