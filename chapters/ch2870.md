## Chapter 2870 — Gathering of Demons

On the desolate plain, before the entrance to the Small Xuan Realm—disguised as the place where a Demon Saint had fallen—Bao Qi sat alone in meditation, eyes tightly shut, as if waiting for someone. The once-visible vortex entrance had now been concealed within the void by Yang Kai’s methods, making it nearly impossible to detect. Only experts at the Demon King level could faintly sense the pure Demonic Qi emanating from within that hidden space.

Yang Kai and Shaya were nowhere to be seen.

After waiting for less than half a day, Bao Qi suddenly opened his eyes, gazed deeply into the void ahead, and said in a low voice, “Since you’ve come, why hide your head and show only your tail? Are you afraid?”

A strange laugh suddenly echoed from the void, and then a mass of shadow appeared out of thin air. The newcomer was shrouded in pure Demonic Qi, concealing their figure so thoroughly that it was impossible to discern their appearance or even their gender. Their voice was magnetic and androgynous, seemingly imbued with a peculiar demonic power.

“Mo Ke Tuo!” Bao Qi’s eyes narrowed as he instantly recognized the newcomer’s identity.

Shadow Demon Mo Ke Tuo! The strongest of the Shadow Demon clan, whose true strength surpassed even Bao Qi’s. The Shadow Demons were naturally skilled in stealth and assassination, and as a Demon King, Mo Ke Tuo was the best among them.

Among all the Demon Kings, he might not be the most powerful, but he was definitely the most troublesome.

Because once he set his sights on someone, even the strongest Demon King wouldn’t dare claim they could survive. He always managed to find the perfect moment and place to deliver a fatal blow to his enemies.

Bao Qi hadn’t expected that the first to arrive would be Shadow Demon Mo Ke Tuo, which made him a bit nervous. In a direct confrontation, he wasn’t afraid, but this guy was always elusive. If it really came down to a life-and-death battle, Bao Qi felt he’d only be able to offer up his neck.

“Looks like I’m pretty early!” Mo Ke Tuo chuckled, his temperament seeming especially lively. Even though his form was wrapped in black mist, Bao Qi still felt those eyes staring right at him. “You said there’s a place where an Ancient Saint fell here? Where is it? Why can’t I see it?”

“It’s behind me. Try sensing more carefully,” Bao Qi replied calmly.

Sure enough, Mo Ke Tuo carefully probed the area and immediately discovered the hidden entrance. He couldn’t help but let out a surprised sound, clearly sensing the pure Demonic Qi as well. His tone instantly grew serious: “Are you sure this is really the place where an Ancient Saint fell?”

Bao Qi snorted, “With such obvious signs, what else could it be? But I can’t guarantee it. I’m about eighty percent sure.”

Mo Ke Tuo didn’t respond, seemingly accepting Bao Qi’s explanation. Before entering, no one could be completely certain. Still, the aura leaking out was truly irresistible.

“How did you find such a hidden place?” Mo Ke Tuo asked casually, though Bao Qi knew it was just his cautious nature showing.

“This is my territory. I discovered it by accident,” Bao Qi answered, clearly prepared for such questions.

“You’re pretty lucky. Since you found it, why not go in yourself? Why invite me? If I’m not mistaken, you must have invited others too, right?”

“Of course you’re not the only one,” Bao Qi replied slowly. “But who actually shows up, I can’t say. After all, this is the place where an Ancient Saint fell. There might be secrets inside that could let us ascend to Sainthood, but there could also be danger. I, Bao Qi, haven’t lived long enough yet and want to live a few more years. I don’t dare go in alone.”

Mo Ke Tuo sneered, a hint of mockery in his tone, his body still shrouded in black mist as he circled the vortex entrance, probing it repeatedly.

Bao Qi said, “If you want, you can go in now. With the skills of your Shadow Demon clan, I doubt there’s much danger for you.”

He was hoping Mo Ke Tuo would lose patience and rush into that world. Once inside, his fate would no longer be his own.

Mo Ke Tuo seemed tempted by the suggestion, but after pausing for a moment, he still refused, “Let’s wait for the others. You haven’t lived enough, and neither have I.”

The suspected place where an Ancient Saint had fallen was bound to be dangerous. Why would he want to be the vanguard for others?

Bao Qi grunted in acknowledgment and said no more, closing his eyes again to rest and recover.

Mo Ke Tuo wandered around nearby for a while, clearly bored, and eventually settled down beside Bao Qi, unmoving, like a shadow.

The two of them waited here for two days, during which more and more Demon Kings arrived.

Each of these Demon Kings was a top figure among their respective clans. Even if Bao Qi hadn’t interacted much with some of them, he was still very familiar with their names.

The number of arrivals exceeded his expectations, making him a bit anxious.

He’d thought that, with his invitations, getting ten people to come would be good enough. After all, Demon Kings weren’t idle, and he wasn’t close with some of them. But in just two days, fifteen Demon Kings had already arrived, and more than half were stronger than him.

It seemed he had underestimated how attractive the chance to ascend to Sainthood was to everyone.

Could Lord Shaman Niu really handle so many Demon Kings? He'd better not end up losing the rice while trying to catch the chicken.

With so many Demon Kings gathered, the Demonic Qi soared into the sky above the wasteland. Every Demon King who arrived would first ask Bao Qi about the situation, then check for themselves. Only after confirming would they stay.

It had to be said, the vortex entrance Yang Kai created was quite convincing. At the very least, it hadn’t aroused any suspicion among the Demon Kings.

Since it was rare for so many to gather, naturally, the Demon Kings who were familiar with each other chatted loudly, boasting about how many enemy experts they’d killed and how many beautiful women they’d captured. In their excitement, spittle flew everywhere, and at the slightest verbal clash, they would take fighting stances, ready to brawl at any moment.

Fortunately, everyone was still fairly restrained. Even if there were conflicts, it was just verbal sparring—no real fights broke out.

“Bao Qi, what are we still waiting for?”

Suddenly, a Demon King wreathed in flames stood before Bao Qi. The scorching fire seemed capable of melting the void itself. This guy looked different from the other Demon Kings—his skin and flesh were bright red, like molten lava from a volcano.

Flame Demon Lai Wen! His strength ranked in the upper middle among the Demon Kings, and he was known for his impatience. After waiting two days, he was getting anxious.

The other Demon Kings also looked to Bao Qi for answers. Although Bao Qi wasn’t the strongest among them—he was only average—this was his territory, and he was the one who initiated the exploration of the Ancient Saint’s resting place, so everyone gave him some respect.

Bao Qi glanced around and said, “The agreed date is tomorrow, but since everyone’s in a hurry, let’s start now. We have enough people, and those who haven’t arrived are just unlucky. What do you all think?”

Naturally, the Demon Kings had no objections. With fifteen Demon Kings gathered here, even if there was danger inside, they had enough power to handle it. The more people, the fiercer the competition, but who knew what awaited inside?

Bao Qi’s suggestion was quickly accepted by everyone.

Just as Bao Qi was about to say more, a coquettish voice suddenly rang out from afar, “Oh dear, I’m late. Sorry to keep you waiting, brothers.”

As soon as she finished speaking, Shaya appeared before the Demon Kings, exuding charm with every step as she approached.

Seeing Shaya arrive in time, Bao Qi secretly breathed a sigh of relief. Their eyes met, and everything was understood without words.

“Useless!” A low shout suddenly rang out, and a towering skeleton strode toward Shaya.

This skeleton had no flesh at all, its bones pitch-black as if dyed in ink. Only in its eye sockets did eerie ghostly flames flicker.

Bone Demon Fei Li!

As he spoke, he strode toward Shaya, his aura dangerous.

This sudden turn of events startled both Bao Qi and Shaya. They had no idea why Fei Li seemed so hostile toward her. While Succubi didn’t have the best reputation, there’d never been any word of a grudge between Fei Li and Shaya.

Bao Qi shot Shaya a questioning look, but her expression was full of innocence.

As she hesitated, Fei Li had already reached her, stretching out a skeletal hand to grab Shaya’s slender, fair neck and lift her high into the air.

Shaya immediately couldn’t breathe, struggling desperately like a child, completely unlike a powerful Demon King.

Shaya was helpless. If it were any other Demon King, she might have had a way to save herself, but against the emotionless, fleshless Bone Demon Fei Li, she had no way to resist.

The charm techniques she excelled at were useless against the Bone Demon clan.

Unable to break free, Shaya could only ask with difficulty, her face flushed red and her expression pitiful, “You... what are you doing?”

The other Demon Kings watched coldly, none showing any sympathy for Shaya. They all knew her pitiful act was just a cheap disguise.

“You’ve betrayed the trust of the Demon Saints, and you still have the nerve to live?” Fei Li’s pitch-black jaw opened and closed, producing a strange sound.

At these words, both Shaya and Bao Qi were shocked. Could it be that the matter of Shaya being subdued by Lord Shaman Niu had been exposed? But that couldn’t be—if it had, Bao Qi would never have remained safe. The other Demon Kings would have attacked him long ago.

But if not, why was Fei Li targeting Shaya like this?

“What do you mean?” Shaya asked, her face ashen, half from Fei Li’s grip and half from trying to hide her unease.

“Fei Li, Shaya is still a Demon King. How did she betray the Demon Saints’ trust? We’d all like to know. Do you know something?” another Demon King asked.

The others also turned their attention to Fei Li.

Fei Li snorted coldly and said, “Her territory has already fallen, and all three thousand of her subordinates have been wiped out. Isn’t someone like that useless? Doesn’t she deserve to die?”
