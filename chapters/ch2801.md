## Chapter 2801 — Arrows Never Miss

Ah Hua stopped, her expression turning cold. “If you can’t prove it, then jump off the wall yourself.”

As soon as she finished speaking, an arrow shot out, its immense force blasting a charging giant beast to death in midair. Yet those cold, disdainful eyes remained fixed on Yang Kai's face, never shifting from beginning to end.

"A good-for-nothing, huh..." Yang Kai muttered, lowering his head to look at his own physique, then at the other villagers on the fence.

Indeed, with this kind of build, he truly was a good-for-nothing in this era.

Yet the hunched old man was actually the village chief, which surprised Yang Kai a little.

He had no idea what kind of Shamanic Arts the village chief had used to make those giant beasts ignore him, but it definitely wasn’t just a stroke of luck. As for the so-called Shaman Apprentices… judging by the name, they were probably the lowest rung of the hierarchy.

It seemed that in this ancient era, Shamans were truly rare.

On the fence, Yang Kai and Ah Hua worked together seamlessly. After overcoming the initial awkwardness, Yang Kai calmed down and no longer felt at a loss.

No matter what, he had to get through this ordeal first. Perhaps this was a test of the secret realm here.

Arrow after arrow was handed by Yang Kai to Ah Hua, and the pile of arrows at their feet dwindled rapidly. Soon, though, a barbarian youth came over with a fresh bundle of arrows to replenish them.

The boy looked about thirteen or fourteen, clearly underage and unable to fight the giant beasts directly, so he could only help with logistics. Still, even at that age, he was more robust and muscular than most people Yang Kai had ever met. Those bulging muscles were simply not something a boy his age should have.

In the village, there were quite a few other underage barbarians busy just like him.

Time passed slowly. With the chief casting the Bloodlust Art, the beast tide's assault was greatly suppressed. The villagers, at minimal cost, slaughtered the giant beasts in droves, leaving countless dead and wounded.

Every villager was extremely excited, as if they could see the hope of victory. Ah Hua's bow was drawn again and again, sending out a series of arrows with the sound of air being split.

Yet Yang Kai's brow furrowed.

Unlike these naive villagers, Yang Kai felt the situation was not in the village's favor.

The giant beasts seemed suppressed on the surface and had suffered heavy casualties, but in reality, the villagers were exhausting themselves. Prolonged, intense fighting was wearing everyone down.

Ah Hua was a good example.

At first, her arrows flew like the wind, one after another without pause, each one killing a giant beast.

But now, she couldn't even fully draw the bowstring. Both hands trembled, and it often took two arrows to kill a single beast. The intervals between shots grew longer and longer.

Ah Hua was like this, and so were the other archers.

The situation was even more worrying for the villagers outside the fence.

With the chief's Bloodlust Art, they were burning through their own Essence-Blood. Once the technique wore off and the backlash hit, the hundred-plus villagers fighting outside would instantly become prey for the giant beasts, unable to resist at all.

In contrast, although the beast tide’s assault remained fierce, Yang Kai could clearly sense that the beasts were deliberately stalling for time. It was as if some cunning will lurked behind the scenes, quietly commanding the countless savage creatures—waiting patiently for the perfect moment to turn the tide in a single, devastating strike.

It was time to act!

Yang Kai hadn't acted rashly at first because he didn't fully understand his situation, nor what consequences his actions might bring. But after watching for so long, he had been infected by the villagers' passion.

The identity of Ah Niu, which he had inexplicably acquired, now felt a bit more real. He even felt a faint sense of belonging to this unfamiliar village.

These barbarians were truly admirable. It was they who, in ancient times, survived against all odds, resisting disasters and calamities, allowing the human race to flourish and become the world's dominant force. They passed down their strength from generation to generation, enabling humanity to stand firm in this world.

Protect this land, protect this village!

My name is Ah Niu!

In that instant, the blood in his veins boiled.

Yang Kai bent down and picked up a giant bow lying nearby.

This bow had originally belonged to another villager, but that man had his head bitten off by a giant leopard that leapt at him.

Yang Kai plucked the bowstring. With a metallic twang, it was as if war drums thundered in his heart, stirring an urge to charge forward.

"Arrow!" Ah Hua reached out, her bloodshot eyes fixed ahead, burning with hatred.

But Yang Kai did not hand her an arrow.

Ah Hua whipped her head around, about to curse, but the words caught in her throat as she stared, dumbfounded, at Yang Kai and the giant bow in his hands. "What are you doing?" she blurted out in astonishment.

Yang Kai grinned, revealing a mouthful of white teeth. "Take a break, you're too tired."

The hand Ah Hua extended was trembling uncontrollably, her fingers covered in split wounds, blood streaming down, a shocking sight.

Their eyes met, and Ah Hua exploded, "Rest? How can I rest now? Do you know what will happen to our brothers and sisters below if we archers don't support them?"

"I know!" Yang Kai still smiled, picking up an arrow and nocking it to the bowstring. Tilting his head, he looked at Ah Hua and said, "Leave this to me."

"You..." Ah Hua wanted to say more, but her eyes suddenly widened in shock.

Heavens, what was she seeing? The so-called village good-for-nothing, Ah Niu, had actually drawn the bowstring? And... to full draw?

With those skinny arms and legs, where did he get such strength? Ah Hua instinctively thought she was seeing things, because she clearly remembered that during the last training, Ah Niu had struggled just to pick up the bow and arrows, let alone draw and shoot.

But now, the scene before her completely overturned her understanding of Ah Niu.

It didn’t even look like Ah Niu was using much strength—Ah Hua couldn’t sense any explosive power from his muscles at all. It was as if he’d drawn the bowstring with casual ease, and yet, the very bow that even she found difficult to pull was now fully drawn in his hands.

With the bow fully drawn and the arrow released, Yang Kai's expression turned solemn.

Ah Hua couldn't help but follow the arrow's flight with her eyes.

Ahead of the arrow, the shockwave in the air was clearly visible. Obviously, this shot contained tremendous power, no less than one of her own full-strength attacks. Such a shot could pierce any giant beast.

For some reason, Ah Hua suddenly had confidence in this good-for-nothing, believing that this arrow would surely kill a giant beast.

Whoosh—

The arrow grazed a giant beast and vanished in the blink of an eye.

The villager locked in a life-and-death struggle with that beast was startled, for in that instant, had the arrow been off by just a bit, it could have taken his life.

Ah Hu turned his head, looking at the people on the fence, and roared, "Ah Hua, watch it!"

"I..." Ah Hua opened her mouth, full of grievance.

How could such a lousy shot be hers? She wouldn't make that mistake even with her eyes closed! Clearly, it was Ah Niu's doing, wasn't it?

After grievance came anger. She glared at Yang Kai, furious that she had actually placed her faith in this good-for-nothing. She must have been blind.

"I'm getting the hang of it," Yang Kai said as he picked up another arrow and nocked it.

"This isn't a place for you to fool around! If you don't want to help, just get down!" Ah Hua snapped. Though she was surprised that this good-for-nothing could fully draw the bow, what use was it if he couldn't aim? He might as well go fight the beasts head-on and at least be of some use.

Outstanding archers were forged through countless trials and life-and-death battles. Ah Niu clearly had none of that experience.

"Trust me!" Yang Kai looked at Ah Hua seriously, his hands steady as stone as he drew the bow.

Ah Hua's anger faltered a little. She sighed, "Shoot where there are no people!"

Compared to his past performance, the fact that he could pick up the bow and help share the burden was at least commendable.

Yang Kai smiled. "That won't happen again."

His target was still the giant beast fighting Ah Hu. As the arrow was released, Ah Hua almost didn't dare look, afraid she would see Yang Kai accidentally injure Ah Hu.

But in fact, as the arrow flew, the giant beast happened to leap into the air, roaring. The zhang-long arrow shot straight into its mouth and out its tail, the terrifying force churning its organs into a mess. It collapsed to the ground, whimpering, blood gushing like a spring.

"He really did it?" Ah Hua stood frozen, dumbfounded.

If she remembered right, Ah Niu had never even managed to draw a bow before, let alone shoot. This should have been his first real shot. The first arrow was off, giving everyone a scare, but the second was dead on.

What was with this good-for-nothing?

While she was still in a daze, Yang Kai had already picked up another arrow, barely pausing before drawing and shooting again.

In an instant, a distant giant beast was killed.

Although Yang Kai couldn't sense any Emperor Yuan in this secret realm, his foundation as an Emperor Realm cultivator was still there. The giant bow and arrows were hard to handle, but for someone at the Emperor Realm, what did that matter?

The first arrow was just a test. Now that he had a feel for it, everything else was easy.

Not a single arrow missed.

In less than three breaths, Yang Kai shot out a bundle of ten arrows. Except for the first, which failed, the other nine all hit their mark and killed their targets.

"Brother Ah Niu, you're amazing!" The barbarian youth in charge of logistics ran up with several bundles of arrows, just in time to witness Yang Kai's display of might, and was instantly filled with admiration.

Weren't all the adults always spitting on Brother Ah Niu, calling him the village's good-for-nothing, saying he contributed nothing and only wasted food? Many even wanted him banished to fend for himself.

If not for the chief's kindness and Ah Hu always sharing his food, Ah Niu would have starved long ago.

Yet the so-called good-for-nothing, Ah Niu, was actually this formidable—seemingly no less skilled than the village's best archer, Sister Ah Hua. The boy was left utterly bewildered.
