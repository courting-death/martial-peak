## Chapter 2946 — Cold Tide

The three of them worked together, quickly clearing away all the accumulated snow nearby. The massive traces left by the fallen dragon were immediately revealed before their eyes.

Just as Li Jiao had said before, there were only traces here, but no dragon corpse to be seen. That was because the dragon’s flesh, blood essence, and even its bones had already become nourishment, giving rise to a Dragon Blood Flower.

There was no information left by Zhu Qing in this place, and no one knew whether she had ever been here at all.

Yang Kai silently sensed for a long while, then shook his head and said, “The Origin Power isn’t here.”

Although he was not of the Dragon Clan, ever since he had cultivated the Dragon Transformation Art Zhu Qing had taught him, the Golden Sacred Dragon’s Origin Power had already begun to truly fuse with him. His bloodline and body were gradually transforming toward that of the Dragon Clan, so if there were any Dragon Clan Origin here, he would definitely be able to sense it.

Zhu Lie glanced at him and said, “I’m going to use a Dragon Clan secret art. Guard me.”

Yang Kai nodded solemnly and stepped aside, while Li Jiao’s expression shifted, curiosity and longing filling his gaze as he looked at Zhu Lie.

The Dragon Clan’s secret art was a truly mysterious technique, one that only those with Dragon Clan blood could perform. Yang Kai could suppress Zhu Lie and Zhu Qing in terms of bloodline power, but without systematic study, even he could not perform the Dragon Clan’s secret art, as it involved the use of dragon essence and the incantations of the dragon language.

Li Jiao possessed a trace of mixed Dragon Clan blood, so he was naturally both curious and eager about this.

Zhu Lie closed his eyes, calming his mind and focusing his spirit.

After a moment, he suddenly opened his eyes. With fingers like blades, he lightly slid them across his other hand, the sharp nails slicing open his palm. Instantly, a thick scent of blood filled the air.

Zhu Lie clenched his wounded hand tightly and raised it high. Dragon essence surged within him, and a low incantation sounded from his mouth. The incantation was profound and complex, and hearing it made one feel it was extremely awkward, as if Zhu Lie’s mouth was stuffed with something as he spoke.

Li Jiao, ears pricked, wore a blank look, anxiously scratching his head. It was rare to encounter a pure Dragon Clan member performing a Dragon Clan secret art right before him, so of course he wanted to secretly learn, but he couldn’t even understand what was being said—how could he possibly learn it?

Yang Kai couldn’t understand either, but he felt that when Zhu Lie performed this Dragon Clan secret art, it was somewhat similar to the Shamanic Art of ancient times, both requiring the chanting of incantations.

Under the gaze of the other two, drops of fresh blood dripped from Zhu Lie’s palm. His dragon blood was different from ordinary blood, as if it were a burning flame, radiating scorching heat.

As soon as it hit the ground, a sizzling sound was heard, and it melted into the earth, vanishing from sight.

A fierce wind rose, snowflakes danced, and Zhu Lie’s incantation grew louder and louder, gradually resembling a dragon’s roar, then thunder rolling across the sky, making one’s eardrums ache. His red hair whipped wildly, his clothes flapped, and it seemed as if the entire world was filled with that dragon’s roar.

Suddenly, Zhu Lie’s voice stopped, and the surging momentum that had reached its peak deflated like a punctured ball, weakening all at once. Even his delicate face was now beaded with sweat.

Though the exact nature of this Dragon Clan secret art remained a mystery, it was undoubtedly taking a tremendous toll on him.

Yang Kai and Li Jiao both stared at him, but Zhu Lie’s gaze was lowered, fixed on the ground.

The earth, soaked with his blood, was now crimson, and the scorching energy clashed with the cold here, sending up steaming mist.

At a certain moment, Zhu Lie’s eyes lit up. At the same time, Yang Kai and Li Jiao also sensed a subtle change.

The blood-stained ground was writhing, as if something was about to break through the surface.

Under the three’s watchful eyes, a spot of crimson bloomed from the soil, then quickly rose, its branches and leaves spreading. In the blink of an eye, a one-zhang-tall, blood-red plant appeared on the ground, as if it had been forged from blood. At first glance, it looked just like a blood coral.

Zhu Lie drew up the one-zhang-tall blood coral and held it in his palm. Misty red light gathered at the crown, and, as if it had a will of its own, drifted in a certain direction, like a red ribbon hanging from the treetop.

Strangely, the direction in which the red ribbon floated was completely different from the wind’s direction here.

Zhu Lie pointed in the direction indicated by the red ribbon and said, “Zhu Qing is over there, and she’s still alive.”

No one knew how he had determined this information, but it was undoubtedly related to the Dragon Clan secret art he had performed.

Without another word, Yang Kai summoned the Flowing Cloud Shuttle and shouted, “Let’s go!”

Li Jiao stood motionless in place, casting an ingratiating look at Yang Kai. "Palace Master Yang, I've already brought you both to this place. As for what comes next... Hey, hold on—a gentleman uses words, not hands! I'm still a—"

Before he could finish, Zhu Lie grabbed him and tossed him directly into the Flowing Cloud Shuttle, leaving him with a face full of bitterness and resentment.

He didn’t know why this Dragon Clan member was still unwilling to let him go. He had already completed his task by bringing Yang Kai and Zhu Lie to this place. What happened next should have nothing to do with him—he should be able to leave this wretched place and return to Li Dragon Palace. Yet, things never went as one wished.

As a Third-Stage Emperor Realm master, it had been many years since he’d tasted such powerlessness. He secretly resolved that if he survived this trip, he would never deal with the Dragon Clan again. They were simply too overbearing.

The Flowing Cloud Shuttle tore through the sky, but its speed grew slower and slower.

After Zhu Lie performed his secret art, he became very weak, so Yang Kai took the blood coral and, relying on the guidance of the drifting red light, searched for Zhu Qing.

They had only gone less than two thousand li deeper into the Frozen Earth when, mid-flight, the Flowing Cloud Shuttle suddenly shook violently and then plunged downward.

Yang Kai was startled, thinking they were under attack, but he quickly realized what had happened. He kicked Li Jiao out and called out to Zhu Lie, who was meditating to recover.

Immediately after, he put away the Flowing Cloud Shuttle and hovered in the air.

A biting cold wind blew, enveloping them in chill so intense it seemed it could freeze even one’s Divine Soul.

In such a perilous environment, even experts who cultivated ice laws would likely be unable to remain for long.

Li Jiao shivered as he flew over, looking gloomy. “What happened?”

Hiding in the Flowing Cloud Shuttle earlier had provided some insulation, but now, directly exposed to the Frozen Earth, he could clearly feel his vitality slowly draining away. Though it wouldn’t be fatal for a while, staying here too long would surely shorten his life.

“It’s nothing. From here on, we’ll have to walk ourselves,” Yang Kai said, glancing at Zhu Lie.

For a flying Imperial Treasure like the Flowing Cloud Shuttle to be frozen and unable to function properly in this place—if he hadn’t experienced it himself, he wouldn’t have believed it.

Zhu Lie’s expression didn’t change, but Li Jiao’s face turned pale. After hesitating a moment, he said, “Gentlemen, I have an unreasonable request!”

Zhu Lie looked at him, his expression colder than the Frozen Earth itself. Li Jiao’s words changed at once: “I can bring up the rear.”

“That’s for the best.” Yang Kai smiled slightly, holding the dragon blood coral in one hand and leading the way forward.

Zhu Lie reached out, grabbed Li Jiao, and tossed him forward, making him follow closely behind Yang Kai, while he himself strolled along at the rear.

Flying through the Frozen Earth was by no means pleasant, but soon Yang Kai discovered something interesting: the higher he flew, the greater the consumption, and the more terrifying the life-extinguishing cold became.

Realizing this, he immediately flew lower, skimming just above the ground, and sure enough, the resistance was much less.

However, this approach came at a cost—their speed slowed considerably, though in exchange, they were relatively safer.

After a whole day, the three of them had only advanced a few hundred li. For a group of Emperor Realm cultivators, this was almost unbelievable.

After a day, all three were shivering, their hair and faces covered in ice. No matter how they circulated their Emperor Yuan, they couldn’t resist the cold invading their bodies.

Li Jiao wanted to leave several times, but whenever he met Zhu Lie’s gaze, all the courage he’d mustered collapsed at once. He couldn’t say a word and could only desperately keep up with Yang Kai.

He couldn’t understand it. Yang Kai was only at the First Stage of the Emperor Realm—how could he last so long? By rights, someone at the First Stage of the Emperor Realm should have frozen to death here long ago, yet among the three, he seemed to be in the best condition. Even Zhu Lie, a Dragon Clan member, couldn’t compare.

Another day passed, and Yang Kai, leading at the front, suddenly stopped and listened intently.

He vaguely sensed something.

Li Jiao, following behind, nearly bumped into him. Seeing Yang Kai’s grave expression, he nervously asked, “What is it, Palace Master Yang?”

Yang Kai’s gaze passed over Li Jiao and landed on Zhu Lie. “Something’s coming!”

Zhu Lie nodded slightly. He felt it too—something was indeed approaching. The air and cold around them carried an abnormal fluctuation.

“What is it, what is it?” Li Jiao asked, face pale. The Frozen Earth was devoid of life—what could possibly be here? But seeing how serious Yang Kai and Zhu Lie looked, he couldn’t help but worry.

“It should be that thing!” Yang Kai looked into the distance, narrowing his eyes.

Li Jiao followed his gaze and gulped.

At the horizon, a dark mass had appeared at some point, like a curtain covering the sky, rapidly sweeping toward them.

“Cold Tide!” Li Jiao seemed to recall something, and with a cry of alarm, he quickly retreated.

At the edge of life and death, he finally suppressed his fear of Zhu Lie and fled back the way they’d come, wanting only to get as far from the cold tide as possible.

Yang Kai and Zhu Lie chased after him. The fear of death overrode all else, and this time Li Jiao was actually in the lead, running faster than anyone.

“What is the Cold Tide?” Yang Kai asked as he ran.

“Heaven’s might!” Li Jiao replied grimly, cursing his parents for not giving him two more legs. “It’s the unique heaven’s might of the Frozen Earth!”
