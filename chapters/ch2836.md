## Chapter 2836 — A Grand Gift for You

This was no Sea of Consciousness; it was clearly hell.

A swarm of heedless little ghosts caught the scent of a stranger and rushed at Yang Kai, but before they could get close, a flash of his blade wiped them out completely. Shaman Chi, who was secretly observing, was startled by the sight, his gaze lingering on that long blade with a complex expression. He instinctively felt a sense of dread toward it.

What kind of shaman artifact was this? How could it possess such miraculous power?

And yet, he had actually managed to bring it into his own Sea of Consciousness. How could such a thing be possible? Where in the world could there be such a wondrous shaman artifact?

Although the Yin-Souls in his Sea of Consciousness had once been ordinary souls, he had personally nourished and refined them. They were nothing like ordinary Yin-Souls. Even if a Shaman King's Spirit Body barged in, they could swarm and devour it.

Yet, as the blade flashed a few times, Shaman Chi clearly sensed the Yin-Souls in his Sea of Consciousness growing terrified, like mice encountering a cat, not daring to come too close.

Yang Kai held the blade in one hand, swept his gaze around, and sneered, "Hiding and refusing to come out? Shaman Chi, oh Shaman Chi, you are at least a Shaman King. Don’t disappoint me by cowering in the shadows.”

Shaman Chi ignored him. No one who could cultivate to the Shaman King Realm was a fool. He still hadn’t figured out what was so strange about that shaman artifact blade, so he naturally didn’t dare to recklessly attack.

As if seeing through his fear, Yang Kai raised the long blade, flicked it with a finger, and said, "This blade is called the Soul-Severing Blade. It does not harm the flesh, only severs the Divine Soul. For you to let me break into your Sea of Consciousness is your greatest mistake, Shaman Chi. Prepare to die. Out of my boundless mercy, I’ll make it quick for you."

Not harm the flesh, only sever the Divine Soul? Shaman Chi truly didn’t want to believe such nonsense, but what he saw forced him to admit the other was right. This was by far the most sinister shaman artifact he had ever seen.

"Fine, fine, you won’t come out? Then stay hidden!" As Yang Kai sneered, he raised the Soul-Severing Blade high, poured Divine Soul power into it, and slashed down.

The massive blade light seemed as if it would tear apart this entire Sea of Consciousness. As it swept out, countless Yin-Souls in its path were instantly slain, and even Shaman Chi, hiding in the shadows, felt a sharp pain, as if he were being ripped apart.

This was his Sea of Consciousness, so whatever harm Yang Kai caused here would affect him directly. Though the injuries were minor for now, they would become difficult to bear if Yang Kai continued.

As Shaman Chi’s face turned ashen, Yang Kai was already brandishing the Soul-Severing Blade, slashing about madly, laughing maniacally as he struck, "Does it feel good? Does it? I’m asking you, does it feel good?!"

As if it could possibly feel good.

With every slash, Shaman Chi felt waves of excruciating pain, his Spirit Body trembling uncontrollably.

Realizing that hiding any longer was pointless and letting this madman continue to wreck his Sea of Consciousness would only cause him greater harm, Shaman Chi finally couldn’t hold back and roared, "Enough!"

As soon as he spoke, he appeared before Yang Kai, raised his hand, and blocked Yang Kai’s attack.

Although the Soul-Severing Blade was extremely bizarre and truly effective at damaging the Sea of Consciousness, this was his domain, and he ruled over everything here. If it came down to a real fight, the most likely outcome would be mutual destruction.

He was not without the power to fight back.

"You like hiding? Keep hiding then!" Yang Kai pointed his blade at Shaman Chi, his face full of mockery and ridicule.

"Get out!" Shaman Chi’s expression was cold as he issued the order to leave. Having an outsider invade his Sea of Consciousness was never a good thing. The Sea of Consciousness was not only the home of his Divine Soul, but also the hiding place for many of his secrets. No one wanted their Sea of Consciousness to be invaded and inspected by others.

"Beg me. Beg me and see if I’ll leave." Yang Kai grinned, his expression infuriating Shaman Chi, who felt as if a volcano was brewing in his chest, ready to erupt at any moment.

"I’ll say it again—get out!" Shaman Chi stared coldly at Yang Kai.

Yang Kai curled his lip and said, "You wanted to seize my Divine Soul, and now you want me to leave? Make up your mind—what do you really want?!"

Shaman Chi’s eyelids drooped slightly as he said coolly, "It seems you want me to drive you out by force. I hope you can withstand my wrath!"

After this brief exchange, Shaman Chi knew that he would have to act to drive this Great Shaman Master out. After all, Yang Kai had finally seized the initiative and would not give it up easily. Shaman Chi also didn’t want to waste more words; he resolved that, even if he got hurt, he would expel him. Otherwise, with that bizarre shaman artifact lingering here, Shaman Chi would always feel as if his life hung by a thread, completely ill at ease.

As soon as he finished speaking, Shaman Chi’s figure dispersed, as if merging with the world itself.

Yang Kai’s expression grew solemn, knowing Shaman Chi was about to go all out.

Space suddenly solidified, and in the next instant, a tremendous repulsive force crashed toward him. This was the manifestation of Shaman Chi’s power as the master of this Sea of Consciousness.

Invading another person’s Sea of Consciousness could indeed allow one to harm them at any time, but fighting within someone else’s domain placed one at a disadvantage. Because of this, such tactics were rarely used; only when there was a considerable gap in strength would the stronger party dare to invade the weaker one’s Sea of Consciousness.

But then again, if the gap was truly that great, there would be no need for such methods to kill the enemy.

In a flash, Yang Kai felt the surrounding pressure surge, as if he was about to be squeezed out of this world.

He forcibly steadied himself, but no matter what, he couldn’t remain any longer.

Just as he was about to be expelled by Shaman Chi, Yang Kai suddenly moved. A sharp light flashed in his eyes, the Soul-Severing Blade buzzed, erupting in a burst of radiance, and he slashed forward: "Here’s a grand gift for you—hope you like it!"

"How is this possible..." Shaman Chi’s voice rang out, shocked and furious. The power of that slash had suddenly more than doubled compared to before.

Caught off guard, Shaman Chi failed to defend himself. His defenses were instantly broken, his Divine Soul was severed, and he was left in unbearable agony.

In a blur, Yang Kai rushed out of Shaman Chi’s Sea of Consciousness. The next moment, a pitch-black light enveloped him. When the darkness dispersed, Shaman Chi was nowhere to be seen.

Shaman Chi had actually fled.

"Smart move!" Yang Kai snorted coldly, not recklessly pursuing. This time, he was only able to drive Shaman Chi back by unleashing a secret Divine Soul technique at the last moment.

Shaman Chi had clearly sensed things were going badly, so he made a feint and used the opportunity to escape.

If he had stayed, the outcome would not have been good for him.

Yang Kai sighed, sensing the location of his physical body. He dared not leave his Spirit Body exposed any longer, so with a flicker, he merged body and soul.

When he opened his eyes, he felt utterly drained. Looking up, he saw that he was cradled in Die’s arms. It seemed that when his Divine Soul had left his body earlier, Die had noticed something amiss and had come specifically to guard his physical body.

"How are you?" Die asked with concern.

Yang Kai shook his head, tried to stand, but felt dizzy and collapsed back into Die’s embrace, forcing a bitter smile. "I won’t die."

Die gazed at Yang Kai with a complicated look, feeling a sense of unreality. As a Lower-Level Great Shaman Master, he had managed to repel and seriously wound a Shaman King. Such a feat was nothing short of miraculous. Though he had paid a price, it was clearly not too severe.

After just a month, he had become a Great Shaman Master.

How had he cultivated?

Die was full of doubts.

"What about the villagers?" Yang Kai asked after resting for a while and regaining some strength.

"They’re all safe," Die replied. Earlier, she had led the villagers of Cangnan Village away through a passage. Once everyone was safe, she returned to check on Yang Kai, arriving just in time to see his Divine Soul leave his body, so she immediately protected his physical form.

"Call them back. It should be safe now," Yang Kai breathed a sigh of relief.

Even with a Shaman King from the Bone-Eater Tribe personally taking action, he had managed to injure and drive him off. The Bone-Eater Tribe likely wouldn’t make any moves for a while, unless an even stronger Shaman King or a Shaman Saint came.

But a Shaman Saint probably wouldn’t bother with such matters, and even if another Shaman King from the Bone-Eater Tribe came, it might not help. After revealing his trump cards, Shaman Chi’s strength was on par with an Upper-Level Shaman King, yet he still couldn’t do anything to Yang Kai. What could the others do?

Besides, as long as Yang Kai had some time to recover, he would only grow stronger. Next time he faced a Shaman King, it wouldn’t be such a hard fight.

As Yang Kai was speaking with Die, he suddenly frowned and looked in a certain direction.

Die clearly sensed something as well. Following his gaze, her face turned wary. "Someone else is coming!"

Yang Kai’s expression darkened as he grew alert.

Cangnan Village was just a small, isolated place, rarely visited by outsiders. Shaman Chi had only found it thanks to Chi’s guidance.

And Yang Kai had just driven off Shaman Chi, but now another person appeared. There was no guarantee this one wasn’t with Shaman Chi. If it was another Shaman King, things would be dire.

Die clearly thought of this as well, her pretty face turning pale.

But soon, she heard Yang Kai let out a surprised sound.

"What is it?" Die turned to look at him.

"It’s just a Shaman Master. No need to worry—maybe he’s just passing by."

Hearing this, Die couldn’t help but breathe a sigh of relief.

But that Shaman Master was clearly not just passing by. In no time, he flew straight toward Yang Kai and Die.

Moments later, a sturdy-looking member of the Barbarian Tribe appeared in midair before Yang Kai.

"Was there a fight here just now?" The Shaman Master glanced around from above and asked.

The traces of battle remained. Though he couldn’t tell how strong the combatants had been, the lingering force left him shocked.

This was clearly a battle of the highest level, far beyond his reach. Even brushing against it might have been enough to destroy him.

With that thought, he quickly looked at Yang Kai and Die.

Yang Kai looked extremely weak, his shamanic power flickering, making it easy for the newcomer to discern his cultivation.

"Great Shaman Master?" The man was startled and hurriedly descended from the sky.
