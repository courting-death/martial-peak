## Chapter 2898 — Pillar That Holds Up the Sky

"Heh heh." Yang Kai gave a bitter laugh, raising his head to look at the mountain-like Mo Duo before him. "You’ve won. So, what will you do now? Kill me? Then hurry up and do it, or else you might get another surprise in a moment."

Mo Duo’s expression was cold as he lifted a foot and stomped down toward Yang Kai.

For an enemy who had caused him so much trouble, such a death was far too easy. But Mo Duo had already wasted too much time on Yang Kai; he had no time to torment him further and could only kill him first and deal with the rest later.

The massive foot came down, splitting the earth for a hundred li, leaving a web of crisscrossing ravines.

Mo Duo frowned slightly. When he lifted his foot, a huge footprint was left on the ground, but there was no trace of blood or flesh.

Just now, he sensed a ripple of space. That Shaman Niu had clearly escaped again, and this time even more stealthily. Mo Duo glanced around but failed to spot his whereabouts right away.

Shaking his massive head, Mo Duo strode off in the direction of the Divine Tree.

He no longer paid attention to that ant-like existence. Having been struck by his divine ability and already gravely wounded, that ant could no longer pose any threat to him. For now, eradicating the giant tree was the most important task.

His enormous body, thirty zhang tall, rampaged across the battlefield. Every living being in his path was crushed into meat paste beneath his feet, whether they were from the Barbarian Tribe or the demons—it made no difference to Mo Duo. With every step, members of both races died under his trampling.

At first, his pace was not fast, but as he continued, he grew swifter and swifter, soon breaking into a run, moving like the wind and lightning.

Dong dong dong dong…

The sound, like war drums being struck, echoed in all directions, making everyone’s hearts pound violently in their chests.

Wherever Mo Duo passed, both the Barbarian Tribe and the demons retreated in fear. No one dared to stand in his way. The corpses beneath his feet were proof enough.

Fewer than eight thousand of Shaman Niu’s tribe remained, all now gathered around the Divine Tree, relying on its power to resist the demons’ assault. And how many demons surrounded them? More than eight hundred thousand! With such a disparity in numbers, if not for the Divine Tree’s protection, the remnants of Shaman Niu’s tribe would have long since been wiped out.

As Mo Duo rushed toward the Divine Tree, all eyes turned toward him. Every member of Shaman Niu's tribe felt despair wash over them.

The six Demon Kings who were still barely holding on were the first to flee. Although Yang Kai had previously ordered them to defend to the death, they simply lacked the courage to face a Demon Saint head-on—especially one in a rage.

The Demon Kings fled, but the Barbarians could not. The Divine Tree was their only hope of sealing the passage between the two worlds. It had not been easy to hold out until now—how could they give up halfway?

The defensive circle of Shaman Niu’s tribe shrank again, all retreating beneath the roots of the tree.

Low chanting rose from all around the trunk. The Shaman Masters of the Frost Snow Tribe began to act, borrowing the power of the Divine Tree to resist the Demon Saint Mo Duo’s assault.

Suddenly, a layer of emerald light appeared, enveloping an area fifty zhang in radius, forming a thick barrier. The barrier looked incomparably solid, keeping all the demons outside. Even the secret arts unleashed by the Demon Commanders, Demon Generals, and Demon Kings could only create faint ripples on its surface, unable to break through at all.

This gave the Barbarians a glimmer of hope.

But that hope quickly turned to terror.

Mo Duo crashed into it, his thirty-zhang-tall body like a small mountain, slamming fiercely into the emerald barrier.

With a thunderous boom, the barrier shook violently. Every Barbarian’s heart leapt into their throat as they looked up at the point where Mo Duo collided with the barrier, forgetting to breathe, their faces full of worry.

They feared the barrier would break. If it did, no one would be able to stop the destructive power of a Demon Saint, and the Divine Tree might be destroyed here. The Barbarians’ last hope would vanish like smoke.

The barrier still held, but its color had clearly dimmed by a third.

The Barbarians first breathed a sigh of relief, but then grew even more anxious.

The barrier could withstand one collision from Mo Duo, but what about two? Three? Countless times? Sooner or later, Mo Duo would break through this shield and bring his thirty-zhang-tall body crashing down upon the Divine Tree.

As everyone in Shaman Niu’s tribe watched anxiously, Mo Duo took a few steps back, cocked his head to look at the emerald barrier, and then slowly drew back a fist. In the next instant, like a fully drawn bowstring, his fist shot forward like a dragon.

Boom…

The barrier shook again, its color dimming further.

Before the barrier could stabilize, Mo Duo’s second punch landed.

Boom boom boom…

Each time his house-sized fist struck the barrier, it unleashed an unparalleled shockwave. Everyone in Shaman Niu’s tribe felt their blood surge and their chests tighten, as if Mo Duo were pounding not the barrier, but their own chests.

Crack…

At last, a crisp sound reached everyone’s ears. All the Barbarians’ faces changed as they clearly saw a crack appear on the barrier.

Mo Duo grinned silently, as if pleased with his handiwork. His final punch landed squarely on the crack.

With a crash, as if a mirror had shattered, the barrier provided by the Divine Tree finally succumbed to the Demon Saint’s repeated assaults and collapsed.

Without the barrier’s protection, all the Barbarians were exposed to the Demon Saint’s gaze. No one could withstand even his breath; Mo Duo only needed to blow on them to wipe them out completely.

Yet Mo Duo paid no attention to these ant-like beings. As a Demon Saint, he had no need to care about the lives of these rabble. His only target was the Divine Tree. Striding forward, Mo Duo charged straight at the trunk.

Amid the Barbarians’ terrified cries, the thirty-zhang-tall body of the Demon Saint slammed into the trunk. With a thunderous rumble, the Divine Tree shook violently, countless green leaves drifting down, turning to withered husks before they even touched the ground.

At the same time, the Shaman Masters of the Frost Snow Tribe hidden within the Divine Tree were struck as if by lightning. Countless muffled groans rang out, and more than a dozen shamans died instantly from the impact.

The Shaman Masters of the Frost Snow Tribe had always remained hidden within the tree's body. Though they could rely on the Divine Tree's protection and draw upon its power, they were equally bound to its fate—their lives intertwined with its own.

This was a secret art developed by the Frost Snow Tribe over tens of thousands of years of coexistence with the Divine Tree.

The force of Mo Duo’s collision was dispersed throughout the trunk, and naturally, some shamans could not withstand it and died on the spot.

Mo Duo was extremely dissatisfied with this attack, a trace of anger appearing on his face. It seemed that, in his view, this blow should have toppled the giant tree, and merely knocking down some leaves was far from enough.

But before he could strike again, the Frost Snow Tribe’s counterattack arrived.

From the top of the tree, one after another, soft branches descended, transforming into long whips that lashed through the air at Mo Duo.

Previously, the Shaman Masters of the Frost Snow Tribe had relied on this method to help Shaman Niu’s tribe hold out under the siege of hundreds of thousands of demons, killing countless enemies.

But such attacks were clearly too weak against a Demon Saint. Those seemingly world-shattering whips struck his body but had little effect, only further enraging Mo Duo.

He grabbed branch after branch, yanking them off with force. Dozens were snapped in succession. Growing bored with these attacks, Mo Duo ignored them and continued to slam his body against the trunk with all his might.

The thunderous crashes were endless. Each time he struck, the Divine Tree shuddered violently, more leaves fell, and more Shaman Masters of the Frost Snow Tribe died.

Yet no matter how hard Mo Duo tried, the Divine Tree still stood tall like a pillar that held up the sky, unshakable.

At last, Mo Duo flew into a rage. He stopped ramming the tree and instead began climbing up the trunk with both hands and feet, wreaking destruction as he ascended. One branch after another was torn off and thrown to the ground.

The Shaman Masters of the Frost Snow Tribe could no longer stop his rampage. Wherever Mo Duo passed, only the bare main trunk of the Divine Tree remained, all the branches destroyed.

The surrounding demons, seeing this, followed suit. Those who could fly soared up to higher branches to destroy whatever they could, while those who could not climbed the trunk, biting and hacking with weapons, using every means at their disposal.

The Divine Tree, the guardian spirit of the Frost Snow Tribe for tens of thousands of years, was instantly swarmed by demons. It looked like a candied hawthorn stick stuck in the ground, attracting countless ants. The scene from above was spectacular.

Now, the Shaman Masters of the Frost Snow Tribe could do very little. Even with the Divine Tree’s power, they could only sweep away some ordinary demons and provide a bit of protection for the remnants of Shaman Niu’s tribe below.

In a sense, they had already done enough. Without them, Shaman Niu’s tribe would have been wiped out long ago, and the Divine Tree would not have survived until now.

But this was their limit.

Defeat was imminent, and every Barbarian was filled with sorrow.

The twin sisters Yu and Lu scanned the battlefield, hoping to find that figure. Though they knew that person’s strength could not change the situation, they had no other hope left.

But they could not find him. After being gravely wounded, that figure had not appeared again—perhaps he was already dead, or perhaps he was hiding somewhere.

They would rather he truly be dead than believe he had fled the battlefield.

Clang…

A deafening boom suddenly erupted from the sky. In its wake, the Divine Tree shuddered violently, and countless demons who had been climbing its trunk came cascading down like rain, their forms tumbling through the air. In an instant, the massive trunk became noticeably clearer.

Hearing this familiar sound, Yu and Lu’s spirits were lifted. They looked up together. Though the huge canopy blocked their view, making it impossible to see anything, they knew—the figure they were searching for had returned.
