## Chapter 2856 — Slaughter Feast

Flesh flew in all directions, blood gushed like a river, and the battlefield seemed to have become a massive meat grinder, shredding every living being that entered it.

One after another, the demonized members of the Barbarian Tribe fell.

Though they too were born of the Barbarian Tribe, strong and robust, they had not received any Shamanic Art blessings. After being demonized, they lost all independent thought. Now, they were nothing more than humanoid weapons consumed by slaughter.

How could such beings possibly stand against a true Barbarian Tribe force, well-equipped, brimming with killing intent, and empowered by Shamanic Arts?

So, as soon as the two armies clashed, the Demon Race suffered heavy casualties, while the warriors of Shaman Niu's tribe rampaged like bulldozers, leaving no survivors in their wake.

Ten regular squads, each with around two hundred fifty to three hundred members, under the command of their respective captains, plunged into the Demon Race's ranks like ten sharp nails, instantly throwing everything into chaos.

Amidst the clash of weapons, heads flew high into the sky one after another, while sturdy bodies slowly crumpled to the ground. In their wide-open eyes lingered a trace of affection for this land, yet more prevalent was utter bewilderment.

The demonized tribesmen died without ever knowing what they were doing, why they blocked the path of their former kin, or why they raised their blades against them.

Like crushing dry weeds, in less than the time it takes for an incense stick to burn, the Demon Race vanguard was shattered.

"A bunch of trash!" Seated at the rear of the army, Shaya showed not the slightest panic. She remained dignified, her beautiful eyes coldly watching the deaths of the demon soldiers ahead, her expression utterly unmoved.

This wasn’t her first time clashing with the Barbarian Tribe.

Previously, when the four great Barbarian Tribes were destroyed, it was always under the leadership of the Demon Saints. The Demon Race was unstoppable, meeting no real resistance wherever they went, easily wiping out the four tribes. But now, with her in command, she finally realized how troublesome these tribesmen could be.

In terms of individual strength, these tribesmen were about the same as the demons, but with the blessings of those damned shamans, the difference was clear.

Those various Shamanic Arts truly were wondrous.

Still, she didn't care. They were just some demonized slaves—no matter how many died, it didn't pain her. The real key to this battle wasn't those demon soldiers, nor even these two armies, but rather a Shaman Master entangled by Green Demon Poison.

Gazing into the distance, her eyes pierced space. Shaya saw that green-skinned fellow, the corner of her mouth curling up. He should be reaching his limit by now, shouldn't he?

"Scatter!"

In the midst of the battlefield, Ah Hu roared, his voice soaring to the heavens.

Hearing the shout, the Barbarian Tribe warriors all halted in unison, turned, and began to spread out in an orderly fashion.

The reason was that a group of short, round, red-skinned creatures had suddenly sprung out from behind the Demon vanguard. Each of them wore a ferocious, frenzied expression, as if pumped full of blood, their bodies bouncing across the ground like lumps of flesh. With every leap, they covered more than a dozen zhang, fearlessly charging straight toward the Barbarian Tribe.

Red Demons!

Thanks to the knowledge Yu and Lu had drawn from the Shaman God Hall, the Barbarian Tribe warriors immediately responded correctly upon seeing these red-skinned creatures. Otherwise, if they'd charged in unaware, the casualties would have been disastrous.

Each regular squad skirted around the red-skinned demons and pressed forward.

The Red Demons, relentless in their pursuit, also scattered, bouncing toward the nearest groups.

Whoosh, whoosh, whoosh…

From high above, volleys of sharp arrows rained down, whistling through the air, carrying deadly intent.

Each arrow struck a Red Demon with perfect accuracy. The wounded creatures would then swell, their already crimson skin turning even bloodier as a dangerous aura surged from within. Their round bodies would suddenly expand several times over, and with a series of booming explosions, they burst apart, as if tearing holes in the very void.

"Good grief…" Yang Kai watched the fireworks blooming in the sky, unable to help wiping the cold sweat from his brow. The power of these exploding demons was truly no joke. One or two might not be much, but if a hundred or so went off together, even a Shaman King would suffer.

The Demon Race's hundred clans truly could produce some peculiar specimens. The Green Demons were one example, and the Red Demons were no different.

On the other side, Shaya's triumphant smile suddenly froze. Her beautiful eyes turned icy as she glared at the airborne cavalry, gritting her teeth, "Damn it!"

According to what she knew, these tribesmen should have had no knowledge of their side’s forces at all. Yet when the Red Demons moved out just now, they reacted with perfect precision, rendering the ambush utterly useless and even costing them quite a number of casualties for nothing.

The whistling of arrows continued. The tribesmen riding giant eagles were all expert archers—even in midair, their aim never faltered. Every arrow felled a leaping Red Demon, saving their own warriors' lives.

"Kill them!" Shaya waved her hand.

Clusters of black mist behind her suddenly vanished into the void, disappearing from sight.

Moments later, beside a Sky Cavalry soldier, a short figure silently appeared, materializing in midair without warning. The black dagger in his hand slashed straight for the unsuspecting Sky Cavalryman's neck.

The dagger was razor-sharp—even Yang Kai had been lightly wounded by it before. If these cavalrymen were struck, their heads would surely be severed.

The Sky Cavalryman, seated atop his giant eagle and drawing his bow, showed no reaction. His hawk-like eyes remained locked on a Red Demon below as he loosed his arrow.

Just as the demon assassin was about to succeed, another dagger suddenly appeared from the void, skillfully and precisely blocking the attacker's blade before it could reach the cavalryman's neck.

With a clang, the two daggers clashed. The demon assassin frowned and retreated warily.

He was a born killer and assassin, only striking when absolutely certain. For someone to block his attack was a surprise.

Looking up, he saw that beside the Sky Cavalry soldier, a petite young girl had suddenly appeared. Her slender figure seemed fragile, her long hair streaming like a waterfall in the wind. Strangely, she held a dagger in each hand—one forward, one reversed—her stance seeming so frail that a single gust could topple her.

Dual daggers? The demon assassin frowned.

As an assassin, he knew how difficult it was to wield two daggers. She was a fellow assassin, and an extremely skilled one at that. He couldn't help but lick his lips, excitement welling up at the thought of testing his skills against hers.

"Slaughter…" the girl suddenly spoke, her voice soft and low. She tilted her head back slightly, her expression devout, as if praying to some unknown deity.

"What?" The demon assassin was stunned.

"Feast!"

As the words fell, the girl danced like a butterfly, her movements graceful and mesmerizing, making anyone who saw her unconsciously lose themselves in the beauty of her dance.

Black hair flew through the air, cold light scattering in all directions.

The demon assassin had no time to appreciate it. He only felt a chill run through his body, his limbs trembling. As an assassin, he had a strong mind, and he was among the best. Normally, nothing could shake him.

But at this moment, he felt as if he were witnessing something truly shocking. Amidst his terror, he even felt a sense of fulfillment, as if he could die content after seeing such mastery.

He had witnessed the ultimate essence of assassination.

Blades of cold light flashed densely in midair, each one vanishing in an instant, covering a range of thirty zhang.

The eerie sound of slicing filled the air, and strange sprays of blood drifted from the void.

When the girl finally stopped, beads of blood dripped from the daggers in her hands.

Thud, thud, thud…

Blood spurted wildly in the void.

One after another, the hidden demon assassins suddenly appeared, each with a dazed look, as if they couldn't believe someone had found them in the void.

Each demon assassin bore only a single wound, but every wound was fatal.

Their eyes full of disbelief, the short demon assassins tumbled headfirst from the sky, crashing to the ground in bloody heaps, too gruesome to behold.

"You… what's your name?" The demon assassin facing the girl asked calmly.

The girl glanced at him and replied just as calmly, "Die."

The demon assassin murmured the name, as if to remember it. The next moment, a jet of blood shot from his chest, and he plummeted to the ground, his life fading fast.

Although Die's display had shocked the demon assassins and saved many Sky Cavalrymen, more demon assassins remained hidden in the void.

A beam of light shot up from below, forming a halo that enveloped all the Sky Cavalry.

In the next instant, the eyes of the Sky Cavalrymen suddenly brightened, as if a torch had ignited within, its light exposing every shadow hidden in the darkness, leaving nowhere to hide.

Eagle Eye Art!

With the support of the Shaman Masters, the Sky Cavalrymen immediately locked onto the demon assassins lurking around them, loosing arrows that reaped their lives before they could react.

The assassins, unwilling to sit and wait for death, launched their attacks the moment they were exposed.

The Sky Cavalrymen, riding their giant eagles, deftly dodged the assassins' attacks in midair, rendering their ambushes futile.

Regaining their composure, Yu and Lu chanted in unison. After three breaths, a bolt of lightning like a rampaging serpent shot forth from the twins, crackling and snapping as it snaked forward.
