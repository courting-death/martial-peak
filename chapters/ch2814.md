## Chapter 2814 — This Shaman God Is Not Normal

In a mountain hollow, Yang Kai and Yu’s group quietly lay in ambush.

Yu pointed ahead and said, “The Condensed Snow Flower we need to collect is just up there, and those Bone-Eating Wolves are there too.”

Yang Kai glanced over and saw clear traces of battle, with large pools of bright red blood on the ground and messy footprints scattered across the snow.

“Shaman Niu, in a moment, let’s cast Shamanic Arts together for my tribesmen. You take the left, I’ll take the right. There are six Bone-Eating Wolves, but they’re not hard to deal with as long as we’re careful not to get bitten. Last time, we…” Yu stared ahead, seriously discussing the battle plan with Yang Kai, but halfway through, she suddenly sensed something was wrong. Turning her head, she found that Shaman Niu, who had been beside her, had vanished without a trace.

“Where did he go?” Yu exclaimed, panic coloring her face as she asked her tribesmen.

The tribesmen seemed to only now realize it, looking around in confusion. One barbarian warrior’s eyes widened as he pointed ahead and stammered, “He, he, he…”

Yu followed his finger and her expression changed. “That fool!”

Shaman Niu was swaggering straight over. If he startled those Bone-Eating Wolves, they’d be plunged into a bitter fight. She had hoped to cooperate, ambush, and kill two first.

Now, it seemed the battle plan was impossible to carry out.

Just as Yu was about to call Yang Kai back, she saw him suddenly stop in the hollow, spread his arms wide as if to embrace the whole world, take a deep breath, and shout with exuberant warmth, “My dear friends, here I come!”

The echo reverberated through the hollow, shaking snow from the trees.

The Bone-Eating Wolves, sensing the presence of strangers, emerged from their hiding places, agile and light, baring their fangs, drool dripping from their mouths, their eyes gleaming with ferocity.

The always serene Yu suddenly twitched her lips.

“My Lady, what do we do?” a tribesman asked.

“Retreat!” Yu decided instantly.

This Shaman Niu was mentally unstable—how had she not noticed before? If she’d known, she would never have come with him. Now, with no preparation, he’d provoked all six Bone-Eating Wolves. Even as a Shaman Warrior, he was doomed. Though he’d saved her tribesmen, as their leader, Yu couldn’t follow him into madness.

Now, they could only leave first. As for the debt of gratitude, once her tribesmen recovered, they’d avenge him, giving him a proper send-off.

The group moved like the wind. As soon as Yu finished speaking, several barbarian warriors followed her lead to retreat.

As she looked back, Yu cast a pitying glance toward Yang Kai.

After the Bone-Eating Wolves appeared, they wasted no time, rushing at him like the wind, their gaping jaws wide enough to swallow a whole sheep.

Then Yu witnessed an astonishing scene.

Yang Kai, acting completely out of sorts, burst into hearty laughter. He threw punches left and right, left and right, each blow landing with wild abandon…

Blood blossomed in midair, accompanied by the sound of skulls cracking and the Bone-Eating Wolves’ brief, urgent whimpers.

In just a moment, four Bone-Eating Wolves were sent flying, their heads exploding in midair.

Yu was stunned, standing dumbly as if immobilized by an invisible Shaman King’s spell, her mouth agape, eyes nearly bulging out.

“My Lady!” The retreating barbarian warriors suddenly realized their Shaman hadn’t followed and quickly turned back.

The next moment, they too were frozen in place.

What had just happened? Why were four Bone-Eating Wolves lying dead on the ground? Why had every one of their heads burst apart? In the brief instant they had turned away, what exactly had taken place?

On the battlefield, the death of their four companions didn’t make the Bone-Eating Wolves retreat. Instead, it enraged them. The remaining two charged, leaping high, one left, one right, aiming to bite Yang Kai, their massive bodies like cannonballs.

Yang Kai swung a fist, smashing the left wolf’s skull, brain matter splattering, killing it instantly. But his right arm was bitten hard by the other wolf.

With a crunch, something broke.

Before Yang Kai could react, an icicle shot over, piercing the last Bone-Eating Wolf’s eye socket, sinking more than a foot deep.

Yu had finally recovered, supporting from afar with an icicle spell. A simple strike revealed her strength and rich combat experience as an Upper-Level Shaman Warrior.

The Bone-Eating Wolf whimpered, releasing Yang Kai’s arm.

Yang Kai stepped forward, swung his fists, and killed it with two blows.

He took a light breath, a look of disdain on his face.

“You were bitten, hurry and expel the poison!” Yu rushed over, not bothering to ask how Yang Kai had performed such a miracle, and reminded him.

But as soon as she spoke, she was stunned—Yang Kai’s bitten right arm was completely unharmed!

How could that be? She’d clearly seen him bitten. With the Bone-Eating Wolf’s terrifying strength, how could he resist? Thinking back, that crunch wasn’t the sound of his bone breaking, but… the Shamanic Art Shield being bitten through.

So that’s it!

He must have cast a Shamanic Art Shield at the moment he was bitten—such quick reflexes!

“Don’t come over!” Yang Kai suddenly shouted, excitement flashing in his eyes as he looked deeper into the hollow, licking his lips. “There’s a big one still.”

“What?” Yu was confused, but as she looked, she instantly felt cold all over, her hands and feet numb, and cried out, “Bone-Eating Wolf King!”

Over there, a Bone-Eating Wolf larger than the others walked over, its powerful aura making even Yu feel suffocated.

How could there be a Bone-Eating Wolf King here?

Wolves are pack creatures—whether wild beasts or barbaric beasts, this holds true. Even though six Bone-Eating Wolves have gathered here, such a small group could never give rise to a Wolf King!

But Yu quickly understood—the Bone-Eating Wolf King seemed to have suffered heavy injuries, its body covered in wounds, some fresh, likely from the past month.

This Wolf King had been defeated in a struggle within its pack and exiled here.

A defeated Wolf King is no longer the king; a younger, stronger Wolf King takes its place.

But even so, a Bone-Eating Wolf King is not something a Shaman Warrior can withstand. It’s a Barbaric Beast comparable to a Shaman Master! Though it’s not fully recovered, its might remains.

“Run!” Yu shouted to Yang Kai, deeply regretting not retreating sooner.

She had wanted to leave, but seeing the miracle, she couldn’t help but return. Who knew she’d step right into a quagmire.

Now, it was truly over. They’d all die here.

She couldn’t blame Yang Kai anymore—only herself for not checking the situation beforehand, failing to notice a Bone-Eating Wolf King was hiding here.

The Wolf King’s pressure was terrifying. Yu felt her body trembling.

Facing such a terrifying presence, Shaman Niu wore only a mocking, disdainful expression. He stretched his arms and legs, making a series of crackling sounds, leaving the onlookers speechless.

This display clearly enraged the Bone-Eating Wolf King. It opened its mouth, and a ball of green venom shot toward Yang Kai like an arrow.

Yang Kai raised his hand, the air temperature suddenly rising. A fireball, as large as a basin, flew out to meet it.

With a boom, the fireball and poison collided in midair, erupting in sparks and splattering venom. As it hit the ground, it sizzled, melting the snow into huge holes at an alarming rate.

While Yu’s group was stunned, Yang Kai seized the opportunity, charging at the Wolf King for a fierce and thrilling melee.

The barbarian people of the Frost Snow Tribe were dumbfounded.

Yu’s eyes were glazed.

Is this really a Shaman? No matter the opponent, he always charges in fearlessly!

He’d done it with the six Bone-Eating Wolves, and now with the Bone-Eating Wolf King!

How could such a Shaman exist? Yu felt her worldview collapsing. Decades of cultivation and beliefs crumbled in an instant. She’d never imagined a Shaman could fight so brutally, outshining even the tribe’s strongest warriors.

It was clear that Shaman Niu’s close-quarters combat skills were exceptionally refined—this was far from his first time fighting like this. Not only did he possess the courage and meticulousness found only in the tribe’s mightiest warriors, but he also wielded the unique powers of a Shaman. 

In melee, he casually used simple, practical Shamanic Arts, which proved invaluable at critical moments. For a time, he fought the Wolf King, a match for a Shaman Master, to a standstill.

“My Lady… what do we do now?”

It wasn’t the first time the Frost Snow Tribe’s people had asked Yu this, but everything before them was beyond their understanding, leaving them unable to judge.

Yu hesitated only for a moment before decisively saying, “Help!”

As soon as she spoke, she began chanting in an ancient, rough language, casting Bloodlust, Agility, Bone Armor, and other Shamanic Arts on several barbarian warriors. Their bodies swelled, eyes reddened, madness mixed with calm. After exchanging glances, they moved toward the battlefield in perfect sync.

Yu took a deep breath, raised her hand, and a giant icicle appeared before her eyes.

In this frigid environment, her ice-type Shamanic Arts received the blessing of heaven and earth, becoming more powerful and easier to cast.

With a whoosh, the giant icicle trembled, vanished like lightning, and reappeared, striking the Bone-Eating Wolf King locked in combat with Yang Kai, piercing deep into its flesh.

The Wolf King roared, shook its body, and shattered the icicle.

At that moment, the barbarian warriors flanked it, swinging their weapons fiercely at the Wolf King, each blow powerful and mighty.
