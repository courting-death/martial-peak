## Chapter 2854 — The Poison of the Green Demon

As Shaya found her footing, a Sand Demon suddenly emerged from underground, grabbed the wounded Shaya, and instantly burrowed away with her. In the blink of an eye, they vanished without a trace.

By the time Yang Kai lunged forward again, there was no sign of the enemy left.

He released his Divine Sense, but could only sense Shaya’s aura fleeing underground at great speed, delving deeper and deeper until it escaped the range of his perception, leaving him unable to pursue.

The abilities of these Sand Demons were truly troublesome, and Yang Kai couldn’t help but frown. Fortunately, these Sand Demons seemed rather dim-witted; otherwise, with just this special ability, the Barbarian Tribe would be at a complete loss.

With Shaya gone and Yang Kai unable to pursue her, rumbling sounds echoed from all directions. Clearly, the other demons had noticed the commotion, or perhaps received Shaya’s orders to surround this place, intending to capture Yang Kai with sheer numbers.

In the darkness, clusters of shadowy figures flickered in and out of sight. All were from the same clan as the short demon from before, skilled in stealth and assassination.

But before they could finish preparing, Yang Kai let out a fierce laugh and, like a tiger descending the mountain, charged into the enemy ranks. Everywhere he passed, he swept all before him, blood mist bursting in the air like blooming blood flowers—dazzling and cruel.

The shadows panicked and quickly retreated into hiding.

Meanwhile, tall demon warriors rushed out from the surrounding tunnels, raising their weapons and launching a fearless assault on Yang Kai.

Yang Kai stood his ground, summoned the Myriad Sword, and swept it with disdain. None could withstand even a single blow; any demon who dared approach was cut in two, blood and entrails spilling everywhere, filling the cave with a pungent stench of blood.

After a fierce battle, Yang Kai remained unscathed, while the demons suffered over a hundred casualties.

All the demons realized something was wrong. The man before them was not someone they could contend with. No matter how reckless they were, none dared approach. They simply stood in place, faces full of terror, warily watching Yang Kai.

“Are you coming or not?” Yang Kai tilted his broad sword and grinned. “If you’re not coming, then I will!”

The demons gave no answer.

Yang Kai moved at once, his figure flickering as he darted around the cave like a gust of wind. When he returned to his original spot, the Myriad Sword was dripping with fresh blood.

One by one, figures collapsed to the ground, silent and still.

Yang Kai sneered, “A bunch of trash!”

As soon as he finished speaking, his expression suddenly changed. A green hue appeared on his cheek, and he cried out, “You wretch, you actually used poison. Shameless!”

The green color seemed almost alive, spreading from Yang Kai’s face to the rest of his body in an instant. In the blink of an eye, he was covered in a green sheen, even his head glowing with green light.

Yang Kai was so angry his nose was crooked.

“Hahahaha!” Shaya’s crazed laughter echoed through the cave, her voice drifting unpredictably. No one knew where she was hiding, and even with Yang Kai’s powerful Divine Sense, he couldn’t pinpoint her. “The Poison of the Green Demon may not be the strongest, but it’s the most insidious and difficult to guard against among my demon race’s hundred clans. Junior Brother, you’re dead for sure! Maybe you can beg me to let you live as my slave.”

Yang Kai frowned, recalling that among the demons he’d just killed, there was indeed one with a greenish hue, looking different from the others. So that must have been the Green Demon Shaya mentioned.

Yet Yang Kai hadn’t seen any sign of an attack, nor had he actually come into contact with that demon. To be poisoned like this—truly, the poison of the Green Demon was insidious and invisible, impossible to guard against.

With a cold snort, Yang Kai shouted, “Sorry, I’m not interested in sluts.”

Shaya’s voice suddenly turned icy. “I’ll tear your mouth apart and make you pay for your arrogance.”

“Slut, slut, slut! Your whole family are sluts!”

Shaya fell silent, likely seething with rage.

All around was silent; there was no sign of any demons or Shaya. Yang Kai curled his lip and mocked, “If you don’t come out, I’m leaving.”

Still no response.

Yang Kai nodded. His figure flickered, and he vanished on the spot, leaving just as he said he would.

Only after confirming Yang Kai had been gone for a long while did Shaya slowly emerge from a certain spot in the void, still completely naked. But now, there was no trace of her former seductive weakness; instead, her face was full of anger, and the chill radiating from her seemed enough to freeze fire.

The short, shadowy demon silently appeared beside her, nervously asking, “My Lady, are we just going to let him go?”

Shaya snorted coldly. “You don’t want him to leave?”

The short demon said, “He killed a lot of our people.”

“Then why didn’t you stop him just now?” Shaya turned to ask.

The short, shadowy demon's face flushed with embarrassment. He thought to himself—if even she was unwilling to face such a madman, what use could his small frame possibly be? He'd been struck by that man's fist moments ago and nearly lost his life. He couldn't fathom how such a thin, frail-looking body could harbor such domineering power.

“Hmph, once you’re poisoned by the Green Demon, unless you become one of my demon race, you’re dead for sure. Just wait—he’ll come back to beg me.” Shaya sneered, a look of confidence on her face.

The short demon agreed that this made sense.

Among the demon race’s hundred clans, the Green Demons had little status, being vassals of the Succubus clan. But their poison was a deadly weapon against non-demon beings. Once tainted by the Poison of the Green Demon, unless you became a demon, there was no way to remove it.

Since that terrifying Barbarian had already been poisoned, there was no need to keep pressing him.

Shaya’s figure flickered and she disappeared without a trace. The short demon glanced at the corpses around, sighed softly, and said, “Throw these into the beast pit!”

Immediately, several Sand Demons emerged from underground, silently carrying the corpses toward the beast pit, clearly intending to feed them to the demon beasts.

…

“My Lord, wh-what is going on?”

A hundred li from the Demon Cave, as soon as Yang Kai appeared, more than a dozen shamans stared at him in shock. The surrounding Barbarian Tribe warriors all gaped, dumbfounded.

His entire body glowed green, creating an oddly striking sight, with emerald light radiating even from the crown of his head. Standing among the crowd, he couldn't have been more conspicuous.

In any other situation, this appearance would have made everyone laugh until their sides ached. But everyone knew Yang Kai had gone to the Demon Cave to gather intelligence. Now, seeing him return covered in green light, it was obvious something had gone wrong.

This left everyone shocked—no one could laugh. All eyes were filled with concern and tension as they watched him.

Yang Kai was the leader of this group, their spiritual pillar. If anything happened to him, there would be no one left to lead. On a battlefield between two races, a leaderless team was useless. Even a minor demon counterattack could wipe out all three thousand of them.

“I got poisoned by accident,” Yang Kai said darkly, waving his hand to push back the shamans who tried to approach. “Don’t get too close, it might be contagious!”

Contagious…

Hearing this, the shamans instinctively took several steps back, but then realized this was a bit inappropriate and looked slightly embarrassed.

“The Poison of the Green Demon?” Yu suddenly asked.

“Eh?” Yang Kai looked at her in surprise. “How did you know?”

For Yu to immediately identify the poison and its name, she clearly wasn’t guessing—she really knew. But even Yang Kai had only just learned about it, so how could Yu know?

Yu nodded slightly. “It really is the Poison of the Green Demon.”

Lu added, “Then there’s no cure.”

The twin sisters spoke one after another, directly pronouncing Yang Kai’s death sentence. Their beautiful eyes looked at him with pity.

“How do you two know about the Poison of the Green Demon?” Yang Kai asked again.

The twins exchanged a glance. Yu said, “There’s a book in the Shaman God Hall that records some things about the demon race. The information is sparse and incomplete, but it does mention the Green Demons. We just happened to see it by chance.”

So it was the Shaman God Hall.

Yang Kai nodded slightly. From the knowledge passed down through Qing's inheritance, Yang Kai understood that demon invasions had occurred on these lands more than once before. In other words, the inhabitants of this realm had dealings with the demons in ages past. Though tens of thousands of years had elapsed and few remembered the details of that ancient war, it was only natural that the Shaman God Hall—the most important institution of the entire Barbarian Tribe—would preserve records concerning the demon race.

In Shaman Niu’s tribe, the oldest Shaman Apprentice trembled as he asked, “Ladies, is there really no way to cure Shaman Niu of the Poison of the Green Demon?”

Yu and Lu shook their heads in unison. Yu said, “There’s no way to cure it, but you don’t need to worry about it being contagious. The Poison of the Green Demon isn’t infectious.”

As she spoke, as if to prove her point, she actually stepped forward and touched Yang Kai’s cheek. Yang Kai frowned but didn’t dodge. When Yu withdrew her hand, there was indeed no trace of contamination.

That was at least some good news.

Still, all the shamans looked at Yang Kai as though he were already a dead man.

Yang Kai asked in a deep voice, “What are the symptoms after being poisoned by the Green Demon?”

Yu shook her head. “The book’s records are incomplete. It only mentions that you’ll glow green all over like you are now. The only way to cure it is to become a demon; otherwise, you’ll surely die. That’s all it says.”

Lu said, “Sir, do you have any last words? We can pass them on for you.”

Yang Kai couldn’t help but laugh. “I’m not dead yet.”

Lu replied seriously, “But if you die, you won’t have a chance to speak.”

Yang Kai nodded. “I won’t die. I don’t know why, but I feel fine right now. Please don’t worry.”

Yu and Lu both frowned and exchanged a glance, each seeing the unease in the other’s eyes.
