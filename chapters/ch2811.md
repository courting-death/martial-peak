## Chapter 2811 — Long-Term Plans

The bonfire flickered, and the aroma of roasted meat filled the canyon. The villagers of Lianhuo Village and Cangnan Village mingled together, chatting and laughing in harmony.

The two leaders, both Shamans, set aside their past grievances, shook hands, and made peace. Their villagers followed suit, lowering their guard and animosity, allowing everyone to coexist joyfully.

The Barbarians of Lianhuo Village brought out their fruit wine, and the villagers from both tribes feasted heartily, devouring meat and drinking deeply, getting along as if they had grown up together. Around each beautiful woman from either village, men from the other side gathered, already beginning their courtship, clearly intent on leaving behind a fleeting spring dream in this valley.

Meanwhile, Ah Hu and another Lianhuo villager were sparring in an open area.

Entertainment was scarce in the daily life of the barbarians, and the most common was close combat. Friendships among barbarians were forged with fists. The Lianhuo villager fighting Ah Hu was much stronger, but Ah Hu had the advantage of youth and vigor. For a while, the two were locked in a fierce struggle, neither gaining the upper hand, drawing cheers from the onlooking tribesmen.

Yang Kai glanced at the battlefield and saw Ah Hu take a heavy punch, bending over like a shrimp. But seizing the moment when his opponent rushed in carelessly, Ah Hu suddenly attacked, kicking the villager’s knee and causing him to kneel unexpectedly.

Turning his head, Yang Kai said, “Lord Ya, is the Shamanic Art Shield you mentioned like this?”

As he spoke, he pointed forward. A ripple of spiritual power spread out, and the air in front of him transformed into a transparent shield.

Ya’s mouth hung open for a while before he finally said, “Yes, that’s exactly it!”

“I see.” Yang Kai nodded, then added, “But this Shamanic Art Shield seems only able to guard against Shamanic Arts a rank lower. If a Shaman of the same level were to cast a Shamanic Art, I’m afraid it wouldn’t be able to hold.”

Ya replied, “At least it provides a buffer, enough time to dodge. In critical moments, this is a life-saving Shamanic Art.”

Yang Kai said, “Lord Ya makes a good point, but I think casting the Shamanic Art Shield this way might be even better.”

As he spoke, Yang Kai began to demonstrate…

Ya was nearly driven mad!

After discussing and exchanging ideas with this Shaman, Ya found the other’s understanding of shamanism shallow. Many common Shamanic Arts were unfamiliar to him, as if he had only just started learning. Yet the Shaman’s insight and creativity shocked Ya.

Ya explained and demonstrated several common Shamanic Arts, and Yang Kai immediately cast them with ease.

If not for Yang Kai’s sincere expression and lack of ulterior motives, Ya would have suspected he was pretending.

Moreover, Yang Kai could improve every common Shamanic Art. After his modifications, both casting speed and power were clearly elevated!

Is this guy really just an Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice?

Only Great Shaman Masters can do such things, right?

As a Middle-Level Shaman Warrior, Ya had always followed in others’ footsteps, never able to improve his own Shamanic Arts. Yet with this Shaman, he witnessed miracle after miracle.

The two Shamans spent the entire night exchanging ideas, both gaining much.

Yang Kai learned several simple yet practical Shamanic Arts, while Ya, inspired by Yang Kai’s demonstrations, gained a deeper understanding of those arts. Though his realm didn’t advance, Ya felt his strength had grown. Given time to digest tonight’s gains, he would be able to challenge Upper-Level Shaman Warriors.

At dawn, the two villages prepared to depart, parting ways at the canyon entrance.

Ya looked at Yang Kai with some reluctance and said, “Lord Niu, please visit Lianhuo Village as our guest. We’ll treat you and your tribesmen to the best food!”

Yang Kai chuckled, “If there’s a chance, Lord Ya, you should also visit Cangnan Village.”

Ya’s eyes lit up. “Alright, when the snow melts and spring arrives, I’ll definitely come.”

“Until we meet again!” Yang Kai cupped his fists.

Ya was stunned. He didn’t know what this gesture meant, but he imitated it anyway.

Yang Kai turned, grabbed the hind legs of two wild beasts, dragged them across the snow, and led over a hundred villagers away.

Ya watched Yang Kai leave and murmured, “I’ve seen the rise of a new star!”

“What?” a tribesman beside him asked suspiciously.

“Nothing, let’s go home!” Ya waved his hand, and the villagers of Lianhuo Village each dragged a wild beast, returning fully loaded.

…

Dragging two giant wild beasts through the snow was no easy task. The group from Cangnan Village returned from the canyon two days later.

Over a hundred villagers were welcomed as heroes. Upon returning, they immediately began cleaning and storing their haul.

The village chief walked over shakily and asked if anything unexpected had happened during the trip.

Ah Hu recounted the encounter with the people from Lianhuo Village to the chief.

The chief nodded to Yang Kai. “Ah Niu, you did well. Though they’re from another tribe, they’re still our people. Help when you can, don’t be stingy.”

“That’s what I thought too,” Yang Kai nodded.

“Alright, you’re tired. Go rest. I’ll talk to you tomorrow.”

The next day, Yang Kai went to see the chief early.

“Ah Niu, I want you to become the chief of Cangnan Village. Are you willing?” The chief got straight to the point.

Yang Kai was stunned, then immediately refused, “I can’t…”

“Don’t rush to refuse!” The chief smiled, as if he expected this, and sighed, “Let me finish.”

Yang Kai nodded.

The chief continued, “I’m old now, and I don’t have many years left. When I pass, you’ll have to take over as chief anyway. I used to worry that without a Shaman to protect us, our village would surely be swallowed up by others. You know what life would be like for our people if that happened. Fortunately, the Barbarian God has blessed us, awakening your shamanic power. Now you’re already an Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice, stronger than me, and fully capable of leading the village.”

“You’re young, so I was uneasy about handing over the chief’s position. But seeing how you handled Lianhuo Village, I know you won’t disappoint me or the villagers. You’re honest and tactful. I never noticed before, but you’ll be a better chief than me, and the villagers will trust you.”

After the beast tide invasion and the duel with Ya, the villagers revered Yang Kai. If he became chief, no one would object.

Yang Kai shook his head. “Chief, I can’t accept this.”

“Why?” The chief looked disappointed. He hadn’t expected Yang Kai to refuse after all he’d said.

Yang Kai sighed. He couldn’t say he was only here for training, and that the chief and villagers might just be products of the illusion. He would eventually leave.

“I want to travel while I’m young. I won’t be able to stay in the village for long.”

“You… want to leave?” The chief was surprised, but soon understood. “You’re talented. Staying in one place won’t help you. Traveling will benefit your growth.”

“Chief, I’m sorry.”

The chief smiled. “No need to apologize. You’re a Cangnan villager. Wherever you go, you’ll always be one. Enough about that, let’s learn some characters.”

As usual, the chief taught Yang Kai ancient characters, as if nothing had happened.

Two days after Yang Kai led the villagers back from the canyon, snow began to fall, blanketing the world in white and turning everything into a frozen land.

In such conditions, even fierce beasts were reluctant to roam outside, let alone people. The villagers stayed in their homes, rarely venturing out.

Yang Kai’s daily routine was very regular. In the morning, he studied ancient characters with the chief for an hour, then returned to his cabin to temper his body and accumulate shamanic power.

Ten days later, Yang Kai suddenly felt a barrier break inside him. Power surged through his meridians, and his strength seemed to soar in an instant.

Shaman Warrior!

It took Yang Kai only one night to go from awakening shamanic power to becoming an Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice, but half a month to advance from Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice to Shaman Warrior.

For more than ten days, Yang Kai had been waiting for the secret realm to expel him, but it never happened. He began to suspect he might truly be trapped here.

Regardless, Yang Kai had to make long-term plans.

This world was strange. It suppressed all his Emperor Realm power, and even his Divine Sense couldn’t be released. Otherwise, he would be able to open his Spatial Ring.

According to the chief, only by raising his power to the level of Shaman Master would he gain Divine Sense.

So his current goal was to quickly cultivate to Shaman Master Realm, then open his Spatial Ring and retrieve the Inner Cores of the million demonic beasts. With that as a foundation, his advancement would accelerate, and Shaman King and Shaman Saint Realms would be within reach.

Perhaps, when his shamanic power reached a certain level, he could find a way to leave this world.

But advancing from Shaman Warrior to Shaman Master was a major leap. At his current pace, Yang Kai estimated it would take at least a year and a half.

He couldn’t wait that long.

Trapped in this inexplicable secret realm, his own strength was the key to survival. He was eager to become stronger.

The best way to improve was to hunt demonic beasts, obtain their flesh and Inner Cores, especially the Inner Cores, which he could now directly consume to rapidly increase his strength. With enough Inner Cores, breaking through to Shaman Master Realm would be easy.
