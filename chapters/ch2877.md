## Chapter 2877 — Black Sun Extinguishes the World

The tribesmen did not know why they had stopped here. They all looked toward Yang Kai, then followed his gaze into the distance.

More and more tribesmen turned their gazes toward that distant sky, and for reasons they could not fathom, each of them sensed a bone-chilling aura emanating from that direction.

At a certain moment, a black dot suddenly appeared on the horizon. Though tiny, it was extremely conspicuous.

At first, the small black dot hovered quietly in midair, emanating a terrifying aura, but after just three breaths, it suddenly plummeted straight down.

As it fell, the black dot rapidly expanded, like a black hole that wanted to devour everything. Everything in its path was reduced to nothingness.

“Black Sun!” Bao Qi’s eyes widened in shock as he cried out. Shaya’s face also turned deathly pale, completely bloodless, her gaze full of dread and terror as she stared at the rapidly expanding black hole, unable to utter a word. If Yang Kai hadn’t been standing beside her, she would have turned and fled on the spot.

Yang Kai’s heart sank.

Though Yang Kai did not know what this "Black Sun" that Bao Qi spoke of truly was, even from such a great distance, he could sense a destructive aura emanating from it—an aura that threatened to obliterate all things. And the direction in which the Black Sun descended was unmistakably toward Jiaohe, where all the Southern Barbarian Tribe had assembled!

This was a conspiracy—a plot to wipe out the Southern Barbarian Tribe's entire fighting force!

The Shaman Kings of the Southern Barbarian Tribe didn’t know, nor did the Great Shaman Masters. Yang Kai knew, but it was already too late.

“How is this possible, how is this possible…” Bao Qi muttered in shock, as if everything before his eyes was beyond comprehension.

“What is that?” Yang Kai turned to look at him.

Bao Qi took a deep breath before explaining, "That is a natural disaster of the Demon Domain, possessing terrifying corrosive power capable of reducing even the earth to nothing. Whenever it appears, ordinary demons are powerless to resist it, and even Demon Kings must retreat in fear. But how... how could the Black Sun possibly appear here?"

The Demon Domain was not a paradise. Compared to this land, the Demon Domain's environment was extremely harsh, with natural disasters appearing from time to time.

The Black Sun was one of many natural disasters in the Demon Domain. No one knew what it truly was, but each time it appeared, it claimed countless demon lives and devastated vast stretches of land.

So the Demon Race needed to constantly wage war and explore new great worlds. On one hand, conquest and belligerence were inherent to their very nature. On the other hand, they were also making plans for their own survival.

However, every great world they conquered would crumble into fragments within mere tens of thousands of years. Then they would cast their gaze toward new great worlds, and the cycle would repeat endlessly.

Bao Qi could not comprehend why the Black Sun, a natural disaster belonging to the Demon Domain, would manifest in this land. Yet the reality before him was undeniable—wherever the Black Sun's darkness shrouded, nothing could possibly survive.

As they spoke, the Black Sun had already expanded to cover nearly half the sky. To the naked eye, it looked as if a star had flown in from beyond the heavens and crashed heavily into the earth.

There was no strange sound. That stretch of land was instantly shrouded in pitch blackness, which gradually spread outward. The demonic beasts of Shaman Niu’s tribe grew increasingly restless, their hooves flailing, refusing to advance no matter how the warriors on their backs tried to urge them forward.

“Die!” Yang Kai called out.

Die turned her gaze toward him.

Yang Kai spoke calmly, “If I haven’t returned in half an hour, take everyone and leave this place.”

Die nodded lightly.

“Sir, what are you going to do?” Bao Qi stared at Yang Kai in astonishment, but received no reply.

In the next instant, his vision blurred—Yang Kai, who had been sitting atop a demonic beast, had vanished without a trace.

Shaya said uneasily, “What is he…” Her gaze turned to the area shrouded by the Black Sun, and sure enough, she saw a faint trace darting away at incredible speed, disappearing from her sight in the blink of an eye.

Bao Qi’s face darkened. “What is he trying to do!”

With the Black Sun erupting, the barbarians gathered over there would surely die with no remains left. The best course now was to retreat immediately, yet Lord Shaman Niu, as the commander, was risking his life alone. From his perspective, this was truly incomprehensible.

“It’s over, it’s all over.” Shaya seemed soulless, her eyes vacant.

If not for the Soul Brand Yang Kai had planted in her, she would have wished for his early death and reincarnation. But with that connection on her Divine Soul, she had no choice but to worry about Yang Kai’s safety.

This was clearly not a wise move. Yang Kai might not know the terror of the Black Sun, but the demons knew better than anyone.

Even Demon Kings had to give it wide berth—Lord Shaman Niu was merely equivalent to a Demon Commander in strength. What good could possibly come from him going there?

The two Demon Kings exchanged a glance and could only pray in secret that by the time Yang Kai arrived, the Black Sun would have disappeared.

Perhaps their prayers really worked, for the distant darkness actually dissipated in less than the time it takes to burn an incense stick. This surprised both Bao Qi and Shaya, because in the Demon Domain, once the Black Sun erupted, it could last anywhere from a few days to ten days, half a month, or even several months. During that time, vast lands would vanish, turning to nothingness, and the laws of heaven and earth would be destroyed. The ruin of the Demon Domain was closely tied to the existence of the Black Sun.

This was the first time they had seen—or even heard of—a Black Sun that lasted only an incense stick’s time before vanishing.

The appearance of the Black Sun on this land was already unusual. The two speculated that either Demon Saint Mo Duo or the Great Demon God had used some heaven-defying method to transfer the Black Sun from the Demon Domain to this place. Though the means were astonishing, there must have been all sorts of limitations—this was likely the main reason for its short duration.

At the same time, Yang Kai noticed this as well.

He continued to approach that area. Even though he had learned that a Demon Saint had suddenly descended, it did not make him feel any fear. He didn’t even know why he insisted on going over—there might not even be a single survivor there—but an impulse within him drove him to press forward with all his might.

Suddenly, he realized he had completely adapted to the identity of a barbarian, to the role of Great Shaman Master.

Shaking his head to clear away distractions, Yang Kai focused his vision and saw, from afar, that in a massive pit, countless corpses lay scattered, and at the edge of the pit, more people were gathering, surrounding it tightly.

The distance was far, far too great. Even with Yang Kai’s eyesight, he could not make out the situation over there.

But he knew that the Southern Barbarian Tribe was not completely wiped out—there were still survivors. The demons had clearly anticipated this, which was why they surrounded the pit, preparing for a massacre!

With the Laws of Space surging, Yang Kai teleported again and again.

The demons surrounding the pit began to charge downward, using their high vantage to press their advantage, like primordial beasts intent on devouring all the barbarians.

The surviving barbarians, gathered by their respective leaders, mustered all their strength to resist with everything they had.

The sounds of unyielding roars and the fevered clamor of battle echoed in his ears.

The pit corrupted by the Black Sun instantly became a scene of bloody carnage. The demons closed in from all sides, and the survivors of the Southern Barbarian Tribe gradually contracted their defensive line, showing not the slightest sign of weakness.

But the overwhelming numerical disadvantage and the damage caused by the Black Sun at the outset left them unable to withstand the demons’ long-planned assault.

The destruction of the Southern Barbarian Tribe was only a matter of time.

It was at this moment that Yang Kai arrived at the battlefield.

He charged down from the outermost edge of the pit, wielding the massive Myriad Sword. Wherever the sword light flashed, nothing could stand in his way. Amid the carnage, Yang Kai carved path after path of death through the demon army, sending countless demon souls fleeing in terror.

Many demons noticed his presence. Demons of all kinds charged at him fearlessly, but none could stop his advance, falling one after another beneath his sword. Even the most powerful Demon Commanders were the same—none could withstand a single blow from Yang Kai, and the aftershock of the Myriad Sword shattered them to pieces.

Thirty li away, a pair of deep eyes watched him. Their owner was a fair-skinned young man, tall and slender. Yang Kai’s appearance seemed to inject new life into this stagnant battlefield, piquing his interest.

Several Demon Kings gathered respectfully at his side. Whenever they glanced at him, their eyes were full of awe.

For Demon Kings to show such humility, the young man could only be a Demon Saint.

Demon Saint—Mo Duo!

Before seeing his true face, no one would have imagined that the one who had summoned the Black Sun and slaughtered two to three hundred thousand members of the Southern Barbarian Tribe was actually a harmless-looking, fair-skinned young man.

There was not the slightest trace of demonic qi about him—he seemed like the most ordinary human.

But the Demon Kings who knew him understood the terror of the Demon Saint. That gentle face was nothing but a meaningless disguise.

One of the Demon Kings, noticing Mo Duo’s gaze, cautiously asked, “My lord, do you need me to go teach him a lesson?”

This was a battle to establish the might of the demons. The outsiders only needed to stand there obediently and be slaughtered. This battle was meant to crush the morale of the outsiders, to show them the consequences of resisting the demons. There could be no tolerance for any insolence. That youth rampaging across the battlefield was clearly going too far, making this Demon King eager to step in and kill him.

Mo Duo shook his head and smiled. “This is fine.”

The Demon King who spoke immediately fell silent, his gaze coldly fixed on that side, as if waiting to see how Yang Kai would die.

The pit was enormous, spanning at least a hundred li.

And this entire hundred-li expanse was a battlefield!

Three hundred thousand people of the Southern Barbarian Tribe, gathered here by the order of the Shaman King, had nearly all assembled at this place. When the Black Sun erupted, two-thirds or more died in an instant. Those who survived were mostly maimed—there was not a single one unscathed.

The demons also had many people here. All the forces under the command of more than a dozen Demon Kings had gathered, nearly a hundred thousand demons in total.

Nearly two hundred thousand people were now gathered here. This was almost the largest battle since the war between the two races began, but the situation was extremely unfavorable for the barbarians.
