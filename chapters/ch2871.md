## Chapter 2871 — Please Enter the Trap

All the Demon Kings were startled by what they heard, both because of the information Fei Li revealed and because they had been completely unaware of it. They had all dealt with the foreign race before and knew what those burly, muscular outsiders were capable of. Some had even personally slain so-called experts among them, so none of them thought the foreign race was particularly difficult to handle.

If not for the Demon Saints’ defensive orders before they went into seclusion to heal, they would have already taken the initiative to conquer this land. Now, hearing Fei Li say that Shaya’s territory had fallen and her subordinates were completely wiped out, they were naturally surprised.

“No way? Are the outsiders really that formidable?”

“Yeah, Fei Li, did you make a mistake? Shaya may not be that strong, but she’s still a Demon King. How could the foreigners possibly have such ability?”

Several Demon Kings who didn’t know the truth began to question, while more chose to watch from the sidelines.

Facing the doubts, Fei Li just sneered, “If you don’t believe me, ask her yourself. I have no grudge against her, so why would I target her?”

Instantly, all the Demon Kings turned their gazes toward Shaya.

Knowing she couldn’t hide it, Shaya could only speak in a hoarse voice, “Fei Li is right. My territory has indeed fallen, and all three thousand of my subordinates died in battle. This is my failure and incompetence!”

Hearing Shaya admit it herself, the Demon Kings who had questioned her fell silent. Their gazes grew increasingly cold, filled with contempt and disdain.

Since their invasion of this land, the foreign race had resisted, but it seemed they had never suffered any major losses. Even if the foreigners occasionally won, it was always insignificant. Not a single Demon King had ever tasted defeat.

But on Shaya’s side, not only had she lost her territory, even her people had been wiped out. This was simply a disgrace to the demon race.

She had truly let down the Demon Saints’ expectations. Such a failure was enough to strip her of her qualifications as a Demon King.

“She deserves to die!” Fei Li’s voice grew even colder, as if Shaya’s defeat had made him lose face as well.

This time, no one spoke up for Shaya. Even Bao Qi didn’t dare to say a word, lest he reveal any flaws.

Shaya was still being held aloft, feeling the force of Fei Li’s skeletal hand around her neck. She couldn’t help but laugh, “I do deserve to die, but I want you to know that my people did not die in vain. They fought to the death against more than ten thousand outsiders. Not a single one retreated or fled. Before dying, they made them pay a heavy price!”

Hearing this, Bao Qi naturally knew she was making it up.

But the other Demon Kings were visibly shaken, as if they could see those three thousand subordinates fighting desperately against enemies several times their number, refusing to yield, and ultimately being annihilated due to the disparity in strength. The coldness on their faces faded a little.

“Then why didn’t you die with them?” Fei Li seemed unmoved, only the ghostly fire in his eye sockets flickering slightly.

Shaya opened her mouth, but didn’t try to argue. She simply said, “Go ahead and kill me. Having lost my territory and my people, I truly have no right to be a Demon King anymore.”

She stopped struggling, looking completely resigned to her fate.

The ghostly fire in Fei Li’s eyes suddenly turned icy cold. He nodded slightly, “At least you still have some sense of shame. Don’t worry, I’ll make it quick.”

Just as he was about to deliver the fatal blow, Bao Qi, who had been silent all along, suddenly called out, “Wait!”

Fei Li turned to look at him. Shaya secretly breathed a sigh of relief, but her face remained unchanged, still wearing a fearless expression lest anyone see through her act.

Fei Li said nothing, just stared at Bao Qi, as if waiting to hear his opinion. Of course, pleading would be useless. Since he had decided to kill Shaya, no one’s plea would change his mind—unless the Demon Saints gave the order.

“Shaya deserves to die, but she shouldn’t die by our hands,” Bao Qi said calmly, pointing at the disguised entrance. “Why not let her go in first and see what’s inside? I believe none of us wants to be the first to enter, right?”

At these words, the Demon Kings’ eyes lit up. They looked at each other, and several even lowered their heads to discuss something before nodding in agreement. Clearly, they thought this was a good idea.

The place where the Ancient Saint had fallen was indeed extremely tempting to the Demon Kings, but the unknown dangers made them uneasy. Even an Ancient Saint had perished there, so there must be risks inside.

Although they hadn’t discussed it openly, everyone had wondered in their hearts—who would be the first to go in?

There was no denying it was extremely dangerous. The first person in might encounter unique opportunities, but it was far more likely they’d face deadly peril! All the Demon Kings cherished their lives; none were foolish enough to volunteer as a scout for others.

But now, there was a perfect candidate.

Fei Li didn’t move. The ghostly fire in his eyes calmed, as if he was deep in thought.

Bao Qi seized the moment, “Fei Li, if you insist on killing her, I won’t stop you. But then you have to be the first to go in.”

According to Bao Qi’s original plan, he would volunteer to go in first to dispel everyone’s doubts. After all, he knew exactly where the vortex led and that there was no danger. But if he did that, his actions would be far less convincing, since he was the one who discovered this ‘place where an Ancient Saint fell.’

But if Shaya did it, it would be different.

As long as she looked around inside and came back out, no one would have any more suspicions.

Fei Li snorted coldly, flung his hand, and threw Shaya heavily to the ground. His pitch-black jaw opened and closed, “Shaya, you heard him. I won’t kill you this time, but you have to prove your worth!”

Shaya coughed lightly, clutching her neck. It took her a while to catch her breath. She looked up at the Demon Kings with a pleading expression, clearly unwilling to be the scout, but who would care?

In the end, Shaya could only sigh, summon her demonic qi, and walk toward the vortex.

The Demon Kings held their breath and watched. Shaya suddenly stopped and said, “If I don’t come back within one hour, it means there’s danger inside.”

With that, Shaya rushed into the vortex, swallowed by chaos and nothingness, vanishing from sight.

In front of the vortex, the Demon Kings were silent, waiting quietly. But every pair of eyes was fixed on the entrance, faces full of anticipation.

Shaya said that if she didn’t return within an hour, it meant there was danger inside. But even if she really didn’t come back, the Demon Kings present probably wouldn’t give up easily. They’d just look for a safer way to investigate.

Meanwhile, inside the Small Xuan Realm, Yang Kai calmly watched Shaya walk in and nodded, “You all did well!”

Shaya was startled, only now realizing that even though Yang Kai was hiding inside, he was fully aware of what was happening outside. She thought to herself that it was lucky she hadn’t harbored any stray thoughts earlier, or she’d be facing a storm now.

“It was Bao Qi’s quick thinking. I almost ruined everything,” Shaya said, not daring to take credit.

Yang Kai shook his head, “There will always be small accidents, but it doesn’t matter. This is even better than the original plan.”

Shaya smiled, “Then I’ll wait here for half an hour, and then go out to tell them there’s no danger?”

Yang Kai said, “Words alone aren’t enough. That won’t make them lose their minds and rush in.”

Shaya asked in confusion, “Then what should I do?”

“You’ll know soon enough,” Yang Kai replied with a mysterious smile.

He didn’t explain, and Shaya didn’t dare ask. She could only stand quietly, waiting for time to pass.

More than half an hour later, out on the wilderness, the Demon Kings watching the vortex suddenly all tensed up, as if they’d noticed something. Their eyes grew brighter as they stared at the vortex.

Under their gaze, the vortex suddenly rippled strangely, and a graceful, scantily clad figure emerged from within, a short, delicate tail trailing behind her.

“She’s out!” The Flame Demon’s expression brightened, and he called out in a low voice.

Shaya had come out safely. In other words, even if there was some danger in the place where the Ancient Saint fell, it was nothing the Demon Kings could not handle. This was excellent news.

The other Demon Kings were also excited, though a bit puzzled.

Because Shaya had come out with her back to them, and at an extremely slow speed, as if something on the other side of the vortex was pulling her back.

“What’s going on?” someone asked.

“What’s Shaya doing? If she’s coming out, she should hurry up. Why is she just sticking her butt out?”

“Is she trying to seduce us? Haha, I have to say, that butt is pretty perky. Must feel nice to touch.”

The crowd started jeering, their words crude.

Suddenly, Fei Li shouted, “She’s in trouble!”

At that moment, Shaya’s demonic qi surged, and her whole body lurched farther out, leaving only her arms still in the vortex, as if she was tightly gripping something and trying to drag it out.

“Quick… help me!” Shaya turned her head and shouted to the Demon Kings.

No one moved. No one knew what trouble Shaya had run into. If they helped and got caught up in it, it wouldn’t be worth the risk.

“What are you doing?” Bao Qi was more anxious than the others. The plan was going perfectly—once Shaya came out and told everyone there was no danger, his task would be complete.

But at this critical moment, Shaya suddenly started acting up. He was both anxious and angry, unsure if this was Shaman Niu’s plan or Shaya’s own idea.

"A Sacred Artifact!" Shaya cried out in astonishment, "I've grabbed hold of a Sacred Artifact!"

“What?”

The Demon Kings’ expressions all changed dramatically.
