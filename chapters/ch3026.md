## Chapter 3026 — Borrowing the Tiger's Might

Yang Kai narrowed his eyes at the sinister man flying toward them and asked, "What is so special about this man? Why is he able to speak with those from the Dragon Palace?"

Lu Sanniang sighed upon hearing this. She knew Yang Kai and Li Jiao probably would not be able to escape. With a member of the Dragon Clan here, where could they flee? Her face filled with guilt as she realized she had implicated the two of them, and she immediately explained, "His name is Yuan Wu. There is nothing special about him; he is simply fortunate. He possesses a Second-Tier Upper-Level Dragon Bloodline and is quite eloquent, so he has always been responsible for communication between the Dragon Palace and Half-Dragon City."

"What about that green Dragon?" Yang Kai glanced at the member of the Dragon Clan standing in midair.

Lu Sanniang's expression became complicated. "His name is Fu Qi. He has a Fifth-Tier bloodline."

After all, she had lived in the Dragon Palace for two or three hundred years. The Dragon Clan had few members, only a handful even when every last one was counted, so she naturally knew this Fu Qi.

"His surname is Fu?" Yang Kai was stunned.

He had thought every member of the Dragon Clan on Dragon Isle bore the surname Zhu.

Li Jiao said, "Rumor has it that the Dragon Clan on Dragon Isle indeed has two surnames: Zhu and Fu."

Yang Kai nodded. "Fifth-Tier. Passable, I suppose."

Lu Sanniang was shocked and stared blankly at Yang Kai, wondering just what background Big Brother Li's friend had. How could he speak so boastfully? A Fifth-Tier Great Dragon was actually only passable in his eyes? One had to know that the strongest Dragon Bloodline in all of Half-Dragon City was only Second-Tier Upper-Level, worlds apart from the Fifth-Tier. That was a true pure-blooded member of the Dragon Clan, an entirely different existence from the Dragon Descendants in Half-Dragon City.

She thought Yang Kai was merely spouting nonsense.

Little did she know that Yang Kai was simply judging the matter as it was. The only members of the Dragon Clan he had encountered were the siblings Zhu Qing and Zhu Lie, both of whom possessed Eighth-Tier Dragon Bloodlines. After joining with him, Zhu Qing had even advanced to the Ninth-Tier. What did a mere Fifth-Tier amount to?

In fact, even without Lu Sanniang's explanation, Yang Kai could tell that the green Dragon named Fu Qi did not possess a particularly high-ranking bloodline. His true Dragon form just now had only been a little over ten zhang long, far smaller than Zhu Lie's roughly thirty-zhang body.

"Why has a member of the Dragon Clan come here?" Li Jiao asked with a frown.

Lu Sanniang returned to her senses and explained, "He should be here for the Dragon Blood Flowers."

"Dragon Blood Flowers?" Li Jiao's eyes lit up, and he exclaimed softly, "There are Dragon Blood Flowers in Half-Dragon City?"

Lu Sanniang's experience had left him feeling humiliated. His enemy was a member of the Dragon Clan. In terms of cultivation, he felt he would definitely not lose to that Dragon, for he was at the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm. In terms of bloodline, however, he was not even worthy to carry the other's shoes.

If he wanted revenge, he first had to improve his bloodline.

The best way to improve his bloodline was through Dragon Blood Flowers, so when he heard Lu Sanniang mention them, Li Jiao could not help taking an interest. If he could raise the rank of his bloodline, he might have a chance to contend with the Dragon Clan.

Unexpectedly, Lu Sanniang shook her head. "How could there be Dragon Blood Flowers in Half-Dragon City? Dragon Blood Flowers are the foundation of the Dragon Clan and can only be found inside the Dragon Palace."

"Then what did you mean just now..."

Lu Sanniang explained, "Dragon Blood Flowers must be watered and cultivated with Dragon blood. There are only so many members of the Dragon Clan in the Dragon Palace, so even if they drained every drop of their blood, they would not be able to cultivate many flowers. Moreover, doing this too often would affect their own strength, so unless absolutely necessary, the Dragon Clan will not use their own blood to cultivate Dragon Blood Flowers. He came here for the Dragon Descendants. Although their bloodlines are impure, there are many of them, and using their blood to water Dragon Blood Flowers can still produce some effect."

Hearing this, Yang Kai and Li Jiao immediately understood.

Li Jiao had also cultivated Dragon Blood Flowers before, using his own blood to do so.

There were tens of thousands of Dragon Descendants throughout Half-Dragon City, so they were indeed a good choice.

From the looks of it, the Dragon Palace regarded Half-Dragon City as a source of Dragon blood for watering its Dragon Blood Flowers. What a fine scheme they had devised.

No wonder the two of them had previously noticed that among the stronger cultivators in Half-Dragon City, there were fewer Dragon Descendants. They had seen several Humans and Yao at the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm, but not a single Dragon Descendant at the same Stage.

This was clearly the reason. Dragon Descendants with high cultivations were all taken to Dragon Isle to cultivate Dragon Blood Flowers. After having their blood drawn year after year, they were fortunate if their cultivations did not fall, so how could they hope to advance any further?

Lu Sanniang turned to Li Jiao and said uneasily, "Big Brother Li, Yuan Wu will definitely take you away."

Li Jiao's expression became extremely ugly.

When he fought Yuan Wu earlier, he had exposed his identity as a Dragon Descendant, and both his bloodline and cultivation surpassed Yuan Wu's. Now that Yuan Wu had been ordered to gather Dragon Descendants to cultivate Dragon Blood Flowers, he naturally would not let Li Jiao off easily.

This was obvious from Yuan Wu's flight path. The first person he intended to find was Li Jiao.

Li Jiao immediately grew restless and looked to Yang Kai for help.

He knew that if the Dragon Clan truly took him to the Dragon Palace to cultivate Dragon Blood Flowers, they might well drain him of every last drop of blood.

The only person who could save him now was Yang Kai. Even an Eighth-Tier Red Dragon could not stir up any waves before him, much less a mere Fifth-Tier green Dragon.

"Let us wait and see!" Yang Kai took a sip of tea and spoke softly.

Li Jiao's heart settled at once, knowing Yang Kai would not abandon him.

Things unfolded just as they had expected. Yuan Wu flew straight toward the teahouse and landed on the second floor. He glanced coldly at Li Jiao, showing none of his previous fear or panic. Instead, he wore the smug look of a petty man who had suddenly gained power.

Li Jiao had humiliated him earlier because his bloodline and cultivation were both inferior. Now, however, he had a Fifth-Tier green Dragon backing him, so what did he have to fear?

He wished Li Jiao would attack him. If he did, Li Jiao's death would not be far away.

"By order of Sir Fu Qi, two hundred Dragon Descendants are to be selected to serve at the Dragon Palace. All Dragon Descendants at or above the Dao Origin Realm are to gather here immediately. Anyone who fails to appear within half a cup of tea will be killed without mercy!" Yuan Wu's tongue burst with spring thunder. His voice was not particularly loud, but it was enough to spread throughout Half-Dragon City.

As soon as these words rang out, every Dragon Descendant at or above the Dao Origin Realm became anxious and uneasy, their expressions turning ugly.

Of course they knew why Fu Qi wanted to take them to Dragon Isle. It could only be to water Dragon Blood Flowers with their own blood. This was a bitter assignment. Throughout the generations, every Dragon Descendant sent to the Dragon Palace had suffered grievous damage to their vitality. In severe cases, their cultivations fell, and some had even died on the spot.

Naturally, no one was willing to undertake such a bitter assignment.

But Fu Qi was hovering above Half-Dragon City, so no matter how unwilling they were, no one dared resist. They could not escape this place, nor could they escape Dragon Isle. For generation after generation, they could only endure the oppression of the Dragon Palace.

Thus, as soon as Yuan Wu finished speaking, every Dragon Descendant at or above the Dao Origin Realm in Half-Dragon City began moving, converging on the teahouse from all directions.

"You, get out here!" Yuan Wu pointed at Li Jiao and barked.

Li Jiao's expression changed slightly. Although he had known Yuan Wu would target him, he still felt irritated now that the moment had arrived.

If not for that green Dragon, he would have slapped Yuan Wu already. Doing so under the green Dragon's very eyes, however, would be tantamount to declaring war on the Dragon Clan, and he did not have that kind of courage.

"The rest of you, get over here too!" Yuan Wu suddenly pointed again, sweeping his finger over Yang Kai, Lu Sanniang, and her daughter.

Lu Sanniang's expression changed drastically.

Yang Kai also frowned, not knowing what this fellow intended.

Dragon Isle should be looking for Dragon Descendants to cultivate Dragon Blood Flowers, but neither he nor Lu Sanniang and her daughter were Dragon Descendants. Why was Yuan Wu calling them over?

Was he borrowing the tiger's might to settle a personal grudge? Yuan Wu could not be that bold, could he?

Although the green Dragon had kept his eyes closed, the commotion here certainly could not escape his notice. If Yuan Wu truly tried to bully others by relying on his backing, Fu Qi probably would not agree. After all, in Fu Qi's eyes, Yuan Wu was no one worth caring about, so why would he allow Yuan Wu to borrow his prestige?

"I'm talking to you! Are you deaf?" Seeing that no one at the table moved, Yuan Wu's face sank and he shouted loudly. He even stole a glance at Fu Qi, clearly intending to make a spectacle of the matter.

If Yang Kai and the others truly disobeyed the order, Yuan Wu would be able to kill them. Thus, rather than growing angry at their attitude, he was delighted and wished they would act even more arrogantly.

Acting arrogant before the Dragon Clan was simply courting death.

Li Jiao looked at Yang Kai, completely at a loss.

Yang Kai, however, continued sipping his tea, his expression utterly indifferent.

"What audacity! You even dare defy an order from the Dragon Palace? You are courting death." Yuan Wu appeared furious, but inwardly he was beside himself with joy. Go on, keep acting arrogant. In a moment, I will make you wish you were dead.

The people in the teahouse were also filled with doubt and alarm. They all looked toward Yang Kai's table, thinking these people truly had no fear of death. In all the years Half-Dragon City had existed, such defiant troublemakers had never appeared before.

Yuan Wu continued shouting, "Do not blame me for failing to warn you. If you delay Sir Fu Qi's business, none of you can bear the consequences."

As soon as he finished speaking, Fu Qi, who had been hovering above Half-Dragon City with his eyes tightly closed, suddenly opened them and said angrily, "What are you prattling on about? Hurry up and get to work!"

Yuan Wu was startled, and his face went pale. Realizing that Fu Qi was displeased with his performance, he no longer had any thought of flaunting his power before Yang Kai and immediately announced, "Sir Fu Qi has ordered five hundred mortals to report to the Dragon Palace and await further instructions. Those I select are to prepare to depart immediately. Anyone who dares resist will be killed without mercy!"

As he spoke, he pointed toward Yang Kai and the others again, calling out, "You, you, you..."

His hand swept in a circle, selecting everyone in the teahouse.

Everyone's expression turned ugly, and each of them became anxious and uneasy.

In the past, whenever the Dragon Clan came to Half-Dragon City, they only sought Dragon Descendants. In their eyes, mortals had no value whatsoever except as objects for venting their lust. No one knew why the Dragon Palace had suddenly ordered five hundred mortals to be sent over this time.

Everyone Yuan Wu had selected wore a gloomy expression, secretly thinking that if they had known this would happen, none of them would have come here to drink tea. Was the Dragon Palace a place one could casually visit? Once they went there, they might never escape for the rest of their lives.

Yang Kai and the others were undoubtedly responsible for this outcome. If they had not clashed with Yuan Wu earlier, he would not have come straight to the teahouse. This truly was an undeserved calamity.

In an instant, many people shot dark looks toward Yang Kai and the others, secretly beginning to hate them.
