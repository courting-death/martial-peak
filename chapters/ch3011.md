## Chapter 3011 — Encountering a Bottleneck

Inside the room, the Spiritual Qi was as dense as mist. Yang Kai sat cross-legged and circulated his profound art, causing the surrounding Spiritual Qi to surge toward him like ten thousand streams returning to the sea. It poured into his body and flowed through his Meridians, flesh, and blood.

Gradually, the place where Yang Kai sat seemed to transform into a vortex capable of devouring everything, drawing in the surrounding Spiritual Qi before making it vanish without a trace.

The foundation of his First-Stage Emperor Realm cultivation slowly accumulated and strengthened, gradually showing signs of a quantitative change becoming a qualitative one. The energy within his body surged and ebbed, his Meridians swelled, and his flesh writhed.

These were clearly signs that a breakthrough was imminent.

Yang Kai carefully guarded his mind, undisturbed by the outside world. His expression remained neither sorrowful nor joyful, while his aura grew stronger and stronger as time passed.

The shackles of the First-Stage Emperor Realm were like a great mountain standing in his path. The beautiful scenery all around represented the trials along the road to advancement. If he became captivated by that scenery and stopped to linger, his journey along the Martial Dao would end there. The fierce winds, thunder, and lightning descending from the summit were further obstacles to his growth. Countless cultivators had halted before their own barriers, leaving behind lifelong regrets. By the time they grew old and beat their chests in remorse, they no longer had another chance to choose.

Yang Kai climbed the mountain, winding his way upward. He paid no heed to the breathtaking scenery, while neither the fierce winds nor the thunder could hinder him in the slightest. Only one thought lingered in his heart: to cross the great mountain before him, behold a broader world, stand at a greater height, and look down upon all living beings.

After an unknown length of time, a slight change suddenly appeared on Yang Kai's calm face. His brow furrowed faintly, and then the change became increasingly obvious.

At last, accompanied by a soft pop, Yang Kai spat out a mouthful of blood. His aura, which had climbed to its peak, instantly withered like a punctured balloon.

If the road to advancement was compared to climbing a mountain, then at that moment he felt as though he had tumbled down from the summit. His goal was clearly within reach, and he only needed to exert himself a little more to surmount the obstacle before him, yet he had failed to take that final step. Instead, all his efforts came to nothing, and he fell from the cliff. The resulting turmoil in his energy even left him slightly injured.

His expression was rather unsightly. Opening his eyes, he looked at the Source Crystals around him and discovered that seventy to eighty percent of those he had prepared were already gone.

He wiped the blood from the corner of his mouth, then took out several Emperor Pills and swallowed them.

With a wave of his hand, Yang Kai once again filled the room with countless Upper-Level Source Crystals.

Again!

He was even more cautious this time. In the Emperor Realm, every breakthrough to a new Stage was exceedingly difficult, and each advancement brought a tremendous leap in strength. He was stuck at the peak of the First-Stage Emperor Realm and was on the verge of touching the threshold of the Second-Stage Emperor Realm, yet he remained just that tiny bit short. Naturally, he could not accept it.

He first spent some time healing his injuries and adjusting his condition. Once everything was ready, he attempted another breakthrough.

The Spiritual Qi churned once more. Drawn by him, the Source Crystals inside the room began to turn into mist and pour into his body.

His aura continued to climb. It was not fast, but it was steady.

In less than half a day, his First-Stage Emperor Realm aura once again climbed to its peak. Yang Kai rapidly formed several hand seals, guiding his strength to assault the barrier.

A thunderous boom rang out.

The impact came from within his mind and resounded directly through his head. Yang Kai's vision blurred, and he felt dizzy and disoriented. An uncontrollable sweetness surged up his throat, and he spat out another mouthful of blood.

His aura withered once again.

He was just a little short again!

A fierce look flashed across Yang Kai's face. Again! Ever since stepping onto the Martial Dao, his experiences had been rich and varied, and his trials unending. How many times had he escaped death? How many miracles had he created? Compared with everything he had endured before, what did this minor obstacle amount to? He refused to believe he could not even break through the barrier of the First-Stage Emperor Realm.

Another day later, Yang Kai grunted as blood leaked from the corner of his mouth. When he opened his eyes, a fierce light flashed through them. He was actually still just a little short!

Again!

Just a little short!

Again!

The stubbornness buried deep in his bones was thoroughly aroused, and fierce determination filled his heart. If he failed, he would simply try again. As long as he was not dead, there would always be another chance.

...

After an unknown number of days, Yang Kai had made attempt after attempt and suffered failure after failure. At last, he was forced to recognize a fact.

His cultivation had encountered a bottleneck!

Once he realized this, Yang Kai took several deep breaths and calmed the roiling Qi and blood in his chest. He made no further attempt to break through and simply sat there cross-legged and motionless, his expression so indifferent that no joy, anger, sorrow, or happiness could be discerned.

Although his journey had been fraught with danger and he had nearly lost his life many times, he had never encountered anything like this before. Every one of his breakthroughs had basically come naturally, each advancement steady and secure. This was the first time he had ever been obstructed by a bottleneck.

He clearly felt that he was only the tiniest bit short. It was as if a thin membrane stood before him, preventing him from glimpsing the mysteries of a higher Martial Dao. It seemed as though he could break it with a mere touch, yet no matter what he did, it would not yield.

This was no longer a problem that brute force and ruthless determination could solve. He had already tried dozens of times; if he could have succeeded, he would have done so long ago.

So this was the Emperor Realm? The obstacles encountered here were indeed far greater than those in other realms.

Yang Kai guessed that this was also related to how smoothly his cultivation had progressed in the past. He had never encountered a bottleneck during any previous breakthrough, so he had never known what it felt like to have the will but lack the strength. After accumulating over time, it had finally erupted in the Emperor Realm, allowing him to experience the unavoidable trial that ordinary cultivators had to overcome.

Now this was fair. A rainbow came only after the storm, and the joy of success could only be savored after suffering setbacks.

Only after experiencing such setbacks could a cultivator's path be considered complete.

Yang Kai was not discouraged. Instead, he felt somewhat fortunate that this bottleneck had emerged while he was still in the First-Stage Emperor Realm. Although it now appeared extremely difficult to break through, that did not mean there was no hope. If it had emerged when he was in the Third-Stage Emperor Realm, it might have been even more terrifying.

After understanding this, his mood settled. He knew that as long as he could cross this barrier, his future path along the Martial Dao would certainly be smoother than before.

Reaching out, he collected the remaining Source Crystals in the room, rose, and walked outside.

Since effort alone could not produce a breakthrough, it was clearly not a question of whether he was trying hard enough, but that the right opportunity had yet to arrive. Naturally, Yang Kai could not force it.

When he pushed open the door, the little black dog raised its head and looked at him.

No one knew how many days the little black dog had waited there, but it had not changed much. When it saw him emerge, it merely glanced at him before closing its eyes again and dozing off.

Yang Kai gave it a kick, but the little black dog remained completely still and played dead.

With a scoff, Yang Kai's figure flickered and abruptly disappeared.

When he reappeared, he was already atop a mountain peak.

A clamour of shouting immediately rang in his ears. "Big, big, big!"

"Big, big, big!"

Yang Kai swept his gaze over and saw the three Yao Kings shouting until their faces were red and their necks swollen, their eyes fixed on a dice cup. Hou Yu held the cup in one hand and shook it vigorously, producing a series of clear, melodious clinks that even had the effect of refreshing the mind and calming the spirit. As the dice cup shook, ripples spread through the air in every direction.

Lingxiao Palace’s foremost Imperial Artifact Master, the renowned “Master Hou” Hou Yu, had rolled her sleeves up to her elbows, revealing fair, slender arms. One foot rested atop a stone stool, and though her long dress fluttered about her, even her calves were exposed. Her posture was extremely indecent.

She paid it no mind whatsoever. Her beautiful eyes smiled as she stared at the three Yao Kings before her, her hand moving faster and faster while her face bore an expression of absolute confidence.

When Yang Kai arrived, neither the three Yao Kings nor Hou Yu noticed him. All their attention was focused on the dice cup.

Yang Kai's face darkened at the sight.

He knew Hou Yu loved gambling. Back then, she had accumulated an unknown amount of debt in Source Crystals through gambling and had eventually been cornered on an island. She had relied on an Array that Nanmen Dajun had refined for her many years ago to evade pursuit until Yang Kai came to her rescue. Otherwise, she would certainly still be trapped on that island.

He had recruited her into Lingxiao Palace because of her status as an Imperial-Grade Artifact Refiner, and the facts proved that she had not disappointed him. She had easily refined the Flowing Cloud Shuttle.

Ever since entering Lingxiao Palace, she had not gone a single day without thinking of escaping. Unfortunately, with the three Yao Kings taking turns guarding her, where could she possibly flee? More often than not, she was caught and brought back before she could even leave Lingxiao Palace's mountain gate.

But now it seemed this woman was nothing but a rotten apple spoiling the barrel.

The three Yao Kings possessed such lofty status, yet she had actually dragged them into gambling with her. Judging by their expressions, they were clearly deeply invested and wildly excited.

Yang Kai suddenly began to worry about his Lingxiao Palace, feeling for some reason that its future was uncertain.

Had recruiting Hou Yu been right or wrong? When he took a closer look at the dice cup, Yang Kai's face darkened even further.

This thing was actually an Imperial Treasure!

Moreover, it was an Imperial Treasure that blocked probing by Divine Sense!

It made sense when he thought about it. Every Yao King was tremendously powerful. If the cup could not block their Divine Sense, how could they gamble at all? A single sweep of Divine Sense would reveal the result.

"Bets are final, hands off!" After shaking it for a long while, Hou Yu slammed the dice cup onto the stone table before her. A cold smile hung from the corner of her lips as she swept her gaze over the three Yao Kings, looking as though she alone reigned supreme throughout Heaven and Earth.

"Hurry up and open it!" Xi Lei urged.

"Little Hou, if you lose, you had better refine this Lord's eagle claws properly!" Yingfei smiled faintly.

"Me, lose? I, Hou Yu, could lose? What a joke!" Hou Yu scoffed.

Xie Wuwei said, "And mine too!"

Hou Yu snorted. "Don't worry. If I really lose, just leave that crab shell of yours to me."

"Then open it!"

"You're certain?"

"Certain!"

“Then watch closely!” Hou Yu exclaimed, as though she had been pumped full of stimulants. Her eyes shone with excitement as she lifted the dice cup, revealing the three carefully refined dice inside.

After taking a close look, the three Yao Kings smiled, while Hou Yu's expression shifted rapidly. A layer of frost soon covered her face, as though both her parents had just died.

"Little Hou, you must keep your word!" Xi Lei smiled faintly.

Hou Yu turned her head aside, her face filled with resentment. Just as she was about to say something, her expression suddenly changed, and she forced a smile. "Pa... Palace Master, you're here. Why didn't you say anything?"
