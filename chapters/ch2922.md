## Chapter 2922 — Elusive, Subtle Means

Gong Yue had already entered a demonic state, and no one knew if he would return to destroy the barrier. The formation on the blood-red gate was already ancient, and it had been damaged once before by Gong Yue. If it was tampered with again, it might truly be broken, so Yang Kai had to stay behind to guard against this.

This was something that only Yang Kai could do, and only he had the ability to handle it.

After a brief discussion, everyone hurried to meditate and recover.

Although Hua Yulu and the others were not injured, being used for a blood sacrifice in the Sole Sovereign Formation had greatly depleted them. If they wanted to leave for Tianhe Valley, they had to recover for a while. All of them were Emperors and naturally carried precious spiritual pills for emergencies. Now was not the time to be frugal, so each took out their pills and consumed them.

Half a day later, Hua Yulu and the others had barely recovered sixty to seventy percent of their strength. Not daring to delay, they bid farewell to Yang Kai and retraced their steps, preparing to leave this ancient cave residence together.

Before long, Yang Kai was the only one left in the stone cavern. The surroundings were so quiet that even his breathing was barely perceptible, leaving only the steady drip of water falling from the hanging stalactites, echoing softly.

Although this venture into the ancient cave residence had been more of a scare than actual danger, whenever Yang Kai recalled those harrowing moments, his heart still trembled with lingering dread.

Seven had come, but in the end only four were left alive. Wu Kuangyi had been sacrificed in Yang Tai’s Blood Sacrifice, Yang Tai had died through self-destruction, and Gong Yue had been eroded by demonic intent. The remaining four were utterly exhausted, in a miserable state.

Now, finally having some time to recover, Yang Kai naturally seized every moment.

Another day passed before he slowly opened his eyes, silently sensing his current condition. Feeling that he was almost recovered, he took out his Elder Jade Token.

This was something Wen Zishan had given him during the conferral ceremony. The Elder Jade Token not only represented his status as an elder of Qingyang Shrine, but also had many other wonderful uses.

He gently activated his Emperor Yuan, causing the jade token to float in front of him. His hands formed seals, tapping the token repeatedly. The jade token buzzed continuously with his actions, and hidden messages were transmitted through it to Qingyang Shrine.

This method of communication was not perfect; it could not convey overly complex matters and was limited by distance, usable only within the same territory. However, it was currently the only means of long-distance contact in the Star Realm. Every major sect had something similar, with only minor differences.

After busying himself for a while, Yang Kai put away the jade token, believing that Qingyang Shrine had received his message. Before long, someone would come to investigate.

He continued to meditate and recover.

More than ten days passed peacefully. Yang Kai’s injuries gradually improved, and the wounds on his divine soul also slowly healed. Given a bit more time, he felt he would fully recover.

He did not let his guard down, always keeping part of his mind alert to the blood-red gate. Any disturbance would not escape his notice.

One day, while Yang Kai was meditating with his eyes closed, he suddenly felt a strange sensation, as if he had gained some insight into the Martial Dao. Delighted, he immersed himself in this inexplicable and rare opportunity, chasing after the deeper understanding and profound intent.

The mysterious feeling became clearer, like a delicious dish placed before him—within reach, yet separated by an invisible barrier, allowing only the aroma to be sensed but not the taste.

Yang Kai was neither anxious nor impatient. Step by step, he steadily broke through the obstacle before him.

He had a premonition that if he could truly comprehend this insight, he would surely advance to the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm.

He had broken through to the Emperor Realm in the Shattered Star Sea. Although it had not been long, and by rights he should not be able to advance again so soon, his experiences over these days had been so rich that he had already accumulated the foundation for the Second Stage. All he lacked was an opportunity.

Now that the opportunity was before him, he naturally had to seize it.

Time flowed by. Yang Kai did not know how long had passed—it seemed like an instant, or perhaps half a year. At a certain moment, when he felt the barrier suddenly clear, a new world unfolded before him.

All sorts of wondrous sensations surged from unknown corners into his mind, giving him the illusion of omnipotence. These feelings brought a marvelous profound intent, making him want to immerse himself, to explore the mysteries at the peak of the Martial Dao.

At this moment, Yang Kai’s surroundings felt strange. He seemed to be standing in the starry sky, surrounded by countless stars. Each star was an elusive profound intent and a mystery of the Martial Dao—a step toward its ultimate peak. He could reach out and grasp them, and by plucking these stars, he could achieve feats that would shake the ages.

Yang Kai slowly reached out to touch those stars.

Suddenly, a mass of flames ignited in the starry sky. Yang Kai frowned and stared intently at the fire.

At the sight, he felt an intense heat, as if he were being roasted over blazing flames. The scorching sensation grew stronger and fiercer, to the point that Yang Kai could barely endure it.

At that moment, a wave of coolness arose, as if a basin of cold water had been poured over him, bringing immense relief.

The dramatic shift in his state of mind made him suddenly alert. He withdrew his outstretched hand, breaking out of his deep meditative state and becoming aware of his physical body.

A flash of insight struck his mind, and he instantly understood what had happened. This was not his own enlightenment—it was a trap, a snare from the demonic intent behind the blood-red gate! Everything he saw was an illusion.

Not daring to be careless, he shouted loudly.

The stars trembled, bursting into light and converging at one point, transforming into a dazzling, radiant character that stood in the void! The script was utterly different from the current Star Realm’s writing—profound and complex, yet seemingly containing immense mysteries.

“Demon Script!” Yang Kai was shocked. Despite his caution, he had still fallen into the trap. He averted his gaze, but the light from the character gathered into a stream, as if it had a will of its own, trying to force its way into Yang Kai’s mind and brand the essence of the Demon Script into him.

At the critical moment, Yang Kai’s mind became unprecedentedly calm. A layer of seven-colored radiance suddenly blossomed from his body, enveloping him like a rainbow-hued robe.

The light from the Demon Script was blocked outside by the seven-colored robe.

Yang Kai sneered and slowly withdrew from the illusion, his divine sense returning to his body.

Unexpectedly, it went smoothly—there was no resistance, or rather, the demonic intent did not have the power to stop him.

In the pitch-dark cave, a light shone. Yang Kai glanced over and saw that it was the blood-red gate emitting a glow. But as his divine sense returned, the light gradually faded and finally disappeared.

Cold sweat broke out on his back, and Yang Kai was filled with lingering fear.

His right eye burned as if scorched by fire, but his mind felt as cool as if doused in icy water. It was this alternation of heat and cold that awakened him and freed him from the entanglement of the Demon Script.

Yang Kai did not know if the Demon Script was the same one used by Yang Tai, because he had not seen it clearly. The protection formed by the Warm Spirit Lotus had blocked the essence of the Demon Script from entering him at all.

Thinking back, the reason Yang Tai could comprehend the essence of an ancient Demon Script and become possessed was probably similar to what Yang Kai had just experienced. But Yang Tai was not as fortunate—he did not have a treasure like the Warm Spirit Lotus at his side. So, after comprehending the Demon Script, he thought he had gained an opportunity and mastered the mysteries of the Martial Dao, not realizing it was just bait, which ultimately led to tragedy.

This experience had broadened Yang Kai’s horizons. He had fought the demon race for two years in the Thousand Illusion Dreamscape, but had never seen the demon race use such elusive, subtle means. This was his first encounter with such a method.

This was not an attack; it merely made one believe they had gained some benefit, only to lead them step by step down the wrong path until there was no turning back.

After this ordeal, Yang Kai was certain that the barrier on the blood-red gate had been severely damaged and needed urgent repair and reinforcement. Otherwise, if anyone else found this place, the barrier might be broken and the demonic intent behind the door released.

In the days that followed, he became even more cautious, always keeping part of his mind on the blood-red gate and guarding against any traps set by the demonic intent.

However, after one failed attempt, the demonic intent behind the door seemed to recognize how troublesome Yang Kai was and did not make any further moves.

Yang Kai’s injuries improved day by day, and he finally made a full recovery.

After a full month, the experts from several major sects of the Southern Territory finally arrived.

A reply came from the Elder Jade Token, and Yang Kai quickly left the cave residence to meet them at the stone forest on the surface.

At this time, poisonous miasma shrouded the area around the island, sealing all directions. It was not yet the time for the yao beasts that could merge and grow stronger to hunt, but after receiving Yang Kai’s response, the experts from the major sects still decided to force their way through and join up with him.

No one knew what methods they used, but as Yang Kai stood on the island, he heard a rumbling sound, as if the world were being overturned. Then, from the multicolored poisonous miasma, a mysterious light shot out like a dragon boat riding the wind and waves, breaking through the sea of clouds and landing on the island.

Powerful auras spread out, and as the light faded, a group of more than twenty figures appeared.

Yang Kai swept his gaze over them and saw several old acquaintances.

Led by Xiao Yuyang, an elder of Star God Palace, with Gao Xueting of Qingyang Shrine, Chen Wenhao of Tianwu Sacred Land, and Feng Ming of Wuhua Hall, all gathered here. Of the four, only Xiao Yuyang was at the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm; the other three were at the Second Stage.
