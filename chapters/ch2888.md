## Chapter 2888 — Two Years

The earth groaned, and all living things suffered.

The demon invasion had already lasted a full two years. In that time, the two races had clashed thousands upon thousands of times. One Barbarian Tribe after another was wiped out, and countless demon armies were slaughtered. In the north of this land, battlefields lay everywhere, and nearly every moment, people died.

The Shaman Saints and Demon Saints had all finished healing and emerged from seclusion a month ago. They fought another great battle, but this time, both sides held back. Unlike the ferocity of two years prior, this clash was mostly a test of strength.

The Barbarian Tribe was at a disadvantage, because the demons now had one more Demon Saint—Mo Duo. This new Demon Saint broke the balance between the two sides.

Two years of bloody war had made everyone who survived much stronger.

The last remaining group of the Southern Barbarian Tribe had grown steadily during the war, now numbering a hundred thousand. Shaman Niu’s fame had spread throughout both races. It was no secret that he had ten Demon King slaves at his side, and it was thanks to these ten Demon Kings that Shaman Niu’s tribe had remained undefeated in this long war.

Two years was enough time for many changes to occur.

The greatest change was the demons’ reinforcements, which kept pouring in from the boundary passage. Although the passage was unstable and couldn’t allow truly powerful experts through, there was no limit to how many demon foot soldiers could pass.

Yet this still could not shake the Barbarian Tribes’ determination to reclaim their lost lands.

The demon clans numbered in the hundreds, and nearly every clan had its own innate abilities. If not for the ten Demon Kings Yang Kai had subdued, the Barbarian Tribe would have suffered far greater losses. The Demon Kings knew all the demons’ secrets, and Yang Kai had already gathered and passed on all their intelligence to the leaders of the major tribes. With this information, the Barbarian Tribe’s losses were greatly reduced; otherwise, the war would not have gone so smoothly.

Even though demons kept pouring out of the boundary passage, by now, two years later, the demons’ defensive line had shrunk to a territory with a radius of two thousand li centered on the passage.

All the demons were gathered there, while beyond those two thousand li, the major Barbarian tribes faced them from afar—millions of people locked in a standoff.

In the sky, a massive rift stretched across, refusing to close. Within the rift, Demonic Qi surged and rolled, terrifying to behold. Everyone could clearly see its presence.

It was like an eternal, unhealed scar across the sky.

This was the passage torn open by the Great Demon God of the Demon Domain with earth-shattering means, known to the Barbarians as the boundary passage.

As long as the boundary passage remained open, this war would never end.

All the Barbarian Tribe leaders understood this well. So, for two years, every Barbarian Tribe had worked toward this goal, pressing the demons hard, hoping to reach the boundary passage and find a way to seal it.

If the boundary passage could be sealed, the demons’ source of strength would be cut off. No more demons would come from the Demon Domain, and those left on this land would weaken, becoming fish on the chopping block.

The Barbarians had launched several massive assaults, but each was repelled by the demons. Both sides suffered losses, but neither could break through to the boundary passage.

Yang Kai himself had gone to investigate the boundary passage several times, but found that it was too vast and connected to another great world. It involved the laws of that world, and even though he was skilled in the Laws of Space, with his current strength, he could not repair it.

Unless he could ascend to the Great Emperor Realm.

For now, he was only an Upper-Level Shaman King. Though it seemed close to the Great Emperor Realm, it was still a distant dream.

In just over a year, he had advanced from Lower-Level to Upper-Level Shaman King. It was a miracle—no one in Barbarian Tribe history had ever advanced so quickly, but Yang Kai had done it. While his Emperor Realm foundation played a part, the greatest help was the sacred artifact Shaman Dang had given him before dying.

When he first received the artifact, he hadn’t noticed anything special about it.

Only after advancing to Shaman King did the artifact reveal its uniqueness.

Every time he entered seclusion to cultivate, the artifact seemed to become a medium, connecting him to the Shaman God Hall.

The Shaman God Hall was the most important place for the entire Barbarian Tribe. There, the memories and precious shamanic arts of all the tribe’s experts over countless years were gathered. With the artifact, Yang Kai could roam the Shaman God Hall with his Divine Soul, witnessing the wondrous abilities of countless sages and absorbing their knowledge.

This knowledge was even broader and deeper than what the Divine Tree had imparted.

After realizing this, he understood why the Bone-Eater Tribe’s Shaman Kings had wanted to seize his artifact. Clearly, they had long known how much it aided cultivation, which was why they coveted it.

A tribe with an Upper-Level Shaman King at its helm was not to be underestimated—especially when it also had ten Demon Kings.

Shaman Niu’s tribe, as the last surviving group of the Southern Barbarian Tribe, had endured and gained the recognition of all the major Barbarian tribes. The twin sisters Yu and Lu had also successfully advanced to the Great Shaman Master Realm, becoming second only to Yang Kai in the tribe. The other shamans had all improved to varying degrees. Those who had once followed Yang Kai had now become the backbone of Shaman Niu’s tribe.

Die was still the same as ever. Two years of war seemed to have left no mark on her, nor had her cultivation advanced at all.

She seemed indifferent to the war, always following Yang Kai. If he didn’t seek her out, she never appeared, but whenever he did, she would show up right away.

It was as if she could see through Yang Kai’s every thought.

Even after two years together, Yang Kai still felt he couldn’t see through this woman.

A knock sounded at the tightly closed door. Yang Kai let out a gentle breath, ending his journey through the Shaman God Hall with his Divine Sense. He put away the artifact and looked up, saying, “Come in.”

The twin sisters Yu and Lu pushed the door open and entered, their faces unusually serious.

“Do the Shaman Saints have new instructions?” Yang Kai looked up at them. The sisters had just answered the Shaman Saints’ summons, going with the other tribes’ Shaman Kings to a meeting. As Great Shaman Masters, they normally wouldn’t have qualified, but Shaman Niu’s tribe was special, and since Yang Kai rarely showed himself, no one minded.

During this time, Yang Kai had been focused on cultivation, so he had left the tribe’s affairs to Yu and Lu.

From their expressions as they returned, Yang Kai realized something significant was about to happen.

“It’s the final battle!” Yu replied, her voice trembling slightly.

Yang Kai raised his eyebrows. “They’ve found a way to seal the boundary passage?”

This was a difficult problem, one the Barbarians desperately wanted to solve. If they could seal the passage and leave the demons stranded, the Barbarians would eventually win.

But it was also an unsolvable problem. Even the four Shaman Saints didn’t have the power to close the passage. So, though the two races had fought constantly, there had been no final battle—everyone knew the war wouldn’t end unless the passage was sealed.

Now, with the Shaman Saints suddenly calling for a final battle, Yang Kai could only guess they had found a way to seal the passage.

This was good news, yet Yang Kai didn't believe the Shaman Saints possessed such ability—not from underestimation, but from understanding the facts.

“It seems they have.”

“Seems?” Yang Kai frowned. “That’s what the Shaman Saints told you?”

War could not tolerate uncertainty. A single ‘seems’ could mean hundreds of thousands of deaths.

Yu said, “Judging by their expressions, they seem confident, but they didn’t say for sure.”

Lu added, “They said you must go, no matter what.”

Yang Kai stroked his chin. “Looks like they really do have some confidence.”

If they weren’t confident, they wouldn’t have called him over again. They hadn’t said it outright, probably to keep the Shaman Kings from leaking the secret.

Without delay, Yang Kai left the Shaman Niu tribe’s residence and headed straight for the Shaman Saints’ location.

He had been there several times before, so he was familiar with the way. But since he’d been busy cultivating, he’d let Yu and Lu handle it. Whenever the Shaman Saints summoned him, he hadn’t shown himself.

This time, though, things were clearly different.

In a simple great hall, Yang Kai met the Shaman Saint Xu.

Xu was a unique figure among the four Shaman Saints—perhaps unique throughout history. Though a Shaman Saint, he knew few shamanic arts; all his power came from his physical body.

He was a master of the flesh.

The Barbarians valued body refinement, but in hundreds of thousands of years, only he had achieved such heights.

In the battle of the Saints two years ago, nearly all the Barbarian and Demon Saints were wounded—only he was unscathed, leading the other three Shaman Saints to retreat. His strength was clear.

Though Xu was a Shaman Saint, he had none of a Saint’s airs. Yang Kai had dealt with him several times and had a good impression. No matter what happened, this Shaman Saint always smiled kindly and was extremely approachable.

The other three Shaman Saints were nowhere to be seen, and Yang Kai let out a slight sigh of relief.

It wasn’t fear—he just didn’t want to face the Bone-Eater Tribe’s Saint. The Shaman Niu tribe and Bone-Eater Tribe had deep grudges, and Yang Kai had even killed one of their Shaman Kings. That Saint had always disliked Yang Kai. If not for the Shaman Niu tribe’s outstanding performance in the war, killing many demons and earning great merit, the Bone-Eater Saint would have acted against them long ago. This was one reason Yang Kai rarely answered the Shaman Saints’ summons.

Xu looked to be about forty, with a wide mouth, broad nose, square head, big ears, a ruddy face, a burly frame, and arms so long they nearly reached his knees. His hands were enormous, as if they could grasp a mountain or block a river.

Every time Yang Kai saw him, he was shocked by the fierce, surging power of Xu’s Qi and blood—even though Xu did his best to restrain it. If he didn’t, it would be even more terrifying.

Yang Kai had no doubt that even if a giant dragon stood before him, Xu could tear it apart with his bare hands.

If Yang Kai’s body was like the rising sun, then Xu’s was the blazing midday sun. Just standing beside him, one could feel the scorching heat.
