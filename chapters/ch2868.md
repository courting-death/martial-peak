## Chapter 2868 — Getting Along Happily

Atop a tall and imposing demonic beast, the square-faced Demon Commander sat upright, his body wreathed in Demonic Qi.

He led the way, followed by two thousand subordinates. Among these two thousand, some were mindless demonized barbarians who only knew how to obey orders, while the rest were members of the hundred demon clans, a mix of various races.

This was the extent of the power he could command at present.

The procession advanced at a leisurely pace, as if they were out for a stroll rather than heading to war.

The square-faced Demon Commander was in no hurry either. After all, Lord Bao Qi had said it himself—he was only to bring his men along to show some support. Whether they could actually provide real aid to Shaya’s side would be left to fate.

In truth, the square-faced Demon Commander looked down upon that wanton succubus. Of course, the main reason was sour grapes—he couldn't have her, so he belittled her. He even harbored some resentment toward Lord Bao Qi, thinking his own Demon King was making a fuss over nothing. That wretch had only come to fetch reinforcements, yet Lord Bao Qi had ordered him to lead two thousand men out for her. It was far too much honor to bestow.

That damned succubus must have served Lord Bao Qi very well to receive such support.

At this thought, a lewd smile crept onto the square-faced Demon Commander’s face. He had tasted a succubus before and knew their flavor was indeed unique. As for Shaya, being a Demon King among succubi, she was naturally on a different level from the rest.

If he ever managed to advance to Demon King himself, he would have to experience that wretch’s charms for himself.

Just as his thoughts were running wild, he suddenly sensed something unusual.

The square-faced Demon Commander immediately composed himself and looked ahead warily. What he saw made him exclaim in surprise, “Lady Shaya...?”

He saw Shaya, who had left just half a day ago, now approaching from the front. This puzzled him—he didn’t know why this woman had returned so soon. Though he looked down on her in his heart, she was still a Demon King, so the square-faced Demon Commander could only maintain the proper respect.

The demonic beast beneath him halted, and the two thousand behind him stopped as well.

“Bao Qi is quite obedient, isn’t he...” Shaya’s voice was soft and sultry, and the smile on her exposed face was full of hidden meaning.

The square-faced Demon Commander frowned, a strong sense of danger rising in his heart. Shaya’s attitude and expression made him uneasy. Instinctively, he surveyed his surroundings, and his heart jumped in alarm.

He realized this place was a perfect spot for an ambush—mountains rose on all sides, their cliffs reaching into the clouds, with only narrow passages at the front and back for entry and exit.

It seemed as if something was closing in from all directions. The ground trembled faintly. Even the most ordinary demons could sense such movement, let alone the square-faced Demon Commander himself.

He listened carefully, and his face suddenly changed. He shouted, “Prepare for battle!”

As soon as the words left his mouth, huge black shadows swooped down from unknown places, their outstretched wings blocking out the light above. The demons looked up to see one giant eagle after another.

With a whooshing sound, arrows rained down from the giant eagles, and in an instant, dozens of men fell into pools of blood.

An ambush! The thought flashed through the square-faced Demon Commander’s mind, but he couldn’t figure out who had set it up, nor what role Demon King Shaya played in all this.

Wasn’t he supposed to be coming to the rescue? How had he ended up caught so off guard?

The demons reacted quickly, but the enemy was even faster.

After a round of arrows, dazzling lights suddenly flashed from the surrounding forests. Circles of multicolored brilliance dazzled the eyes. Dozens of towering demonic beasts charged in from the flanks, each ridden by a burly, cold-faced warrior of the Barbarian Tribe, all exuding murderous intent.

“Outsiders!”

Their cries echoed to the heavens.

The square-faced Demon Commander turned his mount, drew his weapon, and raised it high, about to shout something to rally his troops, when suddenly he caught a whiff of a bewitching fragrance that seemed to intoxicate the soul.

A soft, bone-melting voice sounded behind him: “I’ll be taking your head now.”

In the next instant, the square-faced Demon Commander’s vision spun. He stared blankly at the scantily clad Demon King Shaya before him, felt a pain in his neck, and when he looked down, he saw a headless corpse tumbling from the back of the demonic beast.

At the outset of the battle, the square-faced Demon Commander was suddenly killed. All the demons who witnessed this were dumbfounded, staring at Shaya in a daze, their minds a complete mess.

Shaya giggled, her figure flickering as she turned into a cloud of black mist and drifted away into the distance.

At that moment, the Barbarian Tribe, empowered by various shamanic arts, closed in. The vanguard cavalry charged into the enemy ranks like a hot knife through butter. As cold light flashed, one demon after another fell.

The ten regular squads that followed quickly sealed off all escape routes, determined not to let a single demon slip away.

The battle erupted in an instant—five thousand against two thousand, the prepared against the unprepared. The outcome seemed decided from the start.

In midair, Shaya’s figure reappeared, holding the square-faced Demon Commander’s head in her hand. She called out crisply, “My lord, my task is complete.”

Yang Kai nodded lightly, his deep gaze cast downward. Die stood quietly behind him, like a shadow.

The battle had erupted into a frenzy from the very start. Shaman Niu's tribe, charging forward with the resolve to exterminate this demon force entirely, crashed against their foes with overwhelming momentum. Though the demons had lost their Demon Commander and lacked guidance, they remained well-trained. They huddled together in groups, desperately holding their ground against the tribe's relentless assault while searching for any possible avenue of escape.

Screams rang out, bodies fell, the stench of blood filled the air, and severed limbs flew. The demons suffered heavy casualties, while Shaman Niu’s tribe barely lost anyone.

After three months of constant fighting, Shaman Niu’s tribe had grown accustomed to the rhythm of war. Their cooperation was seamless, covering each other perfectly. Despite their overwhelming advantage in terrain and numbers, no one acted rashly. They steadily eroded the demons’ defenses, pushing them step by step toward their doom.

Yang Kai watched from above for less than the time it takes to burn a stick of incense before withdrawing his gaze. There was nothing left for him to worry about.

In fact, he hadn’t worried much about this battle at all. He simply had Bao Qi send two thousand men into his encirclement and had Shaya kill the square-faced Demon Commander. Shaman Niu’s tribe had taken care of everything else themselves.

Shaya, holding a severed head, quickly lost interest and tossed it aside.

An hour later, the dust settled.

Without needing Yang Kai to give orders, the captains of Shaman Niu’s tribe immediately tallied the casualties, while over a dozen shamans began treating the wounded.

After resting in place for half a day, Shaman Niu’s tribe set out again, leaving behind a battlefield in chaos, and camped thirty li ahead.

Three days later, another two-thousand-strong force sent by Bao Qi entered Shaman Niu’s tribe’s encirclement. The battle unfolded almost exactly as before. Ambushed by over five thousand, these two thousand demons were quickly routed and utterly destroyed.

With every battle, Shaman Niu’s tribe lost some people, but gained even more reinforcements.

Many among the demon forces were tribesmen who had been corrupted and converted by Demonic Qi. After three months of research, the twins Yu and Lu had mastered some methods, and more and more rescued tribesmen were being healed, with the success rate steadily rising.

This was excellent news.

After another three days of rest, once the aftereffects of the Bloodlust Art faded, Yang Kai immediately led Shaman Niu’s tribe in grand procession toward the Demon City.

Bao Qi had originally commanded six or seven thousand men, but after losing four thousand in two ambushes, only a little over two thousand remained to defend the city—no longer a threat.

When Shaman Niu’s tribe arrived at the Demon City, the demons were thrown into an uproar, not expecting these outsiders to be so bold. Though their numbers seemed great, the demons still didn’t take them seriously.

After all, they had a Demon King holding the fort. Many demons stood atop the city walls, pointing and mocking the crowd below, as if Bao Qi could wipe them all out with a single move.

What happened next left them utterly stunned.

Lord Bao Qi did appear, but not to single-handedly slaughter thousands of outsiders. Instead, he respectfully opened the city gates, allowing the outsiders to charge in and massacre the demons at will.

Many demons could not believe this was real, even as they died.

In front of the city gates, the high and mighty Lord Bao Qi stood with his head bowed, speaking to a young man shrouded in black qi, his attitude as respectful as if facing a Demon Saint.

Many recognized that young man—he was the one who had come with Demon King Shaya a few days ago. At the time, everyone thought he was Shaya’s slave.

But now it was clear that wasn’t the case at all, for even Shaya stood behind him, showing proper respect.

This was a bewildering war. The demons were inexplicably wiped out, and from start to finish, Bao Qi never intended to help them.

In just an hour, all the demons defending the city were slaughtered. Not even the most skilled Sand Demons could escape. Corpses littered the streets and alleys, the carnage horrific.

Shaman Niu’s tribe cleaned up the battlefield, while Yang Kai followed Bao Qi to the palace he had visited last time.

“Shaya will tell you what to do next. I’m going into seclusion to cultivate. Don’t disturb me unless it’s important,” Yang Kai said, then found a side room and went in.

Bao Qi was left staring resentfully at Shaya.

Shaya pursed her lips in a mocking smile. “Let’s hope we get along happily from now on.”

Bao Qi’s face darkened. He thought to himself that there was no way they’d get along happily. He didn’t want to obey Shaya’s orders, but with Yang Kai’s words, he dared not defy her. He felt thoroughly uncomfortable, as if he had swallowed a dead fly.
