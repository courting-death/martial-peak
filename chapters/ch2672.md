## Chapter 2672 — The Fish Has Taken the Bait

“Form the array!” With a sharp command, nearly three hundred disciples of the Flowing Shadow Sword Sect moved in unison, their steps tracing mysterious patterns. In an instant, they formed a world-shaking sword formation, their auras linking seamlessly, sword intent stacking layer upon layer.

Blocking the way before the City Lord’s mansion, it seemed as if there weren’t three hundred cultivators, but a single, world-shaking sword. The chilling sword intent was so clear that every cultivator in Flowing Shadow City could feel it.

Yang Kai looked at the sword formation ahead with disdain, sneering, “Sect Master Li, is this how your sect treats its guests?”

If the other party said yes, he wouldn’t mind slaughtering all three hundred of them.

Though this sword formation was impressive, it still couldn’t stop his advance.

From within the City Lord’s mansion came a warm, steady voice: “Let them in.”

Yang Kai had already reached the gates. No matter what, Li Qingyun couldn’t avoid him. Yet, to everyone’s surprise, Li Qingyun didn’t intend to make things difficult for Yang Kai, but instead ordered his disciples to let them pass.

“Sect Master!”

The disciple leading the sword formation, at the Third Stage of the Dao Origin Realm, looked back at the City Lord’s mansion in disbelief, as if he’d misheard.

With this sword formation set, combining the strength of three hundred disciples, even someone at the Emperor Realm would be skinned alive if they charged in. Yang Kai and his group had killed at the city gates, making them mortal enemies of the Flowing Shadow Sword Sect—how could they be let off so easily?

“Let them in!”

Li Qingyun’s voice rang out again, this time carrying more authority.

The disciple leading the formation hesitated, his expression shifting, then finally gritted his teeth and shouted, “Let them pass!”

Rumble...

The crowd parted to either side, and the overwhelming sword intent weakened considerably. With the crowd’s movement, the sword formation naturally collapsed.

“A wise choice.” A strange light flashed in Yang Kai’s eyes as he secretly found Li Qingyun rather interesting.

Amidst countless hostile gazes, Yang Kai strode inside with Ye Qinghan and Du Xian. Unlike Yang Kai’s calm composure, Ye Qinghan and Du Xian were both tense, swallowing nervously, their Origin Qi surging but held in check, afraid the Flowing Shadow Sword Sect might suddenly attack.

But even after they entered the City Lord’s mansion, nothing happened. The two exchanged glances and couldn’t help but breathe a sigh of relief.

In the main hall of the City Lord’s mansion, a figure sat cross-legged, upright as a sword, exuding the bearing of a grandmaster. Before him lay a three-foot-long green sword, which, for some reason, kept ringing out. As Yang Kai approached, the sound grew more urgent and frequent, as if warning of something.

Yang Kai couldn’t help but glance at the sword a few more times.

Li Qingyun still sat with his eyes closed. Suddenly, he reached out and stroked the sword, and it instantly fell silent. He spoke: “The Yuxu Sword is the Ancestral Treasure of my Flowing Shadow Sword Sect, and only the Sect Master may wear it. It can warn of danger—the stronger the enemy, the more urgent its warning. I’ve avoided many dangers thanks to it, but I fear this time, there’s no escape.”

“Sect Master Li!” Yang Kai cupped his fists and nodded slightly.

Standing behind Yang Kai, Ye Qinghan and Du Xian’s eyes were bloodshot, glaring at Li Qingyun with hatred, as if they wished to kill him on the spot.

“Who exactly are you?” Li Qingyun looked up at Yang Kai.

“Just a nameless junior. Even if I told you, Sect Master Li, you wouldn’t know.” Yang Kai replied calmly.

Li Qingyun said, “Reputation is earned. After today, your name will surely spread far and wide.”

Yang Kai smiled, “Fame and vanity are but dust. Life is a dream—only the Martial Dao is eternal!”

Li Qingyun’s eyes narrowed, a sharp light flashing as he looked at Yang Kai. Each word struck him like a heavy hammer, as if he’d glimpsed some profound truth, a feeling of clarity breaking through the clouds.

But when he tried to think it through, he found nothing, and couldn’t help but frown.

Li Qingyun sighed softly and said, "Indeed, each generation brings forth new talent, as the old give way to the new."

He looked dejected, as if struck by a blow. After a pause, he said, “Are you here to stand up for the Thousand Leaves Sect? If so, then do it. Though I know I’m no match, I won’t just sit and wait for death.”

Yang Kai turned to Ye Qinghan and Du Xian, asking, “Do you want me to kill him? Just nod, and I’ll take his life.”

Du Xian and Ye Qinghan gritted their teeth, the red in their eyes deepening, killing intent almost tangible.

It was this man who, a year ago, led a large group of Flowing Shadow Sword Sect disciples to invade the Thousand Leaves Sect, slaughtering countless disciples. For the past year, the two had wanted nothing more than to kill him and avenge their fallen brothers and sisters. Now, with their enemy before them and believing Yang Kai could do as he said, they still couldn’t bring themselves to nod.

As if their hearts were connected, Ye Qinghan closed her eyes and softly said, “Senior Brother!”

Du Xian took a deep breath and said, “A blood feud must be avenged by one’s own hand!”

Yang Kai smiled at them, “Then you’d best work hard at your cultivation. He’s an Emperor Realm cultivator.”

Du Xian gritted his teeth, “We’re younger than him.”

"Youth is the greatest advantage."

Li Qingyun nodded slightly, looking at the two with a complicated gaze. “This Lord will await your challenge at any time.”

Du Xian pointed at him, “Don’t worry. One day, the Flowing Shadow Sword Sect will fall by my hand, and your head will be mine.”

After saying this, Du Xian seemed to let out a long breath, his expression complicated.

Li Qingyun said slowly, “Sect Master Ye isn’t dead.”

“What?” Ye Qinghan and Du Xian were shocked. Ye Qinghan trembled with excitement, “Is that true?”

Li Qingyun nodded, “Sect Master Ye is currently in my Flowing Shadow Sword Sect. If you wish to see him, you can take him away at any time.”

Ye Qinghan was stunned, then burst into tears. Du Xian pulled her into his arms, face cold as he asked, “Why is that?”

Li Qingyun frowned, “There’s no deep hatred between our sects. We only acted against the Thousand Leaves Sect out of necessity. Someone got what they wanted, and whether Sect Master Ye lived or died no longer mattered.”

“What do you mean?” Du Xian looked at him in confusion.

Yang Kai’s expression changed, “So someone was behind this—was it the Star God Palace?”

Li Qingyun’s face changed, and he fell silent, clearly wary.

Seeing his reaction, Yang Kai knew he was afraid, so he changed the subject: “You just said someone got what they wanted. What did they want?”

Li Qingyun shook his head and didn’t answer.

Yang Kai snorted, “Sect Master Li, do you think I’m easy to deal with? The Flowing Shadow Sword Sect is nothing to me—I could destroy it with a wave of my hand!”

Li Qingyun replied solemnly, “The Flowing Shadow Sword Sect and I are nothing but pawns. If I don’t satisfy you, you’ll destroy us. If I do, others will destroy us. Either way, it’s just death.”

Yang Kai sneered, “It seems the one behind you is truly formidable, to make you so afraid!”

Li Qingyun shook his head, “There’s no need to probe further.”

Yang Kai snorted, “Fine. Since you’re so straightforward, I won’t make things difficult. Let me ask something you can answer. The Star Lord you captured from the Thousand Leaves Sect—where is he now?”

Li Qingyun said, “Since you already suspect, why ask? My Flowing Shadow Sword Sect is just a pawn pushed to the front. The person and the things we seized are no longer in our hands.”

Yang Kai’s face darkened. Though he’d suspected as much, hearing Li Qingyun confirm it still left him disappointed.

If Ai Ou was being held in Flowing Shadow City or the Flowing Shadow Sword Sect, he could rescue him now. But the key was, Ai Ou wasn’t here, and Li Qingyun probably didn’t know where he was being held.

That so-called auction was just a ploy, using the Flowing Shadow Sword Sect’s name and territory as cover.

After a moment’s thought, Yang Kai asked, “Besides the Star Lord, who else did you capture that day?”

Li Qingyun thought for a moment, “A few other cultivators from lower domains, but none as important as the Star Lord.”

“Are they alive or dead now?” Yang Kai asked, suppressing his excitement.

Li Qingyun shook his head, “I don’t know.”

Yang Kai frowned, pondering for a moment, then asked, “One last question—what does the person behind all this want?”

Li Qingyun still shook his head, “I don’t know.”

…

In a pavilion in Flowing Shadow City, an old man with a pale face sat cross-legged. He looked as if he could die at any moment, yet his Qi and blood were vigorous, and the aura he unconsciously released was terrifying—at the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm.

Beside the old man, a beautiful woman in palace attire knelt on the ground, her hair disheveled and her expression listless, as if she’d suffered torment. Her face was pale.

From time to time, a look of intense pain would flash across the woman’s face.

Thud, thud, thud…

Footsteps approached, followed by a knock at the door.

“Come in!” the old man called.

The door opened, and a middle-aged man entered, glanced at the kneeling woman, then quickly looked away and cupped his fists, “Master!”

The old man looked at him, “How is it?”

The middle-aged man replied, “The fish has taken the bait.”

At these words, the woman trembled, while the old man’s eyes flashed with a sharp light. He laughed, “Good, good, very good. My year of planning has finally paid off.”

The middle-aged man said, “Master, that boy is only at the First Stage of the Emperor Realm. Since he’s here, let me capture him. He won’t be able to stir up any trouble.”

The old man smiled faintly and shook his head, “Don’t underestimate him. He’s skilled in space powers. Even though he’s only at the First Stage of the Emperor Realm, if we’re not fully prepared, even I might not be able to capture him.”

The middle-aged man seemed unconvinced, thinking his master overestimated the boy. He himself was at the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm—surely he could capture someone at the First Stage with ease, so why all the caution?

The old man knew what he was thinking. He glanced at the kneeling woman and said, “Your Junior Sister followed him for quite some time. She knows his strength best. If you don’t believe me, just ask her.”
