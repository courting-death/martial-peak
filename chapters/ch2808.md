## Chapter 2808 — Raging Flame Tribe

The one-month period had arrived, yet he was still lingering here.

That was impossible.

Lan Xun had previously said that the opening of the Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda could only last for a month. Once the time was up, the entrance would close, and then the entire Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda would generate an irresistible repulsion, ejecting all martial artists who entered.

In other words, once the time was up, no matter where Yang Kai and the others were, they would return to Star God Palace.

But in reality, the deadline had already passed by three days, and Yang Kai hadn’t felt any repulsion from this world at all.

For three days, Yang Kai tried various methods in the house, but couldn’t leave. After much thought, he figured either the Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda had a problem, or he had been trapped by this secret realm.

The Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda was the foundation of Star God Palace, passed down for tens of thousands of years. If there were going to be problems, they would have happened long ago. It couldn’t just happen to him.

So the latter possibility was more likely.

Was there something special about this secret realm? Why had it trapped him?

After three days of pondering, Yang Kai gradually calmed down.

The Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda was the wealth of Star God Palace. Now that he was trapped inside, Star God Palace would surely make some effort. If the Elders were powerless, then the Great Emperor would definitely act personally. So worrying about his situation was pointless; it was better to wait for help from outside while searching for a way out here.

He tidied up briefly and followed the direction Ah Hu and the others had gone, chasing out of the village.

The canyon was over a hundred li from the village. Last time, Yang Kai had chased for a whole day before arriving. This time, though he didn’t need to rush as much, he couldn’t delay either. If the heavy snow sealed the mountain, the barbarian villagers would be unable to move.

Led by Yang Kai, a group of over a hundred people finally arrived at the canyon before dusk.

Yang Kai suddenly halted his stride, raising his hand high.

The hundred villagers behind him stopped in unison, like a well-trained army, instantly falling silent. Yet the long journey left them all panting.

Ah Hu came up quietly and asked, “What’s wrong?”

“Someone’s here!” Yang Kai replied.

Ah Hu’s expression changed. “Are you sure?”

Every barbarian here was an expert at surviving in the jungle, with almost beast-like instincts for tracking and counter-tracking. Ah Hu hadn’t noticed anything unusual, but since Yang Kai spoke so seriously, he didn’t dare take it lightly.

There were no footprints left in the snow, but perhaps today’s light snowfall had covered them.

Yang Kai tilted his head and signaled to a certain woman in the crowd.

Ah Hua immediately unfastened the bow and arrows from her back, moving like the wind toward the canyon’s side wall, quickly disappearing from sight.

The group waited in place for a long time before Ah Hua returned, braving the biting wind, her face grave. “There are indeed people. There’s a bonfire deep in the canyon. I can’t tell how many; it’s too dark.”

Ah Hu said angrily, “It must be people from another tribe who found the dead beasts here. Damn, if only we’d come a few days earlier.”

Barbarians didn’t coexist peacefully; there were frequent conflicts, especially over food.

There were three hundred beasts in the canyon, all killed by Yang Kai. That was a huge supply, enough to feed a village for an entire winter.

“Hopefully they’re also from the Southern Barbarian Tribe. Then we can negotiate. You all wait here, I’ll go check.” Ah Hu said, about to head into the canyon.

Cangnan Village belonged to the Southern Barbarian Tribe. If everyone was from the same tribe, things would be easy—at worst, split the food in half.

“Wait!” Yang Kai stopped him. “No need for all that trouble.”

Ah Hu looked at him curiously.

Yang Kai gently took a breath, raised a hand, and began to speak in an ancient, awkward language. A faint power stirred within him.

The next moment, the air in front of Yang Kai rippled, like a calm lake disturbed by a stone. As the ripples spread, a semi-transparent image appeared before everyone.

The image seemed to face the canyon entrance, and as Yang Kai controlled it, it moved deeper inside.

“Eagle Eye Art!” Ah Hu’s eyes lit up.

This was the village chief’s Eagle Eye Art, a magical shamanic art that gave the user eagle-like vision—perfect for scouting.

Ah Niu had only become a Shaman a few days ago, yet he’d already mastered such a profound shamanic art. No wonder the village chief said the village had a successor; the future depended on Ah Niu.

Over a hundred villagers stood behind Yang Kai, all excited and full of worship.

Eagle Eye Art silently advanced into the canyon, revealing everything along the way.

When Yang Kai pushed Eagle Eye Art deep into the canyon, the light from more than ten bonfires appeared. Around each bonfire gathered seven or eight robust barbarians, roasting golden beast meat, laughing and chatting.

Ah Hu and the others’ eyes turned red.

That beast meat belonged to Cangnan Village, yet now it was claimed by a bunch of strangers.

The hatred of having one’s food stolen was irreconcilable!

Ah Hua’s expression changed slightly, and she whispered, “It’s the Raging Flame Tribe!”

She had seen the fierce patterns painted on those barbarians’ faces.

“It’s actually the Raging Flame Tribe!” Ah Hu’s face darkened. “Why is it those savage guys?”

Yang Kai cast him a leisurely glance, thinking to himself that in his eyes there was little difference between them—yet they still had the nerve to call others savage.

Just then, beside a bonfire, a burly figure suddenly stood up. His sharp gaze flickered like flames, locking onto the Eagle Eye Art. In the villagers’ view, it was as if this barbarian was staring right at them, making everyone feel a chill.

“Hmph!” The burly man snorted, thrusting a staff-like object forward. The next moment, the Eagle Eye Art was broken, and a burst of fire exploded in front of Yang Kai.

“Hmm… We’ve been discovered.” Yang Kai raised an eyebrow.

“That’s their Shaman!” Ah Hu’s face turned ashen.

Meeting Raging Flame Tribe people in the wild was bad enough, but having a powerful Shaman with them was a disaster for the villagers.

Among all barbarian tribes, the Raging Flame Tribe was ranked in the top three for ferocity, second only to the Bone-Eater Tribe and Blood Tide Tribe.

“Ah Niu, retreat quickly.” Ah Hu urged. Cangnan Village’s villagers were strong, but fighting the Raging Flame Tribe, Ah Hu and the others had little confidence. They’d traveled all day, while the Raging Flame Tribe was rested, with a huge advantage in stamina.

Most importantly, Ah Niu was in the group. He was the village’s future hope and couldn’t be allowed any harm.

So the smartest move now was to leave quickly. As long as Ah Niu was safe, giving up the food didn’t matter.

“Too late.” Yang Kai shrugged, looking up at the canyon walls.

The villagers followed his gaze, and their hearts sank.

On both sides of the canyon walls, a dozen figures appeared, each holding fierce bows, arrows drawn and aimed at them.

However, the other party didn't immediately loose their arrows. Clearly, they wanted to give Cangnan Village a chance to retreat while they still could. The Raging Flame Tribe people were indeed fierce and quarrelsome, but they lacked the savage brutality of the Bone-Eater Tribe.

If they’d met Bone-Eater Tribe people, it would already be raining arrows.

That tribe even ate barbarians! They were outcasts among barbarians. If not for a powerful Shaman Saint guarding them, they would have been wiped out by the other tribes long ago.

Ah Hua and the other archers reacted quickly. Sensing they were being targeted, they immediately spread out, drawing their bows and preparing to counterattack.

But in numbers and position, they were at a huge disadvantage.

If a real fight broke out, Cangnan Village would suffer heavy casualties.

“Since they’re so enthusiastic, let’s go meet them!” Yang Kai grinned, suddenly raising a finger. A spot of bright white light appeared at his fingertip.

“Shaman!” Someone on the canyon wall exclaimed, clearly surprised.

In ancient times, Shamans were rare. Any Shaman was a precious asset to the barbarians. So when the Raging Flame Tribe archers saw Yang Kai use shamanic arts, they all frowned.

Killing ordinary villagers wasn’t a big deal; countless barbarians died every year from internal strife. But if a Shaman died at their hands, it could provoke the wrath of the Shaman Kings and Shaman Saints!

Only Shamans could kill Shamans.

“I want to see your leader!” Yang Kai shouted, his voice echoing at the canyon entrance.

The Raging Flame Tribe didn’t dare ignore a Shaman's request.

Someone immediately ran down from the canyon wall, deep into the canyon, apparently to report.

Soon, the archers on both sides of the cliff withdrew in unison. From the canyon entrance, a towering figure emerged alone, walking fearlessly toward Yang Kai and the others. A sharp aura swept across them like a cutting wind.

Ah Hu snorted coldly and stood at Yang Kai’s side, locking eyes with the Barbarian warrior. The two glared at one another like a pair of stubborn old turtles, neither willing to yield.

Yang Kai patted Ah Hu’s shoulder, signaling him to stay calm, then smiled at the barbarian warrior. “Hello, friend from the Raging Flame Tribe. I want to see your leader.”

The barbarian warrior finally shifted his gaze from Ah Hu, looked down at Yang Kai, and asked in surprise, “You’re their Shaman?”

“Absolutely!”

The barbarian warrior frowned. “Why are you so weak…”
