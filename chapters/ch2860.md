## Chapter 2860 — Aftermath of the Battle

The battle ended swiftly. The demons had already been at a tremendous disadvantage, suffering countless casualties. Shaya pinned all her hopes on Yang Kai, but at the final moment, the seemingly loyal Yang Kai dealt her a fatal blow.

With their commander gone, the demons became a scattered mob, with no coordination between squads, acting only on instinct and charging about wildly. This, in turn, allowed the Barbarian Tribe warriors to reap their lives with ease.

When Yang Kai led the twins back to the battlefield, the situation had already become a one-sided slaughter.

The light of Shamanic Arts flashed and blossomed again and again, felling one demon after another. Even the strongest among them could not stir up any waves before such a united Barbarian Tribe army.

The Sand Demons, skilled at escape, tried to flee, only to find the earth no longer sheltered them for some unknown reason. The vast ground was dyed gold by the light of a Shamanic Art, and no matter how they used their innate divine abilities, they could not escape. In the end, they were exterminated one by one. The Shadow Demons, who came and went without a trace, also lost their advantage. With the aid of the Eagle Eye Art, the Sky Cavalry could always pinpoint their whereabouts and unleash deadly arrows. As for the strongest demon cavalry squad, it had long since been wiped out, leaving only a few Demonic Beasts howling on the battlefield.

The screams echoed endlessly across the wilderness, sending chills down the spine of all who heard them.

This was the victory of the Barbarian Tribe, the victory of Shaman Niu's tribe—and a glorious, resounding triumph!

When the last demon fell, all the Barbarians panted heavily, their eyes bloodshot as they scanned their surroundings, unable to spot a single remaining enemy.

Between heaven and earth, a chilling, murderous aura pervaded.

The tribesmen stood amidst mountains of corpses and seas of blood, like invincible war gods, awe-inspiring and unassailable.

A warrior raised his weapon high and howled at the sky, venting his excitement and joy.

More tribesmen responded, and the chorus of howls was enough to change the color of the heavens and earth, eclipsing sun and moon.

Yang Kai, shrouded in black qi, soared into the air and surveyed the area. His Divine Sense meticulously swept every corner within dozens of li, and only after confirming there were no fish that slipped through the net did he shout, "Clean up the battlefield and count the wounded!"

The ten regular squads received the order and, under their captains' leadership, busied themselves.

After this battle, everyone held the utmost respect for their commander.

However, more than a dozen shamans looked at Yang Kai with some concern. After all, the demonic qi on Shaman Niu was so obvious that no one knew if he would truly be demonized. If that really happened, this victory would be meaningless.

If even the commander was turned into a demon, no matter how great the victory, it would still be a defeat.

Sensing their thoughts, Yang Kai glanced at them but did not explain much. Instead, he said, "Yu, Lu, take people to check the demon lair over there. That will be our base for the next few months."

"Yes!" The twins received the order and immediately led the guards toward the demon lair.

Soon, the casualties from this battle were tallied. Shaman Niu's tribe had nearly three thousand people, with over two hundred dead—about the size of a regular squad. Almost everyone else was wounded. Such losses were nothing short of a miracle. If Shaya had not mismanaged and been overconfident, the Barbarians could not have gotten off so lightly.

She had focused all her attention on Yang Kai, only to be bitterly disappointed in the end.

In this battle, over three thousand demons were slain, and there were even some captives.

True demons would never be captured—they were all dead. The captives were those who had been turned into demonized people by demonic qi. They were originally part of the Barbarian Tribe, sent by Shaya as vanguard and the first to clash with Shaman Niu's tribe.

Although Yang Kai had warned everyone before the battle not to show mercy, when facing their former comrades, many still chose to wound them severely rather than kill them.

Thus, quite a few of the transformed tribesmen survived, but they were still entangled by demonic qi and had little sanity left.

A dozen or so shamans gathered together, seemingly trying to find a way to save these tribesmen, though it was unclear what results they would achieve.

Besides these demonized tribesmen, Shaman Niu's tribe also captured dozens of Demonic Beasts in this battle. Handling Demonic Beasts was much simpler. Previously, when Yang Kai went to scout, he had brought back a Demonic Beast from the demon lair.

After several experiments by the shamans, they were pleasantly surprised to find that Beast Taming Art actually worked on Demonic Beasts as well.

Although Demonic Beasts were harder to tame than Barbaric Beasts, as long as Beast Taming Art worked, these Demonic Beasts could become mounts for Shaman Niu's tribe. With dozens of Demonic Beasts, they could form a small cavalry unit.

The impressive presence of the Sky Cavalry had long made the other tribesmen envious. The sight of the demon cavalry charging in formation was still vivid in their minds, and naturally, they fantasized about one day doing the same themselves.

Now, the opportunity had arrived.

However, dozens of Demonic Beasts were still too few for an army of three thousand. Moreover, not everyone could ride a Demonic Beast. Even if the shamans could tame them, the demonic qi on the beasts could not be dispelled. To ride such a creature, one first needed the ability to resist the erosion of demonic qi.

All signs indicated that only the strongest warriors were qualified to ride these Demonic Beasts and become cavalry.

Without rest, Shaman Niu's tribe remained busy for an entire day after the great battle before finally cleaning up the battlefield.

Over a thousand people worked together to dig a large pit not far away, burying all the demon corpses within. The shamans then set them ablaze, the fire lighting up half the sky. The smell of roasting flesh filled the air, making all who smelled it want to retch.

The situation at the demon lair was also clarified. Shaya had never imagined she would lose this battle, so she had committed almost all her forces to the field. Only a few were left in the lair, and when Yu and Lu led the guards over, those remaining demons were slaughtered before they could react.

The demon lair hidden in the mountain instantly changed hands, becoming the main base of Shaman Niu's tribe!

In the days that followed, the tribesmen recuperated. After such a battle, everyone was exhausted, especially after the effects of Bloodlust Art wore off—even the strongest could not resist the fatigue.

In other words, for the next few days, they had little fighting strength. If a large force of demons attacked now, they would be at the enemy's mercy.

The dozen or so shamans were so busy their feet barely touched the ground, their faces haggard.

They had to heal the demonized tribesmen, tame the captured Demonic Beasts, and cast Shamanic Arts to treat the wounded—each shaman wished they could split themselves into a dozen or twenty copies. The twins Yu and Lu had been pale-faced these past few days, their haggard expressions a clear sign of insufficient rest and depleted shamanic power.

Even Die was the same, not to mention the other Shaman Warriors and Shaman Apprentices, who would sometimes collapse from exhaustion and start snoring on the spot.

After only a short nap, they would wake with a start, wipe the drool from their mouths, and continue working.

Yang Kai was nowhere to be seen. Since arriving at the demon lair, he had occupied the stone house that once belonged to Demon King Shaya, ignoring all visitors.

No one knew what his condition was.

Yet in private, the shamans worried for their commander, fearing he might suddenly turn into a demon and attack his own people.

Of course, Yang Kai could not possibly become a demon.

His condition was unexpectedly good, aside from being shrouded in demonic qi.

Yet when he had been afflicted by the Green Demon's poison, he found it strange. The Green Demon's poison was not the mightiest toxin among the demon clans, but it was absolutely the most insidious—silent and inescapable. Once poisoned, there was no cure unless one transformed into a demonized person.

The Green Demon clan held little status among the hundred demon clans because their poison did not affect demons, only outsiders.

Yet, although Yang Kai had been poisoned, he felt no abnormality, which gave him a vague suspicion.

To test this, he even risked his life and actively swallowed a drop of Shaya's demon blood.

As expected, even Shaya's demon heart blood had no effect on him. It only changed his aura somewhat, and the effect of the demon heart blood actually dispelled the Green Demon Poison entirely.

"Ancient Demon Qi, huh," Yang Kai muttered to himself.

In his Dantian, he still had a seal of extremely pure Ancient Demon Qi. Back outside Fenglin City, he had been eroded by that ancient demonic qi and turned into a mindless demon. Thanks to the sealing power of the gold and silver tree, he had regained himself.

Having experienced such a transformation, Yang Kai had developed a strong resistance to demonic qi.

Unless the demonic qi he encountered was purer than what was sealed in his Dantian, it could not affect him.

Whether it was Green Demon Poison or Shaya's demon heart blood, both were far inferior to what was in his Dantian and naturally could not affect him.

Even Shaya's demon heart blood could not compare. Just what was that ancient one-eyed giant demon? Could it have been a Demon Saint? Yang Kai vaguely felt that the one-eyed giant demon he saw in the memories within the ancient demonic qi—whose body was like a mountain—was an anomaly even among the demons.

After all, the Incarnation now possessed the demon battle axe, which had belonged to that ancient one-eyed giant demon. The grade of that demon battle axe was extremely high, not inferior to a top Imperial Treasure.

Ordinary demons could not possess such a weapon; only experts among the demons were qualified to wield it.

In those memories, countless ancient powerhouses had charged forward, sacrificing their lives in hopes of killing the one-eyed giant demon. Yet, after paying a tremendous price, they still could not destroy it completely, only managing to seal its single eye with every means at their disposal.

Countless years later, the seal weakened, Fenglin City suffered disaster, and Yang Kai obtained the demon eye, fusing with it and refining a black pupil.

This was his trump card, one he dared not use lightly.
