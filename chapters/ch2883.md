## Chapter 2883 — Torrent Fortress

Yang Kai's figure suddenly appeared, as if he had been hiding nearby all along.

The eight Demon Kings led by Fei Li all stiffened, bowing in unison. "My lord!"

Yang Kai nodded, offering neither praise nor pleasantries, only saying, "I want not a blade of grass left growing in Torrent Fortress, not even a chicken or dog left in peace!"

As soon as he finished speaking, he waved his hand, and the eight Demon Kings were thrown out of the Small Xuan Realm, returning to the grand hall.

Fei Li, Mo Ke Tuo, and the others exchanged glances, a chill rising from the depths of their hearts, as if it could freeze their very blood. All eight Demon Kings knew that Lord Shaman Niu was avenging his tribesmen. The curse and oath he left a month ago were no joke—he truly intended to carry them out, and they were the sharpest tools in his hands.

"Let's discuss," Fei Li said, developing a headache. Though he had no flesh, only a skeleton, he still felt that long-forgotten ache—Lord Shaman Niu had given these Demon Kings quite the problem.

Their strength was indeed formidable, but with over a hundred thousand demons in Torrent Fortress, there was no way they could slaughter them all by themselves. To complete the task, they would have to employ some schemes.

The only advantage they had now was that all the Demon Kings originally stationed at Torrent Fortress were dead. Torrent Fortress was leaderless. If the eight of them acted wisely, perhaps they could seize control of Torrent Fortress and then find a way to fulfill the mission.

The eight Demon Kings gathered in the grand hall, discussing in earnest—not how to deal with outsiders, but how to wipe out every last demon in Torrent Fortress.

Pooling their ideas, the eight Demon Kings quickly came up with a plan and then dispersed.

A day later, rumors began to swirl throughout Torrent Fortress. All sorts of gossip spread among the demons—some said an army of outsiders was invading, some claimed the Demon Kings had defected, others that the demons were doomed to a crushing defeat.

No one knew where the rumors came from, but they kept spreading and fermenting among the crowd.

The demons stationed in Torrent Fortress were already idle, longing to charge onto the battlefield. The rumors only magnified their pent-up emotions, shrouding the entire fortress in a strange atmosphere.

At first, the rumors didn't have much effect, but as time passed and no Demon King stepped forward to clarify anything, many demons began to grow suspicious.

After three days, the emotions stirred up by the rumors finally exploded. No demon could remain uninvolved—willingly or not, all were dragged in, unable to think straight.

It was at this moment that Fei Li and the other Demon Kings stepped out, each gathering a group and dividing up the more than a hundred thousand demons in the city.

Immediately, civil war broke out.

Inside Torrent Fortress, large and small groups of demons clashed in the streets and alleys, fighting without reason. Demons fell in droves, and the fortress was nearly awash in blood. Those who had shared drinks just days before were now mortal enemies.

No one knew why this was happening—only that it was the Demon Kings' order. They simply obeyed, never questioning the reason.

No one would have imagined that the lofty Demon Kings would betray them, let alone turn their blades on their own kind.

Some Demon Commanders and clever Demon Generals sensed something was wrong, but smart ones never lived long. Fei Li and the other Demon Kings kept a close eye on those Demon Commanders and Demon Generals—if anyone grew suspicious, they would find any excuse to kill them.

Torrent Fortress became a giant meat grinder, trapping and shredding over a hundred thousand demons within.

The civil war never stopped from the moment it began. By the end, every demon's eyes were blood-red, their minds lost, mechanically raising their weapons to hack at any enemy in sight.

The slaughter lasted three days and nights. More than a hundred thousand demons were split into eight factions, fighting each other. The streets and alleys of Torrent Fortress piled up with countless corpses, swarmed by flies and mosquitoes, reeking of rot.

After three days, fewer than ten thousand survived, all wounded, many missing limbs, moaning softly.

The eight Demon Kings hovered in the sky, silently gazing down at the scene below—a vision of hell.

Then, they acted together, sending the remaining ten thousand wounded demons to the underworld, erasing Torrent Fortress from existence.

A flicker of movement—Yang Kai appeared like a ghost.

The eight Demon Kings bowed in salute.

"From now on, you will follow me," Yang Kai said. After what happened at Torrent Fortress, Fei Li and the others could never return to the demon ranks.

The eight Demon Kings nodded in complicated silence.

Yang Kai opened the entrance to the Small Xuan Realm, letting them enter one by one, then turned and flew off in another direction.

Ten days later, Yang Kai arrived above a stretch of forest. His deep gaze searched below, and after a moment, as if discovering something, he dove straight down.

Landing, he looked in a certain direction and shouted coldly, "Come out!"

From behind a large tree, a graceful figure slowly emerged. Upon seeing Yang Kai, her face lit up with joy and surprise. She called out softly, "My lord, it really is you! I thought I was mistaken."

As she spoke, she rushed toward Yang Kai.

Yang Kai looked at her with icy eyes.

When Shaya was just three zhang away, she suddenly stopped, as if sensing something. Her expression changed, and she hurriedly explained, "My lord, I did not betray you, truly! Please believe me."

Yang Kai said coldly, "If you had any thought of betrayal, you'd already be dead."

Shaya's face turned pale with fear. The joy of seeing Yang Kai vanished in an instant.

"Explain. What happened? Why are you here alone? Where is Bao Qi? Where is Shaman Niu's tribe?" Yang Kai fired off several questions.

In his perception, Shaya and Bao Qi were not together, which puzzled him—so he had come straight here. At first, he thought one of them had betrayed him and left Shaman Niu's tribe to act alone, but upon seeing Shaya, he realized his guess was wrong.

Shaya had no intention of betrayal—she was just anxious and uneasy, like a lost rabbit.

"Bao Qi is with the others," Shaya replied quickly. "They were all captured."

Yang Kai's eyes narrowed. "Captured? By whom?"

Shaman Niu's tribe had twenty thousand people, dozens of shamans, a thousand cavalry, and several dozen Sky Cavalry. Even if they couldn't compare to a demon stronghold like Torrent Fortress, they were still a formidable force among the Barbarian Tribe.

Who could possibly capture all twenty thousand?

"It was your Barbarian Tribe—seems it was the Bone-Eater Tribe!" Shaya recalled.

Yang Kai's expression changed. "Are you sure it was the Bone-Eater Tribe?"

"I don't know what the Bone-Eater Tribe looks like, but Die said so. She said those people were all from the Bone-Eater Tribe."

"Why would the Bone-Eater Tribe target my Shaman Niu's tribe? You all..." Yang Kai suddenly realized the problem before finishing his sentence.

Shaya said, "Bao Qi and I were discovered by them. They seemed to think Shaman Niu's tribe was under our control, so they attacked. No matter how the twins tried to explain, it was useless. Instead, those Shaman Kings injured them. Bao Qi and I couldn't beat them—Bao Qi was captured, and I escaped, hiding ever since."

Shaya spoke sadly, on the verge of tears. Just a few months ago, she had commanded thousands of demons as a lofty Demon King. Now, she was a stray dog, hunted by all.

The Barbarian Tribe despised her background and identity—if they saw her, they'd attack. She didn't dare return to the demons either, fearing Mo Duo would discover something. A dignified Demon King, yet forced to hide like a rat, never daring to show her face.

"Were there any casualties in Shaman Niu's tribe?" Yang Kai asked gravely.

Shaya shook her head. "Only Yu and Lu were slightly injured. Everyone else is fine. Die ordered everyone not to fight back."

"Not fighting back was the right choice," Yang Kai nodded. In that situation, if Shaman Niu's tribe had resisted, they would never have cleared themselves of suspicion. Just being with two Demon Kings was already suspicious enough. The Bone-Eater Tribe's doubts were understandable. Not fighting was one thing—if they had resisted, the two tribes would have gone to war.

For the Bone-Eater Tribe to capture Bao Qi and drive off Shaya, they must have sent many experts. Shaman Niu's tribe had no chance of victory.

"They were all taken to the Bone-Eater Tribe's camp. I don't know what their situation is now," Shaya said worriedly, sneaking glances at Yang Kai. "My lord, are we going to rescue them?"

Yang Kai said, "Of course I will rescue my tribe."

Shaya said, "But I..."

"You don't need to show yourself. Just stay put." As he spoke, Yang Kai reached out and grabbed her, tossing her straight into the Small Xuan Realm.

Yang Kai turned and looked in another direction.

He didn't need Shaya to point the way—he could sense it. Bao Qi was over there. He had planted a Soul Brand in Bao Qi's mind, a bond that couldn't be easily severed.

Without delay, he sped off at full speed.

Whether it was Heaven’s Will or fate, Shaman Niu's tribe of twenty thousand had fallen into the hands of the Bone-Eater Tribe. This made him uneasy. Not only was the Bone-Eater Tribe an outlier among the Barbarian Tribe—if not for a Shaman Saint holding them together, they would have been wiped out by the other tribes long ago—but Yang Kai had also clashed with them before, making him worry even more for his subordinates' safety.

The Bone-Eater Tribe was notoriously vengeful. Last time, that Shaman Chi fellow was wounded by Yang Kai and escaped—he surely held a grudge. If not for the demon invasion, the Bone-Eater Tribe would have already taken harsher revenge.
