## Chapter 2837 — The Great Calamity

He had previously thought that such a remote corner would not attract any experts, so he hadn’t paid much attention. But now, upon sensing that Yang Kai was actually a Great Shaman Master, he no longer dared to act recklessly.

“Greetings, honored sirs!”

Supported by Die, Yang Kai stood up, frowned at him, and nodded, “No need for such formalities.”

Hearing this, the Shaman Master straightened up and asked curiously, “Which village are you two from, honored sirs?”

He didn’t recognize Yang Kai or Die, but two Great Shaman Masters could not be obscure figures—he should have at least heard of them. Especially since, as the messenger connecting the tribe with the various villages, he was familiar with all the Shaman Masters in the tribe, if not intimately, then at least by reputation.

“Cangnan Village!” Yang Kai replied.

“Huh?” The Shaman Master was stunned, looking at Yang Kai with suspicion, “Cangnan Village?”

How could Cangnan Village have two Great Shaman Masters? He remembered that the village chief of Cangnan Village was merely a Shaman Apprentice, and a Lower-Level one at that. So Yang Kai’s words were hard to believe, but as a Great Shaman Master, there was no reason for him to lie about such a thing.

He asked in a daze, “Honored sir, are you from Cangnan Village?”

“That’s right,” Yang Kai looked at him and said, “Is there something you need?”

“What about Shaman Li?”

Shaman Li was the old village chief’s name. Hearing this, Yang Kai immediately understood that this man knew the old chief, and that he had probably come looking for him.

With a slightly somber expression, he said, “The old chief is no longer here. I am now the village chief!”

“Shaman Li is actually...” The man was momentarily lost, but didn’t dwell on it. After all, Shaman Li was old and not highly cultivated, so it was natural he wouldn’t live long. Perhaps his lifespan had ended and he had returned to the embrace of the Barbarian God. He then asked, “How should I address you, honored sir?”

“Shaman Niu!”

The man nodded, his expression turning solemn as he declared, “Honored Shaman Niu, by order of the Shaman King, you are to gather all the villagers of Cangnan Village within five days and assemble at the royal city!”

“The Shaman King’s order?” Yang Kai was taken aback.

Although the Southern Barbarian Tribe was not the strongest among the Barbarian Tribes, it still had a Shaman King overseeing it. As far as Yang Kai knew, this Shaman King was an Upper-Level one. The place where the Shaman King resided was the royal city of the Southern Barbarian Tribe, just as Frost Snow City was for the Frost Snow Tribe. All the small tribes and villages of the Southern Barbarian Tribe were clustered around this royal city, and both the city and the Shaman King held absolute authority over them.

This sudden order made Yang Kai sense something unusual, as if something major was about to happen.

“It is indeed the Shaman King’s order!” the man said seriously. “I originally came to inform Shaman Li, but since you are now the chief of Cangnan Village, it’s the same telling you.”

Yang Kai narrowed his eyes and asked, “Has a tribe declared war on us?”

In ancient times, conflicts among the Barbarian Tribes were common, as they fought over the limited land on which they depended for survival. For the sake of their own tribe’s interests, wars between tribes were frequent.

And whenever such things happened, everyone had to be mobilized—no one could avoid it.

So upon hearing this order, Yang Kai instinctively thought that some tribe had declared war on the Southern Barbarian Tribe. The most likely culprit was the brutal and inhuman Bone-Eater Tribe. If that was the case, Yang Kai wouldn’t mind being the vanguard.

It just so happened that today’s duel with Shaman Chi had ended without a decisive outcome, leaving Yang Kai feeling rather regretful.

The messenger gave a bitter smile and slowly shook his head. In a low voice, he said, “If it were only a tribe declaring war on us, it wouldn’t be such a big deal.”

Yang Kai was startled, “Is there something even more serious?”

The messenger hesitated, then said, “This matter is supposed to be confidential and not to be leaked. But since you two are both Great Shaman Masters, you’ll be summoned by the Shaman King at the royal city anyway, and you’ll learn the truth then.” Lowering his voice, he said, “It’s a demon invasion!”

“W-what? Say that again?” Yang Kai almost thought he’d misheard.

The messenger’s expression was extremely grave as he repeated, “A demon invasion!”

Yang Kai was stunned for a long while before he drew a sharp breath, his face full of shock. “Is it true?”

The messenger replied, “Three great tribes have already been wiped out. There can be no mistake.”

Yang Kai’s expression instantly grew heavy. Although the Ancient Barbarian Tribe had a sparse population, three great tribes still meant at least hundreds of thousands of people. To be wiped out just like that—this was truly a world-shaking event.

Demon invasion.

This term was not unfamiliar to him, but he couldn’t say he knew it well. From the knowledge inherited from Qing, he knew that this land had been invaded by demons more than once. The most recent was thirty thousand years ago, when Qing was just a sapling that had gained sentience and was striving to cultivate on this earth.

He had personally witnessed the devastation caused by the demon invasion, seen countless deaths, and watched as the people of this land sacrificed their lives to halt the demons’ advance. Generation after generation threw themselves into the fray, paying with their lives. It took nearly a thousand years to wrest this land back from the demons.

During that thousand years, the land was filled with wailing. After the war, it took ten thousand years for the mountains and rivers to recover, eventually giving rise to the Ancient Barbarian Tribe, who became the masters of this land.

And now, the events of thirty thousand years ago were repeating themselves. No one knew what the outcome would be this time.

The term “Demons” referred to many kinds of beings. As for what they actually looked like, Yang Kai had no idea. He only knew they came in all shapes and were extremely ferocious. Compared to them, the Bone-Eater Tribe was as gentle as lambs.

There were even rumors that the Bone-Eater Tribe was considered an oddity among the Barbarians because they had some demon blood. During the great war thirty thousand years ago, some demons defiled the women of this land, and their descendants became the ancestors of the Bone-Eater Tribe.

But a rumor is just a rumor—no one knows if it’s true. Especially in this era, the story had long faded into history. Only someone like Qing, a tree yao who had stood for tens of thousands of years, might know the truth.

Still, as the saying goes, there’s no smoke without fire. Countless Barbarians had proposed exterminating the Bone-Eater Tribe and removing them from the Barbarian ranks, likely because of this rumor. But by now, no one really knew why the Bone-Eater Tribe should be wiped out—only that they ate anything, even Barbarians themselves.

A demon invasion—this was an enormous calamity for this world!

No one knew where the demons came from. Even the most knowledgeable, like Qing, knew little. Thirty thousand years ago, the demons seemed to appear overnight, wantonly slaughtering and rampaging across the land.

That thousand-year-long, arduous war left the beautiful land gravely wounded, and humanity was nearly wiped out.

And now, thirty thousand years later, another demon invasion was unfolding. Yang Kai felt as if he saw the end of days, as if he saw mountains of corpses and rivers of blood.

Compared to that, the feud with the Bone-Eater Tribe seemed insignificant.

“Honored sir, honored sir—” The messenger called out several times. Seeing Yang Kai still lost in thought, he could only smile wryly in resignation.

Yang Kai took a breath and said, “Within five days, I will lead the villagers of Cangnan Village to the royal city!”

The messenger was overjoyed, saluted, and said, “Thank you, honored sir. I must go inform the other villages, so I’ll take my leave.”

Yang Kai nodded, not stopping him. After watching him leave, he turned to Die and asked, “How much do you know about the demons?”

Die replied, “I don’t know. Do you?”

Thinking about it, it made sense. She was only in her teens. Even if she was raised by Qing, there was no way she could know much. Qing wouldn’t have told her about the demons for no reason.

“Rest in the village for today. Tomorrow, go find Senior Qing,” Yang Kai said, then turned and walked toward the village.

With the demon invasion, the whole world would be thrown into turmoil. If there was any place left untouched, it would be Frost Snow City, guarded by Qing. Perhaps that would be the last safe haven.

“You’re kicking me out?” Die’s eyes widened in anger. “You’re actually kicking me out?”

“Go or don’t, as you like!” Yang Kai couldn’t be bothered with her and added, “Oh, right, call Ah Hu and the others over. I have something to say.”

Die stomped her foot in frustration, bit her lip, and left.

Before long, the villagers gathered in the village again. Seeing that Yang Kai was pale but unharmed, they were naturally overjoyed.

But their joy didn’t last long before Yang Kai’s face turned stern. “Everyone, I’ve just learned from the royal city’s messenger that the demons have invaded. Three of our Barbarian Tribes have already been wiped out. The Shaman King has ordered all villagers of Cangnan Village to assemble at the royal city within five days to await further orders.”

At these words, the more than two hundred villagers fell silent.

Ah Hu asked, “What are demons?”

Everyone wanted to know, since they had never heard the term before.

Yang Kai said, “I’m not too sure what demons are either. I only know they are enemies a hundred or a thousand times more ferocious than the Bone-Eater Tribe. They are the enemies who bring us calamity—the greatest foes of our Barbarian Tribe, and even of all humanity!”

A hundred or a thousand times more ferocious than the Bone-Eater Tribe.

Hearing this, the villagers’ faces changed. They had just been captured by the Bone-Eater Tribe and knew how vicious they were. Now, to hear there were enemies a hundred or a thousand times more ferocious—were they even human?

“Though the royal city’s order stands, I won’t force anyone! Tomorrow, I’ll set out for the royal city. If you wish to come with me, I’ll take you. If not, you can stay in the village.” Yang Kai’s voice was not loud, but it rang in everyone’s ears. “I won’t lie to you—this journey is a near-certain death. We may never return. Think it over carefully.”

After saying this, Yang Kai didn’t wait for the villagers to respond. He went straight to his house, took out a Healing Pill, and swallowed it.

The battle with Shaman Chi had been dangerous, but his injuries weren’t too serious. With a night’s rest, he would be fine. Besides, such intense battles benefited him greatly—more of them would be far more effective than shutting himself away to cultivate.

So he gladly accepted the royal city’s order. First, he wanted to see these so-called demons for himself. Second, he hoped to grow quickly through battle.
