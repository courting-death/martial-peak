## Chapter 2973 — Zhu Lie's Confusion

The dense blood-red light was sucked into the enormous mouth, forcing the Blood Demon to reveal his true form in a panic. This only made it easier for the little black dog to devour him. Its great maw suddenly snapped shut around the Blood Demon's waist, and no matter how he struggled, he could not break free.

The Blood Demon's face went pale with terror as he cried loudly for help, but which member of the Demon Race would dare save him? The little black dog's aura truly was not strong, but its unique presence sent a chill deep into their bones. Coupled with the Shadow Demon's earlier cry of "Guixu," it left them trembling with fear. At this moment, they only wanted to get as far away from this little creature as possible.

The Blood Demon was devoured little by little, unable to display any of his abilities. He seemed no different from an ordinary demon who had never cultivated before, unable even to circulate the Demonic Qi within his body.

Looking on at this scene, one could see it was remarkably similar to how a great serpent swallowed its prey—creatures like pythons could consume prey far larger than themselves, and the little black dog was no different.

Crimson flames blossomed as scorching power erupted. Zhu Qing and her younger brother had made their move.

Both of them were Red Dragons who cultivated Fire Elemental Origin Power. The moment they acted, the temperature in the surrounding space soared. The world seemed to transform into a furnace that would melt all the demons present.

Dragon Breaths spewed forth, spinning fireballs flew through the air, and fire blades slashed down from above, crisscrossing unpredictably. The members of the Demon Race were instantly thrown into chaos. One after another, they fell like wheat beneath a scythe.

Although these demons were numerous and most of their abilities were strange and difficult to guard against, even if Yang Kai and Zhu Qing had not come, Zhu Lie would not have suffered any loss with his strength.

An Eighth-Tier Red Dragon, an existence beyond the Third-Stage Emperor Realm, was more than capable of slaughtering this entire group of demons.

In just one clash, more than half of the hundred demons were dead or wounded. Not one of those swept up by the scorching flames survived; all of them perished on the spot.

The Succubus shrieked, the Shadow Demon's expression changed, and the Sand Demon attempted to flee underground. However, the surrounding space had been sealed, making his earth-escaping ability completely unusable. The instant he plunged into the ground, he was bounced back out.

Zhu Qing lunged forward in a single stride, her delicate fist crashing into the scantily clad Succubus. The violent force erupted, instantly blasting her into a cloud of blood mist, leaving neither corpse nor bones behind.

Zhu Qing had long found this Succubus displeasing. She kept casting flirtatious glances, though who knew whom she was trying to entice? Worse still, a certain someone had been watching with great relish.

The Sand Demon had failed to escape underground and had only just regained his footing when Zhu Lie sent a flaming blade slashing down from the air, cleaving him in two.

The remaining Shadow Demon could not hold out alone. He turned his head and looked around, only to find that his army of a hundred had somehow been nearly wiped out, leaving him as the sole survivor. His whole body went cold, and his hands and feet turned icy.

Two members of the Dragon Clan possessed such might without even revealing their true forms. Had they assumed their true forms, they could have swept aside ten times as many enemies.

Zhu Lie charged toward the Shadow Demon. The Shadow Demon tried to repeat his old trick and use his life-preserving divine ability, but the impact of Zhu Lie's Dragon Pressure left him dizzy and disoriented. How could he possibly display it now? Zhu Lie punched him, smashing his head apart and sending red and white matter splattering in every direction. It was a truly ghastly sight.

At that moment, the little black dog finally swallowed the Blood Demon into its belly. A gulp came from its throat, yet it still seemed unsatisfied. When it turned to look around, however, not a single living demon remained. All hundred or so demons had been completely slaughtered.

It snorted through its nose and paid no attention to the corpses, merely raising its head to glance at Yang Kai.

Yang Kai had not acted from beginning to end. Aside from creating a spatial cage, he had not killed a single demon, because he knew there was simply no need for him to intervene.

He landed beside Li Jiao and looked down at him.

Li Jiao happened to open his eyes just then. Upon seeing Yang Kai, he immediately revealed a smile uglier than crying. "Palace Master Yang... save me!"

Yang Kai raised his brow. "What does Brother Li mean by that? You look as lively as a dragon and fierce as a tiger, brimming with energy. Why would you need me to save you?"

Li Jiao said anxiously, “Palace Master Yang, you can’t deny the obvious. This Li… Li has been invaded by Demonic Qi! Surely you can see that, can’t you?”

He and Zhu Lie had ended up in the Revolving World, but they had not been as fortunate as Yang Kai. No one had guided them to Human Sovereign City, so they had remained outside all this time. Whenever the world's laws changed and Demonic Qi filled the world, even Li Jiao, a Third-Stage Emperor Realm Master, found it somewhat difficult to withstand. In the end, Demonic Qi invaded his body.

That trace of Demonic Qi was extremely troublesome, clinging to him like a maggot gnawing at a bone. No matter what he tried, he could not expel it. Otherwise, with his cultivation, he could never have been controlled by a Succubus just now. His injuries from Zhu Qing were a minor matter; he had only broken a few bones and coughed up a few mouthfuls of blood, so he would recover after recuperating for some time. Being corroded by Demonic Qi, however, could cost him his life. If things went poorly, it might even cause him to suffer Qi Deviation.

Seeing Yang Kai now was like finding a life-saving straw to clutch at.

"I can see it, but there is nothing I can do about your condition." Yang Kai stroked his chin as he spoke.

Li Jiao almost burst into tears when he heard this. "Palace Master Yang, you cannot just stand by and watch me die! This Li only came here because of you. If Palace Master Yang had not repeatedly invited me, how could this Li have ended up stranded in this place? Would I not have been perfectly comfortable throwing my weight around and enjoying life in my Li Dragon Palace? Now that I have fallen into such a state, Palace Master Yang... bears some responsibility for it." As he spoke, he secretly observed Yang Kai's expression, afraid of angering him.

Yang Kai's face darkened immediately. "You may eat carelessly, but you can’t speak carelessly. You came here with me because we agreed on terms beforehand—it is not as though you gained nothing from it. Moreover, it was not I who caused the Demonic Qi to corrode you. It is your own weakness and lack of resolve that brought you to this state. What responsibility should I bear?"

Li Jiao said with a mournful expression, "Palace Master Yang, can you really bear to watch this Li be corroded by Demonic Qi, lose all reason, and degenerate into a demon?"

"Why would I be unable to bear it?" Yang Kai asked in surprise. "Rest assured, if you truly degenerate into a demon, this Young Master will personally free you from your suffering and grant you a swift death."

Li Jiao cursed furiously in his heart, his intestines turning green with regret. Had he known this would happen, nothing could have persuaded him to follow Yang Kai to that damned place, the Frozen Earth. Now he had ended up in such a state and did not even know what path lay ahead of him, leaving him utterly lost.

"All right, all right." Yang Kai suddenly chuckled, patted him on the shoulder, and pulled him to his feet.

His expression changed so quickly that Li Jiao found it difficult to adjust. He stared at Yang Kai blankly and asked, "Palace Master Yang, you... What do you mean?"

Yang Kai said, "You have merely been corroded by Demonic Qi. Anyone else would probably have degenerated into a demon long ago. Fortunately, Brother Li's cultivation is profound. Although you have been corroded, you are not beyond saving."

Li Jiao's eyes lit up, and he hurriedly said, "This Li knew Palace Master Yang would not stand by and watch me die! Palace Master Yang, please teach me how to save myself!"

"The method to save you may seem far away, but it is right before your eyes!" Yang Kai chuckled mischievously.

Li Jiao did not understand.

Yang Kai pursed his lips toward Zhu Lie nearby and whispered, "Go find Zhu Lie and ask him to spare you some Dragon Blood. Dragon Blood possesses divinity, so it will be enough to expel the Demonic Qi from your body."

Li Jiao jumped in fright, his face turning pale as he hurriedly waved his hands. "Palace Master Yang, please do not joke about such a thing! How could Dragon Blood be so easy to obtain? If Zhu Lie learned I harbored such thoughts, he would likely slap me to death. This Li is merely a lowly Jiao, so how could I dare set my sights on him?"

"That is precisely why you should set your sights on him. Given your origins, if you could drink even a mouthful of Dragon Blood, tsk, tsk..."

"Gulp!" Li Jiao could not help swallowing.

Strictly speaking, he could be considered a descendant of the Dragon Clan. Although he was heavily suppressed by the Dragon Clan's bloodline, drinking even a mouthful of Dragon Blood would greatly benefit his strength and definitely raise his bloodline by an entire level. Yang Kai's words sounded inconceivable at first, but they echoed in his ears as though imbued with magic, leaving him eager to try.

Excitement gleamed in his eyes, and the Demonic Qi around his body seemed to grow somewhat denser.

Yang Kai saw this but did not expose it. He merely chuckled and turned to look to the side.

Over there, no one knew what Zhu Qing and Zhu Lie were doing. One looked as though she had committed some terrible wrong, her gaze wandering as she did not dare look the other in the eye. The other stared at his Elder Sister in utter shock, constantly examining her as though he had to get to know her all over again.

Zhu Qing's face reddened slightly, and her gaze became even more evasive.

"You..." Only after a long while did Zhu Lie finally speak. "How did you advance to a Ninth-Tier bloodline?"

Zhu Lie had sensed that something was amiss from the moment they met. Zhu Qing's bloodline seemed much purer than his. This had not been the case when she last left Dragon Isle. Back then, both siblings had possessed Eighth-Tier bloodlines. Advancing the Dragon Clan's bloodline was extremely difficult. It depended partly on what one inherited from one's parents, partly on one's own efforts, and partly on the support of Dragon Isle. Not one of these three could be lacking.

The siblings were among the few experts on Dragon Isle, and reaching the Eighth Tier was already extremely impressive. Even the Great Elder was only at the Tenth Tier.

The Great Elder had once declared that the siblings would most likely remain at the Eighth Tier for the rest of their lives, with only an extremely slim chance of breaking through to the Ninth Tier. The Great Elder had lived for countless years and possessed exceptionally keen judgment, so his predictions generally did not deviate much from reality.

And that was indeed the case. Ever since the siblings had advanced to the Eighth-Tier, improving their bloodline had become increasingly difficult with each passing year. No matter how many Dragon Blood Flowers they consumed or how long they secluded themselves within the Dragon Hall, the results were never particularly significant.

Yet now, he had unexpectedly seen his Elder Sister become a Ninth-Tier Great Dragon!

It was simply inconceivable. Zhu Lie was certain he had not seen wrongly. Members of the Dragon Clan were exceptionally sensitive to one another's bloodlines. Even though Zhu Qing had not revealed her true form, there was no mistaking the aura of the Ninth Tier.

Zhu Lie's heart felt like a cat was scratching at it—he was both jealous and envious of his sister's breakthrough, desperately wanting to know the secret behind her ascension to the Ninth Tier.

Zhu Qing did not answer, but her expression became even more awkward. Zhu Lie asked her again.

Only then did Zhu Qing whisper, "It was a fortuitous opportunity..."

"Impossible!" Zhu Lie immediately rejected her explanation. He felt that Zhu Qing was being too stingy and wanted to keep this secret to herself, making him even more curious.

"Qing'er, you have reached the Ninth Tier?" Yang Kai walked over and asked in surprise.
