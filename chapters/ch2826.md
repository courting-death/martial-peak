## Chapter 2826 — Sudden Change

When Yang Kai awoke, he was no longer in that tree hollow, but on a stretch of wilderness.

The lingering pain in his head made Yang Kai shudder as he recalled what had happened.

Although he had only just reached the Shaman Master Realm and awakened his Divine Sense, his foundation was far beyond that of an ordinary Shaman Master. With a single touch, Qing sent his Divine Soul into turmoil, clearly transmitting an immense amount of information that Yang Kai was utterly unable to bear, causing him to faint instantly.

There was no time to carefully examine what Qing had passed on. As Yang Kai turned his head, he met a pair of clear, bright eyes.

After a long moment, Yang Kai finally asked, "Why are you here?"

The petite girl shrugged and said, “Grandpa Qing asked me to come out and see the world with you. He said I’ve lived in Frost Snow City all my life and know too little about the outside.”

"So this is the entrusted thing..." Yang Kai muttered to himself, rubbing his chin.

Previously, Qing had mentioned needing a small favor and something to entrust to him. Now it seemed Qing’s entrusted thing was Die. Sixteen years ago, Die was abandoned beneath the Divine Tree, and Qing raised her. But Die and Qing were different; she couldn’t stay in Frost Snow City forever. Yet Die’s identity was too sensitive, and traveling alone might not be safe—she could easily become a target.

With Yang Kai looking after her, it was a different story.

Before he lost consciousness, Qing’s words seemed to mention Die as well...

Understanding this, Yang Kai felt at ease.

After all, Die was a Great Shaman Master, likely not much weaker than himself. Even if he wanted to look after her, he probably couldn’t do much. Qing letting her go with him was likely just to broaden her horizons.

"Where are we?" Yang Kai stood up, looked around, and asked in confusion.

Die replied, "Grandpa Qing sent us out with a clone. We’re five hundred li from Frost Snow City, to the east." As she spoke, Die pointed to the side.

Yang Kai turned to look and saw a tree, as tall as a person, standing by the roadside, radiating the same aura as Qing himself.

Yang Kai raised his brows, examining the tree with interest.

Die said from the side, "Don’t worry, the people of the Frost Snow Tribe don’t know you’ve left yet. They probably still think you’re in the tree hollow."

"Senior Qing’s methods are impressive," Yang Kai praised.

Die smiled, "That’s Grandpa Qing!" Her words showed obvious admiration for him. She turned to Yang Kai and asked, "Where are we going?"

"Let me see..." Yang Kai said, taking out the map the village chief had given him. After studying it for a while, he found his location and determined the direction to travel.

When he left the village in winter, he’d told the chief he’d return in spring.

Now that spring was near and his cultivation goals were met, it was time to go back. Cangnan Village was remote and rarely visited—a perfect place for secluded cultivation. Once he regained his peak strength, he could explore the world again.

"Can you fly?" Yang Kai put away the map and glanced at Die.

Die snorted. As she chanted, a pair of dazzling, multicolored wings appeared behind her. The girl seemed to transform into a beautiful butterfly, dancing gracefully and magnificently.

With a flap of her wings, Die soared into the air, looking down at Yang Kai and beckoning, "Come on, what are you staring at?"

Yang Kai quickly composed himself, hiding the amazement in his eyes. With a flicker of his figure, he took the lead, guiding the way forward.

It was Die’s first time leaving Frost Snow City, and she was very curious about the outside world. Her beautiful eyes constantly scanned her surroundings, her pretty face full of joy. Everything seemed new to her.

But she didn’t disturb Yang Kai, simply enjoying the scenery along the way and occasionally exclaiming in wonder.

Yang Kai, meanwhile, traveled while digesting the information Qing had imparted to him.

He’d thought it was just a cultivation technique, but the more he examined it, the more shocked he became. The information was far too vast to be summed up by a single technique—it contained the essence of Qing’s entire life.

Qing was a tree yao, guarding Frost Snow City for countless years without ever leaving. His lifespan stretched thousands, even tens of thousands of years. Through the passage of time, the world changed, but Qing remained steadfast.

He had seen all kinds of people and experienced the changes of eras and the tides of history.

The knowledge he possessed was immense and profound; his very being was a microcosm of the ancient era.

What he imparted to Yang Kai was all his knowledge and experience. Cultivation techniques and Shamanic Arts were secondary—the most important thing was the essence distilled from endless years.

What’s going on?

Yang Kai didn’t feel that helping with a small favor was enough for Qing to give him such a precious gift.

Such a gift would tempt even a Great Emperor. It would greatly benefit his future cultivation. If he could digest this knowledge, Yang Kai’s path would be smooth from now on.

He was certain this was the greatest benefit he’d gained from this secret realm trial—nothing else could compare.

He was also grateful for the chief’s tireless teaching, which made him so familiar with ancient characters. Otherwise, he wouldn’t be able to absorb this knowledge now. He secretly rejoiced; truly, having an elder at home is a treasure. If not for the chief’s insistence, Yang Kai wouldn’t have learned ancient script.

As he traveled and pondered, Yang Kai didn’t notice the passage of time. He wished he could find a place to retreat and fully digest Qing’s knowledge.

But even the longest journey has an end, and he was only returning to Cangnan Village. Previously, he couldn’t fly and the trip took longer, but now, flying all the way, it took less than five days to arrive.

Yang Kai gathered his thoughts and couldn’t help but feel a sense of returning home, amused by himself.

Die seemed a bit nervous: "Will your village people dislike me? Will they drive me away? Should I use a transformation spell?"

After leaving the Frost Snow Tribe and losing Qing’s protection, Die couldn’t always appear as a burly man. But as a Great Shaman Master, mastering a transformation spell was easy. With her cultivation, once she cast a Shamanic Art, no one in the village could see through it.

She chattered on like a nervous bride meeting her in-laws. Yang Kai couldn’t help but laugh, but just as he was about to answer, he suddenly frowned and looked ahead.

The next moment, Yang Kai’s expression changed, and he sped up, leaving Die behind.

"Hey..." Die called out in confusion, but soon sensed something was wrong. Not far ahead, a place that looked like a village was billowing with smoke.

It wasn't cooking time, so why was there so much thick smoke?

Frowning, Die felt a sense of foreboding and silently sped up to catch up.

Soon, as Yang Kai and Die landed in the village, Die looked around and sighed.

The village had been attacked. Ruins were everywhere, and the ground was stained with blood, which had been there for several days judging by its color.

Such weak villages were easily attacked. Every year, countless villages in the Barbarian Tribe were wiped out for various reasons.

Die sighed, "It wasn’t beasts—it was humans."

She hadn’t investigated closely, but it was obvious. There were no beast tracks or bite marks; clearly, it wasn’t the work of beasts.

"And the battle ended quickly. Either there were many attackers, or they were very strong... The latter is more likely."

Though Die was inexperienced, as a member of the Drifting Tribe, tracking and counter-tracking were almost instinctive. She could glean much from the remnants in the village.

"Does your village have any enemies?" Die asked.

Yang Kai shook his head. His Divine Sense surged like a tide, spreading madly in all directions.

Sensing the strength of his Divine Sense, Die was full of shock.

Such Divine Sense seemed even stronger than her own.

After a moment, Yang Kai’s gaze sharpened and he rushed in a certain direction.

Die didn’t know what he was doing, but followed.

Soon, the two arrived at a pile of firewood. Yang Kai reached out, moved the logs aside, and precisely found a hole covered by a plank. Lifting the plank, several terrified, pale faces appeared below.

They were all children, about seven or eight in total. The oldest was no more than ten, the youngest only four or five.

When the oldest child saw Yang Kai’s face, he cried out in delight, "Brother Ah Niu!"

"Come out first!" Yang Kai said, and the oldest child nodded, lifting each companion and handing them to Yang Kai.

Soon, the children were rescued. But after so long in fear and without food or water, they were all weak.

"What happened? Who attacked the village?" Yang Kai asked the child.

"The chief said those people were from the Bone-Eater Tribe," the child replied, biting his lip. As he spoke, he seemed to recall the events of a few days ago and trembled.

"What? Bone-Eater Tribe?" Yang Kai’s face changed dramatically.

Die’s expression also turned grim.

The Barbarian Tribe’s various tribes had different temperaments due to their locations and cultivation methods. For example, the Raging Flame Tribe was extremely combative, while the Wood Spirit Tribe was gentle.

But if one were to ask which tribe was the most bloodthirsty and brutal, the Bone-Eater Tribe would undoubtedly be the first to come to mind.

The reason was simple: the people of this tribe even regarded their fellow barbarians as food, using them to fill their bellies.

In other words, they were a cannibal tribe. When food was scarce, they would even slaughter and feast upon their own kin—let alone outsiders.
