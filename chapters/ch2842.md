## Chapter 2842 — The Scenery Above Is Uniquely Beautiful

“Three thousand people? Shaman Niu, as a Lower-Level Great Shaman Master, how could you possibly control so many? Be careful not to bite off more than you can chew, or you’ll burst your belly.”

As the voice rang out, a burly figure suddenly crashed down from the sky, landing beside the mountain of weapons. The tremendous impact made the earth tremble and sent dust flying, while the entire mountain of weapons clanged noisily.

Yang Kai glanced at the thick black hair on the man’s chest and raised an eyebrow. “Shaman Tu? What do you want?”

Shaman Tu laughed heartily and said, “I just saw that you’re young and weak, so I’m a bit worried for you.”

“Then I must thank you for your concern, but there’s no need to worry.”

Shaman Tu shook his head. “You can’t say that. We’re all Great Shaman Masters of the Southern Barbarian Tribe, so we should help each other. Hmm, how about this: since I’m a bit stronger than you, I’ll take on a little more work. You take another thousand people, and I’ll help you digest the remaining thousand. As thanks... the extra weapons can go to me.”

As he spoke, he reached straight for the mountain of weapons, not giving Yang Kai any chance to react.

“Stop!” Ah Hu shouted angrily when he saw this. These weapons had been bought from the royal city at great cost by Ah Niu. Although he didn’t know where Ah Niu got so many Inner Cores to trade, now that someone dared to reach out for them, he couldn’t just sit by and watch.

What Great Shaman Master? Anyone who dares to snatch his things is an enemy.

As he roared, Ah Hu had already swung his axe at Shaman Tu.

“Get lost!” Shaman Tu didn’t even turn his head. He just shouted, and a visible shockwave spread from under his feet. The force was so great that as soon as it touched Ah Hu, he was sent flying like a paper kite and crashed to the ground.

At the same time, Shaman Tu grabbed a refined steel spear. With a slight exertion, the entire mountain of weapons trembled, and nearly half the weapons floated into the air, clearly flying toward the warriors under Shaman Tu’s command.

Shaman Tu’s followers were overjoyed at the sight, their faces full of excitement as they prepared to receive the many weapons.

But just then, a figure suddenly appeared atop the mountain of weapons. Without any visible movement, the thousand or so weapons that had been flying up seemed to be pressed down by a great mountain and all fell back, clattering against each other.

Hovering in midair, Yang Kai looked down at Shaman Tu, his face full of mockery. “Trying to steal?”

Shaman Tu’s face flushed as he retorted, “How is this stealing? I said, we’re all from the Southern Barbarian Tribe. I’m just trying to help you with your burdens. If you don’t thank me, fine, but to slander my good name—Shaman Niu, you’ve got some nerve.”

Yang Kai sneered, “I never agreed to let you help with my burdens.”

Shaman Tu shook his big head. “Whether you agree or not doesn’t matter. I’ve always been this helpful. Now move aside, don’t fail to appreciate my kindness!”

Yang Kai exclaimed, “Shaman Tu, have you no shame? Spouting such blatant lies with a straight face—aren’t you even a little embarrassed?”

Shaman Tu’s face did turn red, but he still spoke forcefully: “Fine, fine, since you’re so ungrateful, as an Upper-Level Great Shaman Master, I have the responsibility and duty to teach you a lesson.”

As he spoke, he began chanting, and with a wave of his hand, shamanic power surged. Two visible chains of shamanic power shot toward Yang Kai, binding him tightly before he could react.

He couldn’t help but laugh, lecturing, “Remember, failing to recognize your own limitations now is merely embarrassing, but on the battlefield, it’ll cost you your life. So don’t thank me—this is what I should do.” As he spoke, he yanked hard, clearly intending to drag Yang Kai down from midair.

But in the next instant, his expression changed, because despite his efforts, Shaman Niu, standing atop the mountain of weapons, didn’t move at all. He just looked at him with a smile, as if nailed in place by some force.

He was slightly shocked, instinctively feeling it was impossible. The other was just a Lower-Level Great Shaman Master, two ranks below him, and already bound—how could he possibly resist his strength?

Unwilling to accept it, he tried again, only to be horrified to find it still had no effect.

It was as if he had bound not a Lower-Level Great Shaman Master, but a real mountain. After two failed attempts, his face turned red and his neck thickened.

The surrounding tribesmen all looked confused, not knowing what Shaman Tu was doing. Several other Great Shaman Masters even shot him urgent looks, signaling him to end this farce quickly.

Earlier, they had gathered to discuss that the best way to break the current stalemate was to make Shaman Niu lose face. The fact that he had brought several thousand weapons had already attracted the attention and interest of many strong Barbarian Tribe warriors.

But that interest was based solely on the weapons. If the tribesmen saw that Shaman Niu was actually powerless, those who were ready to follow him would surely reconsider.

Weapons were tempting, but a weak Great Shaman Master wasn’t worth entrusting their lives to.

After all, the Barbarian Tribe was about to face a life-and-death battle. A strong Great Shaman Master leader was a better guarantee of safety than any weapon.

That’s why Shaman Tu jumped out to cause trouble, deliberately picking a fight with Yang Kai. According to their plan, as long as he could crush Shaman Niu in front of everyone and make him lose face, his advantage would vanish.

But the current situation was not quite what they had discussed.

Shaman Niu was already bound, so what was Shaman Tu doing?

In a flash, another Upper-Level Great Shaman Master descended, also casting a chain-like shamanic art to bind Yang Kai, shouting, “Shaman Niu, Shaman Tu is too kind and doesn’t want you to lose face. I’m not so nice—get down here!”

With a tug, he nearly lost his balance and almost fell to the ground himself.

The Upper-Level Great Shaman Master’s eyes nearly popped out. Only then did he realize it wasn’t that Shaman Tu had held back, but that he was truly powerless.

What was going on?

While he was still stunned, Yang Kai grabbed both of their shamanic chains, grinned, and said, “The scenery above is uniquely beautiful. I don’t want to come down—why don’t you two come up instead!”

With a sudden yank, and to the shock of all watching, Shaman Tu and the other Great Shaman Master were pulled off the ground and sent flying toward Yang Kai.

In midair, the two flailed about before finally managing to steady themselves.

But then their hearts sank.

Because they saw Shaman Niu grinning wickedly, cracking his knuckles, clearly up to no good.

Forcing themselves to focus, both simultaneously channeled shamanic power to form shields on their bodies.

The light of the shamanic shields had just appeared when Yang Kai threw his fists.

Left hook, right hook…

His fists moved like the wind.

Crack…

With a crisp sound, the shamanic shields on the two Upper-Level Great Shaman Masters shattered. Against Yang Kai’s seemingly effortless punches, they had no resistance at all.

Both Great Shaman Masters grunted, flying out like ragged sacks and crashing heavily to the ground, their faces covered in blood.

The world instantly fell silent. Everyone stared at Yang Kai in shock, as if they’d seen a ghost—especially the other Great Shaman Masters, whose faces twitched in disbelief at what they’d just witnessed.

The ordinary Barbarian Tribe warriors had never cultivated shamanic power and couldn’t possibly grasp the vast gulf between a Lower-Level Great Shaman Master and an Upper-Level one. But the Shamans present understood all too well.

Normally, an Upper-Level Great Shaman Master could crush a Lower-Level one with a wave of the hand. The gap between the ranks was no joke.

But what they’d just seen was so real, they couldn’t find any explanation.

The shamanic shields cast by two Great Shaman Masters had been broken by Shaman Niu as easily as if they were nothing. If the shields were really that weak, why bother learning them at all?

In truth, it wasn’t that the shields were weak, but that Shaman Niu was terrifying.

All the Great Shaman Masters felt their scalps tingle. Earlier, seeing him bring thousands of weapons and gain such an advantage in recruiting, some had felt uneasy, and some, like Shaman Tu, had even planned to cause trouble and snatch some weapons. But now, who would dare think of that?

Two Great Shaman Masters working together had suffered such a crushing defeat—who could possibly be his match?

On the other side, the two who’d been beaten were still dazed.

Shaman Tu struggled to his feet, shook his head, and finally came to his senses. Meeting the gazes of the surrounding tribesmen, his face turned blue, then red, and he shouted in embarrassment and anger, “You dare launch a sneak attack?”

He refused to admit he wasn’t Yang Kai’s match, insisting his loss was just a moment’s carelessness. He’d never expected Yang Kai to actually strike back. If he’d been fully prepared, there was no way he’d have lost so badly.

The more he thought about it, the angrier he became. This time, he’d stepped up to suppress Yang Kai’s advantage and boost his own prestige, but instead, he’d ended up making a fool of himself. If he admitted defeat now, his reputation would plummet, and he’d never be able to gather followers again.

“Surround them!” Shaman Tu roared.

As his voice fell, nearly two thousand Barbarian Tribe warriors rushed out, spreading out to encircle Yang Kai and his thousand men.

The other Great Shaman Master, seeing this, silently waved his hand, and his fifteen hundred men also gathered around.

In an instant, three to four thousand people moved at once. Ah Hu and the others tensed up, gripping their weapons tightly.

The other unrelated Barbarian Tribe members immediately realized this matter wouldn’t end easily and quickly retreated to the side, clearing the field for the two opposing sides.
