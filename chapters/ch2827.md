## Chapter 2827 — Fortuitous Encounter

Such a terrifying and brutal tribe made all the major Barbarian Tribes keep their distance, and some even believed the Bone-Eater Tribe was not truly part of the Barbarian Tribe, but a group of savage beasts that should be wiped out.

Unfortunately, the Bone-Eater Tribe was guarded by a powerful Shaman Saint. Unless the entire Barbarian Tribe united, no one was willing to provoke the Bone-Eater Tribe. Moreover, the Bone-Eater Tribe was vengeful and not easily trifled with.

Cangnan Village was attacked by such an enemy, so the consequences were easy to imagine.

"So many people died, Ah Niu, please save the village chief and the others!" The largest barbarian child cried, looking at Yang Kai with hope, as if Yang Kai could do anything.

"How long ago did this happen?" Yang Kai asked in a deep voice.

"Three days."

Three days... Yang Kai didn’t hesitate, unsure if he was already too late. He turned and instructed Die, “Take care of them.”

Die grabbed his arm and whispered, "What are you doing? Don’t go looking for death!"

Yang Kai grinned fiercely, "Who lives and who dies isn’t certain yet."

Die shook her head, "It’s been three days already. Even if you chase them now, you might not catch up. If you’re captured by the Bone-Eater Tribe, there’s only one outcome—becoming food. Maybe the people from your village are already..."

"Then they’ll pay the price." Yang Kai shook off her hand, stepped forward, and soared into the air.

Die watched his back, sighed softly, and slowly shook her head.

The barbarian children all gaped in astonishment, staring at the sky. The largest one said with admiration, "Ah Niu can fly now..."

…

Cangnan Village had nearly three hundred villagers, men, women, and children alike—all were fighters. To capture so many, the Bone-Eater Tribe must have sent a large force, so their retreat left obvious traces.

Yang Kai followed the trail without difficulty.

The snow on the ground had melted, and everything was coming back to life. Perhaps the Bone-Eater Tribe’s winter stores were running low, so they came out to search for food and happened upon the remote Cangnan Village, making it their target.

As Yang Kai knew, the Bone-Eater Tribe was utterly unrestrained—even eating their own people—but they were still human, so they preferred fresh meat. In other words, they wouldn’t kill all the captives at once, only slaughtering them as needed.

This gave Yang Kai a chance to save people.

As long as he intercepted them or found their lair before things became irreversible, he could rescue everyone.

As night fell, Yang Kai arrived before a stone fortress.

The stone fortress was built against the mountain, its back pressed to a sheer cliff face as smooth as a mirror. It sprawled across a wide area, and within its walls, burly, fierce-looking members of the Barbarian Tribe paced back and forth, weapons of all shapes and sizes in hand. Their faces were painted blue, their fangs bared, making them look no different from vengeful ghosts haunting the night.

Around the stone walls, something seemed to be hanging. Yang Kai focused his gaze and was instantly enraged.

The hanging things were all corpses—at least a hundred by rough count. Cangnan Village only had three hundred people, and here a hundred bodies were already displayed. Yang Kai’s heart chilled, and the cold radiating from him was almost tangible.

He was still a step too late!

Taking a deep breath to calm his anger, Yang Kai stood up from his hiding place, preparing to sneak into the fortress under cover of night. Now that he had advanced to Shaman Master, even an ordinary Great Shaman Master was no match for him, and there shouldn’t be a Shaman King here, but caution was always wise.

Just then, Yang Kai sensed something, quickly crouched down again, held his breath, and looked in another direction.

Over there, there was some movement—though faint, Yang Kai heard it clearly.

He quietly released his Divine Sense, swept it over, and showed a surprised expression.

After a moment’s thought, Yang Kai slipped over like an eel.

Soon, a startled cry rang out in the darkness: "Who!"

Before the startled cry had even faded, a large hand clamped over the warrior’s mouth. Yang Kai pressed a finger to his lips and whispered, “Shh—don’t be afraid, I’m not your enemy.”

The Barbarian Tribe warrior was terrified. Though Yang Kai used only one hand, no matter how he struggled, he couldn’t break free. After several attempts, he felt cold all over, his limbs numb, and despair set in.

Moonlight spilled down, and the warrior suddenly calmed, staring wide-eyed at Yang Kai in astonishment, mumbling, "Lord Niu?"

"Hm?" Yang Kai was taken aback. "You know me?"

As he spoke, he released his grip. Seeing the tattoos on the man’s face, he realized, "From the Raging Flame Tribe?"

The Barbarian Tribe warrior said excitedly, "Yes, Lord Niu! We met in the canyon last time."

When he mentioned the canyon, Yang Kai immediately understood and brightened, "Is Ya here?"

"Just ahead, I’ll take you."

"Alright." Yang Kai nodded.

He had sensed a gathering here and knew they were friends, not foes, but hadn’t expected to meet old acquaintances. Luckily, this Raging Flame Tribe warrior recognized him, or explaining things would have been troublesome.

Following behind the Raging Flame Tribe warrior, Yang Kai quietly made his way forward. Suspicious glances flickered from all around, as if the others couldn’t fathom how an extra person had suddenly appeared among them.

Soon, the Barbarian Tribe warrior reached the front and called softly, "Lord Ya, Lord Niu is here."

Ya’s voice came from ahead, "Lord Niu? Which Lord Niu?"

As he finished speaking, a familiar face appeared. Yang Kai managed a smile, "Lord Ya, we meet again."

Ya was astonished, eyes wide. "Shaman Niu, how are you here?" Their duel in the canyon had left a deep impression, and he hadn’t forgotten.

Yang Kai said gravely, "Probably for the same reason as you!"

Ya paused, then understood, "Looks like your village was attacked too."

"You were attacked as well?"

Ya’s face was grim. "I took some tribesmen out to find food. When we returned, the villagers had been abducted. We followed the trail here."

“Then it seems our goals are the same!” Yang Kai grinned, revealing a set of gleaming white fangs that looked especially sinister under the moonlight. Ya’s heart skipped a beat at the sight, and he quickly changed the subject, asking, “Did you come alone?”

"I’m the only one left in my village with any fighting strength."

Ya showed admiration. If his own village had only him left, he might not have had the courage to track the Bone-Eater Tribe here. Coming might mean never leaving.

"Come with me, I’ll take you to see the others!" Ya beckoned, then led the way, crouching low.

Yang Kai was puzzled, unsure who Ya wanted him to meet, but could only follow.

Before long, a deep voice came from ahead, "Ya, what are you doing here?"

Ya replied respectfully, "Lord Chi, a friend from the Southern Barbarian Tribe has come. His villagers were also taken by You’s tribe."

"Oh?" The one called Chi sounded surprised. "Come over."

Ya acknowledged, then signaled Yang Kai to follow.

In the thick grass, a man and a woman sat cross-legged, their auras strong but contained, blending with the surroundings.

Two Great Shaman Masters! Yang Kai raised his brows in surprise.

The man was likely Chi, as he bore the Raging Flame Tribe’s tattoos and his aura was fiery, clearly trained in fire Shamanic Arts. The woman, though broad and burly, had an ethereal grace—a striking contrast that seemed almost comical.

As Yang Kai approached, the sharp gazes of the two immediately swept over him, scrutinizing him with a piercing intensity that made him feel distinctly uncomfortable.

"Shaman Niu from Cangnan Village greets the lords!" Yang Kai placed a hand on his chest.

Chi nodded slightly, approving, "A Shaman Master—not bad."

"A Shaman Master?" Ya was surprised. He thought Shaman Niu was an Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice. Did Lord Chi make a mistake?

He didn’t know that in just one winter, Shaman Niu had advanced from Upper-Level Shaman Apprentice to Shaman Master. If he knew, he’d probably cry out at the injustice of fate.

"How can there be such a small tribesman? Are you from the Drifting Tribe?" The woman frowned, openly showing her dislike for Yang Kai.

Before Yang Kai could reply, Ya said, "Shaman Niu isn’t from the Drifting Tribe. He’s from the Southern Barbarian Tribe. He’s generous and magnanimous—unlike those thieves from the Drifting Tribe."

He still remembered Yang Kai’s kindness in giving him a hundred wild beasts last time.

"That’s best." The woman nodded, apparently believing Ya.

"How should I address you, lord?" Yang Kai asked the woman.

Chi said, "She’s Lord Zhu from the Gale Tribe."

Ya whispered, "The Bone-Eater Tribe raided three villages this time—yours, mine, and one from the Gale Tribe. Lord Chi is here because I called him, and we happened to meet Lord Zhu, so we’re all together."

"Strength in numbers." Yang Kai nodded and asked, "May I ask, what do the two lords plan to do?"

Chi said, "Wait for dawn."

Yang Kai mused, "Dawn is when people are most relaxed—it’s the best time to act. But both lords are Great Shaman Masters. Can’t you take the fortress head-on?"

Zhu glanced at him, "How much do you know about this fortress?"

Yang Kai admitted, "Nothing at all."

He hadn’t even known there was a Bone-Eater Tribe settlement here.

Zhu said, "This fortress is a small Bone-Eater Tribe settlement, with about seven to eight hundred people. There are more than twenty shamans, and the strongest is called You, who has the realm of a Middle-Level Great Shaman Master."

Yang Kai noticed that when Zhu mentioned You, Chi’s expression became wary—clearly he didn’t feel confident against the Bone-Eater Tribe’s Great Shaman Master.
