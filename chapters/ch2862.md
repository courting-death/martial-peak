## Chapter 2862 — Demon Domain

"What have you done to me?" Shaya gasped softly, her face shrouded in despair as she glared at the figure before her—the one she now feared most—and gritted out the question.

Ever since she started running, several hours had passed, yet Shaya sorrowfully discovered that no matter how she tried to escape, she could not get beyond this area of several dozen li. It was as if she were endlessly circling a single spot, always inexplicably ending up right in front of this Shaman Niu, delivering herself into his hands.

She belonged to the Succubus race, and the strength of Divine Sense was an innate talent of theirs. At first, she thought she had fallen under some Shamanic Art or was hallucinating, but after checking herself several times, she found no trace of anything wrong.

It was not an illusion, nor did she feel anything unusual, yet no matter how she ran, she always returned to the starting point.

This inexplicable predicament filled her with fear, just as she had feared the power Shaman Niu had displayed earlier.

Her eyes were dim, as if covered by dark clouds, unable to see even a glimmer of hope.

Overwhelmed by terror and despair, she gave up on fleeing, for after hours of effort, she understood she could not escape!

"I told you, in this world, I am the master. If I want you to live, you live. If I want you to die, then no matter what, you will not survive." As he finished speaking, Yang Kai took a step forward.

That step seemed to land directly on Shaya's heart, making her whole body tremble as she coughed up fresh blood.

Yang Kai walked toward her from the sky, his expression indifferent, devoid of any emotion. With each step he took, Shaya's aura grew weaker, and she spat out blood even more violently.

It was as if the entire world had turned against Shaya, unable to accept her existence, vowing not to rest until she was ground to dust.

At the brink of life and death, Shaya made the wisest choice. Suddenly, she dropped to one knee, her demeanor devout, and pleaded, "Spare me, my lord. Shaya is willing to serve you!"

She was clever, so it was not hard to guess Shaman Niu's intention in sparing her life. Since he neither coveted her beauty nor was in a hurry to kill her, there could only be one reason—to subdue her!

So, knowing she could neither escape nor resist, Shaya immediately lowered her head. She had no lofty ideals of dying with honor; to survive, she would do anything. This was the instinct of all living things.

Yang Kai halted his steps and smiled faintly. "Good."

Only then did Shaya feel the omnipresent pressure around her suddenly ease. She could not help but let out a breath, raising her head slightly and gazing at Yang Kai with a pitiful look on her stunningly beautiful face. "My lord, how would you like me to prove it?"

Just as before, when Yang Kai had sought her surrender and she needed him to show sincerity, now that she had chosen to abandon her pride and allegiance, she naturally had to prove herself. No one would be foolish enough to believe empty words.

"Open your Sea of Consciousness!" Yang Kai ordered.

"Yes..." Shaya hesitated for a moment, but ultimately gave up defending her Divine Sense. In an instant, all the secrets within her Sea of Consciousness became unguarded, ready for Yang Kai to take at will.

Of course, doing so could cause irreparable harm to Shaya. A single misstep could leave her a complete idiot.

Yang Kai did not pry into those secrets. Instead, his Divine Sense surged, rushing into Shaya's Sea of Consciousness and imprinting his own Soul Brand within.

Shaya's delicate body trembled, and she sensed an invisible, intangible connection had formed between herself and this Shaman Niu, as if a rope had tied her, with the other end firmly in his grasp.

She inexplicably felt a sense of closeness to Shaman Niu, no longer finding him as repulsive as before. Shaya knew this was due to the Divine Soul technique. She quietly probed the strength of that technique, and in the next moment, her heart sank.

She had thought that, relying on her own Divine Soul strength, even if he left a mark in her Sea of Consciousness, she would find a chance to erase it. After all, he was only equivalent to a Demon Commander.

But in reality, Shaya found the imprint was so solid that she could not shake it at all. The last bit of hope she had instantly vanished.

"You should know, if you have any bad thoughts, I will sense it," Yang Kai said with a smile, looking at her.

Shaya blushed slightly, raising her eyes coquettishly. "My lord, I was wrong. I won't dare again. From now on, Shaya is yours. I hope my lord... will show me mercy."

She looked endlessly delicate and charming, her allure so great it seemed to drain the world of color.

Yang Kai simply raised his hand. Shaya's expression changed drastically as a sharp pain pierced her chest. She lowered her head to look down, and her face went pale—a thumb-sized hole had appeared in her chest, piercing clean through from front to back, with fresh blood spraying profusely.

Shaya's face instantly turned pale.

"If you dare use your charm technique on me again, the next hole will be in your head," Yang Kai said coldly, utterly merciless.

"Yes!" Shaya straightened up, her expression serious.

Although a hole in her head would not be fatal, it was certainly not a pleasant experience, so she immediately became obedient.

Yang Kai tilted his head at her, frowning. “You said before that as long as the demon heart is not destroyed, a demon cannot die. Since the demon heart is your foundation, why didn’t destroying it twice kill you?”

Earlier on the battlefield, Yang Kai had ambushed her from behind, and the Myriad Sword had clearly pierced Shaya's demon heart. Just now, he had again pierced her demon heart with a single finger, yet aside from looking a bit weak, she showed no signs of being fatally wounded.

Shaya replied, "I have two demon hearts. As long as either one exists, I will not die."

Yang Kai raised an eyebrow. "Are other demon experts like this too?"

Shaya said, "Most have multiple demon hearts; only a very few have just one. If my lord wishes to kill anyone above the Demon King level in the future, you must make sure to destroy all their demon hearts."

Having just surrendered, she was eager to prove herself, so she did not hide this information.

"I understand," Yang Kai nodded. He raised his hand, and Shaya instinctively flinched, fearing she had angered him again. But soon she realized she was overthinking it—a delicate jade bottle floated toward her and landed gently on the ground.

Shaya looked up, suspiciously eyeing Yang Kai.

"Take the pill inside. I have more questions for you."

Only then did Shaya pick up the jade bottle, open the lid, and pour out a round, plump, crystal-clear Spiritual Pill. The fragrance wafted over, making her want to swallow it immediately.

She did not hesitate and swallowed the Spiritual Pill at once.

Very soon, Shaya sat cross-legged in shock, silently regulating her breath.

In less than the time it takes to burn a stick of incense, her injuries had fully healed, and even her previous internal wounds and depleted energy were greatly restored. She became lively and radiant again.

The vitality of the demon race was indeed formidable, especially since Shaya was a Demon King. But most of her rapid recovery was thanks to that miraculous pill.

Could outsiders really refine such things? Just now, Shaman Niu had called it a pill.

Shaya frowned deeply. Her understanding of the Barbarian Tribe's medicine was limited to some herbal pastes that looked like glue. She had never seen, nor even heard of, a fragrant Spiritual Pill like this.

There was no denying the pill's incredible effects. If outsiders could casually refine such pills, their wounded would receive excellent treatment.

This would be very disadvantageous for the demon race.

As she thought about it, Shaya smiled wryly. Why was she worrying about this? Now that she was a prisoner, her only hope for survival was to beg Shaman Niu for mercy. Thinking about these things was meaningless.

Suddenly, she had become someone else's slave!

Shaya sighed softly, collected her thoughts, stood up, and obediently walked over to Yang Kai. When she reached him, she said, "My lord, I am ready."

Yang Kai turned around, looked at her, and asked his first question: "Where did you come from?"

"Demon Domain!"

"Demon Domain?" Yang Kai frowned.

Shaya smiled slightly. "The myriad worlds stand side by side. The world you are in is not the only one. The Demon Domain is an existence equal to your world."

Yang Kai took a deep breath. "You mean, there are more worlds like this?"

Shaya nodded. "Of course. Although I have not been to many places, according to the records left by our ancestors, there are indeed many great worlds in this endless time and space. But there are barriers between worlds, so they are not easily aware of each other. Only those whose power has reached the peak can sense them."

"How did your demon race discover those worlds, and how do you enter them?"

“The world barriers vary in strength. In some worlds, the boundary is as good as nonexistent—so long as one finds the entrance, one may come and go at will. But in others, the barrier is extremely firm, and not just anyone can pass through. If one wishes to enter or leave, a certain price must be paid. Mm, the barrier between your world and the Demon Realm is rather sturdy. Previously, it was Lord Demon God himself who took action to break it open.”

"Demon God!" Yang Kai's eyes narrowed.

He had heard Shaya mention these words before, but Yang Kai had always thought the so-called Demon God was just a symbol or spiritual pillar, like the Shaman God for the Barbarian Tribe.

But now, from Shaya's words, it seemed the Demon God truly existed and had even personally broken through the barrier between the two worlds.

This news made his skin crawl. What kind of power would it take to do that? Even among the famed Ten Great Emperors of the Star Realm, he wondered if any had such an ability.

Shaya said solemnly, "In the Demon Domain, only the strongest can become Demon Gods."

"There's more than one?" Yang Kai's brow twitched violently.

"Of course there's more than one." Shaya looked at him strangely, as if she could not understand why he would ask such a question.
