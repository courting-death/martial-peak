## Chapter 2841 — Recruiting Members

At this thought, many Great Shaman Masters could no longer remain calm. Why was it that, though they were all Great Shaman Masters, only Shaman Niu received gifts and not themselves? They too were Great Shaman Masters, and none present were any weaker than Shaman Niu.

“Hey, what’s the meaning of this? Why are you all giving things to him? How about giving some to this Shaman as well?” a Great Shaman Master shouted from midair.

“That’s right, with a great battle imminent, these things should be divided equally. He shouldn’t be the only one to get them.” Someone immediately echoed.

This sentiment gained the approval of more Great Shaman Masters, and for a time, everyone clamored incessantly, the scene descending into chaos.

“What’s all this commotion?” The Barbarian Tribe warrior who had led the way earlier barked coldly, showing no intention of giving the Great Shaman Masters any face. With a stern expression, he looked up at them and snorted, “Who said we’re giving these weapons to Lord Shaman Niu as gifts? Every single one of them was traded for—he bought all of them.”

“Bought... bought them?”

“Impossible!”

“With so many weapons, what could he possibly use to trade for them?”

No one had such immense wealth. The pile of weapons on the ground numbered at least a thousand, and more were still being brought in. Even a Shaman King couldn’t possibly possess enough riches to exchange for so many weapons.

The leading Barbarian Tribe warrior shook his head. “I don’t know what Lord Shaman Niu used to trade with the others, but he gave me this.”

As he spoke, he took out a round object. The many Barbarian Tribe warriors looked on, still confused, but the Great Shaman Masters’ faces all changed: “A Barbaric Beast Inner Core!”

And it was a high-quality Barbaric Beast Inner Core at that. Such a core would greatly benefit even a Shaman King’s cultivation, let alone a Great Shaman Master. If it was such a core, it could indeed be traded for quite a few weapons.

But no Great Shaman Master would use such a treasure to exchange for weapons they didn’t need. And to trade for so many weapons, just how many of these Inner Cores did Shaman Niu have?

The very thought was terrifying.

“Lord Shaman Niu traded the same thing with me,” another shop owner chimed in, taking out another Inner Core and waving it before them. The sight nearly blinded the gathered Great Shaman Masters.

If all those various weapons were irresistibly attractive to every Barbarian Tribe warrior, then these Inner Cores were just as tempting to the Great Shaman Masters.

In that moment, many people’s gazes toward Yang Kai changed, a single question echoing in their minds: Where did he get so many Barbaric Beast Inner Cores?

No Great Shaman Master had the ability to slay so many Barbaric Beasts. Even a Shaman King couldn’t do it. In other words, where could so many Barbaric Beasts be found to kill?

As time slowly passed and the truth of the transaction became known, the noisy Great Shaman Masters felt too embarrassed to keep shouting. They could only watch longingly as the weapons were distributed, faces full of envy. Some even began to wonder if they should get on Shaman Niu’s good side. If nothing else, just getting a Yao Beast Inner Core from him would benefit them immensely.

Only after a long while did the last Barbarian Tribe member bringing weapons arrive, and the pile on the ground had grown to several thousand pieces!

The quickened breathing of the many Barbarian Tribe warriors betrayed their inner turmoil.

The villagers from Cangnan Village were so excited they kept swallowing their saliva. They had already guessed what these weapons were for.

After all, they weren’t fools. With the demon invasion and a great battle looming, Yang Kai suddenly acquiring so many weapons was clearly to arm them. It was just that his miraculous methods left them in disbelief.

Ah Hu sidled up to Yang Kai, quietly tugged at him, and asked in a low voice, “Ah Niu, are these things... for us?”

Yang Kai smiled, patted his shoulder, and said, “Of course they’re for you.” Then, with a wave of his hand, he said, “Pick for yourselves, one per person.”

At his words, over a thousand people immediately straightened up.

Those Shaman Apprentices who had previously grumbled about Yang Kai’s inaction now had nothing to say, their faces full of reverence.

Outside the royal city, more than a hundred Great Shaman Masters had gathered. But who among them could mobilize so many weapons at once? Who had such boldness?

To accomplish great things, one must first sharpen their tools. With so many sharp weapons, even a thousand people would have an advantage that teams of two or three thousand could not match. And the weapons here numbered far more than a thousand.

Several Shaman Apprentices could already guess what would happen next. Any lingering complaints or dissatisfaction they’d once felt toward Yang Kai had long since vanished. Now, they only felt admiration for this Shaman Niu—though he looked young, his methods were seasoned and decisive. He never acted rashly, but when he did, it struck right at the heart of the matter. It was impossible not to be impressed.

Exchanging glances, the Shaman Apprentices from different villages all shared a knowing smile.

The sound of gulping echoed one after another. The thousand under Yang Kai’s command gathered beside the mountain of weapons, but none rushed to snatch anything. They all gazed dreamily at the pile, their eyes full of gentle light, as if what they saw was not cold steel, but a beautiful woman stripped bare.

“You all go first!” Ah Hu suddenly turned his head to the villagers from the other villages. As a warrior from the same village as Yang Kai, Ah Hu felt this was the moment to bring honor to Yang Kai and establish his prestige, so he didn’t rush to grab anything, instead giving priority to the other villagers.

Hearing this, the leaders of those villages exchanged glances. One of them smiled and shook his head, saying, “These are weapons brought by Lord Shaman Niu. Cangnan Village should have first pick. You go ahead.”

“That’s right, Ah Hu, you all take first!”

“Yes, Ah Hu, no need to decline!”

Ah Hu shook his head. “It’s precisely because Ah Niu got these things that you should pick first. These aren’t just for Cangnan Village, but for everyone.”

The leaders of the various villages immediately began to argue, none willing to give in, and the onlooking Barbarian Tribe warriors could only curse inwardly, full of envy and helplessness.

The oldest Shaman Apprentice suddenly smiled and said, “No need to argue. Now that we’ve all gathered here, there’s no longer any distinction between villages. We’re all one now, part of Shaman Niu’s tribe. Who goes first doesn’t matter. Besides, there are so many weapons here, even if everyone takes one, there’ll still be plenty left. There’s no need to fight over it.”

“Shaman Niu’s tribe...” Everyone’s eyes lit up at the words, and they fell into deep thought.

The old Shaman Apprentice was right. Now that everyone had gathered together, there was no need to distinguish between villages. Only by truly becoming one could they unite in the coming war. Deliberately dividing by village would only breed selfishness, and any selfishness could cost lives in battle.

Ah Hu pondered for a moment, then nodded and said, “The elder is right. Since that’s the case, let’s all choose together.”

No one else objected, and everyone surged forward to divide up the weapons, each finding one that suited them and making way for others to choose.

In an instant, the place was filled with laughter and chatter. Though lively, it was not chaotic; everyone acted in an orderly and restrained manner.

This scene, seen by the Great Shaman Masters, left each with a different expression.

Envy and jealousy went without saying. Shaman Niu had united over a thousand people with nothing more than a gift of weapons—a method they had to admire. Looking at their own subordinates, the Barbarian Tribe warriors who watched with red eyes, they could only sigh.

There was nothing to be done. Though the method was obvious, it couldn’t be copied. None of them had Shaman Niu’s immense wealth.

Alongside their admiration, a sense of crisis arose.

Earlier, Lord Shaman King’s order had been clear: each Great Shaman Master was to recruit two to three thousand tribesmen. Half a day had passed, and many had completed the task, but even more were still working at it.

There were also many unattached warriors still watching. Originally, everyone was competing fairly, but now, with Shaman Niu’s sudden move, he had left the other Great Shaman Masters far behind.

It was certain that once those thousand finished dividing the weapons, even more tribesmen would want to join them. If all the strong warriors were recruited by Shaman Niu, what would the others do?

Realizing this, several Great Shaman Masters on good terms with one another gathered to discuss countermeasures.

After the time it takes for an incense stick to burn, all of Yang Kai’s more than a thousand Barbarian Tribe warriors had received their own weapons. Each warrior now gripped a mighty axe, broadsword, or spear forged from refined steel, their spirits soaring and their bearing transformed. The archers, too, had exchanged their old bows for powerful new ones, their firepower greatly enhanced.

Yet the mountain of weapons had only shrunk by a third, with even more left waiting for new owners.

Groups of Barbarian Tribe warriors gathered around, eyeing the various weapons, licking their lips, their fingers itching. Then they looked at Yang Kai, eyes full of longing.

Yang Kai smiled faintly, snapped his fingers, and said, “Shaman Niu’s tribe is recruiting!”

“Lord Shaman Niu, I wish to join!”

“I want to join, I want to join!”

“I want to join too! Lord Shaman Niu, please accept me! I’m the strongest warrior in my village!”

As soon as Yang Kai finished speaking, countless voices drowned him out. The scene nearly spiraled out of control, but Ah Hu and the others quickly drew their gleaming weapons and blocked the surging tribesmen.

Yang Kai raised his hand and said, “Don’t rush, and don’t push. Lord Shaman King ordered us to gather two to three thousand tribesmen, and my goal is three thousand, so many will have a chance. Anyone who wants to join my Shaman Niu tribe, please line up in order, come forward to receive your weapon, and you’ll be one of us.”

With those words, the scene immediately quieted. The next moment, the Barbarian Tribe warriors surged forward and quickly formed a long line.
