## Chapter 2742 — Changes at Linghu Palace


"Is this Primordial Cauldron Mountain?" Ji Ying stared in astonishment at the nearly collapsed peak, unable to believe his eyes.

There was hardly any trace left of the former Primordial Cauldron Mountain. All that remained were dark red fragments of stone, the aftermath of a volcanic eruption.

"Yes!" Yang Kai nodded. "Back then, I received Senior Gongsun Mu's legacy here. The original cave mansion was deep within the mountain, but later, the earth fire surged and magma erupted, destroying everything."

It was by harnessing the power of that earth fire that Liu Yan had once gained strength comparable to the Dao Origin Realm.

Yang Kai sighed, "Back then, my strength was lacking, and I couldn't give Senior Gongsun Mu a proper burial. I only managed to lay him to rest in haste. Thinking about it now, I feel I've let him down."

Ji Ying offered comfort, "Brother Yang, you needn't worry. Third Senior Brother owes his return to Medicine Pill Valley to you. If he knew, he'd be endlessly grateful—he wouldn't mind such minor details."

Yang Kai said, "The place where Senior was buried is quite far underground. Let's clear things up first."

"Alright!"

The two immediately set to work, swiftly clearing the area with their techniques.

Both were Emperor Realm cultivators. At full strength, they could move mountains and overturn seas. In no time, the remaining half of Primordial Cauldron Mountain vanished completely.

Each picked a spot and dug downward, extending their Divine Sense to search carefully.

They worked for an entire day. After a full day, Yang Kai suddenly made a discovery, changed direction, and began digging to the left, sending a Divine Sense message to Ji Ying.

Before long, Ji Ying followed the tunnel Yang Kai had dug and descended, landing beside him. "Did you find it?" he asked.

Yang Kai pointed in a certain direction. "Try searching over there with your Divine Sense."

Ji Ying immediately extended his Divine Sense in the indicated direction. After a moment, his expression grew solemn and he nodded, "It's just as you said."

He had clearly detected a skeleton buried deep in the earth.

With the location confirmed, things became much easier. Yang Kai gripped the Myriad Sword, gently channeling Emperor Yuan. Wherever the blade passed, the hard stone was reduced to powder by a mysterious force, allowing the two to move quickly.

After the time it takes for an incense stick to burn, they finally reached Gongsun Mu's burial site.

Ji Ying stepped forward and carefully cleared the soil around the remains. A set of pale white bones was soon exposed to view.

"Third Senior Brother, your Junior Brother Ji Ying, by order of Master, has come to bring you home!" Ji Ying solemnly cupped his fists to the skeleton, then stepped forward, took out a prepared jade urn, placed it nearby, and began gently collecting Gongsun Mu's bones, placing them one by one into the urn.

Before long, the task was done. Ji Ying sealed the urn and stored it in his Spatial Ring.

"Now that this is settled, I need to return to the Northern Territory to report to Master. Brother Yang, what are your plans?" Ji Ying looked at Yang Kai.

"I still have some business here," Yang Kai replied. "But it's nothing major. I'll see you off first."

Ji Ying smiled, "If you have things to do, there's no need to trouble yourself. I remember the route, I can go alone."

Yang Kai said, "You may remember the route, but you can't activate the Space Array."

"What do you mean by that?" Ji Ying asked in surprise.

Was there a Space Array in this world he couldn't activate?

Yang Kai didn't explain in detail, only saying, "I made some modifications to that cross-territory Space Array. Without me, no one can activate it."

"Is that possible?" Ji Ying was shocked. To tamper with a cross-territory Space Array—wasn't that something only a Great Emperor could do? How had Yang Kai managed it?

He didn't know that the cross-territory Space Array had been set up by Yang Kai himself.

"But take this. With it, you can activate the array." Yang Kai took out a token and handed it to Ji Ying. It was a specially refined Teleportation Token, imbued with his own Laws of Space.

Ji Ying accepted it solemnly. "Rest assured, Brother Yang. Once I reach Lingxiao Palace, I'll return this token to Chief Steward Hua."

This token was no ordinary item—it was a pass that allowed one to travel directly from the Northern Territory to the Southern Territory, a privilege few possessed. Ji Ying would never dare keep such a treasure for himself. Yang Kai entrusting it to him spoke volumes about his trust and regard for Ji Ying’s character.

Yang Kai smiled and waved his hand. "No need. Now that you're the Chief Alchemist of Lingxiao Palace, you have the right to own a Teleportation Token. If you ever want to come to the Southern Territory, it'll be much more convenient."

Ji Ying was moved. "In that case, thank you, Brother Yang. Rest assured, once I've handled Third Senior Brother's affairs, I'll head to Lingxiao Palace. By the time you next visit, the Dragon Blood Pill will be ready."

"I almost forgot about that!" Yang Kai grinned. "I'll be counting on you, then."

High above Primordial Cauldron Mountain, the two clasped fists in farewell and parted ways.

Only after Ji Ying's figure disappeared from sight did Yang Kai turn and look in another direction.

Fenglin City... He hadn't been back since he left all those years ago.

Strictly speaking, Fenglin City was Yang Kai's first stop in the Star Realm. It was here he met his first friend. Now, he wanted to visit again—partly to see how the place had changed, and partly to buy a few things.

After using the Dragon Transformation Secret Technique last time, his body had grown, and his clothes were ruined. He was thinking of buying a new set of armor.

His Ethereal Crystals and Ethereal Jade were nearly depleted. These two materials were essential for setting up Space Arrays. Even the most skilled couldn't work without resources.

Yang Kai's plan was to connect all Four Great Territories of the Star Realm with cross-territory Space Arrays. That way, disciples of Lingxiao Palace could travel anywhere they wished, making it much easier to go out for training or to purchase cultivation materials.

The price of the same material varied in each territory. Some were expensive here but cheap elsewhere.

Many clever cultivators risked their lives traveling between territories, buying low and selling high, making huge profits. If Yang Kai's plan succeeded, it would be invaluable for Lingxiao Palace's future development.

Pushing his speed to the limit, Yang Kai couldn't help but feel a sense of returning home in glory, and he laughed at himself for the thought.

Fenglin City wasn't his hometown, but the feeling was the same. When he left, his strength was only at the First Stage of the Dao Origin Realm. Now, he was an Emperor Realm expert admired by all.

Primordial Cauldron Mountain wasn't far from Fenglin City. Back then, Kang Siran had taken Yang Kai to the mountain in just a few days. Now, with Yang Kai's greatly increased strength, his speed was incomparable.

After half a day of travel, he could already see the outline of Fenglin City in the distance. Not far from it stood another city, just as large.

Linghu Palace!

Yang Kai suddenly remembered.

Linghu Palace only existed because of him.

There hadn't been a Linghu Palace outside Fenglin City before, but one day, eighteen Spirit Spring Sources erupted from underground, flooding the area with Spiritual Qi and forming a spiritual lake. The event alarmed all the top sects of the Southern Territory—even Star God Palace got involved.

But no one could monopolize such a gift of heaven and earth.

In the end, the top sects of the Southern Territory led a joint effort to build Linghu Palace above the lake, dividing the cultivation chambers into different grades. Any Southern Territory cultivator could enter to train, provided they paid Source Crystals.

The profits were split among those top sects of the Southern Territory.

Xiaoxiao's weapon, the Demonic Warhammer, had been unearthed by Yang Kai from beneath Linghu Palace. In those days, he had fought a desperate battle against the Great Demon God’s remnant soul there, escaping death by the narrowest of margins.

Linghu Palace had become a landmark of the Southern Territory. Its abundant Spiritual Qi attracted countless cultivators. Such a prime cultivation spot was rare, and the Heaven-Grade chambers there were nearly as good as those in the top sects. It was the best place for breaking through bottlenecks.

Countless cultivators stuck at bottlenecks spent fortunes to train in Linghu Palace, hoping for a breakthrough.

Over the years, some succeeded and soared, but more lost everything with no gain. It was a gamble—win and you could rise, lose and you fell into ruin. Still, more and more came each year. The Earth-Grade and Human-Grade chambers were always in short supply, with long queues for a spot.

As for the top Heaven-Grade chambers, not just anyone could enter. You needed enough Source Crystals and someone to vouch for you.

Many disciples of the top Southern Territory sects would come to Linghu Palace's Heaven-Grade chambers for closed-door cultivation.

Every year, Linghu Palace's profits were counted in the billions.

Linghu Palace not only drew the attention of all Southern Territory cultivators, but even many from other territories traveled great distances to come. As the crowds grew, more buildings sprang up nearby, and in a few years, the area had become a city in its own right.

Fenglin City, not far away, also benefited greatly from Linghu Palace's presence.

In the past, Fenglin City's Spiritual Qi was thin, cultivation was slow, and the strongest was City Lord Duan Yuanshan, only at the Second Stage of the Dao Origin Realm. The deputy city lords and family heads were all at the First Stage.

Now, Fenglin City's Spiritual Qi was rich, rivaling top cities in the Star Domain. The population had exploded, and with it, prosperity. Emperor Realm experts were now a common sight, with many staying in the city year-round. In the past, local cultivators could only hope to catch a glimpse of such powerhouses by chance.

Linghu Palace was too small, so many who couldn't find a place to stay settled temporarily in Fenglin City. The two cities were close, making travel between them extremely convenient.
