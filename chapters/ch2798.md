## Chapter 2798 — Yours to Claim
The corridor before them was the buffer zone of the Fifth Layer, a place anyone could enter—provided they could find the entrance on the Fourth Layer.

No wonder Lei Ting was waiting here; he had clearly anticipated that Yang Kai would come to the Fifth Layer.

“Brother Lei, don’t you want to go in and take a look?” Yang Kai glanced at the deep, pitch-black entrance and tilted his head to ask.

“There’s no rush.” Lei Ting replied blandly.

Yang Kai calculated on his fingers and said, “If I remember correctly, this Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda trial will end in just a few days. If we don’t hurry, we might miss our chance.”

Lei Ting sneered, “That only applies to you.”

He was the nephew of the Great Elder of the Star God Palace, and one of the leading figures of the younger generation in the palace. The Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda opened once every three years, and as long as he wished, he could enter almost every time. As for exploring the Fifth Layer, he was in no hurry.

On the other hand, Yang Kai had only gotten this opportunity by a stroke of luck. If he didn’t seize it, he might never have another chance to enter the Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda.

“That’s true.” Yang Kai stroked his chin and smiled, “In that case, I’ll go on ahead.”

“Where do you think you’re going!” Lei Ting shouted, locking his aura tightly onto Yang Kai, making it clear that if he dared to move, he’d pay the price.

Yang Kai frowned, a bit impatient. “Brother Lei, there’s no enmity between us. Why be so aggressive?”

Lei Ting sneered, “Indeed, there’s no grudge between us. But you’re a rare opponent—I can’t just let this chance slip by.”

Yang Kai thought for a moment, then looked at him seriously. “Is that just an excuse? Isn’t the real reason the Princess?”

Lei Ting’s face flushed as he waved his hand. “What are you talking about? Don’t spout nonsense.”

Yang Kai grinned, “It’s obvious, Brother Lei. You care deeply for the Princess and admire her. You’re afraid my presence will catch her interest, so you want to prove your strength with force and make her see you differently?”

Lei Ting looked embarrassed. “Is it that obvious?”

Yang Kai pointed at his own eyes. “I’m not blind, am I?”

Having been exposed, Lei Ting seemed to relax a bit and said openly, “You’re right. Xun’er and I grew up together, and I’ve never seen her so enthusiastic toward any man before. You give off a dangerous feeling, so I have to take you seriously.”

Yang Kai’s eyes flashed with a strange light as he smiled, “That’s quite an honor. But Brother Lei, you’re mistaken…” He shook his head as he spoke.

Lei Ting was taken aback. “Mistaken about what?”

Yang Kai said seriously, “The Princess is indeed stunning and amiable, easy to like. But with her status and vision, how could she fall for just any man? Matters between men and women require mutual affection and matching backgrounds.”

Lei Ting thought for a moment and nodded. “You’re right.”

Yang Kai continued, “You and the Princess are childhood friends, so you know her temperament best. Besides, you’re the nephew of the Great Elder—who is second only to the Great Emperor and above all others. With your status, you’re a good match for the Princess. As long as you can win her heart, you’ll surely succeed.”

Lei Ting burst out laughing. “Didn’t expect you to be so interesting.” His face suddenly turned cold as he changed his tone. “Do you take me for a fool? You think I’ll believe you just because you say a few words?”

Yang Kai was stunned and sighed, “Brother Lei, I just want to tell you—I’m not your enemy. Your real rival is someone else.”

Lei Ting snorted, “Who?”

Yang Kai blinked and said, “Who else could it be? Xiao Chen, of course! Brother Lei, haven’t you heard the saying, ‘the pavilion nearest the water sees the moon first’? While you and I are locked in this standoff, Brother Xiao is probably right by the Princess’s side. In terms of cultivation and background, he’s your equal, and I’ve seen him attending to the Princess more than once, always at her beck and call. When a man and woman spend so much time together, feelings are bound to grow! So tell me, Brother Lei—aren’t you worried?”

Lei Ting sneered, “Xiao Chen? Him? He’s nothing! I could crush him any time. But you… Xun’er’s attitude toward you is unsettling.” As he spoke, he clenched his fists, making cracking sounds, and approached Yang Kai with a sinister grin. “Don’t worry, I’m not going to kill you. At most, I’ll cripple you. Once you’re out of the picture, Xun’er will be mine.”

“Oh?” Yang Kai raised an eyebrow, his expression turning serious. “Brother Lei, have you never heard that disaster comes from the mouth? Aren’t such reckless words a bit too much?”

Lei Ting curled his lip. “No need for more words. Let’s fight!”

“Oh… So in Senior Brother Lei’s eyes, I’m already yours to claim?”

Suddenly, a crisp, pleasant voice came from behind Lei Ting.

Lei Ting’s fierce expression froze instantly. His hair stood on end, and all his fighting spirit was doused as if a bucket of cold water had been poured over him.

Gulping with difficulty, Lei Ting felt cold sweat pouring down his forehead like a waterfall. He turned his head stiffly, almost mechanically, and saw Lan Xun and Xiao Chen standing side by side atop the Space Array. Lan Xun gazed at him with calm indifference, while Xiao Chen wore a mocking, gloating smile.

“Wh… when…” Lei Ting was utterly shocked, feeling as if his heart was sinking into the Nine Nether Purgatory, never to rise again. He wailed inwardly—he was finished. Now that Xun’er had heard his words, she would never look kindly on him again.

What he couldn’t believe most was that two people had appeared behind him without him noticing. If not for that, he wouldn’t have made such a fool of himself.

It was Yang Kai!

He had deliberately distracted him. Earlier, Yang Kai had activated the Laws of Space; Lei Ting had thought it was caution, but now it was clear Yang Kai had been interfering with his judgment.

“Lei Ting! How could you say such things? Hurry and apologize to the Princess!” Xiao Chen suddenly shouted righteously.

Lei Ting’s face turned beet red, and he stammered, “I… I…”

Lan Xun slightly raised her chin, gazing at him as if waiting for an explanation.

Lei Ting couldn’t say a word. After struggling for a while, he finally gritted his teeth, stomped his foot, and rushed toward the entrance, disappearing in the blink of an eye.

“Lei Ting, get back here! You think you can leave without explaining yourself?” Xiao Chen shouted angrily, knowing full well Lei Ting wouldn’t return, but a subtle smile curled at the corner of his mouth.

Lei Ting was finished now—he’d never stand a chance again. He’d never been good with words in front of the Princess, and after being set up by Yang Kai like this, he probably wouldn’t have the face to appear before her ever again.

A rival in love was gone! Xiao Chen felt extremely pleased and glanced lazily at Yang Kai.

He was different from Lei Ting. Although Yang Kai was a bit of a threat, he didn’t take him too seriously. Yang Kai was right—being close to the Princess was the real advantage!

After this Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda trial, Yang Kai would leave the Star God Palace. Who knew if he’d ever see the Princess again? There was no threat at all.

Only that idiot Lei Ting would fall for such a trick.

“Wasn’t that a bit cruel…” Yang Kai looked in the direction Lei Ting had fled, stroking his chin and muttering to himself.

Lan Xun said, “It was Senior Brother Lei who wouldn’t let go. Senior Brother Yang, you don’t need to take it to heart.”

Yang Kai chuckled and said, “Well, if he can see through the matters of the heart, his future achievements will surely be greater. Let’s just consider this a test for him.”

Lan Xun pursed her lips and smiled, her beautiful eyes shining as she looked at him. “Senior Brother Yang, you sound like you’re speaking from experience.”

Yang Kai coughed lightly. “I’ve just experienced a bit more in life.”

Xiao Chen, who had been watching coldly from the side, saw the two getting along so well and felt uneasy. He quickly interjected, “Brother Yang, you found the entrance too? Congratulations.”

“Just a stroke of luck.” Yang Kai smiled and then looked at them suspiciously. “But how did you two end up together?”

Xiao Chen smiled faintly. “Fate!”

After entering the Fourth Layer of the Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda, he had been searching for Lan Xun, passing through more than a dozen shattered spaces before finally finding her a few days ago and taking up the role of her protector.

Lan Xun said, “There are only a few days left before the one-month deadline. The Fifth Layer is just ahead. Senior Brother Yang, do you want to take a look?”

“Of course.” Yang Kai nodded. “But I wonder, how is the Fifth Layer different from the Fourth?”

Lan Xun replied, “Of course there are differences!”

“Please explain!” Yang Kai asked humbly.

Xiao Chen interjected, “The spaces in the Fourth Layer are all fragmented worlds with incomplete laws of heaven and earth. But in the Fifth Layer, although there are many spaces, they’re not like the Fourth Layer. Instead, each is an independent secret realm!”

“Secret realms!” Yang Kai raised his eyebrows.

Xiao Chen said, “Exactly. Those secret realms were born from the shattered worlds of the previous stages. When the ancient masters forged the Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda, they fused many broken worlds together to form the first four stages. The secret realms left behind in those worlds were extracted and placed in the Fifth Layer.”

Yang Kai pondered for a moment before saying, “So, the secret realms in the Fifth Layer might be related to some of the broken worlds in the previous stages?”

“That’s right, though the connection isn’t strong. The many secret realms in the Fifth Layer are as varied as the countless broken worlds in the earlier stages. Which one you enter depends on luck—you can’t choose for yourself.”

Yang Kai was silent for a long time before sighing, “The Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda truly is extraordinary.”

Before understanding the truth about the Five-Colored Treasure Pagoda, Yang Kai had only thought its existence was of great significance to the Star God Palace. But now that he fully understood, he realized it was a true treasure!

In the Star Realm, billions of cultivators pursued nothing but opportunity. And where did opportunity come from? Secret realms were the greatest source.
