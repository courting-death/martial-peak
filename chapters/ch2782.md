## Chapter 2782 — The Creditor Comes Knocking

Sure enough, after a short while, a not-too-large, not-too-small island came into view. In this endless sea, the sudden appearance of such a verdant island was truly a feast for the eyes.

The island wasn’t very big, only a few dozen mu in size. On the beach, waves crashed in succession, and birds paused to rest.

Yang Kai and Nanmen Dajun drifted down lightly.

"Eh?" Yang Kai suddenly furrowed his brow and looked at Nanmen Dajun in surprise. "Wasn’t that master supposed to be unaffiliated? Why are there so many people on this island?"

When he had just swept the island with his Divine Sense, he had clearly detected signs of activity—at least twenty people, and even five or six Emperor Realm cultivators among them.

If not for the presence of so many Emperors, Yang Kai would have thought this was the base of some small sect.

But then again, no small sect could possibly have so many Emperor Realm cultivators.

Hearing Yang Kai’s question, Nanmen Dajun was just about to explain when a streak of light suddenly shot over. Clearly, someone had sensed the arrival of Yang Kai and Nanmen Dajun and came to investigate.

"Palace Master, don’t say a word later. I’ll handle this!" Nanmen Dajun hurriedly sent a message, as if wary of something.

Yang Kai frowned, feeling that this trip was getting stranger and stranger.

The streak of light dispersed, revealing a middle-aged man who radiated the aura of the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm.

The newcomer glanced at Yang Kai and Nanmen Dajun, asking casually, "Who are you two?"

These were two cultivators at the First Stage of the Emperor Realm, but he didn’t pay them much mind. He remained rather nonchalant, a faint trace of arrogance still lingering on his face as he asked his question.

Nanmen Dajun immediately gritted his teeth and replied, "We’re here to collect a debt!"

Collect a debt? Yang Kai turned to look at Nanmen Dajun and saw that he wore a solemn expression, full of indignation, looking every bit the part of a creditor come to collect.

What’s going on here? Yang Kai was full of doubts but couldn’t ask for clarification.

Unexpectedly, after hearing this, the middle-aged man actually nodded slightly. "So you’re fellow sufferers. How much does that guy owe you?"

Nanmen Dajun didn’t even hesitate: "Five million Upper-Level Source Crystals. What about you?"

The middle-aged man snorted coldly. "Twenty million! If this Lord finds that bastard, I’ll skin him alive."

"He’s truly despicable," Nanmen Dajun nodded in agreement. "Skinning him alive would be letting him off too easily. If he falls into my hands, I’ll make him taste every torture under the sun."

"Heh." The middle-aged man smiled faintly and nodded. "Excellent."

Nanmen Dajun asked, "Everyone here is after a debt?"

"That’s right," the middle-aged man nodded. "We’ve all come to an agreement: if anyone finds that bastard, we’ll restrain him immediately and sound the alarm. The rest will rush over, and there’s no way he’ll escape. Since you two are here for a debt as well, you’re in. But let me warn you, there’s a very advanced formation on this island. Don’t overlook anything suspicious."

"Many thanks," Nanmen Dajun cupped his fists.

"No need. We’re all victims here, so we should help each other." The middle-aged man replied politely, gave Nanmen Dajun a few more reminders, then turned and flew off into the distance.

Nanmen Dajun watched him leave, then gave Yang Kai a look and quietly slipped toward the other side of the island.

"What’s the deal with that artificer, anyway?" Yang Kai sent a message. "With so many people coming to collect debts, just how much does she owe?"

"Who knows." Nanmen Dajun wore a look of utter exasperation.

Yang Kai clicked his tongue. "You handled that pretty smoothly. Looks like you expected this scene?"

Nanmen Dajun’s expression turned sour. “That’s what makes it so maddening. This isn’t the first time it’s happened.”

Yang Kai looked surprised. “Wouldn’t refining a few Imperial Treasures be enough to clear the debt?”

Nanmen Dajun sighed. “However many Imperial Treasures are forged, it still won’t keep up with the gambling.”

“That master actually gambles?” Yang Kai asked, stunned.

“Addicted beyond saving. That alone would be bad enough, but every wager ends in defeat.” Nanmen Dajun clicked his tongue in disdain. “That’s why I said this one is trouble.”

Yang Kai suddenly realized something. "That flying Imperial Treasure—don’t tell me you won it in a bet?"

Nanmen Dajun’s face flushed. "I was forced! It wasn’t my choice."

In just a few words, Yang Kai had already formed a vivid impression of this Artifact Refiner he had never met. Tsk tsk… to gamble away a flying Imperial Treasure worth over a hundred million—what an extraordinary figure.

No wonder creditors had the place surrounded. An Emperor-Grade Artifact Refiner reduced to such a miserable state—truly pitiful.

As they spoke, the two arrived at a cliff. Nanmen Dajun furtively released his Divine Sense to check the surroundings. Once he was sure no one was watching, he suddenly took out a token and waved it.

Instantly, ripples spread across a section of the cliff, revealing a passage just wide enough for a person to enter.

Nanmen Dajun quickly slipped inside, calling for Yang Kai to follow.

Once Yang Kai entered, he closed the entrance behind them.

Noticing Yang Kai’s puzzled look, Nanmen Dajun said, “I arranged this formation myself. Without the Illusion Array, there wouldn’t be a place left to hide.”

"You two seem pretty close…" Yang Kai chuckled.

“Palace Master, don’t lump us together.” Nanmen Dajun’s expression was one of pure disdain. “If word spread of any connection, disaster would surely follow.”

"Is it really that bad?" Yang Kai’s face darkened.

Nanmen Dajun complained, “Palace Master has never seen just how shameless that scoundrel can be. Back then, I had to settle quite a few debts out of my own pocket. Creditors kept coming to the door—I can still remember it clearly.” He gave an involuntary shudder.

Yang Kai didn’t know what to say. It seemed this artificer had left Nanmen Dajun with quite a shadow in his heart.

They continued deeper inside, walking for the time it takes several incense sticks to burn, before the path suddenly opened up into a huge cavern. The cavern was empty, but there were many side passages leading off in all directions, their destinations unknown.

"Palace Master, wait here a moment." After saying this, Nanmen Dajun turned to examine the side passages. After a while, his gaze locked onto one, and he coughed lightly. "Little Hou, come out."

No one responded; only Nanmen Dajun’s voice echoed in the cavern.

"Stop hiding! You think I can’t see you?" Nanmen Dajun’s face darkened as he shouted.

"Don’t come any closer! If you do, I’ll kill myself right here!" Suddenly, a woman’s voice rang out, but it sounded extremely weak, as if she’d suffered a serious injury. Her words were full of despair.

"A woman?" Yang Kai was stunned.

Earlier, when Nanmen Dajun had spoken of this Emperor-Grade Artifact Refiner, the disgust on his face and the way he cursed that person as a scoundrel had made Yang Kai assume it was a man. Who would have thought it would turn out to be a woman!

This surprised him, but then he relaxed. Female artificers weren’t unheard of—Yang Yan was a top-tier artificer and formation master.

Nanmen Dajun’s face darkened. "Idiot! You can’t even recognize my voice?"

The woman hiding in the side passage was silent for a moment, but Yang Kai could clearly feel a pair of eyes watching them. After a while, a figure wrapped in a breeze darted out and landed not far away.

"Dajun, it’s you!" the woman cried out in delight.

Yang Kai looked closely and saw that, while this woman wasn’t a peerless beauty, she was still quite attractive. She wore a floral dress that made her look playful and cute, but her figure was full and mature. Yet, for some reason, her expression seemed listless and weary, as if weighed down by exhaustion.

Reunited after a long separation, the woman was overjoyed. After a brief pause, she rushed straight toward Nanmen Dajun.

"What are you doing!" Nanmen Dajun was clearly prepared, reaching out to press her head down. Emperor Yuan surged from his palm, pinning her firmly in place.

The woman put on a fawning smile and cooed, "Dajun…"

Yang Kai got goosebumps all over and immediately felt uncomfortable.

"Wretch, how dare you act up!" Nanmen Dajun shouted, his palm surging with Emperor Yuan as he tried to push her away.

But the woman pressed forward a few steps against the pressure, slipped past him, spun into Nanmen Dajun’s arms, then floated lightly onto a smooth stone nearby, giggling.

"My ring!" Nanmen Dajun’s face turned black as he touched his now-bare middle finger. Looking up, he saw the ring already in the woman’s hand, which she waved at him triumphantly.

Yang Kai’s eyes flashed with surprise.

The change had happened so quickly that, although he hadn’t seen it clearly, he’d still caught a trace of it.

This artificer’s hands were truly quick. Even though Nanmen Dajun was on guard, she’d still snatched his Spatial Ring in an instant. Was this really an artificer?

"Give it back!" Nanmen Dajun said coldly, reaching out to the woman.

The woman pouted her rosy lips, and with a casual flick, wiped away Nanmen Dajun’s Soul Brand from the ring. She rummaged through it, then burst out laughing and pulled out several jars of fine wine. Delighted, she exclaimed, “I knew you’d treat me best!”

As she spoke, she sprawled out, tore open a seal, and poured the wine straight into her mouth.

As for the Spatial Ring, she flicked it back without a sound. Nanmen Dajun caught it with a sullen face, saying nothing.

Only the sound of gulping echoed through the cavern.

Yang Kai rubbed his forehead, suddenly feeling a headache coming on.

When he’d first heard that this artificer was addicted to gambling, it hadn’t really affected him. But now, seeing her act like a seasoned drunkard, Yang Kai felt like leaving immediately.

Damn! Another woman who drinks!

Just days ago, Gao Xueting’s drunken episode had left a scar on Yang Kai’s mind. Now, the mere sight of a woman drinking brought that wretched experience rushing back.

His heart was pounding with anxiety.
