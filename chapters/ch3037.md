## Chapter 3037 — Doesn't Know Any Better

The timid-looking young girl seemed extremely familiar, but Madam Hua could not remember where she had seen her before.

She had been on this Spirit Island for almost two hundred years. If she truly had seen the girl before, it would have been more than two hundred years ago.

Yet judging by the girl's cultivation and appearance, she was clearly only in her teens. In other words, she had not even been born two hundred years ago, so how could Madam Hua possibly have seen her? Moreover, the girl's face and eyes... gave her an intensely unpleasant feeling. They bore a strong resemblance to someone else she knew.

Madam Hua’s beautiful eyes shifted, and she looked once more at Lu Sanniang, who was hiding behind Yang Kai. Seeming to have realized something, she sneered, “Is skulking around so amusing? Get the hell out here!”

Lu Sanniang trembled at her words. How could she dare come out?

Seeing this, Madam Hua became even more certain of her guess. The corners of her mouth curled into a faint smile. "Sanniang, we haven't seen each other in more than ten years. Don't tell me you no longer recognize your elder sister?"

Sanniang?

Yuan Wu froze and looked at Madam Hua in surprise. He quickly realized that Madam Hua and Lu Sanniang knew each other. His heart thumped as he inwardly cried, This is bad! If Madam Hua was on good terms with Lu Sanniang, he might be in serious trouble.

However, after taking a closer look at Madam Hua's expression, Yuan Wu's heart settled. How could that expression possibly mean she was on good terms with Lu Sanniang? There was clearly some bad blood between them.

His eyes darting about, Yuan Wu pretended not to know and asked, "Madam knows her?"

Madam Hua chuckled coldly. "I do, of course I do. This Madam and Sanniang lived together as sisters for more than two hundred years, and Sanniang always took such excellent care of me. How could I possibly not know her?"

Yuan Wu smiled ingratiatingly. "What a coincidence."

He understood now. Madam Hua and Lu Sanniang had probably both been kept as playthings by the Dragon Clan member who lived here. Judging by Madam Hua's sarcastic tone, the two must have competed fiercely for favor on this island in the past. Women only ever had so much to fight over, after all. Later, Lu Sanniang had been expelled from the Dragon Palace by the Dragon Clan and ended up in Half-Dragon City, while Madam Hua had remained behind. Now that enemies had met again, how could Madam Hua possibly have any goodwill toward her?

"Sanniang, aren't you going to come out and greet your elder sister? Why keep hiding? Your elder sister has already seen you."

Realizing there was no avoiding this, Lu Sanniang had no choice but to step out from behind Yang Kai, her head slightly lowered and her expression deeply uneasy.

Madam Hua gave a soft snort, while the two women beside her both exclaimed in low voices, "So it really is you!"

The two of them clearly knew Lu Sanniang as well. Influenced by Madam Hua's attitude, neither showed Lu Sanniang any courtesy. They stared at her coldly, their gazes even carrying traces of contempt and malicious delight.

Back when Lu Sanniang still lived on the island, Fu Chi had doted on her greatly. Her status on this Spirit Island had been exactly equal to Madam Hua's current standing. Madam Hua had competed with her for two hundred years, yet had always remained a step behind. Now their fortunes had reversed. Madam Hua had risen to the top, while Lu Sanniang had been ruthlessly expelled. The victor was king and the loser a bandit, so there was naturally no need to think highly of her anymore.

"Elder Sister Hua, Younger Sisters..." Lu Sanniang greeted them softly with her head lowered, her expression growing even more awkward and uneasy. If possible, she would not have come out at all, but Madam Hua had already guessed her identity, so hiding was pointless. Moreover, if today's matter was not properly resolved, it would become a serious problem.

Yet as she looked at these faces that were both familiar and strange, Lu Sanniang could not help recalling the past, and her spirits sank.

"Insolence!" The woman holding the fish tank shouted, glaring coldly at Lu Sanniang. "What gives you the right to call us elder and younger sisters? Take a look at yourself in a mirror! How dare someone who was expelled address us as sisters? You truly have no shame."

Although the other woman did not rebuke her aloud, her expression clearly conveyed the same meaning.

They had feared Lu Sanniang in the past because Fu Chi doted on her, but now they were the ones standing high above her. In their eyes, Lu Sanniang was nothing more than a piece of rotten meat discarded by the Dragon Clan. Since she was only rotten meat, why should they fear her?

Madam Hua smiled faintly and said with apparent magnanimity, "Enough, enough. We lived together as sisters for so many years. Why make things difficult for Sanniang?"

Only then did the woman fall silent.

Assuming the posture of a victor, Madam Hua looked down at Lu Sanniang from above and asked, "We haven't seen each other in more than ten years. Have you been well, Younger Sister?"

Lu Sanniang lightly pursed her red lips and replied softly, "Thank you for your concern, Elder Sister. Sanniang has been well."

Madam Hua nodded lightly. "I'm glad to hear that. Ha... when you left this island back then, you have no idea how heartbroken your elder sister was. I even went to Lord Fu Chi to plead for you, hoping he would let you stay. Unfortunately, His Lordship's mind was made up, and there was nothing I could do. I hope you don't blame me, Younger Sister."

Lu Sanniang shook her head. "How could Elder Sister say such a thing? Sanniang has never blamed you."

Madam Hua smiled. "I knew Sanniang was always the most understanding. No wonder His Lordship doted on you the most back then. Thinking of the old days..." As she spoke, she looked sidelong at Lu Sanniang. Seeing the sorrow on her face, Madam Hua felt extremely satisfied and continued, "How imposing you were in those days, Younger Sister. Even your elder sister had to live at your mercy. Yet who could have known that misfortune would strike without warning? The Heavens are truly unjust."

Lu Sanniang stood there without saying a word. She knew Madam Hua was mocking her, but when beneath another's roof, one had no choice but to lower one's head. Besides, she had never been someone who enjoyed fighting with others, whether in the past or now.

"Oh, right. How did you return to the island this time, Younger Sister? Did His Lordship allow you to come back?" After speaking for a while, Madam Hua suddenly changed the subject.

"It wasn't His Lordship's order." Lu Sanniang shook her head lightly.

Madam Hua feigned surprise. "If it wasn't His Lordship's order, how dare you return? Aren't you afraid he will blame you when he finds out?"

Yuan Wu chuckled from the side. "Madam, we came to the island this time to build a palace for His Lordship. They are also here to contribute, not because anyone summoned them."

Madam Hua nodded in realization. "So that's how it is. Then you must be very careful. This palace is being prepared for His Lordship's grand wedding, so there can be no carelessness whatsoever. If anything goes wrong, none of you will escape punishment."

"Grand wedding?" Yuan Wu froze.

He truly had not known about this. After Fu Qi brought all five hundred of them here several days ago, he had merely assigned them their task and left without explaining what the palace was for. Only now, after hearing it from Madam Hua, did Yuan Wu realize that the palace was actually being prepared for a grand wedding of the Dragon Clan.

A grand wedding of the Dragon Clan!

This was an extraordinary event. The Dragon Clan had exceedingly few members, their growth periods were unbearably long, and the imbalance between males and females meant there was very little chance of two of them becoming partners. As far as Yuan Wu knew, the last grand wedding of the Dragon Clan had taken place roughly ten thousand years ago. One could easily imagine how important this occasion was.

Realizing this, Yuan Wu immediately declared, "Rest assured, Madam. This Yuan will spare no effort in constructing the palace and ensure that the Dragon Clan Lords are satisfied."

"Mm." Madam Hua nodded lightly, then sighed with a hint of helplessness. "His Lordship is going to marry as well. Who knows what our lives will be like from now on? Your elder sister is actually a little envious of you, Younger Sister. You left this place more than ten years ago and regained your freedom, while your poor elder sister and the others still have to remain here. We don't even know which mistress we'll have to serve in the future."

"Then you can leave too! No one is forcing you to stay!"

Lu Yuqin suddenly cried out indignantly.

Although she was young and inexperienced, she could still tell good intentions from bad. From the moment Madam Hua and the other two women arrived, they had targeted her mother at every turn. Their every word carried an inexplicable air of superiority, and their mocking barbs had never ceased. Lu Yuqin had long since been furious and finally erupted.

Lu Sanniang's expression changed. She tugged at Lu Yuqin and rebuked her in a low voice, "Don't speak recklessly."

Madam Hua's long, narrow eyes fixed on Lu Yuqin as she asked softly, "Sanniang, is this that little bastard?"

Lu Yuqin had already been born when Lu Sanniang was expelled from the island. It was precisely because Lu Yuqin did not possess even the slightest trace of the Dragon Bloodline that her mother had been ruthlessly expelled.

Considering her age and appearance, how could Madam Hua fail to realize the relationship between Lu Yuqin and Lu Sanniang?

After Lu Yuqin talked back to her, Madam Hua no longer felt like maintaining a pretense of civility with Lu Sanniang. She had never liked Lu Sanniang in the first place, so naturally she would show Lu Yuqin no courtesy either. Moreover, Lu Yuqin was the child born from Lu Sanniang's union with His Lordship. Even without the Dragon Bloodline, she was still His Lordship's descendant.

Lu Yuqin's very existence was like a thorn lodged in Madam Hua's heart, making her long to be rid of the girl.

Lu Sanniang's face went pale. "She's only a child and doesn't know any better. Please forgive her, Elder Sister."

The woman holding the fish tank said coldly, "A child may not know any better, but shouldn't an adult?"

Lu Sanniang bit her red lip lightly. "Please show mercy, Elder Sister. Sanniang apologizes to you on her behalf."

Madam Hua sneered. "What do you mean by that, Younger Sister? You make it sound as though your elder sister is completely unreasonable. Am I truly so frightening?"

"Then Elder Sister means..."

Madam Hua waved her hand. "I won't hold this matter against you."

Lu Sanniang was somewhat surprised, but still said gratefully, "Many thanks, Elder Sister."

Madam Hua said, "I won't hold that matter against you, but Xiao Hong's injury cannot simply be forgotten. You don't know this, Younger Sister, but ever since you left, your elder sister has thought of you day and night, missing you so much that I could neither eat nor sleep in peace."

Lu Sanniang naturally did not believe her. Back when she still lived on the island, Madam Hua had often tripped her up and constantly fought with her. Even when Lu Sanniang ignored her, she had shown no intention of stopping. Madam Hua would have been overjoyed by her departure, so how could she possibly have missed her day and night? It was plainly nonsense.

"Fortunately, your elder sister found Xiao Hong." Madam Hua's gaze shifted, and she looked tenderly at the red carp in the fish tank. "Over the past ten years or so, Xiao Hong has brought your elder sister a great deal of comfort. I have long regarded it as family. Now that someone has injured Xiao Hong, your elder sister will not let the matter rest. I hope you can understand that, Younger Sister."

She stared coldly at Lu Sanniang and asked in a chilling voice, "Who injured Xiao Hong?"
