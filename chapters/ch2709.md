## Chapter 2709 — I’ll Die Before I Talk

Three days later, Yang Kai slowly opened his eyes and glanced at the Golden Armored Heavenly Tome in his hand, feeling rather helpless.

He had spent three full days refining it, yet the Golden Armored Heavenly Tome showed not the slightest reaction, giving no sign of accepting his Emperor Yuan or Divine Sense.

This made him realize that refining the Golden Armored Heavenly Tome was absolutely not something that could be accomplished in a short time.

Tan Junhao had been able to wield the tome’s power—who knew how many years he’d spent refining it?

Still, Yang Kai wasn’t discouraged. In fact, he was quite pleased. The harder it was to refine, the more it proved the Imperial Treasure’s extraordinary power. Back in the Shattered Star Sea, it had taken him over a year to refine the Mountain and River Bell.

Though he made no progress with the tome, three days of rest had left him fully recovered. The benefits of the recent battle were also gradually showing: not only had his Emperor Yuan increased, but even his Divine Sense had grown stronger. Advancing to the Second Stage of the Emperor Realm would surely be easier in the future.

Putting away the tome, Yang Kai swept his Divine Sense outside, sent a few messages, and waited quietly.

Before long, Hua Qingsi, Chi Yue, Ai Ou, Ghost Ancestor, and Gu Cangyun gathered in the side room. Hearing the commotion, Yingfei and Zhu Qing soon joined them as well.

“Everyone, Tan Junhao may be dead, but the Heart-Controlling Gu he planted is still inside you. I need to extract the Gu, so I’ll need your full cooperation,” Yang Kai said solemnly, looking at the group.

Chi Yue’s eyes lit up at his words. “Boy, you can get the Gu out?”

“I can give it a try!” Yang Kai nodded.

Normally, only the one who raised the Gu could control it at will. Yang Kai wasn’t versed in Gu techniques and didn’t know how the Heart-Controlling Gu was cultivated, but he had the Slave-Insect Bracelet.

This Imperial Treasure, like the Soul-Severing Blade, was a relic of the Insect Emperor. While the Slave-Insect Bracelet couldn’t be used for combat, it was the nemesis of all strange insects and beasts listed in the Strange Insects Manual—it could control any of them.

As luck would have it, the Heart-Controlling Gu was indeed one of the insects listed in the Strange Insects Manual, ranking high on the Earth List.

Even Soul-Devouring Worms, which were on the Heaven List, were suppressed by the Slave-Insect Bracelet—let alone the Heart-Controlling Gu, which was only on the Earth List.

“If that’s the case, what are we waiting for? I’ll go first!” Gu Cangyun immediately volunteered, moving to sit in front of Yang Kai.

Ai Ou suddenly reached out, grabbed him, and tossed him aside with a cold snort. “Second Brother, I haven’t even moved yet and you dare to? Where are your manners?”

Ghost Ancestor let out a raspy chuckle from the side. “If we’re going by seniority, I should be first. All of you, move aside!”

As he spoke, he surged with ghostly energy, unceremoniously shoving Ai Ou and Gu Cangyun aside and sitting down in front of Yang Kai.

The three’s squabble lasted only a moment, but Yang Kai was left dumbfounded. Only after a beat did he realize why they were fighting to go first.

Yang Kai had only said he could try—he hadn’t given any guarantees. If something went wrong, the first to be tested would be in the most danger.

That was exactly why the three of them were so eager to push each other forward.

Chi Yue looked at Yang Kai nervously. “Boy, you’d better be careful. Eldest Brother is getting on in years and can’t take much more.”

Ghost Ancestor protested, “Nonsense! This Lord is still young. Sect Master, just do what you need to!”

Yang Kai couldn’t help but laugh. “There’s no need to be so tense. It’s merely a Heart-Controlling Gu—I’m confident I can handle it. If a test is needed, I still have more than twenty test subjects suppressed over there. No matter what, it won’t be your turn.”

The Mountain and River Bell was still on the battlefield, suppressing those twenty-some Emperor Realm cultivators. It wasn’t that Yang Kai couldn’t retrieve the bell, but he had other plans for those people and didn’t want to release them just yet.

Hearing this, everyone finally relaxed.

“Elder, clear your mind!” Yang Kai instructed Ghost Ancestor in front of him in a low voice.

Ghost Ancestor nodded, closed his eyes, and emptied his mind.

With a flip of his hand, the Slave-Insect Bracelet appeared in Yang Kai’s palm. He gently infused it with Emperor Yuan and activated it. In the next instant, a peculiar force was born from within the bracelet—it was precisely the Slave-Insect Qi of the Slave-Insect Bracelet.

Yang Kai extended a finger, winding the Slave-Insect Qi around its tip before guiding it into Ghost Ancestor’s body. Divine Sense and Emperor Yuan burst forth in unison as he cautiously directed the Slave-Insect Qi to circulate within Ghost Ancestor.

Ghost Ancestor let out a muffled grunt, but was otherwise unharmed.

Yang Kai’s cultivation was far superior to Ghost Ancestor’s, so he could easily control the Slave-Insect Qi. If Ghost Ancestor’s strength had been closer to Yang Kai’s, it wouldn’t have been nearly so effortless.

Soon, under Yang Kai’s guidance, the Qi reached Ghost Ancestor’s chest and precisely located the hiding place of the Heart-Controlling Gu.

Sensing something, the Gu suddenly grew restless and tried to flee, but Yang Kai’s fingers moved rapidly, tapping several times on Ghost Ancestor’s chest. The Slave-Insect Qi immediately spread out, completely enveloping the Gu.

The Slave-Insect Qi was a natural nemesis to these strange insects and beasts. Once wrapped in it, the Gu couldn’t escape no matter how it struggled.

Yang Kai slowly drew his finger upward, guiding the Qi to Ghost Ancestor’s throat, then pinched lightly.

Ghost Ancestor’s face changed, and in the next instant, he spat out a jet of blood.

Yang Kai was ready. With a wave of his hand, he deflected the blood aside, sending it flying toward Zhu Qing. He called out, “Miss Qing, if you would!”

Though Zhu Qing hadn’t been warned in advance, she reacted instantly. Her red lips parted, and a stream of scorching flame shot out, incinerating the blood and the Gu hidden within it in an instant.

She couldn’t help but roll her eyes, knowing Yang Kai had only roped her in because he’d seen her True Dragon Blood and realized her fire attribute a few days ago.

With the Gu expelled, Ghost Ancestor let out a long breath, feeling completely relaxed. Overjoyed, he jumped off the bed.

“Fifth Sister, your turn!” Ai Ou looked at Chi Yue and gestured.

Now that it was clear Yang Kai could remove the Gu and there was no danger, there was no need to fight for position. Instead, everyone became more courteous.

Unexpectedly, Chi Yue blushed and said, “You all go first. Lady Hua and I will go last.”

Ai Ou was taken aback, but quickly understood and said no more, giving Gu Cangyun a push forward.

With the first experience under his belt, Yang Kai was even more adept the second time.

In no time, he had removed the Gu from both Gu Cangyun and Ai Ou.

“We’ll wait for you outside,” Ai Ou said, then left with the others. Everyone else took the hint and filed out as well—even Zhu Qing obediently left at Yingfei’s signal.

Only Hua Qingsi and Chi Yue remained.

“I’ll go first,” Hua Qingsi said with a smile, sitting cross-legged in front of Yang Kai.

“Close your eyes,” Yang Kai instructed.

Hua Qingsi shot him a playful glare, as if letting him take advantage, then slowly closed her eyes.

Everyone had seen Yang Kai’s method: poking and prodding with a finger, especially on the chest—a sensitive spot. It was fine for the men, but for Hua Qingsi and Chi Yue, it was a bit awkward, so it was best not to have an audience.

There was no helping it. The Heart-Controlling Gu targeted the heart meridian, so it naturally hid near the chest. Yang Kai couldn’t avoid that.

Following the same method, Hua Qingsi soon spat out a jet of blood.

Yang Kai reached out and caught the blood in midair, his Emperor Yuan surging. After a flurry of actions, he finally killed the Gu—much more effort than it had taken Zhu Qing.

Hua Qingsi rose gracefully, gave Yang Kai a meaningful look, and left with a smile.

Chi Yue let out a helpless sigh and, unable to avoid it, sat down in front of Yang Kai. Feeling a bit uneasy, she muttered, “Boy, you’d better not have any funny ideas.”

Yang Kai’s face darkened. “Of course not, Senior. Don’t overthink it. I could do it from a distance, but it wouldn’t be as safe.”

“I know! Let’s begin,” Chi Yue replied.

As she spoke, she closed her beautiful eyes, looking resigned. Still, her expression betrayed her nervousness.

Yang Kai hesitated for a moment. After all, this was his mother-in-law, and he didn’t quite know where to start. In the end, he gritted his teeth and focused on the task.

As his finger passed over Chi Yue, Yang Kai could clearly feel her body trembling, her cheeks flushing as if on fire. When he reached her chest, her face turned even redder, her body tensed, and her power instinctively resisted.

If Yang Kai hadn’t been much stronger than her and easily suppressed her strength, there would have been trouble—she might even have suffered backlash.

After much effort, he finally forced the Gu out of her body.

Chi Yue got up and headed for the door. At the threshold, she suddenly turned back and solemnly warned, “You must never tell Qingluo about this.”

“Absolutely, absolutely!” Yang Kai nodded repeatedly. “I’ll die before I talk.”

He quickly realized he’d only helped her remove a Gu—nothing scandalous had happened. Why act so guilty?

Standing there in a daze for a while, Yang Kai finally shook his head and straightened up.

Now that everything was settled, it was time to check on those dozens of Emperor Realm cultivators. He couldn’t keep them suppressed in the Mountain and River Bell forever.

With a flicker, Yang Kai returned to the battlefield from a few days ago.

The place was deserted and ruined, with only an ancient bell standing guard.

With a gesture, Yang Kai summoned the Mountain and River Bell. At his command, it shrank in midair and landed in his palm.

Where the bell had been, two or three dozen Emperor Realm cultivators paced anxiously. A few looked listless, clearly badly injured and meditating to heal, but none of them seemed at ease.

Yang Kai glanced at the seriously wounded Emperor Realm cultivators and asked in surprise, “What happened to you all?”

He remembered that when he’d suppressed them with the Mountain and River Bell, they’d all been fine. How had a few days passed and some ended up injured? Had they fought inside?
