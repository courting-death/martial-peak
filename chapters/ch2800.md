## Chapter 2800 — Ancient Barbarian Tribe

All around, rows of wooden huts stood close together, just as rough and rugged as the men who lived here. Yang Kai gazed into the distance and saw a fence made of logs and stones not far away. This must've been a village, and it looked like a very primitive, ancient one at that.

Waves of beastly roars rolled in from outside the fence.

On top of the fence, many villagers stood, some hefting huge stones to hurl down, others drawing bows and loosing arrows.

The air was thick with the chaos of battle and the stench of blood, so strong it made one want to retch.

Suddenly, Yang Kai’s eyes narrowed.

He saw a spot along the fence where a massive leopard, at least three zhang long and rippling with muscle, shot up like a whirlwind. Its blood-red maw gaped wide and clamped down on a villager’s head. That villager, burly as an iron tower, struggled with all his might, but how could he possibly resist such a beast?

After only a brief struggle, the leopard bit off the man’s head. Blood spurted from his neck like a fountain, dazzlingly bright under the blazing sun.

The villagers around the fence were shocked and furious. They all roared and lunged at the leopard, while over a dozen sharp arrows whistled through the air, burying themselves in the beast’s body.

In pain, the leopard roared and lost its footing. A villager tackled it off the fence, and both of them fell. What became of them was unknown.

This sudden turn left a gap in the fence’s defenses, and the remaining villagers were thrown into chaos, shouting frantically.

“Quick, quick, quick!” Ah Hu dragged Yang Kai, racing to the base of the fence. With a powerful heave, he tossed Yang Kai up onto the wall and called to a sturdy woman standing there, “Ah Hua, Ah Niu is yours.”

The woman called Ah Hua glanced at Ah Niu with a slight frown, seeming a bit displeased. She was about to say something, but saw that Ah Hu had already dashed off toward the village gate.

“Whatever, better than nothing.” Ah Hua muttered impatiently, then reached out to Yang Kai and said curtly, “Arrows!”

Yang Kai was momentarily stunned. To be honest, ever since entering this secret realm, he’d been in a daze, unable to get his bearings. The dizzying chaos left him no chance to figure out his current situation.

All he knew was that he’d inexplicably appeared in this primitive, ancient village, gained the name Ah Niu, and been swept into a bloody battle.

He’d explored many secret realms before, but never had any of them been as complicated as this one.

Vaguely, he felt that this secret realm was no ordinary place. He secretly resolved to stay on high alert—perhaps there would be some unexpected gains.

As for whether these villagers were his own hallucinations or something else, he had no time to worry about that.

We’ll cross that bridge when we come to it. There’s always a solution.

“Arrows! Are you deaf?” Seeing Yang Kai still in a daze, the woman called Ah Hua couldn’t help but roar at him. Her eyes, like Ah Hu’s, were bloodshot and blazing red, her anger and hatred almost tangible.

This anger and hatred weren’t aimed at Yang Kai, but at the sight of so many villagers dying to the beasts, their bodies left in pieces, while she herself was powerless to avenge them.

Shouted at like that, Yang Kai finally snapped out of it. He quickly looked around and saw a pile of wooden arrows at his feet, each one a zhang long and as thick as a baby’s arm—truly intimidating.

He picked one up and exclaimed in surprise. The arrow was incredibly heavy, no lighter than those forged from fine steel.

It was hard to imagine ordinary people without any Yuan Qi handling them.

“Your job is to hand me these arrows!” Ah Hua stared at Yang Kai sternly and barked, “Did you hear me?”

“I heard you!” Yang Kai nodded honestly, taking the chance to size up the woman called Ah Hua.

The villagers here were generally rough and mature-looking, but this Ah Hua, though tall and strong, had delicate features and a striking figure. Her beast-hide clothes hugged her chest, revealing a deep cleavage, her waist was slender and supple, her belly flat, and her hips round and full.

Hm, if she weren’t so tall, she’d actually be quite a beauty.

But in this survival-of-the-fittest environment there was no room for delicate prettiness; ordinary people without strength were doomed to die.

Even so, Ah Hua had a unique kind of beauty.

With an arrow in hand, Ah Hua immediately drew her bow. The massive bow bent into a full moon in her grasp, making Yang Kai’s eye twitch.

This massive bow was made to shoot arrows a full zhang long and as thick as a baby’s arm—one could imagine just how enormous it was. Yet in Ah Hua’s hands, it seemed weightless.

Weng…

As the bowstring twanged, the arrow spun forward. Yang Kai clearly saw a shockwave burst from the tip as it flew.

The power of this shot was truly terrifying.

A giant beast leaping through the air was struck in the eye by the arrow, which pierced straight through its skull, spraying hot blood everywhere. The beast crashed to the ground, struggled a few times, and then lay still.

“Impressive!” Yang Kai praised sincerely.

Ah Hua shot him a cold look and held out her hand again.

Yang Kai hurriedly picked up another arrow and handed it to her.

Ah Hua drew her bow and fired again.

Every shot killed a giant beast. Ah Hua’s archery was incredibly precise—almost every arrow struck a beast in the eye, piercing straight through its head. It was no exaggeration to say she never missed.

There were more than a dozen archers like Ah Hua on the fence, each one a master. Yang Kai was amazed by their skill.

In such a harsh environment, if the villagers didn’t have these rare abilities, they’d never survive here.

The fighting on the fence was relatively orderly. Ah Hua and the other archers drew their bows and fired in turn, not needing to face the beasts head-on. But outside the village, over a hundred villagers wielded all kinds of weapons, locked in bloody combat with the beasts.

Beastly roars and human shouts mingled together.

Blood splattered, flesh was torn, giant beasts fell, and villagers died. In front of this nameless village, a desperate battle of life and death played out, as if both sides bore a hatred that could only end with one’s extinction.

Amid the battlefield, an old voice began to chant an ancient melody.

A hunched old man, leaning on a staff, walked calmly across the blood-soaked ground. As he chanted, mysterious lights flickered from his staff, flowing into the bodies of the fighting villagers as if alive.

Every villager touched by the light suddenly became incredibly brave and strong, as if injected with life. Their skin turned red, a crimson glow enveloped their bodies, and with furious roars, they knocked the beasts to the ground.

Strangely, though the old man looked frail, he walked through the battlefield completely unguarded, and the beasts ignored him as if they couldn’t see him at all.

Yang Kai’s eyes widened in shock. “Barbarian Arts! Barbarian tribe? Ancient Barbarian Tribe!?”

He hadn’t known what this primitive, ancient village was, but after witnessing the old man’s miraculous technique, a flash of insight struck him.

Legend had it that in ancient times, before martial artists flourished, disasters were constant and humanity struggled to survive against the heavens.

Barbarian Arts were born from this struggle.

The Ancient Barbarian Tribe focused on tempering the body. It was said that if one’s body reached the peak, they could become a Saint in flesh alone, undying and indestructible, no weaker than a Great Emperor today. In that era, there were no endless secret techniques or dazzling treasures—people could only rely on the strength of their own flesh and blood.

The people of that age were called the Ancient Barbarian Tribe, the ancestors of today’s humans.

Of all the Barbarian Arts, the most famous and practical was the Bloodlust Art.

Just like what the old man was displaying now—using some mysterious power to awaken the strength slumbering within the human body, igniting one’s Essence-Blood to gain a sudden, explosive surge of power.

But the Bloodlust Art was extremely dangerous. Once used, it left one weak for a long time and shortened one’s lifespan. Use it too often, and it could even lead to death.

That was because the Bloodlust Art burned one’s innate Essence-Blood.

No wonder the villagers here all looked so rough and mature—it clearly wasn’t their first time using the Bloodlust Art. With their innate Essence-Blood depleted, they would age much faster.

In the Ancient Barbarian Tribe, only a very few could master the Barbarian Arts. Most could only train their bodies.

Those who could practice the Barbarian Arts were called Shamans!

Barbarian Arts were also known as Shaman Arts!

That old man was undoubtedly a Shaman, but what level of Shaman he was, Yang Kai couldn’t tell. These things were too ancient, and Yang Kai had only heard bits and pieces during his years in the Star Realm. He had no idea how the powers of those distant times were divided.

So this was the Ancient Barbarian Tribe!

In other words, was he now in ancient times?

How could that be? The ancient era was so long ago, already lost to the river of history. Yet this secret realm preserved its ancient customs perfectly.

Was this place real, or just an illusion?

Was he really called Ah Niu now? Then what about Yang Kai? Was he dreaming?

“The village chief is a Shaman Apprentice, and you’re just now realizing it? What have you been doing all these years?” Ah Hua snapped at Yang Kai, spitting as she scolded him. “Remember this—the village doesn’t keep good-for-nothings! If Ah Hu hadn’t been sharing his food with you, you’d have starved long ago. Today, prove you’re worth keeping alive. Don’t embarrass Ah Hu!”
