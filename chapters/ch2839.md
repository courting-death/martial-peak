## Chapter 2839 — Time Waits for No One

As they brushed past each other, Yang Kai revealed a faint smile and nodded in greeting.

That Barbarian Tribe warrior stared at his back for a long while before withdrawing his gaze and chuckling softly. "Interesting!"

Although he was not a shaman, he had indeed cultivated his physical body, and as the captain of the royal city guards, his strength was in no way inferior to that of an Upper-Level Great Shaman Master. What he lacked compared to those Great Shaman Masters was only the shamanic arts to empower his tribesmen. In single combat, no Great Shaman Master could be his match, yet that seemingly frail Great Shaman Master just now actually stirred a fighting spirit within him.

Only those opponents he could not easily crush could ignite his fighting spirit!

This much was clear from how calmly the other man had met his gaze. The captain’s glance had carried a sudden burst of fighting intent; no Great Shaman Master could easily withstand it, yet the other man had done so.

What an interesting Great Shaman Master. He looked so weak, yet within that thin frame was a power beyond imagination. That fellow called Shaman Tu might even owe him thanks; it was precisely his timely appearance that spared Shaman Tu from public humiliation.

In the grand hall, a person whose aura was as deep as the sea stood quietly, hands clasped behind his back, waiting for everyone to arrive.

Only after more than a hundred people had entered and some time had passed did he slowly turn around, his brows tightly furrowed, as if troubled by something.

Raising his head to glance at the crowd, he declared in a loud voice, "I am Shaman King Dang, the Sixth Shaman King of the Southern Barbarian Tribe. Some of you may know me, some may have heard of me, and some may not know who I am, but that does not matter, for from today onward, we will be working together often."

"Greetings, Shaman King!" Everyone placed a hand to their chest and bowed in salute.

Yang Kai was among them, his gaze flickering slightly. The Sixth Shaman King—in other words, among all the Shaman Kings of the Southern Barbarian Tribe, his strength ranked sixth. As for how many Shaman Kings were beneath him, that was unknown.

Yang Kai only knew that the Southern Barbarian Tribe did not have any experts at the Shaman Saint level. In fact, throughout the entire Barbarian Tribe, there were only four Shaman Saints, each guarding one of the four greatest tribes. As for the Southern Barbarian Tribe, its strongest Shaman King was at the Upper-Level, but Yang Kai didn’t even know his name.

Most of what he knew about the Southern Barbarian Tribe came from the village chief’s teachings. Given the chief’s status as a Lower-Level Shaman Apprentice, the information he could access was truly limited.

"I believe you have all heard the reason for this gathering: the demon invasion, and the destruction of three tribes." Shaman Dang’s voice was low, not loud, but it reached every ear clearly.

When he mentioned the destruction of three tribes, all the Great Shaman Masters’ expressions turned grim.

Each of those three tribes was no weaker than the Southern Barbarian Tribe, each had an Upper-Level Shaman King overseeing them, each had one or two hundred Great Shaman Masters, and thousands upon thousands of Shaman Apprentices. Yet they were still wiped out. In other words, if the Southern Barbarian Tribe faced such an enemy, their fate would likely be no better.

This was no longer just the concern of the three destroyed tribes, but an issue for the entire Barbarian Tribe.

"However, that news is already outdated!" Shaman Dang’s words sent shockwaves through the crowd. As everyone looked at him in confusion, he continued, "In just the few days since we gathered, the Lanli Tribe has also been destroyed!"

"What?"

"Is that true?"

"The Lanli Tribe was wiped out as well."

"It’s said that the Shaman King of the Lanli Tribe was the one most likely to advance to the Shaman Saint Realm within a hundred years. What happened to him?"

The crowd of Great Shaman Masters cried out in shock. It was no wonder their faces looked so grim; the overall strength of the Lanli Tribe surpassed most Barbarian Tribe clans, second only to those guarded by the four Shaman Saints.

To suddenly hear that the Lanli Tribe had also been exterminated—how could they not be shocked?

Shaman Dang’s voice was low: "No one in the Lanli Tribe survived."

The hall instantly fell silent, so quiet that a pin could be heard dropping. No survivors meant everyone was dead, including that powerful Shaman King who was likely to become a Shaman Saint.

With a great war looming, such news shook everyone’s confidence. If even the Lanli Tribe was wiped out, just how terrifying were the demons? Who in this world could possibly resist them?

Sensing the crowd’s thoughts, Shaman Dang said, "The four Shaman Saints have all set out. I believe their strength will inflict some damage on the demons. But this is a war that concerns the entire Barbarian Tribe. As strong as the Shaman Saints are, we cannot place all the burden on their shoulders. We, too, must do our part. That is why the royal city has urgently summoned all tribesmen here."

Hearing that the four Shaman Saints had already set out, the crowd finally felt some relief. The Shaman Saints were the strongest beings in this world; with them taking action, the demon invasion would surely be contained. But Shaman Dang was right—this was a war for the entire Barbarian Tribe, and no one could remain uninvolved. There were only four Shaman Saints, after all.

"I believe you all want to ask what the demons are, where they come from, and why they want to destroy our Barbarian Tribe." Shaman Dang swept his gaze across the hall and said solemnly, "I must tell you, I do not know much more than you. But remember this: the demons are our mortal enemies. As long as a single member of the Barbarian Tribe draws breath, the demons must never be allowed to taint this land!"

The Great Shaman Masters were roused by his words, their blood surging, faces filled with resolve, as if they wished to rush to the battlefield that very instant and stain the sky with their blood to halt the demon invasion.

Yang Kai watched coldly from the side, thinking that the Barbarian Tribe truly had a simple mindset. Shaman Dang’s words contained no deliberate incitement, only a plain statement, yet they resonated with so many Great Shaman Masters. If he had tried to stir them up a bit more, the effect would have been even greater.

"Sir, what should we do now?"

"Yes, sir, are we to head to the battlefield right away?"

"I can’t wait to see how fierce the demons really are."

"Hahaha, I hope the demons don’t wet themselves when they see me!"

The Great Shaman Masters clamored, not taking the demons who had already destroyed four tribes seriously at all. Shaman Dang smiled slightly and said, "You will all have that chance. When the time comes, I hope you remember today’s words. However—" his tone turned stern, "it is not yet time. We must wait for news from the Shaman Saints, and our Southern Barbarian Tribe has not yet fully assembled. Many tribesmen are still on the road. If we had more time, our Southern Barbarian Tribe would be even better prepared, but time waits for no one. For now, your task is simple: gather all the tribesmen of the Southern Barbarian Tribe for me. Each of you is to assemble two to three thousand tribesmen and train them to obey orders."

The Southern Barbarian Tribe had several hundred thousand tribesmen.

For each Great Shaman Master to gather two or three thousand tribesmen was just enough to organize the entire tribe. Normally, these tribesmen lived scattered in various villages, and bringing them together suddenly would inevitably cause all sorts of conflicts. It would take time to smooth things out, and that was the job of the Great Shaman Masters as leaders.

Yang Kai nodded slightly at these words. A group of two or three thousand was just the right size for a team—neither too many nor too few, and about the limit a Great Shaman Master could control.

He currently had about a thousand people under him. According to Shaman Dang’s instructions, he needed to gather at least another thousand. That would be easy enough; with the Shaman King’s order, the tribesmen would surely cooperate. Villages without powerful shamans would naturally seek strong backers, but such things had to be mutually agreeable. With so many Great Shaman Masters gathered in the royal city, those scattered warriors would only seek to join someone after seeing their strength.

No one wanted to follow an unreliable Great Shaman Master.

At this thought, Yang Kai already had a plan in mind.

Shaman Dang did not say much more. This gathering was mainly to assign this task, so after a few more reminders, he dismissed everyone.

In an instant, over a hundred Great Shaman Masters rushed out of the hall, all eager to recruit followers. The more people one commanded when war broke out, the greater the security. At this critical moment, no one wanted to fall behind, and all wished to use every means at their disposal to secure the strongest warriors for themselves.

Only Yang Kai, after everyone else had left, walked forward unhurriedly.

A voice suddenly called out from behind, "What is your name?" It was Shaman Dang.

Yang Kai turned his head and replied, "Shaman Niu!" Then he added, "I was born with this appearance, but I am indeed from the Southern Barbarian Tribe. I hail from Cangnan Village!"

Shaman Dang nodded, "Physical stature matters little. You are a Great Shaman Master. If anyone questions your status again, just mention my name."

Yang Kai was taken aback, not knowing why Shaman Dang was being so considerate, but he still nodded, "Thank you, sir."

"Go on!" Shaman Dang waved his hand.

Only then did Yang Kai leave the hall.

A moment later, the captain who had locked eyes with Yang Kai earlier entered, looking at Shaman Dang curiously.

Shaman Dang asked, "Is there something you wish to ask?"

The captain said, "Sir, what is special about that man?"

He had clearly heard Shaman Dang’s words earlier and did not understand why the Shaman King had singled him out.

Shaman Dang pondered for a moment before replying, "Special, very special! He is a Great Shaman Master favored by the Barbarian God."

The captain frowned, "Anyone who becomes a shaman is favored by the Barbarian God."

Shaman Dang smiled slightly, "He is different. Before last winter, he was just an ordinary person—less than an ordinary person, even."

"What?" The captain was shocked. "Sir, are you sure?"

To have been an ordinary person before last winter, and to become a Great Shaman Master after just one winter—how could such a thing be possible?

Shaman Dang said, "When I was still a Great Shaman Master, I once guided a man named Shaman Li in cultivation for a time. Shaman Li was the previous village chief of Cangnan Village."
