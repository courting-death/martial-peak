## Chapter 2784 — A Scourge Lasts a Thousand Years

The floor of the cave was incomparably hard, yet when the sword fell from the air, it sank a full foot into the ground by inertia alone—proof enough of its sharpness. What was even more remarkable was that the blade had been forged from a single piece of Red Feather Steel, without any other materials added. While such a sword could be expected to possess great toughness, it would rarely be praised for its keen edge.

Just from this, Yang Kai had already formed a rough assessment of this woman’s artifact refining skills.

With a flick of his hand, the Red Feather Steel sword landed steadily in his palm. When Yang Kai activated his Emperor Yuan, the sword’s body vibrated with a hum. With a shake of his wrist, the long sword actually rippled like a wave, as supple as a rope.

"Dao Origin-Grade Lower-Level!" Yang Kai’s brows lifted slightly.

"Hahahaha!" The woman stood atop the rock, arms spread wide, laughing wildly. "All of you, bow before this lady’s miraculous and magnificent artifact refining arts!"

Nanmen Dajun said with a dark face, "Palace Master, why don’t we just go back now?"

If Nanmen Dajun had made this suggestion before the woman started refining, Yang Kai might have just gone along with it and headed home. But after witnessing her artifact refining skills, he had other plans.

This woman before him certainly looked unreliable, but her skills were real—and not just ordinary, either.

"Junior Brother, are you convinced now?" The woman looked at Yang Kai with pride, her face practically demanding he come worship her.

"Not bad," Yang Kai nodded slightly.

"Not bad?" The woman’s expression turned fierce, and she enunciated each word: "Not... bad?"

Such miraculous skill, and all she got was a mere ‘not bad’? The woman’s face darkened. She waved her hand as if shooing a fly. "Go, go, go, get out of here. You’re not welcome. The farther you go, the better."

Originally, seeing this brat was a guest brought by Nanmen Dajun, the woman had given him some face. Otherwise, why would she have deliberately shown off her artifact refining skills? But now, Yang Kai’s lukewarm evaluation had ignited her fury. She directly told him to leave, no longer interested in speaking to him at all.

She really hated those who didn’t know how to appreciate things, especially those who pretended to understand when they didn’t.

Yang Kai smiled faintly. "It really is not bad."

The woman’s expression turned cold. She said flatly, "Junior Brother, be careful what you say. Don’t be fooled by how I look now. With just a word, I could make sure you die without a grave."

Nanmen Dajun chuckled, "Hou, you’re talking big. Be careful you can’t back it up."

The woman snorted, "This lady is an Imperial-Grade Artificer. If I really wanted to kill someone, do you think no one would help me? Don’t be fooled by those people outside who are red-eyed trying to collect debts from me. If I really talked to them, they’d all turn into my thugs."

Nanmen Dajun said blandly, "The Palace Master is an Imperial-Grade Alchemist!"

"Uh..." The woman looked at Yang Kai in shock, then glanced at Nanmen Dajun, as if trying to spot a lie on his face. But she was disappointed—Nanmen Dajun looked completely serious.

And based on what she knew of Nanmen Dajun, he would never lie about something like this.

Imperial-Grade Alchemist? So young?

The woman was a little shaken, but still stubbornly said, "So what? Imperial-Grade Artificer, Imperial-Grade Alchemist, it’s all about the same."

Thinking about it, it was almost funny. On this nameless island, there was an Imperial-Grade Artificer, an Imperial-Grade Alchemist, and an Imperial-Grade Formation Master. Such a lineup was terrifying. Any sect that saw it would have their eyes light up and would stop at nothing to recruit them.

Yang Kai said, "Master Hou, right? Looks like Master Hou is very dissatisfied with my evaluation."

The woman snorted, "Whatever. We’re not related. How you evaluate me is your business."

"I said ‘not bad’ because I’ve seen even better artificers."

At these words, the woman immediately jumped up and cursed, "Bullshit! Who in this world could be a better artificer than me? In artifact refining, this lady is number one under heaven!" She turned to glare at Nanmen Dajun, gritting her teeth. "Can I beat this brat up?"

Nanmen Dajun shook his head. "Who can really claim to be number one?"

In any field, no one dared claim to be the best. Maybe Great Emperor Miaodan could boast so in alchemy, but this woman clearly didn’t have that weight.

"Master Hou doesn’t believe me?" Yang Kai’s lips curled into a subtle smile.

The woman snorted, "If you say so, then take me to meet this better artificer. I want to see who’s stronger."

Yang Kai shook his head. "I don’t know where that person is now."

The woman curled her lip, her face full of disdain. Clearly, she thought Yang Kai was just making things up.

"But..." Yang Kai changed the subject, "If I could prove that someone in this world is better than you at artifact refining, what then?"

"Then I'll marry you! And why not?" The woman sneered, tugging at her flowery dress and thrusting out her chest, her words shocking: "I've got the looks, I've got the figure, and I've still kept my innocence to this day. Don't you dare say I'm not good enough for you!"

Yang Kai glanced at Nanmen Dajun. The latter covered his face with his hand, looking helpless—clearly, he was used to her shamelessness.

"Alright." Yang Kai nodded, then casually took something from his Spatial Ring and tossed it to the woman. "Then, Master Hou, please take a look at this."

The woman caught it, glanced at it, and sneered, "Origin-Grade Secret Treasure? Where’d you get this toy? Hm, it’s even a warship..."

An Origin-Grade Secret Treasure was something only Origin Returning Realm cultivators would use. At her current level, it really was just a toy, completely useless to her.

Yang Kai smiled faintly. "The simpler and lower-grade something is, the more it shows the refiner’s skill. Isn’t that so?"

The woman frowned slightly, for once agreeing with Yang Kai. "You’re not wrong."

It was just like cooking—the true test of a chef’s skill wasn’t in rare delicacies, but in the simplest stir-fried shredded potatoes...

"Hm?" Suddenly, the woman’s expression grew serious. The contempt in her eyes vanished, replaced by caution and surprise. She turned the small warship over and over in her hands, Divine Sense surging as she examined its inner structure.

"Palace Master... do you really intend to recruit this woman into Lingxiao Palace?" Nanmen Dajun quietly sent a voice transmission. "You’ve seen what she’s like. If she joins, who knows what trouble she’ll cause."

"She acts so unruly and does whatever she wants only because she’s never met someone who can subdue her."

"Palace Master, are you confident you can subdue her?"

"No!" Yang Kai shook his head. "To subdue her, you’d have to beat her in her own field. I don’t have that ability."

Nanmen Dajun understood this deeply. The reason he agreed to join Lingxiao Palace wasn’t because Yang Kai was sincere or treated him well, but because there was something in Lingxiao Palace related to his own field that he was interested in but couldn’t unravel. That was the main reason.

"But even if I can’t, someone else can." Yang Kai revealed a mysterious smile.

"This Origin-Grade warship?" Nanmen Dajun frowned, realizing the key point.

Yang Kai chuckled and said nothing more.

An Origin-Grade warship was far too low-level for Yang Kai, but it had been personally refined by Yang Yan. It was something he’d brought from Dark Star.

Yang Kai had experienced Yang Yan’s artifact refining skills firsthand. If this Master Hou was even better than Yang Yan, then Yang Kai would have nothing to say. But he thought that was unlikely.

One could tell just by looking at the woman’s serious expression now.

To an outsider, it was just a spectacle; to an expert, a marvel of craftsmanship. In the eyes of Yang Kai and Nanmen Dajun, this Origin-Grade warship was nothing more than a low-tier Secret Treasure. But to the woman, it was a masterpiece of exquisite refinement.

Time passed slowly. The cave was silent. The woman’s expression grew more and more serious, and a fine sheen of sweat appeared on her forehead.

This detail left Nanmen Dajun both shocked and alarmed.

The woman was like this clearly because the Origin-Grade warship had given her a huge shock.

Although Nanmen Dajun didn’t accept her claim of being the world’s number one artificer, deep down, he also felt that in artifact refining, there was basically no one in the Star Realm who could surpass her. But now, it seemed there was always a higher mountain.

Who had refined this Origin-Grade warship, and where had the Palace Master gotten it? To have such a miraculous effect...

"Activate it," the woman suddenly ordered Yang Kai, her tone completely unceremonious.

Yang Kai didn’t mind. He formed a hand seal, and the palm-sized warship instantly expanded to five zhang in length, its whole body pitch black. Yang Kai opened the cabin door, and the woman slipped inside in a flash.

Judging by her actions, she was going to examine it thoroughly inside and out.

Yang Kai wasn’t in a hurry, so he simply waited outside with Nanmen Dajun.

By this point, Yang Kai was eighty percent sure of the outcome. It all depended on whether the woman would admit it herself.

Half an hour later, the woman suddenly walked out of the warship, her face blank, like a puppet, as if she were a maiden who’d just been ravaged by dozens of burly men. Even her steps were stiff and mechanical.

Only her beautiful eyes shone with a strange light. Her lips moved slightly, but no one knew what she was muttering.

Yang Kai and Nanmen Dajun exchanged a glance, both wisely choosing not to disturb her.

One hour, two hours, half a day, a night...

After two full days, the woman finally changed. Her eyes grew even brighter. She suddenly jumped up and clapped her hands. "Hahaha, I get it! So that’s how it is!"

She had actually gained some insight.

Seeing this, Nanmen Dajun finally let out a long breath.

He’d been worried for the past two days. Although he’d known her for years and she often did infuriating things, she was still an old friend. Seeing her so obsessed, he’d really feared she’d fall into Qi Deviation.

But now, it seemed all his worry had been for nothing. As the weight finally lifted from Nanmen Dajun’s heart, he couldn’t help feeling a bit annoyed at himself.

Good people die young, but a scourge lasts a thousand years. With all the things this woman had done, she’d probably live as long as heaven and earth, shining with the sun and moon. Why was he even worried about her?
