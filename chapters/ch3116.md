## Chapter 3116 — Check, Check

Inside the lava cave, the heat baked everything. The mist gradually thinned, and the once-blurred scene came back into view.

At the entrance to the lava cave, Han Qiancheng's eyes suddenly went wide. He stared ahead in horror, almost unable to believe what he was seeing.

The right Elder stood five steps from Su Yan, facing her with his bronze hammer in hand. Yet now a sword tip had pierced straight through his chest. A halo rippled over the point, spotless and clean, without so much as a trace of blood.

Cold air surged out, spreading rapidly from the wound to freeze the right Elder's body, limbs, head...

"You..." The right Elder opened his mouth, unable to believe he would actually die here. To him, the strike had been utterly inexplicable. It felt as though he had only lost focus for an instant, and by the time he came back to himself, his chest had already been pierced through.

Su Yan was a little surprised as well, clearly not expecting to kill the right Elder so easily.

She had meant to trade wound for wound. Who would have thought the other party would fall so easily?

Had he truly fallen that easily... A strange feeling began to grow inside her.

She had no time to think about it. Ice-cold energy poured madly into the right Elder's body, freezing him into an ice sculpture in the blink of an eye. When the Profound Frost Divine Sword was drawn out, the right Elder fell onto his back and shattered across the ground.

Su Yan turned. The Profound Frost Divine Sword swept forward, and a frigid aura wrapped in snowflakes came bearing down over the left Elder's head.

The left Elder had not yet recovered from the right Elder's sudden death. When he saw the killing move descend, he dared not be careless and hurried to block, only for his movements to stall. The snowflakes covered him from head to toe, and in an instant he followed in the right Elder's footsteps.

Those flakes of snow had been condensed from Su Yan's Origin Qi. They were incomparably pure, and their killing power was terrifying. The icy aura invaded the left Elder's body through his pores, making him shudder from head to toe. Even his Origin Qi could no longer circulate smoothly, so how could he possibly resist or defend himself?

When Su Yan came for him with her sword, the left Elder could not even raise his hand. Regret and pleading flashed through his eyes before Su Yan split him in two with a single stroke, spilling blood across the ground.

Su Yan stood with her sword in hand, her brow slightly furrowed. She felt no sense of accomplishment from killing two cultivators of the same level, nor any joy at escaping death. Instead, an indescribable sense of wrongness lingered inside her.

Something seemed to be surging inside her body.

She suddenly snapped awake and looked up toward the cave entrance, an unusual light blooming in her beautiful eyes.

She had been locked in a life-and-death battle with Yunxia Sect's two Great Elders all this time, and had failed to notice that faint abnormality inside her body. Only now, once the dust had settled, did she discover it. She understood better than anyone what it meant, and even with her cold temperament, she could not hide the change in her expression.

Expectation, surprise, fear, uncertainty...

"Ah!" Han Qiancheng cried out and stumbled backward, waving his hands. "Junior... Junior Sister, don't kill me. What happened today... it was only, only a misunderstanding."

The left and right Elders had died just like that. If he had not seen it with his own eyes, he would never have believed it. He had counted on those two capturing Su Yan so he could do whatever he wanted with her, but now his wishful thinking had come to nothing. If things went badly, he might even die here himself.

He was terrified, not knowing whether Su Yan would kill him too.

Two useless fools. How could one woman kill them so easily?

He suddenly stopped mid-retreat, as though he had run into a solid wall.

Han Qiancheng turned back, his mouth falling open. "You... how are you here?"

The young man called Yang Kai was standing right behind him with his arms folded. Who knew how long he had been there? Han Qiancheng had not sensed even the slightest trace of his aura until this moment. Wasn't he supposed to be at Yunxia Sect? Wasn't he supposed to have been taken down already? How could he appear here completely unharmed?

With danger before and behind him, Han Qiancheng went deathly pale. Then he spotted Ruan Biting standing off to the side and cried out, "Elder Ruan, save me!"

Ruan Biting looked at him coldly, making no attempt to hide the disgust in her eyes.

"Hello, Young Sect Master!" Yang Kai grinned at him, showing a mouthful of white fangs. Then he raised his hand slightly. "Goodbye, Young Sect Master!"

His palm came down. Without making a sound, Han Qiancheng turned into a puddle of minced flesh. Inside the lava cave, the stench of blood surged up, nauseatingly thick. Reunited with Su Yan after so many years apart, Yang Kai had no time to waste talking nonsense with this trash.

Yang Kai lifted his eyes to the woman inside the cave. The Su Yan in his memories gradually overlapped with the one before him until the two could no longer be separated. He swallowed. He clearly had a thousand things to say, yet did not know where to begin, and for some reason he grew nervous.

Su Yan gazed at him with clear eyes, as if he was all that remained in the world, leaving no room for anything else.

"Ahem..." Ruan Biting coughed lightly. "You two talk. I will take my leave first."

Even a fool could see the atmosphere had turned intimate, so naturally she would not stay. She only felt rather rueful. Because of one wrong thought, Yunxia Sect was probably truly finished this time. Where should she go from here?

A reunion after so long apart should have been deeply warm, but the three corpses sprawled across the ground rather spoiled the mood.

But so what? As long as they could be together, even a sea of blood and purgatory could taste sweet.

"What are you standing there staring for? Come here already!" Su Yan beckoned to Yang Kai, her voice trembling faintly and revealing that she was not as calm as she appeared.

Now she understood. She had killed the left and right Elders so easily not because those two had made mistakes, nor because she had suddenly broken through in the heat of battle, but because the man before her had secretly interfered.

Yang Kai rushed over, stretched out both arms, and held her tightly, with such force that he seemed to wish he could draw her into his own body and never be separated from her again.

The familiar fragrance, the familiar touch. Even after several dozen years apart, he had not forgotten the slightest detail. The instant they touched, all those sealed memories opened again, as vivid as yesterday, every scene clear before his eyes.

Su Yan wrapped her arms around him in return and buried her head against his shoulder. In that moment, time seemed to become eternal.

Yang Kai greedily breathed in her fragrance, unable to get enough of it.

A quarter hour, half an hour...

"All right." Su Yan lightly patted Yang Kai's back, her voice as gentle as a mother soothing a child.

"Not all right." Yang Kai still held her. Suddenly unsatisfied, he reached out, clasped her shoulders, met her eyes, lowered his head, and kissed her.

Her red lips were cool, just like her icy temperament, yet Yang Kai savored them like the sweetest nectar. He pried open her teeth, and their breaths mingled as they tasted years of longing. At first, Su Yan's body was still a little stiff. After all, they had not seen each other for so many years, nor been intimate in just as long. But once she adjusted, her body softened too, and she took the initiative to wrap her arms around Yang Kai's neck.

It was all wind, flowers, snow, and moon; if not for the surroundings and the corpses on the ground, it could almost have been a scene of poetic romance.

After kissing for a while, he grew unsatisfied again. One hand climbed to her hidden place, while the other covered the fullness of her chest. Astonishing softness filled both hands as he kneaded wantonly.

Su Yan clenched her fists and hammered his shoulder several times.

It had no effect. Instead, it only stirred Yang Kai's aggressiveness further.

Steeling herself, she bit Yang Kai's lip, but Yang Kai still remained unmoved.

"Don't force me," Su Yan mumbled. Before she could finish, her soft, fragrant tongue was caught and sucked fiercely.

Yang Kai's wanton movements suddenly froze. The intoxicating sensation in his hands vanished, replaced by a burst of icy aura, as if he were holding blocks of ice. Not only was it hard, it was bone-chillingly cold. His hands felt it, and so did his mouth. Even the person in his arms seemed to have become a block of ice.

Yang Kai leaned back slightly, fixed his eyes on her, and said in astonishment, "Senior Sister, what are you doing?"

At this moment, Su Yan's entire body had become crystalline and translucent, as though carved from ice. There was no trace of flesh and blood left at all. If not for her dazed eyes and faint panting, anyone would probably have taken her for an ice sculpture.

"Master is still outside." Su Yan stretched out a finger and tapped him on the forehead.

Although they had reunited after a long separation and longing had overwhelmed her, she was still a woman. How could she leave Ruan Biting waiting outside while she and Yang Kai indulged themselves in here?

Yang Kai caught her finger and played with it for a while, then asked in surprise, "Is this the Ice-Crystal Yu Body?"

Su Yan nodded, drew back her finger, then dispersed her Divine Ability and restored her flesh-and-blood body. She set him upright and said, "Don't move. Let me look at you."

Yang Kai stood in place, grinning from ear to ear.

Only after examining him from head to toe did she set her mind at ease. He had been wandering outside for several dozen years and had come back whole. That was enough. No matter how high or low his cultivation was, none of it mattered more than his safety.

Yang Kai said, "I'm injured."

Su Yan asked in alarm, "Where are you injured?" She had not seen the slightest sign of it.

Yang Kai looked as if he were in unbearable agony. "I don't know. Senior Sister, you have to check me carefully. My whole body hurts. I feel like I'm about to die."

"Where exactly are you injured?" Su Yan grew anxious.

Yang Kai reached out, wrapped an arm around her waist, and grinned. "This place isn't convenient for checking. I'll take you somewhere better." The two vanished in a flicker.

Inside the Small Xuan Realm, near the medicine garden.

In a few quick motions, Yang Kai stripped himself bare. He pulled Su Yan's hand over, placed it on his chest, and said shamelessly, "Senior Sister, hurry and check me. My heart is beating so hard."

Su Yan stared at him, blush rising over her fair cheeks. She lowered her head, gritted her teeth, and said, "You look perfectly fine to me. You aren't injured anywhere." To think she had actually worried a little just now. Thinking about it now, since he could silently influence those two Yunxia Sect Elders, his strength had clearly grown to an extremely terrifying degree. How could he be injured so easily?

His heart was pounding only because he was thinking about things he should not be thinking about.

Yang Kai swallowed, gazing at the blush on Su Yan's face as he pressed closer. "Senior Sister, you have to let me check you too."

"I... I'm not injured." Su Yan looked away.

"How would we know without looking? You just fought a great battle. If you were left with some hidden injury, that would be terrible." As Yang Kai spoke, his hands had already begun to move. How could a white dress withstand Yang Kai's rough handling? It was soon stripped away.

Skin like mutton-fat white jade appeared before his eyes, and Yang Kai leaned down.

A muddy passage, flower stamens wet with dew, soft moans rising and falling, clouds and rain coming in waves. A layer of alluring pink spread over her snow-white body, and even the air seemed to turn sweet...
