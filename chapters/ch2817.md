## Chapter 2817 — A Humiliating Disgrace

Alchemy and medicine-making had always been Yang Kai’s old trade, so in an unfamiliar place with limited funds, preparing medicines was the best way to obtain the supplies he needed.

In Frost Snow City, several barbarian warriors covered in wounds stopped in their tracks. The leader glanced around, then looked down at Yang Kai, his face fierce as he said, "You... are talking to us?"

Yang Kai smiled warmly, appearing harmless: "Indeed!"

The barbarian warrior frowned. Although he hadn’t understood much of what this fellow said just now, the last sentence was simple enough.

This guy had something to give him!

Free things were hard to refuse, but seeing the man’s demeanor, the barbarian warrior felt a bit distrustful. He shook his head and turned to leave.

Yang Kai hurriedly called out, "Brave warrior, wait."

"What do you want?" The barbarian sounded impatient.

Yang Kai said, "You’re badly injured. I happen to have some healing medicine here and am willing to give it to you for your wounds!" As he spoke, he pointed with his hand.

The barbarian’s eyes widened, glancing at the stone jars in front of him. He frowned, "You call these... medicine?"

"As genuine as it gets—guaranteed!"

The barbarian sneered, utterly disdainful.

The neighboring stall owner couldn’t bear to watch any longer, packing up to leave so as not to sully his eyes further.

"Trying it won’t hurt, it’s free anyway. If it works, great. If not... you can smash my stall." Yang Kai said with a cheerful smile.

"Is this really medicine?" The barbarian, seeing Yang Kai’s earnestness, grew curious.

"Top-grade healing medicine!"

"If it doesn’t work, I’ll really smash your stall!"

Though the man claimed to be a Shaman, the barbarian didn’t care. This was Frost Snow City, and they were people of the Frost Snow Tribe. If an outsider Shaman broke the rules and provoked them, they wouldn’t show mercy.

"What are you waiting for? Come try it!" Yang Kai called out energetically.

The barbarian nodded and squatted in front of Yang Kai. A foul stench hit him, making him frown and hesitate, unsure whether to try this suspicious stuff.

Yang Kai coaxed, "Good medicine tastes bitter. Mine may smell bad, but the effect is unquestionable."

"Let’s hope you’re right." The barbarian, looking resolute, scooped some paste from a stone jar and, under Yang Kai’s guidance, carefully smeared it on his abdominal wound.

The wound was over a foot long, flesh rolled back. Such an injury wasn’t too serious for a barbarian, but left untreated, it would be troublesome.

As the foul paste was applied, the barbarian’s brows shot up, his expression utterly surprised.

His companions watched curiously, eager to see if the medicine worked.

A cool sensation spread from his abdomen, easing the pain. The next moment, the barbarian exclaimed in surprise, feeling something, and quickly looked down, his eyes going wide.

He saw the flesh at his wound wriggling, and as it moved, new flesh buds grew. The rolled flesh began healing at a speed visible to the naked eye, scabbing over...

His companions noticed too, all exclaiming in disbelief at the wound’s transformation.

The neighboring stall owner, who had been about to leave, was now frozen in place, eyes wide, as if he wanted to pluck them out and stick them to the man’s belly for a closer look.

Their unusual reactions quickly attracted passing barbarians. Once they understood what was happening, they watched the wound’s change with curiosity.

Half an hour later, the barbarian who tried the medicine wiped his belly. The residue and blood scab fell off, revealing a scar over a foot long. Though not fully healed, the miraculous effect was enough to astonish everyone.

Just half an hour ago, his wound had been hideous.

"It really works!" The warrior who tried the medicine looked up, his gaze burning as he stared at Yang Kai, full of gratitude and reverence.

Yang Kai smiled without a word.

The medicine he refined was naturally effective, but only for these strong, blood-rich barbarians. If it were modern martial artists, the effect wouldn’t be nearly as good.

Because much of the medicine’s efficacy was in stimulating blood flow at the wound, speeding up self-healing. Only the Ancient Barbarian Tribe could achieve such immediate results with this healing medicine.

"Are you an apothecary?" the barbarian asked.

"You could say so."

Hearing this, the crowd immediately put away their disdain. No matter how weak Yang Kai looked, as an apothecary, he was worthy of respect.

Yang Kai looked up at the crowd surrounding him, smiling: "Brave warriors often fight. If you’re wounded in the wild, your combat power will suffer. But if you carry my healing medicine, you’ll have no worries. My medicine heals wounds instantly, making you as vigorous as dragons and tigers. This is my first time in Frost Snow City, so I’m offering it cheaply to make friends."

Before the barbarian tried the medicine, no one would have cared for Yang Kai’s words. But after witnessing the miracle, no one doubted its effectiveness.

The apothecary was right. Barbarians wounded in the wild faced trouble. Everyone knew some healing tricks, but how could they compare to real medicine? If they could carry some, they could heal anytime, anywhere, and the effect was extraordinary.

"How much?" someone asked immediately.

"Ten Qing Coins per portion!" Yang Kai had already planned, and quoted the price without hesitation.

Ten Qing Coins—neither high nor low. Any adult barbarian warrior could afford it, and it maximized Yang Kai’s own profit.

Sure enough, as soon as he finished, countless people shouted, "I want one!"

Looking at the crowd, Yang Kai grinned. This was all money.

Several jars of medicine sold out in less than the time it takes to burn a stick of incense. Those who bought were overjoyed; those who missed out beat their chests in regret.

Yang Kai packed up, smiling: "Today’s medicine is sold out. If you want to buy, come early tomorrow!"

As Yang Kai packed up his stall and left, the neighboring vendor gazed after him with envy, feeling that his departing figure seemed unfathomably profound...

Selling medicine once made Yang Kai’s assets multiply tenfold. As in previous days, he took all his Qing Coins, swept the stalls in Frost Snow City for supplies, then returned to his tree hole to refine more medicine.

The next day, when Yang Kai arrived at his stall, he found countless customers already gathered.

Some were those who had tried to buy yesterday but missed out, while others had simply heard the rumors and come out of curiosity.

A miraculous apothecary had appeared in Frost Snow City, whose medicine could heal wounds quickly. The news had spread throughout the city overnight, confirmed by many warriors of the Frost Snow Tribe.

People of this era were simple, without much scheming. The wild rumors couldn’t possibly be false.

So even though Yang Kai brought ten times more medicine than yesterday, it sold out in less than half a day. His assets, as expected, snowballed and grew ever larger.

For five days straight, the frenzy never abated.

This situation delighted Yang Kai. He quietly calculated his wealth, thinking that in half a month he could wrap up. By then, he’d have enough Qing Coins to buy enough Inner Cores. As long as he raised his Lower-Level Shaman Warrior realm to Shaman Master, the early hardships would be gone, and his future cultivation would be smooth.

One day, after selling medicine, Yang Kai walked the streets of Frost Snow City to buy raw materials. Suddenly, he frowned, sensing something wrong. He reached for his waist, and his face darkened.

The beast skin pouch he used for Qing Coins was gone.

That beast skin pouch had held all the Qing Coins Yang Kai earned from selling medicine today—over a thousand in total. 

Thankfully, the bulk of his fortune was safely stashed away in his tree hollow, so there was no need to worry about losing everything.

A thief? There are actually thieves in this era?

If not for the empty feeling in his hand, Yang Kai wouldn’t have believed it.

Since entering this secret realm, all the barbarians he’d met, good or bad, were simple and straightforward. Yang Kai never imagined someone would steal his money pouch here.

One careless moment, and he’d been caught off guard.

What a humiliating disgrace! A dignified Emperor Realm expert had his money pouch stolen without him noticing. This shame couldn’t be washed away even with all the water of the Three Rivers and Five Lakes.

Yang Kai’s expression hardened. His gaze swept the crowd, quickly locking onto a burly barbarian weaving through people. Barbarians dressed simply, regardless of gender, but this one was wrapped up tightly—a glaring flaw Yang Kai couldn’t miss.

With a snort, Yang Kai didn’t intend to alert anyone. He quietly followed the man.

The fellow was alert, weaving left and right through the crowd, never taking a normal path. After half an hour, he suddenly turned into a deserted, secluded area.
