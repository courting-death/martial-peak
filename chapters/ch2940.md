## Chapter 2940 — Probably Dead

The sound of explosive impacts echoed incessantly. In mid-air, Zhu Lie was tossed about like a punching bag by Yang Kai's relentless barrage, dragon blood splattering everywhere as he cut a thoroughly pathetic figure.

Yet no matter how fiercely he was beaten, Zhu Lie never once thought of yielding or begging for mercy. On the contrary, the harder Yang Kai hit him, the colder and fiercer his gaze became. It was as if, unless Yang Kai beat him to death, he would repay this pain a hundredfold.

The pride of Dragons was evident. Even though Yang Kai had transformed into a four-zhang-tall half-dragon form, in Zhu Lie’s eyes, he was no different from an ant.

With another loud crash, Zhu Lie was knocked from the sky and slammed heavily to the ground. Before he could bounce back up, Yang Kai had already stomped a foot down on his face.

“Do you submit?” Yang Kai looked down at him. “Say the word, and I’ll let you go right now!”

“I do not!” Zhu Lie gritted his teeth and growled, “What skill is there in relying on bloodline suppression? If you have guts, let’s both fight in human form!”

Yang Kai chuckled, “What kind of bullshit logic is that? Don’t you Dragons always judge superiority by bloodline?”

“You are not a Dragon!” Zhu Lie sneered.

"That's not right either. In human form, you're at the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm, while I'm only at the First Stage. If we fight like this, wouldn't I be at a massive disadvantage?" Yang Kai shook his head repeatedly.

“To deal with trash like you, would this Dragon need the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm? I will suppress my cultivation to the First Stage!” Zhu Lie wore a look of utter disdain.

“Oh?” Yang Kai raised an eyebrow. “You seem quite confident. Let’s be clear—if you lose, what then?”

“Lose to you? Impossible!” Zhu Lie wanted to shake his head, but with Yang Kai’s big foot pressing down, he couldn’t even manage that, only sneering contemptuously. “On equal footing, no one is a match for a Dragon.”

“That’s not certain.” Yang Kai snorted. “How about this: just as you said, we’ll both fight in human form, no bloodline suppression. But you must suppress your cultivation to the First Stage. If you lose, everything before is written off.”

“And if you lose?”

“Do as you please.”

“You mean it?” Zhu Lie’s eyes lit up.

“I mean it!”

“Good! If you lose, I want you to come with me to Dragon Isle, and kowtow three times. That’s a great humiliation for you humans, isn’t it?”

Yang Kai burst out laughing. “Not just three, even three hundred and thirty-three. It's up to you.” As he spoke, Yang Kai stepped back a few paces, releasing the subdued Zhu Lie.

Zhu Lie leaped to his feet, fury surging across his face as if he were ready to pounce and exact his revenge at any moment. Yet he forcibly suppressed the impulse in his heart, simply speaking coldly: “If you’re a man, don’t back down now. I hope you keep your word.”

Yang Kai snorted, “You’ll have to beat me first.”

As soon as he finished, he actively dispelled the Dragon Transformation Art, his four-zhang-tall body rapidly shrinking back to his original appearance.

At that moment, Zhu Lie charged forward, first spitting a mouthful of dragon flame. Though he hadn’t transformed into a giant dragon and couldn’t use most Dragon Clan secret arts, spitting a few blasts of dragon flame was no problem.

He truly kept his agreement with Yang Kai, suppressing his cultivation to the First Stage of the Emperor Realm. So this dragon flame was weaker than when he was in his dragon form.

Even so, such dragon flame was not something just anyone could withstand, containing extremely scorching power.

After the dragon flame, he punched at Yang Kai, his expression excited, as if he already saw Yang Kai being trampled by him. A mere First Stage Emperor was nothing to him. The only reason he’d been so thoroughly beaten by Yang Kai these days was because his bloodline was suppressed.

After Yang Kai used the Dragon Transformation Art, Zhu Lie couldn’t even bring out seventy percent of his strength—how could he be a match?

But now things were different. Both were in human form, with no bloodline suppression. He decided to give Yang Kai a good beating to vent his anger, then take him back to Dragon Isle to hand over to the Great Elder.

His fist followed the dragon flame, striking directly into it, and something unexpected happened.

Zhu Lie’s fist seemed to ignite, and instead of weakening the dragon flame, the two powers reinforced each other, their might surging.

“Ssshh…”

A flash of sword light—an enormous longsword slashed down on Zhu Lie’s fist, producing a harsh screech of metal on metal. The dragon flame wrapped around his fist was instantly sliced apart, growing faint, as if it might go out at any moment.

“What?” Zhu Lie was shocked, his expression changing.

These past days, Zhu Lie had been enduring Yang Kai's relentless assault, so he had gained some understanding of this human's strength. He knew Yang Kai was considerably more powerful than an ordinary First Stage Emperor Realm cultivator.

However, even though he hadn't expected this strike to accomplish much—it was merely a probe—he never thought the other party would parry it so effortlessly.

As he looked up, Zhu Lie’s face changed again.

Because he saw that Yang Kai showed no sign of strain, a faint mocking smile on his face, as if ridiculing him.

Zhu Lie was furious and roared, “Stop putting on an act!”

He threw another punch, fierce as thunder, looking as if he’d smash Yang Kai’s face to a pulp.

Yang Kai spun the Myriad Sword, lightly tapping forward, striking Zhu Lie’s second fist head-on.

An overwhelming force suddenly struck, and Zhu Lie actually felt pain. This unexpected change left him astonished.

He was a Dragon—his body was tough, every part comparable to an Imperial Treasure. Yet this human’s casual blow actually made him feel pain. How strong did one have to be to do that?

Was he the Dragon, or was Yang Kai?

In that moment of hesitation, Yang Kai had already slashed down with the Myriad Sword, his expression cold.

A chill ran from the top of Zhu Lie’s head, and his face changed dramatically as he hurriedly retreated.

The screech of friction rang out as the Myriad Sword sliced across Zhu Lie’s body, sparks flying. When he had retreated a hundred zhang and looked down, he saw a long white mark from his abdomen to his crotch—stark and glaring.

"You should be grateful you're of the Dragon Clan," Yang Kai's voice drifted forward like a specter. "Otherwise, you'd be dead already!" Zhu Lie looked up to find him standing five zhang away, the broad longsword gone from sight. With a clap and pull of his hands, a crescent-shaped blade of pure darkness, a zhang in length, coalesced smoothly and slashed directly toward him.

Zhu Lie tried to move, but found the space around him had become viscous, as if he’d fallen into a mire, unable to dodge the attack no matter what.

He growled, his aura surging, dragon scales appearing on his skin, preparing to take the blow head-on.

The pitch-black Moon Blade tore through the air, making Zhu Lie’s heart race with dread.

He truly didn’t know if he could withstand such an attack. Within it, he sensed clear fluctuations of the Laws of Space, condensed entirely from the power of the void. Wherever it passed, even space itself was devoured.

If that attack struck him, it would likely devour part of his body. For the first time in his life, Zhu Lie felt powerless. Even when suppressed by Yang Kai’s Dragon Transformation Art, he hadn’t felt this way.

Just as Zhu Lie’s eyes were about to split with rage, the Moon Blade shattered with a snap, only an inch from him.

The chill of a narrow escape washed over him. Cold sweat slid down his forehead as Zhu Lie looked up at Yang Kai, who was smiling at him. “You lost!”

Zhu Lie kept a sullen silence.

He had indeed lost, but not to that Moon Blade-like attack—he had lost to himself. In the instant before the crisis struck, he had raised his strength to the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm and even revealed some Dragon Clan traits, breaking his agreement with Yang Kai.

In the instant before the crisis struck, he had raised his strength to the Third Stage of the Emperor Realm and even revealed some Dragon Clan traits, breaking his agreement with Yang Kai.

He hadn't even managed to gauge Yang Kai's true strength without the Dragon Transformation Art before losing in such a muddled fashion.

An indescribable sense of shame and defeat washed over him, leaving him utterly dejected. His lips moved a few times, but he couldn’t say a word.

Yet it was only now that Zhu Lie realized that at some point, others had already risen above the Dragons themselves.

“You’re not thinking of going back on your word, are you?” Yang Kai glanced sidelong at Zhu Lie. “We agreed—if you lost, our grudge is settled. Or can the Dragon Clan not afford to lose?”

These words stung Zhu Lie, and his pride made him respond, “Who says I can’t afford to lose! Lost is lost. As you said, our grudge is settled.”

Yang Kai immediately smiled. “Good, good. Since that’s settled, you can go back to where you came from. Oh, and pass a message to Dragon Isle for me: I’ll visit when I have time, so stop sending people over every few days.”

Zhu Lie said in a deep voice, “I’ll pass your words to Dragon Isle. What the island decides, I don’t know.”

Yang Kai nodded, “No problem. Also, tell Zhu Qing for me, you two should be able to contact each other, right? Tell her not to come looking for me again—it’s dangerous.”

Every time he was with Zhu Qing, Yang Kai couldn’t control the agitation in his bloodline. The thing hidden deep in his heart seemed ready to erupt at any moment, making him feel uneasy and in danger. Yang Kai knew this was dragon nature, and as he cultivated the Dragon Transformation Art further, it would only grow stronger. One day, he wouldn’t be able to suppress it.

So he felt Zhu Qing should stay as far away from him as possible—best if they never met again.

“Zhu Qing is probably dead. I can’t pass on your message.” Zhu Lie slowly shook his head.

Yang Kai's smile gradually faded from his face. "What do you mean by 'probably dead'?"
