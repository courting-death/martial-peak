## Chapter 2850 — Demonization

Not far away, a warrior had already been demonized by Demonic Qi. The weapon in his hand hacked mercilessly at a fellow tribesman who had once been as close as a brother, severing his head without the slightest hesitation.

Could it be... that just as the expedition began, Shaman Niu's tribe was about to be completely wiped out here?

As the screams rang out, all the members of the Barbarian Tribe were shocked. No one knew what was happening around them, so they could only huddle together in groups of three or five, standing back to back and watching their surroundings warily.

The demonized tribesman, after killing one companion, did not pause at all. He raised his weapon again and swung it at another person.

Yang Kai flicked his hand, and a chain of Shamanic Art shot out, binding the tribesman tightly so he could not move.

More screams echoed, one after another, clearly showing that many had fallen victim at the hands of once-trusted companions.

Yang Kai's Divine Sense enveloped the entire village. Chains of Shamanic Art flew from his hands, binding every demonized tribesman.

After this brief delay, Yang Kai suddenly realized the situation was much better than he had expected.

Of the three thousand members of Shaman Niu's tribe, only about a hundred had been demonized. The rest of the warriors remained clear-headed, showing little reaction.

However, the ever-present Demonic Qi continued to erode their bodies. If it was not dispersed quickly, total annihilation was only a matter of time.

With this thought, Yang Kai dared not delay any longer. He continuously cast large-scale purification spells, and with each casting, the surrounding Demonic Qi faded a little more.

Aside from him, the remaining shamans were also unleashing their shamanic power and Shamanic Arts without restraint.

The Shamanic Arts cast by the shamans truly seemed to be the nemesis of this Demonic Qi. In less than ten breaths, the darkness shrouding the world was swept away, and light returned.

Only now did the tribesmen finally see the situation in the field clearly.

Dozens, perhaps even over a hundred companions, lay dead in pools of blood. Many others were bound in place by chains of Shamanic Art, struggling constantly. The eyes of these companions were all a terrifying red-black, as if they had lost their reason, and they let out meaningless low growls.

This scene left everyone horrified.

Elsewhere, dozens of strange beings that had emerged from underground finally took full form, turning into sand demons formed from mud and sand.

The sand demons all shuddered, and overwhelming Demonic Qi surged from their bodies, howling as they moved swiftly to attack the surrounding Barbarian Tribe members.

No one knew what was so special about these sand demons, but since they dared to attack, they were enemies.

The Barbarian Tribe warriors roared, raising their weapons high and launching a fearless charge.

The cry of an eagle sounded as the Sky Cavalry soared into the sky, circling above the village. Arrow after arrow shot down with precision. Meanwhile, led by Yu and Lu, more than a dozen shamans chanted incantations, and Shamanic Arts blossomed with dazzling light.

The battle began suddenly and ended just as quickly.

Although the sand demons managed to launch a surprise attack under the cover of Demonic Qi, apart from causing some initial confusion and casualties among Shaman Niu's tribe, the ensuing direct confrontation was a complete rout.

Not another person died; only a dozen or so were lightly injured, and the dozens of sand demons were all slain.

After the fierce battle, everyone looked at each other, gazing at the dead and demonized companions around them, feeling no sense of victory at all.

This was the first battle Shaman Niu's tribe had faced since leaving the royal city, and the opponents were only a few dozen sand demons. Yet even in such a fight, the losses were hard to accept.

All the Barbarian Tribe members were deeply ashamed.

"Ah Hu, come here!" Yang Kai called out solemnly.

Ah Hu responded and stepped forward to stand before Yang Kai, saying in a deep voice, "What are your orders, my lord?"

Although they had both come from the same village and were once close friends, now that Yang Kai was the commander, Ah Hu could no longer address him casually in front of others. A commander must have the dignity of a commander.

"Do you feel anything strange?" Yang Kai asked as he placed his hand on Ah Hu's wrist, shamanic power surging as he carefully examined his body.

Ah Hu was silent for a moment, then said, "I feel terrible."

Yang Kai was startled. "Where does it hurt?"

Ah Hu turned his head to the side and said, "If I had been more careful just now, maybe fewer people would have died."

Yang Kai breathed a sigh of relief and patted his shoulder. "There's no need to blame yourself. Everyone bears responsibility for what happened this time."

Yang Kai gestured for him to step back, his expression full of confusion. In his examination, Ah Hu showed no abnormalities at all—there was no trace of Demonic Qi.

Yet just now, Demonic Qi had enveloped the entire village. All three thousand people should have been affected.

Unable to believe it, he checked several more tribesmen and found the same result as with Ah Hu. Each one was perfectly fine, which made Yang Kai frown.

It was a good thing that the tribesmen had not been tainted by Demonic Qi, but he could not understand why.

If all the tribesmen were like this, it would be one thing, but there were still over a hundred who had been turned into demons.

With this in mind, he approached one of the restrained demonized tribesmen. The man was clearly delirious, growling lowly with red-black eyes. Yu stood nearby, continuously casting purification and calming spells, but with little effect.

More than a dozen other shamans were busy with other demonized tribesmen, casting various Shamanic Arts, but none produced any meaningful results.

"My lord, we can't save them," Lu said as she walked over, a rare trace of sorrow in her usually indifferent eyes.

Yang Kai did not reply. Sweeping his Divine Sense over each demonized tribesman, he quickly discovered a common trait.

Compared to the others, these tribesmen were all rather weak.

Although every member of the Barbarian Tribe was strong, there were still differences in strength. Those who had been demonized were basically the weakest among the three thousand. Clearly, their demonization was related to their own strength.

Barbarian Tribe warriors did not cultivate Shamanic Arts, only their bodies. The stronger the body, the more vigorous the Qi and blood; the weaker the body, the less powerful the Qi and blood.

"Qi and blood..." Yang Kai muttered, and a sudden thought flashed through his mind, making his eyes light up.

He walked straight to a demonized tribesman and reached out to tap the man's forehead.

Everyone watched nervously, not knowing what Yang Kai intended to do.

Soon, they saw a crimson aura of Qi and blood erupt from the demonized tribesman after Yang Kai's touch, his body suddenly growing taller and stronger.

Bloodlust Art!

Yu and Lu's eyelids twitched, and they exchanged shocked glances.

They had not heard Yang Kai chant any incantation, yet the Bloodlust Art had been cast. In other words, Shaman Niu's mastery of the Bloodlust Art had reached the level where the art was born from the heart.

This was not a realm just anyone could reach. Only a few who had achieved the highest understanding of a Shamanic Art could do so.

Even they, who came from the Shaman God Hall, could not achieve this.

Shocked as they were, they wondered why he would use Bloodlust Art now. The tribesman was already demonized and hard to control; if Bloodlust Art was cast on him, wouldn't things get worse...

Before they could finish the thought, Yu and Lu stared in astonishment at the tribesman who had received the Bloodlust Art.

After the Bloodlust Art took effect, a layer of crimson appeared in the man's red-black eyes. Though not obvious, it was striking against the black.

The tribesman, who had been growling and struggling, suddenly froze, stopping all movement.

Next, a faint, painful moan escaped his throat, and a glimmer of reason returned to his once lifeless eyes.

He looked around, finally fixing his gaze on Yang Kai, and called out with great difficulty, "Shaman Niu... my... lord..."

Yang Kai's expression was solemn as he said, "You are a child of the Barbarian God, a warrior of the Barbarian Tribe. You possess a strong body and a resolute heart. I believe you can overcome the evil within and find yourself again. Your brothers are waiting for you."

All eyes turned to him.

As if encouraged, the demonized tribesman let out a low roar. The crimson light on his body flared, and the red in his eyes burned like fire. The blackness in his eyes melted away like snow under the sun.

In the next moment, a jet-black light suddenly flowed from the man's seven orifices—the Demonic Qi that had invaded his body was forced out.

Yang Kai raised his hand and cast a purification spell, cleansing the Demonic Qi completely.

Once the Demonic Qi left his body, the tribesman immediately collapsed, forcing a weak smile before fainting.

Several familiar tribesmen rushed over to check on him and quickly reported, "He's not dead, just passed out."

Yang Kai nodded. "He'll be fine once he wakes up."

Examining him with his Divine Sense, Yang Kai found that the Demonic Qi within the tribesman had completely vanished—in other words, the hidden danger had been completely eliminated.

"My lord, can Bloodlust Art also restrain Demonic Qi?" Yu asked curiously.

While directing the tribesmen to bring over the other demonized companions, Yang Kai casually explained, "I'm not entirely sure about the specifics, but I don't think it's the Bloodlust Art itself that restrains Demonic Qi. Rather, it's the power of Qi and blood in our Barbarian Tribe warriors that resists the invasion of Demonic Qi. The stronger the Qi and blood, the better the resistance."
