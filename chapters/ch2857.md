## Chapter 2857 — Surrender

One assassin failed to dodge in time and was struck by the serpent-like lightning. In an instant, his hair stood on end, and the smell of burning filled the air. The lightning exploded from his body, transforming into a net of electricity that blanketed the entire sky.

One by one, the demon assassins' bodies went numb, trembling uncontrollably. The Sky Cavalry soldiers chasing them didn't even look back, simply nocking arrows and firing in reverse. The powerful projectiles pierced through the demon assassins' chests and burst out from their backs, leaving gaping wounds in their wake.

A single streak of dazzling lightning revealed the whereabouts of all the demon assassins, yet not a single Sky Cavalry soldier was harmed. The twins, Yu and Lu, demonstrated their exquisite mastery of Shamanic Arts.

With no more worries at their backs, the Sky Cavalry once again unleashed astonishing combat power, providing strong and steady support to their companions below.

Shaya sent out three waves of assassins in succession, yet failed to kill a single Sky Cavalry soldier. Instead, she suffered considerable losses in manpower, which was enough to drive her mad with rage.

On the battlefield, the Demons and the Barbarian Tribe had already clashed head-on. The strategy of using the demonized warriors as the vanguard with the Red Demons covering the rear had failed to produce any effect; instead, the Barbarian Tribe surged forward, driving deep into their lines. Left with no other choice, the demons could only resist with all their might.

The Sand Demons, relying on their ability to move through the earth, continuously stirred up chaos on the battlefield. This did have some effect, causing unavoidable casualties among Shaman Niu's tribe.

However, with the Chain of Life in effect, the vitality of all members of the ten Barbarian Tribe squads was shared. Even if someone suffered what should have been a fatal wound, they wouldn’t die immediately. Instead, the injury would be shared, and other tribesmen would replenish their life force.

By this point in the battle, hundreds of demons had been killed or wounded, while not a single member of the Barbarian Tribe had fallen.

The Chain of Life truly lived up to its reputation as the most important Shamanic Art in large-scale Barbarian Tribe battles. With such a technique linking them, the people of Shaman Niu's tribe would dare to challenge even a giant dragon!

Shaya was starting to lose her composure.

She paid a heavy price for her rash decision.

At the start of the battle, she thought that with the Red Demons’ surprise attack, victory would be quickly decided. But the Red Demons she sent out failed to achieve anything at all.

The Shadow Demons sent out next were also slaughtered by the enemy and utterly defeated.

Although the two armies were similar in number, their true fighting strength was worlds apart.

Shaya had no choice but to deploy her strongest unit.

A bestial roar echoed across the battlefield, and the earth trembled as if a thousand armies were stampeding forward. The demon cavalry emerged from their lines—towering figures wreathed in black miasma, mounted upon grotesque Demonic Beasts. They charged across the battlefield with reckless abandon, and not a single member of the Barbarian Tribe could withstand their onslaught. Everywhere they passed, warriors and mounts were sent tumbling. The demons' losing position began to reverse, bit by bit.

The remaining demons, led by this cavalry force, surged forward in a counterattack against the Barbarian Tribe, gradually driving the battle line forward.

The arrows fired by the Sky Cavalry had little effect. Each Demonic Beast had thick skin and flesh; the arrows merely tickled them.

“It’s our turn!” Yu and Lu said in unison, their expressions solemn.

The dozen or so shamans of varying ranks stood tall. The older ones were calm, but the younger shamans were clearly nervous. It was their first real battle, and seeing people die again and again was a tremendous shock.

To suppress the momentum of the demon cavalry, they were now the only hope.

The guards escorted the shamans toward the battlefield. Shaya’s gaze turned in their direction, her brows furrowing slightly. She didn’t know what the Barbarian Tribe was up to, but a sense of unease lingered in her heart.

“You’re finished.” A voice suddenly sounded not far away.

Shaya was startled. She turned her head and immediately saw a green-skinned fellow inexplicably appear at her side.

Shaya nearly jumped in shock. With her strength, she hadn’t even noticed how he appeared. If he had attacked just now, could she have stopped him?

Maybe… perhaps…

But… with the two armies locked in battle, why had this guy left his own rear lines to come here?

As if realizing something, Shaya pursed her lips and smiled. “Have you decided to embrace the Great Demon God?”

Yang Kai glanced at her and grinned. “I’m still considering it. Of course, if you could help me remove the green poison, I’d be even more sincere.”

Shaya sneered. “Don’t delude yourself. The Green Demon Poison can’t be cured—unless… you become my slave.”

Yang Kai frowned. “Is that the only way?”

Shaya replied, “It’s the only one!”

Yang Kai nodded.

Shaya smiled seductively. “These past two days haven’t been easy, have they? Honestly, I’m surprised you’ve managed to stay clear-headed for two days. But the longer you hold out, the more painful it will be.”

Yang Kai sighed. “Right now, I’m already in agony.”

Shaya tilted her head. “Doesn’t look like it.”

Yang Kai grinned. “A man always has to act tough, doesn’t he?”

Shaya laughed. “You really are qualified to be my slave. I can promise you that after your transformation, you’ll retain your own mind. There won’t be any difference from before.”

Yang Kai raised an eyebrow. “I’ll still have my mind? Then why did my tribesmen who were demonized…”

Shaya curled her lip in disdain. “They’re nothing—just a bunch of trash. Naturally, I wouldn’t bother with them. But you’re different. A strong expert like you is better off with a clear mind. I’m willing to pay a price for that.”

“You’re so good to me, I really don’t know how to repay you.” Yang Kai smiled gently.

Shaya giggled. “Just be obedient.”

“So, shall we start now?” Yang Kai turned to look at her.

Shaya’s smile faded as she looked toward the battlefield. The demons were suddenly at a disadvantage again. After the shamans took action, powerful Shamanic Arts were unleashed, forcing the demon cavalry to retreat step by step. The advantage they’d gained vanished. If not for the Sand Demons constantly causing trouble and restraining the Barbarian Tribe, the cavalry would have been wiped out by now.

Even so, the demons’ situation was grim.

By now, more than half their forces had been lost, and the casualties kept mounting. Meanwhile, the Barbarian Tribe only grew braver, their morale soaring.

“To make you my slave, I have to pay a price. What can you offer me?” Shaya suddenly turned to Yang Kai.

“What do you need?” Yang Kai asked.

Shaya raised her hand and pointed ahead. “I want to see your sincerity. Only with enough sincerity can I trust you. Decide for yourself—you don’t have much time.”

Yang Kai chuckled. “That’s easy.”

As soon as he finished speaking, he charged into the battlefield. Anyone who tried to block him was killed with a single punch. Not a single powerful demon could withstand his advance.

Rather than displeasing Shaya, this bloody slaughter made her watch with even greater interest. Her gaze on Yang Kai sparkled with a strange light.

The demons clearly knew he was not to be trifled with and quickly retreated.

In no time, Yang Kai reached the front lines where the two armies clashed.

“Sir, why did you…” Yu suddenly noticed Yang Kai and asked in confusion, “Why did you come from that side?”

Their own camp was far in the rear, but Yang Kai had clearly come from the demons’ side. As the commander, he should have been directing from the rear, yet he’d inexplicably appeared on the front lines. It really wasn’t a wise move.

Just as she was about to speak, she suddenly felt a sense of unease.

Lu felt it too, looking up at Yang Kai warily. “Sir, what are you doing?”

Yang Kai smiled at the twins. “Come with me for a moment.”

The twins fell silent, then slowly shook their heads.

The dozen or so shamans were also confused by the situation, exchanging bewildered glances.

"Disobedience is unacceptable. Have you forgotten what Shaman Dang said when he entrusted you to my care?" Yang Kai's gaze turned cold as he fixed the twins with a stern look, his presence radiating unmistakable authority.

Yu said gravely, “If you truly serve the Barbarian Tribe, we would never disobey. But now… don’t you intend to defect to the demons?”

“What?” The shamans were all shocked, unable to believe what they were hearing.

They all looked up at Yang Kai, hoping he would deny it. But to their dismay, Yang Kai just smiled. “You’re quite perceptive.”

Yu said, “The Green Demon Poison can only be removed by becoming a demon. Sir… you’ve really let us down.”

In just a few words, the shamans instantly understood what was happening.

Shaman Niu had been poisoned by the Green Demon Poison and clearly didn’t want to die, so he had to defect to the demons. It seemed the twins had already seen through his intentions.

What… what should they do?

This was truly the first real battle for Shaman Niu's tribe. All twelve teams were united, and they were on the verge of a great victory. But at this critical moment, their leader was about to defect.

The sudden turn of events left everyone unable to process what was happening, their minds blank.

“You’re very clever. No wonder you made it into the Shaman God Hall. But even if you know, what can you do?” Yang Kai chuckled, reaching out a hand toward the twins. As his hand descended, it was like a curtain blocking out the sky, plunging everyone into darkness.

The twins’ faces changed dramatically. They had always known Shaman Niu was strong, but they never imagined he was this powerful.

Before they could even react, their bodies went rigid and they couldn’t move at all. No one knew what technique Yang Kai had used.
