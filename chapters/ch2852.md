## Chapter 2852 — Deep into the Demon Lair

"My lord, where are we going?" Yu asked curiously, looking at Yang Kai.

Ever since they left that village three days ago, the three-thousand-strong group had been marching in a strange manner, veering left and right, sometimes south, sometimes north, with no discernible pattern. It seemed as if Yang Kai was simply giving orders for their direction on a whim.

By rights, Yang Kai was the leader of this group, so naturally everyone had to obey his commands.

But this aimless and bizarre marching always left people confused.

After enduring it for several days, Yu finally couldn't help but ask what was going on.

"Pass the order, turn southeast, full speed ahead!" Yang Kai shouted, then glanced lazily at Yu and grinned, "Where else would we go? Of course, to take revenge!"

Revenge? Yu and Lu, who stood together, were both taken aback.

Who were they avenging? Where were they going for revenge?

These past few days, the group hadn't discovered any useful information, and apart from that one ambush in the village, things had been peaceful for three days, making people wonder if the demons had all retreated. Now that Yang Kai suddenly mentioned revenge, it was only natural for everyone to be puzzled.

But Yang Kai had no intention of explaining. In his mind, a faint connection was guiding his direction. He gazed that way, eyes narrowing, a cold light flashing within.

There was still a Sand Demon hidden in that village—Yang Kai had known this at the time.

However, Yang Kai hadn’t killed the Sand Demon right away. Instead, he quietly split off a wisp of his Divine Sense and attached it to the Sand Demon. Not long after his own group left the village, he sensed the Sand Demon also departing in a certain direction. Half a day later, it stopped somewhere, and for the past few days, it had remained in that same spot. Clearly, that place was a demon lair.

That cave was his target!

The outcome of their first battle had been far from ideal. Facing just a few dozen Sand Demons, their force of three thousand had still lost over a hundred warriors. These past days, every Barbarian Tribe fighter had been simmering with rage, longing to encounter more demonic foes and unleash their fury in a true battle, to avenge their fallen comrades.

That demon cave was the perfect target for their rage. And Shaman Niu's tribe also needed a real victory to boost morale.

These days of marching were not aimless. If viewed from high above, one would see that Shaman Niu's tribe had circled the demon cave within a three-hundred-zhang radius, conducting a sweep.

The situation was somewhat better than Yang Kai had anticipated. There were no other demons in the vicinity, and within a radius of a thousand li, it seemed only that single demon cave existed. This meant that once Shaman Niu's tribe engaged the cave in battle, they wouldn't need to worry about being ambushed from behind.

The only remaining question was how many demons were inside the cave, and what kind of experts were stationed there.

Half a day later, the group reached a mountain hollow. Yang Kai raised his hand slightly, and the three-thousand-strong group came to an abrupt halt.

He ordered the tribesmen to rest in place and eat to recover their strength. Then Yang Kai summoned a dozen or so shamans for a secret discussion. When they learned there was a demon cave a hundred zhang ahead, everyone was shocked, almost unable to believe it.

These past days, they hadn't seen Yang Kai do anything unusual, nor had he left the main group. So where had he gotten this information?

When the dozen or so shamans learned that Yang Kai intended to scout the demon cave himself, worry flickered across their faces. Yu and Lu, in particular, stepped forward eagerly, volunteering to take on the task themselves. As Shaman Masters of the Southern Barbarian Tribe who had cultivated rigorously in the Shaman God Hall for years, they had gained some mastery of concealment and stealth arts. This sort of intelligence-gathering work didn't demand exceptional strength, but if their movements were exposed, the consequences would surely be dire.

Yang Kai naturally refused the sisters' good intentions.

After giving a few more instructions to the shamans, Yang Kai's figure suddenly flickered and vanished from sight. Die was used to this trick, but the other shamans all gaped, looking around for Yang Kai, but he was nowhere to be found.

Moments later, inside the demon cave a hundred zhang away, Yang Kai drifted in like a ghost. The demons guarding the entrance might as well have been decorations.

This was a mountain cavern, its interior seemingly hollowed out, vast in space, with winding passages that were easy to defend and hard to attack.

Yang Kai held his breath, hiding his form as he scouted for information.

Not knowing if any experts were stationed here, Yang Kai didn't dare to release his Divine Sense recklessly and could only investigate bit by bit.

There were indeed many demons inside the mountain cave, and they didn't all seem to be of the same race. Some were huge, even more imposing than the strongest Barbarian Tribe warriors, while others were only about three chi tall, like tiny dolls. Without exception, all of them exuded a demonic qi that Yang Kai found extremely repulsive. The varying intensity of their demonic qi revealed their differing strengths.

This was his first time dealing with demons at such close range, and he had no idea what abilities they possessed. Yang Kai dared not let his guard down in the slightest.

As he moved through the mountain cave, Yang Kai soon discovered something else that gave him a headache.

There were actually quite a few barbarian tribesmen who had been demonized here.

These barbarian tribesmen were clearly from the four tribes that had been wiped out. The intelligence said those four tribes had been completely destroyed with no survivors, but now it seemed that wasn't the case. Some of the tribesmen had simply been turned into demons.

It was clear that these demonized barbarians held a much lower status than the true demons. Any demon could order them around, and the demonized barbarians showed no resentment, instead acting extremely cautious and submissive.

They were like slaves.

Not only had ordinary tribesmen been demonized, but Yang Kai even saw a few shamans among them. The strongest was clearly a Shaman Master! Yet now, this Shaman Master, who was once so noble among the Barbarian Tribe, was being ordered about by a short demon, enduring beatings and scoldings without a word of complaint.

Shaking his head slightly, Yang Kai continued deeper inside.

Not long after, a beast-like roar suddenly rang out, filled with an indescribable ferocity.

Yang Kai frowned and followed the sound, soon arriving at a massive cavern.

In the darkness, pairs of crimson eyes suddenly lit up, as if sensing something, all turning to stare at Yang Kai.

Yang Kai's expression hardened, and he quickly used a Nothingness secret art, banishing himself into the void.

The pairs of crimson eyes stared for a while, then slowly withdrew.

Yang Kai saw clearly that the owners of those pairs of crimson eyes were enormous beasts wrapped in Demonic Qi. Could it be that the Demon Race also had Yao Beasts? Wouldn’t that make them Demonic Beasts? He just didn’t know what the difference was between these Demonic Beasts and Yao Beasts.

After observing the Demonic Beasts for a while, Yang Kai released his Divine Sense, and after confirming no one was around, he suddenly appeared beside one of the Demonic Beasts, grabbed it, and enveloped it completely with his Divine Sense, collecting it into the Small Xuan Realm.

This action clearly startled the other Demonic Beasts. In an instant, over a hundred of them rose up in the cavern, roaring incessantly.

The sound of hurried footsteps approached from afar—clearly, some demons had noticed the commotion and were coming to investigate.

But Yang Kai's figure had already flickered away.

By the time the demons arrived, Yang Kai was long gone.

At this moment, Yang Kai felt a bit awkward.

Just now, a teleportation had accidentally brought him to a very unusual place. It was extremely spacious, with strange rocks scattered about, giving the stone cave a hazy, misty feel.

From a room ahead came a series of breathy, seductive moans that made one's face flush and heart race.

The sounds were melodious and low, extremely pleasant to the ear. Hearing them was like having an invisible hand pluck at one's heartstrings, making them itch uncontrollably.

Yang Kai's expression turned peculiar as he hid within the shadows of darkness. Even without using his brain, he could guess what was happening over there.

He quietly swept the area with his Divine Sense, and sure enough, it was just as he had guessed.

In that stone cave, on a stone bed, a sturdy, muscular barbarian man lay completely naked, while a shapely, curvaceous woman sat astride him. Her delicate, fair body rose and fell, her head thrown back, her skin glistening with sweat, and her breath coming in gasps.

The sight of so much bare skin made Yang Kai dizzy and left his mouth dry.

The barbarian lying motionless on the stone bed was clearly experiencing immense pleasure, his soul seemingly leaving his body. The contrast between his strong physique and the woman's delicate form created a striking sight that could make any man lose control.

Wretch! Yang Kai cursed inwardly, carefully withdrawing his Divine Sense.

Even without a thorough investigation, he could sense that this woman was not to be trifled with. She likely had the strength of a Shaman King, and an Upper-Level one at that. She was definitely the strongest expert he had encountered in this demon cave, and perhaps its leader.

The woman's demonic nature was obvious—not to mention the faint demonic qi within her delicate body, the short tail behind her alone marked her as a member of another race.

Fortunately, the woman was lost in her pleasure, or his earlier actions would surely have alerted her.

Someone on par with an Upper-Level Shaman King was not someone Yang Kai truly feared, but since he was here only to gather intelligence, it would be unwise to alert the enemy.

Lying in wait, Yang Kai prepared to find an opportunity to leave.

On the stone bed, the woman's movements grew more intense, her hair flying, her moans rising ever higher.

Just as she was about to reach a climax and Yang Kai was preparing to leave, a sudden change occurred—a black shadow appeared behind him, and a dagger slashed swiftly at his neck.

The dagger was pitch black, without the slightest reflection, mysterious and traceless. The shadow had given no warning before acting, and since Yang Kai was in the enemy's lair, he hadn't kept his Divine Sense on constant alert.

Caught off guard, he was actually struck.
