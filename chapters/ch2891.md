## Chapter 2891 — Reunion with the Divine Tree

Bao Qi and Shaya could not possibly stop those dozen or so Demon Kings; everyone knew this, including the Demon Kings themselves and Yang Kai.

Although they were the first Demon Kings to submit to Yang Kai, their allegiance was not of their own volition, and their so-called loyalty was highly questionable. Yang Kai had never expected them to truly surrender to him or obey his every command; it was enough for him to control their lives and deaths.

Facing many with few, the odds were grim, but if they disobeyed Yang Kai’s orders, they would likely die on the spot.

So Bao Qi and Shaya hesitated only briefly before moving to intercept the Demon Kings.

Fortunately, these Demon Kings seemed extremely wary of Yang Kai’s strength and deliberately allowed him to pass through, not immediately attacking Bao Qi and Shaya, but instead pursuing and entangling them to buy time.

This made Bao Qi and Shaya overjoyed, and they unleashed their full power, charging left and right through the encirclement of Demon Kings.

The hundred-thousand-strong Shaman Niu’s tribe thundered forward.

It was an arduous advance; the further they went, the greater the resistance. The Demon Kings they encountered dared not get too close, but trailed from afar, constantly harassing Shaman Niu’s tribe with their techniques.

Tribe members kept falling behind, like snowflakes landing on the ground and quickly melting away.

Even with Demon Kings like Mo Ke Tuo and Fei Li protecting the ranks, the situation could not be changed.

Yang Kai glanced toward the distant location of the boundary passage, then raised his hand and summoned the Slave-Insect Bracelet, releasing the Soul-Devouring Worms harbored within. Over the past two years of war, the Soul-Devouring Worms had grown considerably, and Shaman Niu's tribe had relied on these peculiar insects many times to achieve brilliant victories.

The Soul-Devouring Worms were not very effective against those above the Demon King level, but against demons below that, their effect was immediate.

Swarms of insect clouds flew out, buzzing loudly. Wherever the clouds passed, the demons screamed in agony and fell dead. The Soul-Devouring Worms devoured almost everything—Demonic Qi, Divine Soul—all were their food.

Though seemingly weak, these insects were extremely tenacious; even the sharpest weapons left only a white mark when striking them.

With the insect clouds released, the surrounding demons retreated, greatly reducing the losses of Shaman Niu’s tribe.

Thunderous sounds continued to roll across the sky—Saints were locked in a life-and-death struggle. The sky changed color, the winds and clouds churned, and the Saints’ actions had altered the very laws within a ten-thousand-li radius.

Shaman Niu’s tribe pressed on, paying a heavy price with every step forward.

One hundred thousand dwindled to ninety thousand, eighty thousand, fifty thousand…

An hour later, when Shaman Niu’s tribe reached the area directly beneath the boundary passage, Yang Kai looked back—of those who had followed him here, fewer than thirty thousand remained.

This was now the heart of the demon army; not a single ally could be seen. Shaman Niu’s tribe was truly fighting alone.

Of the ten Demon Kings, only Mo Ke Tuo and Fei Li remained; the other eight had been sent out by Yang Kai along the way. In his perception, half of those eight were already dead, and the rest were not faring well.

“Defend!” Yang Kai shouted, raising his sword.

The thirty thousand tribe members immediately formed up, encircling Yang Kai at the center in a tight ring, defending with all their might.

The insect clouds gathered at the outermost edge of the formation. Having previously demonstrated terrifying lethality, they now served as a powerful deterrent to the demons.

Though their numbers had decreased, the formation of Shaman Niu’s tribe became tighter and their defense more effective.

Yang Kai flipped his hand and took out the seven-colored clay, glancing up at the sky. Directly above him was the location of the boundary passage, a rift that had remained open for years. Yang Kai could even feel the surging, violent Demonic Qi pouring from it—a chilling, oppressive presence.

“Let’s hope this works,” he muttered, tossing the seven-colored clay to the ground and staring at it intently.

This action immediately drew the attention of the surrounding Demon Kings. Though they didn’t know what he was doing, they instinctively sensed unease. Shaman Niu’s tribe had charged all the way here with a clear goal: to do something with the boundary passage.

How could the Demon Kings just stand by and watch?

Orders were issued, and the demons encircling Shaman Niu’s tribe finally launched their assault.

The insect clouds surged, devouring swathes of demons, but still could not stem the iron tide of war.

The thirty thousand members of Shaman Niu’s tribe held their ground. The Shaman Warriors chanted incantations, bestowing new shamanic blessings on all their kin.

The bloody battle erupted in an instant.

Mo Ke Tuo’s figure suddenly vanished. Only Yang Kai could sense her position; even the strongest Demon Kings could not track her. She flitted through the demon ranks, and with every strike, someone died by her hand.

She was the most powerful assassin among the demons, a Demon King-level assassin. Apart from those on her level, no one could survive her attacks.

One by one, the Demon Kings commanding from the rear fell to Mo Ke Tuo’s assassinations, throwing the demon ranks into chaos.

Fei Li also joined the fray. The ghostly flames he wielded with his skeletal hands were so fearsome that even Demon Kings dared not touch them. Wherever the flames swept, groups of demons were incinerated.

Yang Kai’s gaze remained fixed on the seven-colored clay, then he suddenly turned and shouted to Die, “Why isn’t it working? Does it need to be buried underground?”

Die had said this was the key to victory, the chance to seal the boundary passage, but Yang Kai saw no reaction from the clay at all.

Die looked at him and said calmly, “It will work.”

“When?” Yang Kai pressed.

Under his command, the hundred-thousand-strong Shaman Niu’s tribe had charged into the heart of the demon army, losing seventy percent to get here. If the seven-colored clay was a trick, or just a ruse by the Shaman Saints to distract the demons, the price paid would be far too great.

Receiving no answer from Die, Yang Kai suppressed his anger, raised his hand, and sent out several Moon Blades, instantly carving a path through the demon army.

He turned his attention away from the seven-colored clay. Whether or not it could seal the boundary passage, all he wanted now was to fight to his heart’s content.

As the leader of Shaman Niu’s tribe, his targets were not the demon rabble, so he set his sights on the Demon Kings lurking nearby.

Yang Kai’s figure flickered across the battlefield. Wherever the Myriad Sword swept, demons were sent flying, and every Demon King he targeted died by his blade—none escaped.

He was like the god of death, and the Myriad Sword was his soul-reaping weapon.

The deaths of the Demon Kings threw the demon ranks into further chaos, greatly easing the pressure on Shaman Niu’s tribe. Their defensive circle even began to expand, but this was only an illusion.

Demons gathered from all directions, countless in number. The thirty thousand members of Shaman Niu’s tribe could not possibly win in such an environment. Even if Yang Kai was unstoppable, in the end, not one of the thirty thousand would survive.

Just then, a strange sound rang out, like the heartbeat of an ancient being awakening. The sound struck directly at the heart, making all who heard it feel a tightness in their chest and their qi and blood surge.

Countless eyes turned toward the source of the sound.

Yang Kai glanced over, and a look of joy quickly appeared on his face.

The seven-colored clay he had thrown to the ground finally reacted, radiating multicolored light, with a surging vitality hidden within.

This strange scene astonished both sides.

The rainbow light slowly faded, replaced by a soft emerald glow.

A tiny green sprout suddenly grew from the ground, so delicate it seemed it would break at a touch. Yang Kai flashed to its side, feeling the aura grow more and more familiar.

“It’s working,” Die said softly, her eyes fixed on the sprout, her expression complex.

“We’re relying on this?” Yang Kai looked doubtful.

How could such a small sprout do anything? How could it possibly seal the boundary passage that spanned the sky?

Before he finished speaking, the sprout began to grow rapidly. In the blink of an eye, a sapling appeared before Yang Kai’s eyes.

And it didn’t stop there—the sapling’s growth was astonishing. In another breath, it had grown to a zhang tall, its crown spreading wide and its branches and leaves growing lush, with no sign of stopping.

Crack, crack, crack…

Fissures appeared in the ground as the roots spread and burrowed. Yang Kai instinctively retreated farther and farther, his gaze rising ever higher.

One zhang, three zhang, ten zhang, twenty zhang, fifty zhang…

In less than the time it takes to burn a stick of incense, the little sprout had become a towering tree that reached the clouds, connecting heaven and earth. Its lush green stood in stark contrast to the surrounding black Demonic Qi.

A verdant glow emanated from the massive tree, forming a powerful and steadfast barrier that enveloped the surviving members of Shaman Niu's tribe, shielding them from the wind, clouds, and thunder above.

Vast swaths of Demonic Qi were purified by the verdant glow, dissipating into the sky.

The demons stared in shock, their minds blank.

“Senior Qing!” Yang Kai cried out in surprise.

He finally understood—this tree had not grown out of nowhere. There was no such miraculous tree in the world.

It was the Divine Tree! The guardian deity of the Frost Snow Tribe.

At first, Yang Kai had found the vitality within the seven-colored clay familiar, but hadn’t dared connect it to Qing, as there were still differences between the two.

But when the fifty-zhang-tall tree appeared before him, Yang Kai recognized it instantly.

Qing was the means by which the Barbarian Tribe would seal the boundary passage.

Qing did not stop growing, but continued to rise and expand. The green barrier it formed grew ever more magnificent. Judging by its momentum, it seemed determined to reach the boundary passage and use its mighty body to seal it.
