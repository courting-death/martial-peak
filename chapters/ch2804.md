## Chapter 2804 — The Hero Returns

When daylight broke, Ah Hu appeared outside the village with an ashen face. The bloody battle from yesterday had left him unable to recover, especially with the aftereffects of the Bloodlust Art taking their toll. Even though he was among the village's finest warriors, he would remain weakened for several days and was in no condition to fight again. 

He looked up and called to the top of a large tree at the village entrance, “See anything?”

A head poked out from the tree’s crown—it was the barbarian youth who had been in charge of logistics on the fence yesterday.

“Brother Ah Hu, I haven’t seen anything,” the youth replied.

Ah Hu frowned, his face full of anxiety.

Ah Niu had chased after the Barbaric Beast yesterday and hadn’t returned all night. Everyone in the village was worried about him. The youth was hiding in the tree both to keep watch and to look for Ah Niu’s tracks, but his answer left Ah Hu deeply disappointed.

“Didn’t the village chief say Ah Niu is blessed by the Barbarian God? What are you so worried about?” Ah Hua appeared behind Ah Hu, a longbow slung across her back.

Ah Hu said, “Ah Niu was empowered by the Bloodlust Art. Now that a night has passed, that art must have faded. You know what that means.”

Even if the Barbarian God had bestowed power and transformed Ah Niu, the aftereffects of the Bloodlust Art were something no barbarian could ignore. If he was surrounded by beasts while weakened, he would only end up as food for them.

“Worrying won’t help. Trust the Barbarian God, trust Ah Niu.” Ah Hua patted Ah Hu’s shoulder and turned back into the village. Although yesterday’s battle hadn’t reached inside the village, there was still much to do.

“Brother Ah Hu... I see someone...”

Just then, the barbarian youth suddenly shouted.

“Who is it!” Ah Hu looked up, asking nervously.

“I can’t see clearly!” the youth replied.

“Open your eyes wider!” Ah Hu roared.

The youth was speechless, thinking that opening his eyes wider wouldn’t help him see any better.

But soon, he cried out in alarm, “It’s a Barbaric Beast!”

In his field of vision, he could clearly see the beast’s form rushing toward them at great speed.

“What?” Ah Hu and Ah Hua were both shocked. They exchanged glances, and the same terrifying thought surfaced in their minds—the village was doomed!

The beast had returned, and as for Ah Niu, who had chased after it, his fate was obvious. Yesterday’s bloody battle had left most of the village’s warriors unable to fight again. Now, with the beast attacking, who could resist?

“No, wait...” The youth changed his tune, his voice trembling with excitement. “It’s Brother Ah Niu! It’s Brother Ah Niu!”

Ah Hu didn’t even know how to scold him anymore. He said weakly, “Did you see clearly or not? Is it Ah Niu or the beast?”

One is a person, the other a beast—how bad did your eyesight have to be to mix them up? Ah Hua said nothing, but shot up the tree in an instant, climbing quickly with both hands and feet. Standing atop the crown, she looked out into the distance.

As a sharpshooter, her eyesight was beyond question. Just because the youth couldn’t see didn’t mean she couldn’t.

Ah Hu stood anxiously in place, craning his neck to wait.

After a moment, Ah Hua looked down and smiled. “It’s Ah Niu! Ah Niu is coming back with the beast!”

Ah Hu was stunned, barely able to believe it. “Ah Niu... killed the beast?”

Ah Hua had already come down from the tree, tossing her long braid behind her as she nodded. “That’s right, Ah Niu killed the beast!”

Ah Hu smiled in relief, then burst out laughing, and then laughed even harder...

He turned and shouted toward the village, “Everyone come out! Ah Niu killed the beast and came back! Ah Niu killed the beast!”

The busy villagers all froze, hardly daring to believe their ears. But soon, they put down their work and gathered at the village entrance, asking each other questions.

“Did Ah Niu really kill the beast?”

“Who saw it?”

“Ah Hu, are you lying to us?”

“Did that useless Ah Niu really do something so great?”

“He’s not useless anymore. Yesterday, thanks to him, the beast tide was repelled!”

“Wasn’t he the one who couldn’t even draw a bow before? Why did he suddenly change so much?”

“The village chief said the Barbarian God bestowed him with power.”

Yang Kai carried the battered Yao Beast on his shoulder. Before he even reached the village entrance, he could see a large crowd gathered, all pointing in his direction.

At the front of the crowd, Ah Hu, still pale, was smiling broadly and waving at him.

Ah Hua had said that it was thanks to Ah Hu sharing his food with him in the past that he hadn’t starved to death.

Although Yang Kai himself hadn’t experienced this, the body he now inhabited had received Ah Hu’s kindness. He could feel the care this barbarian youth had shown him.

He called back, then lifted the beast from his shoulder and held it high, showing it off to the villagers from afar.

The next moment, the villagers erupted in cheers and rushed over to welcome their returning hero.

In the blink of an eye, Yang Kai was surrounded by more than a hundred villagers. Each one warmly patted his shoulder, their eyes full of admiration and gratitude, then made way for him to walk forward.

Yang Kai didn’t know if this was some kind of ritual, but with every pat on his shoulder, he could feel the villagers’ respect.

At the village entrance, the old village chief appeared, leaning on his cane. His hunched body trembled, looking as if he was at death’s door.

With countless villagers behind him, Yang Kai walked up to the chief, set the Yao Beast on the ground, and grinned. “Village chief, I killed the beast.”

The chief carefully examined the beast, then nodded. “Well done.”

There weren’t many words of praise, but it felt all the more sincere and warm.

Yang Kai kept grinning.

At this moment, many barbarian youths, both boys and girls, gathered around, all staring at the beast on the ground as if seeing such a fierce creature for the first time. Their eyes were full of curiosity.

A few bold ones wanted to reach out and touch it, but their parents glared and pulled them back.

Just then, a girl stepped out from the crowd, holding a bowl of murky liquid. A faint aroma of wine drifted out. She walked up to Yang Kai, staring at him shyly, her cheeks flushed, but her gaze never left him. Her fiery look made Yang Kai a bit uneasy.

Strictly speaking, barbarian women didn’t really fit Yang Kai’s aesthetic, but this girl was different from the others. Though she was also very tall, she still gave Yang Kai a sense of stunning beauty.

Yang Kai tilted his head, curiously looking at the village chief.

The chief said, “Only the village’s finest warriors are qualified to drink this bowl of Barbarian God Wine. Go ahead and drink it.”

So it really was wine...

Although he could smell the wine, if the chief hadn’t said so, Yang Kai wouldn’t have dared to believe this murky liquid was actually wine.

Taking the stone bowl from the girl, Yang Kai drank it all in one go. A burning sensation shot from his throat down to his belly, and a strange power spread from his stomach, rippling through his Meridians.

Yang Kai raised his eyebrows, but before he could savor the feeling, the villagers grabbed his hands and feet and lifted him high into the air.

The villagers shouted Ah Niu’s name, tossing him up and catching him again and again...

Yang Kai had no idea how many women’s hands took the opportunity to grope his exposed muscles and sensitive spots. Barbarian women were always this bold and direct, making Yang Kai’s face flush red.

It was one thing for the young girls to take advantage, but even some obviously elderly grannies joined in—what were they doing!

The whole village was jubilant, as though a grand festival had descended upon it.

Only after a long while did the villagers finally set the dizzy Yang Kai down. He staggered, feeling that even the aftereffects of the Bloodlust Art weren’t this intense.

The chief smiled and extended his hand. The young barbarian on watch in the tree swiftly removed the stone dagger at his waist and handed it over.

The chief bent down and began dissecting the beast. It was clear he was skilled at this, and before long, he pulled out a round, fingernail-sized object from inside the beast and handed it to Yang Kai. “Soak it in wine and swallow it. You’ll become even stronger!”

Countless villagers looked on with envy, their eyes fixed on the bloodstained orb.

Wasn’t this an Inner Core? And it was a low-grade one at that.

Yang Kai reached out and took it.

So the Ancient Barbarian Tribe really did just swallow Inner Cores directly. It made sense—martial arts weren’t widespread in this era, and alchemy was still in its infancy. How could they possibly refine Spiritual Pills?

Swallowing a Yao Beast’s Inner Core could indeed increase one’s strength, but it also carried risks. The Inner Core was the essence of the beast, containing not only its power but also its animal nature. If a human swallowed it, it could easily affect their mind.

“This beast is your trophy. Take it back with you. Eating it over time will also make you stronger,” the chief said, pointing at the beast Yang Kai had brought back.

“Alright!” Yang Kai didn’t refuse. Although he’d only been here a day and hadn’t had time to fully experience the ancient era, he could feel from the villagers’ straightforward ways that they were truly sincere and simple people.

After a pause, Yang Kai said, “By the way, chief, in the canyon where I killed the beast, there are over three hundred wild beast corpses. Should we send people to bring them back? If needed, I can lead the way.”

At these words, many villagers’ faces lit up with joy.

The chief said, “We can’t waste precious food, but let’s rest for a few days first. Everyone is too tired.”

Ah Hu grinned. “Chief, now that Ah Niu is back and we have food for the winter, shouldn’t we celebrate today...?”

As he spoke, he was practically drooling.

The other villagers didn’t say anything, but their eyes were full of anticipation as they looked at the chief.

“All you think about is eating!” The chief raised his cane and gently tapped Ah Hu on the head.

Ah Hu didn't dodge or try to deflect the tap. He took the gentle strike on his head and immediately rubbed the spot, grinning sheepishly with a cheeky expression.

The chief said, “Alright, today everyone can eat their fill!”

The villagers immediately cheered.
