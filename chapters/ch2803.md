## Chapter 2803 — Ruthless Extermination

"Village Chief, take care of yourself. You're getting on in years, so don't run around outside. Go back and get some rest!" Yang Kai grinned at the old man, then turned and charged once more into the herd of beasts.

The old man's mouth twitched. Shocked as he was, he still remembered to cast another concealment spell on himself.

He was the village chief, but also a highly respected Shaman. Everyone in the village held him in great esteem. In the past, this Ah Niu didn't even have the courage to look him in the eye—how could he dare speak to him like this today?

Ah Niu had never contributed anything to the village, yet he still took his share of food and shelter.

He had even considered, just as the villagers suggested, banishing Ah Niu and letting him fend for himself.

But in the end, a life was a life, and he was still a villager. The chief always felt that one day Ah Niu would grow up and prove his worth.

Yet after all these years, Ah Niu's performance had been nothing but disappointing.

Until today, when he suddenly shone with a brilliance that no one could look at directly!

"The Barbarian God blesses us! The Barbarian God manifests!" the village chief murmured, his lips trembling as he watched Yang Kai charge back into the beast horde. In a single assault, he shattered the tide of beasts that had gathered like an ocean into complete chaos.

He was like the sharp sword in the hand of the Barbarian God, rampaging across the battlefield, unstoppable wherever he went.

The dazzling golden light could not be concealed even by the thick snow. It darted left and right across the battlefield, quickly carving out swathes of empty ground.

The giant beasts howled in terror and slowly began to retreat.

The villagers, however, fought with increasing courage. Three formed a wall, five became a shield, using the simplest joint attack techniques and fearless bravery to drive the wild beasts from their home.

Yang Kai had already changed his stone axe and spear several times.

At last, as the wind died down and the snow weakened, the beast tide receded.

Only corpses and blood remained, strewn across the ground.

The villagers chased the beasts for dozens of zhang before finally stopping, then let out a thunderous roar, like wild beasts themselves, making the fleeing giants even more panicked.

"We won!"

"We won!"

The villagers cheered, overjoyed to have survived another beast tide. Every face was lit with joy as they jumped and shouted.

"Ah Niu, it really is you!" Ah Hu emerged from somewhere, his body covered in blood. Though he bore no serious wounds, only a bite mark was visible on his abdomen. He stared at Yang Kai with wide eyes and gave his shoulder a heavy pat. "I thought my eyes were playing tricks on me!"

How could he be mistaken? In the whole village, only Ah Niu had such a physique.

Ah Hu had noticed Yang Kai's presence earlier, but hadn't dared believe it until now, face to face with him, when the truth finally became undeniable.

The surviving villagers also looked curiously at Yang Kai, each with gratitude and admiration in their eyes.

At the last moment, if Yang Kai hadn't charged into the beast horde and turned the tide, who knows what fate would have befallen the village today. In the world of the barbarians, the strong ruled! This rule had endured from ancient times to the present, though it no longer carried the pure and unadorned simplicity of the Ancient Barbarian Tribe.

"This is the Barbarian God's blessing!" The old chief trembled as he walked up, leaning on his pitch-black cane.

Yang Kai thought to himself, "This is all thanks to me—what does it have to do with the Barbarian God!" But of course, he couldn't say that aloud. No matter how much he'd contributed, saying such a thing would make him the enemy of all barbarians.

To blaspheme the Barbarian God was the greatest sin among the Ancient Barbarian Tribe.

"Chief, we've killed so many beasts this time. Shouldn't we celebrate properly?" Ah Hu looked at the chief with excitement, and the other villagers were full of anticipation.

Food was scarce in the village. This time, hundreds of wild beasts had died in the beast tide—enough to feed the village for a long time. Just days ago, the chief had worried about winter rations. Every winter, many villagers starved to death, and this year would have been no exception. No one expected fortune to come so suddenly. With these carcasses, there was no need to worry about food this winter.

The chief smiled slightly and was about to speak when Yang Kai stepped forward and said, "Chief, don't you think this beast tide was a bit strange?"

The chief frowned and looked at Yang Kai. "You noticed too?"

Yang Kai was surprised. "What did you see, Chief?"

The old chief was silent for a while before saying, "Barbaric Beast—there was a Barbaric Beast commanding the tide."

At these words, many villagers' faces changed. Barbaric Beasts were powerful, only Shamans could deal with them. Ordinary flesh and blood meant nothing before such a creature.

"So that's called a Barbaric Beast!" Yang Kai followed up. What he sensed was just a Yao Beast that had gained a bit of intelligence, enough to command the horde and make this beast tide so troublesome.

"Winter has come, and the wild beasts are searching for food. Though we've driven them off this time, if we don't root them out, they'll surely return."

The villagers' faces changed again. They knew Yang Kai was right. Without a Barbaric Beast, the wild beasts weren't much to fear, but with one commanding them, it was a different story.

If the beast tide attacked again one day, it would be another disaster for the village. Too many warriors had died today; they might not withstand another assault.

"Chief, why not ask the tribe for help?" Ah Hu suggested.

The chief shook his head. "If we ask for help, more than half of today's spoils would have to be offered to the tribe. Are you willing?"

The villagers all shook their heads. So many had died for these spoils—no one would agree to give away more than half. Here, food was the foundation of survival!

"Then what do we do!" Ah Hu frowned.

Yang Kai said, "I'll go kill it!"

With that, he turned and chased after the retreating beast tide.

Ah Hu was shocked and grabbed Yang Kai's arm. "Ah Niu, don't be reckless! The Barbaric Beast isn't something you can handle."

Yang Kai gently pried his hand away and smiled. "It's just a Barbaric Beast. Wait for good news."

Ah Hu froze in place. Just moments ago, when Yang Kai had gently pried his hand away, he felt a surge of power that left him utterly shocked.

Was this still the Ah Niu he knew?

"I'll go with you!" Ah Hu tried to follow, but after just two steps, he felt dizzy and staggered. The Bloodlust Art had ended, and the aftereffects were about to hit. At this point, even if he went with Yang Kai, he'd only be a burden.

He could only watch as Yang Kai disappeared into the wind and snow.

He turned to the chief. "Chief, what's happened to Ah Niu?"

The villagers all wanted to know. Today's Ah Niu was like a different person.

The chief was silent for a long time before saying, "The Barbarian God bestowed power, allowing Ah Niu to be reborn."

The villagers suddenly understood, and no one doubted the explanation. Not a hint of jealousy appeared on their faces.

Yang Kai walked alone, stone axe in hand. This trial was an experience like never before—real people, real battles. It felt as if he'd broken through the shackles of time, returned to ancient days, become one of the ancients, and witnessed the era's splendor.

He didn't know what he would gain from this trial, but he knew this village was key. Winning the villagers' favor might bring unexpected rewards.

So he traveled alone, hunting that cunning, hateful Barbaric Beast.

The retreating beast tide left many clues. The snow was full of wild beast tracks. Though the snow was heavy, it hadn't yet covered the traces.

Yang Kai kept up the pursuit, easily falling to the rear of the beast horde.

But these creatures moved quickly. Catching up wouldn't be easy.

Not until dusk did Yang Kai chase them into a canyon.

The snow had stopped. The canyon floor was covered in countless tracks and stained with blood.

From the outside, the canyon looked like a gourd—narrow at the mouth, wide inside, surrounded by sheer cliffs shining like mirrors. Even at night, Yang Kai's eyes could see ten li away.

He saw the end of the canyon—a dead end.

Things just got simple! Yang Kai grinned, not planning to make things complicated. It was just a Yao Beast with a bit of intelligence.

Golden bloodlust light shone from him as he stood at the canyon entrance, stuck his stone axe at his waist, then raised his fists and pounded the cliffs on both sides.

Boom, boom, boom…

The thunderous noise echoed through the gourd-shaped canyon, startling the beast horde that had just returned. Countless wild beasts roared and charged from their hiding places.

From afar, they saw the dazzling golden light. The wild beasts all stopped, terrified, seeing the canyon's only exit blocked by fallen rocks.

During the day, their companions had died in droves at the hands of this golden figure. Now, seeing that familiar, deadly light, they were too scared to advance.

If they could escape, they'd have fled at once, never wanting to meet this golden man again.

But now, with the canyon's only exit sealed, they couldn't run even if they wanted to.

They had always attacked barbarian villages, never imagining that one day a barbarian would come to their doorstep. Lacking intelligence and experience, the wild beasts were at a loss, not knowing what to do.

At that moment, a low roar came from deep within the canyon.

It sounded like a command. Only then did the wild beasts reluctantly move, surrounding Yang Kai.

Yang Kai grinned, his smile bright, revealing sharp white fangs. He took the stone axe from his waist and strode toward the beast horde.

Moments later, the slaughter began anew. Wherever the golden light passed, the beast horde collapsed like bubbles. Giant beasts fell in pools of blood as Yang Kai pressed forward, fast and resolute, killing his way deeper into the canyon.

Roars and howls echoed together, quickly filling the canyon with a thick, choking stench of blood.

Yang Kai's stone axe had already shattered, so he simply abandoned his weapon and swung his fists. Each punch broke the bones and tendons of the wild beasts.
