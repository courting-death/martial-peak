## Chapter 2828 — Dawn Raid

"With You's strength, even if Chi and I joined forces, we might not be his match," Zhu explained calmly, showing no shame for her own lack of power. "But fortunately, our main goal this time is to rescue people. As long as Chi and I keep him occupied, the rest of you can free our clansmen. There's no need to fight him to the death."

After a pause, Zhu continued, "You're a Shaman Master and can help a lot in battle. Since your clansmen were also taken, let's act together when the time comes."

Ya's expression changed dramatically, almost unable to believe his ears as he stared at Yang Kai in shock. A Shaman Master—he really was a Shaman Master? Just now, Ya thought Chi had made a mistake, but now Zhu said the same, so there was only one possibility: Shaman Niu truly was a Shaman Master.

Yang Kai nodded and asked, "Are you two planning a dawn raid?"

"That's right."

"If that's the case, I might be able to help even more," Yang Kai said, his eyes flickering.

Chi raised his brow. "Let's hear it!"

A moment later, Ya and Yang Kai withdrew.

Ya looked dazed, as if struck by a huge blow. After a long time, he finally came to his senses. "Master Niu, be careful in everything."

Yang Kai smiled and patted his shoulder. "You too!"

As he finished speaking, a chant sounded from his mouth. As shamanic power surged, his body seemed to turn into a vortex, absorbing all the moonlight pouring down from the sky. Ya, standing before him, was surprised to find that as the moonlight flowed in, Yang Kai's figure gradually became transparent, then vanished completely.

The sound of footsteps grew distant. Ya knew Shaman Niu must have left, but seeing him perform such a miraculous Shamanic Art left Ya in awe.

That must be the legendary Moon Concealment Art of the Moonlight Tribe, but the Moonlight Tribe was wiped out thousands of years ago, so the Moon Concealment Art should have been lost. Where did Shaman Niu learn it? A small place like Cangnan Village couldn't possibly have such advanced Shamanic Arts for him to study.

Yang Kai's Moon Concealment Art wasn't learned from the village chief, but was taught to him by Qing.

On the way back, Yang Kai had been digesting Qing's knowledge. He learned many Shamanic Arts, and now this lost Moon Concealment Art was just what he needed.

Under the moonlight, Yang Kai was almost invisible. Only those who got close or scanned with Divine Sense could detect his movements.

He made his way to the stone fortress, silently scaled the stone wall, and slipped inside.

On the stone wall, Bone-Eater Tribe archers patrolled every three steps, with guards every five. Clearly, they knew that after raiding three villages, they might be attacked. Though these archers lacked Divine Sense, each was highly alert to danger and anything suspicious.

As Yang Kai climbed over the wall, he nearly caught an archer's attention. The archer glanced his way, but fortunately didn't investigate further, or Yang Kai might have been exposed.

Inside the fortress, the air was thick with the scent of blood—clearly human blood.

Earlier, Yang Kai had seen hundreds of corpses hanging from the stone wall. They belonged to three tribes, and blood dripped down, making a chilling sound.

At this moment, inside the fortress, Bone-Eater Tribe members feasted, the smell of meat everywhere. Yang Kai saw Bone-Eater Tribe barbarians gnawing on cooked flesh, much of it clearly human body parts. Ordinary people would lose their appetite, but these Bone-Eater Tribe members ate with relish—even the children fought over meaty bones, wrestling fiercely, while the adults watched and laughed.

He had long heard that the Bone-Eater Tribe were outcasts among barbarians, a group of uncivilized beasts. Seeing it firsthand, Yang Kai could only agree with the rumors.

He took a deep breath, suppressed his agitation, and began to quietly search the fortress.

According to his agreement with Chi and Zhu, he had to first find where the captives from the three villages were held. At dawn, he would open the fortress gate from inside to create chaos, then the Raging Flame Tribe and Gale Tribe would coordinate with him from outside.

Then he would release the warriors of the three tribes, catching the Bone-Eater Tribe off guard.


There was a Great Shaman Master in the fortress, and several Shaman Masters. Yang Kai couldn't be too reckless; he didn't dare use Divine Sense, lest he alert them. He could only search little by little.

As midnight passed, Yang Kai had nearly searched the entire fortress but still hadn't found where the captives were held.

Three tribes, three villages—at least seven or eight hundred captives. Such a large number couldn't be kept in a small place, but after searching, Yang Kai found nothing.

In the end, he could only focus on the very center of the fortress, the Bone-Eater Tribe's core, where a strong and ferocious aura emanated. It must be where You lived.

After some thought, Yang Kai sent his Divine Sense underground.

He withdrew it instantly.

Sure enough, there was another space beneath the fortress. Yang Kai sensed many living people hidden below, but he didn't probe too deeply, lest You notice.

As long as he confirmed the location of the captives, the rest would be easy.

He held his breath and stayed still for a moment, making sure his probing hadn't alerted You. Then Yang Kai slipped into a hidden spot, waiting for the right moment.

Inside the fortress, the Bone-Eater Tribe celebrated their bounty after a harsh winter. Outside, the Raging Flame Tribe and Gale Tribe lay in wait, like two venomous snakes ready to strike with their fangs.

As time passed, the lively sounds in the fortress gradually faded. Thunderous snores rose and fell, and even the archers on the walls began yawning.

As dawn approached, the world was at its darkest.

A figure emerged from hiding, rushing to the fortress gate like a gale. With several muffled thuds, the heads of the Bone-Eater Tribe barbarians guarding the gate split open, brains splattering, dying in their sleep.

On the wall, several archers were startled by the commotion and turned to look down.

They saw a stranger standing below, punching the stone gate.

With a crash, the stone gate shattered, stones flying as the ground trembled, like thousands of horses galloping. Disordered, urgent footsteps quickly approached.

The startled archers whipped their heads around, only to see, beneath the cover of darkness, pairs of blood-red eyes—enchanted by the Bloodlust Art—glowing with a terrifying light. Death seemed to swirl around them as they surged forward like a monstrous flood, overwhelming all in their path.

"Enemy attack!"

Someone shouted in alarm.

The howling sounded like a warning bell, waking the sleeping Bone-Eater Tribe barbarians. At the same time, the archers on the walls drew their bows and shot down.

The barbarian archers were all sharpshooters, and so were the Bone-Eater Tribe. With one volley, more than a dozen people fell dead.

But behind the rushing flood, two figures soared out. One's hands blazed with fire, and as he waved, two fire dragon-like attacks descended, incinerating a group of Bone-Eater Tribe archers.

The other chanted, and a terrifying tornado swept across the wall, sharp as knives. Anyone caught in it was torn apart, their flesh stripped away in an instant, leaving only skeletons—ghastly and pale.

Chi and Zhu struck together, instantly eliminating the threat from the archers.

Following the two Great Shaman Masters were the Shaman Masters and Shaman Warriors of both tribes. There were only seven in total, but as they rushed, they chanted spells, empowering their warriors with various Shamanic Arts, making them faster, stronger, fiercer.

Yang Kai turned, hovering in midair, his gaze cold as he watched the Bone-Eater Tribe members rushing from all directions.

Among the crowd, Bone-Eater Tribe shamans shouted at their clansmen, seemingly directing them to attack Yang Kai.

No instructions were needed. Yang Kai was such an obvious target that the Bone-Eater Tribe barbarians saw him clearly. Before they got close, arrows whistled through the air, aiming to bring him down.

The shield of Shamanic Art glowed, and not a single arrow succeeded, despite their immense force.

The next moment, all sorts of Shamanic Arts attacked.

The shield still stood firm, rippling as it blocked every attack.

The shamans in the crowd were dumbfounded.

The fierce Bone-Eater Tribe warriors charged at Yang Kai. A dozen ferocious fighters leapt high, weapons raised, slashing down at him.

Yang Kai reached out, and the Myriad Sword appeared. With a casual swing, a sword light over ten zhang long seemed to shatter the void, leaving a dazzling trail in the air.

Crash...

The dozen leaping Bone-Eater Tribe warriors were all cut in half at the waist. As muffled groans sounded, they fell, organs spilling, blood gushing like fountains.

The charging Bone-Eater Tribe warriors all froze in their tracks, staring at Yang Kai in utter shock, as if beholding the most fearsome demon. These Bone-Eater barbarians, who feared neither heaven nor earth, now felt a chill of terror creep into their hearts beneath the sweep of his sword.

"How dare you act up in my territory! Courting death!"

An angry roar came from inside the fortress. Next, a half-naked, terrifyingly fierce man burst out like thunder, flying forward. Before he arrived, he punched out.

Yang Kai's eyes narrowed. The Myriad Sword spun, blocking sideways.

With a loud bang, the Myriad Sword rang, its blade bending, nearly breaking. Before Yang Kai could counterattack, two figures suddenly darted out from both sides.

It was Chi and Zhu, joining forces. The two Great Shaman Masters from different tribes said nothing, directly targeting You. Their Shamanic Arts, refined over many years, instantly burst forth with dazzling brilliance.
