## Chapter 3051 — Frantic Cultivation

Half a day later, Yang Kai had more than a thousand Dragon Blood Flowers in his Spatial Ring, having traversed nearly the entire Spirit Island.

Under Fu Ling's watchful gaze, Li Jiao also picked a few. Whether from excitement or fear, both his hands trembled incessantly, and he found the experience incredibly thrilling.

Suddenly, Yang Kai looked in a certain direction, his attention drawn to two Dragon Blood Flowers there.

Those two Dragon Blood Flowers were clearly different from those he had gathered earlier. Both were crystal clear, with rich, lustrous blood-red hues and pure Dragon auras. The hazy red mist rising from each flower even showed signs of coalescing into a remarkably lifelike Jiao.

"Supreme-Level Dragon Blood Flowers?" Yang Kai's eyes narrowed. Although he had never seen a Supreme-Level Dragon Blood Flower before, he still recognized them the instant he saw them.

"Brother-in-law, those two flowers..." Fu Ling grew anxious.

Even on Dragon Isle, Supreme-Level Dragon Blood Flowers were treasures that could only be encountered by chance. Only the Elders had ever been qualified to consume them. These two Supreme-Level Dragon Blood Flowers had long since been claimed and were merely awaiting harvest in two months, after which they would be presented to the Great Elder and Second Elder. Now that Yang Kai had set his sights on them, how could they possibly be saved?

Although she knew she could not stop him, Fu Ling still had to try persuading him.

"Get out of the way!" Yang Kai reached out and swept her to one side, then walked over to the two Dragon Blood Flowers, his eyes blazing.

He reached out and plucked them, and the red Dragon phantoms above the flowers actually let out faint, barely perceptible roars.

Fu Ling sighed, knowing she was truly finished this time. Once what had happened here was exposed, the only fate awaiting her would be exile to the Dragon Tomb.

Another hour later, every mature Dragon Blood Flower on the entire Spirit Island had been swept away by Yang Kai.

"Remain here and guard this Spirit Island. I'll call for you if I need you. Do your best to keep what happened here from being exposed," Yang Kai instructed Fu Ling as he left the Spirit Island.

"Understood." Fu Ling answered listlessly, though inwardly she wished she could slap Yang Kai to death. He had left behind such an enormous mess, yet he actually expected her to clean up after him. He had no sense of decency whatsoever.

Yang Kai nodded, then flew away with Li Jiao.

Although Li Jiao was somewhat weak, Yang Kai had rescued him in time, so his foundation had not suffered much damage. He only needed to recuperate for a while to make a full recovery, and there was no risk of his cultivation falling or his realm regressing.

Along the way, Li Jiao tried to speak several times, only to stop himself.

"Sanniang is fine." Yang Kai knew what he wanted to ask and simply spoke first.

Hearing this, Li Jiao's expression became somewhat complicated, but in the end, he still said, "Many thanks for looking after her, Brother Yang."

Yang Kai nodded. "Sanniang is a good person. What happened back then wasn't her fault either."

Li Jiao said, "I know, but... haah." He let out a heavy sigh and continued shaking his head.

Although Lu Sanniang had been entirely innocent in what happened back then, it was likely something no man could easily put behind him.

Yang Kai understood how he felt, so after offering a few casual words, he did not mention it again. After thinking for a moment, he said, "You're a Dragon Descendant, so you stand out too much. If another member of the Dragon Clan discovers you, it might cause some unexpected trouble. You should enter a safe place for now."

Li Jiao asked suspiciously, "What place?"

Yang Kai stopped, turned around, and sent a palm strike toward him, shouting, "Don't resist!"

Li Jiao was startled and instinctively wanted to resist, but after hearing Yang Kai's words, he immediately relaxed. He trusted that Yang Kai would not harm him.

A wave of dizziness swept over him, and Li Jiao found himself inside an extremely strange world...

After returning to Fu Chi's Spirit Island, Yang Kai found Lu Sanniang and her daughter. Without explaining much, he took both of them into the Small Xuan Realm so they could reunite with Li Jiao, then flew toward the depths of the sea.

Just as before, Yang Kai plunged into the sea, immediately summoned the Xuan Realm Pearl, and slipped into the Small Xuan Realm.

There was not much time left, only about twenty days even by the most generous estimate, so he did not want to waste a single moment.

After inspecting his medicinal herbs, Yang Kai took out all the supplementary ingredients needed to refine Dragon Blood Pills and piled them beside him.

He possessed plenty of spirit flowers and rare herbs, so refining Dragon Blood Pills posed no problem. However, he had now gathered more than a thousand Dragon Blood Flowers, enough to refine more than a thousand batches of Dragon Blood Pills.

However, he did not have nearly enough supplementary ingredients. At most, he could refine about another twenty batches before they were exhausted, so he had to plan carefully.

Among the more than a thousand Dragon Blood Flowers, one or two hundred were Middle-Level, twenty or thirty were Upper-Level, and only two were Supreme-Level.

To maximize their medicinal efficacy, Yang Kai directly used Upper-Level Dragon Blood Flowers for this round of Dragon Blood Pill refinement.

He summoned his pill furnace, controlled the flame, inscribed Spirit Arrays, and added the medicinal ingredients...

An hour later, the first batch of Dragon Blood Pills emerged from the furnace.

The results were passable. A yield of four Dragon Blood Pills was neither high nor low, but their quality was considerably better than that of those he had refined before. After all, these had been made from an Upper-Level Dragon Blood Flower, so his previous pills simply could not compare.

Yang Kai remained perfectly calm. After briefly restoring his strength, he immediately began refining the second batch of Spiritual Pills.

Then came the third batch, the fourth...

As he grew more practiced, the quality of the Dragon Blood Pills he refined afterward seemed to improve with every batch.

By the tenth batch, he actually produced five pills, one more than before.

This was good progress. One additional Dragon Blood Pill meant an additional measure of assurance, so Yang Kai was naturally pleased.

However, five Dragon Blood Pills seemed to be the limit. Even after refining a full twenty batches, the number of pills produced did not increase again. The quality of the Dragon Blood Pills also seemed to have reached a limit, and the medicinal efficacy contained within them far surpassed that of the first batch.

He cleared his heart and stilled his mind, then sat cross-legged and circulated his Secret Art to restore his strength.

One day later, Yang Kai opened his eyes and started the furnace again.

He had only two sets of ingredients for refining Dragon Blood Pills left, and naturally, these final two batches would use the two Supreme-Level Dragon Blood Flowers as their main ingredients. He held extremely high expectations for these two batches of Spiritual Pills.

He was thoroughly familiar with every step. After refining twenty batches of Dragon Blood Pills, Yang Kai knew exactly what to do at every moment even with his eyes closed. He would not make the slightest error or allow the smallest flaw.

When the first batch of Spiritual Pills emerged from the furnace, even Yang Kai could not help feeling expectant. He collected the pills and examined them carefully, immediately beaming with joy.

His luck was good. This batch of Spiritual Pills refined from a Supreme-Level Dragon Blood Flower had produced a full six pills, two of which had even formed Pill Patterns. Intricate and profound lines covered those two Spiritual Pills like the Meridians within a human body.

A Spiritual Pill with Pill Patterns was naturally far more valuable than one without them. This was also one of the standards by which an Alchemy Grandmaster's proficiency was judged. Although some Alchemists knew how to refine pills, they would never produce a Spiritual Pill with Pill Patterns in their entire lives, because they merely followed recipes by rote and possessed no unique techniques or insights of their own. In other words, their Alchemy lacked spirituality, and everything they did was rigid and inflexible.

Yang Kai was naturally different from those Alchemists.

After briefly composing himself, Yang Kai started the furnace again and prepared to refine the final batch of Spiritual Pills.

He was in even better condition this time. Once the pills formed, Yang Kai knew without examining them closely that the results would be no worse than before.

The outcome was just as he expected.

Six Spiritual Pills, three of which had formed Pill Patterns.

With this, Yang Kai's Alchemy finally came to an end.

Counting the Dragon Blood Pills he had refined earlier, he had produced a full twenty-five batches of Spiritual Pills over this period without a single failure. Even Great Emperor Miaodan would likely praise such an achievement.

He now possessed nearly a hundred Dragon Blood Pills, each of higher quality than the last, and five of them had even formed Pill Patterns.

He spent a moment admiring his masterpieces.

Then, without the slightest delay, Yang Kai began his grand cultivation plan. With a low shout, his body abruptly expanded, transforming into a towering five-zhang Dragon body as majestic as a mountain.

Taking out a Dragon Blood Pill, Yang Kai tossed it into his mouth and swallowed it. He then circulated the Dragon Transformation Art, and his Dragon aura spread outward.

While Yang Kai cultivated by consuming Dragon Blood Pills as though they were candy, elsewhere in the vast sea of the Dragon Palace, on a certain Spirit Island...

This Spirit Island was somewhat different from the others. A biting chill filled the air for a thousand li in every direction, and the entire island seemed frozen solid. Even the sea around its shores was covered by a thick layer of ice.

This was clearly an ice island.

Everything on the ice island seemed frozen. There was no birdsong or fragrance of flowers, nor any lush greenery. Apart from its extraordinarily rich Spiritual Qi, the entire Spirit Island was pervaded by an unsettling aura that made it feel unbearably oppressive.

Even among the Dragon Clan, this ice island was not a place anyone could casually approach.

That was because it was the territory of the Dragon Clan's Second Elder, Fu Zhun.

Every member of the Dragon Clan, whether from the Fu Lineage or the Zhu Lineage, held the Second Elder in great reverence. This was especially true after what had happened more than ten years ago, when that reverence had almost risen to the level of fear.

More than ten years ago, the Second Elder had personally exiled someone to the Dragon Tomb, and no one on all of Dragon Isle had shared a closer relationship with the Second Elder than that woman had.

The two had been almost as close as mother and daughter, yet even then, the Second Elder had shown no mercy when punishing her.

That incident had shown every member of the Dragon Clan just how iron-blooded and merciless the Second Elder could be.

At this moment, Zhu Qing was under house arrest on this ice island.

At the very center of the ice island stood a Ten-Thousand-Year Ice Bed, upon which Zhu Qing sat cross-legged. Her cultivation had been suppressed and her Dragon Bloodline sealed. Now, Zhu Qing could not draw upon the slightest bit of strength; only her physical body remained relatively powerful.

Yet as the cold from the Ten-Thousand-Year Ice Bed seeped into her body, she still shivered from time to time. Her face was pale, and every breath she exhaled condensed into mist.

She knew this was the Second Elder's punishment, so she had no complaints and merely endured it in silence.

Recalling every moment she had shared with Yang Kai, her heart throbbed with pain. She did not know whether she would ever have the chance to see him again in this life. Now that she had ended up like this, she feared that even if the chance arose in the future, she would be too ashamed to face him.

Before returning, she had already prepared herself for this, knowing that her relationship with Yang Kai would inevitably meet with tremendous opposition.

However, she was a member of the Dragon Clan and had no choice but to return. If she had stubbornly insisted on remaining by Yang Kai's side, she would inevitably have brought a great calamity upon him. Thus, she had returned to Dragon Isle with the faint hope that she could persuade the Second Elder.
