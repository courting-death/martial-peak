## Chapter 2930 — Heaven Devourer Breaks Free

Gao Xueting reached out and steadied Yang Kai's arm, preventing him from stumbling. Yang Kai nodded in gratitude and took several deep breaths, working to calm the turbulent qi and blood roiling within his chest.

Suddenly, there came two mournful whimpers. Guixu, in the form of a little black dog, actually let out cries of pain, as if enduring something excruciating.

At this moment, its appearance was utterly miserable. Previously, no one knew what method Gong Yue had used to control and stimulate the power within its bloodline, allowing it to contend with so many Emperor Realm masters on its own for quite some time. But after reaching its limit, it seemed to pose no further threat. When swept up by the many divine abilities, its body was left skinless and bloody, making it unbearable to look at.

Gong Yue glanced at it, clearly sensing something. He smiled faintly and said, “So be it. The time is about right anyway.”

No one knew what he meant by these words, but as soon as he finished speaking, Guixu suddenly retched a few times, then its mouth swelled enormously, transforming instantly into that sky-filling giant maw.

From within the giant maw, a cold gleam burst forth. A man and a spear seemed to break free from the abyss, spinning rapidly as they shot out.

The instant he returned to the light, Feng Ming was clearly dazed. After being swallowed whole just now, he had found himself surrounded by endless darkness—no light, no sound. Fortunately, he hadn’t been harmed; it was as if he’d simply been swallowed into a strange world.

Occasionally, a faint glimmer would appear in that world. He had fought his way along that light with all his strength and finally escaped.

With a sweep of his gaze, Feng Ming made a decision. He kicked the retching Guixu beside him, sending the little black dog flying out of sight. Then, spear in hand like a dragon, he thrust straight at the seated Gong Yue.

At the critical moment between life and death, Gong Yue did not dodge. He didn’t even try to resist; his aura had always been weak. His ability to contend with so many Emperor Realm masters was entirely due to Guixu’s bizarre powers. Now that Guixu was spent, even if he resisted, he could not withstand the furious Feng Ming.

He faced death’s summons with a calm expression, as if he had long since seen through life and death.

With a hiss, the Yanlei Spear blasted into Gong Yue’s chest, blowing open a gaping hole. Through that hole, one could vaguely see his organs writhing inside.

Gong Yue was still smiling. “Killing me alone is useless. Time will prove my choice was right. You are all wrong.”

“Putting on airs!” Feng Ming snorted coldly. With a surge of Emperor Yuan, a beastly roar erupted from the Yanlei Spear. Immediately after, Gong Yue’s body exploded into a cloud of blood mist, leaving no corpse or bones behind.

A streak of black light shot out. Feng Ming, quick of eye and hand, jabbed at it with his spear, shattering it. Only then did he breathe a sigh of relief and turn to ask the others, “Are you all alright?”

Chen Wenhao’s expression did not look very good. He shook his head and said, “It’s nothing, thanks to Elder Yang. Brother Feng, how are you?”

Feng Ming shook his head. “I’m fine as well, but I should check carefully. That thing was too strange.”

“What did Gong Yue mean by what he said just now?” Gao Xueting’s brows were tightly knit. She had been pondering Gong Yue’s words but couldn’t make sense of them. Instead, she felt increasingly uneasy, as if something bad was about to happen.

No one knew what Gong Yue’s last words truly meant.

As everyone was lost in thought, a rumbling sound came from afar, growing closer. The earth and mountains began to shake and tremble, as if something had been broken open. Suddenly, streaks of black light soared from somewhere on Spirit Sword Peak, turning into streams of light that shot in all directions.

“Demonic Intent… Why is there so much Demonic Intent?” Gao Xueting looked up, her face instantly paling in shock.

These streaks of black light were clearly the same as the force that had corrupted Gong Yue—remnants of Demonic Intent. But their number was in the hundreds or even thousands, like fireworks exploding in the sky and scattering everywhere.

“How can this be!” Chen Wenhao was also dumbfounded.

Qiu Ran and the others were seeing Demonic Intent for the first time. Though they didn’t know how dangerous it was, they could sense these things were not to be trifled with, and all wore grave expressions.

Yang Kai coughed lightly, his voice weak and strained. "Spirit Sword Peak... it's a place for sealing demons. Gong Yue came here for one reason—to break the seal and release the Demonic Intent."

Gao Xueting was startled by his words and turned to look at him. “Junior Brother, are you saying there’s a place here just like the Southern Marsh?”

Yang Kai replied, “There’s no other explanation.”

Everyone’s attention had been drawn to the ancient cave residence in the Southern Marsh. No one had expected that within Spirit Sword Peak of Qingyang Shrine, there was also a place for sealing demons. Gong Yue, corrupted by Demonic Intent, clearly knew this, which was why he came all this way, using his mastery of formations to break the restriction and release even more Demonic Intent.

When he died, he didn’t resist—not because he couldn’t, but because there was no need. Even if he died, his task was already complete. Just as he said, many more would follow in his footsteps.

“Damn it!” Gao Xueting ground her silver teeth.

“Activate the Grand Guardian Array! We must stop these things at once. If they escape…” Yang Kai urged anxiously, but before he could finish, another rumble sounded, followed by a burst of wild laughter that reached everyone’s ears.

Yang Kai’s words were cut off. He stared in astonishment toward the source of the laughter.

He had already recognized whose voice it was.

At first, the laughter was somewhat restrained, as if surprised, but as time passed, it grew more and more unbridled and arrogant.

A stream of light shot over from the distance, and the laughter drew rapidly closer.

In the blink of an eye, an old man with white hair and beard, disheveled and ragged, appeared not far from the group. Suddenly, an indescribable pressure descended from the sky, as if a great mountain pressed upon everyone’s chest. Even the Emperor Realm masters found it hard to breathe. Yang Kai, already in poor condition, coughed up blood at the corner of his mouth.

The old man stood suspended in the air, his laughter ceasing. His gaze swept briefly across the crowd before fixing upon Yang Kai, a faint smile playing at his lips. "Oh? So you're here as well, little brat. It seems we meet again, just as fate would have it."

Yang Kai frowned deeply. Something felt off. The old man before him looked no different from his memory, but his aura was completely changed. The old man he remembered was wise and carefree, but this one exuded nothing but evil.

Gao Xueting’s expression grew solemn as she hurriedly said, “Greetings, Great Emperor!”

Qiu Ran also quickly saluted.

The others were startled, including several other elders of the shrine. They knew nothing of this man, but Qiu Ran and Gao Xueting both addressed him as Great Emperor. There were only a handful of Great Emperors in the world—who was this one?

Feng Ming and Chen Wenhao exchanged glances, both seeing shock in each other’s eyes.

Gao Xueting explained, “This is Lord Hongchen!”

“What?” The two were startled but dared not be negligent, cupping their fists together. “Greetings, Senior Hongchen.”

Among the ten Great Emperors, aside from those who founded sects, the rest were all elusive figures, especially Mortal Great Emperor, the most mysterious of all. Because he cultivated the Dao of Mortal Life, he needed to temper himself in the mortal world, taking on countless forms—beggar on the street, woodcutter on the mountain, brothel keeper, court official—perhaps any of these could be him. Yet no one could ever recognize him; perhaps they brushed shoulders with him, perhaps they drank together, but no one ever truly knew his identity.

Feng Ming, Chen Wenhao, and the others had never seen Mortal Great Emperor, but they believed Gao Xueting’s words without doubt.

It was said that Qingyang Shrine had a close relationship with Mortal Great Emperor, especially Hall Master Wen Zishan, who was as close as a son to him. Otherwise, how could Qingyang Shrine have risen to become one of the top sects in the Southern Territory in just a few thousand years? Among those top sects, apart from Qingyang Shrine, the rest all had ancient legacies—Tianwu Sacred Land could even trace its history back tens of thousands of years.

As all the powerful figures paid their respects, Duan Hongchen merely smiled faintly, ignoring them, his gaze fixed with interest on Yang Kai.

Gao Xueting said, “My Lord, Demonic Intent has reemerged and the shrine is in crisis. Please lend us your aid!”

Yang Kai gently patted Gao Xueting’s arm and shook his head. “Say no more. He is not Senior Hongchen.”

“Huh?” Gao Xueting was stunned, then as if recalling something, her body trembled and she stared at Duan Hongchen in terror.

Feng Ming and Chen Wenhao were both bewildered, completely lost. Gao Xueting said this was Mortal Great Emperor, but Yang Kai said he was not. Yet the old man did not refute either, leaving the two outsiders utterly confused.

“Wu Kuang, how did you get out? What have you done with Senior Hongchen?” Yang Kai asked coldly, his gaze icy.

“Duan Hongchen” smiled faintly, tapping his own forehead. “Since this Lord is already out, what do you think happened to old Hongchen? He’s been devoured by this Lord! Hahahaha!”

“Impossible!” Yang Kai shouted sternly.

Wu Kuang’s laughter abruptly stopped, his voice turning cold. “Brat, how many years have you lived? How much do you know of this Lord’s divine abilities? How laughable that old Hongchen thought he could imprison this Lord in that mirror, only to end up helping this Lord instead. Truly foolish! Under this Lord’s Heaven Devouring Battle Art, how could he resist? Who in the world could resist?”

“Heaven Devouring Battle Art?” Feng Ming and Chen Wenhao were shocked, retreating several steps in unison. This legendary technique was renowned as the most powerful and most evil art in the history of the Star Realm. It had sparked the Great Emperor War and led to the formation of the Shattered Star Sea. Even after tens of thousands of years, its name was still known to many experts.

Chen Wenhao frowned, hesitating. “Could it be that you are…”

Chen Wenhao did not dare continue, his voice trailing off. A tremor of fear ran through him at the mention of that supremely powerful name from tens of thousands of years past.

Wu Kuang said proudly, “That’s right. This Lord is Heaven Devourer!”
